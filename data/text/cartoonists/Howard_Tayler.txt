
Howard V. Tayler (born February 29, 1968, in Florida)<ref name="schlock-bio">{{cite web url=http://www.schlockmercenary.com/schlock_author.html  title=Howard V. Tayler: Author, creator, and "artist" for Schlock Mercenary  first=Howard  last=Tayler  publisher=The Tayler Corporation  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archivedate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9O1HBGK?url=http://www.schlockmercenary.com/schlock_author.html  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref> is the award-winning creator of the webcomic Schlock Mercenary.<ref name="comix411">{{cite web url=http://comics.gearlive.com/comix411/article/q308-hugo-awards-neil-gaiman-phil-foglio-howard-tayler/  title=Hugo Awards: Neil Gaiman, Phil Foglio, Howard Tayler and Captain Britain  first=Tom  last=Mason  date=April 7, 2010  publisher=Comics 411  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9K1hB08?url=http://comics.gearlive.com/comix411/article/q308-hugo-awards-neil-gaiman-phil-foglio-howard-tayler/  archivedate=April 21, 2010  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref> He worked as a volunteer missionary for the LDS Church, then graduated from Brigham Young University.<ref name="schlock-bio"/> Using his degree in Musical composition music composition, he started an independent record label.

While working at Novell, Tayler began online publication of Schlock Mercenary. He quit his job at Novell several years later in order to work on the webcomic List of professional webcomic artists full-time.<ref name="daily-herald2009"/> Schlock Mercenary has been nominated multiple times and won the Web Cartoonists Choice Awards in two different categories, and the webcomic has been nominated four times for a Hugo Award.

Tayler spends time regularly during the week drawing at a local comic book and gaming store,<ref name="daily-herald2009"/> as well as producing a weekly writing tips podcast called Writing Excuses with fellow authors Brandon Sanderson, Dan Wells (author) Dan Wells, Mary Robinette Kowal, and producer Jordan Sanderson.<ref name="writing-excuses"/> The podcast has been nominated for a Hugo Award in 2011, 2012 and has won in 2013.

==Early life==
Tayler was born in Florida. In 1985 he moved to Utah (where he still resides) to attend Brigham Young University. After studying there for two years, he volunteered to be a Missionary (LDS Church) missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) before returning to BYU to finish a bachelors degree in Musical composition Music Composition.<ref name="schlock-bio"/>

==Career==
Tayler co-founded an independent record label called Sanctus Records,<ref name="schlock-bio"/> which was aimed primarily at an LDS market. Tayler also worked for Novell as a project manager,<ref name="daily-cartoonist">{{cite web url=http://dailycartoonist.com/index.php/2009/02/12/howard-taylors-journey-to-profitable-webcomics/  title=Howard Taylers journey to profitable webcomics  publisher=The Daily Cartoonist  first=Alan  last=Gardner  date=February 12, 2009  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9Ld3MpH?url=http://dailycartoonist.com/index.php/2009/02/12/howard-taylors-journey-to-profitable-webcomics/  archivedate=April 21, 2010  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref> where he co-authored a guide to administering GroupWise.<ref name="schlock-bio"/>

In 2000, he began publishing his webcomic, Schlock Mercenary. Four years later, Tayler left his job as product line manager at Novell in order to work full-time on the webcomic.<ref name="schlock-bio"/> By 2009, he was able to make enough from the sales of the compilation books and other merchandise to make ends meet and reduce his weekly workload from 80–100 hours down to 40-50.<ref name="daily-herald2009">{{cite web url=http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/article_634a85c6-4f1a-5033-926b-fad53bbaff68.html  title=Orem cartoonist a rising figure in Web-comic world  first=Michael  last=Rigert  publisher=Daily Herald (Utah) The Daily Herald  date=February 11, 2009  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9MgpE6v?url=http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/article_634a85c6-4f1a-5033-926b-fad53bbaff68.html  archivedate=April 21, 2010  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref>

He has been a guest of honor at many genre conventions, including Balticon, CONduit (convention) CONduit, LepreCon, and Life, the Universe, & Everything. His wife, Sandra Tayler Sandra, is also a published author.<ref name="daily-herald2009"/>

==Works==
In March 2000, Tayler coauthored with Ross Phillips and Tay Kratzer the guidebook Administering GroupWise 5.5, written to assist system administrators in managing Novells GroupWise.

Taylers most well-known work is his webcomic, Schlock Mercenary, a comedic webcomic which follows the tribulations of a star-travelling mercenary company in a satiric, mildly dystopian 31st-century space opera setting. Since its debut on June 12, 2000, the comic has updated daily, begun to List of professional webcomic artists support its author,<ref name="daily-cartoonist"/> and been nominated for three Hugo Awards.<ref name="hugonom2009"/><ref name="hugonom2010"/>

Tayler also produces Writing Excuses, along with novelist Mary Robinette Kowal, best-selling fantasy author Brandon Sanderson and horror author Dan Wells (author) Dan Wells.<ref name="writing-excuses">{{cite web url=http://www.writingexcuses.com/2008/02/10/writing-excuses-episode-1-brainstorming/  title=Writing Excuses Episode 1: Brainstorming  date=February 10, 2008  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archivedate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9NUhoTV?url=http://www.writingexcuses.com/2008/02/10/writing-excuses-episode-1-brainstorming/  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref> 
Running weekly since February 10, 2008, this 15-minute writing tips podcast has featured a number of notable guests including Steve Jackson (US game designer) Steve Jackson, Phil Foglio, Brandon Mull, Tracy Hickman, and Patrick Rothfuss.<ref name="writing-excuses"/>

Along with best-selling fantasy author Tracy Hickman and his son, Curtis Hickman Curtis, he illustrated and published XDM: X-Treme Dungeon Mastery.<ref name="roleplaying-pro">{{cite web url=http://www.roleplayingpro.com/2009/07/16/xdm-x-treme-dungeon-mastery-a-dms-review/  title=XDM X-Treme Dungeon Mastery: A DM’s Review  date=July 16, 2009  first=John  last=Lewis  publisher=Roleplaying Pro  accessdate=April 21, 2010  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5p9L1dsOQ?url=http://www.roleplayingpro.com/2009/07/16/xdm-x-treme-dungeon-mastery-a-dms-review/  archivedate=April 21, 2010  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref>
