Yvonne Hutton (née Mullins; born October 31, 1941, died March 1992), was a British comics artist best known for her work on football series Roy of the Rovers.

While attending Poole Art College,<ref name="sque">[http://www.fustar.info/2006/03/23/120/ David Sque interview part 1] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110728132500/http://www.fustar.info/2006/03/23/120/  date=July 28, 2011 }}, Fústar, 23 March 2006</ref> she got work in comics through Colin Pages studio.<ref name="lambiek">{{Cite web url=http://lambiek.net/artists/h/hutton_yvonne.htm title=Yvonne Hutton last= first= date= website=lambiek.net language=en archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180106064648/https://www.lambiek.net/artists/h/hutton_yvonne.htm archive-date=2018-01-06 dead-url= access-date=2018-07-24}}</ref> She drew the "Roy of the Rovers" strip in Tiger (Fleetway) Tiger from 1967–75, and again in 1976–78,<ref>[http://www.royoftherovers.com/behindthescenes/artists.aspx#hutton Roy of the Rovers: The Artists] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715205705/http://www.royoftherovers.com/behindthescenes/artists.aspx#hutton  date=July 15, 2011 }}</ref> as well as a number of back-up strips in Roy of the Rovers weekly, including "Durrells Palace" (1981–85), "Waynes Wolves" (1985–86) "Kevins Chance" (1986–87) "City" (1987–88), and "Terrible Twins" (1988–89).<ref>[http://www.royoftherovers.com/comicstories/rotrstories.aspx Roy of the Rovers Stories] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100515143004/http://www.royoftherovers.com/comicstories/rotrstories.aspx  date=May 15, 2010 }}</ref> She also drew the Roy of the Rovers daily strip in the Daily Star (United Kingdom) Daily Star until she had a car accident in December 1991, which eventually resulted in her passing away three months later in March 1992.<ref name="lambiek" />
