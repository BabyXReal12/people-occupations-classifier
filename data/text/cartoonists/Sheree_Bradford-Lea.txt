Sheree Bradford-Lea is a Canadians Canadian freelance cartoonist and mixed-media artist, based in Ottawa, Ontario.

==Career==
Sheree Bradford-Lea received a M.A. in Psychology from Wilfrid Laurier University.

Her cartoons and humour have been published in various publications since 1994, starting with The Best Contemporary Womens Humor. Her cartoon strip Life Outside The Box was a regular feature in the quarterly publication Homebase: A Forum for Mothers from 1998 until Homebase ceased publication in 2003, and in the monthly publication Cahoots magazine from 2005 until Cahoots ceased publication in 2011; she also wrote humour pieces for both.<ref>Homebase: A Forum For Mothers, Lisa Menard, Editor, Homebase, P.O. Box 4104, Station E, Ottawa, ON K1S 5B1</ref><ref>Cahoots: Conspire to Inspire, Carla Atherton, Managing Editor, Cahoots Magazine, PO Box 6025, Saskatoon, SK S7K 4E4</ref> She was the Editorial Cartoonist at the weekly newspaper The Winchester Press<ref>http://winchesterpress.on.ca/cartoon.html</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.sdglibrary.ca/docs/NewsletterVol4Iss2.pdf  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-10-31  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141031085328/http://www.sdglibrary.ca/docs/NewsletterVol4Iss2.pdf  archivedate=2014-10-31  df= }} page 2</ref> and at the weekly online news publication Blacklocks Reporter,<ref>http://www.blacklocks.ca/sheree/</ref> and is a member of the Association of Canadian Editorial Cartoonists.<ref>http://acec.4ormat.com/members</ref> Her freelance cartoons have been printed and reprinted in various publications, including Horizon Magazine: A Digest of Good Stories and Humour, Herizons, Hip Mama and The New Quarterly. As Randy Glasbergen says: "Having an editor accept one of your cartoons is comparable to having an audience laugh at a joke told by a comedian. Getting that cartoon reprinted in another publication is like a standing ovation." <ref>Glasbergen 1996 p.74</ref> Bradford-Lea artworks have been part of group exhibitions in Ottawa galleries since 2008.<ref>Small Wonders V, Art Gallery, Nepean Sportsplex</ref><ref>Luscious, Britannia Art Gallery</ref><ref>http://sculptureottawa.ca/2014/02/05/2014-best-of-ncns-exhibition/</ref><ref>http://sculptureottawa.ca/2013/09/09/dimensions-artist-profiles-sheree-bradford-lea/</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://nature.ca/en/about-us/museum-news/news/press-releases/canadian-museum-nature-launches-2012-2013-season  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-11-03  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141103062422/http://nature.ca/en/about-us/museum-news/news/press-releases/canadian-museum-nature-launches-2012-2013-season  archivedate=2014-11-03  df= }}</ref> She has also created and donated artworks to raise money for Habitat for Humanity,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://ottawastart.com/story/22362.php  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-10-29  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029083228/http://ottawastart.com/story/22362.php  archivedate=2014-10-29  df= }}</ref> The Canadian Stone Carving Festival (2010-2012,2014),<ref>http://myalgonquin2.algonquincollege.com/articles/algonquin-college-professor-sculpting-for-a-good-cause/</ref>  and the Ikea & OCRI Pull Up A Chair for Breakfast Campaign, with her daughters, Zoë and Monica.<ref>https://www.flickr.com/photos/ocri/4388896022/</ref> Her work is also sold through her own company, SABL Cartoon Creations.

A play The Devil Is In The Details was produced by Flush Ink Productions in 2013, and a stand-alone monologue I Am Supergirl was produced by Sarasvàti Theatre in the same year.I Am Supergirl proved to be a popular piece; additional performances were requested by the United Nations Platform Action Committee and the Legal Education and Access Fund for women. She also teaches classes and workshops on cartooning, other visual art and drama in various locations in Ottawa,<ref>{{cite web  url=https://cca-acc.ca/canterbury/index.php/children-and-youth  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-10-29  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141030004809/https://cca-acc.ca/canterbury/index.php/children-and-youth  archivedate=2014-10-30  df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www7.algonquincollege.com/wellness/wellnessWeek2010/Cartooning.html  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-10-28  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121116122351/http://algonquincollege.com/wellness/wellnessWeek2010/Cartooning.html  archivedate=2012-11-16  df= }}</ref> was the Creative Cartooning and Illustration Professor at Algonquin College from 2009 to 2013, gives talks and writes articles on cartooning.<ref>http://artsaccolade.ca/2014/09/15/guests-can-make-a-worthwhile-detour-to-meet-artists/</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.advancinglearning2012.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/AdvancingLearning2012_ConferenceSchedule.pdf  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-10-29  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029094103/http://www.advancinglearning2012.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/AdvancingLearning2012_ConferenceSchedule.pdf  archivedate=2014-10-29  df= }}</ref><ref>http://issuu.com/greatrivermedia/docs/kt_favourites_20140515_low Kitchissippi Times, May 15, 2014, p. 21</ref>
