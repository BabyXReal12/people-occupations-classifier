
Alexander "Alex" Williams (born 18 October 1967 in London) is an English film animator and cartoonist. He is the son of animator Richard Williams (animator) Richard Williams. He has worked on many animated films, and is the author of the Queens Counsel (comic strip) Queens Counsel cartoon strip in The Times, for which he was awarded the Cartoon Art Trust Awards Cartoon Art Trust Award for Strip Cartooning in October 2017. 

==Early life==
Williams was born in London in 1967. He is the son of Canadian animator Richard Williams.<ref>Mazurkewitch, p.234</ref>, and the grandson of the diplomat George H. Steuart (diplomat) George H. Steuart.<ref>Nelker, p.53</ref> He played the voice of Tiny Tim (A Christmas Carol) Tiny Tim in his fathers 1971 television adaptation of A Christmas Carol (1971 film) A Christmas Carol.<ref name="IMDB"/> He was educated at Westminster School, Camberwell School of Arts and Crafts, and Merton College, Oxford.

==Career==

===Cartoons===
File:Sir Geoffrey Bentwood QC.jpg thumb right 200px Sir Geoffrey Bentwood QC, one of the Queens Counsel characters
Williams and Graham Francis Defries are co-creators of the comic strip Queens Counsel (comic strip) Queens Counsel. It is a satire on law and lawyers, which has appeared in the law pages of The Times newspaper since 1993. It is written under the pseudonyms Steuart and Francis.<ref>[http://www.qccartoon.com/ Queens Counsel Official Website] Retrieved June 2011</ref><ref>[http://www.flipanimation.net/fliplissue12.htm Interview at Flip magazine] Retrieved June 2011</ref> The strip was begun when Williams and Defries were working as research assistants for members of parliament at the House of Commons.<ref>The Jester, Issue 463, p.17</ref>

A number of collections of the cartoons have been published, by Robson Books and Harper Collins.<ref>[https://www.amazon.co.uk/Alex-Steuart-Williams/e/B003CFSPUW Queens Counsel at www.amazon.co.uk] Retrieved June 2011</ref>

Other cartoon strip work includes Writers Block (comic strip) Writers Block, published in the books section of The Times from 2005–6, and The Dealers (comic strip) The Dealers, published in Tatler (1901) The Tatler from 1994–95.<ref>[http://www.alex-williams.com/index.php?page=showart&ID1=5&ID2=3 The Dealers at www.alex.williams.com] Retrieved June 2011</ref> He also illustrated the characters for the Baby Barista blog by fellow ex-barrister Tim Kevan.<ref>[http://www.babybarista.com/ Baby Barista Blog] Retrieved June 2011</ref>

===Animation===
File:TheGreatEggscape 0005 LoRez.jpg thumb right 200px Design by Williams for The Big Egg Hunt.
Williams was a barrister at 12 Kings Bench Walk Chambers in London before leaving in 1996 to pursue a full-time career in film animation,<ref>[http://www.leavinglaw.com//136UserExp83.aspx Interview with Williams at www.leavinglaw.com] Retrieved June 2011</ref> joining Warner Bros. Animation Warner Bros Feature Animation, where he was lead animator on the villain "Ruber", voiced by Gary Oldman, in Quest for Camelot. Williams gave the character a "nervous twitch", a "wrestlers strut" and "big hands with broken nails that look creepy on close-ups".<ref>Mazurkewitch, p.255-6</ref>

His work as an animator includes Who Framed Roger Rabbit (1988), The Princess and the Cobbler (1993), The Lion King (1994), Quest for Camelot (1998), The Iron Giant (1999), The Road to El Dorado (2000), Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron (2002), Piglets Big Movie, Sinbad: Legend of the Seven Seas (2003), Robots (2005 film) Robots (2005) and Open Season (2006 film) Open Season (2006).<ref name="IMDB">[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0929989/ Williams at IMDB.com] Retrieved June 2011</ref>

Williams has also worked on visual effects in Racing Stripes (2005), Monster House (film) Monster House (2006), Underdog (film) Underdog (2007), Beverly Hills Chihuahua, Inkheart (film) Inkheart (2008), Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film) Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2009), Marmaduke (film) Marmaduke (2010) and The Chronicles of Narnia: The Voyage of the Dawn Treader (2010)<ref name="IMDB"/>

He has contributed designs to Deckchair Dreams, a fundraising event organised by the Royal Parks Foundation in support of the London Royal Parks.<ref>[http://www.deckchairdreams.org/alex_williams.htm Deckchair Dreams] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330160937/http://www.deckchairdreams.org/alex_williams.htm  date=30 March 2012 }} Retrieved September 2011</ref> In 2012 he contributed a design for The Big Egg Hunt, a charity fundraiser billed as the worlds largest ever Easter egg hunt.<ref>[http://auction.thebigegghunt.co.uk/lots/the-great-eggscape-by-alex-williams The Big Egg Hunt Official Website] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120306103755/http://auction.thebigegghunt.co.uk/lots/the-great-eggscape-by-alex-williams  date=6 March 2012 }} Retrieved March 2012</ref>

===Teaching===
He lives in London and teaches at various academies and studios including Escape Studios, however now tutors animation at Bucks New University in High Wycombe as a senior lecturer.<ref>[http://www.escapestudios.co.uk/alex-williams/ Escape Studios website] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110709200445/http://www.escapestudios.co.uk/alex-williams  date=9 July 2011 }} Retrieved June 2011</ref><ref>[https://bucks.ac.uk/whoswho/profile/alexander_williams Bucks new Uni Website] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518093551/https://bucks.ac.uk/whoswho/profile/alexander_williams  date=18 May 2015 }} Retrieved May 2015</ref>

Williams has also founded the worlds first online based MA in animation at Buckinghamshire New University, beginning in September 2015.<ref>[http://bucks.ac.uk/courses/postgraduate/MT13DA9/ Bucks Online MA in Animation] Retrieved May 2015</ref><ref>[http://www.alex-williams.com/blog/worlds-first-online-ma-animation.html Worlds first online MA in Animation] Retrieved May 2015</ref>

In 2012 he founded an online animation school, Animation Apprentice.<ref>[http://www.animationapprentice.org Animation Apprentice Official Site] Retrieved August 2012</ref>
