Charles Pugsley Fincher (born December 30, 1945, in San Benito, Texas) is an American cartoonist and lawyer. His cartoons and comics focus on the law.

==Education==
Fincher received his bachelors degree from the University of Texas at Austin in 1968, and he was a Distinguished Military Graduate of St. Marys University School of Law, where he received his Juris Doctor juris doctor in 1971.

==Career==
He was admitted to the State Bar of Texas in 1971 and admitted to practice before the Fifth Circuit and the U.S. District Court, Southern District of Texas. He has practiced law ever since.<ref>[http://www.texasbar.com/AM/Template.cfm?Section=Advanced_Search&template=/Customsource/MemberDirectory/MemberDirectoryDetail.cfm&ContactID=167481 State Bar of Texas lawyer listing]</ref>

In the 1970s, Fincher began publishing his law-related comics and strips. During 1987 he began his long-running Texas-themed political newspaper comic strip, Thadeus & Weez, which ran in 19 Texas newspapers before being retired in 2005.<ref>[http://www.thadeusandweez.com Thadeus & Weez]</ref> Also in 2005, he won the “Best Web Cartoons” in the "Peoples Pick" category from About.com for The Illustrated Daily Scribble.<ref>[http://dailycartoonist.com/index.php/2005/12/31/aboutcom-award-winners-include-garry-trudeau-tom-tomorrow/ The Daily Cartoonist, "Section Awards," Dec. 31, 2005]</ref> Besides Texas newspapers, his editorial cartoons have appeared in the Arizona Daily Star and the Boston Herald, and his work has been reprinted in textbooks and legal journals.<ref>[http://www.baylor.edu/lib/poage/bullock/index.php?id=55849 Baylor University, Collection of Political Materials, cartoon collections]</ref>

For five years, Finchers monthly lawyer strip "Fenwilder & Jones" ran in the ABA Journal.<ref>[http://legalblogwatch.typepad.com/legal_blog_watch/2006/02/new_cartoon_bla.html Law.com, "New cartoon blawg," February 2006]</ref><ref>[http://heinonline.org/HOL/LandingPage?collection=journals&handle=hein.journals/abaj65&div=190&id=&page= HeinOnline, ABA Journal, 1979, with Charles Fincher editorial cartoon]</ref>

Fincher ended his long-running political comic strip, "The Illustrated Daily Scribble," in 2006, because, he was quoted as saying, "The Bush administration is hard to satirize."<ref>[http://dailycartoonist.com/index.php/2006/03/23/illustrated-daily-scribble-comes-to-a-close/ The Daily Cartoonist article, "Illustrated Daily Scribble Comes to a Close," March 23, 2006]</ref> Then, a year later, he resumed "Scribble" and launched two new strips, "Scribble-in-Law" and "Scribble Beach".<ref>[http://www.allbusiness.com/services/business-services-miscellaneous-business/4694744-1.html Editor & Publisher, "Cartoonist Resumes One Scribble and Starts Two Others," Feb. 6, 2007]</ref> His most recent cartoon characters include Bitcher & Prickman, comic law partners.<ref>[http://www.greenlaw.com/Christopher-Green-LL.B/some-lawyers-are-stand-up-cartoons Greenlaw.com, "Some lawyers are stand-up cartoons," Aug. 1, 2009]</ref> He is also the founder of LawComix, a cartoon site for lawyers. And he is the creator of "Legal Isms," 10  comic strips about lawyers, contracted by LexisNexis.<ref>[http://www.theallisonlawfirm.com/HTMLpages/Charles.html Allison law firm biography]</ref>

In 2008, Fincher was commissioned by the ABA’s Council of Appellate Staff Attorneys (CASA) to paint a fantasy illustration of Constitutional law professor Erwin Chemerinsky as a shortstop with the Chicago Cubs, the scholars favorite team. The painting was presented to Chemerinsky at a conference.<ref>[http://texaslawyer.typepad.com/texas_lawyer_blog/2008/11/lawyer-paints-portrait-of-con-law-legend.html Texas Lawyer, "Lawyer paints portrait of Con Law legend," November 18, 2008]</ref>

Fincher practices at The Allison Law Firm, a private firm that specializes in serious injury and wrongful death lawsuits,<ref>[http://theallisonlawfirm.com/HTMLpages/Attorneys.html The Allison Law Firm, list of lawyers]</ref> in South Padre Island, Texas.
