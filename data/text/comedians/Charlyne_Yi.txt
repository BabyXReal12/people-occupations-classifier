
Charlyne Amanda Yi<ref name=cali/> (born January 4, 1986)<ref name=cali>According to the State of California. California Birth Index, 1905-1995. Center for Health Statistics, California Department of Health Services, Sacramento, California. Her performances include music, magic, games, and often audience participation.<ref name="variety">{{cite news  url = http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117960233?refCatId=2453  last = Boyd  first = Betsy  title = Charlyne Yi: 10 Comics to Watch  work = Variety (magazine) Variety  date = 2007-02-26  accessdate = 2007-04-24}}</ref>

Her screenwriting debut, the feature film Paper Heart, won the List of Sundance Film Festival award winners Waldo Salt Screenwriting Award at the 2009 Sundance Film Festival. She is also known for her role as Dr. Chi Park on the Fox medical drama House (TV series) House, the voice of every Ruby on the Cartoon Network animated series Steven Universe, the voice of Chloe Park on We Bare Bears and the voice of Alice on Summer Camp Island.

==Early life==
Yi was born in Los Angeles, California. Her mother, a native of the Philippines, is of Filipino people Filipino and Spanish people Spanish descent{{citation needed date=October 2014}}, and her father is of Chinese Mexican, Indigenous peoples of Mexico Native-Mexican, Koreans Korean, Irish people Irish, Germans German, French people French, and Indigenous peoples of the Americas Native American ancestry.<ref name="variety"/><ref>Black, John, [http://www.colormagazineusa.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=257&Itemid=67 "Falling in Love with Charlyne Yi"], Josefina Bonilla Color Magazine USA</ref><ref>Prentice, Claire, [http://edinburgh-festivals.com/viewpreview.aspx?id=1343 "Preview: Charlyne Yi - Dances on the Moon"], edinburgh-festivals.com, 3/8/2010</ref> She attended the University of California, Riverside before leaving to pursue a full-time career in comedy. She had an early interest in performing when she attended Bloomington High School (California) Bloomington High School and was involved in theatre.

==Career==
File:Charlyne Yi at UCB.jpg thumb Performing in the Garfunkel and Oates Christmas Show at the UCB Theater
Yi began by performing in Bloomington, California.<ref name="variety"/> After high school, she has performed shows in Los Angeles at The Steve Allen Theater, and The Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre. In 2005 and 2006, she performed in the New York Comedy Festival,<ref name="nytimes">{{cite news  url = https://www.nytimes.com/2005/11/03/arts/03come.html?ei=5088&en=cb95649bf2ccab44&ex=1288674000&adxnnl=1&partner=rssnyt&emc=rss&adxnnlx=1163187155-nSxp69Qc6Ss0sEIF+4OcEQw   date = 2005-11-03  accessdate = 2007-04-24  last = Keepnews  first = Peter  work = The New York Times  title = Upstart Comedians Goofing on Andy }}</ref> and in 2007 HBOs U.S. Comedy Arts Festival in Aspen, Colorado Aspen, Colorado.<ref name="variety"/> Yis film debut was in Judd Apatows 2007 film, Knocked Up. In 2008, Yi performed as part of the Apatow for Destruction Live comedy show at Montreals Just For Laughs Festival.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.variety.com/index.asp?layout=festivals&jump=features&id=3182&articleid=VR1117988754   work=Variety   title=Just For Laughs highlights   date=2008-07-10}}</ref>

In the 2009 film Paper Heart, a "hybrid documentary"<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/film-reviews/film-review-paper-heart-1003934187.story  title=Film Review: Paper Heart  accessdate=2009-03-10  deadurl=bot: unknown  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100110055445/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/film-reviews/film-review-paper-heart-1003934187.story  archivedate=January 10, 2010  df= }}</ref> which Yi executive produced in addition to co-writing, she starred as a fictional version of herself, along with Michael Cera.

She has also appeared alongside Saturday Night Live cast member Fred Armisen in the music video for the song "Rabbit Habits" by Philadelphia experimental band Man Man.

Yi and Paul Rust formed the band The Glass Beef. The duo share one electric guitar and both sing lead vocals. In 2006, they released their debut album, The Farewell Album, produced by John Spiker, bassist of Tenacious D.

Yi was featured in the video "Song Away" by Hockey (band) Hockey.<ref>{{cite web  title=Song Away by Hockey with Charlyne Yi  url=http://www.channelapa.com/2009/08/song-away-by-hockey-with-charlyne-yi.html}}</ref> She is currently in the band Sacred Destinies.

In January 2010, Yi performed a duet about break-up lines (the opposite of pick-up lines) with Demetri Martin on the episode "2" for the Comedy Central sketch series Important Things with Demetri Martin. However, the duet was cut from the final edit of the episode.{{fact date=May 2017}} . She also appeared in Lucifer season 3 episode 25 as the angel of death.

From October 2011 to May 2012, Yi starred in Fox Broadcasting Company Foxs series House as Dr. Chi Park, a young doctor with anger-management issues.<ref>[http://www.fox.com/house/bios/charlyne-yi.htm "Bio: Charlyne Yi / Dr. Chi Park"] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111016022834/http://www.fox.com/house/bios/charlyne-yi.htm  date=October 16, 2011 }}, FOX website for the House MD series</ref><ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.channelapa.com/2011/11/charlyne-yi-as-dr-chi-park-on-house.html   title = Charlyne Yi as Dr. Chi Park on House  accessdate = November 9, 2011   date = November 1, 2011   publisher = channelAPA.com}}</ref>

==Non-profit work==
Yi has been involved with Oxfam Oxfam America since 2010, working to raise awareness about poverty and hunger around the world.<ref name="Oxfam">[http://www.oxfamamerica.org/articles/actress-charlyne-yi-fights-hunger-with-oxfam Actress Charlyne Yi fights hunger with Oxfam — Oxfam America<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100921131244/http://www.oxfamamerica.org/articles/actress-charlyne-yi-fights-hunger-with-oxfam  date=September 21, 2010 }}</ref> Yi started the non-profit Caring is Cool in 2011, and hosted a benefit show to raise money for the organization.<ref>[http://charlyneyi.tumblr.com/post/2750901881/caring-is-cool-is-my-new-non-profit-i-made-to-help Charlyne Yi   Caring is Cool is my new NON-PROFIT I made to help<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717112253/http://charlyneyi.tumblr.com/post/2750901881/caring-is-cool-is-my-new-non-profit-i-made-to-help  date=July 17, 2011 }}</ref>
