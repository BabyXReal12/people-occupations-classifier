Keenen Ivory Desuma Wayans, Sr. (born June 8, 1958) is an American actor, comedian, filmmaker, and a member of the Wayans family of entertainers. He first came to prominence as the host and the creator of the 1990–1994 Fox Broadcasting Company Fox sketch comedy series In Living Color. He has produced, directed and/or written a large number of films, starting with Hollywood Shuffle, which he co-wrote, in 1987. A majority of these films have included him and one or more of his brothers and sisters in the cast. One of these films, Scary Movie (2000), which Wayans directed, was the highest-grossing movie ever directed by an African American until it was surpassed by Tim Storys Fantastic Four (2005 film) Fantastic Four in 2005. From 1997 to 1998, he hosted the talk show The Keenen Ivory Wayans Show.<ref>{{cite news title= Will Hip Equal Hit?  work= Los Angeles Times date=August 3, 1997 url= http://articles.latimes.com/1997/aug/03/entertainment/ca-18836 accessdate=2011-11-06 first=Greg last=Braxton}}</ref> Most recently, he was a judge for the eighth season of Last Comic Standing.

==Life and career==
Wayans was born in Harlem, New York City, son of Howell Stouten Wayans, a supermarket manager, and his wife Elvira Alethia (Green), a homemaker and social worker.<ref>Stated on Finding Your Roots, January 19, 2016, PBS</ref><ref>[https://www.pbs.org/weta/finding-your-roots/blog/keenen-ivory-wayans-interactive-family-tree/ PBS]</ref> His father was a devout Jehovahs Witness.<ref>[http://www.filmreference.com/film/9/Keenen-Ivory-Wayans.html Keenen Ivory Wayans Biography (1958–)<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,997920,00.html   work=Time   title=Marlon Wayans   first=Joel   last=Stein   date=September 11, 2000}}</ref><ref>[http://www.themoviechicks.com/mid2004/mctladykillers.html The Movie Chicks – Interview – Marlon Wayans<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> The family later moved to  Manhattans Fulton Houses Fulton housing projects.<ref name= ready>{{cite news  title= Is prime time ready for rudeness? Wayans attended Tuskegee University on an engineering scholarship.<ref name= ready/> He entertained his friends at college with made-up stories about life in New York.<ref name= laughing>{{cite news  title= Laughing with Living Color  work= USA Today  date= April 13, 1990  first= Mike  last= Hughes  url= http://infoweb.newsbank.com/iw-search/we/InfoWeb?p_product=AWNB&p_theme=aggregated5&p_action=doc&p_docid=128129675C049B80&p_docnum=850&p_queryname=3 }}</ref> One semester before graduation, he dropped out of school to focus on comedy.<ref>{{cite news  title= Keenen Wayans gambles and wins on an offbeat Sucka  work= The San Diego Union  date= February 16, 1989  first= Mike  last= McIntyre  page= E3  url= http://infoweb.newsbank.com/iw-search/we/InfoWeb?p_product=AWNB&p_theme=aggregated5&p_action=doc&p_docid=1178C747FA9CF8B5&p_docnum=78&p_queryname=3 }}</ref>

During his first set performing at The Improv in New York, Wayans met Robert Townsend (actor) Robert Townsend, who helped him learn about the comedy business.<ref name= laughing/> Townsend and Wayans drove cross country to Los Angeles together when Wayans moved to Los Angeles in 1980. <ref name= laughing/> They didnt speak for a year afterwards. Wayans worked there as an actor. He had a regular role as a soldier on a television series named "For Love and Honor".<ref name= laughing/>

Townsend wrote, directed, and starred in the movie Hollywood Shuffle; Wayans was co-star and co-writer.<ref name= laughing/> The movies success allowed him to raise the money to make Im Gonna Git You Sucka.<ref name= laughing/>

Fox Broadcasting Company approached Wayans to offer him his own show.<ref name= pokes>{{cite news  title= New Fox Show Pokes Fun at Black Stereotypes  publisher= Greensboro News & Record  location= Greensboro, North Carolina  work= Associated Press  page= B6  date= April 12, 1990  url= http://infoweb.newsbank.com/iw-search/we/InfoWeb?p_product=AWNB&p_theme=aggregated5&p_action=doc&p_docid=0EB191E9F73E3517&p_docnum=837&p_queryname=3 }}</ref> Wayans wanted to produce a variety show, similar to Saturday Night Live, with a cast of people of color that took chances with its content.<ref name="ready"/> Fox gave Wayans a lot of freedom with the show, although Fox executives were a bit concerned about the shows content prior to its television debut.<ref name= pokes/> Wayans created, wrote, and starred in the show, called In Living Color, an American sketch comedy television series that originally ran on the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox Network from 1990 to 1994.
