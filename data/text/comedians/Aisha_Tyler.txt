Aisha N. Tyler (born September 18, 1970)<ref name=filmref /><!--cited source, FilmReference.com, also gives middle initial N.--> is an American actress and comedian. She is known for portraying Andrea Marino in the first season of Ghost Whisperer, voicing Lana Anthony Kane Lana Kane in Archer (TV series) Archer, portraying Dr. Tara Lewis in Criminal Minds and portraying Mother Nature in the The Santa Clause (film series) Santa Clause film series, as well as recurring roles in CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, Talk Soup and Friends. She is a former co-host of CBSs The Talk (U.S. TV series) The Talk,<ref name="eonline1">{{cite web  first= Gina   last= Serpe url = http://www.eonline.com/news/from_talk_soup_talk_aisha_tyler_makes/271160 title = From Talk Soup to The Talk: Aisha Tyler Makes Her Morning-Show Debut! publisher = E! News date = October 24, 2011 accessdate = 2012-01-01}}</ref> and the host of Whose Line Is It Anyway? (U.S. TV series) Whose Line Is It Anyway? Tyler also hosted Ubisofts Electronic Entertainment Expo E3 press conferences from 2012 to 2016, and has made various video game appearances including Halo: Reach and Ubisofts Watch Dogs where her voice and likeness are featured.

==Early life==
Tyler was born in San Francisco, California, the daughter of Robin Gregory, a teacher, and James Tyler, a photographer.<ref name=filmref>{{cite web   title=Aisha Tyler (1970-&nbsp;)   url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/35/Aisha-Tyler.html   website=Film.Reference.com   accessdate=July 28, 2008 archivedate= February 28, 2017  archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20170228194250/http://www.filmreference.com/film/35/Aisha-Tyler.html deadurl=no}}</ref> The family spent one year in Ethiopia and later spent time living in an ashram in the US.

She pursued an early interest in comedy at McAteer High School in San Francisco, which had a special program called School of the Arts, now named Ruth Asawa San Francisco School of the Arts.<ref>{{cite web   title=Adam Carolla Podcast   url=https://archive.org/details/2009.03.10acp-Tyler   date=March 9, 2009   accessdate=April 13, 2008}}</ref> Tyler attended high school with Sam Rockwell, and Margaret Cho. She had a crush on Rockwell, and followed him into acting class one day, leading to her interest in improv and sketch.<ref>{{cite web title=In Bed With Joan - Episode 18: Aisha Tyler url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W4oqc8OdppY website=YouTube accessdate=23 September 2015}}</ref>

Tyler graduated from Dartmouth College in 1992.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.cbswatchmagazine.com/2013/05/talk-the-talk/  title=Talk the Talk  work=CBS Watch!  accessdate=April 15, 2016  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160427031901/http://www.cbswatchmagazine.com/2013/05/talk-the-talk/  archivedate=April 27, 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://now.dartmouth.edu/2013/07/aisha-tyler-92-power-mistakes-forbes title=Aisha Tyler ’92 on the Power of Mistakes (Forbes) publisher= Dartmouth College  website= dartmouth.edu accessdate=April 15, 2016 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160425225313/http://now.dartmouth.edu/2013/07/aisha-tyler-92-power-mistakes-forbes archivedate=April 25, 2016 df=mdy-all}}</ref> She was a member of The Tabard (fraternity) The Tabard, a co-ed fraternity.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web url=http://www.msn.com/en-us/entertainment/gallery/celebs-in-the-ivy-league/ss-AA8guno#image=26 title=Celebs in the Ivy League website= msn.com access-date=2017-03-24}}</ref> At Dartmouth, she co-founded and sang in the Dartmouth College student groups#Dartmouth Rockapellas Dartmouth Rockapellas, an all-female a cappella group devoted to spreading social awareness through song.<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

After briefly working for a San Francisco advertising firm, she toured the country pursuing a comedy career before moving to Los Angeles in 1996.{{Citation needed date=April 2016}}

==Career==
{{BLP sources section date=September 2015}}
File:AishaTyler01.JPG thumb left Tyler signing at a Barnes & Noble bookstore in New York
Tylers career in television took off in 2001 with jobs as the host of Talk Soup and the reality-dating series The Fifth Wheel, although Talk Soup was canceled the following year and Tyler left The Fifth Wheel in 2002 to pursue other interests. Tyler has devoted a significant amount of her time to independent projects, including a role in the play Moose Mating, for which she received an NAACP Image Award. She also wrote, directed, and starred in the independent short film The Whipper. Moving into acting, Tyler featured in Friends as Dr. Charlie Wheeler, Joey Tribbiani Joeys and then Ross Geller Ross girlfriend, in the ninth and tenth seasons. She followed this up with guest spots on CSI: Miami and Nip/Tuck, as well as balancing season-long recurring roles on both CSI: Crime Scene Investigation and 24 (television) 24 during the 2004–2005 television season. She also filmed her own sitcom pilot for CBS, which was not picked up. She has guest-starred on MADtv.

Following her regular role on the CBS series Ghost Whisperer during its first season, Tyler appeared in several films, including The Santa Clause 2, The Santa Clause 3: The Escape Clause, .45 (film) .45, and the comedy Balls of Fury. In 2007, she filmed the thriller Death Sentence (2007 film) Death Sentence and the crime drama Black Water Transit. She also continues to appear on television, with appearances on Boston Legal, Reno 911!, The Boondocks (TV series) The Boondocks, and as a guest film critic on several episodes of At the Movies with Ebert & Roeper, filling in for the absent Roger Ebert while he recuperated from surgery.
File:The Ladies of TV 2012.jpg thumb The Talk co-hosts Julie Chen, Aisha Tyler, Sharon Osbourne, Sara Gilbert, and Sheryl Underwood in 2012

Tyler has moved into print media as a regular contributor to Glamour (magazine) Glamour, Jane (magazine) Jane, and Entertainment Weekly magazines. Her first book, Swerve: A Guide to the Sweet Life for Postmodern Girls, was released in January 2004.<ref>{{cite book last=Tyler first=Aisha title=Swerve: A Guide to the Sweet Life for Postmodern Girls location=New York publisher=Plume year=2004 isbn=0-525-94806-6}}</ref> Tyler plays on the World Poker Tour in the Hollywood Home games for the Futures Without Violence charity. She also made a guest appearance on Kanye Wests single "Slow Jamz", which featured Twista and Jamie Foxx. Philanthropy and charity work are very important to Tyler, and she regularly does volunteer work for the American Red Cross, The Trust for Public Land, Planned Parenthood Federation of America, and the International Rescue Committee.

Tyler appeared in a nude pictorial, along with other celebrities, in the May 2006 issue of Allure (magazine) Allure.<ref>{{cite web  title=Stars Line Up For Naked Spread  url=http://www.contactmusic.com/carla-gugino/news/stars-line-up-for-naked-spread_1028957  publisher=Contactmusic.com  date=April 24, 2007  access-date=July 29, 2008  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080731202413/http://www.contactmusic.com/news.nsf/article/stars%20line%20up%20for%20naked%20spread_1028957  archive-date=July 31, 2008  dead-url=no}}</ref> The annual Nude Issue raises money to combat skin cancer.

In May 2009, it was announced that ABC had given Tyler her own talk show pilot, The Aisha Tyler Show.<ref>{{cite web url = http://www.tvguide.com/News/Aisha-Tyler-Talk-1005895.aspx title = Aisha Tyler Nabs Talk Show Pilot publisher = TVGuide.com accessdate = May 8, 2009 archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20090520111614/http://www.tvguide.com/News/Aisha-Tyler-Talk-1005895.aspx archivedate = May 20, 2009 deadurl = no date = May 8, 2009 last = Joyce Eng}}</ref> In early May 2010, she presented the "Welcome to the Beta" video for Halo: Reach. She also voiced a minor character in the game.<ref>[https://twitter.com/aishatyler/status/9559941260 Off to voice a character in Halo:Reach tomorrow. Dreams really do come true], twitter.com, February 24, 2010.</ref>

In 2009, she began her starring role voicing Lana Kane in the FX (TV channel) FX series Archer (TV series) Archer, which premiered on January 14, 2010<ref>{{cite web last=Eng  first=Joyce  url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/fx-sets-midseason-1012661/  title=FX Sets Midseason Schedule - Todays News: Our Take  publisher=TVGuide.com  date=2009-12-01  accessdate=2017-08-07}}</ref> and has been renewed for a sixth and seventh season, to premiere through 2016.<ref>{{cite web last=Molloy  first=Tim  url=http://www.thewrap.com/fxs-archer-renewed-2-seasons/  title=FXs Archer Renewed for 2 More Seasons  publisher=Thewrap.com  date=  accessdate=2017-08-07}}</ref> In August 2010, Tyler began appearing in a recurring guest spot on The Stephanie Miller Show. The segment is named "Tuesdays With Tyler".<ref>stephaniemiller.com</ref> Tyler appears either in-studio or via phone when she is not otherwise committed to one of her acting roles. While Hal Sparks was out of the country, Tyler filled in as the third member of the Stephanie Miller Sexy Liberal Comedy Tour on three shows in August 2011.<ref>{{cite web url = http://www.sexyliberal.com/2011/06/08/detroit-mi-082011-detroit-opera-house-sexy-liberal-show-with-stephanie-miller-john-fuglesang-and-aisha-tyler title = Stephanie Miller Sexy Liberal Comedy Tour date = June 8, 2011 accessdate = September 21, 2015 website = sexyliberal.com archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20110627004724/http://www.sexyliberal.com/2011/06/08/detroit-mi-082011-detroit-opera-house-sexy-liberal-show-with-stephanie-miller-john-fuglesang-and-aisha-tyler/ archivedate = June 27, 2011}}</ref>

Also in 2009, Tyler performed her stand-up comedy routine, live at the Fillmore Theatre.<ref>https://m.imdb.com/title/tt1361808/</ref>

Starting on July 26, 2011, Tyler started her own weekly podcast, Girl on Guy, where she interviews her favorite celebrity friends and discusses topics guys love. Girl on Guy is available on her website for download using iTunes, mp3, and RSS. The show launched as the No. 4 comedy podcast on iTunes and currently is the No. 2 comedy podcast, and the No. 7 overall podcast on iTunes. The first weekly installment of Girl on Guy featured guest H. Jon Benjamin, the second featured the host of Current TVs former show InfoMania Brett Erlich on August 1, 2011, and the third featured Archer creator Adam Reed on August 9, 2011.

In October 2011, it was announced that Tyler would join the cast of The Talk (U.S. TV series) The Talk as a permanent co-host, replacing Holly Robinson Peete. Her first full week as a co-host was October 24 through October 28, 2011.<ref name="eonline1"/> Tyler is known for being expressive and outspoken on The Talk, especially about African American culture and stereotypes, LGBT rights, and womens rights. Tyler presented Ubisofts press conference at Electronic Entertainment Expo E3 Electronic Entertainment Expo 2012 2012 in June, which received some backlash from fans who didnt believe Tyler was a gamer.<ref>{{cite news title = Aisha Tyler Rants "Ive Been a Gamer Since Before You Could Read." url = http://kotaku.com/5918084/aisha-tyler-rants-ive-been-a-gamer-since-before-you-could-read accessdate = September 19, 2013 newspaper = kotaku.com date = June 13, 2012 last = Evan Narcisse}}</ref> This caused Tyler to respond with a poem about how she has been playing video games "since you were a twinge in the left side of your daddys underoos".<ref>{{cite web title = Dear Gamers: url = https://www.facebook.com/notes/aisha-tyler/dear-gamers/10151040991508993 work = facebook.com accessdate = 19 September 2013 date = June 12, 2012}}</ref> She returned to host the publishers press conference Electronic Entertainment Expo 2013 the following year. Tylers second book, Self-Inflicted Wounds: Heartwarming Tales of Epic Humiliation, debuted in July 2013, later becoming a The New York Times Bestseller List New York Times bestseller; it was inspired by questions asked of guests on Girl on Guy.

In March 2013, Tyler was confirmed to be the new host of the American version of Whose Line Is It Anyway? (U.S. TV series) Whose Line Is It Anyway?.<ref>{{cite news title = His line: Improv master Ryan Stiles returns to the PAC url = http://www.wenatcheeworld.com/news/2013/feb/28/his-line-improv-master-ryan-stiles-returns-to-the/ accessdate = September 19, 2013 newspaper = wenatcheeworld.com date = February 28, 2013 last = Rachel Hansen}}{{subscription required}}</ref> She also appears briefly as herself in the video game Watch Dogs and is featured in the music video for the "Weird Al" Yankovic song "Tacky (song) Tacky". Tyler was also the original voice for the character List of characters in the BioShock series#Daisy Fitzroy Daisy Fitzroy in the 2013 video game BioShock Infinite, but her recordings were not used<ref>{{Cite tweet  user=aishatyler  number=324534447804399617  date = 17 April 2013  title=@Vinci_022 youre right! I recorded that role, but just found out they didnt use my recording. theres 47 minutes Ill never get back. }}</ref> and the role went to Kimberly Brooks. She was nominated for Personality of 2014 in Golden Joystick Award Golden Joystick Award 2014.

In June 2015, it was announced that Tyler landed a recurring role on the eleventh season of Criminal Minds. Although she served as a temporary replacement for Jennifer Love Hewitt, who was on maternity leave,<ref>{{cite web url = http://www.inquisitr.com/2193773/criminal-minds-season-11-aisha-tyler-recurring-as-best-replacement-for-kate/ title = Criminal Minds Season 11: Aisha Tyler will recur as Hotch looks to replace Kate date = June 22, 2015 accessdate = September 21, 2015 website = inquisitr.com last = Meredith Jacobs}}</ref> her status was elevated to main cast member in season 12.

In 2016, Tyler started a Kickstarter campaign to fund her directorial feature film debut, Axis (film) Axis.<ref>[https://variety.com/2016/film/news/aisha-tyler-directing-debut-kickstarter-axis-1201752057/ "Aisha Tyler to make feature directing debut on Kiickstarter-funded Axis."] "Variety", Retrieved January 16, 2017.</ref> The film was shot over seven days in May 2016.<ref>{{cite web   url = https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/d3wq7k/axis-director-aisha-tyler-is-proving-hollywood-doesnt-know-its-audience   title = Axis Director Aisha Tyler Is Proving Hollywood Doesnt Know Its Audience   date = 6 March 2018   accessdate = 24 April 2018   publisher = VICE   author = Candice Frederick }}</ref><ref>{{cite web   url = http://deadline.com/2018/01/aisha-tyler-axis-sundance-video-interview-1202266730/   title = Aisha Tyler Shot 65 Pages A Day For Ambitious Directorial Debut, ‘Axis’ — Sundance Studio   date = 25 January 2018   accessdate = 24 April 2018   publisher = Deadline   author = Matt Grobar }}</ref> Axis was released via video-on-demand on April 10, 2018.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.talkhouse.com/aisha-tylers-guide-to-recovering-from-disaster/ title=Aisha Tyler’s Guide to Recovering From Disaster last=Tyler first=Aisha work="Talkhouse" date=April 10, 2018 accessdate=April 11, 2018}}</ref>

On the June 15, 2017, episode of The Talk (TV series) The Talk, Tyler announced that she would be leaving the show at the end of the seventh season due to her busy schedule with three other television shows and directing films. She said she would return as a guest host and to promote her various projects.<ref>{{cite web author=Maureen Lee Lenker  url=http://ew.com/tv/2017/06/15/aisha-tyler-leaving-talk/  title=Aisha Tyler leaving The Talk  publisher=Ew.com  date=2017-06-15  accessdate=2017-08-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web author=Hilary Lewis  url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/aisha-tyler-leaving-cbs-talk-1013879  title=Aisha Tyler Leaving CBS The Talk  work=The Hollywood Reporter  date=2017-06-15  accessdate=2017-08-07}}</ref>
