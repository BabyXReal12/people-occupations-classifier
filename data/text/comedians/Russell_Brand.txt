
Russell Edward Brand<ref name="Family">{{cite news last=Barratt first=Nick title=Family Detective: Russell Brand url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/portal/main.jhtml?xml=/portal/2007/03/24/nosplit/ftdet124.xml work=The Daily Telegraph accessdate=24 March 2007 location=London date=24 March 2007}}</ref> (born 4 June 1975)<ref>{{cite news title=Monitor newspaper=Entertainment Weekly date=1 June 2012 issue=1209/1210 page=35}}</ref> is an English comedian, actor, radio host, author, and activist.

After beginning his career as a stand-up comedian and later becoming an MTV presenter, Brand first achieved renown in 2004 as the host of Big Brothers Big Mouth, a Big Brother (UK) Big Brother spin-off. In 2007, he had his first major film role in St Trinians (film) St Trinians, and the following year he landed a major role in the romantic comedy-drama Forgetting Sarah Marshall; the film led to him starring in a spin off, the rock comedy Get Him to the Greek, alongside Jonah Hill in 2010. He also worked as a voice actor in the animated films Despicable Me in 2010, Hop (film) Hop in 2011, and Despicable Me 2 in 2013, and played the title character of the 2011 remake of the romantic comedy Arthur (2011 film) Arthur. In 2013, he released the successful stand-up special Messiah Complex.

Since guest editing an edition of British political weekly New Statesman in 2013,<ref name=NS1>{{cite web last1=Editorial title=In this weeks New Statesman: Russell Brand guest edit url=http://www.newstatesman.com/staggers/2013/10/weeks-new-statesman-russell-brand-guest-edit website=New Statesman publisher=New Statesman accessdate=5 September 2014}}</ref> Brand has become known as a public activist and campaigner, and has spoken on a wide range of political and cultural issues, including wealth inequality, substance addiction addiction, corporate capitalism, climate change, and media bias.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2013/nov/05/russell-brand-democratic-system-newsnight title=Russell Brand: we deserve more from our democratic system author=Russell Brand date= work=the Guardian accessdate=11 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bigissue.com/features/interviews/5153/russell-brand-interview-capitalism-is-causing-more-suffering-than-isis title=Russell Brand interview: "Capitalism is causing more suffering than Isis" author= date= work=Big Issue accessdate=11 March 2016}}</ref> In 2014, Brand launched his political-comedy web series The Trews (Russell Brand) The Trews, released a book entitled Revolution (book) Revolution, and began work on a The Emperors New Clothes (2015 film) documentary about financial inequality with Michael Winterbottom.

Over the course of his career, Brand has been the subject of frequent media coverage and controversy for issues such as his promiscuity and drug use, his outrageous behaviour at various award ceremonies, his dismissal from MTV and resignation from the BBC, and his two-year marriage with singer Katy Perry. He has incorporated many of his controversial public antics into his comedic material. In 2015 a biographical documentary called Brand: A Second Coming was released.

==Early life==
Russell Edward Brand was born in Orsett Hospital in Grays, Essex, England. He is the only child of Barbara Elizabeth (née Nichols) and photographer Ronald Henry Brand.<ref name=Family/> Brands parents separated when he was six months old, and he was raised by his mother.<ref name="Sawyer">Miranda Sawyer [https://www.theguardian.com/media/2008/nov/09/russell-brand-sachsgate "Brand on the run"] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160506122617/http://www.theguardian.com/media/2008/nov/09/russell-brand-sachsgate  date=6 May 2016 }}, The Observer, 9 November 2008.</ref><ref>{{cite web author=Sean ONeill Crime Editor url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/stage/comedy/article4950820.ece title=Russell Brand: Im a spiritual gent with a crazed lust for glamour publisher=Entertainment.timesonline.co.uk accessdate=17 January 2012}}</ref>

When Brand was 8, his mother contracted uterine cancer and then breast cancer one year later. While she underwent treatment, Brand lived with relatives. When he was 14, he suffered from bulimia nervosa. When he was 16, he left home because of disagreements with his mothers partner. Brand then started to use illegal drugs such as cannabis, amphetamines, Lysergic acid diethylamide LSD, and MDMA ecstasy.<ref name="Relative">{{cite web author=Sean ONeill Crime Editor url=http://women.timesonline.co.uk/tol/life_and_style/women/the_way_we_live/article3074117.ece title=Relative values: Russell Brand and his mother, Barbara publisher=Women.timesonline.co.uk accessdate=17 January 2012}}</ref>

Brand says he had a "strange relationship" with his father, whom he saw sporadically and who took him to visit prostitutes during a trip to Thailand when Brand was a teenager.<ref name="Sawyer"/><ref name="NPR Booky Wook">{{cite web url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=101881014 title=A Comedians Memoir Of Sex, Drugs And Stand-Up last=Simon first=Scott date=14 March 2009 work=Weekend Edition Saturday publisher=National Public Radio accessdate=14 July 2009}}</ref> He made his theatrical debut at the age of 15 in a school production of Bugsy Malone, and then began work as a film extra. Brand attended Grays School Media Arts College and in 1991, he was accepted to the Italia Conti Academy and had his first year of tuition funded by Essex County Council. After his first year at Italia Conti Academy, Brand was expelled for illegal drug use and poor attendance.<ref name=ddrb>{{cite news last=Brand first=Russell title=Desert Island Discs publisher=BBC}}</ref>

==Political activism==

Brand was selected by the Dalai Lama to host the Buddhist leaders 2012 youth event in Manchester. In April 2012, Brand testified in front of a parliamentary committee about drug addiction, sharing his experiences and view that drugs should be decriminalised. He said, "Im not a legal expert. Im saying that, to a drug addict, the legal aspect is irrelevant. If you need to get drugs, you will. The criminal and legal status, I think, sends the wrong message. Being arrested isnt a lesson, its just an administrative blip."<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/russell-brand-testifies-before-a-parliamentary-committee-about-drug-addiction  title=Russell Brand testifies before a parliamentary committee about drug addiction agency=Associated Press  publisher=CBS News  date=24 April 2012 accessdate=8 February 2015}}</ref> Part of this testimony was included in a BBC Three documentary, Russell Brand: From Addiction to Recovery, that aired in December 2012. In June 2013, Brand appeared in a video in support of Chelsea Manning.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/06/celebrity-bradley-manning-video-93041.html#ixzz2WgfpNmys  title=Celeb video: ‘I am Bradley Manning’ - Patrick Gavin  publisher=Politico.Com  date=20 June 2013  accessdate=30 July 2013}}</ref>

===Substance use===
File:Russell Brand.jpg thumb upright Brand in March 2008
The media published articles on Brand during his drug-using period, typically in relation to incidents, and his public profile has since been associated with this era. Drug-related issues led to Brands arrest on 12 occasions.<ref name=BarbaraEllenInterview>{{cite news last=Ellen first=Barbara title=Interview with Russell Brand url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2006/jun/18/broadcasting.arts publisher=The Observer accessdate=18 June 2006 location=London date=18 June 2006}}</ref> Brand was ejected from the Gilded Balloon in Edinburgh, Scotland and following a subsequent show in the city in 2004, a reviewer stated that "youd rather hug him than hit him", as he had embraced recovery by this point. Following the cessation of his use, Brand revealed through his stand-up performances that he introduced his drug dealer to Kylie Minogue during his time at MTV<ref name=TimeOut>{{cite web last=Brand first=Russell title=Russell Brand on Heroin url=http://www.au.timeout.com/sydney/books/features/4329/russell-brand-on-heroin publisher=Time Out Sydney deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714181622/http://www.au.timeout.com/sydney/books/features/4329/russell-brand-on-heroin archivedate=14 July 2014 df=dmy-all}}</ref> and masturbated a stranger in a public toilet for a television programme.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.mediaite.com/tv/russell-brand-did-what-in-a-public-bathroom-when-he-was-a-crack-and-heroin-addict/ title=Russell Brand Did What In A Public Bathroom When He Was A Crack And Heroin Addict? work=Mediaite last=Wilstein first=Matt date=11 June 2013 accessdate=13 December 2014}}</ref> In January 2014, Brand described his first experience with heroin as "blissful".<ref name="Cam" />

Brand has abstained from drug use since 13 December 2002.<ref>{{cite web url= https://twitter.com/rustyrockets/status/543780258965889024  title=Today I am 12 years clean from drugs&alcohol  author=Russell Brand publisher=Twitter date=13 December 2014 accessdate=26 January 2015}}</ref> He is a patron of the Focus 12 drug treatment programme after his own use of the service. Brands sobriety was instigated by his agent, John Noel (producer) John Noel, after Brand was apprehended using heroin in a bathroom during a Christmas party. Brand cites his practice of transcendental meditation as a significant factor in his recovery from drug dependence.<ref>{{cite news work=The New York Times title=Look Whos Meditating Now date=20 March 2011 url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/20/fashion/20TM.html first=Irina last=Aleksander}}</ref> Brand organised three fundraisers for Focus 12 in London, Dublin and Belfast in 2009, and has also acted as a "Twelve-step program#Sponsorship sponsor" for numerous people during the rehabilitation stage of their treatment process.<ref>{{cite web title=Russell Brand url=http://www.focus12.co.uk/russell-brand/ work=Focus 12 publisher=Focus 12 accessdate=8 April 2014 year=2014 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307115259/http://www.focus12.co.uk/russell-brand archivedate=7 March 2014 df=dmy-all}}</ref>
