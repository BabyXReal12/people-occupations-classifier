
Seth Morris (born May 21, 1970) is an American actor, comedian, and writer known for his recurring roles on programs such as Go On (TV series) Go On, Happy Endings (TV series) Happy Endings, The Hotwives of Orlando, Kroll Show, and Childrens Hospital (TV Series) Childrens Hospital.

==Early life==
Morris is originally from Marin County, California - the younger of two boys - and attended Novato High School. After graduating, not knowing what he wanted to do, he went back and forth between traveling and community college.<ref name="BA">{{cite web title=BA #027: Seth Morris url=http://boxangeles.com/2014/06/02/ba-027-seth-morris/ website=Box Angeles podcast}}</ref> This included a stint at University of California, Santa Cruz UC Santa Cruz and a six-month bicycling trip across the country, from San Francisco to Florida.<ref name="BA"/>

Morris has worked numerous odd jobs including working with developmentally disabled people at group homes, teaching kids and a health food / yoga store in NYC.<ref name="BA"/>

==Career==
Morris started a career in comedy after moving to New York City in 1983, performing at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre (UCB) as an improviser and sketch comedian.

He later moved to LA and became the first Artistic Director of the theaters Los Angeles division for many years and was a member of the four-man sketch group "The Naked Babies" with comedians Rob Corddry, Brian Huskey, and John Ross Bowie. After stepping down from that position, he started out as the first staff writer hired by Funny or Die.<ref name="BA"/>

He currently writes and appears with a recurring role on Corddrys Adult Swim series Childrens Hospital (TV Series) Childrens Hospital. Morris also recurred as Scotty on the American Broadcasting Company ABC comedy series Happy Endings (TV series) Happy Endings and as Danny on the NBC comedy series Go On (TV series) Go On. Other programs Morris appeared in regularly included Late Night with Conan OBrien, the HBO sketch series Funny or Die Presents, and the MTV prank show Damage Control (TV series) Damage Control.

Morris has also made numerous guest appearances on comedy programs such as Curb Your Enthusiasm, Parks and Recreation, The League, Reno 911!, Crossballs, Its Always Sunny In Philadelphia, Maron (TV series) Maron, How I Met Your Mother, Broad City, Big Lake (TV series) Big Lake, Human Giant, and Nick Swardsons Pretend Time. He has appeared in films such as The Dictator (2012 film) The Dictator, Step Brothers (film) Step Brothers, Blackballed: The Bobby Dukes Story, and I Love You, Man. In 2012, Morris starred in the comedic web series First Dates with Toby Harris, produced by Yahoo! and Funny or Die.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://screen.yahoo.com/firstdates/  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2012-06-05  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120605064628/http://screen.yahoo.com/firstdates/  archivedate=2012-06-05  df= }}</ref>

Morris is a frequent writer and director for the humor website Funny Or Die. He is also known for his regular appearances on the Comedy Bang! Bang! podcast and television series, most often playing Bob Ducca, the hypochondriac ex-stepfather of host Scott Aukerman. Morris also hosted his own podcast on the Earwolf podcasting network, Affirmation Nation with Bob Ducca, which debuted in 2011, ran for 144 episodes, and stopped airing on February 29, 2012. As of October 1, 2014, Affirmation Nation released its first of a new run of episodes. However, on May 6, 2015 he published the last episode of Affirmation Nation.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.earwolf.com/episode/releasing-the-bully/ title=Releasing the Bully, episode #223 of Affirmation Nation with Bob Ducca on Earwolf website=www.earwolf.com language=en-US access-date=2017-08-20}}</ref> He wrote for the Comedy Central sketch series Kroll Show. Morris has also costarred in the Hulu series The Hotwives The Hotwives of Orlando.
