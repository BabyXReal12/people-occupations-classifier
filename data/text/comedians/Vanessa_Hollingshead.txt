
Vanessa Hollingshead is an United States American actress and stand-up comedian who has performed on many comedy programs including Comedy Central Presents, Jongleurs Live At Jongleurs, Just For Laughs, and Jim Breuer The Jim Breuer Show. She has also acted in a number of films, including a small role in the 1999 film, Ghost Dog: The Way of the Samurai.<ref name="IMDb.com">{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0391127/ title=Vanessa Hollingshead Filmography at IMDb.com date=2009-03-28 publisher=IMDb.com accessdate=2009-03-28}}</ref><ref>{{ cite web   url = https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0165798/awards   year=2001   publisher = Internet Movie Database   accessdate = 2009-03-28   title = Awards for Ghost Dog: The Way of the Samurai }}</ref>

==Early life==
Hollingshead was born in New York City. Her father is Michael Hollingshead, a British-born researcher who performed some of the first experiments with psychedelic drugs and hallucinogens, and who introduced Timothy Leary and Paul McCartney to LSD.<ref>{{cite web url=http://events.nbcnewyork.com/new-york-ny/events/show/86355483-vanessa-hollingshead-john-fugelsang title=Vanessa Hollingshead Biography at NBC date=2009-03-25 publisher=NBC accessdate=2009-03-28}}</ref>

==Career==
After years working as an temporary work office temp, in 1994 Hollingshead performed in an open mic night at the end of a four-hour-long night, claiming at the time "Even Richard Pryor wouldnt be funny at this point".<ref>{{cite web url=http://vocationvacations.com/DreamJobHolidays/vanessa-hollingshead.php title=Test the dream job with comedian Vanessa Hollingshead in New York City year=2008 publisher=VocationVocation accessdate=2009-03-28}}</ref> Nine months later Hollingshead performed a twelve-minute slot at The Comic Strip Live on Second Avenue (Manhattan) Second Avenue, New York City. As a result of this performance she was offered a number of development deals and an offer to appear on The Drew Carey Show, which she turned down. 
In 1996 she appeared on the Tompkins Square show hosted by Jeffrey Ross in her first television appearance. Further television appearances followed in 1997 and 1998, on Jongleurs Live At Jongleurs and Jim Breuer The Jim Breuer Show.
Whilst continuing with both live stand-up and television appearances (most notably when writing and appearing in Comedy Central Comedy Central Presents in 2004), Hollingshead also appeared in several films. As well as appearing in Ghost Dog: The Way of the Samurai, she appeared with ventriloquist act Otto & George and Jim Breuer again in the 2002 film American Dummy, and opposite Greg Fitzsimmons and Pete Correale in the 2003 film The Gynecologists.<ref name="IMDb.com"/>
Hollingshead also runs comedy workshops for aspiring comedians.<ref>{{cite news url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/northamerica/usa/newyork/740976/Stand-up-for-the-virgin-comic.html title=Stand Up For The Virgin Comic date=2007-04-27 publisher=Daily Telegraph accessdate=2009-03-28   location=London   first=Kieran   last=Falconer}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hollingshead was married to Lucien Hold, the founder and manager of The Comic Strip Live in New York, whom she had first met when she appeared there in 1995. Hold died in 2004 from complications arising from scleroderma. This led to Hollingshead becoming an active member of The Scleroderma Foundation, an organization which campaigns for stem-cell research to find a cure.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.scleroderma.org/news/news2007/news2007_comedy-event2.shtm title=Roster Set For Scleroderma Foundation Comedy Benefit date=2007-05-09 publisher=Daily Telegraph accessdate=2009-03-28  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071121092935/http://www.scleroderma.org/news/news2007/news2007_comedy-event2.shtm  archivedate=2007-11-21}}</ref>
