
Jason Mantzoukas ({{IPAc-en m æ n ˈ z uː k ə s}}; born December 18, 1972) is an United States American comedic actor best known for his recurring role as Rafi in the FX (TV channel) FX comedy series The League and as Nadal in The Dictator (2012 film) The Dictator.

Mantzoukas has appeared in such films as Sleeping with Other People, They Came Together, and Conception (film) Conception. He had a recurring role on Parks and Recreation as Dennis Feinstein and has appeared on Brooklyn Nine-Nine as Adrian Pimento and on The Good Place as Derek Hoffstetler. He also co-hosts the popular film discussion podcast How Did This Get Made? alongside Paul Scheer and June Diane Raphael.

==Early life and education==
Mantzoukas was born in Nahant, Massachusetts Nahant, the son of Cynthia (née Mourousas) and William Mantzoukas.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/jason-mantzoukas/bio/673037/ title=Jason Mantzoukas Biography website=TV Guide}}</ref> He has described himself as being of "100 percent Greeks Greek" descent.<ref name="entertainmentweekly"/><ref>{{cite web last=Jones first=Patty url=https://www.straight.com/article-679936/vancouver/dictator-wields-comic-arsenal title=The Dictator wields a comic arsenal website=The Georgia Straight date=May 9, 2012 accessdate=October 12, 2013}}</ref> Mantzoukas was an accomplished drummer during his time at Swampscott High School, anchoring a band named Slygoul. He attended Middlebury College in Middlebury, Vermont.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.middlebury.edu/giving/difference/toolkit/Arts title=Arts {{!}} Middlebury website=Middlebury College accessdate=August 2, 2016}}</ref> Mantzoukas lived in the Middle East for a few years after graduation, studying musicology.<ref name="entertainmentweekly">{{cite web last=Rottenberg first=Jason url=http://www.ew.com/article/2012/05/18/dictators-jason-mantzoukas title=The Dictators Jason Mantzoukas date=May 18, 2012 website=Entertainment Weekly accessdate=August 2, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Mantzoukas began performing improvisational comedy while attending Middlebury College as a part of the Otter Nonsense Players. After college, Mantzoukas studied music and lived abroad for several years, traveling to North Africa and the Middle East. After moving to New York in 1998, he began performing comedy regularly at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater (UCBT), where he was a member of the improv team "Mother", one of the UCBTs earliest house teams. He continues to perform in the weekly show "Soundtrack", an improvised show based on iPod playlists from audience members.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.jesterjournal.com/IntMantzoukas.htm title=The Jester Interview: Jason Mantzoukas last=Shashoua first=Michael website=The Jester Journal}}</ref> Mantzoukas continues to perform and teach at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater in Los Angeles.

Mantzoukas has appeared in movies such as Baby Mama (film) Baby Mama, I Hate Valentines Day, and The Dictator (2012 film) The Dictator and has made guest appearances in comedy programs such as The Life & Times of Tim, Parks and Recreation, Enlightened (TV series) Enlightened, Broad City, Kroll Show, Modern Family, The Good Place, and Brooklyn Nine-Nine. He currently co-hosts the Earwolf podcast How Did This Get Made? with Paul Scheer and June Diane Raphael. 

Although he is often cast as characters of Middle Eastern ethnicity, Mantzoukas is of Greek descent.<ref>{{cite web last=Jones first=Patty url=https://www.straight.com/article-679936/vancouver/dictator-wields-comic-arsenal title=The Dictator wields a comic arsenal website=Straight.com date=May 10, 2012 accessdate=May 30, 2012}}</ref> Mantzoukas and comedian Ed Herbstman performed as comedy duo "The Mantzoukas Brothers" for a number of years, and they were named "Best Improv Duo" by Time Out New York magazine in 2006.<ref>{{cite web url=http://bestnewyorkcomedy.com/tag/the-mantzoukas-brothers/ title=The Mantzoukas Brothers website=Best New York Comedy accessdate=October 12, 2013}}</ref> Mantzoukas appeared in the recurring roles of Rafi on the FX (TV channel) FX comedy The League and Eagle Wing in the "Gigolo House" sketches on Comedy Centrals sketch series Kroll Show.

Mantzoukas co-wrote the screenplay for the 2014 Ice Cube and Kevin Hart comedy Ride Along (film) Ride Along. He has also served as a consulting writer and producer on comedy programs such as Childrens Hospital and Portlandia (TV series) Portlandia.<ref>{{cite web url=http://sfsketchfest.com/x/performers/jason-mantzoukas/ archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140226190100/http://sfsketchfest.com/x/performers/jason-mantzoukas/ title=Jason Mantzoukas website=SF Sketchfest year=2012 archivedate=February 26, 2014}}</ref>

Since 2018, he has replaced T.J. Miller as the voice of Mr. Mucus for the Mucinex commercials.

He recently began doing live readings of movie monologues at live How Did This Get Made? podcast recordings for fans if they bring him scripts.
