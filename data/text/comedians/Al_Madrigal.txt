
Alessandro Liborio "Al" Madrigal (born July 4, 1971)<ref name=TheCaptain>[http://www.cbs.com/primetime/the_captain/bio/al_madrigal/bio.php "Welcome to The Captain, Al Madrigal bio," CBS.com] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090126202308/http://www.cbs.com/primetime/the_captain/bio/al_madrigal/bio.php  date=2009-01-26 }}</ref> is an American comedian and actor. He was a regular correspondent on The Daily Show with Jon Stewart and is a co-founder of the All Things Comedy podcast network, alongside Bill Burr. He is known for his co-starring roles in the CBS prime-time situation comedies Gary Unmarried and Welcome to The Captain, as well as NBCs About a Boy (TV series) About A Boy.<ref name=TheCaptain /> He was a regular sketch performer on The Late Late Show (CBS TV series) The Late Late Show<ref>{{IMDb name 1371339}}</ref> with Craig Ferguson, and has also appeared on Conan (talk show) Conan and Jimmy Kimmel Live!. Madrigal is a series regular in the Showtime (TV network) Showtime dark comedy Im Dying Up Here.

==Early life==
Madrigal was born in San Francisco, California. He grew up in San Franciscos Sunset District, San Francisco, California Inner Sunset District, where his neighbors included future comedians Michael Pritchard (comedian) Mike Pritchard and Margaret Cho. He is of Mexicans Mexican (from Tijuana) descent.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.sfgate.com/entertainment/article/Al-Madrigal-to-play-the-Punch-Line-3271245.php  title=Al Madrigal to play the Punch Line  author=Tamara Straus  publisher=San Francisco Chronicle  accessdate=2013-06-25  date=March 4, 2010}}</ref><ref>[https://twitter.com/almadrigal/status/413468097345880065 Twitter]</ref> He attended Ecole Notre Dame Des Victoires,<ref name=":0">{{Citation last=Comedy Central title=This Is Not Happening - Al Madrigal - Becoming a Latino Comic - Uncensored date=2016-03-21 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pgsRw4M6Ogo&feature=youtu.be accessdate=2018-01-16}}</ref> a private Catholic school in San Francisco that emphasizes instruction of French language and culture.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.ndvsf.org/school/about/ title=About date=2015-10-22 work=Ecole Notre Dame des Victoires access-date=2018-01-16 language=en-US}}</ref> He attended St. Ignatius College Preparatory High School for the class of 1989. He then attended the University of San Francisco, but dropped out shortly afterward.

Madrigal worked for 10 years in a human resources staffing agency run by his family, where one of his main responsibilities was firing people. He often worked humor into the job. For example, he once called a man into his own office and started shouting racial epithets while doing a Nicolas Cage impression. He credits his experiences at the staffing company with preparing him for stand-up comedy: "I was in so many scary situations ... by the time I got on stage, I had no stage fright. Speaking in front of a group was nothing."<ref>{{cite web last1=Borrelli first1=Christopher title=Al Madrigal: A Comic Who Knows How to Let Go url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-08-01/entertainment/ct-ent-0801-al-madrigal-20130801_1_john-hodgman-comic-al-madrigal website=Chicago Tribune publisher=chicagotribune.com accessdate=31 May 2015}}</ref> In 1998, he decided to pursue a full-time career in comedy.<ref>[http://www.latinostandup.com/pages/almad.html “Al Madrigal,” LatinoStandup.com] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080727003611/http://www.latinostandup.com/pages/almad.html  date=2008-07-27 }}</ref>

==Career==
===Stand-up comedy===
Madrigal’s stand-up comedy is story-based, centering on his personal life, family, and the confusion caused by his multiethnic background.<ref>{{cite web last1=George first1=Doug title=In English, Madrigal is doing just fine url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2009-07-31/entertainment/0907290432_1_comedy-arts-festival-madrigal-mexican website=Chicago Tribune publisher=chicagotribune.com accessdate=1 June 2015}}</ref> Early in his comedy career, he was often pigeonholed as a “Latino comic.” Madrigal says he has been criticized as not being Latino enough, such as for not speaking Spanish.<ref name="V Digital Services">{{cite web last1=Minazad first1=Orly title=THE DAILY SHOW CORRESPONDENT AL MADRIGAL IS FIGHTING LATINO STEREOTYPES — WITH COMEDY url=http://www.laweekly.com/arts/the-daily-show-correspondent-al-madrigal-is-fighting-latino-stereotypes-with-comedy-5534030 website=LA Weekly publisher=V Digital Services accessdate=1 June 2015}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

Madrigal began his career in San Franciscos comedy clubs, both as a solo performer and as a member of the sketch group Fresh Robots, which he co-founded. In 2002, he enjoyed his first major exposure in two comedy festivals: SF Sketchfest, as part of Fresh Robots,<ref>[http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/chronicle/archive/2002/01/03/DD29268.DTL&type=printable "Jokers Run Wild," January 3, 2002]</ref> and the "New Faces" showcase of the Just for Laughs Festival in Montreal.<ref name=BG>[http://www.boston.com/ae/theater_arts/articles/2008/06/27/hes_starting_over____again/ "Hes Starting Over – Again," The Boston Globe, June 27, 2008]</ref>

In 2004, Madrigal won a jury award for best stand-up comedian at the U.S. Comedy Arts Festival in Aspen, Colorado. After winning the award, he signed a talent holding deal with CBS.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070808113343/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/search/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1000797090 "Comedy ice," The Hollywood Reporter, March 1, 2006]</ref>

Madrigals Comedy Central Presents half-hour special premiered in July 2005. In April 2013, Madrigal’s first one-hour special, "Why Is The Rabbit Crying?," also premiered on Comedy Central. The special was named one of the top 10 comedy specials of 2013 by both Westword and The Village Voice and was praised for "deconstructing stereotypes rather than enforcing them" and "milking incongruity between expectations and reality to hilarious effect."<ref>{{cite web last1=Graham first1=Byron title=Ten Best Comedy Specials of 2013 url=http://www.westword.com/arts/ten-best-comedy-specials-of-2013-5798899 website=Westword publisher=Denver Westword, LLC accessdate=10 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last1=Frank first1=Aaron title=Top 10 Stand-Up Comedy Specials of 2013 url=http://www.villagevoice.com/music/top-10-stand-up-comedy-specials-of-2013-6593202 website=The Village Voice publisher=Village Voice, LLC accessdate=10 June 2015}}</ref>

Madrigal taped his latest stand up special, "Shrimpin Aint Easy" in December 2016 in the Masonic Lodge at Hollywood Forever Cemetery. Directed by Neal Brennan, the special will premier on Showtime (TV network) SHOWTIME in 2017.

Madrigal has been a guest on Jimmy Kimmel Live! and The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson. He appeared on The Tonight Show with Conan OBrien on July 8, 2009.<ref>[http://www.tonightshowwithconanobrien.com/video/clips/al-madrigal-070809/1133907/] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090712004122/http://www.tonightshowwithconanobrien.com/video/clips/al-madrigal-070809/1133907/  date=July 12, 2009 }}</ref> He later appeared on Conans TBS Show, Conan (talk show) Conan, on May 10, 2011.<ref>[http://teamcoco.com/video/10289/comedian-al-madrigal-knows-how-to-pick-em Comedian Al Madrigal Knows How To Pick Em - Video @ Teamcoco.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

===Acting===
File:Al Madrigal by Gage Skidmore.jpg right upright thumb Madrigal performing at Politicon in 2016.
In 2003, Madrigal successfully auditioned for a starring role on The Ortegas, a comedy series for the Fox Network. The series, which was based on the BBC comedy The Kumars at No. 42, cast Madrigal as the son of a Mexican American family in California who hosts a TV talk show from a studio he operates in the backyard of his parents home.<ref>[http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117892338?refCatId=1681 "Al Madrigal: The Ortegas" Variety, September 11, 2003]</ref> However, the network dropped the series from its schedule before broadcasting any of its six filmed episodes.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2003/10/06/business/media-fox-drops-the-ortegas-but-insists-the-show-has-not-been-canceled.html "Fox Drops The Ortegas but Insists the Show Has Not Been Canceled," The New York Times, October 6, 2003]</ref>

In January 2008, Madrigal was cast as a building attendant named Jesus (pronounced “Hey-Soose”) in the CBS comedy Welcome to The Captain.<ref>[http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/tv/ny-ettel0204,0,1956874.story?track=rss "The Captain not welcome on my television," Newsday, February 4, 2008]{{dead link date=July 2011}}</ref> The series was cancelled after five episodes.<ref>[http://tvseriesfinale.com/tv-show/cbs-announces-their-2008-09-schedule-whos-been-cancelled/ "CBS Announces Their 2008-09 Schedule. Whos Been Cancelled?" TVSeriesFinale.com]</ref>

He co-starred in the CBS series Gary Unmarried (originally titled Project Gary), which debuted on September 2008.<ref name=BG />

On March 14, 2013, it was announced that Madrigal would be joining NBCs About a Boy (TV series) About A Boy as Andy, the main characters best friend.<ref>http://www.deadline.com/2013/03/al-madrigal-boards-about-a-boy-delirium-adds-a-friend-reign-casts-nastradamus/</ref> He received a 2014 Imagen Awards nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his work in the role.

Madrigal is a series regular on the Showtime (TV network) Showtime series Im Dying Up Here. He plays a stand up comedian named Edgar in this dark comedy about Los Angeles infamous stand up comedy scene of the 1970s. The show, which is based on a novel of the same name, is executive produced by Jim Carrey.<ref>[https://variety.com/2016/tv/news/im-dying-up-here-showtime-series-1201678320/ "Jim Carrey’s 1970s Dark Comedy ‘I’m Dying Up Here’ Greenlit at Showtime," "Variety", January 12, 2016]</ref>

===The Daily Show with Jon Stewart===
On May 17, 2011, it was announced that Madrigal would be joining The Daily Show The Daily Show with Jon Stewart. He is often presented as the “Senior Latino Correspondent.”

Madrigal auditioned for the show on the recommendation of stand-up comedian Adam Lowitt, one of the show’s producers. Madrigal and Lowitt performed a piece at Carolines on Broadway Carolines on Texas Representative Debbie Riddle, who proposed a bill that would create state punishments for those who “intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly” hired unauthorized immigrants except for domestic workers.<ref>{{cite web last1=Castillo first1=Mariano title=Texas immigration bill has big exception url=http://www.cnn.com/2011/POLITICS/03/01/texas.immigration.bill/ website=CNN publisher=Turner Broadcasting System, Inc. accessdate=31 May 2015}}</ref> Madrigal later did a reading of the piece with Jon Stewart, who hired him on the spot.

===All Things Comedy===
In 2012, Madrigal and comedian Bill Burr founded All Things Comedy, a comedy podcast network and artist cooperative.<ref>{{cite web last1=Espinoza first1=Russ title=New Model for a Funny Business url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/arts/2015-03-13/new-model-for-a-funny-business/ website=The Austin Chronicle publisher=Austin Chronicle Corp. accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref> Madrigal and Burr started the network as a way to help comedians maintain full ownership of their work.<ref>{{cite web last1=Espinoza first1=Russ title=New Model for a Funny Business url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/arts/2015-03-13/new-model-for-a-funny-business/ website=The Austin Chronicle publisher=Austin Chronicle Corp. accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref>  The network hosts over 50 podcasts and garners nearly 5 million listeners per month.<ref>{{cite web last1=McGlynn first1=Katia title=5 Things Comedians Can Learn from Bill Burr, Al Madrigal and the Power of All Things Comedy url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/18/all-things-comedy_n_6896750.html website=Huffington Post publisher=TheHuffingtonPost.com, Inc. accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref>

At South by Southwest South by Southwest 2015, Madrigal, Burr, and comedian Doug Benson spoke on the “Owning Your Work: The Future of All Things Comedy” panel, where they “discussed the ins and outs of their operation and how they are working to help comics carve out their own paths in show business and avoid traditional gatekeepers.”<ref>{{cite web last1=McGlynn first1=Katia title=5 Things Comedians Can Learn From Bill Burr, Al Madrigal and the Power of All Things Comedy url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/18/all-things-comedy_n_6896750.html website=The Huffington Post publisher=TheHuffingtonPost.com, Inc. accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref>

From 2010-2014, he co-hosted a podcast called “Minivan Men” with comedians Maz Jobrani, Aaron Aryanpur, and Chris Spencer, in which they discussed marriage, parenting, and domestic issues.<ref>{{cite web last1=N/A first1=N/A title=Minivan Men url=http://allthingscomedy.com/channels/1/minivan-men website=All Things Comedy publisher=All Things Comedy accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref>

Madrigal and Burr host the “All Things Comedy Live Podcast,” which streams monthly. The podcast has featured comics including Sinbad (comedian) Sinbad, Nick Thune, Felipe Esparza, Doug Benson, Pete Holmes, Ian Edwards (comedian) Ian Edwards, and Fred Stoller.<ref>{{cite web last1=N/A first1=N/A title=All Things Comedy Live Podcast url=http://allthingscomedy.com/channels/55/all-things-comedy-live-podcast website=All Things Comedy publisher=All Things Comedy accessdate=15 May 2015}}</ref>

===Half Like Me===
On January 22, 2015, Madrigals one-hour comedic documentary special, Half Like Me, premiered on Fusion (TV channel) Fusion. The program follows Madrigal on his quest to get closer to his Mexican roots in preparation for a family reunion in Tijuana, Mexico.<ref name="Los Angeles Times Media Group">{{cite web last1=Lloyd first1=Robert title=Critics Pick TV Picks: Nightly Show, Half Like Me, Nova, The Fall, more url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/tv/showtracker/la-et-st-tv-picks-nightly-show-half-like-me-togetherness-nova-fall-20150117-column.html website=LA Times publisher=Los Angeles Times Media Group accessdate=9 June 2015}}</ref> During the course of the program Madrigal explores different aspects of Latino culture in the U.S. The A.V. Club called it "solid and thought-provoking" while the Los Angeles Times listed the special as a "Critics Pick".<ref name="Los Angeles Times Media Group"/><ref>{{cite web last1=Kallison first1=David title=Al Madrigal’s new special explores how the other half lives url=http://www.avclub.com/review/al-madrigals-new-special-explores-how-other-half-l-214050 website=A.V. Club publisher=Onion Inc. accessdate=9 June 2015}}</ref> In an interview with LA Weekly, Madrigal said, "people are actually reaching out and wanting to teach this in their classrooms."<ref name="V Digital Services"/>
