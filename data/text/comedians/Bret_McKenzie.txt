Bret Peter Tarrant McKenzie, New Zealand Order of Merit ONZM (born 29 June 1976) is an Oscar and Grammy winning New Zealand comedian, actor, musician and producer. He is one half of musical comedy duo Flight of the Conchords along with Jemaine Clement. The duos comedy and music became the basis of a Flight of the Conchords (radio series) BBC radio series and then an oft-lauded Flight of the Conchords (TV series) American television series, which aired for two seasons on HBO. McKenzie served as music supervisor for two The Muppets Muppet films, The Muppets (film) The Muppets (2011) and Muppets Most Wanted (2014), the former of which won him an Academy Award for Best Original Song for the song "Man or Muppet". His latest work has seen him write the lyrics to the 2016 Sainsburys Christmas advert featuring James Corden on vocals. <ref>
https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/sainsbury-s-christmas-advert-2016-5-things-you-need-to-know-about-the-greatest-gift-a3393691.html</ref>

As an actor, he portrayed Lindir in Peter Jacksons The Lord of the Rings (film series) The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit (film series) The Hobbit film trilogies, respectively: in the first he remained unnamed with fans naming him Figwit, a character originally cast as an extra who gained attention thanks to the trilogys Fandom fan community, and in The Hobbit is credited as Lindir, a small character who originally appears in the book of The Fellowship of the Ring.

==Background==
McKenzie was born in Wellington, New Zealand. He is a former member of The Black Seeds. He released an album called Prototype as Video Kid<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.videokid.tv/  title=Video Kid}}</ref> and is a member of the Wellington International Ukulele Orchestra.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.ukulele.co.nz  title=Wellington International Ukulele Orchestra}}</ref> McKenzie attended Clifton Terrace Model School ("model" refers to a standard school for training teachers as opposed to model (person) modelling), Wellington College (New Zealand) Wellington College and then Victoria University of Wellington where he met Jemaine Clement who was also studying film and theatre. Together, they were members of So Youre a Man and they later formed Flight of the Conchords.

As Flight of the Conchords they have toured internationally and released four CDs: Folk the World Tour in 2002,The Distant Future (which won the Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album) in 2007, the Grammy nominated Flight of the Conchords (album) Flight of the Conchords in 2008, and I Told You I Was Freaky in 2009. The Conchords produced a six-part improvisational comedy radio program for the BBC and have appeared on Late Night with Conan OBrien, the Late Show with David Letterman and The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson. After a successful appearance in 2005 on Home Box Office HBOs One Night Stand (US TV series) One Night Stand, the Conchords were offered their own 12-part HBO series Flight of the Conchords (TV series) Flight of the Conchords.  Its first season ran from June to September 2007, and its second season premiered on HBO 18 January 2009.

McKenzie has appeared in the first and third films in Peter Jacksons The Lord of the Rings (film series) The Lord of the Rings trilogy. His silent role in the first film as Figwit achieved some minor internet fame, which led to Jackson giving him a line in the third film. In April 2011, McKenzie was cast as the elf Lindir for The Hobbit (film series) The Hobbit. His father Peter McKenzie (actor) Peter McKenzie played the role of Elendil in Lord of the Rings.

Along with Clement, McKenzie was featured as one of 2008s "100 Sexiest People" in a special edition of the Australian magazine Who (magazine) Who.

McKenzie and fellow Conchord Clement guest starred as a pair of camp counselors in "Elementary School Musical (The Simpsons) Elementary School Musical", the season premiere of the The Simpsons (season 22) 22nd season of The Simpsons, which aired on 26 September 2010.<ref name="Simpsons">{{Cite web title=Gleeful over Glee: Cory Monteith to appear on The Simpsons author=Strachan, Alex url=http://www.canada.com/Gleeful+over+Glee+Cory+Monteith+appear+Simpsons/3350970/story.html publisher=Canada.com date=2 August 2010 accessdate=2010-08-02 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111028000202/http://www2.canada.com/gleeful+over+glee+cory+monteith+appear+simpsons/3350970/story.html archivedate=28 October 2011 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

During the summer of 2010, McKenzie flew to Los Angeles to serve as the music supervisor for The Muppets (film) The Muppets.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/film/4442796/Conchords-star-flies-south-for-new-film title=Conchords star flies south work=Stuff accessdate=15 October 2014}}</ref> He went on to write four of the five original songs from the films soundtrack including "Man or Muppet" and "Lifes a Happy Song" both of which were nominated for Broadcast Film Critics Association Awards and Satellite Awards for Best Original Song.<ref>Satellite Award for Best Original Song</ref>

At the 84th Academy Awards in 2012 his song, "Man or Muppet", won the Academy Award for Best Original Song.

McKenzie, together with Australian comedian Hamish Blake starred in a New Zealand feature film, Two Little Boys (film) Two Little Boys, finished in late 2011 and released in New Zealand on March 2012.<ref name="DailyTelegraphAust_09Dec2010_SydneyConfidential_HamishBlaketostarwithBretMcKenzieinSarkiesTwoLittleBoys">{{cite news url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/hamish-blake-to-star-with-bret-mckenzie-in-sarkies-two-little-boys/story-e6frewz0-1225968460210 title=Hamish Blake to star with Bret McKenzie in Sarkies Two Little Boys   last=Catanzariti first=Katelyn date=9 December 2010 work=The Daily Telegraph (Sydney) publisher=News Corporation accessdate=9 December 2010}}</ref>

McKenzie wrote the original songs for the 2014 movie Muppets Most Wanted.

They have two children; daughter Vita (born 2009), and son Leo (born 2011).<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/music/news/article.cfm?c_id=264&objectid=10607282  title=Fatherhood keeps Conchords stars busy  work=New Zealand Herald  date=5 November 2009  accessdate=2009-11-08  first=Jacqueline  last=Smith}}</ref>
