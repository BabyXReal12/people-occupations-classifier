
Alan Whitney Brown (born July 8, 1952) is an American writer and comedian best known for work on Saturday Night Live in the 1980s. In addition to writing for the program, he appeared opposite Dennis Miller in a biting satirical Weekend Update commentary segment called "The Big Picture."<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120309081159/http://www.basicfamouspeople.com/index.php?aid=3095 BasicFamousPeople.com (archive)]</ref> He won a 1988 Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing in a Variety or Music Program, along with Al Franken, Tom Davis (comedian) Tom Davis, Phil Hartman, Mike Myers, Lorne Michaels and Conan OBrien.<ref>[http://www.popculturemadness.com/Trivia/Emmies/Top-1988-E.html "1988/89 Emmy Award Winners"]. Pop Culture Madness. Retrieved December 18, 2012.</ref> He was also one of the original correspondents on Comedy Central Comedy Centrals The Daily Show from 1996 to 1998.<ref>[http://www.thedailyshow.com/news-team/a-whitney-brown The Daily Show: Official Website]</ref>

==Career==
Brown began his career as a street juggler, and became a stand-up comedian after entering the 1977 San Francisco Comedy Competition. He became a member of the Writers Guild of America Writers Guild when he was hired by Lorne Michaels to join the writing staff of Saturday Night Live in 1985; he was also a featured performer. He left for personal reasons six years later. He worked briefly for the liberal radio network Air America Radio during its start-up period in early 2004 and remains active in online political discourse.<ref>http://awhitneybrown.dailykos.com/</ref>

He also wrote the screenplay for an episode of HBOs horror anthology show Tales from the Crypt (TV series) Tales From the Crypt named List of Tales from the Crypt episodes#Season 1 (1989) Collection Completed (Season 1, Episode 6; first aired in America on 28 Jun. 28, 1989).

==Personal life==
On March 4, 2011, Brown married Carolyn Wonderland, a blues singer and guitarist, in Austin, Texas.  The marriage was officiated by Michael Nesmith of The Monkees.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/13/fashion/weddings/13VOWS.html New York Times: Website]</ref> Brown was previously married to Cynthia Swanson, a New York newspaper designer, from 1976 to 2011. He has a daughter, Serena, and a granddaughter, Thea Lenore.
