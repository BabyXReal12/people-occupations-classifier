
Patrick John Warburton (born November 14, 1964) is an American comedic actor and voice artist. In television, he is known for playing David Puddy on Seinfeld,<ref>{{cite web title=David Puddy url=http://www.seinfeldonline.com/puddy.html work=seinfeldonline.com accessdate=December 30, 2010}}</ref> the Tick (comics) title role on The Tick (2001 TV series) The Tick, Jeb Denton on Less Than Perfect, Jeff Bingham on Rules of Engagement (TV series) Rules of Engagement, and Lemony Snicket on A Series of Unfortunate Events (TV series) A Series of Unfortunate Events.<ref>{{cite journal url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/patrick-warburton/169214 title=Patrick Warburton Bio and Photos work=TV Guide accessdate=May 27, 2010}}</ref> His voice roles include List of The Emperors New Groove characters#Kronk Kronk in The Emperors New Groove and its sequels, Agent T in Men In Black 2, Paraplegia paraplegic police officer List of Family Guy characters#Joe Swanson Joe Swanson on Family Guy, List of The Venture Bros. characters#Brock Samson Brock Samson on The Venture Bros, Lok in the Tak and the Power of Juju video games and Tak and the Power of Juju (TV series) television series, Flynn in Skylanders<ref name="imdb">{{IMDb name 911320}}</ref> and Hugo Vasquez in Tales From the Borderlands. In advertising, he has played a "control enthusiast" in a series of commercials for National Car Rental.<ref>{{cite web title=National Car Rental Control Enthusiast url=https://www.nationalcar.com/en_US/car-rental/xp/control-enthusiast.html accessdate=February 10, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
Warburton was born in Paterson, New Jersey,<ref>{{cite news last=Yannis first=Alex url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9407E4D61239F93BA35753C1A963958260 title=HOCKEY;The Devils, And Fans, Ignite First Match work=The New York Times date=October 8, 1995 accessdate=November 25, 2007 quote=Moments after the banner was raised, Patrick Warburton, the actor who portrayed a fanatic Devils’ fan in a segment of the “Seinfeld” television show, was called upon to drop the puck. With his face painted in Devils red and black, the native of nearby Paterson dropped the puck, then stripped the Brodeur jersey he was wearing to display the letter D on his chest.}}</ref> the son of orthopedic surgeon John Charles Warburton Jr. and Barbara Jeanne Gratz (an actress credited as Barbara Lord).<ref>{{cite news publisher=ESPN.com work=ESPN.com title=10 Burning Questions For..... Patrick Warburton url=http://espn.go.com/page2/s/questions/warburton.html accessdate=April 5, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/1961/03/31/archives/gratz-warburton.html work=The New York Times title=Gratz – Warburton date=March 31, 1961}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=https://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=FA0B10FC3C541B728DDDAD0894DE405B818AF1D3 work=The New York Times title=Warburton — Gratz date=June 4, 1961}}</ref> He and his three sisters, Mary, Lara, and Megan, were raised in a "very religious" and "conservative" Catholic family in Huntington Beach, California,<ref>[http://articles.philly.com/1998-08-07/news/25724723_1_david-puddy-new-cartoon-series-seinfeld "Patrick Warburton Awaits A New Series Of Options"], Articles.philly.com (August 7, 1998); retrieved on July 25, 2012.</ref> where he attended Saints Simon and Jude Catholic School. He later attended Servite High School (in Anaheim, California), and later transferred to Newport Harbor High School (in Newport Beach, California). He studied marine biology at Orange Coast College in Costa Mesa, California, but dropped out to pursue modeling and acting.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.moviefone.com/celebrity/patrick-warburton/1838008/biography title=Patrick Warburton biodata publisher=Movies.aol.com date=November 14, 1964 accessdate=May 27, 2010 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091004011051/http://www.moviefone.com/celebrity/patrick-warburton/1838008/biography archivedate=October 4, 2009 df=}}</ref>

==Career==
Warburtons acting roles often rely on his deep, booming voice and large physique. In the 1990s, he was known for his recurring role as David Puddy on Seinfeld, the On-again, off-again relationship on-again, off-again boyfriend of Elaine Benes. He had a small role in the 2002 film Men in Black II as Js new partner, T.

Warburton starred in Fox Broadcasting Company FOXs short-lived series The Tick (2001 TV series) The Tick, in which he held the Tick (comics) title role. He has repeatedly criticized FOXs mismanagement of the series after its cancellation, stating that the network "apparently didnt have a clue."<ref>Bell, Josh [http://tvcomedies.about.com/od/interviews/a/warburtonqa_2.htm Patrick Warburton interview] About.com. Retrieved on 2-04-18.</ref>

He played Nick Sharp in 8 Simple Rules and joined the cast of Less Than Perfect in 2003, as anchorman Jeb Denton. He also played Jeff Bingham in Rules of Engagement (TV series) Rules of Engagement, and Rip Riley in the FX (TV channel) FX series Archer (TV series) Archer.

File:PatrickWarburtonJan07.jpg thumb left 200px Warburton in January 2007
Warburton has put his voice to use for several animated films and TV programs, including a lead character in Game Over (TV series) Game Over, Buzz Lightyear and the Little Green Men in Buzz Lightyear of Star Command, and List of The Emperors New Groove characters#Kronk Kronk in The Emperors New Groove, with David Spade and John Goodman; Spade and Warburton would reunite for the CBS sitcom Rules of Engagement (TV series) Rules of Engagement, which aired from 2007 to 2013, and Joe Dirt 2: Beautiful Loser in 2015. Warburton reprised his role as Kronk for the direct-to-video sequel, Kronks New Groove, and the subsequent TV series The Emperors New School. He voiced Steve Barkin in the Disney Channel show Kim Possible.

Warburton was also cast in The Venture Bros., in which he voices Brock Samson. He voices Joe Swanson on Fox Broadcasting Company Fox’s Family Guy and Detective Cash in The Batman (TV series) The Batman. He lent his voice talent to the Computer animation computer-animated feature films Hoodwinked, Chicken Little (2005 film) Chicken Little, Bee Movie, Open Season (2006 film) Open Season, and Tak and the Power of Juju Tak (all three games),Tak and the Power of Juju (TV series) Tak and the Power of Juju.{{citation needed date=May 2014}} He was also the voice of Commander Blaine H. Tate on the Comedy Central animated series Moonbeam City in October 2015.

Warburton appeared on Game Show Network GSN’s Poker Royale Celebrities vs. the Pros tournament in 2005, winning the tournament and the $50,000 grand prize. On November 8, 2009, Warburton appeared as the wild west character Cal Johnson on Seth & Alex’s (Almost Live) Comedy Show, hosted by Seth MacFarlane and Alex Borstein, on Fox.  He was also cast as the sheriff in the Cartoon Network series Scooby-Doo! Mystery Incorporated. He appeared as Bowser (character) Bowser in a video by CollegeHumor entitled "The Roast of Mario".<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tPUgZlN6cdA&ob=av3e The Roast of Mario (featuring Patrick Warburton)]. YouTube (December 20, 2010). Retrieved on July 25, 2012.</ref>

He appeared on the American version of Top Gear (U.S. TV series) Top Gear on February 21, 2012. He set the “Big Star, Small Car” lap record, beating previous record-holder Arlene Tur.<ref>[http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/02/22/top-gear-patrick-warburton-angelina-jolie-car-video_n_1293171.html Top Gear: Patrick Warburton Named His 1969 Dodge Charger After Angelina Jolie (VIDEO)], Huffingtonpost.com, February 22, 2012; retrieved July 25, 2012.</ref>

Warburton starred in the 2016 NBC sitcom Crowded (TV series) Crowded, in which he played alongside Carrie Preston as parents who are about to enjoy their empty nest years only to find their daughters and his parents are all moving back in to live with them. The show ran for one season of 13 episodes.<ref>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2016/05/crowded-canceled-nbc-one-season-1201755553/ title=Crowded’ Canceled By NBC After One Season website=Deadline Hollywood first=Nellie last=Andreeva date=May 13, 2016 accessdate=May 13, 2016}}</ref>

He is featured in the "preboarding" video shown to guests at Soarin Soarin Around the World in Disney California Adventure in Anaheim, California and Epcot at the Walt Disney World Resort, in Orlando, Florida where he explains the rides requirements. He lent his voice to the droid G2-4T in the queue of Star Tours: The Adventures Continue at Disneys Hollywood Studios in Orlando, Florida and Disneyland in Anaheim, California.

He currently plays Lemony Snicket in the series A Series of Unfortunate Events (TV series) A Series of Unfortunate Events.

He appeared at a New Jersey Devils playoff hockey game on April 18, 2018 with his face (and chest) painted similar to that of his Devils crazed role in an episode of "Seinfeld", exhorting the crowd in a manner alike to that of his "Puddy" character.

== Commercial voice-over ==
In the commercial and advertising world, Warburton is the voice in the Carrier Corporation commercials for their air-conditioning and HVAC units, and was the voice of Superman in The Adventures of Seinfeld and Superman for American Express. He is the voice of "Lewis" in the "Clark & Lewis Expedition" radio commercials for Horizon Air. In August 2009, Warburton played a high-end PC in a "Get a Mac" ad for Apple Inc.<ref>[http://www.aoltv.com/2009/08/25/patrick-warburton-is-a-top-of-the-line-pc-in-the-latest-mac-ad/ Patrick Warburton is a top of the line PC in the latest Mac ad]. Aoltv.com (August 26, 2009). Retrieved on July 25, 2012.</ref> Warburton became the spokesman for Japanese automaker Hondas "Good Reasons" advertising campaign in September 2011.<ref>[http://eon.businesswire.com/news/eon/20110912006333/en/honda/honda-civic/honda-crv "New Ad Campaign Provides Plenty of Good Reasons to Buy a New Honda"], eon.businesswire.com, September 12, 2011; retrieved July 25, 2012.</ref> He is the voice in NAPAs "Can of Know How" commercial. In 2015, Warburton became the star of National Car Rental commercials "Be the Boss of You". And he became the voice in the current "Discover Fresh" radio ad campaign by Souplantation, a California-based buffet-style restaurant chain (the restaurant is known as "Sweet Tomatoes" in other markets).<ref>{{cite web last1=Harper first1=R. title=Patrick Warburton is the New Voice of Souplantation and Fresh Produce is VERY Afraid url=http://blog.souplantation.com/patrick-warburton-is-the-new-voice-of-souplantation-and-fresh-produce-is-very-afraid/#.VydplnErKUk website=Souplantation accessdate=2 May 2016 date=March 16, 2015}}</ref>

Warburton is also the voice of Ranger, a forest ranger, in a series of radio spots for the national Smokey Bear campaign sponsored by the United States Forest Service U.S. Forest Service and the Ad Council.<ref>[http://audioarchives.oc.usda.gov/radnewsfeaturedetail.asp?ID=2468 USDA Radio News: Smokey Bear Updates Part of New Ad Campaign] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111015000104/http://audioarchives.oc.usda.gov/radnewsfeaturedetail.asp?ID=2468  date=2011-10-15 }}. U.S. Dept of Agriculture. Retrieved on September 6, 2012.</ref>
