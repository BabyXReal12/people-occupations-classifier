
Noël Kristi Wells (born December 23, 1986)<ref>[https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:VDVB-YHY]</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/cast/noel-wells/796035/  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921162832/http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/cast/noel-wells/796035/  dead-url=yes  archive-date=2013-09-21  title=Noel Wells Bio &#124; Featured Player &#124; Saturday Night Live  publisher=NBC  date=  accessdate=2013-09-29  df= }}</ref> is an American actress, comedian, director, musician, and writer. She is known for her television roles on Master of None and Saturday Night Live, as well as writing, directing, and starring in the film Mr. Roosevelt (film) Mr. Roosevelt.

==Early life==
Wells was born in San Antonio, Texas.<ref>{{cite web url=http://notcommonpeople.blogspot.com/2010/11/noel-kristi-wells.html archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120527051044/http://notcommonpeople.blogspot.com/2010/11/noel-kristi-wells.html dead-url=yes archive-date=27 May 2012 title=Noel Wells publisher=Notcommonpeople.blogspot.com accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref> Her father is a Tunisian people Tunisian immigrant, and her mother is of half Mexicans Mexican descent.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/tv/a50625/noel-wells-master-of-none-interview/  title=Noël Wells Master of None Interview  publisher=Cosmopolitan.com  date=2015-12-10  accessdate=2016-12-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web author=&nbsp;Sep 12, 2016 3:50PM EDT  url=https://www.colorlines.com/articles/meet-melissa-villasenor-snls-first-latina-cast-member  title=Meet Melissa Villaseñor, SNLs First Latina Cast Member  publisher=Colorlines  date=2016-09-12  accessdate=2016-12-20}}</ref><ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/as-saturday-night-live-cast-reboots-questions-about-diversity-emerge/2013/10/03/b91b7294-2c72-11e3-8ade-a1f23cda135e_story.html "As ‘Saturday Night Live’ cast reboots, questions about diversity emerge"] Paul Farhi, Washington Post, October 3, 2013. Retrieved June 16, 2014.</ref> She says her parents named her Noël because she was born two days before Christmas.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/saturday-night-live-christmas-noel-wells-1074160.aspx title=Noël Wells Rings In the Holidays With Saturday Night Lives Christmas Special work=TV Guide accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref>

She attended Memorial High School (Victoria, Texas) Memorial High School in Victoria, Texas, where she was active in speech and debate and graduated as salutatorian.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.crossroadstoday.com/content/news/local/story/Local-Girl-Makes-Big-Time/B5lOYodhM0O7K2XU02rY0w.cspx?rss=3666 archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005013517/http://www.crossroadstoday.com/content/news/local/story/Local-Girl-Makes-Big-Time/B5lOYodhM0O7K2XU02rY0w.cspx?rss=3666 dead-url=yes archive-date=2013-10-05 title=Local Girl Makes Big Time publisher=Crossroadstoday.com accessdate=2013-10-07}}</ref> She graduated from University of Texas at Austin in 2010<ref name="abromavitch1">{{cite news last=Abromavitch first=Seth title=How Saturday Night Live Found 5 New Castmembers url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/saturday-night-live-new-cast-625424 accessdate=11 September 2013 newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter date=11 September 2013}}</ref> with degrees in Plan II Honors and University of Texas at Austin Department of Radio-Television-Film Radio-Television-Film.<ref>{{cite web title=RTF Student Noël Wells Thanks Family for IFC Internship url=http://rtf.utexas.edu/news/rtf-student-noël-wells-thanks-family-ifc-internship?page=2 publisher=Radio Television Film College of Communications}}</ref> While attending college, she was a cast member of Esthers Follies,<ref>{{cite web last=Kiest first=Jim title=UT grad may join ‘SNL’ cast url=http://blog.mysanantonio.com/redletter/2013/08/ut-grad-may-join-snl-cast/ archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131022203835/http://blog.mysanantonio.com/redletter/2013/08/ut-grad-may-join-snl-cast/ dead-url=yes archive-date=22 October 2013 publisher=My San Antonio accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref> Austin’s long-running musical satire show, where she performed in sketches and as a magicians assistant.<ref>{{cite web last=Tinkham first=Chris title=Noël Wells - 2013 LA Film Fest Interview url=http://www.undertheradarmag.com/interviews/noel_wells/ publisher=Under the Radar Mag accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref> Before becoming an actress, she worked as an editor and did motion graphics.

==Career==
In 2010, Wells moved to Los Angeles and performed at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre with the sketch team "New Money".<ref>{{cite web url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/21690 publisher=Upright Citizen Brigade Theater title=Noel Wells accessdate=2013-10-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Seth Abramovitch first=Seth title=Cheat Sheet: Get to Know the 5 New Faces of SNL url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/saturday-night-live-get-know-625783 newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter date=11 September 2013}}</ref> She has appeared in numerous Cracked.com and CollegeHumor videos,<ref>{{cite web url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/21690 publisher=Upright Citizens Brigade Theater title=Noel Wells accessdate=2013-10-07}}</ref>  and is also known for her own sketch and parody videos, which have over 15 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/noelkristi  title=Noël  publisher=YouTube  date=  accessdate=2013-10-07}}</ref>

In 2013, Wells joined the cast of Saturday Night Live during its Saturday Night Live (season 39) 39th season as a featured player along with fellow Upright Citizens Brigade performers John Milhiser, Kyle Mooney, and Beck Bennett, as well as writer Mike OBrien (actor) Mike OBrien and stand-up Brooks Wheelan.<ref name=new>{{cite news last=Carter first=Bill title=New Course for ‘Update,’ and All of ‘SNL’ url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/16/business/media/saturday-night-live-setting-its-new-cast.html accessdate=September 16, 2013 newspaper=The New York Times date=September 15, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=Michael Patrick OBrien, Kyle Mooney, Beck Bennett, John Milhiser & Noël Wells Poised to Join Saturday Night Live Cast url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/08/john-milhiser-noel-wells-eyed-as-new-saturday-night-live-cast-additions/ newspaper=Deadline Hollywood accessdate=2013-10-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Luippold first=Ross title=SNL Cast Rumored To Add Noël Wells, Beck Bennett, John Milhiser, Kyle Mooney, Mike OBrien (UPDATE) url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/08/23/snl-cast-rumor-noel-wells-beck-bennett-john-milhiser-kyle-mooney_n_3805279.html newspaper=Huffington Post accessdate=2013-10-07 date=2013-08-23}}</ref> On July 15, 2014, it was announced that Wells would not be returning for a second season.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.deadline.com/2014/07/saturday-night-live-noel-wells-john-milhiser-leaving/ title=Noël Wells & John Milhiser Leaving ‘Saturday Night Live’ - Deadline date=15 July 2014 work=Deadline accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="avclub">{{cite web  url=http://www.avclub.com/article/noel-wells-and-john-milhiser-are-also-leaving-satu-206958  title=Noël Wells and John Milhiser Are Also Leaving Saturday Night Live  last=ONeal  first=Sean  date=July 14, 2014  publisher=The A.V. Club}}</ref>

Wells has made guest appearances on television programs such as The Aquabats! Super Show! and Comedy Bang! Bang! (TV series) Comedy Bang! Bang!, as well as doing recurring voice work on Hulus The Awesomes and Disney XDs Wander Over Yonder. In 2013, Wells starred in the independent film Forev, which premiered at the Los Angeles Film Festival.<ref>{{cite web url=http://filmschoolrejects.com/reviews/forev.php title=Forev (2013) Movie Review work=Film School Rejects accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref>

File:Noel Wells - Master of None premier.jpg thumb left Wells at the Master of None premiere
In 2015, she co-starred on the critically acclaimed Netflix comedy Master of None, created by Aziz Ansari and Alan Yang. Wells played Rachel, the love-interest of Ansaris character. Hitfixs Alan Sepinwall said the "Dev/Rachel story is so smartly developed, with such strong chemistry between Ansari and Wells”, while Vanity Fair’s Richard Lawson said her portrayal of Rachel was a “star-making...performance. it’s subtle, but not minimalist or deadpan”.<ref>{{cite web title = Watch: Aziz Ansari Navigates Romance in Netflixs Master of None Trailer url = https://variety.com/2015/digital/news/aziz-ansari-netflix-master-of-none-trailer-1201624300/ website = Variety accessdate = 2015-10-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last1=Sepinwall first1=Alan title=Review: Aziz Ansari is a Master of None in fantastic new Netflix comedy  url=http://www.hitfix.com/whats-alan-watching/review-aziz-ansari-is-a-master-of-none-in-fantastic-new-netflix-comedy accessdate=February 15, 2016 publisher=Hitfix.com date=November 3, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last1=Andreeva first1=Nellie title=Aziz Ansari Comedy Series A Go At Netflix url=http://deadline.com/2015/04/aziz-ansari-comedy-series-netflix-1201413974/ accessdate=April 27, 2015 publisher=Deadline.com date=April 21, 2015}}</ref> All 10 episodes premiered on November 6, 2015 and it won the 2016 Critics Choice Award for Best Comedy.<ref>{{cite web title = Master of None Trailer: Aziz Ansari Gets a Netflix Show url = http://www.slashfilm.com/master-of-none-trailer/ website = Slashfilm accessdate = 2015-10-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4635276/awards title=Master of None date=6 November 2015 publisher= accessdate=20 December 2016 via=IMDb}}</ref>

As of August 2015, Wells and partner Flint Wainess are developing a sitcom pilot for Comedy Central titled Bad Couple, which Wells would star in if ordered to series. Wells and Wainess are also consulting writers on the upcoming Netflix animated childrens series Green Eggs and Ham (TV series) Green Eggs and Ham, based on the popular Dr. Seuss book.<ref>{{cite web last=Wright  first=Megh  url=http://splitsider.com/2015/08/comedy-central-orders-bad-couple-pilot-starring-noel-wells/  title=Comedy Central Orders ‘Bad Couple’ Pilot Starring Noël Wells  publisher=Splitsider  date=2015-08-03  accessdate=2016-12-20}}</ref>

In March 2017, Wells wrote, directed, and starred in the feature film Mr. Roosevelt (film) Mr. Roosevelt, which premiered at the SXSW film festival in Narrative Spotlight. It has won multiple awards including the Audience Award and Louis Black Lone Star Jury Award at SXSW and Best US Narrative Feature at the Traverse City Film Festival.<ref>{{cite web last1=ONeill first1=Tyler title=‘Most Beautiful Island,’ ‘Mr. Roosevelt’ Steal the Show at 2017 SXSW url=https://www.backstage.com/news/awards-season/most-beautiful-island-mr-roosevelt-steal-show-2017-sxsw/ website=Backstage.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/sxsw-2017-audience-award-winners-1202011563/ title=SXSW Announces 2017 Audience Award Winners date= work= last1=Kelley first1=Seth accessdate=May 4, 2017}}</ref>

She is now the voice of Kelsey Bern in the Cartoon Network series Craig of the Creek since the episode "The Final Book".

==Personal life==
Wells is an avid photographer and has had her photography featured in exhibitions and the literary magazine Oxford American.<ref name="Oxford American">{{cite web title=Oxford American Best of the South url=https://www.flickr.com/photos/prettypertelote/4658133738/ website=Flickr publisher=Noel Wells accessdate=July 15, 2014}}</ref> She has lived in Los Angeles and New York City.<ref>{{cite web last1=Tuck first1=Lauren title=5 Things You Need to Know About SNL New Girl url=https://shine.yahoo.com/photos/5-things-you-need-to-know-about-snl-s-new-girl-slideshow/noel-wells-loves-cats-photo-1378929918067.html website=Yahoo accessdate=July 15, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  last1=Isaac  first1=Jessica  title=Master of Nones Noël Wells Vibrant and Personal LA Rental  url=https://www.apartmenttherapy.com/master-of-none-noel-wells-house-tour-photos-255547  website=Apartment Therapy}}</ref>
