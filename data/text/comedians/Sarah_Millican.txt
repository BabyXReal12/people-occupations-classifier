Sarah Jane Millican ({{nee}} King;<ref>Who Do You Think You Are?, BBC TV, broadcast 11 September 2013</ref> born 29 May 1975)<ref>{{cite news   url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/may/05/sarah-millican-comedy   location=London   work=The Guardian   first=Vanessa   last=Thorpe   title=How Sarah Millican struck comedy gold   date=5 May 2013   deadurl=no   archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161219170851/https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/may/05/sarah-millican-comedy   archivedate=19 December 2016   df=dmy-all }}</ref> is an English comedian. Millican won the if.comedy award for Best Newcomer at the 2008 Edinburgh Festival Fringe.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.metro.co.uk/metrolife/article.html?Sarah_Millican_isnt_resting_on_her_laurels&in_article_id=354075&in_page_id=275  work=Metro  title=Sarah Millican isnt resting on her laurels  author=Mickey Noonan  date=13 October 2008  accessdate=13 May 2009}}</ref> In February 2013 she was listed as one of the 100 most powerful women in the United Kingdom by Radio 4s Womans Hour<ref name="Power 100"/>, and in the same year married fellow comedian Gary Delaney. Her first book, How to Be Champion, was released in 2017, and Millican has performed on various tours mainly throughout the United Kingdom over the years.

==Life and career==

===Early life===
Millican was born and brought up in South Shields, England, the daughter of Valerie Prince and Philip D. King, who was a mining electrician. She attended Mortimer Comprehensive School, later to become Mortimer Community College,<ref name="bbc">{{cite web title=Sarah Millican profile url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/5f6FJvJZxpRQrHzTT7K92Cv/sarah-millican website=BBC Radio 4 accessdate=31 December 2016 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160119145824/http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/5f6FJvJZxpRQrHzTT7K92Cv/sarah-millican archivedate=19 January 2016 df=dmy-all}}</ref> and then worked as a civil servant at a job centre until the age of 29.<ref name=independent>[https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/features/funny-girl-sarah-millican-on-comedy-money-and-marriage-7468496.html Funny Girl: Sarah Millican in The Independent] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307015623/http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/features/funny-girl-sarah-millican-on-comedy-money-and-marriage-7468496.html  date=7 March 2014 }} Retrieved 27 June 2015</ref> She was married on 5 November 1997<ref>8 Out of 10 Cats, 4 November 2014</ref> but divorced in 2004 and moved back in with her parents for two and a half years.

===2009–present: mainstream success and DVDs, TV and radio shows===
Millican has appeared as a panellist on 8 out of 10 Cats, Have I Got News For You, Mock the Week, You Have Been Watching, Would I Lie to You? (TV series) Would I Lie to You?, QI and Never Mind the Buzzcocks, and as a performer at The Secret Policemans Ball 2008 and on 4 Stands Up. She was featured in the Manchester edition of Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow, which was broadcast in June 2009,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2009/04_april/23/mcintyre.shtml  title=New comedy tops bill on BBC One this summer with Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow  date=23 April 2009  work=BBC  accessdate=14 May 2009  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090426121342/http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2009/04_april/23/mcintyre.shtml  archivedate=26 April 2009  df=dmy-all }}</ref> made an appearance on the third episode of David Mitchell (comedian) David Mitchells panel show The Bubble (UK TV series) The Bubble in March 2010,<ref name="bbcpressbubble">{{cite web  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2010/02_february/02/bubble.shtml  title=The Bubble, a brand-new comedy news quiz on BBC Two  work=BBC  date=2 February 2010  accessdate=20 February 2010  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100219171524/http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2010/02_february/02/bubble.shtml  archivedate=19 February 2010  df=dmy-all }}</ref> and has been a guest on six episodes of Frank Skinners Opinionated.

She provided vocal work to the BBCs natural history footage for Walk on the Wild Side (TV series) Walk on the Wild Side, alongside fellow comics Rhod Gilbert, Jon Richardson (comedian) Jon Richardson, Isy Suttie and Gavin Webster.<ref name="scotsman-int">{{cite news  url=http://thescotsman.scotsman.com/features/Interview-Sarah-Millican39s-standup-is.5509942.jp  title=Interview: Sarah Millicans stand-up is in the worst possible taste  work=The Scotsman  date=1 August 2009  accessdate=8 August 2009}}</ref> She appeared on Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo on 11 December 2009, and headlined Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow, recorded at the Empire Theatre, Sunderland on 15 August 2010.<ref name="journal-082010"/> Millican also headlined the fourth episode of Daves One Night Stand.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/tv/one_night_stand/episodes/1/4/  title=Episode 1.4  author=Ian Wolf  publisher=British Comedy Guide  accessdate=9 October 2010  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120418144528/http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/tv/one_night_stand/episodes/1/4/  archivedate=18 April 2012  df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Her radio series, Sarah Millicans Support Group, began broadcasting on 18 February 2010 on BBC Radio 4.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/proginfo/radio/2010/wk7/thu.shtml#thu_sarah  title=R4 Sarah Millicans Support Group  work=BBC Press Office  date=18 February 2010  accessdate=18 February 2010  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100224213605/http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/proginfo/radio/2010/wk7/thu.shtml#thu_sarah  archivedate=24 February 2010  df=dmy-all }}</ref> The format is that studio audience members are encouraged to share problems with her agony aunt character for her to offer tips "in the same way as the busybody at the end of your street who knows everything and dishes out advice whether you want to hear it or not does". Some problems are scripted, the roles being played by guest actors, while others come from real people in the audience, to allow Sarah to improvise. Audience member problems have included "My boyfriend prefers his Blackberry to me". A pair of "therapists", also played by actors, feature in each weeks show and provide scripted and improvised responses to all the "problems".<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2009/11/10/9923/r4_series_for_millican  title=R4 series for Millican  work=Chortle  date=10 November 2009  accessdate=10 November 2009  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091114065112/http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2009/11/10/9923/r4_series_for_millican  archivedate=14 November 2009  df=dmy-all }}</ref> A second series debuted on BBC Radio 4 Extra on 2 May 2011.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/radio/sarah_millicans_support_group/episodes/2/ title=Sarah Millicans Support Group publisher=British Comedy Guide accessdate=14 June 2011 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121013024949/http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/radio/sarah_millicans_support_group/episodes/2/ archivedate=13 October 2012 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

She was a regular co-host of the satirical news show 7 Day Sunday on BBC Radio 5 Live, along with comedians Chris Addison, Andy Zaltzman and one different guest each week. The first episode aired in January 2010; she and Addison left the show on 27 February 2011.

In December 2011, she voiced three Viz (comic) Viz Comedy Blaps alongside Steve Coogan, Simon Greenall and Gavin Webster for Channel 4.<ref>{{cite news   url=http://www.skwigly.co.uk/viz-animation-blap-to-basics/   work=Skwigly   title=Viz Animation – "Blap" to basics   date=12 December 2011   accessdate=6 January 2012   last=Henderson   first=Steve   deadurl=no   archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120114100817/http://www.skwigly.co.uk/viz-animation-blap-to-basics/   archivedate=14 January 2012   df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Also in 2011, Millican became a panellist on the ITV Network ITV programme Marriage Ref and joined the panel on ITV daytime chat show Loose Women, leaving the series in August. She was also a guest on several other panel and chat shows. A live DVD of her Chatterbox tour was released in November 2011 by Channel 4.<ref name="chortledvd">{{cite web title=Sarah Millican signs DVD deal url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2010/08/24/11602/sarah_millican_signs_dvd_deal work=Chortle date=24 August 2010 accessdate=24 August 2010 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100828110420/http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2010/08/24/11602/sarah_millican_signs_dvd_deal archivedate=28 August 2010 df=dmy-all}}</ref> The Chatterbox DVD went on to break an all-time record in sales for a female stand-up comedian selling 172,000 copies in just over one month.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/comedy/8971413/Sarah-Millican-reflects-on-her-remarkable-DVD-sales-success.html  title=Sarah Millican reflects on her remarkable DVD sales success  work=The Telegraph  date=21 December 2011  accessdate=16 February 2013  last=Cavendish  first=Dominic  location=London  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120408032156/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/comedy/8971413/Sarah-Millican-reflects-on-her-remarkable-DVD-sales-success.html  archivedate=8 April 2012  df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Millicans stand-up show, The Sarah Millican Television Programme, was first broadcast on 8 March 2012 on BBC Two. In November 2012, she released a follow-up live DVD, Thoroughly Modern Millican. In early January 2013, she appeared for a couple of nights on The One Show. On 15 January 2013, she began presenting a second series of The Sarah Millican Television Programme.   Millican was nominated for 2013 and 2014 BAFTA Entertainment Performance awards for her work in The Sarah Millican Television Programme.<ref name="bafta 2013">{{cite web title=Television in 2013 url=http://awards.bafta.org/award/2013/television website=BAFTA publisher=British Academy of Film and Television Arts accessdate=31 December 2016 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6WszoVlVp?url=http://awards.bafta.org/award/2013/television archivedate=8 March 2015 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Millican appeared in a celebrity episode of Deal or No Deal (UK game show) Deal or No Deal broadcast on 29 April 2012, where she won £20,000 for charity. She had £100,000 in her box.

Millican also appeared on the Comic Relief 2015: Face the Funny TV Programme, broadcast on Friday 13 March 2015, Where she was a host for the BBC Comic Relief show.

In May 2015 it was announced that Millican would be made the eighth "curator" of The Museum of Curiosity, co-presenting the eighth series of the BBC Radio 4 comedy series.<ref>{{cite web last=Dessau first=Bruce authorlink=Bruce Dessau url=http://www.beyondthejoke.co.uk/content/1677/news-sarah-millican-curates-museum title=News: Sarah Millican Curates Museum publisher=Beyond the Joke date=6 May 2015 accessdate=6 May 2015 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150510160850/http://www.beyondthejoke.co.uk/content/1677/news-sarah-millican-curates-museum archivedate=10 May 2015 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Millicans autobiography, How to be Champion, was published in October 2017.<ref name=OrionBooks>{{cite web title=Sarah Millican: How to be Champion url=https://www.orionbooks.co.uk/books/detail.page?isbn=9781409174332 work=Orion Books accessdate=3 October 2017 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171003231505/https://www.orionbooks.co.uk/books/detail.page?isbn=9781409174332 archivedate=3 October 2017 df=dmy-all}}</ref> That month she also announced dates for her 2018 UK tour, "Sarah Millican: Control Enthusiast".

==Personal life==
After her husband Andrew Millican left her unexpectedly in 2004,<ref>{{cite news   url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/features/funny-girl-sarah-millican-on-comedy-money-and-marriage-7468496.html   location=London   work=The Independent   first=Gerard   last=Gilbert   title=Funny girl: Sarah Millican on comedy, money and marriage   date=2 March 2012   deadurl=no   archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307015623/http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/features/funny-girl-sarah-millican-on-comedy-money-and-marriage-7468496.html   archivedate=7 March 2014   df=dmy-all }}</ref> Millican found solace in attending writing workshops in local theatres, such as at Newcastle upon Tyne Newcastles Live Theatre and the The Customs House, South Shields Customs House in South Shields.
