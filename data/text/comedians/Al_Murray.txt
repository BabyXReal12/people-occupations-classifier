Alastair James Hay Murray (born 10 May 1968), is an England English comedian and TV personality.

He was listed in The Observer as one of the 50 funniest acts in British comedy.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://arts.guardian.co.uk/features/story/0,11710,1101525,00.html  title=The A-Z of laughter  newspaper=The Guardian  publisher=Guardian Unlimited  accessdate=27 December 2006  location=London  date=7 December 2003}}</ref> In 2007, he was voted the 16th greatest stand-up comic on Channel 4s 100 Greatest Stand-Ups.

===The Pub Landlord===
Murrays character is an English publican with Conservatism in the United Kingdom conservative values and an animosity towards Germans and the French people French; he will challenge the audience to name any List of sovereign states country in the world before producing some plausible instance of United Kingdom Britain bettering it. The character has a great love for the British 1970s rock band Queen (band) Queen, often getting musician(s) on his show to perform one of Queens tunes in their own style.

The character first appeared in 1994 during the Edinburgh Festival Fringe, in the show "Pub Internationale", with Harry Hill<ref name="multiple"/> and Matthew Bradstock-Smith (who played "Little Alan" as well as the keyboards in Hills Edinburgh, radio and TV shows). The show featured the "Pub Band", with Murray playing drums and compering. After trying out a character deemed not to have worked, at the opening show Murray suggested saying that the compere had not made it to the show and that the barman in the venue, the Pleasance Cabaret Bar, had offered to fill in. At the Edinburgh Festival,<ref>{{cite web  url=https://edinburghfestival.list.co.uk/event/408504-al-murray-the-pub-landlords-summer-saloon/  title=Al Murray - The Pub Landlords Summer Saloon  work=edinburghfestival.list.co.uk}}</ref> Murray came up with "The Pub Landlord".<ref>Stewart Lee discusses whether all of Murrays audience realise the character is satirical in his book {{cite book title=How I Escaped My Certain Fate  url=http://www.faber.co.uk/catalog/how-i-escaped-my-certain-fate/9780571254804  publisher=Faber & Faber  location=London  year=2010  pages=293–294  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029202728/http://www.faber.co.uk/catalog/how-i-escaped-my-certain-fate/9780571254804  archivedate=29 October 2013 }}</ref>

Murray made his first television appearances on Harry Hill (TV series) Harry Hill in 1997 playing Harrys big brother Alan ("If its too hard, I cant understand it!"), and subsequently featured in a short film, Pub Fiction. He made a brief appearance as the Pub Landlord in Series 2, Episode 6 of Lee and Herrings This Morning with Richard Not Judy. Murrays Pub Landlord theatre show, My Gaff, My Rules was short-listed for an Laurence Olivier Award Olivier Award in 2002.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://theenvelope.latimes.com/extras/lostmind/year/2001/2001_2002lo.htm  title=2001-2002 26th Laurence Olivier Awards  newspaper=Los Angeles Times  accessdate=27 December 2006  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061017162031/http://theenvelope.latimes.com/extras/lostmind/year/2001/2001_2002lo.htm  archivedate=17 October 2006  deadurl=no  df=dmy }}</ref>

The Pub Landlord is central focus of the television series Time Gentlemen Please, as well as a number of other television appearances, including the An Audience with... strand. Subsequent theatre tours were: ...And A Glass of White Wine for the Lady (another catchphrase) and Giving it Both Barrels.

The Pub Landlord has hosted several television programmes including Fact Hunt, named after the fictional quiz machine of the same name from Time Gentlemen Please,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2005/03/19/316/pints_mean_prizes  title=Pints mean prizes  work=Chortle  accessdate=12 February 2007}}</ref> the BBC One show Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo and UKTV channel Dave (TV channel) Daves Al Murrays Compete for the Meat Compete for the Meat, which is co-hosted by Zöe Salmon, with voice-overs from Jim Rosenthal. His chat show Al Murrays Happy Hour began airing 13 January 2007 on ITV (TV network) ITV.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/article/0,,14934-2498641_3,00.html  title=When Harry met Murray  work=Times Online  accessdate=7 January 2007  location=London  first=Philippe  last=Naughton  subscription=yes}}</ref> The show has won a [http://www.britishcomedyawards.com/past-winners/2007.aspx British Comedy Award] for "Best New Entertainment Comedy".<ref>{{cite web title=The British Comedy Awards Past Winners url=http://www.britishcomedyawards.com/past-winners/2007.aspx website=The British Comedy Awards accessdate=26 May 2017}}</ref> Murray was the headliner of a July 2009 episode of Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow, filmed at the Brighton Dome.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00lpkb7  title=Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow  work=BBC One  date=6 April 2010}}</ref>

The Pub Landlord has written four books: The Pub Landlords Book Of British Common Sense, The Pub Landlords Think Yourself British, The Pub Landlords Great British Quiz Book, and Lets Re-Great Britain.
