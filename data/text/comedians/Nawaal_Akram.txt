Nawaal Akram is a Qatari comedian, model, athlete and disability rights campaigner. She was diagnosed with Duchenne muscular dystrophy at the age of six, and was forcefully removed from education against her wishes at the age of 10 by the school . she became wheelchair-bound at age of 12 . She has since founded Muscular Dystrophy Qatar to raise awareness of the condition, and was named one of BBCs 100 Women (BBC) 100 Women programme in 2017.

==Career==
Born in Doha, Qatar to Pakistani parents,<ref name=stero/><ref name="happy"/> Nawaal Akram is one of six children to Mohammed and Saima Akram. <ref name="tribune"/> At the age of six, she was diagnosed with Duchenne muscular dystrophy, and was wheelchair-bound by the age of 12 after falling and breaking her leg.<ref name="happy">{{cite news last1=Nangiana first1=Umer title="I love to make people happy" url=http://www.gulf-times.com/story/432759/I-love-to-make-people-happy accessdate=3 November 2017 work=Gulf Times date=28 March 2015}}</ref> Her condition led to Akram being bullied by other children, which was ignored by the teachers in her school. Akram was then removed from her school, as they did not want to take responsibility for a physically disabled child. Her parents threatened to sue the school but withdrew as it would have affected the education of her siblings who were studying there. Applications to other schools followed, but these were rejected on the basis of her condition or nationality. Special schools were contacted, but she was rejected from those as she did not have a Mental disability (disambiguation) mental disability. Akram eventually tried home school homeschooling, but this was ineffective as it used part-time tutors.<ref name="love">{{cite web last1=Perera first1=Umanga title=Nawaal Akram: Breaking stereotypes and changing narratives on disability url=http://www.iloveqatar.net/news/content/nawaal-akram-breaking-stereotypes-and-changing-narratives-disability publisher=I Love Qatar accessdate=3 November 2017 date=30 August 2016}}</ref>

Because of the lack of access to education, she began to feel depression (mood) depressed. Akram started to work at Mada, a non-project disability organisation in Qatar. While there, she learnt about how social media can be used to promote ideas.<ref name=stero>{{cite news last1=Scott first1=Victoria title=Qatar teen with muscular dystrophy breaks down stereotypes, one at a time url=https://dohanews.co/qatar-teen-with-muscular-dystrophy-breaks-down-stereotypes-one-at-a-time/ accessdate=3 November 2017 work=Doha News date=16 April 2016}}</ref> 

She has since performed stand up comedy as a comedian with Stand Up Comedy Qatar (SUCQ), the countrys first and only collective of stand up comedians, that was started in November 2010.<ref name="sucq">{{cite news last1=Savodnik first1=Peter title="Yes, There Are Comedians in Qatar" url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/27/magazine/yes-there-are-comedians-in-qatar.html accessdate=6 June 2018 work=New York Times date=24 May 2012}}</ref>
Akram also works a Model (person) model. 

Akram took up the Paralympic sport Boccia, and expanded her social media presence including her YouTube channel "Nawaals Adventures". In 2016, she founded the organisation Muscular Dystrophy Qatar to raise awareness of the condition within the country.<ref name="love"/> She was named one of BBCs 100 Women (BBC) 100 Women programme, signifying her as one of the most influential and inspiration women of the year.<ref name="tribune">{{cite news last1=Kiel Lumanglas first1=Marson title=Qatars Girl Conquers the World url=http://www.qatar-tribune.com/PrintNews.aspx?id=89502 accessdate=3 November 2017 work=Qatar Tribune date=11 October 2017}}</ref>
