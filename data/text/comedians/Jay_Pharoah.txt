==Early life== 

Pharoah began doing impersonations at age six and cites Gilbert Gottfrieds character in Aladdin (1992 Disney film) Aladdin, Iago (Aladdin) Iago, as his first voice, explaining, "My father put me in a talent competition a couple of months later, and out of the whole thing I got fifth place."<ref name=FabFour>Moynihan, Rob. "SNL{{}}s Fab Four", TV Guide, March 7, 2011, Pages 44-45</ref>

In 2005, Pharoah graduated from Indian River High School (Virginia) Indian River High School in Chesapeake. One of his characters, Principal Daniel Frye, is heavily influenced by IRHSs former principal,<ref>{{cite web author=The Virginian-Pilot© June 12, 2014  url=http://hamptonroads.com/2014/06/after-27-years-indian-river-principal-james-frye-retires#  title=After 27 years, Indian River Principal James Frye retires publisher=HamptonRoads.com  date=2014-06-12  accessdate=2014-11-11}}</ref> James Frye.<ref>{{cite web author=The Virginian-Pilot© December 14, 2010  url=http://hamptonroads.com/2010/12/jay-pharoah-pays-tribute-his-chesapeake-principal-snl  title=Jay Pharoah pays tribute to his Chesapeake principal on SNL publisher=HamptonRoads.com  date=2010-12-14  accessdate=2012-01-15}}</ref> Pharoah went on to study business at Tidewater Community College and Virginia Commonwealth University.<ref name=WashingtonPost/>

==Career==
File:Jay Pharoah.jpg thumb right Pharoah at The Stand in June 2016.
Pharoah has been performing stand-up comedy in community theaters and at comedy clubs in Virginia since he was 15.<ref>Sources conflict on when he started standup. The [http://voices.washingtonpost.com/reliable-source/2010/09/this_just_in_saturday_night_li.html Washington Post] source indicates he started at 15.</ref> He at one point toured with Corey Holcomb and Charlie Murphy.<ref name=WashingtonPost/> He became known for his many celebrity impressions,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.nbcuniversal.presscentre.com/content/detail.aspx?ReleaseID=1683&NewsAreaId=2  title=NBCS SATURDAY NIGHT LIVE PREMIERES SEPTEMBER 25 WITH SNL ALUM AMY POEHLER & CHART-TOPPING MUSICAL GUEST KATY PERRY  publisher=Nbcuniversal.presscentre.com  date=2010-09-07  accessdate=2012-01-15}}</ref> including Barack Obama, Will Smith, Jay-Z, 50 Cent, Eddie Murphy, Charlie Murphy, Chris Rock, Kanye West, Stephen A. Smith, and Denzel Washington.<ref name=WashingtonPost/>

Pharoah can also be seen on an episode of MTVs Yo Momma. {{Citation needed date=July 2018}} 

Pharoah became an internet phenomenon when his impersonation of Barack Obama became widely seen on YouTube. In early 2010, Pharoah appeared in a series of segments that aired on 106 & Park in promotion for the BET Awards 2010 BET Awards, where his character had to deliver an important package for television personalities Rocsi and Terrence Jenkins Terrence J.<ref name=WashingtonPost/><ref name=FabFour/>

===2010&ndash;2016: Saturday Night Live===
In 2010, Pharoah was hired by Saturday Night Live as a featured player for the shows 36th season. Pharoah debuted on Saturday Night Live on September 25, 2010 and was regarded by Rob Moynihan of TV Guide as the "breakout player" for that season, for his impersonations of Barack Obama, Ben Carson, Kanye West, Jay-Z, Stephen A. Smith, Will Smith, Eddie Murphy, Tracy Morgan, Chris Rock, Chris Tucker, Michael Strahan, Lil Wayne, Kendrick Lamar, and Denzel Washington.<ref name=WashingtonPost/><ref name=FabFour/> He debuted his SNL impersonation of Barack Obama in the 38th season premiere on September 15, 2012, succeeding Fred Armisen in that role.<ref name=TVGuide>"Inbox". TV Guide. October 8, 2012.</ref> Rolling Stone magazine described him as the "Jimmy Fallon of 2 Chainz impressions."<ref name="rollingstone.com"/>

Pharoah appeared in the independent film Lola Versus, released by Fox Searchlight Pictures in June 2012. In 2014, he had a small role in the buddy cop film Ride Along (film) Ride Along, starring Ice Cube and Kevin Hart, and appeared in the independent film Balls Out (2014 film) Balls Out, a sport comedy starring fellow SNL cast members Beck Bennett and Kate McKinnon.<ref name=HuffPost>{{cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/24/kate-mckinnon-shooting-intramural-in-austin-this-summer_n_3646030.html title=Kate McKinnon, Jay Pharoah Shooting Indie Intramural In Austin This Summer last=Luippold first=Ross work=The Huffington Post date=2013-07-24 accessdate=2014-03-10}}</ref>

In 2016, he appeared in a Television advertisement commercial for Old Navy,<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.campaignlive.com/article/old-navy-enlists-snl-alums-improvise-series-online-spots/1393554 title=Old Navy enlists SNL alums to improvise series of online spots publisher=www.campaignlive.com access-date=2016-06-12}}</ref> alongside fellow SNL cast members Nasim Pedrad and Cecily Strong. On August 8, 2016, it was announced Pharaoh alongside fellow cast member Taran Killam would be exiting the show ahead of its 42nd season.<ref>{{cite web url=http://shadowandact.com/2016/08/08/jay-pharoah-is-saying-goodbye-to-saturday-night-live-to-pursue-other-opportunities/ title=Jay Pharoah Is Saying Goodbye to Saturday Night Live to Pursue Other Opportunities website=shadowandact.com}}</ref> Pharoah hosted the American Music Awards of 2016 with model Gigi Hadid. Pharoah has been working on his first album with record producer Myles William.<ref>{{cite web url=http://pitchfork.com/news/67935-jay-pharoah-saturday-night-live-preps-new-mixtape/ title=Jay Pharoah (“Saturday Night Live”) Preps New Mixtape - Pitchfork website=pitchfork.com}}</ref>
