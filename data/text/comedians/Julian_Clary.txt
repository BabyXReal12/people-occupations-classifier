==Career==

===Stand-up comedy===
Clary began his career under the name Leo Hurll, a fake keyboardist for pop band Thinkman (a recording project conceived by Rupert Hine).
He entered the alternative comedy scene in the early 1980s, first under the alias "Gillian Pieface", and later as "The Joan Collins Fanclub". He wore heavy glamour (presentation) glam make-up and dressed in outrageous clothes, often involving leather/PVC and hinting at Bondage (BDSM) bondage.
