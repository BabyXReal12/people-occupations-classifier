
Kevin Delaney Kline (born October 24, 1947) is an American film and stage actor and singer. He has won an Academy Award and three Tony Awards and is a 2003 American Theatre Hall of Fame inductee.<ref>{{cite web url=http://old.post-gazette.com/ae/20040128fameweb0128p1.asp title=Theater honors put women in the spotlight publisher=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette accessdate=December 22, 2015}}</ref>

Kline began his career on stage in 1972 with The Acting Company. He has gone on to win three Tony Awards for his work on Broadway theatre Broadway, winning Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical Best Featured Actor in a Musical for the 1978 original production of On the Twentieth Century, Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical Best Actor in a Musical for the 1981 revival of The Pirates of Penzance, and Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play for the 2017 revival of Present Laughter.<ref name="ibdb">{{IBDB name 67986}}</ref>

He made his film debut opposite Meryl Streep in Sophies Choice (film) Sophies Choice (1982). For his role in the 1988 comedy hit A Fish Called Wanda, he won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. In 2003, he starred as Falstaff in the Broadway production of Henry IV, Part 1 Henry IV, for which he won the Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actor in a Play.

He has been nominated for 2 Emmy Awards, two BAFTA Awards and five Golden Globe Awards. His other films include The Big Chill (film) The Big Chill (1983), Silverado (film) Silverado (1985), Cry Freedom (1987), A Fish Called Wanda (1988), Grand Canyon (1991 film) Grand Canyon (1991), Dave (film) Dave (1993), The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996 film) The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996), The Ice Storm (film) The Ice Storm (1997), In & Out (film) In & Out (1997), The Road to El Dorado (2000), De-Lovely (2004), The Conspirator (2010), My Old Lady (film) My Old Lady (2014), and Beauty and the Beast (2017 film) Beauty and the Beast (2017).<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.freep.com/story/entertainment/movies/2017/03/16/beauty-beast-movie-review/99223164/ title=Review: Live-action ‘Beauty and the Beast’ is lifeless website=freep.com accessdate=August 16, 2017}}</ref> Since 2011, Kline has had a recurring role on the animated comedy series Bobs Burgers.

==Early life==
Kline was born in St. Louis, Missouri, to Margaret Agnes Kirk and Robert Joseph Kline (1909-1996).<ref
name="Robert"/><ref name=obu1>{{cite news url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=SL&p_theme=sl&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0F48277DB5974B5A&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM work=St. Louis Post-Dispatch title=Peggy Kirk Kline profile date=June 8, 2000}}</ref> His father was a classical music lover and an amateur opera singer who owned and operated The Record Bar, a record store in St. Louis that opened in the early 1940s, and sold toys during the 1960s and 1970s;<ref name="Robert">{{cite news title=Robert J. Kline, 87; Was Opera Buff, Owner of Record Stores work=St. Louis Post-Dispatch url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=SL&p_theme=sl&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB04FD636DDED95&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM date=September 7, 1996 accessdate=June 7, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web title=Kevin Kline biography work=filmreference.com url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/88/Kevin-Kline.html year=2008 accessdate=June 7, 2008}}</ref> his fathers family also owned Klines Inc., a department store chain. Kline has described his mother as the "dramatic theatrical character in our family".<ref>{{cite news title=Kevin Kline star bio work=Tribute url=http://www.tribute.ca/bio.asp?id=2164 year=2008 accessdate=June 7, 2008}}</ref> Klines father was Jewish, from a family that had emigrated from German Jew Germany, and had become an agnostic. Klines mother was a Roman Catholic of Irish American Irish descent, the daughter of an emigrant from County Louth. Kline was raised in his mothers Catholic faith.<ref>{{cite web url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=19830211&id=-FEsAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Vc4EAAAAIBAJ&pg=5848,2227600 title=Herald-Journal - Google News Archive Search work=google.com}}</ref> He has an older sister, Kate, and two younger brothers, Alexander and Christopher.<ref name=obu1/> 

He graduated from the Saint Louis Priory School in 1965.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/movies/person/38699/Kevin-Kline "Kevin Kline Biography"] The New York Times, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> In 1997, the school named its new auditorium as the Kevin Kline Theater in his honor. Kline performed selections from Shakespeare as a benefit at the dedication.

He attended Indiana University Bloomington Indiana University, Bloomington, where he was a classmate of actor Jonathan Banks. He began studying composing and conducting music, but switched to a theater and speech major for his last two years, graduating in 1970. Kline remembers: "When I switched to the Theater Department, all I did was theater...I could barely make it to class because this was my passion."<ref>Lindquist, David. [http://www.indystar.com/story/entertainment/movies/2014/09/12/kevin-kline-indiana-university-bloomington-maggie-smith-wanda-chill/15510183/ "Kevin Kline returns to IU, where his acting began"] indystar.com, September 12, 2014</ref> While an undergraduate, he was a co-founder of the Vest Pocket Players, an off-campus theatrical troupe.<ref>[http://www.tribute.ca/people/kevin-kline/2164/ "Kevin Kline"] tribute.ca, accessed February 12, 2016</ref>

==Career==
File:Kevin Kline, No Strings Attached Premiere.jpg thumb Kline at the film premiere of No Strings Attached in January 2011
In 1970, Kline was awarded a scholarship to the newly formed Drama Division at the Juilliard School in New York. In 1972, he joined with fellow Juilliard graduates, including Patti LuPone and David Ogden Stiers, and formed the City Center Acting Company (now The Acting Company), under the aegis of John Houseman.<ref>Klein, Alvin. [https://www.nytimes.com/1992/07/12/nyregion/theater-from-juilliard-to-shakespeare-at-a-pond.html?pagewanted= "Theater. From Juilliard to Shakespeare at a Pond"] The New York Times, July 12, 1992</ref>{{citation needed date=December 2015}}

The Company traveled across the U.S. performing William Shakespeare Shakespeares plays, other classical works, and the musical The Robber Bridegroom (musical) The Robber Bridegroom, founding one of the most widely praised groups in American repertory theatre. At Juilliard, he studied singing with Beverley Peck Johnson.<ref name="nyt">{{cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/2001/01/22/arts/beverley-peck-johnson-96-voice-teacher.html title=Beverley Peck Johnson, 96, Voice Teacher work=The New York Times date=January 22, 2001 author=Anthony Tommasini}}</ref>

In 1976, Kline left The Acting Company and settled in New York City, doing a brief appearance as the character "Woody Reed" in the now-defunct soap opera Search for Tomorrow. He followed this with a return to the stage in 1977 to play Clym Yeobright opposite Donna Theodore as Eustacia Vye in The Hudson Guild Theater production of Dance on a Country Grave, Kelly Hamiltons musical version of Thomas Hardys The Return of the Native.<ref name="ibdb"/> In 1978, he played the role of Bruce Granit, a matinée idol caricature, in Harold Princes On the Twentieth Century, for which he won his first Tony Award. In 1981, Kline appeared with rock diva Linda Ronstadt and singer Rex Smith in the New York Shakespeare Festivals Central Park production of The Pirates of Penzance, winning another Tony Award for Best Leading Actor in a Musical, for his comically dashing portrayal of the Pirate King.<ref name="ibdb"/> In 1983, he played the role in a The Pirates of Penzance (1983 film) film version of the musical, also with Ronstadt, Smith and Angela Lansbury, which had a limited theatrical release.<ref>Maslin, Janet. [https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/65523/The-Pirates-of-Penzance/overview "The Pirates of Penzance OvervieW"] The New York Times, accessed February 12, 2016</ref>

In the ensuing years, Kline appeared many times in New York Shakespeare Festival productions of Shakespeare plays, including starring roles in Richard III (play) Richard III (1983),<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/Archives/Production/1955 "King Richard III, Delacort Theater"] lortel.org, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> Much Ado About Nothing (1988),<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/Archives/Production/1566 "Much Ado About Nothing, Delacort Theater"] lortel.org, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> Henry V (play) Henry V (1984),<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/Archives/Production/1881 "Henry V, Delacort Theater"] lortel.org, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> and two productions of Hamlet, in 1986<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/Archives/Production/1814 "Hamlet 1986, Delacort Theater"] lortel.org, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> and 1990 (which he also directed).<ref>  [http://www.lortel.org/Archives/Production/1253  "Hamlet 1990, Delacort Theater"] lortel.org, accessed February 12, 2016</ref> A videotape of the 1990 production has aired on PBS.

He also appeared in a Lincoln Center production that combined the two parts of Henry IV on Broadway at the Vivian Beaumont Theatre in 2003 as Falstaff. Kline was nominated for the 2004 Tony Award, Actor in a Play.<ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/11223/Henry-IV "Henry 1V Broadway"] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150914031405/http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/11223/Henry-IV  date=September 14, 2015 }} playbillvault.com, accessed February 12, 2016</ref><ref>Simonson, Robert. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/lincoln-center-hears-the-chimes-at-midnight-as-ambitious-henry-iv-opens-116428# "Lincoln Center Hears the Chimes at Midnight as Ambitious Henry IV Opens"] Playbill, November 20, 2003</ref>

Dubbed "the American Laurence Olivier Olivier" by New York Times theater critic Frank Rich for his stage acting,<ref>Charles Isherwood, [https://www.nytimes.com/2008/01/01/theater/01kline.html "This King, This Courtier, These Kevin Klines"], nytimes.com, January 1, 2008.</ref> Kline finally ventured into film in 1982 in Sophies Choice (film) Sophies Choice. He won the coveted role of the tormented and mercurial Nathan opposite Meryl Streep. Streep won an Academy Award for her performance in the film. Kline was nominated for a 1983 Golden Globe award (New Star of the Year)<ref>[http://www.goldenglobes.com/film/sophies-choice "Sophies Choice Golden Globe Awards and Nominations"] goldenglobes.com, accessed February 11, 2016</ref> and BAFTA Award for Most Outstanding Newcomer To Film.<ref>[http://www.hollywood.com/movies/sophie-s-choice-59129594/awards/ "Sophies Choice Awards and Nominations"] hollywood.com, accessed February 11, 2016</ref>

During the 1980s and early 1990s, Kline made several films with director Lawrence Kasdan, including The Big Chill (film) The Big Chill, Silverado (film) Silverado, Grand Canyon (1991 film) Grand Canyon, I Love You to Death, and French Kiss (1995 film) French Kiss. He played Donald Woods in Richard Attenboroughs Cry Freedom opposite Denzel Washington about the friendship between Activist Stephen Biko and editor Donald Woods.

In 1989, Kline won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in the British comedy A Fish Called Wanda, in which he played a painfully inept American ex-Central Intelligence Agency CIA thug opposite John Cleeses genteel British barrister and Jamie Lee Curtis femme fatale/con woman. In 2000, the American Film Institute ranked the film twenty-first on AFIs 100 Years... 100 Laughs.<ref>{{cite web title=100 Years, 100 Laughs publisher=American Film Institute url=http://www.afi.com/tvevents/100years/laughs.aspx year=2000 accessdate=June 7, 2008 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080603155528/http://www.afi.com/tvevents/100years/laughs.aspx archivedate=June 3, 2008}}</ref>

Other awards have included Drama Desk Awards, Golden Globe awards, a Gotham Award, a Hasty Pudding Theatricals Hasty Pudding Theatricals Man of the Year Award, and a St. Louis International Film Festival Lifetime Achievement Award. He was inducted in the American Theatre Hall of Fame in 2003.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.americantheatrecritics.org/theatre-hall-of-fame title=American Theatre Critics Association publisher=Americantheatrecritics.org date=2014-01-29 accessdate=2015-02-07}}</ref> He has a star on the St. Louis Walk of Fame.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.stlouiswalkoffame.org/inductees/?view=achievement title=St. Louis Walk of Fame Inductees last=St. Louis Walk of Fame publisher=stlouiswalkoffame.org accessdate=April 25, 2013}}</ref> Film reviewers have praised Kline. Newsday critic Lynn Darling wrote on July 13, 1988, that Kline "has proved himself to be one of the most talented and versatile American actors of his generation."

Kline played the title role in King Lear at the Public Theater and took the lead role in a Broadway production of Cyrano de Bergerac (play) Cyrano de Bergerac opposite Jennifer Garner.  That production was forced to close temporarily after only 11 performances as a result of the Broadway stagehands strike,<ref name="Cyrano">{{cite news last=Hetrick  first=Adam  title=Morton, Sarandon and Baker Will Join Kline in Broadways Cyrano; Casting Complete  publisher=Playbill  date=September 25, 2007  url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/111357.html  accessdate=September 26, 2007  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071013011153/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/111357.html  archivedate=October 13, 2007  df= }}</ref> but subsequently reopened. Cyrano was filmed in 2008 and aired as part of PBSs Great Performances series in January 2009.

In January 2008, Kline won a Screen Actors Guild award for his portrayal of Jaques in Kenneth Branaghs film As You Like It (2006 film) As You Like It, adapted from As You Like It Shakespeares play. The film premiered theatrically in 2006 in Europe. It bypassed theatres and was sent straight to HBO in the U.S. Klines film The Conspirator premiered during the Toronto International Film Festival in 2010 and was described as an "old-fashioned historical thriller". It was well received by most critics. Kline also starred in the 2012 comedy Darling Companion alongside Diane Keaton. In 2017, Kline returned to Broadway in a revival of the play Present Laughter, for which he received his third Tony Award.

In December 2004, Kline became the 2,272nd recipient of a star on List of stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame Hollywood Walk of Fame,<ref>[http://www.theage.com.au/news/People/Kline-gets-Hollywood-star/2004/12/04/1101923372496.html "Kline gets Hollywood star"], TheAge.com.au, December 4, 2004.</ref> for his contributions to the motion picture industry, located at 7000 Hollywood Boulevard.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://projects.latimes.com/hollywood/star-walk/kevin-kline/ title=Kevin Kline website=latimes.com access-date=2016-06-12}}</ref>

==Personal life==
File:Phoebe Cates 3.jpg thumb right Kline and his wife Phoebe Cates at the Academy Awards Governors Ball party, 1989
Kline met actress Phoebe Cates in 1983. They began dating in 1985 and married in 1989. The couple live on the Upper East Side of Manhattan in New York City. They have two children, Owen (born 1991) and Greta (1994), who fronts the band Frankie Cosmos. <ref>{{cite web last=Pelly first=Jenn title=Frankie Cosmos url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9345-frankie-cosmos publisher=Pitchfork Media accessdate=March 5, 2014}}</ref>

After his son, Owen, was diagnosed with juvenile diabetes, Kline became active with the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation. In November 2004, he was presented with the JDRFs Humanitarian of the Year award by Meryl Streep for his volunteer efforts on behalf of the organization.{{citation needed date=April 2016}}

The Kevin Kline Awards honor theatre professionals in St. Louis in an array of categories, which include best actor and actress, set design, choreography, and new play or musical.<ref>Brown, Dennis. [http://www.riverfronttimes.com/2005-12-14/culture/great-expectations/ "Great Expectations"] Riverfront Times, December 14, 2005</ref><ref>[http://www.kevinklineawards.org "Official website"] kevinklineawards.org, accessed February 11, 2016</ref><ref>Jones, Kenneth. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/st.-louis-kevin-kline-awards-go-to-steven-woolf-red-curtis-holbrook-awake-a-189131# "St. Louis Kevin Kline Awards Go to Steven Woolf, Red, Curtis Holbrook, Awake and Sing!, Immigrant and More"] playbill.com, April 4, 2012</ref>
