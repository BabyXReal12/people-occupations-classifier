Kevin Delaney Kline (born October 24, 1947) is an American film and stage actor and singer. He has won an Academy Award and three Tony Awards and is a 2003 American Theatre Hall of Fame inductee.
Kline began his career on stage in 1972 with The Acting Company. He has gone on to win three Tony Awards for his work on Broadway theatre Broadway, winning Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical Best Featured Actor in a Musical for the 1978 original production of On the Twentieth Century, Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical Best Actor in a Musical for the 1981 revival of The Pirates of Penzance, and Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play for the 2017 revival of Present Laughter.
He made his film debut opposite Meryl Streep in Sophies Choice (film) Sophies Choice (1982). For his role in the 1988 comedy hit A Fish Called Wanda, he won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. In 2003, he starred as Falstaff in the Broadway production of Henry IV, Part 1 Henry IV, for which he won the Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actor in a Play.

He has been nominated for 2 Emmy Awards, two BAFTA Awards and five Golden Globe Awards. His other films include The Big Chill (film) The Big Chill (1983), Silverado (film) Silverado (1985), Cry Freedom (1987), A Fish Called Wanda (1988), Grand Canyon (1991 film) Grand Canyon (1991), Dave (film) Dave (1993), The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996 film) The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996), The Ice Storm (film) The Ice Storm (1997), In &amp; Out (film) In &amp; Out (1997), The Road to El Dorado (2000), De-Lovely (2004), The Conspirator (2010), My Old Lady (film) My Old Lady (2014), and Beauty and the Beast (2017 film) Beauty and the Beast (2017).
Since 2011, Kline has had a recurring role on the animated comedy series Bobs Burgers.

==Early life==
Kline was born in St. Louis, Missouri, to Margaret Agnes Kirk and Robert Joseph Kline (1909-1996).
His father was a classical music lover and an amateur opera singer who owned and operated The Record Bar, a record store in St. Louis that opened in the early 1940s, and sold toys during the 1960s and 1970s;
his fathers family also owned Klines Inc., a department store chain. Kline has described his mother as the "dramatic theatrical character in our family".
Klines father was Jewish, from a family that had emigrated from German Jew Germany, and had become an agnostic. Klines mother was a Roman Catholic of Irish American Irish descent, the daughter of an emigrant from County Louth. Kline was raised in his mothers Catholic faith.
He has an older sister, Kate, and two younger brothers, Alexander and Christopher.
