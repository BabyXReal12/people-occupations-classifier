
Justin Jacob Long<ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/justin-long/bio/159557/  title= Justin Long  publisher= TV Guide TVGuide.com  accessdate=September 30, 2015}}</ref> (born June 2, 1978) is an American actor, comedian and humorist known for roles in such films as Jeepers Creepers (2001 film) Jeepers Creepers (2001), Dodgeball: A True Underdog Story Dodgeball (2004), Accepted (2006), Alvin and the Chipmunks (film) Alvin and the Chipmunks (2007), Live Free or Die Hard (2007), Alpha and Omega (film) Alpha and Omega (2010), Tusk (2014 film) Tusk (2014) and Comet (film) Comet (2014). He played Warren P. Cheswick on the NBC TV series Ed (TV series) Ed and appeared with John Hodgman in TV commercials for Apple Inc. Apples "Get a Mac" advertising campaign.

==Early life==
Long was born the middle son of three boys in Fairfield, Connecticut, to father R. James Long, a philosophy and Latin professor at Fairfield University, and mother, Wendy Lesniak,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.moviesonline.ca/movienews_12217.html  title=Justin Long Interview, Die Hard 4 - MoviesOnline  publisher=Moviesonline.ca  accessdate=2010-03-22  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100906081339/http://www.moviesonline.ca/movienews_12217.html  archivedate=2010-09-06  df= }}</ref> a former actress who has mostly appeared on stage.<ref>{{cite web title=Justin Long biography url=http://www.buddytv.com/info/justin-long-info.aspx publisher=Buddytv accessdate=21 September 2014}}</ref> His maternal grandmother was of Sicilian-American Sicilian descent.<ref>{{cite web last=Murray first=Rebecca url=http://movies.about.com/od/accepted/a/acceptdjl080806_2.htm title=Justin Long Talks About The Sasquatch Dumpling Gang and Strange Wilderness work=About.com accessdate=2010-07-01}}</ref> Long had a "kind of conservative" Catholic Church Roman Catholic upbringing.<ref>{{cite web last=Topel first=Fred url=http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Interview-Justin-Long-3173.html title=Interview: Justin Long work=Cinema Blend quote =My family is Roman Catholic and kind of conservative... date=2006-08-14 accessdate=2010-03-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-465952/Q--A-Die-Hards-Justin-Long.html title=Q & A with Die Hards Justin Long publisher=Daily Mail date=2007-07-03 quote = I grew up in a Catholic, conservative family accessdate=2010-10-19 first= last= location=London}}</ref> His older brother, Damian, is a local stage actor,  teacher, and theatre director at Weston High School (Connecticut) Weston High School.<ref name="Justin">{{cite web title=Justin Long- Biography url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/justin-long/bio/159557 publisher=TV Guide accessdate=21 May 2013}}</ref> Long attended Fairfield College Preparatory School, a Society of Jesus Jesuit school, and Vassar College, where he was a member of the sketch comedy group Laughingstock and starred in several plays, including Butterflies Are Free (play) Butterflies Are Free.<ref name="Justin"/>

==Career==
File:JustinLong07TIFF.jpg thumb left upright Long at the 2007 Toronto International Film Festival
Longs film credits include Idiocracy, Waiting... (film) Waiting..., Jeepers Creepers (2001 film) Jeepers Creepers,  DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story, The Break-Up, Crossroads (2002 film) Crossroads, Galaxy Quest, Dreamland (2006 film) Dreamland and Live Free or Die Hard. He was also a regular on the NBC TV series Ed (TV series) Ed (2000–04), playing socially awkward Warren P. Cheswick. He voiced the character of Alvin in Alvin and the Chipmunks (film) Alvin and the Chipmunks, Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakquel, Alvin and the Chipmunks: Chipwrecked, and Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip.<ref>{{cite web last1=Kroll first1=Justin title=Tony Hale Joins ‘Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip’ (EXCLUSIVE) url=https://variety.com/2015/film/news/tony-hale-alvin-and-the-chipmunks-the-road-chip-1201431279/ website=Variety accessdate=March 14, 2015}}</ref> He also played the main character in the comedy film Accepted (2006).

Long made a guest appearance in the Documentary film documentary, Wild West Comedy Show: 30 Days & 30 Nights – Hollywood to the Heartland Wild West Comedy Show (2006). In 2007, he co-starred with Bruce Willis as a "White hat (computer security) white-hat hacker" in Live Free or Die Hard<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Mac-Computer-And-Maggie-Q-For-Die-Hard-4-3468.html  title=Mac Computer And Maggie Q For Die Hard 4  publisher=Cinemablend.com  date=2006-09-22  accessdate=2010-03-22}}</ref> and had a role in the comedy The Sasquatch Gang.
Long is known for his depiction of a Macintosh Mac in Apple Inc. Apples "Get a Mac" Advertising campaign campaign. The campaign featured commercials in which Long as a Mac and John Hodgman as a Personal computer PC engaged in playful banter about "the strengths of the Mac platform and weaknesses of the PC platform."<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20060820204934/http://www.latimes.com/news/custom/showcase/la-ca-long23jul23,0,1268414.story?track=tottext McNamara, Mary. "Not Strictly Commercial"]. Los Angeles Times. July 23, 2006.</ref>

Long also had a small role in the comedy film Zack and Miri Make a Porno (2008), where he plays Brandon St. Randy, a Homosexuality gay Pornographic film actor porn star. In 2009, he starred in Hes Just Not That into You (film) Hes Just Not That into You along with co-star Ginnifer Goodwin and After.Life opposite Liam Neeson and Christina Ricci. He also provided the voice of Humphrey in Alpha and Omega (film) Alpha and Omega (2010), starring with Hayden Panettiere. Also in 2010, Long starred in the comedy film Going the Distance (2010 film) Going the Distance with Drew Barrymore. He was cast as a one-armed Civil War veteran in Robert Redfords The Conspirator.<ref>{{cite web author=Pics and Justin Long for Redfords Conspirator  url=http://www.newsinfilm.com/2009/10/16/pictures-and-justin-long-for-redfords-conspirator  title=Pics and Justin Long for Redfords Conspirator  publisher=News in Film  date=2009-10-16  accessdate=2010-03-22  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100415003124/http://www.newsinfilm.com/2009/10/16/pictures-and-justin-long-for-redfords-conspirator/  archivedate=2010-04-15  df= }}</ref>

Long read the audiobook version of Judy Blumes Then Again, Maybe I Wont and Stephen Kings Everythings Eventual. From July 7–18, 2010, he appeared in a production of Samuel J. and K. at the Williamstown Theatre Festival.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.wtfestival.org/2010/samueljandk  title=&#39;Samuel J. and K&#39; appearance  publisher=Wtfestival.org  accessdate=2013-07-25  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140501050918/http://www.wtfestival.org/2010/samueljandk  archivedate=2014-05-01  df= }}</ref> On August 16, 2010, he co-hosted WWE Raw with Going the Distance co-stars Charlie Day and Jason Sudeikis at the Staples Center in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.wwe.com/shows/raw/special/allspecialguesthosts/  title=Raw Special Guest Stars - August 16, 2010: The Cast of Going the Distance  publisher=Wwe.com  accessdate=2013-07-25}}</ref>
In 2014 he starred in the independent love drama Comet (film) Comet, which he and his co-star Emmy Rossum also produced.
