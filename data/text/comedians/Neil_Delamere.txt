Neil Delamere ({{IPAc-en ˈ d ɛ l ə m i ər}} {{respell DEL ə-meer}}; born 1979) is an Irish people Irish comedian.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.scotsman.com/7100/Comedy-review-Neil-Delamere.5596325.jp title=Comedy review: Neil Delamere last=Grat first=Martin date= 28 August 2009 work=The Scotsman accessdate=14 May 2011}}</ref> He is a regular on The Blame Game (BBC) Factbook and is represented by Off the Kerb.<ref>{{cite web title=Artist Pages: Neil Delamere url=http://www.offthekerb.co.uk/neil-delamere/ publisher=Off the Kerb accessdate=23 August 2013}}</ref>

==Brief profile==
Delamere is from Edenderry, County Offaly. He has a degree in Computer Applications from Dublin City University (DCU), where his portrait is displayed in the universitys library amongst other famous graduates. He began his career in 2004 at the Edinburgh Festival. Since then he has worked for both Raidió Teilifís Éireann (RTÉ) and the BBC, in programmes such as the BBC comedy series Daves One Night Stand One Night Stand, BBC radio and television panel quiz The Blame Game (radio) The Blame Game and RTÉ topical comedy show The Panel (Irish TV series) The Panel. He hosts Neil Delameres Just For Laughs, a comedy television show in which he interviews acts performing at the Montreal Festival. He featured on Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow on 4 July 2009. Delamere made his debut appearance on Radio Five Lives panel show Fighting Talk, broadcast on 11 December 2010, and won. Since his first appearance on the show, Delamere has made numerous appearances on the Sony award-winning sports show. {{Citation needed date=March 2012}}

In 2011, he hosted The Only Viking in the Village on RTÉ, in which he explored his Viking roots and informed viewers that the Vikings poured urine on their clothes.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.herald.ie/entertainment/tv-radio/neils-notso-horrible-history-the-only-viking-in-the-village-2949575.html title=Neils not-so horrible history the only viking in the village date=30 November 2011 accessdate=30 November 2011 work=Evening Herald}}</ref> The show was nominated in the factual category for an Irish Film and Television Award, and won. He has stood in for John Murray (Irish broadcaster) John Murray on RTÉ Radio 1.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/2012/0223/delamaren.html title=Neil Delamere to Present John Murray Show date=23 February 2012 accessdate=23 February 2012 work=RTÉ Ten}}</ref>

He presented the comedy documentary Theres Something about Patrick which aired on 14 March 2013 on RTÉ 1. In the documentary Delamere travels the length and breadth of Ireland (and beyond) to meet experts, historians and St Patrick’s Day revellers, to discover the truth about the Irish patron saint.<ref>{{cite web url= http://www.rte.ie/presspack/2013/03/14/theres-something-about-patrick/ title= THERE’S SOMETHING ABOUT PATRICK date= work=RTE.ie accessdate=21 March 2013 }}</ref>

Delamere also works for the Irish independent and national radio station, Today FM where he presents his own show titled "Neil Delameres Sunday Best" which airs every Sunday from 11{{nbsp}}am to 1{{nbsp}}pm.
