==Early life==
Carmichael was born and raised in Winston-Salem, North Carolina Winston-Salem, North Carolina,<ref name=biography.com /> the son of Cynthia, a secretary, and Joe Carmichael, a truck driver. He has an older brother, Joe Carmichael Jr.<ref name=NYTimes-CarmichaelShowS2-2016 /> He was raised in the Morningside neighborhood. Carmichael grew up poor, which is a frequent topic in his stand-up comedy. In fifth grade, he hosted a morning news show on his elementary schools local access channel.<ref>{{cite web title=Jerrod Carmichaels Brother Is Too Cool For His Show url=http://teamcoco.com/video/jerrod-carmichael-cool-brother website=Conan (talk show) Conan publisher=TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBS accessdate=May 4, 2017 date=August 25, 2015}}</ref> He graduated from Robert B. Glenn High School in 2005.<ref>{{cite news last1=Clodfelter first1=Tim title=Local comedian to star in NBC sitcom url=http://www.journalnow.com/news/local/local-comedian-to-star-in-nbc-sitcom/article_e8d0bd66-d403-11e4-9ae6-efba2c173d2c.html accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=Winston-Salem Journal location=North Carolina date=March 26, 2015   archivedate= July 19, 2017  archiveurl=https://archive.is/g030A   deadurl=no}} Additional [https://www.webcitation.org/6s5D2eZ5b WebCitation] archive on July 19, 2017.</ref>

Carmichaels early comedic influences were stand-ups Richard Pryor, Bill Cosby, Sinbad (comedian) Sinbad, and George Carlin.<ref>{{cite news title=Comedian Jerrod Carmichael Joins the Dan Patrick Show Live in Los Angeles (Full Interview) 5/1/17 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTky3HHxAN0 accessdate=May 4, 2017 work=Dan Patrick Show Live date=May 1, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
File:Jerrod Carmichael at the Comedy Store.jpg left thumb Carmichael at The Comedy Store in August 2010
At the age of 20, Carmichael moved to Los Angeles to pursue his dream of being a stand-up comic, before he had ever tried stand-up.<ref>{{cite web title=Jerrod Carmichaels Metric Of Success: How Many Cookies Is He Eating? url=http://teamcoco.com/video/jerrod-carmichael-eating-cookies website=Conan (talk show) Conan publisher=TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBS accessdate=May 4, 2017 language=en date=April 26, 2016}}</ref> His first time doing stand-up was an open-mic night at The Comedy Store in West Hollywood.<ref name="jimmyfallon2015">{{cite web title=Jerrod Carmichael Wants to Marry Meryl Streep url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9o1VgR68iXQ website=The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon  The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon publisher=NBC Television accessdate=May 12, 2017 date=August 4, 2015}}</ref> Working his way up through the clubs, Carmichael appeared in the "New Faces" showcase at the 2011 Just for Laughs Festival in Montreal.<ref name=Backstage-10ComicsToWatch-2012>{{cite news last1=Lehman first1=Daneil title=10 Comics to Watch: Jerrod Carmichael Q&A url=http://www.backstage.com/interview/10-comics-to-watch-jerrod-carmichael-qa/ accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=Backstage (magazine) Backstage date=July 9, 2012}}</ref> Carmichaels breakout role was in the 2014 film Neighbors (2014 film) Neighbors.<ref name=NYTimes-CarmichaelShowS2-2016 /><ref name=Paper-CarmichaelShowS1-2015>{{cite news last1=Zei first1=Michelle title=Rising Comic Jerrod Carmichael on Stand-Up, Millennials and Joking About Police Brutality url=http://www.papermag.com/rising-comic-jerrod-carmichael-on-stand-up-millennials-and-joking-abou-1427623931.html accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=Paper (magazine) Paper date=July 31, 2015  archivedate= March 11, 2016  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160311134427/http://www.papermag.com/rising-comic-jerrod-carmichael-on-stand-up-millennials-and-joking-abou-1427623931.html deadurl=no}}</ref> He was also on the TV show The Goodwin Games.<ref name=Backstage-10ComicsToWatch-2012 />

He has released two stand-up HBO comedy specials. The first, Jerrod Carmichael: Love at the Store, was released in 2014. It was directed by Spike Lee and filmed at The Comedy Store, where Carmichael had first performed stand-up.<ref name="jimmyfallon2015"/><ref name=NYTimes-LoveAtTheStore-2014>{{cite news last1=Zinoman first1=Jason title=Stand-Up Through a Filmmaker’s Lens: Spike Lee Enhances Jerrod Carmichael and Katt Williams url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/10/02/arts/spike-lee-enhances-jerrod-carmichael-and-katt-williams.html accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=The New York Times date =October 1, 2014}}</ref> The second, Jerrod Carmichael: 8, was released on March 11, 2017. It was filmed in the Masonic Hall (Manhattan) New York Masonic Halls Grand Lodge Room and directed by comedian Bo Burnham.<ref name=Jerrod-Carmichael-Bo-Burnham-Interview-2017>{{cite news last1=Husband first1=Andrew title=You Can Thank Jerrod Carmichael And Bo Burnham’s Friendship For 2017’s Best Comedy Special (So Far) url=http://uproxx.com/tv/jerrod-carmichael-bo-burnham-interview/ accessdate= March 14, 2017 publisher=Uproxx.com date=March 10, 2017 archivedate= July 5, 2017  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170705140404/http://uproxx.com/tv/jerrod-carmichael-bo-burnham-interview/ deadurl=no}}</ref>

His eponymous NBC The Carmichael Show sitcom, on which he stars and is a producer, has been well-received<ref name=Variety-CarmichaelShow-Review-2015>{{cite news last1=Lowry first1=Brian title=TV Review: ‘The Carmichael Show’ url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/reviews/the-carmichael-show-review-jerrod-carmichael-comedy-nbc-1201572671/ accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=Variety (magazine) Variety date=August 25, 2015}}</ref><ref name=Atlantic-CarmichaelShowS2-2016>{{cite news last1=Sims first1=David title=The Carmichael Show Proves Sitcoms Can Still Be Provocative url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2016/03/the-carmichael-show-is-back-to-push-hot-buttons/473037/ accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=The Atlantic date=March 10, 2016}}</ref> and is notable for its envelope-pushing approach to topical subjects like Black Lives Matter, LGBT issues, gun rights, politics, and the reality of being African Americans African American in the United States.<ref name=RollingStone-CarmichaelShowS1-2015>{{cite news last1=Yarm first1=Mark title=Jerrod Carmichael: Meet the Comic Saving the Old-School Sitcom url=https://www.rollingstone.com/tv/features/jerrod-carmichael-20151124#ixzz42ZLTfnFE accessdate= March 11, 2016 work=Rolling Stone (magazine) Rolling Stone date=November 24, 2015}}</ref> The show is semi-autobiographical.<ref name=HReporter-TCAs-2015>{{cite news last1=Stanhope first1=Kate title=Jerrod Carmichael on His NBC Series, TV Comedies "Empty Calories" and Networks "Growing Pains" url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/jerrod-carmichael-his-nbc-series-817273 accessdate=March 11, 2016 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=August 25, 2015}}</ref>

Carmichael was announced as a executive producer for the multi-sitcom sitcom Rel starring his former Carmichael Show co-star Lil Rel Howery. The show was picked up by Fox Broadcasting Company Fox in May 2018.
