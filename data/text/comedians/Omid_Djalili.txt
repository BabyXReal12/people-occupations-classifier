Omid Djalili ({{lang-fa {{nastaliq امید جلیلی fa}}}}; born 30 September 1965)
is a British people British stand-up comedian, actor, television producer, voice actor and writer.

==Early life and education==
Djalili was born in Chelsea, London, to Iranian Baháí Faith Baháí parents and is a Baháí himself.
He attended Holland Park School and then the University of Ulster at Coleraine University of Ulster in Coleraine, Northern Ireland, studying English language English and Theatre theatre studies.
==Comedy career==
{{BLP sources section date=February 2011}}
File:Omid Djalili at the Arts Depot in 2007.jpg thumb Djalili performing at the Arts Depot in 2007
The first significant success of his stand-up comedy career was at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe in 1995 with "Short, Fat Kebab Shop Owners Son", followed by "The Arab and the Jew" with Jewish comedian Ivor Dembina in 1996.
He has performed in numerous countries, including Australia, Republic of Ireland Ireland, Sweden, Belgium, Canada and the United States, where he had his own HBO Special and did 22 episodes of the NBC sitcom Whoopi with Whoopi Goldberg.

He did his part for Comic Relief (charity) Comic Relief after the 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami and also in 2005 he appeared on the British TV show Top Gear (current format) Top Gear as a celebrity driver. The same year he broke Edinburgh Festival box office records with over 16,500 ticket sales.

In 2006, Sky Digital (UK &amp; Ireland) Sky Television picked him to be the face of their Saturday night film premières, and he also announced a new tour of the UK called No Agenda, from January 2007 until March 2007, covering 23 different dates. The No Agenda tour DVD was released in late 2007.

On 18 March 2007, he was voted by the British public as the 60th best stand-up comedian in a Channel 4 programme "The 100 Greatest Stand-Ups". On 26 October 2007, he guest-presented the BBC political quiz show Have I Got News for You.
The Omid Djalili Show started on BBC1 on 17 November 2007. The series was a mix of sketches and stand-up material. A second series was recorded in late 2008 and began broadcast on BBC 1 on 20 April 2009. He performed on We Are Most Amused on ITV1 to mark Prince Charless 60th birthday in 2008.

==Acting career==
Djalili has appeared in a number of films, most notably Gladiator (2000 film) Gladiator, The Mummy (1999 film) The Mummy, Mean Machine (film) Mean Machine, The World Is Not Enough, Alien Autopsy (film) Alien Autopsy, Spy Game, Sky Captain and the World of Tomorrow, Grow Your Own (film) Grow Your Own, Notting Hill (film) Notting Hill, Mr Nice, Pirates of the Caribbean: At Worlds End, Sex and the City 2 and provides his voice in Over the Hedge (film) Over the Hedge.

He had observed that he usually appears as a generic Middle Eastern background character in many of these films, often commenting that he appears in the The World Is Not Enough James Bond film as the "Second Azerbaijani oil pipe attendant". He appeared as Nasim in the U.S. situation comedy sitcom Whoopi, starring Whoopi Goldberg, and picked up an international film award for Best Supporting Actor in Casanova (2005 film) Casanova, starring alongside Heath Ledger and Jeremy Irons.

On 12 February 2009, producer Cameron Mackintosh announced that Djalili would appear as the second Fagin in the new West End theatre West End production of Oliver! at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane, London. Omid took over from Rowan Atkinson, who was contracted until 18 July 2009.
In 2009, Djalili became the voice of Yusuf Amir in the popular gaming series Grand Theft Auto. He took up the role in the Grand Theft Auto: The Ballad of Gay Tony spin-off game. In 2010, he starred in the David Baddiel scripted film The Infidel (2010 film) The Infidel.

In 2010, Djalili starred in a series of TV and cinema adverts for Moneysupermarket.com.
Djalili appeared in the short-lived NBC sitcom The Paul Reiser Show, which was a midseason replacement for the 2010–11 television season.
For the BBC Learning project Off By Heart Shakespeare, Omid played Lord Capulet from Romeo and Juliet and delivered the speech "Hang thee, young baggage, disobedient wretch!"
Djalili appears in the Sky TV adaptation of Moonfleet shooting in Ireland in 2013.[http://www.rte.ie/ten/news/2013/0610/455765-sky-adventure-to-shoot-in-ireland/]

He appeared in the second season of Thunderbirds Are Go (TV series) Thunderbirds Are Go in October 2016 as Horse Williams.{{citation needed date=December 2016}}

Djalili provided his voice in the 2015 stop motion animated comedy Shaun the Sheep Movie.

In 2017 Djalili appeared in an acclaimed performance of Fiddler on the Roof at the Chichester Festival Theatre
.

==Other activities==
In 2008, he was an official festival judge for the Noor Iranian Film Festival. In June 2010, Djalili appeared in a Meltdown Festival concert
given by the Philharmonia Orchestra at Londons Queen Elizabeth Hall, performing the part of the narrator in Rubaiyat, a symphonic poem tone poem by American classical composer Alan Hovhaness which sets the words of Omar Khayyám to music.
He also appeared on the Graham Norton Show on 17 February 2012, alongside Daniel Radcliffe and Cuba Gooding Jr..

==Awards==
Djalili has won awards for his comedy. These include the EMMA Award, Time Out (company) Time Out Award, and London Weekend Television LWT Comedy Award for Best Stand-up Comedian, Spirit of the Fringe Award as well as the One World Media Award for his Channel 4 documentary, Bloody Foreigners.

He has also been nominated for awards, such as the Perrier Award for Best Comedian, the Gemini Award for Best Comedy Performance of 2003, the South Bank Award for Best Comedy of 2003, the Royal Television Society Award for Best Stand-up, and the European TV Award for his Bloody Foreigners.

==Personal life==
Djalili is a Chelsea F.C. Chelsea fan and has appeared many times in the clubs in-house channel, Chelsea TV.
