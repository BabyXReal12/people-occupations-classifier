Henry Albert Azaria ({{IPAc-en ə ˈ z ɛər i ə}} {{respell ə ZAIR ee-ə}}; born April 25, 1964) is an American actor, voice actor, comedian and producer. He is known for starring in the animated television sitcom The Simpsons (1989–present), voicing Moe Szyslak, Apu Nahasapeemapetilon, Chief Wiggum, Comic Book Guy, Lenny and Carl#Carl Carlson Carl Carlson and numerous others. After attending Tufts University, Azaria joined the series with little voice acting experience, but became a regular in its The Simpsons (season 2) second season, with many of his performances on the show being based on famous actors and characters.

Along with the series, Azaria became more widely known through his live-action appearances in films, such as The Birdcage (1996), Godzilla (1998 film) Godzilla (1998), Mystery Men (1999), Americas Sweethearts (2001), Shattered Glass (film) Shattered Glass (2003), Along Came Polly (2004), Run Fatboy Run (2007), Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian (2009), and The Smurfs (film) The Smurfs (2011). Since 2017, he has  starred as the title character in Brockmire.

He also had recurring roles on the television series Mad About You and Friends, played the title character in the drama Huff (TV series) Huff (2004–2006), and appeared in the popular stage musical Spamalot. Though originally known as a comic actor, Azaria has also taken on more dramatic roles, including the TV films Tuesdays with Morrie (film) Tuesdays With Morrie (1999) and Uprising (2001 film) Uprising (2001). He has won six Emmy Award Emmys and a Screen Actors Guild Award. Azaria was married to actress Helen Hunt from 1999 to 2000 and has been married to actress Katie Wright since 2007.

==Early life==
Azaria was born in Queens, New York City, the son of Eastern Sephardim Eastern Sephardic Jewish parents, Ruth (Altcheck) and Albert Azaria.

His grandparents on both sides hailed from Thessaloniki, from Greeces Spanish Jewish community established there after the Alhambra Decree 1492 exile from Spain. His familys spoken language at home was the Judeo-Spanish Ladino language, which Azaria has described as "a strange, antiquated Spanish dialect written in Hebrew characters."
Azarias father ran several dress-manufacturing businesses, while his mother raised him and his two older sisters, Stephanie and Elise.
