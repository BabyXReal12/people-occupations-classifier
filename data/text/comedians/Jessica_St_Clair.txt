
Jessica St. Clair (born September 21, 1976)<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.earwolf.com/episode/stage-moms-and-dropped-bombs-w-ana-ortiz/  title=Stage Moms and Dropped Bombs (w/ Ana Ortiz)  publisher=Earwolf.com  date=2016-09-22  accessdate=2016-01-03}}</ref> is an American actress and improvisational comedian from the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre. With frequent collaborator Lennon Parham, she co-created and co-starred in NBCs Best Friends Forever (TV series) Best Friends Forever and USA Networks Playing House (TV series) Playing House.

==Life and career==
St. Clair was born in Princeton, New Jersey<ref name="JVClub">{{cite web url=http://jvclub.libsyn.com/jessica-st-clair title=The JV Club with Janet Varney : Jessica St. Clair website=jvclub.libsyn.com}}</ref> and raised in Westfield, New Jersey Westfield, New Jersey, where she attended Westfield High School (New Jersey) Westfield High School, graduating as part of the class of 1994.<ref>{{cite web last1=Carpien first1=Sara title=Westfield Native Jessica St. Clair Talks About Her Hit Show, Playing House, and Hometown Inspiration url=https://www.tapinto.net/articles/westfield-native-jessica-st-clair-talks-about-he website=TAPinto accessdate=19 June 2017 date=18 June 2014 quote=St. Clair, who graduated Westfield High School in 1994, has fond memories of growing up in Westfield and weaves them into the storyline in unique, and sometimes specific, ways.}}</ref> She is a graduate of Middlebury College. She began her comedy career as a regular improviser and sketch performer at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in New York and Los Angeles, originally as a member of the improv team "Mother". Her first major television work was as a regular panelist on the VH1 series Best Week Ever, offering comedic insights on pop-culture and the news of the week.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bestweekever.tv/tag/Jessica+St.+Clair title=Latest Celebrity Gossip, Celebrity News, Entertainment News - VH1 website=VH1 News}}</ref> She co-starred in the short-lived American Broadcasting Company ABC sitcom In the Motherhood in 2009.

St. Clair has gained notice for her frequent guest appearances (often alongside performing partner Lennon Parham) on podcasts such as Comedy Bang! Bang!, where she plays the shows teenage intern Marissa Wompler. In 2015, St. Clair and Parham spun-off their characters in a new Earwolf podcast called Womp It Up! WOMP It Up!, with St. Clair as host Marissa Wompler and Parham co-hosting as Marissas teacher Miss Listler. Frequent guests on the podcast include Brian Huskey as Marissas stepdad "Seth" and Jason Mantzoukas as Marissas former flame Eric "Gutterballs" Gutterman. The podcast ran through the summer of 2015, returning after a hiatus on November 16, 2015.

St. Clair and frequent collaborator Lennon Parham created and starred in the NBC comedy series Best Friends Forever (TV series) Best Friends Forever, which premiered on April 4, 2012 and aired for one season. St. Clair and Parham created and starred in their second comedy Playing House (TV series) Playing House, which premiered on USA Network USA on April 29, 2014 and concluded its final, third season in July 2017.

St. Clair has had recurring roles on television programs such as Veep (TV series) Veep, Weeds (TV series) Weeds, The McCarthys, Marry Me (U.S. TV series) Marry Me, American Housewife, Worst Week, and United States of Tara. She was also part of the recurring cast on the Comedy Central series Review (TV series) Review, playing the role of Suzanne, the long-suffering wife of host Forrest MacNeil (played by Andy Daly). St. Clairs notable work in film includes supporting roles in Bridesmaids (2011 film) Bridesmaids, Shes Out of My League, Afternoon Delight (film) Afternoon Delight, and Enough Said (film) Enough Said.

==Personal life==
St. Clair is married to playwright and poet Dan OBrien (playwright) Dan OBrien.<ref>{{cite news title=Weddings: Jessica St. Clair, Dan O’Brien url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/07/16/fashion/weddings/16stclair.html accessdate=18 January 2018 work=The New York Times date=16 July 2006}}</ref> Their daughter Isobel Kelly OBrien was born on October 1, 2013.<ref>{{cite news last1=Ossad first1=Jordana title=Jessica St. Clair Welcomes Daughter Isobel Kelly url=http://www.eonline.com/news/469231/jessica-st-clair-welcomes-daughter-isobel-kelly accessdate=18 January 2018 work=E! News date=11 October 2013}}</ref>

In 2015, St. Clair said she and Lennon Parham had both learned Transcendental Meditation technique Transcendental Meditation.<ref>{{cite web last1=Corridan  first1=Kara  title=A Convo with Two Really Cool Moms  url=http://www.parents.com/parents-magazine/parents-perspective/a-convo-with-two-really-cool-moms/  website=Parents  accessdate=18 September 2015  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6bddWQKys?url=http://www.parents.com/parents-magazine/parents-perspective/a-convo-with-two-really-cool-moms/  archivedate=18 September 2015  date=2015  quote=Lennon’s husband worked at a school where they taught transcendental meditation and took time out of the school day to meditate. … So Lennon and I learned meditation ... through the David Lynch Foundation, and we try and meditate at least once a day.  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref> In 2017, St. Clair revealed that she had undergone treatment for breast cancer after being diagnosed in September 2015,<ref>{{cite web title=Actress Jessica St. Clair Opens Up About Her Breast Cancer Diagnosis url=http://www.standup2cancer.org/blog/view/actress_jessica_st_clair_opens_up_about_her_breast_cancer_diagnosis date=May 9, 2017 work=Stand Up To Cancer accessdate=May 14, 2017}}</ref> and this was incorporated into the third season of Playing House, when her character Emma was similarly diagnosed.
