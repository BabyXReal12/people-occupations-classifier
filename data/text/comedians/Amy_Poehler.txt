Amy Poehler {{IPAc-en ˈ p oʊ l ər}} (born September 16, 1971)
is an American actress, comedian, director, producer, and writer. After studying Improvisational theatre improv at Chicagos The Second City Second City and ImprovOlympic in the early 1990s, she became part of the Chicago-based improvisational comedy troupe Upright Citizens Brigade. The group moved to New York City in 1996 where their act became a half-hour sketch comedy series on Comedy Central in 1998.
Along with other members of the comedy group, Poehler is a founder of the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre.
She is best known for starring as Leslie Knope in the National Broadcasting Network NBC sitcom Parks and Recreation, for which she won a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Television Musical or Comedy Series in 2014 and a Critics Choice Television Award Critics Choice Award for Best Actress in a Comedy Series in 2012. Poehler was a cast member on the NBC television series Saturday Night Live from 2001 to 2008 and became co-anchor of SNLs Weekend Update in 2004 alongside friend and colleague Tina Fey. She is also known for voicing Joy from Inside Out (2015 film) Inside Out, Sally OMalley from the Horton Hears a Who! movie adaptation, Bessie Higgenbottom in the 2008–2011 Nickelodeon series, The Mighty B! and Homily Clock from the American-English dub of Arrietty The Secret World of Arrietty.

Poehler served as an executive producer on the Swedish-American sitcom Welcome to Sweden (2014 TV series) Welcome to Sweden, along with her brother Greg Poehler. She is also an executive producer on Broad City which airs on Comedy Central, and appeared in the List of Broad City episodes#Season 1 (2014) season one finale.
Since August 2015, she has served as an executive producer on the Hulu original series Difficult People.
In December 2015, Poehler received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame for her contributions in television.
She and Tina Fey both won the 2016 Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series for Saturday Night Live.

==Early life==
Poehler was born in Newton, Massachusetts, to school teachers Eileen and William Poehler.
Her brother, Greg Poehler, is a producer and actor.
She grew up in nearby Burlington, Massachusetts Burlington.
She was raised as a Catholic Church Catholic,
and graduated from Burlington High School (Burlington, Massachusetts) Burlington High School in 1989.
While attending Boston College,
Poehler was a member of the Improvisational theatre improv comedy troupe My Mothers Fleabag.
She graduated from Boston College with a bachelors degree in media and communications in 1993 and moved to Chicago, where she studied improv at The Second City Second City with friend and future co-star Tina Fey.
She also studied with Del Close at ImprovOlympic.
==Career==

===Upright Citizens Brigade===
During her time at Second City and Improv Olympic in Chicago, Poehler studied alongside Matt Besser under Del Close and Charna Halpern. Poehler and Besser joined with Ian Roberts (American actor) Ian Roberts and Matt Walsh (comedian) Matt Walsh to found the Upright Citizens Brigade (UCB).
The group performed sketch and improv around Chicago before moving to New York City in 1996. After moving to New York, the group scored a TV gig, appearing as sketch regulars on Late Night with Conan OBrien, often playing her recurring role as Andy Richters little sister, Stacy.
