Guy Branum (born November 12, 1975)<ref>Branum identified himself as 33 years old in an August 2009 airing of Comedians of Chelsea Lately</ref> is an American comedian, writer, and actor best known as the head writer of, and a sketch performer on, X-Play on the G4 (U.S. TV channel) G4 network and as a regular panelist on Chelsea Lately on the E! network. He currently hosts the podcast Pop Rocket on the Maximum Fun network, and he is the host of TruTVs Talk Show the Game Show.

==Early life==
Branum was born and raised in Yuba City, California to a Protestant father and Jewish mother.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news url=http://www.laweekly.com/content/printView/8089822 title=How Guy Branum Turned a Dinner Party Challenge Into a New TV Show last=Seabaugh first=Julie date=2017-04-05 work=L.A. Weekly access-date=2017-11-12}}</ref><ref>Interview on Midday on WNYC July 31, 2018 https://www.wnyc.org/story/guy-branum-finds-his-way/</ref> He attended the University of California, Berkeley<ref name=":0" /> from 1994-1998 where he was a history and political science major. He wrote a column for the Daily Californian,<ref name=":0" /> one of which brought the United States Secret Service to his apartment in November 1997 before the Big Game (football) Big Game between Berkeley and Stanford University, in which he made suggestions that Berkeley students murder Stanford freshman Chelsea Clinton.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.bulldognews.net/opinion2.html title=Secret Service Roust - Opinion Section last=Foundation first=The Bulldog Newspaper website=www.bulldognews.net access-date=2017-11-12}}</ref><ref>[http://www.rcfp.org/news/1997/1215j.html Secret Service searches college journalists apartment]</ref><ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/topic/organization/stanford-university title=Stanford University work=The New York Times access-date=2017-11-12 language=en-US issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

He then moved to Minnesota, where he attended the University of Minnesota Law School,<ref name=":0" /> and was on the schools Quiz Bowl team that placed third at College Bowl Invitational CBI nationals in 1999.<ref name= "Minnesota">{{cite news  last=Justin  first=Neal  url= http://www.startribune.com/comedian-guy-branum-gets-serious-about-his-debt-to-minnesota/417712843/  title=Comedian Guy Branum gets serious about his debt to Minnesota  work=Star Tribune  date=March 31, 2017  accessdate=July 26, 2018}}</ref> During his time in law school he publicly came out of the closet.<ref>Interview on Midday on WNYC July 31, 2018 https://www.wnyc.org/story/guy-branum-finds-his-way/</ref> After graduating he returned to California.{{Citation needed date=January 2011}}

== Career ==
After being recommended by co-hostess Laura Swisher, Branum was hired as a writer for Unscrewed with Martin Sargent while it was still on TechTV in San Francisco.<ref>Sparkle & Circulate with Justin Sayre podcast episode Guy and Doll, August 28th, 2015</ref> On Unscrewed, he regularly appeared as a sweater model and as The Ambassador of Gay. He was also a writer and producer on G4tv.com,{{Citation needed date=January 2011}} and head writer on the G4 channel program X-Play. He also contributed to the comedy podcast Weezy and the Swish. In December 2007, Branum became a writer and an onscreen comedy performer on Chelsea Lately.<ref name=":0" /> He made his feature film debut in January 2011 in No Strings Attached (film) No Strings Attached. In 2012 Branum became a writer for the show Totally Biased with W. Kamau Bell, performing a recurring segment "No more Mr. Nice Gay."<ref>http://splitsider.com/2013/09/totally-biaseds-guy-branum-calls-out-the-james-franco-roast-for-being-homophobic/</ref> He appeared on the eighth season of Last Comic Standing.

He is currently the host and executive producer of Talk Show The Game Show on TruTV.

Branum released his first comedy album, Effable, in 2014.
