
Kendrick Kang-Joh Jeong (born July 13, 1969) is an American stand-up comedian, actor and former physician.<ref>Ancestry.com. U.S. Public Records Index, Volume 1 [database on-line]. Provo, UT, US: Ancestry.com Operations, Inc., 2010.</ref><ref name="vul">{{cite web url=http://www.vulture.com/2011/05/the_suprisingly_earnest_ken_je.html title=The Surprisingly Earnest Ken Jeong on the Secret Messages of The Hangover publisher=Nymag work=Vulture.com date=May 23, 2011 accessdate=March 18, 2013 author=Barshad, Amos}}</ref> He is best known for playing Ben Chang on the sitcom Community (TV series) Community and the gangster Leslie Chow in The Hangover (film series) The Hangover film series. He was the lead in the ABC sitcom Dr. Ken, in which he was also the creator, writer, and executive producer. Jeong is a licensed physician, <ref name=MedLicense> {{cite web  url=https://search.dca.ca.gov/details/8002/A/65869/833fe63788fbbfdf85673d2f42f77918  title=Medical Board of California - Licensing details for A 65869  publisher=dca.can.gov}}</ref> but has stopped practicing in favor of his acting career.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.redbookmag.com/fun-contests/celebrity/ken-jeong-interview title=A Side of Community<nowiki></nowiki>s Ken Jeong You Havent Seen first=Nicole last=Yorio work=Redbook}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Jeong was born in Detroit to South Korean immigrants.<ref name="newsref">{{cite news last=Lopez first=Robert title=Greensboro native finds funny bone in movie work=News & Record date=June 19, 2009 url=http://www.news-record.com/content/2009/06/19/article/greensboro_native_finds_funny_bone_in_movie accessdate=2010-03-22}}</ref><ref name="ref2">{{cite news last=Lee first=Chris title=Doctor provides best medicine work=The Columbus Dispatch date=June 10, 2009 url=http://www.dispatch.com/live/content/arts/stories/2009/06/10/2_HANGOVER_FUNNYGUY.ART_ART_06-10-09_D5_OQE4LU0.html accessdate=September 13, 2009}}</ref><ref name="twitterbirth">{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/kenjeong/status/650861736254291969 title=Ken Jeong on Twitter: "@S0fak1ng83 born in Detroit, moved to NC when I was 4. My father got his PhD in Economics from Wayne State." author=Ken Jeong date=October 4, 2015 accessdate=August 31, 2016}}</ref> Jeong was raised in Greensboro, North Carolina, and attended Walter Hines Page High School,<ref>{{cite news last1=Lee first1=Luaine title=Ken Jeong was a doctor, and now he plays one on TV url=http://www.greensboro.com/blogs/gotriad_extra/ken-jeong-was-a-doctor-and-now-he-plays-one/article_791035ac-1d7e-515f-b5af-b4937440e441.html accessdate=15 December 2016 work=Greensboro News & Record date=1 October 2015}}</ref> where he took part in the Quiz Bowl team, played violin in the orchestra, and was elected to student council.{{citation needed date=December 2016}} He graduated at 16 and his achievements earned him Greensboros Youth of the Month award.<ref name="newsref"/> Jeong graduated from Duke University in 1990 and obtained his M.D. at the UNC School of Medicine University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill School of Medicine in 1995.<ref name=MedLicense/>

==Career==

===Acting===
Jeong won the Big Easy Laff-Off in 1995, of which NBC president Brandon Tartikoff and The Improv founder Budd Friedman were judges, and they both urged Jeong to move to Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web title = Hangover actor saw his career start in New Orleans -- at Ochsner url = http://blog.nola.com/mikescott/2009/06/ken_jeong_is_a_real.html website = NOLA.com accessdate = 2015-10-24}}</ref> He began performing regularly at the Improv and Laugh Factory comedy clubs. After moving to Los Angeles, he initially practiced medicine for several years as a physician at a Kaiser Permanente hospital in Woodland Hills, Los Angeles Woodland Hills.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/ken-jeong-how-ditch-medicine-817478 title=Ken Jeong: How to Ditch Medicine for a Career in Comedy and Diagnose Castmates date=August 27, 2015 accessdate=8 September 2015}}</ref>

Jeongs background in theater and improv started while he was still a medical student in North Carolina. He was a regular at open mic nights in the Raleigh-Durham circuit, performing at Charlie Goodnights in Raleigh and Tootie’s Durham Comedy Club. He also emceed The Comedy Spot programming series on Duke University campus 1992 and 1993 (produced by his friend by coincidentially same-first named Korean-American, Kendrick Jahng) opening for Margaret Cho, Jeff Dunham and others. His stand up work has led to several television appearances, including NBCs The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office, HBOs Entourage (U.S. TV series) Entourage and Curb Your Enthusiasm. 

He made his film debut in Judd Apatows Knocked Up as Dr. Kuni, which proved to be his breakout performance. From that point forward he was able to transition from medicine into a full-time career in the entertainment industry. Subsequently, he has appeared in Pineapple Express (film) Pineapple Express; Role Models (film) Role Models as King Argotron; All About Steve (film) All About Steve; The Goods: Live Hard, Sell Hard; Couples Retreat; and as The Hangover (film)#Cast Mr. Chow in The Hangover, The Hangover Part II, and The Hangover Part III. He co-starred in Zookeeper (film) Zookeeper and Transformers: Dark of the Moon, released in summer 2011.

Jeong played Señor Chang Señor Ben Chang on the NBC sitcom Community (TV series) Community and was nominated for the shows "Male Breakout Star" at the 2010 Teen Choice Awards. He was nominated for two 2010 MTV Movie Awards, winning the award for Best WTF Moment and for MTV Movie Award for Best Villain for The Hangover. In fall 2010, Adidas basketball began a wide-ranging marketing campaign in which he starred as "Slim Chin", alongside NBA stars Dwight Howard and Derrick Rose. On May 22, 2011, he hosted the 2011 Billboard Music Awards in Las Vegas on ABC<ref>{{cite web url=http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?fareast_movement_will_perform_at_ken_jeong-hosted_billboard_music_awards_16721.aspx title=Far*East Movement will perform at Ken Jeong-hosted Billboard Music Awards publisher=Asia Pacific Arts date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> and was featured in a Hands Only CPR PSA campaign from the American Heart Association on June 15, 2011.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n5hP4DIBCEE title=Ken Jeong AHA Hands-Only CPR video date=June 15, 2011 accessdate=July 18, 2013}}</ref>

Jeong won a Streamy Award for "Best Guest Appearance" for Burning Love (Web series) Burning Love.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.streamys.org/blog/2012/12/nominations-are-announced-for-3rd-annual-streamy-awards title= Nominations Are Announced For 3rd Annual Streamy Awards work=The Streamy Awards date=December 17, 2012 accessdate=July 18, 2013}}</ref>

In 2013, he appeared in Michael Bays Pain & Gain (2013), as Johnny Wu, a motivational speaker,<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/ken-jeong-hangover-michael-bay-pain-gain-308842 work=The Hollywood Reporter first=Borys last=Kit title=Community Star Ken Jeong Joins Michael Bays Pain and Gain (Exclusive) date=April 5, 2012 accessdate=July 18, 2013}}</ref> and returned, in a significantly expanded role, as The Hangover (film)#Cast Mr. Chow in The Hangover Part III.<ref name="Jeong">{{cite news last1=Siegel first1=Tatiana last2=Belloni first2=Matthew date=July 12, 2012 title=Ken Jeong Signs on for Expanded Role in Hangover: Part III (Exclusive) url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/ken-jeong-hangover-3-expanded-role-348694 work=The Hollywood Reporter accessdate=September 7, 2012}}</ref>

In an unusual, if informal, partnership, Jamie Foxx and Jeong have each agreed to star in movies written and produced by the other. Foxx has agreed to take a lead role in a new movie Jeong will produce called After Prom. Jeong will star in All-Star Weekend, a comedy Foxx is developing at his production company about two friends who find themselves fans of opposing NBA stars.<ref>{{cite web url=http://articles.latimes.com/2012/oct/10/entertainment/la-et-mn-jamie-foxx-and-ken-jeong-agree-to-trade-movies-20121010 title=Jamie Foxx and Ken Jeong agree to trade movies work=Los Angeles Times date=2012-10-10 accessdate=July 18, 2013}}</ref>

In 2015, Jeong starred, wrote, and executive produced the pilot of his new American Broadcasting Company ABC medical comedy, Dr. Ken, as a frustrated HMO doctor juggling his career, marriage and parenting, but succeeding at none of them. On May 7, 2015, the series was ordered by ABC to debut in the 2015–16 United States network television schedule 15–16 TV season starring Jeong, Suzy Nakamura, Dave Foley, Tisha Campbell-Martin and Jonathan Slavin.<ref>{{cite web url=http://iamkoream.com/abc-orders-ken-jeongs-comedy-dr-ken-to-series/ title=ABC Orders Ken Jeong’s Comedy ‘Dr. Ken’ to Series date=May 8, 2015 accessdate=September 8, 2015 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150930121603/http://iamkoream.com/abc-orders-ken-jeongs-comedy-dr-ken-to-series/ archivedate=September 30, 2015 df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/medical-comedy-starring-communitys-ken-770179 title=Medical Comedy Starring Communitys Ken Jeong Scores ABC Pilot Order work=The Hollywood Reporter date=2015-02-10 accessdate=September 8, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://abc.go.com/shows/dr-ken/news/updates/dr-ken-series-premiere-20150610  title=Dr. Ken Series Premiere Date Announced  work=ABC Go  date=2015-06-10  accessdate=September 8, 2015  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150904013942/http://abc.go.com/shows/dr-ken/news/updates/dr-ken-series-premiere-20150610  archivedate=September 4, 2015  df= }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Jeongs wife, Tran Ho, is Vietnamese American, a family physician and a breast cancer survivor.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/ken-jeong/mothers-day-for-my-wife_b_858775.html title=For My Wife, in Celebration of Mothers Day first=Ken last=Jeong date=May 7, 2011 publisher=Huffington Post}}</ref><ref name="usatoday">{{cite news last=Deerwester first=Jayme title=Ken Jeong transitions from doctor to in-demand jokester work=USA Today date=September 3, 2009 url=http://iamkoream.com/ken-jeong-is-good-for-us/#comment-4016 accessdate=18 July 2013 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130806090658/http://iamkoream.com/ken-jeong-is-good-for-us/#comment-4016 archivedate=6 August 2013 df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web author=Margarita title=Dr. Ken Jeong Thanks Wife Dr. Tran Ho During MTV Movie Awards url=http://spotlight.vitals.com/2010/06/dr-ken-jeong-wife-dr-tran-ho-mtv-movie-awards work=Vitals date=June 7, 2010 accessdate=July 19, 2013}}</ref>

Jeong became engaged to Tran Ho around 2003 or 2004.<ref name="BET">{{youtube id=1oGTgyhPuUw title=Standup Dr. Ken Old BET Comic}}</ref> They have twin daughters (born 2007).<ref>{{cite news  publisher=The Huffington Post  title=For My Wife, in Celebration of Mother’s Day  url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/ken-jeong/mothers-day-for-my-wife_b_858775.html  date=5 July 2011  accessdate=25 September 2016}}</ref>
