Sarah Burns (born July 26, 1981) is an United States American film and television actress and comedian, best known for playing Krista on HBOs Enlightened (TV series) Enlightened.

==Life and career==
Born on Long Island, New York (state) New York,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.funnyordie.com/sarahburns title=Sarah Burns work=Funny or Die accessdate=May 14, 2015}}</ref> Burns is known for her appearances in comedic films and television series. Her film roles include Anne in Slow Learners (2015 film) Slow Learners (2015) opposite Adam Pally, Hailey in I Love You, Man (2009), Harper in Going the Distance (2010 film) Going the Distance (2010) and Janine Groff in Life as We Know It (film) Life as We Know It (2010).<ref name="imdb"/>

She has also appeared in television productions including Damage Control (TV series) Damage Control (one episode; 2005), Flight of the Conchords (TV series) Flight of the Conchords (one episode; 2007), Party Down (one episode; 2010) and Ben and Kate (one episode; 2012). She had a costarring role in the short film Cried Suicide (2010), which was screened at the 2010 Tribeca Film Festival. Her short film "The First Step" (2012) premiered at the 2013 Seattle International Film Festival.<ref name="imdb"/>

Appears as part of a recurring cast of sketch players for the Comedy Central television series Drunk History, which premiered in July 2013. In 2015, Burns was a series regular in second season of the FX comedy series Married (TV series) Married. Burns was cast as Assistant District Attorney Emily Sinclair in Season Two of the show How to Get Away with Murder How to Get Away With Murder.<ref name="imdb">{{IMDb name 1901220 Sarah Burns}}</ref>

Burns has performed Improvisational theatre improvisational comedy regularly at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in New York City, New York.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.sheknows.com/articles/810504/sarah-burns-exclusive-interview title=Sarah Burns exclusive interview author=Joel D. Amos work=SheKnows accessdate=May 14, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20300978_20694288,00.html title=Emmys website=Ew.com accessdate=May 14, 2015}}</ref>

In 2009, Entertainment Weekly named her one of the "25 Funniest Actresses in Hollywood".<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20271251_21,00.html title=Entertainment Weekly website=Ew.com accessdate=May 14, 2015}}</ref>

In June 2013 Entertainment Weekly named Burns one of "15 Actresses Were Rooting For" as part of its "Emmy Watch".<ref>{{cite web url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2013/06/27/emmy-watch-sarah-burns-enlightened title=Emmy Watch: Sarah Burns discusses that extremely awkward hospital visit in Enlightened website=Insidetv.ew.com accessdate=May 14, 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, Burns appeared as Margie Turley in the Lorne Michaels-produced Brother Nature (film) Brother Nature. In 2017, Burns appeared as Gabrielle in the HBO original miniseries Big Little Lies (TV series) Big Little Lies and in the Netflix series Wet Hot American Summer: Ten Years Later.
