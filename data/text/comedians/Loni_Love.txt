
Loni Love (born July 12, 1971) is an American comedian, actress and author. While working as an electrical engineer in 2003, Love began to pursue a career in music engineering. She was the runner-up on Star Search 2003 and was named among the "Top 10 Comics to Watch" in both Variety (magazine) Variety and Comedy Central in 2009. She is currently one of the hosts of The Real (TV series) The Real talk show along with Jeannie Mai, Tamera Mowry, and Adrienne Bailon, which premiered on July 15, 2013.<ref>{{cite web title=Its Official: ‘The Real’ Talk Show Begins Four-Week Test Run July 15 url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/06/real-talk-show-july-15-premiere-test-run-fox/ work=Deadline Hollywood accessdate=28 July 2013 date=June 12, 2013}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Love was born in Detroit, Michigan, and grew up in the Brewster-Douglass Housing Projects.<ref name=bio>{{cite web url=http://www.lonilove.com/about title=About publisher= accessdate=13 March 2017}}</ref> Prior to her career as a comedian, she was an electrical engineer,<ref>{{citation title=Lonie Love: Laughter For Life url=http://www.singersroom.com/lifestyle/feature-story-157.asp first=Njai last=Joszor date=27 August 2009 accessdate=2009-11-13 publisher=Singersroom.com deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090831090838/http://singersroom.com/lifestyle/feature-story-157.asp archivedate=31 August 2009 df=}}</ref> an experience she talks about in many of her acts.<ref name=detroitweekly>{{citation title=Loni Love Foxy (And Funny) Problem Solver url=http://www.realdetroitweekly.com/content/article_5851.shtml first=Emma last=Richardson date=3 November 2009 accessdate=2009-11-13 publisher=Real Detroit Weekly}}{{dead link date=May 2018  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> After graduating from high school in 1989, she had worked for a time on the General Motors assembly line putting doors on 1993 Oldsmobile Cutlasses, work which ignited her interest in electrical engineering. Love then received her bachelors degree in electrical engineering from Prairie View A&M University in Texas. While at Prairie View, she minored in music and was also a member of the Eta Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta.<ref>{{cite web last=Carnes  first=Jim  url=http://www.sacbee.com/2010/07/30/2921692/loni-love-will-say-whats-on-her.html  title=Loni Love will say whats on her mind at Punch Line&nbsp;— Sacramento Entertainment&nbsp;— Sacramento Movie Theaters, Music &#124; Sacramento Bee  publisher=Sacbee.com  date=  accessdate=2010-08-31}} {{Dead link date=October 2010 bot=H3llBot}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/LoniLove/status/14409286401  title=Twitter / Loni Love: Eta Beta Chapter for life  publisher=Twitter.com  date=  accessdate=2010-08-31}}</ref> It was there that she discovered stand-up comedy after winning a $50 competition and then performed frequently during her college life. After finding work as an engineer at Xerox in California, she continued to do stand-up after work in clubs and became a regular at the Laugh Factory.<ref name=bio/><ref name=tower>{{cite web url=http://www.thetowerlight.com/2010/09/riding-the-love-train/  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2010-10-01  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110131075010/http://www.thetowerlight.com/2010/09/riding-the-love-train/  archivedate=2011-01-31  df= }}</ref> After eight years of working at Xerox, Love resigned to pursue comedy during a layoff to prevent someone else from losing their job.<ref name=tower/> Recently Love did a show on VH1 called I Love the 2000s which she gives her view on 2000-2009s pop culture highlights and became a Roundtable panelist for her friend and fellow comic Chelsea Handler.

Love started her comedic career in 2004, after appearing on Star Search, reaching the finals and losing in a close competition to the winner.<ref>{{citation url=http://www.cbs.com/primetime/star_search/comedian/020603.shtml title=Loni Love & John Roy accessdate=2009-11-16 publisher=CBS CBS Broadcasting Inc.}}</ref> Since then, she has appeared in films and numerous television shows.<ref name=resume/> She has also acted in dramatic theatre plays.<ref name=resume/> Love was named "Hot Comic" for 2009 in Campus Activity magazine and one of the "Top 10 Comics to Watch" in both Variety and Comedy Central. She was awarded the Jury Prize for best stand-up at the 2003 US Comedy Arts Festival.<ref name=bio/> In 2008 Love became the CNN correspondent for D. L. Hughley Breaks the News and covered the inauguration of President Barack Obama. She also appears regularly in comedy clubs and the college comedy circuit.<ref name=resume/> In late 2009, Love recorded her first one-hour Comedy Central special, Americas Sister, which aired on May 8, 2010.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.the-trades.com/article.php?id=11822  title=Interview: Loni Love: American Sister  publisher=The Trades  date=2010-04-20  accessdate=2010-08-31}}</ref> In 2013, Loni released her first comedy advice book titled "Love Him Or Leave Him But Dont Get Stuck With The Tab".  It was published by Simon and Schuster. In 2015, she appeared in the comedy film Paul Blart: Mall Cop 2, with Kevin James, and the action film Bad Asses on the Bayou, with Danny Trejo and Danny Glover.<ref>{{cite web url=http://blackamericaweb.com/2014/04/09/loni-love-lands-a-new-role-in-paul-blart-mall-cop-2%e2%80%b2/ title=Loni Love Lands A New Role In ‘Paul Blart: Mall Cop 2′! date=9 April 2014 publisher=}}</ref> The same year, Love was reported as developing a remake of the dating game show Love Connection, with herself as the host.<ref>{{citation title=Get Ready For A New Love Connection date=2015-01-22 url=http://www.tvnewscheck.com/article/82444/get-ready-for-a-new-love-connection accessdate=2015-01-22}}</ref> However, it fell through at the last minute.
