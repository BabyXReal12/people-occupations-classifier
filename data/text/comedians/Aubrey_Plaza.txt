
Aubrey Christina Plaza<ref name=bullett>{{cite web last=Plaza first=Aubrey url=http://www.bullettmedia.com/article/aubrey-plaza-finally-confronts-her-multiple-personalities/ title=Aubrey Plaza Finally Confronts Her Multiple Personalities date=March 1, 2012 work=Bullett Magazine }}</ref> (born June 26, 1984)<ref>{{cite news url=http://articles.latimes.com/2009/aug/03/entertainment/et-plaza3 title=Aubrey Plaza: Funny person last=Olsen first=Mark newspaper=Los Angeles Times date=August 3, 2009}} – interview lists her as 25</ref> is an American comedian and actress. She is known for her starring role as April Ludgate on the NBC sitcom Parks and Recreation (2009–2015). After appearing in supporting roles in several feature films, Plaza had her first leading role as Darius Britt in the 2012 comedy film Safety Not Guaranteed. Since 2017, she has starred as Lenny Busker in the FX (TV channel) FX drama series Legion (TV series) Legion.

Plaza began her career performing Improvisational theatre improv and sketch comedy at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater. She later appeared in films such as Mystery Team (2008), Funny People (2009), Scott Pilgrim vs. the World (2010), Monsters University (2013), Life After Beth (2014), Grumpy Cats Worst Christmas Ever (2014), Dirty Grandpa (2016), Mike and Dave Need Wedding Dates (2016), The Little Hours (2017), and Ingrid Goes West (2017), the latter two of which she also produced.

==Early life and education==
Plaza was born in Wilmington, Delaware, to Bernadette, an attorney, and David Plaza, a financial advisor.<ref>{{cite web url=http://blogs.delawareonline.com/pulpculture/2010/01/15/from-wilmy-to-hollywood/ title=From Wilmy to Hollywood last=Cormier first=Ryan date=January 15, 2010 work=Delaware Online publisher=The News Journal}}</ref> She has two younger sisters, Renee and Natalie.<ref name="delawaretoday">{{cite web url=http://www.delawaretoday.com/Delaware-Today/March-2012/Aubrey-Plaza-of-NBCs-Parks-and-Recreation-Wilmington-Native-Is-Building-a-Buzz-in-Hollywood/ title=Aubrey Plaza of NBC’s Parks and Recreation: Wilmington Native Is Building a Buzz in Hollywood author=John Micklos Jr. date=February 16, 2012 publisher=Delaware Today}}</ref>

Plaza was named after the song "Aubrey (song) Aubrey" by Bread (band) Bread.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.delawaretoday.com/Delaware-Today/March-2012/Aubrey-Plaza-of-NBCs-Parks-and-Recreation-Wilmington-Native-Is-Building-a-Buzz-in-Hollywood/ title=Aubrey Plaza of NBC’s Parks and Recreation: Wilmington Native Is Building a Buzz in Hollywood access-date=2018-07-27 language=en}}</ref> Her father is Puerto Ricans Puerto Rican and her mother is of Irish people Irish–English people English descent.<ref>{{cite web author=Lee Hernández url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/tv-movies/girl-slouch-article-1.376882 title=This girls no slouch work=New York Daily News  date=June 11, 2009  accessdate=January 2, 2011}}</ref> Plaza has stated: "I was like the only diverse kid in my high school, and Im half-Puerto Rican. But yeah, I have a huge family and tons of cousins in Puerto Rico."<ref name="latina">{{cite web url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/tv/parks-recreation-star-aubrey-plaza-explains-why-shes-so-hot-right-now title=Parks & Recreation star Aubrey Plaza explains why shes so hot right now last=Herrera first=Monica work=Latina (magazine) Latina date=April 9, 2009}}</ref> 

Plaza graduated from Ursuline Academy (Delaware) Ursuline Academy, an all-girls Catholic school, in 2002, and New York Universitys Tisch School of the Arts in 2006.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.delawaretoday.com/Delaware-Today/March-2012/Aubrey-Plaza-of-NBCs-Parks-and-Recreation-Wilmington-Native-Is-Building-a-Buzz-in-Hollywood/ title=Aubrey Plaza of NBC’s Parks and Recreation: Wilmington Native Is Building a Buzz in Hollywood access-date=2018-07-27 language=en}}</ref> In high school she was student council president and participated in productions with the Wilmington Drama League.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.delawaretoday.com/Delaware-Today/March-2012/Aubrey-Plaza-of-NBCs-Parks-and-Recreation-Wilmington-Native-Is-Building-a-Buzz-in-Hollywood/ title=Aubrey Plaza of NBC’s Parks and Recreation: Wilmington Native Is Building a Buzz in Hollywood access-date=2018-07-27 language=en}}</ref><ref name="GQ">{{cite web url=https://www.gq.com/story/aubrey-plaza-gq-interview-august-2013 title=The GQ Interview: Aubrey Plaza author=Mark Byrne,Todd Cole work=GQ accessdate=November 9, 2015}}</ref> During her second year of college in 2004, Plaza suffered a stroke that caused temporary paralysis and expressive aphasia. Since then she has fully recovered.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.delawaretoday.com/Delaware-Today/March-2012/Aubrey-Plaza-of-NBCs-Parks-and-Recreation-Wilmington-Native-Is-Building-a-Buzz-in-Hollywood/ title=Aubrey Plaza of NBC’s Parks and Recreation: Wilmington Native Is Building a Buzz in Hollywood access-date=2018-07-27 language=en}}</ref><ref>{{citation title=interview – "Late Show with David Letterman" date=August 31, 2011  accessdate=September 1, 2011 last=Letterman first=David}}</ref>

==Career==
Plaza has had many internships, and worked as an NBC page.<ref>{{cite web title=Aubrey Plaza on the Job That Really Didnt Work Out url=https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/a13969484/aubrey-plaza-worn-emily-spivack-excerpt/ publisher=Elle accessdate=November 30, 2017}}</ref> 

Plaza has performed Improvisational theatre improv and sketch comedy at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater since 2004. She also performed stand-up and has appeared at Laugh Factory the Laugh Factory and The Improv. 

Plaza starred in the online series The Jeannie Tate Show, and starred as Robin Gibney in ESPNs Mayne Street. She appeared in the first episode of "Terrible Decisions with Ben Schwartz" on Funny or Die.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.funnyordie.com/videos/76b1d33fb0/terrible-decisions-with-ben-schwartz title=Terrible Decisions with Ben Schwartz from Eric Appel, Ben Schwartz, and Aubrey Plaza  publisher=Funnyordie.com date=September 27, 2009 accessdate=January 2, 2011 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110101071219/http://www.funnyordie.com/videos/76b1d33fb0/terrible-decisions-with-ben-schwartz archivedate=January 1, 2011 deadurl=no}}</ref>

She played Seth Rogens love interest, Daisy, in 2009s Funny People directed by Judd Apatow. She appeared in Scott Pilgrim vs. the World, and Derrick Comedy Derrick Comedys Mystery Team, which debuted at 2009 Sundance Film Festival Sundance. She appeared in a CollegeHumor short alongside Jason Bateman and Will Arnett.

She played April Ludgate in Parks and Recreation from 2009 to 2015. For her performance as the deadpan employee, she received universal praise throughout the entire show, being referred to as one of the shows breakout characters. 

File:Aubrey Plaza by Gage Skidmore.jpg thumb Plaza in 2010
On March 12, 2010, Plaza performed at "A Night of 140 Tweets: A Celebrity Tweetathon for Haiti", produced by Rob Huebel, Paul Scheer, Ben Stiller, and Mike Rosenstein, at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in Los Angeles. 

In 2011 she appeared on Portlandia (TV series) Portlandia. Plaza appeared as a guest judge during a roast segment on The Next Food Network Star in 2011. She appeared in Episode 199 of the WTF with Marc Maron podcast. She also had a recurring role as "the Princess" in the comedic sci-fi web series Troopers on CollegeHumor.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.collegehumor.com/troopers title=Troopers Videos on Collegehumor work=CollegeHumor access-date=2018-07-08 language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2012, she was featured in Father John Mistys music video for the song "Hollywood Forever Cemetery Sings" from the album Fear Fun and in 2014 she was in Cassorlas "Bona Fide" video where she also made her saxophone-playing debut.<ref name="tbs">[http://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/la-et-ms-watch-aubrey-plaza-sax-solos-cassorla-bona-fide-video-20140107-story.html "Watch Aubrey Plaza rip sax solos in Cassorlas Bona Fide video"]. August Brown, The Baltimore Sun, January 7th, 2014. Retrieved February 27th, 2018.</ref>

In 2012 Plaza earned her first starring role in a major film, alongside Mark Duplass, in the comedy, Safety Not Guaranteed. Plaza played Darius, a jaded intern who answers a curious want ad "seeking a companion for a time traveling adventure". Her performance in the film was critically acclaimed, and Gary Thompson of The Philadelphia Inquirer questioned whether this role was out of her usual "pretty slacker" range but found her performance "compelling".<ref>{{cite web last=Thompson first=Gary title=Aubrey Plaza shines in ‘Safety Not Guaranteed url=http://www.philly.com/philly/entertainment/20120615_Aubrey_Plaza_shines_in__lsquo_Safety_Not_Guaranteed_.html work=The Philadelphia Inquirer date=June 15, 2012 accessdate=July 6, 2012 quote=Its too early to say if Plazas an actress, since no ones asked her to do anything other than be the pretty slacker with the bored posture and world-weary intonation. But she has an unusual kind of beauty (Anglo-Irish, Latina) that the camera loves to explore, and when she does make the hyperspace jump to sincerity and initiative, the results are compelling.}}</ref>

At the 2014 Sundance Film Festival in January 2014, Plazas film, Life After Beth, which was written and directed by her boyfriend Jeff Baena, premiered.<ref>{{cite web url=http://inthemixxshow.com/4143/youll-never-guess-aubrey-plazas-kinky-on-set-ritual title=Youll Never Guess Aubrey Plazas Kinky On-Set Ritual! publisher=In the Mixx date=October 17, 2013 accessdate=June 26, 2014}}</ref>

On September 17, 2014, Lifetime Network announced that Plaza would do the speaking voice of Grumpy Cat in their upcoming original movie Grumpy Cats Worst Christmas Ever.<ref>{{cite web url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2014/09/17/aubrey-plaza-grumpy-cat-lifetime title=Aubrey Plaza will be the voice of Grumpy Cat in Lifetime movie last=Blake first=Emily date=September 17, 2014 work=Entertainment Weekly accessdate=September 18, 2014}}</ref> 

She starred in Mike and Dave Need Wedding Dates alongside Zac Efron, Anna Kendrick and Adam DeVine. The film was released on July 8, 2016. Her role as the rebellious Tatiana earned her critical praise.

She was a guest star on Harmonquest HarmonQuest in 2016, as a gnome named "Hawaiian Coffee".

In 2016, she portrayed Aaron Burr in the “Hamilton” episode of Drunk History.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.vulture.com/2016/08/see-drunk-historys-new-hamilton-and-burr.html title=Alia Shawkat and Aubrey Plaza Are Drunk History’s New Hamilton and Burr work=Vulture access-date=2018-07-27 language=en}}</ref>

In 2017, it was announced that Plaza would star in indie comedy An Evening with Beverly Luff Linn An Evening With Beverly Luff Linn.<ref>{{cite web last1=McNary first1=Dave title=Aubrey Plaza, Emile Hirsch to Star in Indie Comedy ‘An Evening With Beverly Luff Linn’ url=https://variety.com/2017/film/festivals/aubrey-plaza-emile-hirsch-evening-with-beverly-luff-linn-1201981457/ website=Variety accessdate=February 8, 2017}}</ref>

She first played the role of Cat Adams in Season 11 of the CBS television show Criminal Minds. She returned to the role in Season 12.

Plaza has been starring in the FX (TV channel) FX series Legion (TV series) Legion since 2017.

==Personal life==
Plaza has been dating writer and director Jeff Baena since 2011. They live together in Los Angeles, California and work together often.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://nypost.com/2017/02/08/aubrey-plaza-like-youve-never-seen-her-before/ title=Aubrey Plaza like you’ve never seen her before date=2017-02-08 work=New York Post access-date=2018-07-27 language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2017/07/03/jeff-baena-and-aubrey-plazas-medieval-adventure title=Jeff Baena and Aubrey Plaza’s Medieval Adventure work=The New Yorker access-date=2018-07-08 language=en-US}}</ref>
