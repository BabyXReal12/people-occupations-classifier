Hal Dominic Bart Cruttenden (born 9 September 1969) is an English stand-up comedian, writer and actor<ref>{{cite news first=Nosheen  last=Iqbal  url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2009/apr/28/comedian-hal-cruttenden  title=Comedian profile: Hal Cruttenden  newspaper=The Guardian  date=29 April 2009  accessdate=10 April 2015}}</ref> who has appeared on Mock the Week, Live at the Referendum, Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live At The Apollo, Have I Got News for You, The 11 OClock Show, Richard Herrings interview podcasts Richard Herrings Leicester Square Podcast, and The Great British Bake Off: An Extra Slice. 

He has also written for The Omid Djalili Show and The Rob Brydon Show. He was nominated for Best Newcomer at the 2002 Edinburgh Comedy Awards.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/comics/h/455/hal_cruttenden  title=Hal Cruttenden, comedian  publisher=Chortle : The UK Comedy Guide  date=  accessdate=10 April 2015}}</ref>

Cruttenden grew up in Ealing, west London<ref>{{cite news url=http://metro.co.uk/2008/07/22/five-questions-for-hal-cruttenden-305083/  title=Five questions for ... Having studied acting, he had bit parts on EastEnders and Kavanagh QC in the 1990s, and voiced BBC traffic reports.
