Kyle Christian Kinane (born December 23, 1976) is an United States American stand-up comedian, actor and voice actor from Addison, Illinois.<ref>{{cite news last=Collins first=Sarah date=February 25, 2011 title=Kyle Kinane: The Chicago ex-pat comedian on his stint in a punk band, not being a misanthrope, and growing up in Addison url=http://www.avclub.com/article/kyle-kinane-51805 newspaper=The A.V. Club location=Chicago, Illinois access-date=March 11, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
Kinanes first television appearance was on The Nanny playing "Young Roger Clinton, Jr. Roger Clinton".{{Citation needed date=March 2015}}

His debut album Death of the Party, recorded at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in Los Angeles and released in 2010 on AST Records, was met with critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web url=http://comedians.about.com/b/2010/02/05/kyle-kinane-death-of-the-party-album-review.htm  title=Kyle Kinane: Death of the Party - Album Review  publisher=Comedians.about.com  date=2011-05-11  accessdate=2011-08-11}}</ref> Kinane was named in Variety (magazine) Variety{{}}s "Ten Comics to Watch in 2010".<ref>{{cite web last=Heisler  first=Steve  url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118021687.html?categoryid=15&cs=1&ref=vertlegit  title=10 Comics to Watch: Kyle Kinane - Entertainment News, Legit News, Media  publisher=Variety  date=2010-07-13  accessdate=2011-08-11}}</ref>

Kinane has had a longstanding relationship with the cable channel Comedy Central, including serving as the voice for their on-air announcements since 2011.<ref name="Comedy Central Insider">{{cite web last=Gonzalo first=Cordova title=Comedy Central Insider - Kyle Kinane Is the New Voice of Comedy Central url=http://ccinsider.comedycentral.com/2011/11/14/kyle-kinane-is-the-new-voice-of-comedy-central work=Comedy Central Insider publisher=Comedy Central Insider accessdate=November 15, 2012}}</ref> His first Comedy Central Presents half-hour special was on February 25, 2011. Other Comedy Central programs on which he has appeared include Drunk History (on which he retold the story of the Haymarket affair while drunk), the game show @midnight, which he has won seven times,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.cc.com/shows/-midnight/guests  title=At Midnight &#124; Guest Interviews & Bios &#124; Comedy Central  website=Cc.com  date=2016-09-30  accessdate=2017-01-20}}</ref> and the 2014 animated series TripTank.<ref name="Multichannel News">{{cite web last=Rubino first=Lindsay title=Multichannel News - Comedy Central Greenlights Series on Animated Shorts url=http://www.multichannel.com/content/comedy-central-greenlights-series-animated-shorts/139885 work=Multichannel News publisher=Multichannel News accessdate=October 31, 2012}}</ref>

Kinanes first stand-up comedy DVD special, Whiskey Icarus, was released in 2012.  His second stand-up DVD special, "I Liked His Old Stuff Better," was released in 2015.<ref>{{cite web url=http://kylekinane.comedydirect.com/  title=Kyle Kinane  website=Kylekinane.comedydirect.com  date=  accessdate=2017-01-20}}</ref>

Kinane has performed his stand-up on television programs Last Call with Carson Daly, Live at Gotham, Conan (talk show) Conan and The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon. He has opened for comedians Patton Oswalt and Daniel Tosh on tour.

Kinane also runs his own Podcast along with fellow comedian and outdoor cat, Dave Stone, entitled "The Boogie Monster"

Kinane plays the coke-head ex-boyfriend of Gillian Jacobs in Judd Apatows Netflix series Love (TV series) Love. 

He is the voice of Ice Cream in the short-lived Disney XD series Right Now Kapow.

He plays Rod on the TruTV series, Those Who Cant.

Kinane is an avid needle pointer and crotchet knitter. As a teenager, he was active in 4H and would often enter his creations in county fairs as mentioned in Boogie Monster episode "Dogs & Cats" 2/27/2018.<ref>http://boogiemonster.libsyn.com/</ref>
