Nicholas Roger Swardson<ref>[http://thephoenix.com/mob/Boston/Concerts/Artist/Nick+Swardson/ Nick Swardsons bio on The Boston Phoenix website.] Retrieved February 29, 2012.</ref> (born October 9, 1976)<ref name=STARTRIB>{{cite news  title=Nick Swardson gets intense  first=Neal  last=Justin  url=http://www.startribune.com/entertainment/tv/104535299.html  newspaper=Star Tribune  date=October 9, 2010  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref><ref name=NYTBIO>[https://movies.nytimes.com/person/359411/Nick-Swardson/biography Nick Swardsons biography] via the New York Times</ref>  is an American actor, Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian, screenwriter and producer. He is best known for his recurring role as Terry Bernadino in the comedy series Reno 911!, for his work with Adam Sandlers Happy Madison Happy Madison Productions, and for his own personal sketch comedy series Nick Swardsons Pretend Time.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=NYT1>{{cite news  title=Comedy Central Plans New Sketch Series  first=Dave  last=Itzkoff  url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/31/arts/television/31arts-COMEDYCENTRA_BRF.html  newspaper=New York Times  date=October 30, 2009 accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref><ref name=HOLLYWOOD>{{cite news  title=Pena, Swardson clocking in 30 Minutes  first=Zorianna  last=Kit  url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/pena-swardson-clocking-30-minutes-23558  newspaper=Hollywood Reporter  date=October 14, 2010  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref>

==Early life==
A native of the Minneapolis–Saint Paul area, Swardson was born to Pamela and Roger Eric Swardson, and is the youngest of three siblings — he has a sister, Rachel, and a brother, John.<ref name=CITY2>{{cite news  title=Roger, Over and Out  first=Jennifer  last=Vogel  url=http://www.citypages.com/2003-12-10/news/roger-over-and-out/  newspaper=City Pages  date=December 10, 2003  accessdate=February 6, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140508031034/http://www.citypages.com/2003-12-10/news/roger-over-and-out/  archivedate=May 8, 2014  df= }}</ref><ref name=STARTRIB2>{{cite news  title=Swardson partial to green, gold - and blue  author=C.J  url=http://www.startribune.com/lifestyle/110010379.html?page=1&c=y  newspaper=Star Tribune  date=November 22, 2010  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref><ref name=MPLS>{{cite news  title=Adams boy: Saint Paul standup Nick Swardson takes a seat at Adam Sandlers Hollywood table  first=Steve  last=Marsh  newspaper=MPLS-St. Paul Magazine  date=November 1, 2005}}</ref>  Roger Swardson (1934–2003) was an editor and journalist — having written for publications such as the Cincinnati Enquirer and City Pages, as well as founding the Villager (Saint Paul, Minnesota) Grand Gazette, a former Saint Paul community newspaper.<ref name=CITY2/> Roger Swardson also invested in land development in Saint Paul, revitalizing Grand Avenue — an area now known as Victoria Crossing.<ref name=CITY2/> Roger and Pamela divorced in 1989.<ref name=CITY2/>

Swardson attended Central High School (Saint Paul, Minnesota) St. Paul Central High<ref name=CITY>{{cite news  title=Nick Swardson: Hometown boy headed for comedy big-time  first=Bryan  last=Miller  url=http://www.citypages.com/2010-09-22/news/nick-swardson-hometown-boy-headed-for-comedy-big-time/  newspaper=City Pages  date=September 22, 2010  accessdate=February 6, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121013032246/http://www.citypages.com/2010-09-22/news/nick-swardson-hometown-boy-headed-for-comedy-big-time/  archivedate=October 13, 2012  df= }}</ref><ref name=MPLS/> and started acting and performing Improvisational theatre improv comedy at the age of 16.<ref name=NYT3>{{cite news  title=The Unfunny Business Of Making the Right People (TV Producers) Laugh  first=Neil  last=Strauss  url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/03/04/arts/the-unfunny-business-of-making-the-right-people-tv-producers-laugh.html  newspaper=New York Times  date=March 4, 1997 accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref><ref name=STARPULSE2>{{cite web  url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2007/10/23/nick_swardson_sits_down_with_starpulse_t  title=Starpulse Q&A: Comedian Nick Swardson Talks About New Album, Life & Movie Projects In The Works  last=Kharakh  first=Ben  date=October 23, 2007  work=starpulse.com  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref>  A mischievous student who struggled with alcohol and drugs, Swardson was expelled from school on four occasions for pulling fire alarms in order to go outside and smoke cigarettes, fighting, posting a lewd sign in class, and for smoking marijuana;<ref name=CITY/> he was enrolled in a Drug rehabilitation rehab program while still in school.<ref name=CITY/>

After graduating in 1996, Swardson decided to pursue stand-up comedy rather than attend college.<ref name=ASBURY>{{cite news  title=Comedy is a calling for Nick Swardson  first=Ed  last=Condran  url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/app/access/1770635631.html?FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Mar+18%2C+2006&author=ED+CONDRAN&pub=Asbury+Park+Press&desc=Comedy+is+a+calling+for+Nick+Swardson&pqatl=google  newspaper=Asbury Park Press  date=March 18, 2006  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> Although Swardson was a fan of sketch comedy, he saw stand-up comedy as a stepping-stone to a career in film, more so than he would working within a comedy troupe.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=NYT3/><ref name=SPLITSIDER>{{cite web  url=http://splitsider.com/2011/10/nick-swardson-on-bucky-larson-pretend-time-and-the-critics  title=Nick Swardson On Bucky Larson, Pretend Time and the Critics  last=Kiefer  first=Halle  date=October 3, 2011  work=Splitsider  publisher=The Awl  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
Swardson started performing stand-up at the age of 18, attending open mic nights at the Minneapolis comedy club [http://acmecomedycompany.com/index2.php Acme Comedy Co.].<ref name=CITY/><ref name=SPLITSIDER/><ref name=PRESS1>{{cite press release  last=Runyan  first=Jenni  title=Six-Episode Series Starring Nick Swardson Will Premiere In 2010  publisher=COMEDY CENTRAL Corporate Communications  date=October 29, 2009  url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2009/10/29/idUS183744+29-Oct-2009+PRN20091029  accessdate=February 6, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110206103321/http://www.reuters.com/article/2009/10/29/idUS183744+29-Oct-2009+PRN20091029  archivedate=February 6, 2011  df= }}</ref> Swardson stated in a 2007 interview with The Portland Mercury<ref name=PORTLAND>{{cite news  title=Nick Swardson  first=Katie  last=Shimer   url=http://www.portlandmercury.com/portland/nick-swardson/Content?oid=344605  newspaper=The Portland Mercury  date=June 14, 2007 accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> that he did his first open mic "as a goof" but he was encouraged by the club owner to perform again and he went on to win the comedy clubs award for the "Funniest Person in the Minneapolis–Saint Paul Twin Cities".<ref name=STARTRIB/><ref name=CITY/><ref name="PORTLAND"/> He also went on to perform regularly at Knuckleheads, a defunct comedy club which was located in the Mall of America.<ref name=CITY/> Swardson was also an occasional performer at Balls Cabaret.<ref>{{cite news  title=Balls Cabaret celebrates 20 years  first=Sheila  last=Regan  url=http://blogs.citypages.com/dressingroom/2011/09/balls_cabaret_southern_anniversary.php  newspaper=City Pages  date=September 9, 2011  accessdate=December 16, 2014  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217011615/http://blogs.citypages.com/dressingroom/2011/09/balls_cabaret_southern_anniversary.php  archivedate=December 17, 2014  df= }}</ref>

After garnering some attention within comedy circles, Swardson was selected to perform stand-up at the The Comedy Festival U.S. Comedy Arts Festival at 20 years of age.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=NYT3/>  The festival, which up to 2007 was held in Aspen, Colorado, was a yearly festival attended by entertainment insiders and was a place for comics and comedic actors to get exposure to the industry.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=NYT3/><ref name=NINENEWS>{{cite news  title=US Comedy Arts Festival leaving Aspen for 08   first1=Sara  last1=Gandy  first2=Kirk  last2=Mongomery  url=http://www.9news.com/news/story.aspx?storyid=69818&catid=222  newspaper=9 News Colorado  date=May 11, 2007  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> It was once the largest comedy convention of its kind in the US and became the launch pad for many comedians and comedy writers.<ref name=NYT3/><ref name=ASPEN>{{cite news  title=HBO pulls U.S. Comedy Arts Festival  first=Stewart  last=Oksenhorn  url=http://www.aspentimes.com/article/20070512/NEWS/70512001  newspaper=The Aspen Times  date=May 12, 2007  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref><ref name=DENVER>{{cite news  title=No joke: Comedy fest may leave Aspen behind Insiders worry about growth  first=Mark  last=Harden  url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=DP&p_theme=dp&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EAF44CCA822CD67&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM  newspaper=Denver Post  date=March 9, 1998  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> After Swardsons first performance at the festival comedian Tony Camin, acting as the nights Master of Ceremonies MC, was quoted as saying: "Theres a deal waiting to happen. I can see the TV show now: An Aspen Kid With Altitude."<ref name=NYT3/>

Swardson eventually left the Minneapolis–Saint Paul area for New York City where he would appear in such venues as the Luna Lounge, then on to Los Angeles where he headlined at many nightclubs.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=CITY/><ref name=STARPULSE2/><ref name=PRESS2>{{cite press release  title=OMG, Whats That Smell? The World Television Premiere of Nick Swardson: Seriously, Who Farted? a COMEDY CENTRAL(R) Original One-Hour Stand-Up Special Debuts Sunday, October 11 at 10:00 p.m. ET/PT  publisher=COMEDY CENTRAL Corporate Communications  date=September 29, 2009  url=http://www.sys-con.com/node/1125035  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> In addition to his stand-up, Swardson began to get work in commercials and in small roles in both TV in film, such an appearance in an episode of the Al Franken sitcom LateLine in 1999, and playing the role of a crazed David Bowie fan in the 2000 film Almost Famous.<ref name=IMDB>{{IMDb name 841910 Nick Swardson}}</ref>

In 2001, Swardsons stand-up act was featured in a half-hour Comedy Central Presents special.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=STARPULSE2/> He appeared on the show a second time in 2006 — a performance that was featured on the DVD compilation The Best of Comedy Central Presents: Uncensored II released in 2008.

In 2003, Swardson co-wrote the screenplay for Malibus Most Wanted along with the films star Jamie Kennedy and Adam Small.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=PRESS1/><ref name=PRESS2/>  That same year he took on the role of Terry Bernadino on the Comedy Central series Reno 911!.<ref name=CITY/> A recurring character throughout the series run from 2003 to 2009 — in addition to appearing in the film Reno 911!: Miami — Swardson played the role of a flamboyant Male prostitution gigolo who was often seen wearing roller skates.<ref name=STARTRIB/><ref name=CITY/><ref name=PORTLAND/><ref name=PRESS2/>  2003 also marked the beginning of a longtime working relationship and friendship with Adam Sandler.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=SPLITSIDER/><ref name=SJREG>{{cite news  title=Talking comedy and bank heists with Jesse Eisenberg and Nick Swardson  first=Dana  last=Barbuto  url=http://www.sj-r.com/mysource/entertainment/x782942409/Talking-comedy-and-bank-heists-with-Jesse-Eisenberg-and-Nick-Swardson  newspaper=The State Journal Register  date=August 8, 2011  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref> After having seen Swardsons Comedy Central special, Sandler contacted Swardson to ask if hed be interested in collaborating;<ref name=CITY/><ref name=STARPULSE2/> Swardsons first project with Sandler was co-writing the screenplay for Grandmas Boy (2006 film) Grandmas Boy — Swardson also co-produced and had an acting role in the film which was released in 2006.<ref name=CITY/><ref name=IMDB/>

In 2004, Swardson wrote, produced and starred in a TV show pilot for Comedy Central called Gay Robot, which was based on Gay Robot a comedy bit by the same name that appeared on Adam Sandlers fifth album, Shh...Dont Tell; Comedy Central, however, decided to pass on the TV project.<ref name=STARPULSE2/>  Swardson then had intentions on making it an animated show, but the show never came to fruition.<ref name=STARPULSE2/> A copy of the live-action pilot was however made available on MySpace in January 2007.<ref>[http://www.myspace.com/video/gay-robot/gay-robot/5006295 Gay Robot] on MySpace</ref> Swardson also resurrected the Gay Robot character on his Comedy Central Series Nick Swardsons Pretend Time.

Swardsons sketch comedy show Nick Swardsons Pretend Time, premiered on Comedy Central on October 12, 2010 and ran for two seasons. The show was produced in conjunction with Adam Sandler’s Happy Madison Happy Madison Productions.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=PRETEND>{{cite press release  last=  first=  title=Comedy Centrals "Nick Swardsons Pretend Time" Season Two Premieres on Wednesday, October 5 at 10:30 P.M.  publisher=COMEDY CENTRAL Corporate Communications  date=September 21, 2011  url=http://www.comedycentral.com/press/press_releases/2011/092111_nick-swardson-second-season-premiere-october5.jhtml  accessdate=February 6, 2012}}</ref>  On February 6, 2012 Swardson announced via his Facebook page that there would not be a third season; in his post Swardson stated that "the ratings were solid but it was too expensive for the network and tough creatively," and added that he was "developing a new show where I play a ninja."<ref>[http://www.facebook.com/nickswardsoncomedy/posts/350952918269218 A post on Swardsons Facebook page announcing the cancellation of Nick Swardsons Pretend Time.] Retrieved February 6, 2012</ref>

In 2011, Swardson starred in, co-wrote and co-produced the film Bucky Larson: Born to Be a Star, also produced by Happy Madison Productions.<ref name=SPLITSIDER/><ref name=SJREG/> That same year he starred alongside Jesse Eisenberg, Danny McBride (actor) Danny McBride and Aziz Ansari in the film 30 Minutes or Less.<ref name=SJREG/><ref name=VOLUSIA>{{cite news  title=West Volusia Beacon Movie Review —30 Minutes or Less  first=Patrick  last=Sessoms  url=http://www.beacononlinenews.com/news/daily/4038  newspaper=West Volusia Beacon  date=August 20, 2011  accessdate=February 6, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130117235842/http://www.beacononlinenews.com/news/daily/4038  archivedate=January 17, 2013  df= }}</ref>

In 2014, Swardson performed the voice of the character Troy on the animated series Chozen.<ref>{{cite news  title=Not Just Another Ex-Con Hoping for Hip-Hop Glory  first=Jon  last=Caramanica  url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/13/arts/television/chozen-an-animated-series-on-fx-features-bobby-moynihan.html  newspaper=New York Times  date=January 12, 2014  accessdate=March 15, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news  title=Chozen Is FXs New Animated Gay White Rapper  first=John D.  last=Luerssen  url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/chozen-is-fxs-new-animated-gay-white-rapper-premiere-20131216  newspaper=Rolling Stone  date=December 16, 2013  accessdate=March 15, 2014}}</ref>
