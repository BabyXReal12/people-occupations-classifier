
Susan Elizabeth Perkins (born 22 September 1969) is an English comedian, broadcaster, actress, and writer. Originally coming to prominence through her comedy partnership with Mel Giedroyc in Mel and Sue, she has since become best known as a radio broadcaster and television presenter, notably of The Great British Bake Off (2010–2016) and Insert Name Here (2016–present). ==Early life and education==
Perkins was born on 22 September 1969 in  south London, where she grew up in Croydon with her two younger siblings and parents. Her father worked for a local car dealer and her mother was employed as a secretary.<ref name="The Observer 19 April 2015">{{cite news   title= The Observer Profile: Sue Perkins – Bake Off host under fire url= https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2015/apr/19/sue-perkins-bake-off-host-feels-heat   last= Anthony  first= Andrew  date= 19 April 2015   newspaper= The Observer   page= 34  accessdate= 20 April 2015}}</ref> She was educated at Croham Hurst School, a nearby independent school for girls in South Croydon, Greater London, at the same time as television presenter Susanna Reid.<ref>[https://archive.is/20120724145709/http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediaselector/check/player/nol/newsid_4710000/newsid_4714300?redirect=4714375.stm&news=1&nbwm=1&nbram=1&bbwm=1&bbram=1&asb=1 BBC Breakfast interview with Sue Perkins on 25 July 2005]</ref>

She later studied English at New Hall (now Murray Edwards College) at the University of Cambridge, graduating in 1991. While at Cambridge, she was a member of the Footlights, where she met Mel Giedroyc.<ref>{{cite news   url= https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/how-we-met-mel-giedroyc-and-sue-perkins-1046300.html   title= How We Met: Mel Giedroyc And Sue Perkins   location=London   work=The Independent   date=10 January 1999}}</ref> She was Footlights president during the academic year 1990–91.<ref>{{cite web url=http://footlights.org/alumni-archive?name=1990-1999  title=Footlight alumni 1990-1999  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015035204/http://footlights.org/alumni-archive?name=1990-1999  archivedate=15 October 2012  df= }}</ref>

==Mel and Sue==
Perkins and creative partner Mel Giedroyc took their first steps into television under the name Mel and Sue. The duo began to gain success and were short-listed for the Daily Express Best Newcomers award at the Edinburgh Festival in 1993.

After a few years writing for French & Saunders (and occasionally appearing on their BBC series), they co-hosted a lunchtime show on Channel 4 entitled Light Lunch, and an early evening version, Light Lunch Late Lunch, which ran from March 1997 to February 1998.

In January 2015, Giedroyc and Perkins began hosting their own daytime chat show on ITV (TV network) ITV called Mel and Sue (TV series) Mel and Sue.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2014/aug/14/great-british-bake-off-mel-giedroyc-sue-perkins title=Great British Bake Off stars clinch ITV chat show work=the Guardian accessdate=18 November 2014}}</ref><ref>[http://www.itv.com/beontv/shows/melandsue] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141204202412/http://www.itv.com/beontv/shows/melandsue date=4 December 2014}}</ref> In August 2015, it was announced that Mel and Sue (TV series) Mel and Sue had been cancelled by ITV.<ref>[http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/news/a662812/mel--sues-daytime-chatshow-has-been-dropped-by-itv.html#~pl3QvsuXbrYDmx Mel & Sues daytime chatshow has been dropped by ITV - TV News - Digital Spy<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>  On 23 July 2017, it was confirmed that they would host a new version of The Generation Game for BBC One.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.live.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2017/the-generation-game title=Mel and Sue are bringing The Generation Game back to BBC One publisher=BBC Media Centre accessdate=23 July 2017}}</ref>

==Television==
In 2002, Perkins appeared on the Celebrity Big Brother 2 (UK) second UK series of Celebrity Big Brother (UK) Celebrity Big Brother in aid of four charities, Centrepoint (charity) Centrepoint, Missing People National Missing Persons Helpline, Rethink Mental Illness Rethink and Samaritans (charity) Samaritans. During the series, she interacted with series winner Mark Owen from Take That, and TV presenter Les Dennis. Perkins was evicted from the house on Day 9.<ref>{{cite news title=Sue gets Big Brother shove url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/2523569.stm publisher=BBC accessdate=19 February 2013 date=28 November 2002}}</ref>

She provided the voice for Messenger Bird in Dinotopia, produced for Hallmark Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web title=Full cast and crew for "Dinotopia" url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0310443/fullcredits?ref_=tt_cl_sm#cast publisher=imdb.com accessdate=20 February 2013}}</ref>

In 2003, Perkins joined Channel 4 morning television programme RI:SE.<ref>{{cite news last=Deans first=Jason title=Mel and Sue join RI:SE url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2003/jan/16/broadcasting.channel4 publisher=The Guardian accessdate=19 February 2013 location=London date=16 January 2003}}</ref> In the same year, Perkins also provided additional written material for BBC sitcom Absolutely Fabulous.

Perkins has appeared on several BBC shows including Have I Got News for You, Mock the Week, QI, Room 101 (TV series) Room 101, The Weakest Link (UK game show) Celebrity Weakest Link, Question Time (TV series) Question Time and Newsnight. Perkins has commented that the BBC pay her a regular wage for "blabbering on random shows".{{Citation needed date=February 2013}} She has made appearances as a field reporter for Armando Iannucci vehicle The Saturday Night Armistice.

Perkins hosted the second series of Good Evening, Rockall, a short-lived, news-orientated panel game shown on BBC Choice. In 2006, she appeared in BBC Fours vocabulary quiz show Never Mind the Full Stops. She was also a team captain on ITV Network ITVs Win, Lose or Draw Late. During the same decade she made appearances on Celebrity MasterChef, Celebrity Poker and News Knight with Sir Trevor McDonald.

In April 2007, she participated in the television series, Edwardian Supersize Me for the BBC. She was joined by food critic Giles Coren. The series focused on spending a week eating the equivalent of a wealthy Edwardian couples food, whilst wearing period clothing.

Following the series, Perkins and Coren were commissioned to present a new series called, The Supersizers Go.... The premise of "Edwardian Supersize Me" was replicated and focused on other periods throughout history.

In the first episode, they survived for a week on Second World War rations. It was re-commissioned for a second episode, Perkins and Coren covered the English Restoration period. The third episode covered the Victorian period, the fourth the 1970s, the fifth the Elizabethan era Elizabethan period and the sixth the Regency era Regency period.

In August and September 2008, Perkins appeared in the reality television series Maestro (TV series) Maestro on BBC Two. During the series, a group of eight celebrities attempted (until eliminated) to learn to conduct orchestral, choral and operatic music.<ref>{{cite web title=Profile: Sue Perkins url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/musictv/maestro/whos-who/the-students/sue-perkins/ publisher=BBC accessdate=19 February 2013}}</ref> During the series, Perkins conducted three pieces, two of them with soprano soloist Lesley Garrett.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/musictv/maestro/news/sue-takes-hyde-park-by-storm/  title=Sue Perkins takes Hyde Park by Storm  date=13 September 2008  accessdate=22 April 2012}}</ref> Perkins won the series.<ref>{{cite web title=Congratulations to Sue Perkins! url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/musictv/maestro/news/congratulations-to-sue-perkins/ publisher=BBC accessdate=19 February 2013}}</ref> In 2008, Perkins narrated the series ....And Proud on Virgin 1.

Perkins appeared in a second "Supersizers" series called The Supersizers Eat... with Giles Coren which aired on BBC Two in June and July 2009.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2009/04_april/22/bbctwo_os.shtml  title=Press Office - BBC Two Spring/Summer 2009: Programmes O-S  publisher=BBC  date=  accessdate=27 April 2009}}</ref> In September and October 2009 she hosted the Channel 4 panel game The Big Food Fight.

She then gave a televised lecture for the Royal Television Society. Entitled "Wits End? British Comedy at the Crossroads", the lecture focused on the state of British comedy. The presentation was broadcast on BBC Two.<ref>{{cite web title=Huw Wheldon Lecture 2009: Wits End? British Comedy at the Crossroads url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00f719s publisher=BBC accessdate=20 February 2013}}</ref>

In March 2010, Perkins appeared in a three part mini-series on BBC Two, A Band for Britain, in which she attempted to revive the fortunes of the Dinnington Colliery Band.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00rhghl/episodes/guide  title=A Band for Britain: Episode guide  accessdate=22 April 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news  url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/bbc/7377444/Sue-Perkins-on-A-Band-for-Britain.html  title=Sue Perkins on a Band for Britain  last=West  first=Naomi  newspaper=The Telegraph  date=5 March 2010  accessdate=22 April 2012  location=London}}</ref>

==Radio==
Perkins is a panel member on Radio 4s The News Quiz and has made regular appearances on BBC Radio 2 Radio 2s Its Been a Bad Week. She is also a frequent panellist on another popular Radio 4 show, Just a Minute: in the 2012 television version, she appeared in four out of the 10 episodes (more than any other panellist except Paul Merton who appeared in all 10) and won on all four occasions.<ref>{{cite web url=http://just-a-minute.info/jamtv4.html title=Just a Minute! Television Season 4 accessdate=10 April 2012}}</ref>

She was the chairman of BBC Radio 4 Radio 4s The 99p Challenge until the show finished in 2004. Perkins appeared every day in the last half hour of Mark Radcliffes afternoon radio show on Radio 2, when he sat in for Steve Wright (DJ) Steve Wright.

Since 2006, Perkins has been a panellist on a Radio 4 show, The Personality Test, a quiz show about the host, presented by a different host each week. Past hosts include Gyles Brandreth and Rick Wakeman, and other panellists include Robin Ince, Lucy Porter and Will Smith (comedian) Will Smith. Perkins is a regular cast member of Count Arthur Strongs Radio Show.

Perkins presented a Radio 4 documentary on the Lake Districts competition the "Worlds Biggest Liar", which she won.<ref name="bbcnewsliar">[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/cumbria/6159642.stm Comedienne crowned biggest liar], BBC News Online, Cumbria, 17 November 2006. Retrieved 22 June 2008.</ref>

In December 2008, she was a guest on Private Passions, the biographical music discussion programme on BBC Radio 3.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio3/privatepassions/ BBC Radio 3]</ref>

Perkins was also chairman of the Radio 4 panel game Dilemma, in which four humorous guests discussed moral conundrums she provided for them. The first series ran for six episodes on Sunday evenings from 13 November to 18 December 2011. Another series of this programme ran in February 2013.

On 9 July 2017 Perkins was the guest celebrity on Radio 4s Desert Island Discs.
