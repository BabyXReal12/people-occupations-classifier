==Early life==
Mirman was born in Moscow,<ref>http://eugenemirman.com/about/#FAQ {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115165920/http://eugenemirman.com/about/  date=January 15, 2013 }}</ref> to Jewish parents.  His family emigrated to the United States when he was four and a half years old.<ref>http://eugenemirman.com/about/#Bio {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115165920/http://eugenemirman.com/about/  date=January 15, 2013 }}</ref>

Mirman attended William Diamond Middle School and Lexington High School (Massachusetts) Lexington High School in Lexington, Massachusetts, and Hampshire College in Western Massachusetts. As part of the colleges "choose your own major" program, Mirman graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in comedy, with a one-hour routine as his thesis.<ref>{{cite web last=Mirman first=Eugene title=Extended Biography url=http://eugenemirman.com/about/ accessdate=January 30, 2013 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115165920/http://eugenemirman.com/about/ archivedate=January 15, 2013 df=mdy-all}}</ref>  During summers he attended Camp Tohkomeupog in East Madison, NH. He returned to his high school to deliver its 2009 commencement address.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KZlQd2Eg-9w  title=Eugene Mirman 2009 LHS Commencement Speech  publisher=Youtube.com  date=June 8, 2009  accessdate=May 1, 2013  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130228091747/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KZlQd2Eg-9w  archivedate=February 28, 2013  df=mdy-all }}</ref> He returned to Hampshire to deliver the 2012 commencement speech as well.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-JfKq7clJjw  title=Hampshire College • 2012 Commencement Keynote Speech • Eugene Mirman  publisher=Youtube.com  date=  accessdate=May 1, 2013  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140102110743/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-JfKq7clJjw  archivedate=January 2, 2014  df=mdy-all }}</ref>

==Career==
===Stand-up career===
In 2004, Mirman released The Absurd Nightclub Comedy of Eugene Mirman, a CD/DVD on Suicide Squeeze Records. The album was voted one of the Best Albums of 2004 by both The A.V. Club and Time Out (company) Time Out New York.  His second album, En Garde, Society was released by Sub Pop in 2006. Three years later, Mirman released another comedy album titled God Is a Twelve-Year-Old Boy with Aspergers which was recorded in Chicago at the Lakeshore Theatre.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.subpop.com/bio/eugene_mirman title=Eugene Mirman author=Sub Pop Records work=Sub Pop Records accessdate=December 20, 2015 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131216193715/http://www.subpop.com/bio/eugene_mirman archivedate=December 16, 2013 df=mdy-all}}</ref> In 2012, Mirman released An Evening Of Comedy In A Fake, Underground Laboratory.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2514162/ title=Eugene Mirman: An Evening of Comedy in a Fake Underground Laboratory (TV Movie 2012) date=December 21, 2012 work=IMDb accessdate=December 20, 2015 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160405002838/http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2514162/ archivedate=April 5, 2016 df=mdy-all}}</ref>

Mirman has appeared at the U.S. Comedy Arts Festival in Aspen, Colorado, the Just for Laughs Just for Laughs Festival in Montreal, Bumbershoot, and South by Southwest. He co-produced the weekly standup-variety show Invite Them Up with Bobby Tisdale and Holly Schlesinger, which won a Nightlife Award. He currently produces Pretty Good Friends (formerly Tearing the Veil of Maya) at Union Hall in Park Slope, Brooklyn with Julie Smith, which has been voted the best comedy night in New York City by New York Magazine.

Mirman, much like David Cross and Patton Oswalt, is known to often perform in rock clubs and theaters as opposed to traditional comedy clubs. Often touring the United States, Mirman occasionally opens for the comedy troupe Stella (U.S. TV series) Stella (former members of The State (1993 TV series) The State). He has opened for various bands such as The Shins and toured with Modest Mouse, Yo La Tengo, Gogol Bordello, Andrew Bird, and Cake (band) Cake.  Mirman also toured with Patton Oswalt, Brian Posehn and Maria Bamford on the Comedians of Comedy tour.

He has toured as an opener for Flight of the Conchords and with Andy Kindler and Marc Maron in Stand Uppity.  He plays the character Eugene in the Flight of the Conchords (TV series) Flight of the Conchords HBO television series.

In January 2010, Mirman performed standup on the John Olivers New York Stand Up Show on Comedy Central.<ref>{{cite web  author=Photo courtesy of Comedy Central, Lead photo by Gavin Bond  url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2009/11/john-oliver-gets-his-own-tv-show.html  title=John Olivers TV Show to Feature Eugene Mirman, Paul F. Mirmans Eugene Mirman Comedy Festival has been running since 2008.<ref>{{cite web author= url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/blogs/culture/2011-09-20/the-erudite-absurdist-eugene-mirman title=The Erudite Absurdist: Eugene Mirman – Page publisher=Interview Magazine date= accessdate=April 2, 2012 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111024223140/http://www.interviewmagazine.com/blogs/culture/2011-09-20/the-erudite-absurdist-eugene-mirman archivedate=October 24, 2011 df=mdy-all}}</ref>

On April 10, 2015, Mirman filmed a live stand-up special in Tucson, AZ, directed by Bobcat Goldthwait. The special was released on Netflix in 2015 as Eugene Mirman: Vegan on His Way to the Complain Store.
