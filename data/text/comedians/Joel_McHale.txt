Joel Edward McHale (born November 20, 1971)<ref>{{cite magazine last=Smith first=Grady url=http://www.ew.com/article/2011/11/18/monitor-nov-25-2011 title=Monitor: Nov. 25, 2011 magazine=Entertainment Weekly issue=1182 page=30 date=November 25, 2011 accessdate=April 4, 2015}}</ref> is an American comedian, actor, writer, television producer, and television host. He is known for hosting The Soup and portraying Jeff Winger on the NBC/Yahoo! sitcom Community (TV series) Community. He has appeared in the films Spy Kids: All the Time in the World (2011) and Ted (film) Ted (2012). He also starred in the short-lived CBS sitcom The Great Indoors (TV series) The Great Indoors.

==Early life==
McHale was born in Rome, Italy, where his father, Jack McHale, worked as Dean of Students at Loyola University Chicago Loyola University Rome Center. His mother, Laurie, is from Vancouver, British Columbia Vancouver, Canada; and his father is from Chicago, Illinois.<ref>[http://www.pnwlocalnews.com/east_king/mir/lifestyle/35031314.html Mercer Island dons its autumn cloak] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110131192559/http://www.pnwlocalnews.com/east_king/mir/lifestyle/35031314.html  date=January 31, 2011 }}, pnwlocalnews.com; accessed February 25, 2014</ref> Of Irish people Irish and Norwegians Norwegian descent,<ref>{{cite web url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/1203/15/pmt.01.html title=PIERS MORGAN TONIGHT, One-on-one with Joel McHale; The Great Anjelica Huston date=March 15, 2012 publisher=CNN}}</ref> he was raised Catholic Church Catholic. He grew up on Mercer Island, Washington, and briefly in Haddonfield, New Jersey, near Philadelphia, before returning to his native Washington state.<ref>{{cite web  last=Strawberry  first=Saroyan  url=http://www.details.com/story/soup-host-joel-mchale-is-the-funniest-man-on-tv  title=Joel McHale  work=Details (magazine) Details  date=June 2008  accessdate=December 19, 2015}}</ref> He attended Mercer Island High School.<ref>{{cite web  last=Brodeur  first=Nicole  url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20040822&slug=joelmchale22  title=From Mercer Island, jumping into "The Soup"  work=The Seattle Times  date=August 22, 2004  accessdate=December 19, 2015}}</ref>

McHale received a bachelors degree in History from the University of Washington (1995) and briefly belonged to Theta Chi.<ref>{{cite news last=Curran first=Dan title=An interview with the king of snark Joel McHale url=http://onmilwaukee.com/ent/articles/joelmchale.html accessdate=March 30, 2012 newspaper=onmilwaukee.com date=October 16, 2008}}</ref>

He was recruited to be on the Rowing (sport) rowing team but later joined the Washington Huskies football football team. Though most of his teammates received athletic scholarships, McHale was a Walk-on (sports) walk on playing tight end for two years.<ref>{{cite news last=Steinberg first=Dan title=THECHAT: Joel McHale url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A22047-2005Feb13.html?sub%3Dnew?=AR%20THECHAT page=D02 accessdate=March 30, 2012 newspaper=The Washington Post date=February 14, 2005}}</ref> He received a Master of Fine Arts degree from the Professional Actors Training Program at the University of Washington. He is a fan of the Seattle Seahawks.<ref>{{cite web last=McHale first=Joel title=The Walk-On: The Community star recalls his two-year football career at the University of Washington url=http://www.grantland.com/story/_/id/7035000/the-walk-on work=Grantland publisher=ESPN Ventures accessdate=March 30, 2012 date=September 29, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==

===Comedy===
McHale was part of the Almost Live! cast, a local sketch-comedy television show produced by Seattles KING-TV (broadcast Channel 5). From 1993 to 1997, he was a member of the improv comedy group at Unexpected Productions, participating in Theatersports! at the Market Theater located in Pike Place Market in downtown Seattle.<ref>[http://www.unexpectedproductions.org/about.htm International Improv Festival] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081010084039/http://www.unexpectedproductions.org/about.htm  date=October 10, 2008 }}; accessed February 25, 2014</ref>

===Acting===
After earning his masters degree in acting, McHale moved to Los Angeles and landed small roles in Diagnosis: Murder, CSI: Miami, and Will & Grace. He played a TV reporter in the biopic Lords of Dogtown. He guest-starred as a cast member on the NBC improv comedy show Thank God Youre Here (US TV series) Thank God Youre Here during the pilot episode and had a guest role on an episode of Pushing Daisies. McHale also appeared in Spider-Man 2 in a small role as Mr. Jacks, a bank manager.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0316654/fullcredits?ref_=tt_ov_st_sm] imdb.com Spider-Man 2</ref>

In 2004 McHale began hosting The Soup, a satirical weekly television show on the "E!" television network. Throughout the show, he takes the audience through the oddities and ridiculous happenings of the week in television. He frequently appears as a co-host on Loveline. He has also had a long association with Circle X Theatre, where he has done several plays. McHale was a weekly guest on The Adam Carolla Show (radio) The Adam Carolla Show and Mickey and Amelia, and occasional guest on the Opie and Anthony Show.<ref>[http://www.foundrymusic.com/opieanthony/displayheadline.cfm/id/12764/div/opieanthony/headline/Monday_Opie_and_Anthony_Links__Joel_McHale__New_O_A_Videos__Good_bye_Syracuse__Traveling_Virus_Tickets_On_Sale_Now__New_Videos_Online.html Joel McHale on Opie and Anthony]; accessed February 25, 2014</ref> He has been a judge on Iron Chef America. He was involved in the American version of the British TV show The IT Crowd.<ref>[http://io9.gizmodo.com/the-failed-u-s-remake-of-the-it-crowd-is-fascinating-t-1557311843 The Failed U.S. Remake Of The IT Crowd Is Fascinating To Watch<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://www.bbcamerica.com/anglophenia/2014/11/richard-ayoade-regrets-u-s-pilot-crowd Richard Ayoade Regrets Doing the U.S. Pilot for ‘The IT Crowd’   Anglophenia   BBC America<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> McHale made a guest appearance on the finale of Last Comic Standings sixth season, when he recapped the shows events in his typical format of jokes made popular on The Soup. He occasionally appeared on Countdown with Keith Olbermann in a humor sequence closing the show.

McHale starred in the sitcom Community (TV series) Community,<ref>{{cite news url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/showtracker/2009/05/nbc-unveils-new-programs-for-next-fall-.html last=Fernandez first=Maria Elena title=NBC unveils new programs for fall date=April 4, 2009 accessdate=May 4, 2009 work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref> which premiered in winter 2009, and continued to host The Soup.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/blog/BC_Beat/12945-E_Expects_Joel_McHale_To_Stay_On_Soup_.php title=E! Expects Joel McHale To Stay On Soup  last=Weprin first=Alex date=May 4, 2009 accessdate=May 4, 2009}}</ref>
McHale made an appearance on the first episode of the fifth season of Tosh.0. He played Rex in Ted (film) Ted (2012) and had a short guest stint on the FX show Sons of Anarchy. He played an NYPD officer in the supernatural horror film Deliver Us from Evil (2014 film) Deliver Us from Evil.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.masslive.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2013/08/joel_mchale_talks_community_so.html title=Joel McHale talks Community, Soup and upcoming movie work=masslive.com}}</ref> McHale appeared in 3 episodes between the tenth and eleventh seasons of The X-Files in 2016 and 2018, respectively.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.eonline.com/news/675587/why-joel-mchale-is-even-more-excited-about-the-x-files-revival-than-you-are title=Why Joel McHale Is Even More Excited About The X-Files Revival Than You Are date=July 14, 2015 work=E! Online accessdate=January 29, 2016}}</ref>

McHale played the lead role in the CBS sitcom The Great Indoors (TV series) The Great Indoors, which began airing in the Fall of 2016.<ref>Wagmeister, Elizabeth (February 16, 2016).  [https://variety.com/2016/tv/news/joel-mchale-the-great-indoors-cbs-cast-1201705277/ "Joel McHale to Star in CBS Comedy Pilot The Great Indoors."]  Variety.com. In 2018, McHale was cast in the recurring role of Chris on the second season of the Netflix horror-comedy series Santa Clarita Diet.<ref>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2018/02/santa-clarita-diet-joel-mchale-maggie-lawson-gerald-mcraney-zachary-knighton-guest-star-season-2-1202291505/ title=‘Santa Clarita Diet’: Joel McHale, Maggie Lawson, Gerald McRaney & Zachary Knighton To Guest Star In Season 2 last=Petski first=Denise date=February 16, 2018 work=Deadline accessdate=April 1, 2018}}</ref>

===Hosting===
McHale was the host at the 2014 White House Correspondents Association annual dinner.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/tv/showtracker/la-et-st-joel-mchale-host-white-house-correspondents-dinner-20140214,0,3433623.story#axzz2tKKOOx2y title=Joel McHale will host the White House Correspondents Dinner accessdate=February 14, 2014 work=Los Angeles Times date=February 14, 2014}}</ref> He hosted the 2011 Independent Spirit Awards and the 2015 ESPY Awards. In 2016, he appeared as an occasional co-host alongside Kelly Ripa in the American Broadcasting Corporation ABC morning show Live! with Kelly and Michael Live! with Kelly.  McHale hosted the 43rd Peoples Choice Awards on January 18, 2017 and the 2017 Webby Awards on May 15, 2017.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2017/digital/news/webby-awards-honor-this-years-internet-vips-1202429111/ title=The 21st Annual Webby Awards Honor This Years Internet VIPs publisher=Variety accessdate=May 16, 2017}}</ref>

In January 2018, it was announced that he would be receiving his own talk show on Netflix in February. The Joel McHale Show with Joel McHale combines celebrity guests, pre-taped sketches and video clips in a half-hour series that focuses on pop culture and news from around the world.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/netflix-joel-mchale-talk-show/ title=Netflix Nabs Joel McHale for Weekly Talk Show date=2018-01-19 work=TVGuide.com access-date=2018-01-19 language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
McHale married Sarah Williams in 1996. They have two sons.<ref>{{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080508174518/http://www.psarts.org/Press2007/ExpressYourselfEOnline.html  date=May 8, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite episode series=The Soup network=E! airdate= January 15, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/23611984 title=Countdown with Keith Olbermann for March 12 accessdate=January 15, 2010}}</ref> They live in Hollywood Hills, California.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.latimes.com/features/home/la-hm-brown15-2009aug15,0,1880997.story title=Flipping Outs Ryan Brown settles down in Los Feliz work=latimes}}</ref>
