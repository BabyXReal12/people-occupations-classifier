Rob Deering (born 1972), is an English comedian, musician and writer. 

Deering was the host and writer of three series of  Out to Lunch on BBC Radio 2. He also hosted 99 Things to Do Before You Die on Five (TV channel) Channel 5.

In 2007, Deering appeared on a special edition of The Weakest Link, winning the show and answering all his questions correctly.<ref>{{cite news url=https://www.theguardian.com/profile/robdeering title=Rob Deering  publisher=guardian.co.uk accessdate=20 October 2009  work=The Guardian   location=London   date=23 July 2008}}</ref> Deering also appeared on the Channel 4 quiz show Fifteen to One in 1997.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UKLF6XC8xok title=Rob Deering on 15-1 - outro first= last=Rob Deering date=14 February 2013 publisher= via=YouTube}}</ref> He won an edition of Celebrity Mastermind broadcast on 28 December 2014 with a score of 27 points.

Deering has appeared as a guest on shows including Banter (radio show) Banter, 4 at the Fringe on BBC Radio 4 and Jammin on BBC Radio 2. He has also guest starred in Jason Manfords Comedy Rocks.

Deering grew up in London and Oxfordshire and lives in London with his wife Julia and their children.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thisisnottingham.co.uk/news/Comedy-Rob-Deering/article-890430-detail/article.html title=Rob Deering interview ahead of Nottingham gig publisher=thisisnottingham.co.uk accessdate=20 October 2009}}</ref>

He took part in the Laughing for a Change project in 2014, which aimed to raise awareness of mental health through a comedy tour, also featuring Janice Connolly Mrs Barbara Nice and Seymour Mace. The project was supported by Time to Change (mental health campaign) Time to Change.<ref>{{cite news  first=Becca  last=Gill  url=http://www.gigglebeats.co.uk/2014/03/review-laughing-for-a-change-the-stand-newcastle/  title=Review: Laughing for a Change – The Stand, Newcastle  work=Giggle Beats date=1 March 2014  accessdate=23 March 2014}}</ref>

Deering is an avid runner and has run eight marathons including five London Marathons; so far he has raised nearly £100,000 for Parkinsons UK. He currently cohosts a running themed podcast with fellow comedian and runner Paul Tonkinson called Running Commentary. <ref>{{cite web url=https://www.acast.com/runningcommentary title=Running Commentary on acast first= last=acast publisher=}}</ref>
