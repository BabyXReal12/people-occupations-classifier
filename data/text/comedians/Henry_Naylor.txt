Henry Naylor (born 19 January 1966){{citation needed date=September 2016}} is a United Kingdom British comedy writer, director and performer.<ref name="times_head">{{cite news  url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/tv_and_radio/article3666386.ece  publisher=The Times  date=5 April 2008  title=ITVs satire show Headcases to be more than Spitting Images lookalike  accessdate=2008-05-05    location=London   first=Bruce   last=Dessau}}</ref>

He was head writer for Spitting Image,{{citation needed date=September 2016}} and has written for many shows including, Alas Smith and Jones, Dead Ringers (comedy) Dead Ringers and Big Impression Alistair McGowans Big Impression.{{citation needed date=September 2016}} His work helped these shows to win numerous awards, including the British Comedy Award, and The Sony Gold.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

With his comedy partner Andy Parsons, he has performed satirical shows in live venues in Australia and as part of the Edinburgh Fringe.{{citation needed date=September 2016}} Parsons and Naylor’s Pull-Out Sections broadcast its ninth season on BBC Radio 2 during Spring 2007.{{citation needed date=September 2016}} A compilation CD was released in 2003.{{citation needed date=September 2016}} The duo also set up Londons first comedy sketch club TBA, at the Gate Theatre in the 1990s, and in the process helped discover many of Britains leading sketch performers including Armstrong and Miller, Tony Gardner and The Cheese Shop.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

In 2008, he created, directed and acted as executive producer on Headcases, a satirical ITV show very similar to Spitting Image, but made with Computer-generated imagery CGI rather than puppets.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2007/05/17/5332/a_spitting_image_for_the_digital_age  title=A Spitting Image for the digital age : News 2007 : Chortle : The UK Comedy Guide  publisher=Chortle  date=2007-05-17  accessdate=2010-04-09}}</ref> The show won numerous prestigious TV awards - including the RTS for Design and Innovation, and the C21 Award for Best New Sketch Show at Cannes Mipcom - and was nominated for Best New Programme in the Broadcast Awards.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

On the live circuit, Henry has been regular at the Edinburgh Festival, performing, writing and directing numerous sketch shows and plays (15 in total).{{citation needed date=September 2016}} In 2014 he was awarded one of the Festivals highest accolades, the Fringe First. He was also one of the international acts invited to perform at the Melbourne and Sydney Comedy Festivals.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

He also played Rowan Atkinsons sidekick Bough in a series of 17 commercials for Barclaycard.<ref>https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/henry-naylor-from-spitting-image-to-syria-xqlwr2s8j</ref>

In 2003 he was in the news for throwing a full English breakfast at David Blaine during his Above the Below stunt on the South Bank of the River Thames in London.<ref>https://www.standard.co.uk/news/blaines-strange-friend-6954401.html%3Famp</ref>

==Plays==
Naylor has written and directed award-winning plays for the Edinburgh Fringe, usually playing in The Gilded Balloon Teviot. Finding Bin Laden (2003) was a satire about the medias representation of the 2003 Invasion of Iraq, which also featured co-producer Sam Maynards documentary photography.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.culturewars.org.uk/edinburgh2003/war/findingbinladen.htm title=Finding Bin Laden publisher=}}</ref>

Hunting Diana, his 2004 Fringe offering, was about conspiracy theories surrounding the death of Diana, Princess of Wales.<ref>[http://edinburghnews.scotsman.com/whatson.cfm?id=970512004 The Scotsman] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050125052805/http://edinburghnews.scotsman.com/whatson.cfm?id=970512004  date=25 January 2005 }} Hunting Diana review</ref>

In 2007 he directed Sarah Kendalls Fringe show My Very First Kidnapping.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

In the summer of 2014 he wrote his first drama-tragedy - The Collector - which won one of the leading awards at the Edinburgh Festival, the Fringe First.<ref>[http://www.wow247.co.uk/blog/2014/08/08/scotsman-fringe-first-awards-first-winners-of-2014-announced/]"Fringe First Winner" announcement</ref><ref>[http://www.wow247.co.uk/blog/2014/08/07/cuckooed-the-collector-sochi-2014-the-trial-of-jane-fonda/]"The Collector" review</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://sgfringe.com/2014/08/10/sg2014-review-the-collector/  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2015-01-26  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140923013235/http://sgfringe.com/2014/08/10/sg2014-review-the-collector/  archivedate=23 September 2014  df=dmy-all }}</ref> Set in an Iraqi gaol during the occupation of Iraq in 2003, the play attacked brutality on both sides of the conflict. The show transferred off-Londons West End, and played a sell-out season at the Arcola Theatre in November 2014, to critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thegizzlereview.com/2014/11/the-collector-arcola-theatre.html title=The Collector @ The Arcola Theatre - THE GIZZLE REVIEW publisher=}}</ref><ref>[http://gingerhibiscus.com/review-the-collector-at-the-arcola-theatre/][http://exeuntmagazine.com/reviews/the-collector/]</ref> In autumn of 2016, the show will be going on an extensive 3-month tour of the UK.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}
