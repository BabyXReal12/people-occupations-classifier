
Melvin Edward "Slappy" White (September 27, 1921 &ndash; November 7, 1995) was an American comedian and actor.<ref name=NYT>{{cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/1995/11/09/nyregion/slappy-white-74-who-brought-black-humor-into-white-clubs.html title=Slappy White, 74, Who Brought Black Humor Into White Clubs last=Van Gelder  first=Lawrence  author-link=Lawrence Van Gelder  date=November 9, 1995  newspaper=The New York Times  access-date=December 22, 2017}}</ref> He worked with Redd Foxx on the Chitlin circuit of stand-up comedy during the 1950s and 1960s. He appeared on the television shows Sanford and Son, Thats My Mama, Blossom (TV series) Blossom, and Cybill and in the films Mr. Saturday Night and Amazon Women on the Moon.

==Biography==
White was born in Baltimore, Maryland, on September 27, 1921.<ref name=NYT/> His official biography reported that he "ran away to join the circus" as a child. White was born near the old Royal Theatre (Baltimore) Royal Theatre in Baltimore, where by the age of 10, he used to dance outside for coins and sold candy at the theater. He left Baltimore at the age of 13 because he was in danger of being sent to reform school because of his school absences.<ref name=Afro/><ref name=Baltimore>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=U7s9AAAAIBAJ&sjid=4SsMAAAAIBAJ&pg=2777,17936422&dq=slappy+white&hl=en work=Baltimore Afro-American date=November 4, 1972 page=D-39 title=Slappy White accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> White joined a traveling carnival and made a living as a tap dancer with the troupe.  He was eventually picked up by police and returned to his family, but could not trade show business for school.<ref name=jokes>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=zGFcAAAAIBAJ&sjid=l1YNAAAAIBAJ&pg=3029,3406357&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=My Favorite Jokes author=White, Slappy date=June 23, 1968 page=14 work=Parade magazine accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> He received his nickname from the manager of a local theater where he entered a talent contest with a friend; the manager billed them as "Slap and Happy".<ref name=Afro>{{Cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=oqs7AAAAIBAJ&sjid=7ikMAAAAIBAJ&pg=1352,2860700&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Slappy White...Home for Christmas author=Peters, Ida date=December 15, 1979 pages=12 & 16 work=Baltimore Afro-American accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
He began his career as a dancer and did not turn to comedy until 1940 when he joined with a fellow hoofer and labeled his act the "Two Zephyrs". White and his partner, Clarence Schelle, had appeared on Major Bowes Amateur Hour The Major Bowes Amateur Hour.<ref name=Foxx>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=a7vcX2uNPK4C&pg=PA99&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=BRQyUpKbG5LlqAGPnIH4DA&ved=0CGMQ6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=Black and Blue: The Redd Foxx Story editor-last=Starr editor-first=Michael Seth work=Applause Theatre & Cinema year=2011 isbn=9781557838520 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref> The "Two Zephyrs" made the circuits together for over four years appearing with such notables as Lionel Hampton, Count Basie, Duke Ellington and many others.<ref name=laugh/>  With his team "Slappy" was first introduced to Californians, making his West Coast debut in Los Angeles at the Orpheum Theatre along with Louis Armstrong.

After "Two Zephyrs" came "Lewis and White" his second successful comedy team.<ref name=laugh/>  "Lewis and White" traveled together for several years and appeared with such greats as Johnny Otis, The Ink Spots, Count Basie, Duke Ellington and Lionel Hampton. Lewis and White made a television appearance on The Morey Amsterdam Show.<ref name=Foxx/>

The team with which he is best known is "Redd Foxx and Slappy White".  Foxx and White met in Harlem in 1947 and formed a comedy team.<ref name=Foxx/> They toured from coast to coast with the Billy Eckstine Orchestra for more than four years.<ref name=jokes/><ref name=laugh>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Hvl299p1Yg4C&pg=PA95&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=BRQyUpKbG5LlqAGPnIH4DA&ved=0CEUQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=Black Comedians on Black Comedy: How African-Americans Taught Us to Laugh editor-last=Littleton editor-first=Darryl publisher=Hal Leonard Corporation year=2008 isbn=9781557837301 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref> Some years before, White had met and married Pearl Bailey, when both of them were still relatively unknown professionally.<ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kUBLURuzOxEC&pg=PA107&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=8xwyUr410MSpAbu5gJAN&ved=0CD0Q6AEwBDge#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=Ebony date=April 1966 page=107 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3dXw6gR2GgkC&pg=PA37&dq=slappy+white+pearl+bailey&hl=en&sa=X&ei=Bi4yUuLPGsrgqAHp9IDQAw&ved=0CEsQ6AEwBQ#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white%20pearl%20bailey&f=false title=African-American Lives editor-last=Gates editor-first=Louis Henry editor2-last=Higginbotham editor2-first=Evelyn Brooks page=37 publisher=Oxford University Press year=2004 isbn=9780199882861 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref> Baileys career was on the way up.  She was performing in the better nightclubs with people like Duke Ellington and Cab Calloway while White was still struggling in lesser venues. The marriage was in trouble at the time White and Foxx first met.<ref name=Foxx/> The couple divorced.  Years later, when White and dancer Bill Bailey (dancer) Bill Bailey, who was Pearls brother, teamed up for an act, White joked that the act would be billed as "Rev. Bill Bailey and his ex-brother-in-law".<ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=C78DAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA65&dq=slappy+white+pearl+bailey&hl=en&sa=X&ei=7y4yUoHCHMKcqgHz-YCQDg&ved=0CC4Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white%20pearl%20bailey&f=false title=Jet page=65 date=September 30, 1954 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref>

"Slappys" solo career started in 1951 when Dinah Washington requested him to introduce her act at the Black Hawk (nightclub) Black Hawk in San Francisco.<ref name=jokes/>  White started out as a chauffeur to the singer, entertaining her with jokes as he drove.  When she was late arriving on stage one evening, Washington, worried about the waiting audience, asked White to go on stage and "say something funny" while she prepared for her performance.<ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GcIDAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA60&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=RB0yUqfFH4fgqAGCzYCwDw&ved=0CFEQ6AEwCTgo#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=Jet date=January 9, 1964 page=60 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref> "Slappy" was such a hit with the patrons, she kept him on as her opening act. He was once  also married to Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee and blues belter,  LaVern Baker (1929-1997).<ref name=Afro/><ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=SFdGAAAAIBAJ&sjid=h-UMAAAAIBAJ&pg=6417,1085273&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Lavern Baker marries comedian Slappy White date=February 21, 1959 work=Baltimore Afro-American page=4 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref>
 
White was a performer in Las Vegas for many years. In the 1950s while he was on the bill with Dinah Washington, White performed his routine which made the nightclubs manager the target of his jokes. While there was nothing offensive about the jokes White told, there was an unwritten rule about black comedians targeting whites in this manner.  The manager fired White, but changed his mind about the comedians employment when Dinah Washington indicated she would withdraw her entire act because of Whites firing.<ref name=NYT/> White spent four years with Dinah Washington before establishing himself as a solo act.<ref name=jokes/>

White wrote and performed a comedy routine called "Brotherhood Creed" using one black and one white glove while reciting his poem about equality between men. White performed the routine many times during the civil rights movement in America, and President John F. Kennedy once gave it a personal commendation.<ref name=Afro/> In 1965, White was invited to perform the "Brotherhood Creed" before the Massachusetts State Senate.<ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=S-MlAAAAIBAJ&sjid=__QFAAAAIBAJ&pg=925,31342&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Art Prysock, Slappy White, Sammy Davis bonanza page=6 date=January 5, 1965 work=Baltimore Afro-American accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> He was also well known as a comedian who eschewed offensive material in his nightclub act.<ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=3YclAAAAIBAJ&sjid=yPQFAAAAIBAJ&pg=2214,1395389&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Gregory Helped Us says Slappy White author=Young, Masco date=April 10, 1962 page=15 publisher=Washington Afro-American accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref>

In 1969, he formed a new comedy team called Rossi and White with Steve Rossi, who formerly worked with Marty Allen.<ref>{{cite news url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/courant/doc/550204367.html?FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:AI&type=historic&date=Aug%2017,%201969&author=&pub=Hartford%20Courant&edition=&startpage=&desc=My%20Favorite%20Jokes title=My Favorite Jokes work=The Hartford Courant date=August 17, 1969 page=31H accessdate=September 12, 2013}}"Steve Rossi and Slappy While are a brand-new comedy team, the first such integrated act in show business history, and they re off to a last ...(pay-per-view)</ref><ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=dlxQAAAAIBAJ&sjid=iFcDAAAAIBAJ&pg=6468,1906150&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Slappy White, Rossi Teamed date=November 8, 1971 page=4-A author=Harold, Chuck work=The Evening Independent accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=DIkbAAAAIBAJ&sjid=MlEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7453,5354309&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Rossi, White top team author=Borders, Myram date=October 28, 1971 work=The Pittsburgh Press page=32 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> The pair entertained at the White House in May of the same year.<ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=I7BBAAAAIBAJ&sjid=aqkMAAAAIBAJ&pg=5114,1343558&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=It Happened Last Night! author=Wilson, Earl page=10 date=April 24, 1969 work=Middlesboro Daily News accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> Rossi and White also performed their act aboard a National Overseas Airlines flight from Chicago to Spain.  The airline had hoped that providing live entertainment on their flights would boost ticket sales.<ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Z7EDAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA21&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=hh4yUpPGE4nqrAHPoICABw&ved=0CFEQ6AEwCTgy#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=Jet date= October 21, 1971 page=21 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref>

Later in life, he performed under his given name on his friend Redd Foxxs TV show Sanford and Son as Freds friend, also named Melvin, and had a million dollar contract to perform at the Flamingo Las Vegas Flamingo Hotel in Las Vegas in 1973.<ref name=Baltimore/><ref>{{cite web url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=lNlRAAAAIBAJ&sjid=SG4DAAAAIBAJ&pg=6784,4408390&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=The Two Sides of Redd Foxx work=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette page=14 date=October 14, 1991 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=http://articles.latimes.com/1995-11-09/news/mn-1205_1_slappy-white title=Slappy White:Gained Fame in White-Black Comedy Act author=Oliver, Myrna date=November 9, 1995 accessdate=September 12, 2013 work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref>

In 1986 Milton Berle planned to introduce a new situation comedy called Moscow & Vine, with White as his co-star on the show.  Whites role was to be that of Berles old vaudeville partner and as an employee in the music store owned by Berles character.  At one time, Berle hoped to syndicate the program.<ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=MUxTAAAAIBAJ&sjid=3YMDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3058,3735610&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=White and Berle in Moscow & Vine page=12C work=The Deseret News date=April 14, 1986 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref>

White was best known in later years for the New York Friars Club Friars Club Roast (comedy) roasts, where he routinely appeared along with other comedians, including Milton Berle, Jackie Vernon (comedian) Jackie Vernon, Pat Buttram and Dick Shawn.<ref>{{cite news url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=9NBRAAAAIBAJ&sjid=7m0DAAAAIBAJ&pg=6839,4858383&dq=slappy+white&hl=en title=Red Buttons Gets His Dinner author=Thomas, Bob date=November 19, 1982 work=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette page=34 accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> White enjoyed a minor renaissance after his death owing to bootleg recordings of Friars Club roasts that became available through comedy record outlets. His name was used on an episode of Seinfeld entitled "The Money", in which Seinfeld uses "Slappy White" as his own name.

==Death==
White died of a heart attack at his home in Brigantine, New Jersey, just 38 days after the death of his long-time friend George Kirby. At the time of his death, White was preparing to retire from acting and was in the process of moving from Los Angeles to New Jersey.<ref name=NYT/> He had no children from either of his marriages.<ref>{{cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IUEnJvdQup8C&pg=PA40&dq=slappy+white&hl=en&sa=X&ei=QhcyUu2zBcHYqgG614DYCQ&ved=0CFEQ6AEwBjgK#v=onepage&q=slappy%20white&f=false title=African-American Entertainment in Baltimore page=40 editor-last=Pryor-Trusty editor-first=Rosa editor2-last=Taliaferro editor2-first=Tonya publisher=Arcadia Publishing year=2003 isbn=9780738515137 accessdate=September 13, 2013}}</ref>
