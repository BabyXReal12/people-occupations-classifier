Sean Patrick Hayes<ref name="nyt-selling" /> (born June 26, 1970)<ref name="filmref" /> is an American actor, singer, comedian, and producer. He is best known for his role as Jack McFarland on the NBC sitcom Will & Grace, for which he won an Emmy Award, four SAG Awards, and one American Comedy Award, and earned six Golden Globe nominations.<ref name="actors">Stated in interview on Inside the Actors Studio.{{Verify-inline date=February 2015 comment=Needs episode and time mark.}}</ref>{{Verify-inline date=February 2015 comment=Needs episode and time mark.}} He also runs a television production company called Hazy Mills Productions, which produces shows such as Grimm (TV series) Grimm, Hot in Cleveland, The Soul Man, and Hollywood Game Night.

He is known for his film work in movies such as Billys Hollywood Screen Kiss, Cats & Dogs, Pieces of April, The Cat in the Hat (film) The Cat in the Hat, Win a Date with Tad Hamilton!, The Bucket List, and The Three Stooges (2012 film) The Three Stooges.

He is also known for his work on Broadway such as An Act of God and Promises, Promises (musical) Promises, Promises, where he played Chuck Baxter and received a nomination for Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actor in a Musical.

==Early life==
Hayes was born in Chicago, the youngest child of Mary Hayes (1939-2018),<ref>http://www.dailyherald.com/news/20180126/food-bank-leader---mother-of-sean-hayes---recalled-for-her-dedication</ref><ref name="lat-noteasy" /> the director of a non-profit food bank,<ref name="filmref" /> and Ronald Hayes, a lithographer.<ref name="filmref">{{cite web   title=Sean Hayes Biography   url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/68/Sean-Hayes.html   work=filmreference   year=2008   accessdate=June 11, 2010}}</ref>  He is of Irish people Irish descent and was raised as a Catholic Church Roman Catholic in the Chicago suburbs Chicago suburb of Glen Ellyn, Illinois. His father left when Hayes was five years old, and he and his siblings were raised by their mother.  He has not spoken to his father in many years.<ref name="nyt-selling" /><ref name="lat-noteasy">{{cite news   first=William   last=Keck   title=Its Not Easy Being Jack   url = http://articles.latimes.com/2000/dec/24/entertainment/ca-4036   work=The Los Angeles Times   date=December 24, 2000   archiveurl=http://www.angelfire.com/il3/rachelzworld/latimes.html   archivedate = December 27, 2000   accessdate=February 4, 2015 <!-- and also June 11, 2010 -->}}</ref>

After graduating from Glenbard West High School,<ref name="dh-palteam">{{cite news   url = http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20130122/news/701229961/   title = Sean Hayes, suburban pal team up for TV success   date = 2013-01-22 <!-- 11:56 AM -->   first1 = Jamie   last1 = Sotonoff   first2 = Dann   last2 = Gire   work = Daily Herald (Arlington Heights) Daily Herald   location = Arlington Heights, Illinois   accessdate = 2015-02-05 }}</ref> Hayes attended Illinois State University,<ref name="lat-noteasy" /> where he studied piano performance.<ref name="pantagraph-honorary" />  He left "two or three classes short" of graduation when he became music director at the Pheasant Run Theater in St. Charles, Illinois.<ref name="pantagraph-honorary" />

Hayes worked as a classical music classical pianist.<ref>{{cite book last=Marshall first=Corinne title=The Q Guide to Will and Grace: Stuff You Didnt Even Know You Wanted to Know... About Will, Grace, Jack, Karen, and Lots of Guest Stars url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HikbAQAAIAAJ year=2008 publisher=Alyson Publications isbn=978-1-59350-083-2 page=79 quote=In the early days of his career, he supported himself as a classical pianist.}}</ref>  He practiced improv at The Second City in Chicago.<ref name="advocate-iam">{{cite news   first=Ari  last=Karpel   title=Sean Hayes: I Am Who I Am   url=http://www.advocate.com/arts-entertainment/people/2010/03/08/sean-hayes-i-am-who-i-am   work=The Advocate   date=April 2010   accessdate=February 22, 2014}}</ref>  He also composed original music for a production of Antigone (Sophocles) Antigone at the Steppenwolf Theatre Company in Chicago.<ref>{{cite book last=Magazine first=People title=People: Almanac 2003 url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RMFlLXWVZr8C year=2002 publisher=Cader Books isbn=978-1-929049-96-7 page=380 quote=Composed original music for a Steppenwolf production of Antigone.}}</ref> He moved to Los Angeles in 1995,<ref name="advocate-iam" /> where he found work as a Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian and an actor on stage and {{citation needed date=February 2015}} on television, including a television commercial commercial for Doritos<ref name="advocate-iam" /> which aired during Super Bowl XXXII in 1998.

==Career==
As a teenager, Hayes was an extra in the film Lucas (film) Lucas (1986), which was filmed at Glenbard West High School his high school. He made his professional debut in the independent film Billys Hollywood Screen Kiss (1998), which brought him wide attention. The same year, he was cast as Jack McFarland, a flamboyantly gay and frequently unemployed actor, in the NBC comedy series Will & Grace. The show became a long-running hit and Hayes performance earned him seven consecutive Emmy Award nominations as Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series. He won the award for his first nomination.<ref name="actors" />{{Verify-inline date=February 2015 comment=Needs episode and time mark.}}  He was also nominated for six Golden Globe Awards for his performance.

Hayes also made film appearances in Cats & Dogs (2001), as Jerry Lewis in Martin and Lewis (film) Martin and Lewis (2002), Wayne in Pieces of April (2003), The Cat in the Hat (film) The Cat in the Hat (2003), and Win a Date With Tad Hamilton! (2004). He was also the voice of Brain in the 2008 film Igor (film) Igor, and has guest starred in television shows such as Scrubs (TV series) Scrubs and 30 Rock. In 2005, he was executive producer for Bravo (U.S. TV channel) Bravos Situation: Comedy, a reality television series about sitcoms. He also executive produced The Sperm Donor and Stephens Life, the two winning scripts that were chosen by NBC. He guest-starred in 2006 in the Adult Swim Cameo appearance cameo-filled show Tom Goes to the Mayor.{{citation needed date=February 2015}}

Hayes appeared as Thomas in the film The Bucket List (2007).  On July 5, 2008, he made his New York stage debut as Mr. Applegate / Devil in New York City Centers Encores! production of Damn Yankees.<ref name="nyt-selling">{{cite news   first=Celia   last=McGee   title=Selling His Soul for the Part    url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/06/theater/06mcge.html?8dpc=&_r=3&adxnnl=1&oref=slogin&adxnnlx=1218596404-GpRhqaBreKmsEu7JVLJb1Q   work=The New York Times   date=July 6, 2008   accessdate=June 11, 2010}}</ref>

He also appeared as Mr. Hank Hummerfloob and the voice of "The Fish" in The Cat in the Hat (film) The Cat in the Hat.  In a 2008 New York Times interview, Hayes talked about a television project, BiCoastal, about "a guy with a wife and kids in California and a boyfriend in New York"<ref name="nyt-selling" /> for Showtime (TV network) Showtime.<ref>{{cite news   url = http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/new-deal-cbs-swinger-118540 <!-- formerly http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3i0c5cda0b44613d02796e5cff77532920 as of 2008-10-24 -->   title = New deal for CBS swinger   work = The Hollywood Reporter   last = Andreeva   first = Nellie   agency = Associated Press   date = September 4, 2008   accessdate = 2015-02-05 }}</ref> He made his Broadway debut in the April 2010 Broadway revival of the musical Promises, Promises (musical) Promises, Promises. He received a nomination for the Drama League Award for Distinguished Performance, and was nominated for a Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical.

Hayes was host of the 64th Tony Awards 64th Annual Tony Awards on June 13, 2010 on CBS.<ref>{{cite news   first=Gina  last=DiNunno   url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Sean-Hayes-Tonys-1018881.aspx   title=Sean Hayes to Host the Tonys   work=TVGuide   date=May 24, 2010   accessdate=June 11, 2010}}</ref> In 2010, he reprised the role of Mr. Tinkles, the evil white Persian cat, in Cats & Dogs: The Revenge of Kitty Galore. On November 15, 2010, he appeared in a satirical PSA for the repeal of Dont Ask, Dont Tell on The Daily Show with Jon Stewart. He played Larry Fine in the film The Three Stooges (2012 film) The Three Stooges (2012).<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.atlantadowntown.com/_files/docs/three_stooges.pdf title=Filming at 5 Points Sports Building – Downtown Atlanta/Fairlie Poplar District publisher=atlantadowntown.com date=April 28, 2011 accessdate=June 27, 2011}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hayes refused to discuss his sexual orientation for many years, saying he believed that audiences would therefore be more open-minded about his characters.<ref>{{cite interview   subject=Sean Hayes   interviewer=Terry Gross   title=Will And Grace Star Sean Hayes Steps To Broadway   url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=126219932   type=Interview: audio/transcript   work=Fresh Air   publisher=NPR / WHYY   date=April 29, 2010   accessdate=June 11, 2010}}</ref> In a 2010 interview with The Advocate, he appeared to imply that he is Homosexuality gay, stating: "Really? Youre gonna shoot the gay guy down? I never have had a problem saying who I am. I am who I am." He also indicated that he was in a relationship. He states that he feels like he has "contributed monumentally to the success of the gay movement in America, and if anyone wants to argue that, Im open to it".<ref name="advocate-iam" />

He received an honorary degree honorary Doctor of Philosophy PhD from Illinois State University in February 2013.<ref name="pantagraph-honorary">{{cite web last=Sobota  first=Lenore  url=http://www.pantagraph.com/news/local/education/actor-sean-hayes-former-fairleigh-dickinson-president-receive-honorary-degrees/article_dd585e18-7c84-11e2-98b8-001a4bcf887a.html  title=Actor Sean Hayes, former Fairleigh Dickinson president receive honorary degrees  publisher=Pantagraph.com  date=2013-02-21  accessdate=2014-02-06}}</ref>

In November 2014 Hayes announced that he had married his partner of eight years, Scott Icenogle.<ref>{{cite web   first=Cavan  last=Sieczkowski   title=Sean Hayes Marries Longtime Partner Scott Icenogle   url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/11/20/sean-hayes-marries_n_6192854.html   work=The Huffington Post   date=November 2014   accessdate=November 20, 2014}}</ref>

In October 2017, while appearing on an episode of The Ellen DeGeneres Show, Hayes revealed that he had been hospitalized with a rare condition in which his small intestine burst. The intestine was successfully repaired.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sean-hayes-was-hospitalized-his-small-intestine-burst-1051703 title=Sean Hayes Was Hospitalized After His Small Intestine Burst work=The Hollywood Reporter access-date=2017-10-26 language=en}}</ref>
