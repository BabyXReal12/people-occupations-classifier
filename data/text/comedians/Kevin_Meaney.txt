Kevin Gerard Meaney (April 23, 1956 – October 21, 2016) was an American stand-up comedian and actor.

==Career==
Meaney began his career in comedy in 1980. His big break into mainstream culture is considered to be his first HBO comedy special in 1986, followed by his debut performance on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson in 1987.<ref name="typepad1">{{cite news work=The Miami Herald  url=http://miamiherald.typepad.com/gaysouthflorida/2009/10/comic-kevin-meaney-shuts-door-on-his-closet-life.html title=Comic Kevin Meaney Shuts Door On His Closet Life date=2012-01-30 accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref>

After that, his act was broadcast several times by HBO, Comedy Central and several network television stations with appearances on The Tonight Show, Late Night with David Letterman, Live with Kelly#1988–00: Live! with Regis and Kathie Lee Live! with Regis and Kathie Lee, The Oprah Winfrey Show and Conan (TV Series) Conan.<ref name="sun-sentinel1996">{{cite news first=Jeff last=Rusnak url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1996-03-22/entertainment/9603200429_1_kevin-meaney-peanut-las-vegas title=Kevin Meaney Turns Lifes Mishaps Into His Shtick work=Sun Sentinel date=1996-03-22 accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.kevinmeaney.com title=Official Website Of Kevin Meaney publisher=Kevinmeaney.com accessdate=2013-12-05 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/19991128053134/http://kevinmeaney.com/ archivedate=1999-11-28 df=}}</ref>

His most famous catchphrase was "Thats not right!", delivered while doing an impression of his mother, which was followed by, and usually preceded by, her complaints and remonstrations. Typically, his act consisted of commentary about his family and complaints about hotel service. Often, Meaney closed his show with a rendition of the 1985 song "We Are the World" which included comical impressions of the various singers who originally sang the song.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.newportri.com/features/comedy/happy-and-he-knows-it/article_7290059e-5d87-11e1-9516-001871e3ce6c.html title=Happy And He Knows It publisher=NewportRI.com date=2012-02-22 accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref>

He often ended his performance with a few jokes that intentionally would not get a good response in order to follow them up with a song reminiscent of "I Dont Care" by Jean Lenox and Harry O. Sutton sung about how he doesnt care whether the audience laughs at his jokes.{{citation needed date=October 2016}}

Meaney was involved in a number of television programs, including Ned & Stacey, Dr. Katz, Professional Therapist Dr. Katz, Space Ghost Coast to Coast, Garfield and Friends, Rockos Modern Life, London Underground, and Duckman. He starred as the title character on the short-lived sitcom version of Uncle Buck (1990 TV series) Uncle Buck.

He was also a singer and musician, writing and producing songs for HBO and Comedy Central with his co-writer Martin Olson, with whom he wrote several television series. He intermittently appeared on Jay Thomas The Jay Thomas Show as a co-host. In 1996, he wrote and performed a one-man play titled Vegas Vows based loosely on his brief marriage to a woman he had just met.<ref>{{cite news last=Deffaa first=Chip url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,293272,00.html title=A Veiled Threat publisher=Ew.com date=1996-07-12 accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref>

For seven years in the 2000s, Meaney performed in various roles in the Broadway musical Hairspray (musical) Hairspray.

==Personal life==
Meaney was born the third child of five in White Plains, New York. When he was 39 years old, he married a woman he had just met in Las Vegas. The marriage was annulled shortly afterwards.<ref name="sun-sentinel1996"/> He later married television executive Mary Ann Halford and they had one daughter.<ref name="typepad1"/>

In 2002, Meaney was arrested at the San Francisco International Airport. After his wife set off a metal detector and lifted her shirt high enough to expose her bra, he reportedly got belligerent and was asked twice not to film the security operations of the terminal. A scuffle with police ensued.<ref>{{cite news author=Henry K. Lee  url=http://www.sfgate.com/crime/article/Security-no-joke-for-comic-arrested-at-SFO-2867148.php  title=Security No Joke For Comic Arrested At SFO  publisher=Sfgate.com  date=2002-03-05  accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref>

On XM Radios "Stand Up Sit Down" on May 5, 2008, Meaney stated publicly that he was gay.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.kevinmeaney.com/bio.html title=Kevin Meany biography publisher=Kevinmeaney.com date=2008-05-28 accessdate=2013-12-05 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161025171913/http://www.kevinmeaney.com/bio.html archivedate=2016-10-25 df=}}</ref> He explained that his time on Broadway was where he gained the courage to accept his homosexuality.<ref>{{cite news url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0911/24/joy.01.html title=Joy Behar Show Aired November 24, 2009 publisher=Transcripts.cnn.com accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref> Soon after, he and his wife divorced.<ref name="typepad1"/>

==Death==
Meaney died on October 21, 2016, at age 60 and was unresponsive when found in his home in Forestburgh, New York.<ref name=NYTObit>{{cite news author=Rosenberg, Eli date=22 October 2016 title=Kevin Meaney, Veteran Stand-Up Comedian, Dies at 60 url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/23/arts/television/kevin-meaney-dead.html newspaper=The New York Times location=New York City accessdate= 24 October 2016}}</ref> According to Eastern Daily News the autopsy was inconclusive but shows that he died from a heart attack.<ref>{{cite web work=Eastern News Daily url=https://easterndaily.com/kevin-meaney-dead-uncle-buck-cause-of-death/ title=Kevin Meaney From ‘Uncle Buck’ Dies, Cause Of Death Revealed author=Johnson, Victor date=October 25, 2016}}</ref>
