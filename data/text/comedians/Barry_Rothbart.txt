Barry Rothbart (born May 25, 1983{{citation needed date=January 2015}}) is an American comedian, actor and writer. He currently splits his time between Los Angeles and New York City. He was named one of Variety (magazine) Variety magazines "10 Comics to Watch" in 2013.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2013/film/features/how-barry-rothbarts-ad-lib-skills-impressed-scorsese-1200564542/ title=How Barry Rothbarts Ad Lib Skills Impressed Martin Scorsese work=Variety (magazine) Variety first=Iain last=Blair date=July 17, 2013}}</ref> He played the role of Peter Deblasio in 2013s The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film) The Wolf of Wall Street and currently plays Kevin on ABCs Downward Dog (TV series) Downward Dog.

== Early life ==
Rothbart was born in Forest Hills in Queens, NY.<ref name="BA">{{cite web title=BA #240: Barry Rothbart url=http://boxangeles.com/2018/08/13/ba-240-barry-rothbart/ website=the Box Angeles podcast date=August 13, 2018}}</ref> Rothbarts father was fired from his job for embezzling money, would later bet his life savings on the Red Sox, losing it all in the 1986 World Series, at which his point his mother left his father.<ref name="BA"/> As a result, Rothbart said on This Is Not Happening with Ari Shaffir that his father was a bookie for the Gambino Crime Family but claimed to be a music producer.<ref name="BA"/><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z0zDGie5jpo title=This Is Not Happening - Barry Rothbart - Going to Atlantic City with Papa - Uncensored first= last=Comedy Central date=February 13, 2015 publisher= via=YouTube}}</ref> Rothbart would late do random jobs for his fathers illegal sports betting office.<ref name="BA"/>

Rothbart briefly attended University of Massachusetts Lowell UMass Lowell and started doing stand up there.<ref name="BA"/> He later transferred to Pace University to study business, but left there after a year to focus on film at Hofstra University.<ref name="BA"/>

Rothbart would then take classes at Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre before focusing on stand-up.<ref name="BA"/> After seven years performing around NYC, he decided to move to Los Angeles.<ref name="BA"/>

He is Jewish.<ref>https://twitter.com/barryrothbart/status/62570851157024768</ref>

== Career ==
Rothbart started out in commercials and found some early success there.<ref name="BA"/>

In 2011, Rothbart made his stand up television debut on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno.<ref>{{cite web url=http://splitsider.com/2013/08/talking-to-barry-rothbart/  title=Talking to Barry Rothbart about Leno work=Splitsider first=Phil last=Davidson date=August 27, 2013 accessdate=June 28, 2014}}</ref> In 2013, he was named one of the "Top Ten Comics to Watch" by Variety (magazine) Variety magazine.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.barryrothbart.com  title=Barry Rothbart  publisher=Barry Rothbart  accessdate=September 5, 2013}}</ref>

In 2012, Rothbart landed a role on the revival of Punkd but was fired after four episodes as a result of his management team leaking he had landed the role.<ref name="BA"/>

In 2013 he landed the role of Peter Deblasio in Martin Scorseses The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film) The Wolf of Wall Street. In June 2014, a show he wrote, 300 Sunnyside, was picked up by Comedy Central to air on their fall schedule of that year.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/comedy-central-renews-inside-amy-710369  title=Comedy Central Renews Inside Amy Schumer, Orders Two Series  work=The Hollywood Reporter first=Lesley last=Goldberg date=June 9, 2014 accessdate=June 28, 2014}}</ref> Later that year, he released an album, Streets of Fire, on AST Records.<ref>{{cite web url=http://splitsider.com/2014/12/keeping-busy-with-barry-rothbart/ title=Keeping Busy with Barry Rothbart work=Splitsider first=Phil last=Davidson date=December 1, 2014}}</ref> He later played Kevin in the new ABC comedy Downward Dog (TV series) Downward Dog.
