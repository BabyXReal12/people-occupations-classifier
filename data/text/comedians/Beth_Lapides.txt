
Beth Lapides ({{IPAc-en l ə ˈ p iː d ʌ s}}; {{respell lə PEE dəs}}) is an entertainer, writer, and comedian.<ref>[http://www2.uclaextension.edu/writers/instructors.php?recordID=349  UCLA Extension Writers program, instructor biography] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100705230158/http://www2.uclaextension.edu/writers/instructors.php?recordID=349  date=July 5, 2010 }}</ref>

==Art==
Lapides began her career as a visual and performance artist in New York.<ref>[https://www.jstor.org/pss/1145599 Amy C. Ward, "Beth Lapides A Good American Novel at P.S. 122", The Drama Review, spring 1985, pp. 41-44, available at jstor.org (fee required for full article)]</ref> She continues to exhibit in group shows.

==Un-Cabaret==
By 1990 Lapides and her partner and husband Greg Miller had moved to Los Angeles.  In 1993 they created Un-Cabaret, a live show that became an important venue for the Los Angeles "alternative comedy" movement.<ref>Lisa Rosen, [http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/la-et-un-cabaret-20120305,0,5986877.story "Comedy and music fill the bill at revived Un-Cabaret"], Los Angeles Times, March 5, 2012.</ref><ref name="Rosen">Lisa Rosen, [http://articles.latimes.com/2007/may/17/news/wk-uncabaret17 " Good, honest humor: At Un-Cabaret, theres no joking around, just personal truths."] Los Angeles Times, May 17, 2007.</ref><ref name="Weinraub">Bernard Weinraub, [https://www.nytimes.com/2001/03/05/arts/tired-of-the-same-old-jokes-so-is-this-festival.html "Tired of the Same Old Jokes? So Is This Festival"], The New York Times, March 5, 2001.</ref>  Notable shows that emerged from the Un-Cabaret included Julia Sweeneys God Said, Ha! and Laura Kightlingers book Quick Shots of False Hope.<ref name=Rosen/>  Spin-off productions from the Un-Cabaret have included Say the Word, a story-telling show; The Other Network, a show featuring unaired pilots, and the Un-Cab Lab, a student workshop.<ref name="Rosen"/>

==Did I Wake You?==
Lapidess first book Did I Wake You?--Haikus for Modern Living was published in 2007.<ref name="Rosen"/>

==Theatre==
Lapides has created several one-person shows and performance pieces, including a satirical 1992 campaign to be elected First Lady,<ref>Judith Michaelson, [http://articles.latimes.com/1992-03-21/entertainment/ca-4023_1_beth-lapides " Beth Lapides: The Jokes on Voters. Comedy: Performance artist kicks off shtick to make Americas top female position elective."], Los Angeles Times, March 21, 1992.</ref><ref>Elizabeth Gleick, [http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20108845,00.html "Loose Cannons"], People (magazine) People, October 19, 1992.</ref> a 2007 show based on her book of haiku,<ref name="Rosen"/> and a recent production entitled 100% Happy 88% of the Time.<ref>[http://www.boston.com/ae/events/articles/2010/07/22/nightlife_the_week_ahead/ "100& Happy 88% of the Time"], Boston Globe, July 22, 2010.</ref>
