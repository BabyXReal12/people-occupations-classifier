Beth Lapides ({{IPAc-en l ə ˈ p iː d ʌ s}}; {{respell lə PEE dəs}}) is an entertainer, writer, and comedian.
==Art==
Lapides began her career as a visual and performance artist in New York.
She continues to exhibit in group shows.

==Un-Cabaret==
By 1990 Lapides and her partner and husband Greg Miller had moved to Los Angeles.  In 1993 they created Un-Cabaret, a live show that became an important venue for the Los Angeles "alternative comedy" movement.
Notable shows that emerged from the Un-Cabaret included Julia Sweeneys God Said, Ha! and Laura Kightlingers book Quick Shots of False Hope.
