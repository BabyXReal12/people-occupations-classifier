==Early life==
Friedlander was born in Gaithersburg, Maryland, the son of Shirley and Art Friedlander.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=TRIBLIVE/> His father was of Russian Jewish descent and his mother, a native of Pittsburgh, is Croatian American.<ref name=TRIBLIVE/><ref name=NYMAG>{{cite news  title=Judah Friedlander Thinks Manhattan Pizza Has ‘Gone Downhill’  first=Alexandra  last=Vallis  url=http://newyork.grubstreet.com/2009/05/friedlander.html  newspaper=New York Magazine  date=May 22, 2009  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.jewish-theatre.com/visitor/article_display.aspx?articleID=2517  title=The next generation of Jewish Humor in America  date=October 2007  work=City Pages  accessdate=February 19, 2013  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130127101040/http://www.jewish-theatre.com/visitor/article_display.aspx?articleID=2517  archivedate=January 27, 2013  df= }}</ref>

As a child, Friedlander drew political cartoons and made animated films.<ref name=ANNARBOR>{{cite web  url=http://www.annarbor.com/entertainment/30-rocks-judah-friedlander-offers-his-world-champion-stand-up-on-thursday/  title=30 Rocks Judah Friedlander offering his World Champion stand-up in Ann Arbor  author=Jenn McKee  date=April 9, 2012  work=AnnArbor.com  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> He first tried stand-up comedy in 1989 at the age of 19 while attending New York University.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=HOLLYREP/><ref name=PUNCH/><ref name=PHILLY>{{cite news  title=Judah Friedlander Likes Hats  first=Joel  last=Mathis  url=http://www.philadelphiaweekly.com/screen/tv/Judah-Friedlander-Likes-Hats-42379727.html  newspaper=Philadelphia Weekly  date=April 3, 2009  accessdate=October 3, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130411151627/http://www.philadelphiaweekly.com/screen/tv/Judah-Friedlander-Likes-Hats-42379727.html  archivedate=April 11, 2013  df= }}</ref>

==Career==

===Stand-up comedy===
Friedlander has performed stand-up comedy regularly throughout his entertainment career.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=GOTHAMIST/><ref name=PUNCH/> He continued to perform stand-up several nights a week in New York City while working on the sitcom 30 Rock.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=GOTHAMIST/> Friedlander has stated that he is most relaxed when doing stand-up<ref name=HOLLYREP/> and that he considers stand-up to be his first true love,<ref name=TORSTAR>{{cite news  title=Judah Friedlander: Hats off to standup guy  first=Raju  last=Mudhar  url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/television/article/502734--judah-friedlander-hats-off-to-standup-guy  newspaper=Toronto Star  date=September 21, 2008  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> preferring it to acting.<ref name=VULTURE>{{cite web  url=http://www.vulture.com/2010/09/30_rocks_judah_friedlander.html  title=30 Rock’s Judah Friedlander on Karate and the Hat That Was Too Hot for NBC  author=Alexandra Martell  date=September 23, 2010  work=Vulture  publisher=New York Magazine  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> He stated in 2012 that stand-up comedy is "still the most important thing I do from an artistic standpoint."<ref name=CITYPAGES>{{cite web  url=http://blogs.citypages.com/dressingroom/2012/04/judah_friedlander_world_champion_standup.php  title=Judah Friedlander on comedy, ladies, and karate  author=Patrick Strait  date=April 9, 2012  work=City Pages  accessdate=October 3, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120918221026/http://blogs.citypages.com/dressingroom/2012/04/judah_friedlander_world_champion_standup.php  archivedate=September 18, 2012  df= }}</ref>

Friedlander has cited comedians Steven Wright, Sam Kinison and John Mulrooney as influences.<ref name=KANSAS>{{cite news  title=After ‘30 Rock,’ comic Judah Friedlander is ready for new projects  first=Timothy  last=Finn  url=http://www.kansascity.com/2013/02/06/4050785/judah-friedlander-steps-off-the.html#storylink=cpy  newspaper=The Kansas City Star  date=February 6, 2013  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> He also says that Dave Attell is one of his favorite comics.<ref name=KANSAS/>

Friedlanders comedic performances often feature him making outrageous and bombastic claims about himself with a deadpan delivery.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=NYT2/><ref name=TRIBLIVE/><ref name=TORSTAR/> Many of these claims are reflected in slogans displayed on the many trucker hats he wears — the most common being "World Champion", a slogan he has worn in various languages.<ref name=NYT2/><ref name=PLAIN/><ref name=HOLLYREP/><ref name=NYMAG/><ref name=ANNARBOR/>

One of his claims include being an expert in karate, having achieved an "extra-dark Black belt (martial arts) black belt" in the sport.<ref name=GOTHAMIST/><ref name=THEGRID/><ref name=WASHTIMES>{{cite news  title=Get Out: Judah Friedlander  first=Mike  last=Riggs  url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2011/nov/24/get-out-judah-friedlander/  newspaper=The Washington Times  date=November 24, 2011  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> In 2010, he authored the humor book How to Beat Up Anybody: An Instructional and Inspirational Karate Book by the World Champion.<ref name=NBCBIO/><ref name=ANNARBOR/> Friedlanders claims provide comedic irony when contrasted against his nonchalant attitude and physical appearance.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=PLAIN/> Friedlander wears large, thick-rimmed glasses, is often unshaven and is generally unkempt.<ref name=NYT1/><ref name=IFC/><ref name=ANNARBOR/>  His physique has also been described as "doughy".<ref name=PLAIN/><ref name=TRIBLIVE/>

The look that Friedlander employs during his stand-up has also carried over into some of his television and film roles, including his 30 Rock role as TV writer Frank Rossitano.<ref name=THEGRID/><ref name=ANNARBOR/> Frank is however much lazier than Friedlanders "World Champion" persona.<ref name=THEGRID/><ref name=TIMEOUT>{{cite web  url=http://www.timeoutchicago.com/arts-culture/comedy/15255886/judah-friedlander-at-mayne-stage-comedy-review  title=Judah Friedlander at Mayne Stage  author=Jenna Marotta  date=April 12, 2012  work=Time Out Chicago  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref>

In 2007, Heeb magazine included Friedlander in their list of "100 people you need to know about".<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.jewish-theatre.com/visitor/article_display.aspx?articleID=2517  title=The next generation of Jewish Humor in America  date=October 2007  work=All About Jewish Theatre  accessdate=February 19, 2013  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130127101040/http://www.jewish-theatre.com/visitor/article_display.aspx?articleID=2517  archivedate=January 27, 2013  df= }}</ref>

===Film and video===
In 2001, Friedlander played "the hug guy" in the music video for the Dave Matthews Band single "Everyday (Dave Matthews Band song) Everyday"; his character is seen hugging various people throughout the video.<ref name=NYT2/><ref name=TRIBLIVE/><ref name=PUNCH/> Friedlander was recognized for his "hug guy" role for years after appearing in the video, causing complete strangers to offer him hugs.<ref name=NYT2/>  The same year, he appeared in the comedy film Wet Hot American Summer.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=IMDB/>

In 2003 Friedlander played Toby Radloff in the biopic American Splendor (film) American Splendor, a role that received favorable reviews — his performance was deemed "unforgettable"<ref name=NYTBIO/> by The New York Times and "pitch-perfect" by both Nick A. Zaino III of The Boston Globe<ref name=BOSTON/> and Dan Dinicola of The Daily Gazette.<ref>{{cite news  title=American Splendor as delightful as oddball subject Pekar  first=Dan  last=Dinicola  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=fN8qAAAAIBAJ&sjid=UIgFAAAAIBAJ&pg=2396,2602290&dq=judah-friedlander+american-splendor&hl=en  date=September 12, 2003  accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref> He was also nominated in the category of best supporting actor at the 2004 Independent Spirit Awards.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=NYT2/><ref name=POSTGAZ/>

Friedlander had roles in the Ben Stiller films Meet the Parents, Zoolander and Along Came Polly.<ref name=NYTBIO/><ref name=IMDB/>

He had a starring role in the 2005 independent comedy Full Grown Men and a supporting role in the 2008 drama The Wrestler (2008 film) The Wrestler.<ref name=IMDB/><ref name=NBCBIO/><ref name=THEGRID/>

He has also appeared in films such as Duane Hopwood, Chapter 27, Southern Belles, Live Free or Die (2006 film) Live Free or Die, and Feast (2005 film) Feast.<ref name=IMDB/><ref name=NBCBIO/>

==Personal life==
Friedlander has lived in the New York City area over most of his career, with the exception of two years in Los Angeles.<ref name=GOTHAMIST/> Currently residing in Queens,<ref name=HOLLYREP/><ref name=CITYPAGES/> he has stated that he prefers living in New York.<ref name=GOTHAMIST/>

Friedlander enjoys playing ping-pong.<ref name=GOTHAMIST/><ref name=IFC/><ref name=HOLLYREP/><ref name=NYMAG/> As a child he had played the sport competitively, and he took it up again in 2008.<ref name=GOTHAMIST/><ref name=IFC/> He is a big fan of professional wrestling, which is often used as subject matter for his trademark hats and shirts. He is also a fan of the New York Red Bulls, he has attended games at Red Bull Arena (New Jersey) Red Bull Arena, and has appeared in commercials for the team.<ref name="Major League Soccer">{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWMOp9r0kPY title=Judah Friedlander at LA vs NY website=YouTube publisher=Major League Soccer}}</ref>
