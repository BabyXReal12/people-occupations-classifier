
Craig Phillip Robinson (born October 25, 1971) is an American actor, comedian, and singer. He is best known for his role as Darryl Philbin on The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office (2005–2013) and has appeared in numerous other television shows and films including Pineapple Express (film) Pineapple Express (2008), Zack and Miri Make a Porno (2008), Hot Tub Time Machine (2010), Brooklyn Nine-Nine (2013–), This Is the End (2013), and Sausage Party (2016).

==Early life==
Robinson was born in Chicago, Illinois, to a music teacher mother and an attorney father.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2009-03-11/news/0903110158_1_mug-chicago-roots-horace-mann   work=Chicago Tribune   title=5 questions for Craig Robinson   date=11 March 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.timeoutchicago.com/arts-culture/movies-on-demand/14733133/craig-robinson-on-the-office-interview  title=Craig Robinson on The Office &#124; Interview  publisher=Timeoutchicago.com  date=2011-04-26  accessdate=2015-01-29}}</ref> He was raised on the South Side of Chicago, and was brought up Methodist.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dailynews.com/entertainment/ci_23404916/apocalypse-and-office-craig-robinson-ends-world-twice  title=Apocalypse and The Office: Craig Robinson ends the world. Twice  publisher=Dailynews.com  date=  accessdate=2015-01-29}}</ref> He attended Whitney M. Young Magnet High School. He received his undergraduate degree from Illinois State University in 1994, and his masters of education from Saint Xavier University. He was a music teacher at Horace Mann Elementary School in Chicago.<ref name="Illinois State Alumni Magazine">{{cite web title=Illinois State Alumni Magazine url=http://advancement.illinoisstate.edu/news/downloads/Illinois-State-Alumni-Magazine-vol10no3.pdf publisher=Illinois State University volume=Winter 2009/2010  page=29}}</ref>

==Career==

===Acting and comedy===
Robinson began doing stand-up and taking classes in improv and acting at The Second City in Chicago. Beginning in 2005, he portrayed Darryl Philbin on the American version of The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office, and was promoted to a starring role in the fourth season. He has appeared on other television shows, including Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development, Lucky (TV series) Lucky, Friends, Halfway Home (TV series) Halfway Home, and Reno 911!. He appeared in the music video for the Red Hot Chili Peppers song "Hump de Bump". He also hosted the seventh season of Last Comic Standing. He voiced Cookie in Shrek Forever After (2010) and played Reg Mackworthy on the HBO hit series Eastbound & Down (2009–2012).

Robinson is a frequent collaborator with Seth Rogen, starring alongside Rogen in the films Knocked Up (2007), Pineapple Express (film) Pineapple Express (2008), Zack and Miri Make a Porno (2008), This Is the End (2013) and Sausage Party (2016).

He also appeared in what was then the highest grossing Korean film , "D-War" (Korean: 디워, released in North America as Dragon Wars: D-War.)

Making a departure from his comedy background, Robinson portrayed saxophonist Maceo Parker in the James Brown biopic Get On Up (film) Get On Up (2014). In December 2015, he appeared in several Wal-Mart commercials promoting their credit card while singing and playing the piano at the store.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.ispot.tv/ad/AA9G/walmart-credit-card-satchel-featuring-craig-robinson title=Walmart Credit Card TV Commercial, Satchel Featuring Craig Robinson website=iSpot.tv}}</ref> He received rave reviews for the A24 (company) A24 drama Morris from America (2016),<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/morris_from_america/ title=Morris From America (2016) publisher=Rotten Tomatoes  accessdate=August 12, 2017}}</ref> earning a nomination for the Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Male.

===Music===
File:Craig Robinson 141001-A-QR427-487 (cropped).jpg thumb Robinson performing in 2014
For a number of years, Robinson and comedian Jerry Minor had a comedy act where they performed as musical duo L. Witherspoon & Chucky, with Minor as smooth singing L. Witherspoon and Robinson as Chucky, the keyboard player and back-up singer. They performed the act on HBOs Sketch Pad, several stand-up shows on Comedy Central, Real Time with Bill Maher, and Jimmy Kimmel Live!<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ty8BrKtk_u0  title=L. Witherspoon & Chucky on Real Time  publisher=YouTube.com  date=2007-05-24  accessdate=2015-01-29}}</ref> He occasionally performs with his band, The Nasty Delicious, alongside his brother Chris Rob. They were featured as his band in an episode of The Office, and on the season finale of the seventh season of Last Comic Standing. He has also performed in character in multiple episodes of The Office, and was featured on the Hot Tub Time Machine soundtrack, singing "Jessies Girl" and "Lets Get It Started." He was also in the film Miss March, playing the role of the hit rapper Phil, also known as Horsedick.MPEG. Robinson produced two songs from that same film.

==Personal life==
In June 2008, Robinson was arrested in Culver City, California for possession of MDMA and methamphetamine.<ref name="2008drugs">{{cite news title=Office actor Craig Robinson faces drug charges url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/topstories/2008-08-15-119379010_x.htm accessdate=27 November 2017 work=USA Today}}</ref> He was charged with two felonies but the charges were dismissed after he completed a diversion program.<ref name="bahamas" /> In October 2013, Robinson was arrested by the Royal Bahamas Police Force and fined $1,000 by a Bahamas court for possession of a half gram of marijuana and 18 MDMA pills.<ref name="bahamas">{{cite news title=Office actor Craig Robinson fined in minor drug bust url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/people/2013/10/10/actor-craig-robinson-in-minor-bahamas-drug-bust/2957887/ accessdate=27 November 2017 work=USA Today agency=Associated Press date=October 13, 2013 language=en}}</ref>
