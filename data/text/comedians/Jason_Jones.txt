==Life and career==
Jones was born and raised in Hamilton, Ontario.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.macleans.ca/society/life/five-american-broadcast-personalities-you-might-not-know-are-canadian/  title=Five American broadcast personalities who are Canadian  publisher=Macleans  date=December 21, 2012  accessdate=March 21, 2015 }}</ref> He attended Hill Park Secondary School and then Ryerson University Ryerson Theatre School in Toronto. Jones is married to Samantha Bee, the host of Full Frontal with Samantha Bee and fellow former The Daily Show correspondent, with whom he has three children: daughter Piper Bee-Jones (born 2006), son, Fletcher Bee-Jones (born 2008), and daughter, Ripley Bee-Jones (born 2010). In 2014, he became a United States citizen.<ref>Lisa de Moraes, [http://deadline.com/2014/10/jon-stewart-sick-jason-jones-daily-show-host-847884/ Jon Stewart sick - Jason Jones Daily Show Host] October 8, 2014. ===The Daily Show with Jon Stewart===
In September 2005, Jones joined The Daily Show cast as a contributor. When his wife left the show in late December for family leave, Jones was promoted to a full-time correspondent. Thereafter, he won a significant following at The Daily Show, thanks to a few pieces on the Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy Denmark cartoons, Carl Monday, and Laguna Beach. Before Rob Corddry left The Daily Show, he said: "Jason Jones has raised the bar too high. I just cant say the things he says to people."<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.phelpshawkins.com/2006.08.01_arch.html  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2008-11-15  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081212195707/http://www.phelpshawkins.com/2006.08.01_arch.html  archivedate=December 12, 2008  df=mdy-all }}</ref> His exposé on the real values of Wasilla, Alaska remains one of the most popular pieces on the Daily Show website.{{citation needed date=January 2017}} In 2014, Jones temporarily left The Daily Show to appear in a pilot for the sitcom Love Is Relative.<ref>{{cite news url=https://nypost.com/2014/03/19/jason-jones-leaving-the-daily-show/ title=Jason Jones latest ‘Daily Show’ comedian moving to sitcoms work=New York Post date=March 19, 2014 accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

====2014 Sochi Olympics====
Jones was sent as a mock reporter to cover the 2014 Sochi Olympics. Through his visit, he often lampooned Russian culture and its perceived police state. For example, in one episode, Jones attempted to find a protest area in Sochi and was forced to drive well outside of the city to an abandoned parking lot which was designed as the zone for protesters; however, even then he was approached by police officers and asked to leave due to not having appropriate documentation.{{Citation needed date=August 2016}} During his interview with opposition figure Alexei Navalny, the crews cameras and other electronic equipment was jammed as they entered an apartment to conduct the interview. Jones also managed to hold a mock interview with Mikhail Gorbachev where he insisted Gorbachev put up the wall, a play on president Ronald Reagans plea to Gorbachev to "tear down this wall! tear down" the Berlin Wall. Alexei Navalny was arrested several days after conducting the interview with Jones, which was also satirized by Jon Stewart.<ref>{{cite news url= http://thedailyshow.cc.com/videos/30gza6/jason-jones-live-from-sochi-ish---no-safety-nyet title=Jason Jones Live From Sochi-ish - No Safety Nyet publisher=The Daily Show date=February 24, 2014 accessdate=September 20, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/02/25/did-daily-show-really-catch-putins-spies-action title=Did The Daily Show Really Just Catch Putins Spies in Action? publisher=Take Part}}</ref> Jones was able to interview Sergey Alexandrovich Markov Sergey Markov, a prominent Russian politician and academic. During the interview Jones questioned Markov about Russias stance on gay rights, to which Markov responded that there are no issues with gay rights in Russia and that "you are absolutely free to make sex with this table." The statement has since achieved notoriety with regards to Russias stance on LGBT rights.<ref>{{cite web url=http://theweek.com/articles/450977/watch-daily-show-search-everywhere-straight-russian-who-backs-gay-rights title=Watch The Daily Show search everywhere for a straight Russian who backs gay rights publisher=The Week}}</ref> During the program Jones also described Russia as a conservative paradise, noting the nations relatively low flat tax, pro gun stance, opposition to gay marriage, and the powerful influence of the Orthodox church. He also remarked that Russia may be the "ultimate red state."<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.businessinsider.com/the-daily-show-russia-sochi-conservatives-2014-2 title=The Daily Show Has Discovered The Ultimate Unlikely Conservative Paradise — Russia publisher=Business Insider}}</ref>

===Other work===
Jones has also done various television work with his Canadian sketch comedy troupe The Bobroom, and was the host of Craft Corner Deathmatch on the Style Network in 2005. He also co-wrote, co-starred in, and co-produced the 2004 film Ham & Cheese with fellow Bobroom alumnus Mike Beaver; the film was nominated for six Canadian Comedy Awards in 2005. He has also had minor roles in numerous feature films, such as the 2002 Syfy original film Terminal Invasion and the 2003 film Public Domain (film) Public Domain, the latter of which also starred Don McKellar, Nicole DeBoer, and Beaver.
