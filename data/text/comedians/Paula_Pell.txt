== Life and career ==

Pell has been a writer for Saturday Night Live since 1995. She also was a producer and writer for the sitcom 30 Rock, and is credited for writing the episodes Argus (30 Rock) "Argus" and Floyd (30 Rock) "Floyd". In 2006, a pilot she wrote, Thick and Thin, was picked up by NBC for 13 episodes, with Pell as executive producer.<ref name=htt/> Pell has worked with Judd Apatow providing additional writing for the films Bridesmaids (2011 film) Bridesmaids and This is 40.

As an actress, Pell appeared in several episodes of 30 Rock as the wife of Pete Hornberger, and played the mother of Ron Swanson in a 2011 episode of Parks and Recreation.<ref name=var/> She has also appeared as an Extra (acting) extra or in bit parts in dozens of SNL sketches. Pell voices Gadget Gal in the Hulu original series The Awesomes.<ref>{{cite web url=http://mynews13.com/content/news/cfnews13/on-the-town/article.html/content/news/articles/cfn/2014/9/23/snl_s_paula_pell_wai.html title=SNLs Paula Pell waiting for the action figure work=News 13 (Central Florida) News 13 last=Walker first=Allison date=September 23, 2014 accessdate=January 12, 2015}}</ref>
