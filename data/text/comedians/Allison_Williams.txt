Allison Howell Williams (born April 13, 1988)<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.miamiherald.com/2014/04/13/4053609/celebrity-birthdays-on-april-13.html  title=Celebrity birthdays on April 13  work=The Miami Herald  date=April 13, 2014  accessdate=July 6, 2014  archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20140416182352/http://www.miamiherald.com/2014/04/13/4053609/celebrity-birthdays-on-april-13.html   archivedate= April 16, 2014  quote=Actress Allison Williams (Girls) is 26.}}</ref> is an American actress, comedian, and singer. She is best known for her role as Marnie Michaels on the HBO comedy-drama series Girls (TV series) Girls and her critically acclaimed performance in the 2017 horror film Get Out.

==Early life==
Williams was born and raised in New Canaan, Connecticut,<ref>{{cite web  first=Amy last=Kaufman url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-allison-williams-2017-story.html  title=How Allison Williams mined the horrors of white privilege for Get Out  newspaper=Los Angeles Times publisher=Tronc location=Los Angeles, California  date=February 17, 2017  accessdate=August 16, 2018}}</ref> the daughter of former NBC Nightly News anchor and managing editor Brian Williams<ref name="post">{{cite news  first=Dana  last=Schuster  url=https://nypost.com/2011/08/05/anchors-away/  title=Anchors away  newspaper=New York Post  publisher=News Corp. location=New York City date=August 5, 2011  accessdate=July 6, 2014}}</ref> and TV producer Jane Gillan Stoddard. She has a brother, Doug, three years her junior.<ref>{{cite web first=John last=Koblin url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/20/business/doug-brian-williams-sny.html title=Another Williams Takes His Turn Before the Camera, at SNY newspaper=The New York Times publisher=New York Times Company location=New York City date=July 20, 2017 accessdate=February 5, 2018}}</ref> She attended New Canaan Country School and Greenwich Academy,<ref name=NCAdvertiser>{{cite news  url=http://ncadvertiser.com/41615/new-canaans-sweetheart-set-to-soar/ title=Allison Williams flies high newspaper=New Canaan Advertiser date=December 4, 2014}}
*“I went to the New Canaan Nature Center for preschool,” she said. “I was so into that world, and still think about it all the time. It established my love for nature and animals.” "New Canaan Country School and Greenwich Academy followed."</ref> then graduated from Yale University.<ref name="post"/> While at Yale, Williams was a member of the Improvisational theatre improv comedy troupe Just Add Water (improv troupe) Just Add Water for four years,<ref name="yale">{{cite web first=Danny last=Serna url=https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2011/01/11/hbo-picks-up-new-television-series-starring-allison-williams-10/ title=HBO picks up new television series starring Allison Williams 10/ newspaper=Yale Daily News publisher=Yale University location=New Haven, Connecticut date=January 11, 2011 archive-url= archive-date= dead-url= accessdate=November 16, 2017}}</ref> was involved in the YouTube series College Musical,<ref>{{cite web first=Emily A.  last=Farris  url=http://gawker.com/5590877/brian-williams-daughter-plays-sensual-ta-in-college-musical-web-series  title=Brian Williams Daughter Plays Sensual TA in College Musical Web Series  website=Gawker  publisher=Gawker Media location=New York City date=July 19, 2010  accessdate=April 16, 2013  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130417095057/http://gawker.com/5590877/brian-williams-daughter-plays-sensual-ta-in-college-musical-web-series  archivedate=April 17, 2013  df= }}</ref> and was inducted by St. Elmo (secret society) St. Elmo.<ref>{{cite web  first=Alex  last=Klein  url=http://www.ivygateblog.com/2010/05/web-exclusive-every-yale-secret-society-2008-2009-or-a-tribute-to-rumpus/  title=WEB EXCLUSIVE: Every Yale Secret Society, 2009–2010 (or, A Tribute to Rumpus)  website=IvyGate  date=May 5, 2010  accessdate=April 26, 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2010, Williams performed a mashup of "Nature Boy" set to RJD2s "A Beautiful Mine", the theme song for the television series Mad Men.<ref>{{cite web  last=Shea  first=Danny  url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/10/13/allison-williams-brian-wi_n_761128.html  title=Allison Williams, Brian Williams Daughter, Sings Mad Men Theme Song (VIDEO)  work=The Huffington Post  publisher=Huffington Post Media Group location=New York City date=October 13, 2010  accessdate=December 28, 2011}}</ref> The YouTube video of the performance received widespread praise on the Internet,<ref>{{cite web  first=Rachel  last=Sklar  url=http://www.mediaite.com/online/allison-williams-this-week-the-biggest-star-in-her-family-sorry-briwi/  title=Allison Williams, This Week The Biggest Star In Her Family (Sorry, BriWi)  website=Mediaite  date=October 14, 2010  accessdate=December 28, 2011}}</ref> and convinced Judd Apatow that she should be given a part in the HBO series Girls (TV series) Girls.<ref name="post"/> She was cast shortly after and credited her experience at Just Add Water for passing the audition.<ref name="yale"/><ref>{{cite web  first=Nellie  last=Andreeva url=http://deadline.com/2010/10/trio-cast-in-lena-dunhams-hbo-pilot-including-brian-williams-daughter-allison-79581/  title=Trio Cast In Lena Dunhams HBO Pilot, Including Brian Williams Daughter Allison  website=Deadline Hollywood.  publisher=Penske Media Corporation  location=Los Angeles, California date=October 28, 2010  accessdate=December 28, 2011}}</ref> Girls premiered on April 15, 2012.

Williams wrote a series of Funny or Die sketch comedy sketches, in which she starred as newlywed Kate Middleton,<ref name="post"/> with English actor and model Oliver Jackson-Cohen as Prince William, Duke of Cambridge.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.funnyordie.com/allison_williams  title=Allison Williams  website=Funny or Die  publisher=Gary Sanchez Productions location=Los Angeles, California accessdate=April 16, 2013}}</ref> Williams appeared as Danielle in an episode of the third season of The League titled "The Guest Bong."

Williams has appeared as a recurring character, Cheryl, in the CollegeHumor series Jake and Amir.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.collegehumor.com/video/6660383/jake-and-amir-cheryl  title=Jake and Amir / Cheryl  publisher=CollegeHumor  date=December 1, 2011  accessdate=April 16, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.collegehumor.com/video/6810508/jake-and-amir-cheryl-part-2  title=Jake and Amir / Cheryl Part 2 (starring Allison Williams)  publisher=CollegeHumor  date=August 14, 2012  accessdate=April 16, 2013}}</ref>

On December 4, 2014 she starred in Peter Pan the title role on NBCs live television presentation of the musical Peter Pan Live!, opposite Christopher Walken as Captain Hook.<ref>{{cite web  first=Marc  last=Sneticker  url=http://www.ew.com/article/2014/07/30/allison-williams-peter-pan-live  title=Allison Williams to play Peter Pan in NBCs Peter Pan Live  magazine=Entertainment Weekly  publisher=Meredith Corporation  location=New York City date=July 30, 2014  accessdate=July 30, 2014}}</ref> In November 2016, Williams appeared in Past Forward, a short film collaboration between David O. Russell and Prada.

In 2017, Williams starred in the hit horror film Get Out, directed by Jordan Peele, who became convinced she was right for the role of Rose after seeing her performance in Peter Pan Live! <ref>{{cite news first1=Patrick last1=Hipes title=Allison Williams To Star In Jordan Peele’s Horror Movie ‘Get Out’ url=http://deadline.com/2015/11/allison-williams-get-out-jordan-peele-horror-movie-1201606920/ website=Deadline Hollywood publisher=Eldridge Industries location=Los Angeles, California date=November 4, 2015 accessdate=May 27, 2016}}</ref>

In 2018, Williams was cast in a recurring role throughout the second and third seasons of the Netflix comedy drama series A Series of Unfortunate Events.<ref>{{cite news first1=Joe last1=Otterson title=Allison Williams Joins ‘A Series of Unfortunate Events’ at Netflix url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/get-out-allison-williams-a-series-of-unfortunate-events-netflix-1202731365/ accessdate=April 2, 2018 work=Variety date=March 20, 2018}}</ref>

US}}</ref> She and her husband live in Chelsea, Manhattan Chelsea, New York.<ref name=nyc>{{cite web  url=https://www.brickunderground.com/blog/2015/02/allison_williams_celebrity_neighbor title=Chelsea Girl: Allison Williams makes her home on Manhattans West side publisher=Brick Underground date=February 18, 2015}}</ref><ref name=nyc-gawker>{{cite web  url=http://defamer.gawker.com/allison-williams-might-be-the-most-boring-person-in-new-1685292170 title=Allison Williams Might Be the Most Boring Person in New York publisher=Gawker date=February 11, 2015}}</ref>

===Advocacy===
Williams is an ambassador for Horizons National. She led the 2016 "10 Days of Giving" campaign to raise awareness and funds for the organizations educational goals and is expected to revisit this role in 2017.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.horizonsnational.org/news/allison-williams-sets-the-stage-for-horizons-giving-day/ title=Allison Williams Sets the Stage for Horizons Giving Day last= first= date=May 4, 2016 website=Horizonsnational.org publisher=Horizons National archive-url= archive-date= dead-url= accessdate=June 24, 2017}}</ref> Williams recently talked about her political views, advocacy, millennial activism, and women in Hollywood on the Politico podcast Women Rule.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.politico.com/story/2017/06/21/allison-williams-trump-girls-women-rule-239786 title=Allison Williams re-imagines ‘Girls’ in the Trump era last= first= date=June 6, 2017 website=Politico.com publisher=Politico archive-url= archive-date= dead-url= accessdate=June 24, 2017}}</ref>
