
Kyle James Kozub Mooney (born September 4, 1984) is an American comedian, actor, and writer, who has been a cast member of Saturday Night Live since 2013.<ref>{{cite web title=Mooney on SNL url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/kyle-mooney publisher=NBC accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> His man-on-the-street interviews have been featured on Jimmy Kimmel Live! and Sports Show with Norm Macdonald.<ref>{{cite web url=http://blogs.miaminewtimes.com/cultist/2012/07/draft_kyle_mooney_is_the_ultim.php title=Meet Kyle Mooney, the Worlds Most Awkward Miami Heat Fan (Video) date=3 July 2012 work=Cultist accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref>

Mooney has been a regular writer and performer at Upright Citizens Brigade since 2007.<ref>http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/credits/credit/season-42/kyle-mooney</ref> He also co-starred as Rory on the HBO comedy series Hello Ladies.<ref>{{cite web title=Hello Ladies url=http://www.hbo.com/hello-ladies/cast-and-crew/rory#/ publisher=HBO accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> Mooney also starred in the film Brigsby Bear, which he co-wrote.<ref>http://www.sltrib.com/home/4650047-155/utah-made-brigsby-bear-gets-into-sundance
</ref>

==Early life==
Mooney was born in San Diego, California, to parents Linda (Kozub) and Brian Mooney. Linda is a former The San Diego Union-Tribune reporter, and Brian is an environmental consultant and planner.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.utsandiego.com/news/2013/Oct/02/saturday-night-live-kyle-mooney-bennett-att-cast/ title=SNL season opens with local talent work=U-T San Diego accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> Mooney has two brothers, Sean and Ryan Mooney; he is the youngest of the three. Mooney is left-handed and nearsighted.   

Mooneys Marshall Middle School class chose him as the most likely to become a T.V. star.<ref name="T.V. Star/Class Clown">{{cite web title=T.V. Star/Class Clown url=http://www.utsandiego.com/news/2013/Oct/02/saturday-night-live-kyle-mooney-bennett-att-cast/ publisher=Diane Bell accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> He graduated from Scripps Ranch High School in 2003, where he won Best Actor as Prospero in Shakespeares The Tempest<ref>{{cite web url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/11710 title=UCB Theatre • Kyle Mooney publisher=Losangeles.ucbtheatre.com accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> and was hailed as class clown.<ref name="T.V. Star/Class Clown"/> Mooney studied film at the University of Southern California.<ref name="UCBbio">{{cite news url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/11710 title=Kyle Mooney last= first= date= work=Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre accessdate=2013-08-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thrillist.com/los-angeles/entertainment/2010/12/14/good-neighbor title=USC dudes make videos for your amusement date=6 October 2014 work=thrillist accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> There, he performed improv and sketch comedy with collegiate group Commedus Interruptus. After a six-week audition process in 2013,<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWJL1r18heA title=Commedus audition publisher=Partners Project accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> the three new members added to Commedus were Kyle Mooney, Beck Bennett, and Nick Rutherford.<ref>{{cite web url=http://laist.com/2009/02/11/interview_friendly_comedy_from_good.php title=Interview: Friendly comedy from Good Neighbor work=LAist accessdate=6 October 2014 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171106040222/http://laist.com/2009/02/11/interview_friendly_comedy_from_good.php archivedate=6 November 2017 df=}}</ref>

Mooney is a self described "collector" and owns a VHS collection which he started working on as a child, although the collection started for real during his freshman year of college. He currently has a giant bookshelf as well as a two smaller bookshelves that are packed with VHS tapes. Through connections made while working on SNL, Mooney is able to get his VHS tapes autographed by the actors who have starred in them. For example, Mooney owns a Beetlejuice VHS that has been signed by both Michael Keaton and Alec Baldwin.<ref>https://www.gq.com/story/kyle-mooney-feels-pretty-good-about-his-alf-sweatshirt</ref>

==Career==
===Good Neighbor===
In 2007, Mooney, Bennett, and Rutherford came together to form the sketch comedy group Good Neighbor, along with Mooneys friend, director and editor Dave McCary.<ref>{{cite web url=http://dailytrojan.com/2011/01/23/student-comedy-group-grabs-attention/ title=Student comedy group grabs attention work=Daily Trojan accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> Bennett and Dave McCary joined Mooney on SNL in 2013 (as a performer and director, respectively), and Rutherford was hired as a writer the following year.<ref>{{cite web url=http://splitsider.com/2013/08/here-are-your-new-snl-featured-players/ title=Here Are Your New SNL Featured Players – Splitsider work=Splitsider accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref> In 2014, Good Neighbor was featured on New Media Rockstars Top 100 Channels, ranked at #98.<ref>{{cite web url=http://newmediarockstars.com/2014/12/the-nmr-top-100-youtube-channels-100-76/ title=The NMR Top 100 YouTube Channels: 100 – 76! website=New Media Rockstars}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
On September 28, 2013, Kyle Mooney made his debut on Saturday Night Live as a featured player.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/09/29/tina-fey-snl-new-cast-members_n_4010798.html title=Tina Fey Introduces New SNL Cast Members In Season 39 Premiere date=2013-09-29 work=Huffington Post access-date=2018-01-28 language=en-US}}</ref> At the start of the Saturday Night Live (season 41) forty-first season, Mooney was upgraded to repertory status.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://splitsider.com/2015/09/saturday-night-live-promotes-kyle-mooney-to-repertory-player-for-season-41/ title=‘Saturday Night Live’ Promotes Kyle Mooney to Repertory Player for Season 41 date=2015-09-23 work=Splitsider access-date=2018-01-28 language=en-US}}</ref>

Mooney has brought some of his YouTube characters to SNL, including his person-on-the-street interviews, the "4/20 weed smoking guy" Recurring Saturday Night Live characters and sketches introduced 2013–2014#Chris Fitzpatrick Chris Fitzpatrick, Todd from Inside SoCal, and Recurring Saturday Night Live characters and sketches introduced 2013–2014#Bruce Chandling Bruce Chandling.

His celebrity impressions on Saturday Night Live include Jim Acosta, Woody Allen, Criss Angel, Fred Astaire, David Axelrod, Jeff Bezos, Neil Cavuto, Lincoln Chafee, Tom Cotton, Michael Jackson, Bruce Jessen, Steve Jobs, Chris Kirkpatrick, Joey Lawrence, Macklemore, Chuck Norris, Rand Paul, Pope Francis, Axl Rose, Skrillex, Stephen Stills, and Steven Tyler. <ref>{{Cite web url=http://snlarchives.net/Cast/?KyMo title=SNL Archives {{!}} Cast {{!}} Kyle Mooney website=snlarchives.net access-date=2018-01-28}}</ref>
