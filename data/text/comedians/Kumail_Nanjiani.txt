Kumail Nanjiani (born February 21, 1978)<ref name="nyt091101"/><ref>{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/TVsJonDore/status/39885028154548224 title=Twitter / TVsJonDore: Happy Birthday Kumail Nanjiani!!  publisher=Twitter.com date=February 21, 2011 accessdate=April 22, 2014}}</ref> is a Pakistani-American stand-up comedian, actor, writer, and podcast host. Nanjiani is best known for being a main cast member on HBO HBOs Emmy Award Emmy Award-nominated series Silicon Valley (TV series) Silicon Valley, as well as for providing the voice of List of Adventure Time characters#Prismo Prismo on the Emmy Award-winning animated series Adventure Time. He starred on the TNT (U.S. TV network) TNT series Franklin & Bash and the Adult Swim series Newsreaders. Nanjiani also co-hosted the Comedy Central show The Meltdown with Jonah and Kumail. In addition to his television and film work, he hosted two podcasts: The Indoor Kids and The X-Files Files.

In 2017, Nanjiani starred in the semi-autobiographical romantic comedy film The Big Sick, which he wrote with his wife Emily V. Gordon. They were nominated for the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay at the 90th Academy Awards.

==Early life==
Nanjiani was born in Karachi, Pakistan, the son of Shabana and Aijaz Nanjiani.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2017/05/08/kumail-nanjianis-culture-clash-comedy title=Kumail Nanjianis Culture-Clash Comedy first=Andrew last=Marantz date=May 1, 2017 publisher= via=www.newyorker.com}}</ref> He was raised as a Shia Islam Shia Muslim, but has left the faith.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.slantmagazine.com/features/article/interview-kumail-nanjiani-on-the-big-sick-marriage-comedy-and-silicon-valley title=Interview: Kumail Nanjiani on The Big Sicks Real-Life Love Story date=June 22, 2017}}</ref> The Scottish radio presenter Shereen Nanjiani is his second cousin.<ref name="bbc120901">{{cite web  first=Shereen  last=Nanjiani  authorlink=Shereen Nanjiani  publisher=BBC Radio Scotland  work=Shereen  title=Extended Interview with Kumail Nanjiani  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00y0g8q  date=September 1, 2012  accessdate=February 13, 2013}}</ref><ref name="risk-show">{{cite web  first=Kevin  last=Allison  authorlink=Kevin Allison  work=Risk!  url=http://risk-show.com/podcast/live-from-san-francisco/  title=Live From San Francisco!  date=February 11, 2013  accessdate=February 13, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T_EOB2KE_ys&feature=youtu.be&t=80 title=Interview: Kumail Nanjiani Explains Pakistani Culture (Full Interview) date=Jun 28, 2017}}</ref>

During his childhood, Nanjiani lived in Karachi and attended Karachi Grammar School. At 18, he moved to the United States and attended Grinnell College in Iowa, from which he graduated in 2001. He completed a double major in  computer science and philosophy.<ref name="nyt091101"/><ref name="grim100922"/><ref name="wapo140404"/>

==Career==

===2008–2016: Television work===
In July 2011, he and X-Play staffer Ali Baker began hosting a video game-themed podcast, titled Nerdist Industries#Nerdist Podcast Network The Indoor Kids. In late August of the same year Baker left the show and Nanjiani began hosting with his wife, Emily V. Gordon.<ref name="nerd120715"/> Until other commitments took over, he appeared regularly on Dan Harmons podcast  Harmontown, where he played Dungeons & Dragons with Harmon as a character named Chris de Burgh. He played a delivery man in the 2013 film, The Kings of Summer.<ref>{{cite web url=http://collider.com/kings-of-summer-red-band-trailer/  title=The Kings of Summer Red-Band Trailer  publisher=Collider.com  date=May 20, 2013  accessdate=June 29, 2014}}</ref> In addition to guest-starring in various comedy shows like Portlandia (TV series) Portlandia, Nanjiani was featured in a supporting role in the TNT (U.S. TV network) TNT series Franklin & Bash. He played the role of Pindar Singh, an agoraphobic science fiction nerd working for the title characters.

Nanjiani guest-starred on HBOs Veep (TV series) Veep as a statistician. He had a recurring role of Amir Larussa on Newsreaders, as well as Prismo on Adventure Time. His Comedy Central special titled Beta Male aired in July 2013.<ref name="avcl130723"/> In late June 2013, Comedy Central announced the pickup of a show The Meltdown with Jonah and Kumail hosted by Jonah Ray and Nanjiani.<ref name="tvbn130620"/> The show, featuring Nerd Melt comedy regulars and various comedians, began airing in July 2014.<ref name="melt140629"/> In April 2014, he began playing the character Dinesh in the HBO sitcom Silicon Valley (TV series) Silicon Valley.<ref name="wapo140404"/> He voiced Reggie in the video game The Walking Dead: Season Two.<ref name="nerd140513"/> In July 2014, Nanjiani hosted a weekly podcast titled The X-Files Files, dedicated to discussion and fond reminiscences about The X-Files. Each episode features him and a guest, including former The X-Files writers, producers, actors and directors, in light-hearted banter about one or two episodes of the series.<ref name="avcl140609"/><ref name="itunexff"/> On July 30, 2014, Nanjiani appeared on Ken Reid (comedian) Ken Reids TV Guidance Counselor Podcast.{{citation needed date=May 2015}}

In 2015, Nanjiani guest-starred in an episode of Broad City called "In Heat".<ref>{{cite news last1=Cruz first1=Lenika title=Bed, Bath, and Bong Rips: The Glorious Return of Broad City url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2015/01/the-glorious-return-of-broad-city/384405/ accessdate=May 10, 2015 work=The Atlantic date=January 14, 2015}}</ref> Starting on March 22, 2015 he provided his voice for the character of Mshak Moradi in the Hunt the Truth audio drama, part of the marketing campaign for Halo 5: Guardians.<ref>{{cite web title=Halo 5: Guardians #HuntTheTruth – Has the Master Chief Gone AWOL? author=Xbox Wire Staff date=May 17, 2015 website=News.xbox.com url=http://news.xbox.com/2015/05/games-hunt-the-truth-podcast-episode-8}}</ref> On May 5, 2015, he appeared on Inside Amy Schumer in the episode "12 Angry Men Inside Amy Schumer", a parody of Twelve Angry Men 12 Angry Men, as one of the members of the jury.<ref>{{cite news last1=Holmes first1=Linda title=Amy Schumer Puts Her Own Looks On Trial url=https://www.npr.org/blogs/monkeysee/2015/05/06/404649955/amy-schumer-puts-her-own-looks-on-trial accessdate=May 10, 2015 work=NPR.org date=May 6, 2015}}</ref> In July 2015, Nanjiani provided guest voices on Aqua Teen Hunger Force and Penn Zero: Part-Time Hero.<ref>{{cite web title=New Listings for Disney XD – July 2015 url=http://thedisneydrivenlife.com/2015/06/30/new-listings-for-disney-xd-july-2015/ publisher=The Disney Driven Life accessdate=July 1, 2015 date=June 30, 2015}}</ref> In November 2015, he starred in an Old Navy TV commercial together with Julia Louis-Dreyfus and Snoop Dogg.<ref>{{cite web author=Liviu Marica url=http://dailycommercials.com/old-navy-snoopin-around-featurig-snoop-dogg/  title= Old Navy: Snoopin Around featurig Snoop Dogg  publisher=Daily Commercials date=  accessdate=November 23, 2015}}</ref> In April 2016, he starred in another Old Navy advertisement with Saturday Night Live SNL cast members Jay Pharoah, Cecily Strong, and Nasim Pedrad.

===2017–present: Breakthrough in film===
In 2017, Nanjiani starred in the romantic comedy film The Big Sick, which he wrote with his wife Emily V. Gordon.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.indiewire.com/2017/01/amazon-the-big-sick-bidding-war-sundance-film-acquisition-1201771744/ title=Amazon Wins The Big Sick Bidding War $12 Million Buy Sundance 2017 last1=Winfrey first1=Graham date=January 22, 2017 work=IndieWire accessdate=April 11, 2017}}</ref> The film is about their relationship, with Nanjiani playing himself and Zoe Kazan playing Gordon (renamed Emily Gardner). The film was considered to be one of the most acclaimed films of 2017, and was chosen by American Film Institute as one of the top 10 films of the year and was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.afi.com/afiawards/AFI-Awards-2017.aspx title=AFI Awards 2017 website=American Film Institute AFI accessdate=December 8, 2017}}</ref><ref name="AA18">{{cite web url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/oscar-nominations-2018-complete-list-nominees-1067893  title=Oscars: Shape of Water Leads With 13 Noms  work=The Hollywood Reporter  date=January 23, 2018 accessdate=January 23, 2018}}</ref> The film was also the third highest grossing independent film released in 2017, grossing over 40 million dollars.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.indiewire.com/2017/06/highest-grossing-indie-films-2017-1201764229/ title=The 20 Highest Grossing Indies of 2017 (A Running List) – IndieWire first=Kate last=Erbland work=IndieWire accessdate=September 7, 2017}}</ref> 

In 2019, Nanjiani will lend his voice to Plimpton, an ostrich, in The Voyage of Doctor Dolittle (film) The Voyage of Doctor Dolittle.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/voyage-doctor-dolittle-cast-robert-downey-jr-unveils-full-list-1097797 title=Robert Downey Jr. Unveils Voice Cast for Voyage of Doctor Dolittle work=The Hollywood Reporter access-date=2018-03-27 language=en}}</ref> The film is set for release on April 12, 2019.<ref>{{cite news last1=Kit first1=Borys title=Robert Downey Jr.s Doctor Dolittle Moves Release to Avoid Star Wars url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dr-dolittle-movie-starring-robert-downey-jr-gets-a-new-release-date-998624 accessdate=April 29, 2017 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=April 28, 2017 language=en}}</ref>  Nanjiani will also star in the action-comedy Stuber (film) Stuber, which is set for release on May 24, 2019.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/kumail-nanjiani-star-opposite-dave-bautista-action-comedy-stuber-1095889 title=Kumail Nanjiani to Star Opposite Dave Bautista in Action Comedy Stuber (Exclusive) publisher=}}</ref><ref>[https://deadline.com/2018/06/20th-century-fox-sets-dates-for-untitled-james-mangold-ferrari-project-kenneth-branaghs-death-on-the-nile-1202416198/ 20th Century Fox Sets Dates For Untitled James Mangold Ferrari Project, Kenneth Branagh’s ‘Death On The Nile’]</ref> Nanjiani will also costar in the Untitled "Men in Black" Spin-Off film, which will be released on June 14, 2019.<ref>[https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/men-black-movie-spinoff-adds-cast-1124261 Men in Black Movie Spinoff Adds Jurassic Actor to Cast (Exclusive)]</ref><ref>{{cite news last1=Busch first1=Anita title=‘Men In Black’ Spinoff Gets Summer 2019 Release Date Change url=http://deadline.com/2018/01/men-in-black-untitled-spinoff-release-date-change-summer-2019-1202248296/ accessdate=May 29, 2018 work=Deadline date=January 18, 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Nanjiani has been married to Emily V. Gordon, an author, comedy producer, and former couples and family therapist since 2007.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/sex-love/advice/a6877/secret-life-of-marrieds-kumail-emily/ title=The Secret Life of Marrieds: Why We Kept Our Marriage Secret for Years last=Marie first=Jane date=May 20, 2014 work=Cosmopolitan (magazine) Cosmopolitan accessdate=May 5, 2017}}</ref> Gordon was the booker and producer of The Meltdown with Jonah and Kumail (2014–2016).<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2017/05/08/kumail-nanjianis-culture-clash-comedy title=Kumail Nanjianis Culture-Clash Comedy last=Marantz first=Andrew date=May 8, 2017 work=The New Yorker accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref>
