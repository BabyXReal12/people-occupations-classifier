
Edward   Griffin (born July 15, 1968) is an American comedian and actor. He is best known for portraying Eddie Sherman on the sitcom Malcolm & Eddie and the title character in the 2002 comedy film Undercover Brother.  He also played Pope Sweet Jesus in Norbit.

==Early life==
Griffin was born in Kansas City, Missouri, and was raised by his single mother, Doris Thomas, a phone company operator.<ref>{{cite web title=Eddie Griffin finds his material in his funky, troubled family url=http://www.peninsulaclarion.com/stories/040303/ins_0403030006.shtml}}</ref> His family were Jehovahs Witnesses.<ref>{{cite web last1=Griffin first1=Eddie title=Eddie Griffin on Jehovah Witnesses and the Benefits of Polygamy. Standup Comedy From The Vault url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kx9pp2xZz5A publisher=The Vault accessdate=April 7, 2018}}</ref> In 1984, at 16 years old, he moved to Compton, California to live with his cousins. in 1986, after graduating from Compton High, Griffin enrolled as a biological engineering major at Kansas State University, but later left in 1988 and moved to New York City to pursue a career in comedy. Griffin did not see his mother for four years, after Griffin and his mother got into an argument at his 20th birthday party, where Griffins mother accused him of stealing jewelry from her, which Griffin denied doing. Griffin later moved back to Los Angeles, California in March 1992, to be closer with his family when his mother was injured in a car accident.<ref>{{cite web  title =New Material Drives Comedian Eddie Griffins New Special, Stand-up Tour  url =http://www.recordnet.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20110317/A_ENTERTAIN/103170303/-1/NEWSMAP  accessdate =March 30, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Griffin has starred in films such as The Meteor Man (film) The Meteor Man (1993), The Walking Dead (1995) Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo (1999), Double Take (2001 film) Double Take (2001), Undercover Brother (2002), John Q (2002), Scary Movie 3 (2003), Deuce Bigalow: European Gigolo (2005) and Norbit (2007). In 2007, he starred in Urban Justice, a thriller set in New Mexico. He also starred in the UPN television series Malcolm & Eddie (1996–2000).

Griffin was ranked at number 62 on Comedy Centrals list of the 100 Greatest Stand-ups of All Time.<ref>{{IMDb title 0429332 title=Comedy Central Presents: 100 Greatest Stand-Ups of All Time}}</ref> In 2011 Comedy Central released You Can Tell Em I Said It on DVD. It was 82 minutes of unedited and uncensored content.

Griffin performed on two tracks from Dr. Dres 1999 album, 2001 (Dr. Dre album) 2001, and the intro track from The D.O.C.s 1996 album Helter Skelter (album) Helter Skelter. He also has appeared on commercials for Miller Beers Man Laws. He is well known for his comedic routine of imitating Michael Jackson on crack cocaine. He also made an appearance on Chappelles Show in the skit "World Series of Dice" as Grits n Gravy.

==Personal life==
Griffin has been married three times. He married his first wife, Carla, in 1984; he was 16 years old.<ref name="people03">{{cite web  url=http://people.com/archive/verve-griffin-vol-59-no-15/  title=Verve Griffin  first=Samantha  last=Miller  website=People  date=April 21, 2003  accessdate=May 7, 2018}}</ref> He married his second wife, Rochelle, in 2002,<ref name="people03"/> but they have since divorced. On September 8, 2011, he married Nia Rivers.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.tmz.com/2011/09/12/eddie-griffin-wedding-married-nia-rivers-shawpri-las-vegas-walk-in-sin-city-elope-undercover-brother/?adid=hero3  title=Eddie Griffin - The Walk-In VEGAS Wedding!!!!  publisher=TMZ.com  date=August 28, 2011  accessdate=January 29, 2012}}</ref> However, they filed for divorce after one month of marriage, citing irreconcilable differences. They were officially divorced six months later.<ref>{{cite web   url=http://www.tmz.com/2012/03/24/eddie-griffin-divorced/   title=Eddie Griffin FINALLY DIVORCED After 6 Months of Marriage   publisher=TMZ   date=March 24, 2012   accessdate=October 16, 2014}}</ref>

In March 2007,<ref name="cbsnews.com">{{cite news url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2007/03/27/entertainment/main2611916.shtml   work=CBS News   title=Eddie Griffin Wrecks $1.5 Million Ferrari   date=March 27, 2007}}</ref> Griffin participated in a charity race at Irwindale Speedway to promote the film Redline, using a Ferrari Enzo owned by Daniel Sadek. During a practice run, Griffin accidentally hit the gas pedal instead of the brake and lost control of the Enzo, crashing hard into a concrete barrier.<ref name="cbsnews.com"/> He walked away unscratched, but the $1.5 million supercar was badly damaged.<ref name="cbsnews.com"/> Griffin later criticized reporters who suggested the crash was a publicity stunt.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://people.com/celebrity/eddie-griffin-car-wreck-injuries-no-hoax/  title=Eddie Griffin: Car Wreck, Injuries No Hoax  website=People.com  first1=Pete  last1=Norman  first2=Kimberly  last2=Pfeiffer  date=March 30, 2007  accessdate=April 27, 2018}}</ref> 

During the Bill Cosby sexual assault allegations sexual assault allegations on Bill Cosby, Griffin suggested that Cosby was the victim of a conspiracy to destroy his image and that several other prominent African-American men had been victims of similar conspiracies.<ref>{{Citation  last=Howard  first=Adam date=December 31, 2015  title= Eddie Griffin defends Cosby, alleges systematic effort to destroy black male stars  publisher=MSNBC  url=http://www.msnbc.com/msnbc/comedian-eddie-griffin-defends-cosby-sites-systematic-effort-destroy-black-male-entertainers  accessdate=December 31, 2015 }}</ref>
