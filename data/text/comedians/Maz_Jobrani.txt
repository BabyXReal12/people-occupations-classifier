
Maziyar "Maz" Jobrani ({{lang-fa مازیار جبرانی}}; born February 26, 1972) is an Iranian-American comedian and actor who is part of the "Axis of Evil Comedy Tour Axis of Evil" comedy group. The group appeared on a comedy special on Comedy Central. Jobrani has also appeared in numerous films, television shows, including Better Off Ted, on radio, and in comedy clubs. His filmography includes roles in The Interpreter, Friday After Next, Dragonfly (2002 film) Dragonfly, and Jimmy Vestvood: Amerikan Hero. He appears as a regular character on the 2017 CBS sitcom Superior Donuts (TV series) Superior Donuts. He is also currently a board member of the National Iranian American Council (NIAC).<ref>{{Cite web  url=   https://www.niacouncil.org/about-niac/staff-board/   title= Staff and Board   website= NIAC  access-date=2018-04-05}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Jobrani was born in Tehran, Iran.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/blogs/persianality/detail?blogid=36&entry_id=20080  title=Talking with Maz Jobrani  accessdate=January 22, 2009  date=September 8, 2007  last=Rezaian  first=Jason  work=The San Francisco Chronicle}}</ref> He and his parents moved to California when he was six years old.<ref name="Com Central">Maz Jobrani Comedy Central Special</ref> He was raised in Tiburon, California Tiburon in the San Francisco Bay area. He attended Redwood High School (Larkspur, California) Redwood High School in Larkspur, California Larkspur, and was inducted into the Redwood distinguished alumni class of 2017.<ref>{{cite web last1=Schten first1=Rachel title=Avenue of Giants inductees hail from diverse fields url=http://redwoodbark.org/2017/03/avenue-giants-inductees-hail-diverse-fields/ website=Redwood Bark accessdate=16 March 2017 date=15 March 2017}}</ref> Jobrani studied political science and Italian at UC Berkeley, where he received a B.A. degree. He was enrolled in a Ph.D. program at UCLA when he decided to pursue his childhood dream of acting and performing comedy.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.mazjobrani.com  title=ABCs The Knights of Prosperity bio  first=Gourishankar  last=Subramaniam  work=American Broadcasting Company ABC  year=2007  accessdate=November 7, 2007}}</ref>

==Career==

===Television and radio===
Jobrani has since made appearances on shows like The Colbert Report, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, Talkshow with Spike Feresten, Whitney (TV series) Whitney, and regularly performs at top comedy clubs (in California and New York) such as The Comedy Store. He made an appearance as a dental patient on an episode of Still Standing (TV series) Still Standing, in the pilot episodes of Better Off Ted, The Knights of Prosperity, on an episode of Cedric the Entertainer Presents, on an episode of Malcolm in the Middle as Robber #2 and on an episode of The West Wing as a Saudi prince. He also made an appearance in 13 Going on 30. He has toured with the Axis of Evil Comedy Tour. He provided the voice of Ahmed Farahnakian in the audiobook version of World War Z. Jobrani has written a movie with a friend called Jimmy Vestvood: Amerikan Hero.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.sandiego.com/comedy/interview-with-comedian-maz-jobrani  title=Interview With Comedian Maz Jobrani  first=Gordon  last=Downs  work=SanDiego.com  year=2011  accessdate=February 14, 2011  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110216203627/http://www.sandiego.com/comedy/interview-with-comedian-maz-jobrani  archivedate=February 16, 2011  df= }}</ref>

Jobrani makes occasional appearances on NPRs news quiz show Wait Wait... Dont Tell Me! and American Public Medias Wits (radio program) Wits. He appeared on episode 118, October 28, 2010 of WTF with Marc Maron. Additionally, Jobrani co-hosts his own podcast on the All Things Comedy podcasting network with fellow comedians Al Madrigal and Chris Spencer (actor) Chris Spencer. Titled Minivan Men, the podcast chronicles the lives and experiences of the three hosts with particular focus on fatherhood.

He played Jafar in the 2015 musical fantasy television film Descendants (2015 film) Descendants.

In 2015, Jobrani released a memoir entitled Im Not a Terrorist, But Ive Played One On TV.<ref>{{cite news last1=Mechanic first1=Michael title=Bombs Sometimes, Kills Often, But Maz Jobrani Swears He Isnt a Terrorist url=https://www.motherjones.com/media/2014/11/interview-maz-jobrani-iranian-comedian-not-terrorist-memoir work=Mother Jones date=February 3, 2015}}</ref> 

===Standup style===
Jobranis jokes focus on race and the misunderstanding of Middle Easterners in America. He also talks about his family.

===Philanthropy===
Jobrani sits on the board of the Persian American Cancer Institute ([http://www.paci.org/ PACI.org]) and also works with International Society for Children with Cancer ([http://iscc-charity.org/ ISCC-Charity.org]).

==Personal life==
In 2006, Jobrani married an Indian-American attorney named Preetha. They have a son and daughter and reside in California.<ref name="Com Central" />
