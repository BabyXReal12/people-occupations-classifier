
John Richard Hegley<ref>[http://www.brad.ac.uk/admin/recruitment/student-life/graduate/i-jh.html John Hegley profile, Bradford University website]</ref> (born 1 October 1953) is an English performance poet, comedian, musician and songwriter.

==Early life==
He was born in the Newington Green area of Islington, London, England, into a Roman Catholic household.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/books/2003/apr/27/poetry.comment "Stanza and deliver"], The Observer, 27 April 2003</ref> He was brought up in Luton and later Bristol, where he attended Rodway School.  After school he worked as a bus conductor and civil servant before attending the University of Bradford, where he gained a BSc in European Literature and the History of Ideas and Sociology. Hegley has French ancestry (his fathers name was René) and claims he is descended from the composer Jean-Philippe Rameau.<ref>[http://www.fife.gov.uk/yourtown/index.cfm?fuseaction=news.display&town=78E4E4F9-DA4D-6CAD-5617FBFC91B78425&objectid=33FDD615-9E65-A54E-C470A643C49C03BE "Peoples Poet Laureate Plays Adam Smith Theatre"] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110608015851/http://www.fife.gov.uk/yourtown/index.cfm?fuseaction=news.display&town=78E4E4F9-DA4D-6CAD-5617FBFC91B78425&objectid=33FDD615-9E65-A54E-C470A643C49C03BE  date= 8 June 2011 }}, fifedirect.org.uk, 29 May 2008</ref> His paternal grandmother was a dancer with the Folies Bergère.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2009/jun/20/john-hegley-family-values "My family values: John Hegley, poet"], The Guardian, 20 June 2009</ref>

==Career==
Hegley began his performing career at Londons Comedy Store, London Comedy Store in 1980, and toured as one half of The Brown Paper Bag Brothers with Otiz Cannelloni.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=oFw_eWqvHV8C&pg=PT384&lpg=PT384&dq=Brown+Paper+Bag+Brothers+with+Otiz+Cannelloni&source=bl&ots=Z6nEb4hfoz&sig=2l5RXsr3TcoDXBNgmdfkc5MgMyI&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0CCwQ6AEwA2oVChMIifn20-H7xwIVyyPbCh2zZwTM#v=onepage&q=Brown%20Paper%20Bag%20Brothers%20with%20Otiz%20Cannelloni&f=false Davies, Alan. My Favourite People & Me: 1978-1988], Michael Joseph Ltd (2009).</ref> He received national exposure when he appeared with his backing band the Popticians on Carrotts Lib in 1983, and recorded two sessions for John Peel in 1983 and 1984.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio1/johnpeel/artists/t/thepopticians/ BBC – Radio 1 – Keeping it Peel – The Popticians]</ref> Hegley published his first poetry collection, Visions of the Bone Idol (Poems about Dogs and Glasses), pieces from which were later incorporated into Glad to Wear Glasses, in 1984. Hegley has written a number of collections of poetry, ranging from the surreal through the humorous to the personal and emotional.  There are a number of recurring themes in his poems, notably glasses, dogs and reminiscences of his childhood in Luton.<ref>[http://www.contemporarywriters.com/authors/?p=auth02D4J450112627326 John Hegley at contemporarywriters.com] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070410033753/http://www.contemporarywriters.com/authors/?p=auth02D4J450112627326  date=10 April 2007 }}</ref>

He was presenter of the Border Television series Word of Mouth – in which numerous contemporary poets performed their work – in 1990, and the BBC radio series Hearing with Hegley from 1996 to 1999.  His other television appearances include Wogan and Never Mind the Buzzcocks. In 1998, Hegleys poem "Malcolm" came second in a BBC survey to find Britains most popular comic poem.<ref>{{cite news  last=BBC News url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/190291.stm  title=Top poetry is complete nonsense  publisher=BBC  date=10 October 1998  accessdate=6 January 2009}}</ref> In 1999 he starred in a Simon Callow-directed revival of the musical The Pajama Game in Londons West End.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/464680.stm "Pajama Game pillowtalk"], BBC News, 4 October 1999</ref>

In September 1999 together with Simon Munnery he wrote and performed in a comedy series for BBC Radio 4 called The Adventures of John and Tony.<ref>[http://epguides.com/AdventuresofJohnandTony/ EPGuides.com]</ref>

Hegley frequently performs live and is a regular at the Edinburgh Festival.  His stage act includes elements of poetry, music (he plays the mandolin and is often accompanied by a double bassist), comedy and Luton Town F.C. Luton Town Football Club. He also likes to utilise audience participation in his shows, for example by having a dog drawing competition during the interval, or by asking his audience to try writing poetry themselves.

The University of Bedfordshire University of Luton awarded him an honorary degree honorary Doctor of Laws LL.D. in 2000, and he has also led creative writing courses at the university.<ref>Rampton, J. [https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/review-there-once-was-a-fellow-named-john-1139706.html Review: There once was a fellow named John...], The Independent, 20 January 1998</ref>

Hegley launched "Warning: May Contain Nuts", a project using comedy to increase awareness of mental illness.<ref name="bbc">{{cite web  title=Mackenzie Taylor talks about mental health problems  date=25 May 2010  url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/local/berkshire/hi/people_and_places/arts_and_culture/newsid_8701000/8701610.stm  work=BBC  author=Jenny Minard     accessdate=10 November 2010}}</ref> He performed these shows in 2010 with other performers, including comic Mackenzie Taylor, talking about mental illness.<ref>{{cite news  title=Warning: May Contain Nuts, South Street Arts Centre, Reading  work=The Independent  date=20 May 2010  author=Toby Green  url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/reviews/warning-may-contain-nuts-south-street-arts-centre-reading-1977593.html  accessdate=20 November 2010}}</ref>
