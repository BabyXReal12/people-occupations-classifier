
Aasif Hakim Mandviwala<ref name="tampa">{{cite web url=http://www.tampabay.com/features/media/article545843.ece title=For Aasif Mandvi, cultural irreverence on The Daily Show author=Deggans, Eric publisher=St. Petersburg Times date=June 1, 2008 accessdate=November 7, 2008 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081214212553/http://www.tampabay.com/features/media/article545843.ece archivedate=December 14, 2008 deadurl=yes df=mdy-all}}</ref> (born March 5, 1966), known professionally as Aasif Mandvi ({{IPAc-en ˈ ɑː s ᵻ f _ ˈ m ɑː n d v i}}, {{respell AH sif _ MAHND vee}}), is a British-American actor and comedian. He began appearing as an occasional contributing correspondent on The Daily Show on August 9, 2006. On March 12, 2007, he was promoted to a regular List of The Daily Show correspondents correspondent.<ref>{{cite press release title=COMEDY CENTRALS THE DAILY SHOW WITH JON STEWART TAPS AASIF MANDVI TO JOIN THE DAILY SHOW NEWS TEAM AS A CORRESPONDENT  url=http://www.comedycentral.com/press/press_releases/2007/31207_aasif_mandvi_joins_tds.jhtml  date=March 12, 2007  publisher=Comedy Central  accessdate=November 7, 2008  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080102095837/http://www.comedycentral.com/press/press_releases/2007/31207_aasif_mandvi_joins_tds.jhtml  archivedate=January 2, 2008 }}</ref> He is the lead actor, co-writer and producer of the web series Halal in the Family, which premiered on Funny or Die in 2015, and an actor, writer and co-producer of the HBO comedy series The Brink (TV series) The Brink. Mandvi is also the author of the book No Lands Man.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/1452107912/  title=No Lands Man: Aasif Mandvi  publisher=Amazon.com}}</ref>

==Early life==
Mandvi was born in Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharashtra, India,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.livemint.com/2008/05/31005430/8216In-the-US-they-say-Ind.html title=In the US, they say Indians are always laughing publisher=Livemint date=May 31, 2008 author=Kilachand, Tara accessdate=November 7, 2008}}</ref> to a Dawoodi Bohra<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.mtviggy.com/interviews/interview-with-the-last-airbender%E2%80%99s-aasif-mandvi-part-ii/ title=Interview with The Last Airbenders Aasif Mandvi: Part II}}</ref> Muslim family.<ref name="tampa"/> His family moved to England, settling, when he was a year old, in the West Yorkshire city of Bradford, where his father, Hakim, had originally come to work in textiles research at Bradford University,<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.npr.org/player/v2/mediaPlayer.html?action=1&t=1&islist=false&id=361026157&m=361190374 title=NPR Media Player work=npr.org accessdate=September 2, 2015}}</ref> and later ran a corner shop, while his mother, Fatima, was a nurse.<ref name="Guardian20090607">{{cite news url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2009/jun/08/aasif-mandvi-tv-daily-show-usa title=I get to sit on the fence between cultures: Sarfraz Manzoor talks to Aasif Mandvi, the Daily Shows fake news correspondent author=Sarfraz Manzoor date=June 7, 2009 work=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last1=Deggans first1=Eric title=For Aasif Mandvi, cultural irreverence on The Daily Show url=http://www.tampabay.com/features/media/for-aasif-mandvi-cultural-irreverence-on-the-daily-show/545843 accessdate=28 June 2014 work=Tampa Bay Times date=31 May 2008}}</ref> Although Mandvi identifies himself as a "working-class kid from Bradford", he attended the independent Woodhouse Grove School.<ref name="Guardian20090607"/> His father grew frustrated with Margaret Thatchers Britain and moved his family to Tampa, Florida when Mandvi was 16.<ref name="Guardian20090607"/>

==Career==

===Early===
After graduating from the University of South Florida with a degree in Theatre,<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0541902/bio#trivia title=Asif Mandvi – Biography publisher=IMDB date=December 7, 2011}}</ref> Mandvi worked as a performer at Disneys Hollywood Studios Disney-MGM Studios at Walt Disney World Resort.<ref name="tampa"/> He later moved to New York City, where he began appearing in off-Broadway productions. During this time, he was active in the band Cowboys and Indian. He won an Obie Award for his critically acclaimed one-man show Sakinas Restaurant.<ref name="filmmaker">{{cite web url=http://www.filmmakermagazine.com/summer2008/25faces_5.php title=25 NEW FACES OF INDEPENDENT FILM publisher=Filmmaker Magazine year=2008  accessdate=November 7, 2008}}</ref>

On Broadway, Mandvi appeared as Ali Hakim in the 2002 production  of Oklahoma! directed by Trevor Nunn.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.allbusiness.com/services/amusement-recreation-services/4566365-1.html title=Aasif Mandvi: Giving Broadway a Persian Hello publisher=Backstage (magazine) Backstage date=March 29, 2002 author=Horwitz, Simi accessdate=November 7, 2008}}</ref> He also appeared in the play Homebody/Kabul by Tony Kushner. He portrayed Fritz Haber in the off-Broadway play Einsteins Gift.

Mandvi played Melchior in On the Razzle (play) On the Razzle by Tom Stoppard at the Williamstown Theatre Festival and appeared in the docudrama Guantanamo: Honor Bound to Defend Freedom at the Culture Project.  In 2012 Mandvi starred in Disgraced at Lincoln Centers Claire Tow Theater. He played the lead role of Amir, a Pakistani American lawyer struggling with his identity and Islam in the drama by Ayad Akhtar. The play went on to win the 2013 Pulitzer Prize for Drama.<ref>{{cite news title=Ayad Akhtars Disgraced, play about a dinner party gone awry, wins Pulitzer Prize for drama url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/theater_dance/ayad-akhtars-disgraced-wins-pulitzer-prize-for-drama/2013/04/15/defd1e6c-a5ff-11e2-9e1c-bb0fb0c2edd9_story.html  newspaper=Washington Post date=April 16, 2013 author=Associated Press}}</ref> Mandvi was nominated for a Lucille Lortel Award  for his role.

===Television===
Mandvi made his television debut as a doorman at the Miami Biltmore Hotel in the episode "Line of Fire" of the series Miami Vice. He has since appeared in numerous television shows including ER (TV series) ER, The Sopranos, Sex and the City, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation CSI, Oz (TV series) Oz, Ed (TV series) Ed, The Bedford Diaries, Jericho (2006 TV series) Jericho, Sleeper Cell (TV series) Sleeper Cell and various editions of Law & Order, including Law & Order: Criminal Intent Criminal Intent, Law & Order: SVU Special Victims Unit and Law & Order: Trial by Jury Trial by Jury. He was the book reader for audio editions of Salman Rushdies Shalimar the Clown (2005) and V. S. Naipauls Magic Seeds (2004). In 2011 he appeared in Taco Bell commercials.

In 2006, Mandvi auditioned for The Daily Show. He was hired immediately and appeared on the show the same day.<ref name="tampa"/> Mandvi became a regular correspondent in 2007. He often appears in segments satirizing and commenting on Islamic, Middle-Eastern, and South-Asian-related issues with such titles as "Senior Asian Correspondent," "Senior Middle East Correspondent," "Senior Foreign Looking Correspondent," and "Senior Muslim Correspondent."

In 2013, Mandvi was cast in a recurring role on the FOX romantic comedy, Us & Them.<ref>{{cite web last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=Aasif Mandvi & Michael Ian Black Join New Fox Series Us & Them url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/08/aasif-mandvi-michael-ian-black-join-new-fox-series-us-them/ publisher=Deadline accessdate=August 28, 2013}}</ref>

In October 2013, during a segment on The Daily Show, Mandvis interview with Don Yelton led to Yeltons resignation from the North Carolina Republican Party office.<ref>{{cite web title=GOP’s Don Yelton Resigns After ‘Lazy Blacks’ Remark on ‘Daily Show’ url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/BL-SEB-77898}}</ref>

In November 2014, he appeared on The Dan Patrick Show. On the show, he revealed the story from his book No Lands Man where his father moved his family to the U.S. because the U.S. has brunch.<ref>{{YouTube id=QdxeRfnymgI title=Aasif Mandvi on The Dan Patrick Show (Full Interview) 11/14/2014}}</ref>

Beginning in June 2015, Mandvi portrayed Rafiq Massoud in the HBO comedy series The Brink (TV series) The Brink. Mandvi also serves as a writer and co-producer on the series.<ref>{{cite web url=https://nypost.com/2015/06/21/why-aasif-mandvi-never-wanted-to-fill-jon-stewarts-shoes/ title=Why Aasif Mandvi never wanted to fill Jon Stewart’s shoes - New York Post work=New York Post accessdate=September 2, 2015}}</ref> In April 2015, Mandvi appeared on Person of Interest (TV series) Person of Interest as Sulaiman Khan, the CEO of a software security firm.

In 2016, Mandvi joined the climate change documentary show Years of Living Dangerously as one of its celebrity correspondents.<ref>{{cite web url=http://tvblogs.nationalgeographic.com/2015/09/17/critically-acclaimed-climate-change-series-years-of-living-dangerously-comes-to-nat-geo/  title=Critically Acclaimed Climate Change Series "Years of Living Dangerously" Comes to Nat Geo  accessdate=17 June 2016}}</ref>

In 2017, Mandvi guest-starred in two episodes of the first season of the Netflix series A Series of Unfortunate Events (TV series) A Series of Unfortunate Events as Montgomery "Uncle Monty" Montgomery,<ref name="Mandvi">{{cite web last1=Andreeva first1=Nellie title=Aasif Mandvi Cast In Netflix’s ‘A Series Of Unfortunate Events’ & USA’s ‘Mr. Robot’ url=http://deadline.com/2016/03/aasif-mandvi-cast-a-series-of-unfortunate-events-mr-robot-1201722925/ website=deadline.com publisher=Deadline Hollywood accessdate=15 February 2017}}</ref> a herpetologist and distant relative of the Baudelaire children. He returned to the role for one episode during season two.

===Film===
Mandvi played minor roles in the films The Siege (1998 film) The Siege and Die Hard with a Vengeance as well as the title role in Merchant Ivory Productions film The Mystic Masseur. He had a major supporting role in the independent film American Chai, playing the lead characters roommate, "Engineering Sam." He played the doctor who diagnosed Paul Vittis (Robert De Niro) panic attacks in Analyze This, and had a role as Mr. Aziz of "Joes Pizza" in Spider-Man 2. He was also in commercials by Dominos Pizza and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA). He played the Tone deafness tone deaf doorman Khan in Music and Lyrics.

Mandvi played a dentist alongside Ricky Gervais in the 2008 romantic comedy Ghost Town (2008 film) Ghost Town, as well as office employee Bob Spaulding in The Proposal (film) The Proposal, starring Sandra Bullock. ==Charity work==
Mandvi is involved with disaster-relief organizations such as the charity initiative Relief 4 Pakistan, which assists in flood relief in Pakistan. In 2010, he hosted the "Stand Up for Religious Freedom" comedy event to raise money for the organization.<ref>{{cite news last=Fuerstenberg first=Brittany title=Aasif Mandvi: Comic Relief for Pakistan url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/39832619/ns/us_news-giving/t/aasif-mandvi-comic-relief-pakistan/ accessdate=September 26, 2011 newspaper=MSNBC}}</ref> After the 2010 Haiti earthquake, he performed with fellow Daily Show correspondent Wyatt Cenac at Conan OBriens "Im with Coco" Benefit for earthquake victims. He is also a supporter of the Endometriosis Foundation of America.
