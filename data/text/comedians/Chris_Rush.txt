
Chris Rush  is an American comedian, writer, actor, radio personality and author. He is best known for his stand-up routines and albums, along with having been a writer and editor on the satirical publication National Lampoon (magazine) National Lampoon magazine.<ref name=LH-L>{{cite news title=Lampooner in town last= first= date=October 29, 2004 publisher=Lexington Herald-Leader page=3 accessdate=November 18, 2009}}</ref>

==Biography==

===Early life===
Rush was born in Brooklyn, New York.<ref name="youtube.com">{{YouTube wQGoFM-d-qU clip}}</ref> Rush is of Italian descent and was raised in the Catholic Church Roman Catholic faith.<ref name="youtube.com"/><ref>{{YouTube 0iN-GRTCPXU Second clip}}</ref> He attended Brooklyn Technical High School{{Citation needed date=March 2014}} and graduated from City College of New York in 1968 with a degree in Organic chemistry.<ref name=TMC>{{cite news title=Microscope guy is standing up to scrutiny last=Righi first=Len date=May 10, 1991 publisher=The Morning Call page=D.02 accessdate=November 14, 2009}}</ref> Before becoming a comedian Rush was a Molecular biology molecular biologist, working at Brooklyn Jewish Hospital for two years.<ref name=TMC /> He embarked on a professional comedy career following an open mic night at a comedy club.<ref name="natural-humor-medicine.com">[http://www.natural-humor-medicine.com/Chris_Rush.html Natural Humor Medicine: Chris Rush] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20041121000000/http://www.natural-humor-medicine.com/Chris_Rush.html  date=November 21, 2004 }}</ref>

===Personal life===
Rush is a conservationist and supporter of Greenpeace.<ref name=Record>{{cite news title=Spirituality with a smile last=Solimine first=Donna date= July 25, 1997 publisher=The Record (Bergen County) The Record page=31 accessdate=November 6, 2009}}</ref> Though raised in the Catholicism Catholic faith he left the church when in the seventh grade.<ref name="youtube.com"/> He considers himself to be a Taoism Taoist and can be seen wearing the Yin and yang symbol on his shirt during his performances.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.myspace.com/chrisrushcomedy title=Chris Rush (chrisrushcomedy) on Myspace publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==

===1970s & 1980s===
Chris Rush wrote for National Lampoon (magazine) National Lampoon Magazine in the early 1970s,<ref name=Record /> making his first appearance in the August, 1970 issue.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.marksverylarge.com/issues/7008.html title=National Lampoon Issue #5 - Paranoia publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref> During his time at the Lampoon he was involved in another comedy magazine titled Drool, which came out with just one issue in 1972.<ref>{{cite web url=http://sirrealcomix.mrainey.com/page/d/Drool01-1.htm title=Drool #1 first=Sir last=Real publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref> He left the magazine when he was signed to Atlantic Records by Ahmet Ertegun to release his first comedy album, First Rush in 1973.<ref>[http://www.wamcarts.org/artsched.html Wamcarts.org] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091204153734/http://www.wamcarts.org/artsched.html  date=December 4, 2009 }}</ref> In 1974 he once again contributed to a comedy magazine titled Harpoon.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.gusbooks.com/?page=shop/flypage&product_id=26890&CLSN_2380=13082873752380b769ad4fa179da72b0 title=HARPOON, September 1974 publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref> Towards the later years of the 1970s; Rush, was involved with Head Magazine.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.calibanbooks.com/pages/books/z08733/charlotte-faye-greenberg-mike-colasuonno-chris-rush-alan-earle/six-issues-of-head-magazine-from-1976-1978 title=Six Issues of Head Magazine from 1976-1978 by Charlotte Faye Greenberg, Mike Colasuonno Chris Rush, Alan on Caliban Books first= last=www.bibliopolis.com publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref>

Rush opened for musicians and bands including B.B. King, Meat Loaf, Talking Heads, Twisted Sister and Electric Light Orchestra while becoming a frequent performer at comedy clubs, theaters and colleges throughout the United States and Canada. He performed at such venues as The Bottom Line (venue) The Bottom Line,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.bottomlinecabaret.com/archive_index.html  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20020806115102/http://bottomlinecabaret.com/archive_index.html  dead-url=yes  archive-date=August 6, 2002  title=Welcome To The Bottom Line Nightclub  publisher=  accessdate=July 19, 2016  df=mdy-all }}</ref> Carolines, The Improv and The Comedy Store (California) The Comedy Store.<ref>{{cite news title=New York Comedian Rush A Pearl last= first= date=September 19, 1987 publisher=The News Journal page=2E accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref> In 1979 he was awarded "Best Male Comic" by the Association of Comedy Artists.<ref>{{cite news title=Ribald N.Y. comic set for Monday night last= first= date=November 8, 1984 publisher=The Register-Guard Eugene Register-Guard page=2D accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> He released his second album Beaming In in 1981. His performances landed him on national television with appearances on Comedy Tonight, Night Flight (TV series) Night Flight and Apt. 2C, a pilot for HBO starring his mentor and friend George Carlin<ref>{{cite news title=Comedic Anniversary date=June 23, 1989 publisher=The Post-Standard accessdate=November 6, 2009}}</ref>

===1990s & 2000s===
In the early to mid-1990s, Rush was involved in a series of shorts made for Comedy Central Comedy Centrals show Small Doses, titled Food for Thought. It starred Patton Oswalt and Blaine Capatch as two bumbling store clerks and Chris as the manager.<ref>{{YouTube pg6R1-96w3o Third Clip}}</ref> He was asked to contribute to Tim Allen Tim Allens book Im Not Really Here, which was released in 1996.<ref>{{cite web url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HEoe9MWqGwoC title=Im Not Really Here first=Tim last=Allen date=November 18, 1996 publisher=Hyperion accessdate=July 19, 2016 via=Google Books}}</ref> He released an additional comedy album in 1997. During this time, he made sporadic stand-up comedy appearances. He worked on Bob "Wolf" Wohlfelds show in the late 1990s on PYX 106 with The Wakin Up with the Wolf Show, where he was a co-host. Some of his bits were put onto an album released by the show, titled Chriss Head.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.madhousestudios.com/graphic/pyx106/cd/chrishead.html title=Chriss Head CD publisher= accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref> The relationship ended though when the station decided to fire Rush on December 21, 1998, after apparently speaking to management about Wohlfelds behavior towards him and others.<ref>{{cite news title=Wolf loses yet another sidekick last=McGuire first=Mark date=April 7, 1999 publisher=Times Union (Albany) The Times Union page=D5 accessdate=November 6, 2009}}</ref>

On October 1, 2007, Rush released his first book, Milking the Rhino (Dangerously Funny Lists).

In April, 2009 Chris launched a one-person show one-man show titled, Bliss: An Evening of Laughter with Chris Rush, which was financed by George Carlin before he died in June, 2008.<ref>[http://www.theatermania.com/new-york/shows/bliss-an-evening-of-laughter-with-chris-rush_154317/ Theatermania.com: Bliss: An Evening of Laughter with Chris Rush]</ref><ref>{{cite book title=Seven Dirty Words: The Life and Crimes of George Carlin author=Sullivan, James publisher=Da Capo Press page=224 year=2010 isbn=0-306-81829-9}}</ref>
