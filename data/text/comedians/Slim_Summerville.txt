
George Joseph Somerville (July 10, 1892 &ndash; January 5, 1946), known professionally as Slim Summerville, was an American film actor, best known as a comedy performer.<ref>{{cite book   first=Ray   last=Stuart   title=Immortals of the Screen   page=218   publisher=Sherbourne Press   year=1965}}
</ref>

==Life==
Born George Joseph Somerville in Albuquerque, New Mexico, his mother died when he was five.<ref name=pittpress>{{cite news  title=Sad-Looking Slim Summerville Never Hoped To Be Funny - Just Cant Help It  first=Paul  last=Harrison  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=bnYbAAAAIBAJ&sjid=1ksEAAAAIBAJ&pg=3961,5297533&dq=slim+summerville&hl=en  newspaper=The Pittsburgh Press  date=13 July 1936  accessdate=8 February 2012}}</ref>
Moving from New Mexico to Canada to Oklahoma, he had a nomadic upbringing.<ref name=pittpress/>

He married Gertrude Martha Roell on 19 November 1927.<ref name=warsaw/><ref name=spokesman>{{cite news  title=Sad Slim Summerville Loses Wife at Court  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=BChWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=r-MDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4465,534233&dq=slim+and+summerville+and+divorce&hl=en  newspaper=The Spokesman-Review  date=2 October 1936  accessdate=10 February 2012}}</ref>  In early 1932, the Summervilles adopted a four-week-old baby boy whom they christened Elliott George.<ref>{{cite news  title=Slum Summerville of Movies Adopts Baby  url=http://hollywoodheyday.blogspot.com/2008/10/february-6-1932.html  newspaper=Associated Press  date=6 February 1932  accessdate=9 February 2012}}</ref>  The couple divorced in September 1936,<ref name=spokesman/><ref>{{cite news  title=Wife Divorces Film Comedian  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=jXYbAAAAIBAJ&sjid=0ksEAAAAIBAJ&pg=4438,1061328&dq=slim+and+summerville+and+elliot+and+george&hl=en  newspaper=The Pittsburgh Press  date=2 October 1936  accessdate=10 February 2012}}</ref> and he then married Eleanor Brown (also a divorcee), who was his nurse, who cared for him when he was sick. She fell in love and married him in 1937, to keep him from getting sick again.<ref name=warsaw/><ref name=montreal/>

Summerville died of a stroke on January 5, 1946 in Laguna Beach, California.<ref name=warsaw/><ref name=sarasota/><ref>{{cite journal   journal=Motion Picture Herald   publisher=Quigley Pub. Co.   year=1946}}</ref> He is buried at Inglewood Park Cemetery in South Los Angeles community of Inglewood, California. Twenty years after his death, his beach front house on Sleepy Hollow Lane in Laguna Beach was converted into the Beach House restaurant,<ref>{{cite book   title=Life is a beach: a vacationers guide to the West Coast   first1=Parke   last1=Puterbaugh   first2=Alan   last2=Bisbort   publisher=McGraw-Hill   year=1988   page=74}}</ref> now the Driftwood Kitchen.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.yelp.com/biz/driftwood-kitchen-laguna-beach-2 title=Driftwood Kitchen - Laguna Beach, CA website=Yelp access-date=2016-05-22}}</ref>

==Career==
Summervilles first Job (role) job was as a Courier messenger for the Canadian Pacific Railway#Telegraph Canadian Pacific Telegraphs in Chatham, Ontario where he lived with his English people English grandparents.<ref name=montreal>{{cite news  title=Lanky Screen Comic Gets Romantic Break  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=eHYtAAAAIBAJ&sjid=i5gFAAAAIBAJ&pg=5799,2888429&dq=slim+and+summerville+and+eleanor&hl=en  newspaper=The Montreal Gazette  date=17 September 1941  accessdate=10 February 2012}}</ref>

He was working as a poolroom porter (carrier) porter when found by Edgar Kennedy,<ref name=warsaw>{{cite news  title=Stroke Fatal to Movie Star - "Slim" Summerville Dies Suddenly  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=6x5HAAAAIBAJ&sjid=nXoMAAAAIBAJ&pg=3639,349976&dq=slim+summerville+dies&hl=en  newspaper=Warsaw Daily Union  date=7 January 1946  accessdate=8 February 2012}}</ref> who took him to Mack Sennett where he started at $3.50 per day.<ref name=sarasota>{{cite news  title=Slim Summerville, Of The Movies, Dies  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=SOMcAAAAIBAJ&sjid=mWQEAAAAIBAJ&pg=2978,443417&dq=slim+summerville&hl=en  newspaper=Sarasota Herald-Tribune  date=7 January 1946  accessdate=8 February 2012}}</ref>
His first role was as a "Keystone Kops Keystone Kop" in Hoffmeyers Legacy (1912).

His tall, gangly appearance was well utilized in numerous short comedy films during the silent film era, and in addition to his many acting roles, he directed more than 50 short films.

Occasionally, he played in dramatic films, such as All Quiet on the Western Front (1930 film) All Quiet on the Western Front (1930) and Jesse James (1939 film) Jesse James (1939). However, he was most successful in comedy films, including several with ZaSu Pitts. He also played in films with Shirley Temple, Captain January (1936 film) Captain January (1936) and Rebecca of Sunnybrook Farm (1938 film) Rebecca of Sunnybrook Farm (1938).

==Legacy==
For his contribution to the motion picture industry, Slim Summerville has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6409 Hollywood Blvd.<ref>[http://www.walkoffame.com/slim-summerville Slim Summerville]. Hollywood Walk of Fame. Retrieved 2012-02-11</ref>

Inducted into the New Mexico Entertainment Hall of Fame in 2012.
