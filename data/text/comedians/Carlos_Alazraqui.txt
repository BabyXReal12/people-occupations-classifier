
Carlos Jaime Alazraqui (born July 20, 1962) is an American stand-up comedian, actor, voice actor, singer, impressionist, producer and screenwriter possibly best known as Deputy James Garcia on Reno 911!. His extensive voice-over work includes the role of Spyro from Spyro The Dragon, Bobbi Fabulous on Phineas and Ferb, the Taco Bell chihuahua in the Taco Bell commercials, Denzel Crocker and Juandissimo Magnifico on The Fairly OddParents, Rocko and Spunky on Rockos Modern Life, Lazlo and Clam in Camp Lazlo, Grandpapi Rivera in El Tigre: The Adventures of Manny Rivera, and as Mr. Weed in Family Guy. He is a weekly contributor on The Stephanie Miller Show.

==Early life==
Carlos Jaime Alazraqui was born on July 20, 1962 in Yonkers, New York<ref name=AlazraquiCSUS/> to Argentine American Argentine parents. He moved with his family to Concord, California at an early age. He attended college at California State University, Sacramento California State University, Sacramento, California from 1982-1986,<ref name=AlazraquiCSUS>{{cite web url=http://37prime.com/news/2017/07/28/carlos-alazraqui-qa-birthplace-video/ title=Carlos Alazraqui Q&A: Birthplace (Video) date=December 2009 first= last= publisher=37prime.news accessdate=July 28, 2017}}
*<sup>a</sup> "...then attended school, college in Sacramento after two years of junior college playing soccer and running track. I decided to do four years at Sacramento State from 82-86." — 00:58-01:08.</ref> where he began competing in open mic contests. After winning in his 4th year of competing at the San Francisco International Comedy Competition, Alazraqui took his prize money and moved to Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.sanfranciscocomedycompetition.com/Pages/WholeCarlos.html title=Carlos Alazraqui profile publisher=Sanfranciscocomedycompetition.com accessdate=2013-12-11}}</ref>

==Career==
===Voice work===
Alazraqui has performed several voices for Nickelodeon cartoons, including Rocko (Rockos Modern Life) Rocko on Rockos Modern Life, as well as Denzel Q. Crocker and Juandissimo Magnifico on The Fairly OddParents, Winslow T. Oddfellow and Lube on CatDog, and Scooter on SpongeBob SquarePants. He also voiced several additional voices for the Cartoon Network shows, Cow and Chicken, KIDSCITY: The Village Dome of Kids and I Am Weasel during their runs in the mid-late 1990s. He also voiced Spyro in Spyro the Dragon (video game) Spyro the Dragon. He was later replaced by Tom Kenny, who voiced Spyro in Spyro 2: Riptos Rage!, Spyro: Year of the Dragon, and Spyro: Enter the Dragonfly. He also voiced the Taco Bell chihuahua in the Taco Bell commercials,<ref>{{cite web  last = David  first = Long  title = Taco Bell Dog Story  url = http://www.everwonder.com/david/tacobell/story.html  postscript = <!-- Bot inserted parameter. Either remove it; or change its value to "." for the cite to end in a ".", as necessary. -->{{inconsistent citations}} }}</ref> List of ¡Mucha Lucha! characters Rikochet in the first two seasons of ¡Mucha Lucha! and Mr. Weed (the head of the "Happy Go Lucky" toy factory) on Family Guy. It is mentioned in the DVD commentary track that Alazraqui was reluctant to leave Family Guy. In particular, Seth MacFarlane suggests that the death of Alazraquis character Mr. Weed took the actor by surprise.

In 2005, he added the voices for two Cartoon Network series – The Life and Times of Juniper Lee where he played Monroe, a guardian dog with a Scottish English Scottish accent and three characters in Camp Lazlo: Lazlo (Camp Lazlo) Lazlo, Clam, and Chef McMuesli. In 2006, he added Salty Mike from Squirrel Boy and Walden in Wow! Wow! Wubbzy! on Nick, Jr. to his credits. He played Wisk in Glen Hills 2000 film version of L. Frank Baums The Life and Adventures of Santa Claus. He also provided the voice of Nestor in the Happy Feet fims.

Jeff "Swampy" Marsh, a storyboard writer for Rockos Modern Life, described Alazraquis normal voice as bearing "no accent at all". Marsh describes Alazraquis "Scottish accent" as "one of the best" and that he performs his other accents as "all very well". According to Marsh, Alazraqui uses various accents in his comedy routines.<ref>"[http://www.title14.com/rocko/contributors/interview.html Dan Abrams interview with Jeff "Swampy" Marsh]", The Rockos Modern Life FAQ</ref> He had worked for the Disney Channel on Handy Manny, where he provided the voice for Felipe, the bilingual Phillips screwdriver Phillips-head screwdriver and for Abuelito, Mannys grandfather. He was the original "voice guy" on the Stephanie Miller Show. His replacement was Jim Ward (voice actor) Jim Ward, who had previously substituted for him. In January 2008, Alazraqui returned to fill in for Jim Ward briefly while he recovered from surgery. In 2014, he voiced Mesmo and Torts in Mixels. He voices Tio Tortuga in Sheriff Callies Wild West.{{citation needed date=January 2014}}

In addition, Alazraqui joined Millers Sexy Liberal Comedy Tour in 2014, playing some of the dates with Jim Ward (voice actor) Jim Ward and he has a semi-regular segment on Millers radio show Coffee with Carlos.

===Television===
Alazraqui is known for his live-action work in Reno 911!, in which he portrayed Deputy James Garcia for five seasons (2003–08). He played the same role in the series spinoff film Reno 911! Miami. As a nod to this role, he also played a "bumbling" Reno cop in the CSI: Crime Scene Investigation episode "Let it Bleed (CSI episode) Let it Bleed". He has been a celebrity on the Tom Bergeron version of Hollywood Squares.{{citation needed date=January 2014}}

===Film===
Alazraqui wrote and starred in The Last White Dishwasher, a short film.
