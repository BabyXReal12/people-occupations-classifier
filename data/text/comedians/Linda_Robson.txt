
Linda Robson (born 13 March 1958) is an English actress and presenter, best known for playing Tracey Stubbs in the sitcom Birds of a Feather between 1989 and 1998 and 2014 onwards and her appearances as a weekly panellist on the ITV series Loose Women from 2012. 

In 2011, Robson appeared in the film Anuvahood as Ks mum Pauline. In 2012, Robson took part in the twelfth series of Im a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK TV series) Im a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!, coming in 9th place.

==Early life==
Robson was born in Islington, London to an English father and an Irish mother. Robson was educated at Ecclesbourne Primary School, Shelburne Secondary School for Girls, The Young Actors Theatre and the Anna Scher Theatre School, all in Islington. She has two sisters.

==Early career==
Robson had a non-speaking role as a young girl amidst a group of roaming survivors in the second series of the original BBC Survivors drama screened in 1976 and then appeared in Paulines Quirkes (her first regular appearance with Pauline Quirke) and the drama series The Crezz, the comedy drama Shine on Harvey Moon in 1982 and the IRA drama Harrys Game.

==Career==

===Television===
Robson subsequently appeared in programmes such as The Bill and Crossroads (soap opera) Crossroads. She appeared with Jenny Eclair and Dillie Keane in the original cast of Grumpy Old Women Live, a spin-off from the television series Grumpy Old Women, written by Jenny Eclair and Judith Holder. On 9 August 2007, she narrated a show for ITV, called Britains Youngest Brides. In July 2008, Robson entered Celebrity MasterChef and went out in the first round. 

In July 2010, Robson made a guest appearance in BBC drama series Casualty (TV series) Casualty, playing the mother of a young man with bipolar disorder. Robson was the first celebrity to arrive in Australia ahead of the 2012 series of Im a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK TV series) Im a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!, but was voted out on the 13th day in the camp on 23 November 2012, finishing in 9th place.

Robson made her debut as a guest panellist on Loose Women on 19 August 2003, appearing alongside Kaye Adams, Carol McGiffin & Terri Dwyer. Robson returned to the show as a guest panellist on 2 December 2010, before returning as an occasional panellist from 11 November 2011, soon later regular panellist Jenny Eclair left the show in June 2012, this meant Robson was made a regular panellist on 19 June 2012, replacing Eclair. As of 2016 Robson appears on Loose Women 1 or 2 times a week.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.itv.com/loosewomen/presenters/linda-robson  title=Linda Robson &#124; Loose Women  publisher=ITV  date=4 November 2013  accessdate=11 August 2014}}</ref> Robson made 3 appearances during Series 16, 28 appearances in Series 17, 44 appearances in Series 18, 35 appearances in Series 19 and 57 appearances in Series 20. Robson in Series 21, has so far as of 21 October 2016, made 8 appearances. On 12 November 2016 Robson appeared on Tipping Point Lucky Stars on ITV.

===Birds of a Feather===
{{main article Birds of a Feather}}
Robson played Tracey Stubbs in the BBC sitcom Birds of a Feather from 1989 to 1998, starring with Pauline Quirke and Lesley Joseph. In 2013, it was announced that Birds of a Feather was to return for a tenth series, this time airing on the ITV (TV network) ITV network. The first episode aired on 2 January 2014 to an audience of over 8 million. After a successful tenth series, it was announced in 2014 that Birds of a Feather would return for an eleventh series, which began on 26 December 2014.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/news/a555990/birds-of-a-feather-recommissioned-for-second-itv-series.html#~oB7pp3ytr7NhPj  title=Birds of a Feather recommissioned for second ITV series – TV News  work=Digital Spy  date=6 March 2014  accessdate=11 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.itv.com/presscentre/press-releases/birds-feather-recommissioned-second-series  title=Birds of a Feather recommissioned for second series &#124; presscentre  publisher=Itv.com  date=6 March 2014  accessdate=11 August 2014}}</ref> In March 2015, it was announced that ITV had commissioned a third revived series (twelfth overall) which began airing in January 2016.<ref>{{cite web last=Eames  first=Tom  url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/news/a634764/birds-of-a-feather-renewed-for-12th-series-by-itv.html#~p6JzUcgaezVaUG  title=Birds of a Feather renewed for 12th series by ITV – TV News  work=Digital Spy  accessdate=15 October 2015}}</ref> The Final Series has been ordered but will not air until late 2017 at the earliest.

===Radio===
Robson is an occasional guest presenter for BBC London 94.9. In November 2012, she played the part of Lisa, the hypochondriac mother of Luke (Daniel Mays) in Alexander Kirks play Told You I Was Ill, episode two in the series Living with Mother on BBC Radio 4.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b01nq4j3  title=BBC Radio 4 – Living with Mother, Series 2, Told You I Was Ill  publisher=BBC  date=19 June 2013  accessdate=11 August 2014}}</ref>

===Other work===
In November 2014, Robson was a part of Gareth Malones All Star Choir,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/3GcB6pTQ6QpNLFKFy0RC8JP/gareth-malone-forms-an-all-star-choir-for-the-official-bbc-children-in-need-single  title=Children in Need – Gareth Malone forms an all-star choir for the Official BBC Children in Need single  publisher=BBC  date=1 January 1970  accessdate=15 October 2015}}</ref> who released a cover version of Wake Me Up (Avicii song) Wake Me Up to raise money for the BBCs Children in Need.<ref>{{cite web author=Gareth Malone  url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2014/gareth-malone-all-star-choir  title=Gareth Malone to form All-Star Choir for official BBC Children in Need single 2014 – Media Centre  publisher=BBC  date=25 September 2014  accessdate=15 October 2015}}</ref> The single reached No.1 on the Official UK Singles Chart.

==Personal life==
Robson has lived in Islington, North London all her life.<ref>Loose Women, 29 January 2013</ref> She is married with three children and two grandchildren.

Robsons father was a chain-smoker, who died of lung cancer when he was 57.<ref>This Morning (TV series) This Morning, 24 January 2012</ref> Her mother also died of cancer in August 2012. In October 2013, Robson voiced an interactive video campaign for the British Lung Foundation aiming to ban smoking in cars with children on board in the United Kingdom, in memory of her father.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.blf.org.uk/Linda  title=Smoking in cars – Linda Robson  publisher=Blf.org.uk  accessdate=11 August 2014  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://archive.is/20131011231052/http://www.blf.org.uk/Linda  archivedate=11 October 2013  df=dmy-all }}</ref>{{citation needed date=October 2017}}<!-- WP:RS needed; removed link to WP:SPS (Twitter, FB, WP)-->

On an episode of This Morning (TV programme) This Morning, Robson matched the Guinness World Records Guinness World Record for filling as many pancakes as possible in the space of one minute. She managed to fill six pancakes.<ref>{{cite web author=Kirsty Mccormack  url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-2104321/Linda-Robson-Pauline-Quirk-stick-diets-attempt-break-world-record.html  title=Linda Robson and Pauline Quirk stick to their diets as they attempt to break a world record   work=Daily Mail  date=22 February 2012  accessdate=11 August 2014}}</ref>
