Joshua Michael Thomas (born 26 May 1987) is an Australian actor, writer and comedian living in Melbourne. In 2005, he won the Melbourne International Comedy Festivals Raw Comedy Competition.<ref>{{cite web  author=Stephens, Andrew   url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/comedy-festival/did-you-hear-the-one-about-the-comedian-who-broke-even/2007/04/07/1175366526660.html   title=Did you hear the one about the comedian who broke even?  work=The Age  date=2007-04-08   accessdate=2008-08-23}}</ref> He has since appeared on television numerous times, including as a regular and Generation Y team captain on Network Tens Talkin Bout Your Generation.<ref>[http://ten.com.au/talkin-bout-your-generation-Josh-Thomas.htm Talking Bout Your Generation – Josh Thomas]. Ten.com.au (2009-04-06). Retrieved on 2011-08-20.</ref> In 2013, Thomas created the award-winning ABC2 and Pivot (TV channel) Pivot television series Please Like Me, which he also co-wrote and starred in.<ref name="abc.net.au">[http://www.abc.net.au/tv/pleaselikeme/about/] ABC2 Homepage for Please Like Me TV series</ref>

==Early life==
Thomas was born in Blackwater, Queensland.<ref>{{cite web title=Panellist: Josh Thomas url=http://www.abc.net.au/tv/qanda/txt/s3242481.htm publisher=Q&A (Australian talk show) Q&A accessdate=15 July 2014}}</ref> He lived in Chapel Hill, Queensland Chapel Hill and Westlake, Queensland Westlake in Brisbanes western suburbs and attended Kenmore State High School, graduating in 2004. He studied for a Bachelor of Creative Industries, majoring in Television at the Queensland University of Technology, but dropped out after one year.{{citation needed date=January 2015}}

==Career==
In 2005 he won the Melbourne International Comedy Festivals RAW Comedy Award. He made the finals of So You Think Youre Funny at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe. The following year, he was selected to perform in The Comedy Zone, a showcase of Australias most promising up-and-coming comedians presented by the Melbourne International Comedy Festival.<ref>{{cite web  author=Slattery, Annette  url=http://www.thegroggysquirrel.com/articles/2006/04/13/2006-melbourne-international-comedy-festival-reviews/comedy-zone/  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070321150652/http://www.thegroggysquirrel.com/articles/2006/04/13/2006-melbourne-international-comedy-festival-reviews/comedy-zone/  dead-url=yes  archive-date=2007-03-21  title=Comedy Zone  work=The Groggy Squirrel  date=2006-04-28  accessdate=2008-08-30 }}</ref>

In 2007, Thomas first solo show, "Please Like Me" debuted at the Melbourne International Comedy Festival, where it received the Melbourne Airport Award for Best Newcomer.<ref>{{cite web  author=Ziffer, Daniel   url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/comedy-festival/jokes-aside-kitson-is-the-best/2007/04/29/1177787966861.html  title=Jokes aside, Kitson is the best  work=The Age  date=2007-04-30   accessdate=2008-08-23}}</ref>  His live shows have toured both nationally and internationally, appearing in both Edinburgh and Just For Laughs Montreals comedy festivals.<ref>{{cite web  author=Slattery, Annette  url=http://www.thegroggysquirrel.com/articles/2008/02/07/brisbane-gets-a-dose-of-festival-spirit/  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080724231640/http://www.thegroggysquirrel.com/articles/2008/02/07/brisbane-gets-a-dose-of-festival-spirit/  dead-url=yes  archive-date=2008-07-24  title=Brisbane gets a dose of Festival Spirit  work=The Groggy Squirrel  date=2008-02-07  accessdate=2008-08-30 }}</ref> In 2010, Thomas toured his coming-out themed stand-up show "Surprise", taking it to the Adelaide Fringe, Brisbane Comedy Festival and Melbourne International Comedy Festival.<ref>[http://www.joshthomas.com.au/#/gigs Josh Thomas web page]. joshthomas.com.au</ref>

2011 saw him touring Everything Ever at the Melbourne Comedy Festival, among other locations.<ref>[http://www.comedyfestival.com.au/2011/season/shows/josh-thomas-everything-ever/ Melbourne Comedy Festival webpage] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131215162505/http://www.comedyfestival.com.au/2011/season/shows/josh-thomas-everything-ever/  date=15 December 2013 }}</ref>

===Podcast===
Thomas has a podcast called Josh Thomas and Friend, available from iTunes, which features Thomas and his comedian friends Mel Buttle and Tom Ward. In November 2009 a second series of the podcast was released on iTunes, and the first series was deleted. The third series was released in 2011, and the second series was deleted as well.{{citation needed date=September 2016}}

===Television===
Thomas has made guest appearances on the Australian television shows The Sideshow, Stand Up Australia, Ready Steady Cook, Good News Week, Rove Live, Celebrity Splash, The Project (Australian TV program) The Project and Hughesy, We Have a Problem.<ref name=token-profile />

In 2009, Thomas became a regular and Generation Y team captain on Network Tens Talkin Bout Your Generation.<ref name=token-profile /> He also competed in Celebrity MasterChef Australia, but lost in the first heat to Kirk Pengilly of INXS.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/entertainment/tv-radio/articles/2009/10/01/1253989983149.html title=INXS Kirk Pengilly wins MasterChef heat last=AAP date=1 October 2009 work=The Age publisher=Fairfax accessdate=2009-10-02}}</ref>

In March 2011, Thomas hosted the 2011 Melbourne International Comedy Festival Gala event, which is held annually in support of Oxfam.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ACR_9s1in2E ]{{dead link date=August 2011}}</ref>

Thomas has appeared several times on the ABC discussion panel program Q&A (Australian talk show) Q&A. In a program exploring mental health issues in rural areas, he challenged Federal MP Bob Katters views on homosexuality and his previous declaration that there are no homosexuals in his electorate. Katters refusal to consider the issues of gay young people in a discussion of suicide and mental health problems was received poorly by the audience and fellow panelists.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.smh.com.au/federal-politics/political-news/bob-katter-struggles-for-words-on-qa-about-mental-health-problems-faced-by-gay-community-20141007-10r5n2.html title=Bob Katter struggles for words on Q&A about mental health problems faced by gay community date=7 October 2014 newspaper=Sydney Morning Herald publisher=Fairfax Media author=Kembrey, M. accessdate=7 October 2014}}</ref>

==== Please Like Me ====
{{Main Please Like Me}}
In February 2013, the television series Please Like Me, written by Thomas, debuted on ABC2. Participant Medias television network Pivot (TV channel) Pivot acquired the series for the US and premiered all six episodes as a binge marathon on 1 August 2013 to celebrate the channels launch after premiering the first episode online.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/24/josh-thomas-please-like-me-australian-tv-series_n_3645610.html title=Please Like Me, Australian TV Series, Explores Coming-Of-Age And Sexuality (VIDEO) work=The Huffington Post accessdate=22 March 2015}}</ref>  The initial six-part series is based on his stand-up comedy shows and stars Thomas as himself.<ref name="abc.net.au" />

In 2014, the series was nominated for an International Emmy Award for best comedy series International Emmy Award for Best Comedy Series.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/awards/emmys/please-like-me-from-josh-thomas-could-get-an-international-emmy/news-story/e5ae2d2b0e309acf6fe11322a4d3db89 title=Please like Josh’s show for an Emmy newspaper=NewsComAu access-date=2017-02-10 language=en}}</ref> For his work on the series, Thomas has won various accolades, including an AACTA Awards AACTA Award for Best Television Screenplay in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm3436975/awards title=Josh Thomas website=IMDb access-date=2017-02-10}}</ref>

On 2 February 2017 Josh Thomas announced on social media that season 4 was the last, stating that "we are really happy with what weve made and feel like it is complete".<ref>http://www.facebook.com/officialjoshthomas/posts/10155094048974701</ref>
