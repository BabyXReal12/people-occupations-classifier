Andy Kindler (born October 16, 1956) is an United States American comedian from Queens, New York.<ref>{{cite web  title=Comedy-Contact Biography Page  url=http://www.comedycontact.com/kindler.html  accessdate=2007-10-23  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928075540/http://www.comedycontact.com/kindler.html  archivedate=2007-09-28  deadurl=yes  df= }}</ref>

==Stand up==
His material often covers the comedy industry itself, criticizing other comedians for being too predictable. At his annual state of the industry address at Montreals "Just For Laughs" festival, he criticized Dane Cook, Louis CK, Chelsea Handler and Jay Leno.<ref>{{cite web title=Audio interview: Andy Kindler goes after Louis C.K., state of comedy and more (Exclusive) url=http://www.laughspin.com/2012/07/30/audio-interview-andy-kindler-goes-after-louis-c-k-state-of-comedy-and-more-exclusive/ work=Laughspin accessdate=April 25, 2013 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130617095410/http://www.laughspin.com/2012/07/30/audio-interview-andy-kindler-goes-after-louis-c-k-state-of-comedy-and-more-exclusive/ archivedate=June 17, 2013 df=}}</ref>

==Acting==
He played the character "Andy", a fellow sportswriter and friend of sportswriter "Ray Barone" (Ray Romano) on the TV show Everybody Loves Raymond, was a regular guest on Late Show with David Letterman, contributor to The Daily Show and has performed on HBO. He is also known for his annual "State of the Industry" address at the Just for Laughs comedy festival in Montreal. In 2010, Kindler was a judge on the stand-up reality show Last Comic Standing. In 2010 he appeared on several episodes of Wizards of Waverly Place as Chancellor Tootietootie.

As a voice actor, Kindler has made numerous appearances on Dr. Katz, Professional Therapist Dr. Katz and Home Movies (TV series) Home Movies from producer Loren Bouchard.  He regularly voices the character of Mort, a mortician and the Belchers neighbor, on Bouchards show Bobs Burgers.

Kindler is often thought to have portrayed the character of "Jamison" in the WWE World Wrestling Federation, but the character was portrayed by a different actor by the name of John DiGiacomo.<ref>{{cite web title=Kindler Talks About "Jamison" url=http://andykindler.blogs.com/blah_blah_blog/2008/01/february-made-m.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://pojoslam.blogspot.com/2010/08/interview-with-jameson-part-1.html title=Real Jameson Actor Revealed}}</ref>

He plays a fictionalized version of himself in the IFC series Maron (TV series) Maron.<ref>{{cite web title=Maron url=http://www.ifc.com/shows/maron/blog/2013/05/watch-judd-hirsch-and-marc-maron-in-a-new-clip-from-maron work=IFC accessdate=May 23, 2013}}</ref>
