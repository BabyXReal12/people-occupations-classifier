==Early life==
Zamata was raised in Indianapolis, Indiana, and is a graduate of Pike High School.<ref name="indy"/> She is the daughter of Ivory Steward and Henry Moore, a lieutenant colonel in the Air Force.<ref>{{cite web last=Lindquist  first=David  url=http://www.indystar.com/story/entertainment/movies/2014/01/08/local-snl-mom-says-daughter-is-ready-for-late-night-spotlight/4381769/  title=Sasheer Zamatas mom: SNL player is late-night ready  work =The Indianapolis Star date=9 January 2014  accessdate=2015-03-09}}</ref> She stated her parents named her after the alien flower-like crystal called the "Sahsheer" from the Star Trek: The Original Series Star Trek episode "By Any Other Name".<ref>ABCs Match Game Season 2 Episode 12, aired 14 May 2017; Zamata was a panelist for this episode, and the topic of her name came up when introduced by host Alec Baldwin.</ref> She attended the University of Virginia, and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree.<ref name="nydn-path"/><ref name="uva-graduates"/> Zamata was a founding member of the Universitys long-form improv comedy troupe, Amuse Bouche.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.4mcveys.com/bouche/?page_id=187  title=Friends & Alumni &#124;  publisher=4mcveys.com  date=  accessdate=2015-03-09}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
In 2014, Zamata joined the cast of NBCs Saturday Night Live (SNL) during its Saturday Night Live (season 39) 39th season. She joined the Saturday Night Live cast members SNL cast amid a season in which black cast members Jay Pharoah and Kenan Thompson, TV critics, and other black comedians had criticized Saturday Night Live for not being ethnically diverse (specifically, not hiring any black female cast members at the start of the new season and only having five of them in the near-40 years the show has been on the air).<ref name="time" />  Zamata is the first black female cast member on SNL since Maya Rudolph departed midway through the shows Saturday Night Live (season 33) 33rd season in 2007.<ref name="time"/><ref name=TVGuide>{{cite web last=Gennis first=Sadie title=Kenan Thompson Blames SNLs Diversity Issue on Lack of Talented Black Comediennes url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/SNL-Diversity-Issue-Kenan-Thompson-1072056.aspx publisher=TV Guide accessdate=January 7, 2014}}</ref><ref name="dh2" />

Zamata debuted on SNL as a featured player in the January 18, 2014 episode hosted by Drake (rapper) Drake,<ref name="dh"/> in which she impersonated Rihanna.<ref>{{cite web last=Thomas  first=Rebecca  url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1720740/saturday-night-live-sasheer-zamata-debut.jhtml  title=Saturday Night Live: Sasheer Zamata Debuts, But Howd She Do? - Music, Celebrity, Artist News  publisher=MTV.com  date=January 19, 2014  accessdate=January 31, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last=Chan  first=Robert  url=https://tv.yahoo.com/blogs/tv-news/sasheer-zamata--the-first-black-woman-on--snl--in-6-years--debuts-151818391.html  title=Sasheer Zamata, the First Black Woman on SNL in 6 Years, Makes Her Debut &#124; Yahoo TV - Yahoo TV  publisher=Tv.yahoo.com  date=January 19, 2014  accessdate=January 31, 2014}}</ref> She was promoted to repertory player in the first episode of Saturday Night Live (season 41) Season 41.<ref name=splitsider>{{cite web work=Splitsider.com date=2015 title=SNL Promotes Beck, Bennett and Sasheer to Repertory Players url=http://splitsider.com/2015/10/snl-promotes-beck-bennett-and-sasheer-zamata-to-repertory-players/}}</ref> Some of her recurring characters include Janelle, a teenage girl who hosts a YouTube channel called How 2 Dance with Janelle but is unaware of how her developing sexuality is coming off to viewers, and Keeley, a contestant on an African-American centric version of Jeopardy! called Black Jeopardy! Some of her celebrity impressions have included the aforementioned Rihanna, Michelle Obama, Nicki Minaj, Beyonce Knowles, Solange Knowles, Diana Ross, Lupita Nyongo, and Taraji P. Henson. She left the show in 2017 after the season finale of season 42.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.etonline.com/news/217983_exclusive_sasheer_zamata_leaving_saturday_night_live/ title= Sasheer Zamata Leaving Saturday Night Live date=May 21, 2017 work=Entertainment Tonight author =Antoinette Bueno}}</ref>

===The ACLU and Work with Womens Rights===
Zamata was named a celebrity ambassador to the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) in 2015, and began work with the Womens Rights Project.<ref>http://www.eurweb.com/2015/10/snls-sasheer-zamata-is-aclu-celebrity-ambassador-on-womens-rights/#</ref> The ACLU Womens Rights project seeks to break down gender biases and "ensure equal economic opportunities, educational equity, and an end to gender-based violence".<ref>[https://www.aclu.org/issues/womens-rights "Womens Rights"], American Civil Liberties Union</ref> She has spoken out on gender discrimination in the U.S. today, and has advocated for equal rights among women of color, in particular.<ref>https://www.aclu.org/blog/speak-freely/saturday-night-lives-sasheer-zamata-lets-talk-about-privilege</ref> In an interview with Allure Magazine, Zamata opened up about her experiences with discrimination and colorism.<ref name="allure.com">http://www.allure.com/story/sasheer-zamata-talks-diversity-in-hollywood</ref> Though often stigmatized for her darker skin tone, she hopes to turn her experiences into a message of self-acceptance and body positivity for women in all shades.<ref name="allure.com"/>
