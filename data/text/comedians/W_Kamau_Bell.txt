==Early life and education==
Bell grew up in Alabama, Boston and Chicago, the son of Walter Bell (businessman) Walter Bell, who served as Alabamas Insurance commissioner Insurance Commissioner and Chairman of Swiss Re America Holding Corporation, and author Janet Cheatham Bell, who founded a self-publishing firm.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://articles.philly.com/2013-07-12/news/40516514_1_fxx-chicago-laboratory-schools-one-man-show title=Comic Bell brings his total bias here website=philly-archives access-date=2016-04-22 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150915135437/http://articles.philly.com/2013-07-12/news/40516514_1_fxx-chicago-laboratory-schools-one-man-show archivedate=15 September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.theclimategroup.org/what-we-do/interviews/walter-bell/ title=The Clean Revolution is achievable and essential website=www.theclimategroup.org access-date=2016-04-22 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150906130840/https://www.theclimategroup.org/what-we-do/interviews/walter-bell/ archivedate=6 September 2015}}</ref> Between ages five and 12, Bell lived in Boston, Massachusetts in the Mattapan neighborhood of the city.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.bostonmagazine.com/arts-entertainment/blog/2016/10/14/w-kamau-bell-boston-trump/ title=W. Kamau Bell on Growing Up in Boston, Donald Trumps Rise, and Tom Brady website=Boston Magazine access-date=2016-10-15}}</ref> Bell graduated from the University of Chicago Laboratory Schools and attended the University of Pennsylvania before dropping out. Later he began to pursue a career in comedy.<ref>[http://www.avclub.com/article/w-kamau-bell-bruce-lee-and-why-he-didnt-know-jimi--236560W. Kamau Bell on Bruce Lee and why he didn’t know Jimi Hendrix was black] AV Club.</ref>

He is Cousin#1stOnceRem first cousin once removed of speculative fiction writer N. K. Jemisin.<ref>Bell, W. Kamau. The Awkward Thoughts of W. Kamau Bell. New York, NY: Dutton, 2017. pp. 24, 28. {{ISBN 978-1-101-98587-8}}.</ref>

== Career ==
After establishing himself as a sociopolitical comedian and community activist based in San Francisco, Bell became a founding member of the comedy collective Laughter Against the Machine and has been featured in a number of prominent podcasts and publications, such as WTF with Marc Maron, Citizen Radio, and Current TVs The War Room with Jennifer Granholm, on which Bell was a regular correspondent.

His first comedy album, One Night Only, was released in 2007. Also in 2007, he developed a One-person show one-man show entitled The W. Kamau Bell Curve: Ending Racism in About An Hour, "a comedic exploration of the current state of America’s racism", which he periodically performs in updated versions.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web url=https://thebottomline.as.ucsb.edu/2015/02/ending-racism-in-about-an-hour-with-w-kamau-bell title=Ending Racism in About an Hour with W. Kamau Bell  work=The Bottom Line last=Lemon first=Bryn date=2015-02-11 access-date=2016-06-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.wkamaubell.com/calendar/2016/3/24/st-paul-mn-the-w-kamau-bell-curve-ending-racism-in-about-an-hour title=St. Paul, MN – Presenting The W. Kamau Bell Curve: Ending Racism in about an Hour website=W. Kamau Bell access-date=2016-06-29}}</ref>

His follow-up album, Face Full of Flour, was released in 2010, and was named one of the Top 10 Best Comedy Albums of the year by iTunes and Punchline Magazine.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://tempeimprov.com/event.cfm?id=335501 title=W. Kamau Bell website=tempeimprov.com access-date=2016-06-29 quote=Kamau has also released two stand-up comedy CDs, ONE NIGht ONLY & Face Full of Flour which was named one of the Ten Best Comedy Albums of 2010 by both iTunes & Punchline Magazine.}}</ref> From 2011 to 2012, he wrote the blog "Kamaus Komedy Korner" for the San Francisco Weekly.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.sfweekly.com/sanfrancisco/ArticleArchives?author=2317616 title=San Francisco News and Events  work=SF Weekly access-date=2016-06-29}}</ref> In 2012, Bell was voted San Franciscos best comedian by the SF Weekly, the San Francisco Bay Guardian, and 7x7 (magazine) 7×7 Magazine.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/author/w-kamau-bell title=W. Kamau Bell website=www.huffingtonpost.com access-date=2016-06-29 quote=}}</ref>

His first stand-up comedy Television special TV special, Semi-Prominent Negro, premiered on Showtime on April 29, 2016,<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.sho.com/titles/3429029/w-kamau-bell-semi-prominent-negro title=W. Kamau Bell: Semi-Prominent Negro {{!}} SHOWTIME website=SHO.com access-date=2016-06-29}}</ref> and on September 30, 2016, his third full-length album, also titled Semi-Prominent Negro, was released on Kill Rock Stars.<ref>{{cite web title=W. On November 7, 2017, Bell became the Frank Sinatra Artist-in-Residence of Santa Clara University in Santa Clara, California.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.mercurynews.com/2017/10/05/kamau-w-bell-named-santa-clara-university-artist-in-residence/ title=W. === Television host ===
From 2012 to 2013, Bell hosted a weekly Stand-up comedy stand up comedy television series, Totally Biased with W. Kamau Bell, first on FX and later on FXX.<ref name="LAT">{{cite web work=Los Angeles Times url=http://articles.latimes.com/2013/nov/13/entertainment/la-et-st-chris-rockproduced-totally-biased-with-wkamau-bell-canceled-20131113 title=Chris Rocks Totally Biased with W. Kamau Bell canceled date=November 13, 2013 first=Greg last=Braxton}}</ref> The show, produced by Chris Rock, provided observational comedy and commentary on social and political issues in addition to celebrity interviews. After the show was cancelled in November 2013,<ref name="LAT" /> Bell moved back to California from New York.<ref>{{cite web last1=Sale first1=Anna title=W. Kamau Bell Wonders How Much Is Enough url=http://www.wnyc.org/story/w-kamau-bell-death-sex-money/ website=Death, Sex & Money publisher=wnyc.org accessdate=2 July 2015 ref=wnyc}}</ref>

He is currently the host of the CNN documentary series United Shades of America, which premiered on April 24, 2016 and was renewed for a third season in 2017. The show, of which Bell is an Executive Producer, was awarded the Creative Arts Emmy for Outstanding Unstructured Reality Program in 2017.

=== Implicit bias incident in 2015 ===

On January 26, 2015, Bell and his wife Melissa were involved in an incident at the Elmwood Cafe in Berkeley, California, which Bell alleged was racially motivated.<ref>http://www.berkeleyside.com/2015/01/29/comedian-w-kamau-bell-reports-being-subject-of-racism-at-berkeleys-elmwood-cafe</ref> An employee of the cafe told Bell to "scram", or words to that effect, while he was talking to his white wife and her friends at one of its outdoor tables.<ref>https://www.berkeleyside.com/2015/01/29/comedian-w-kamau-bell-reports-being-subject-of-racism-at-berkeleys-elmwood-cafe</ref> The cafe owner and Bell held a community forum to discuss implicit bias and how to improve.<ref>http://www.berkeleyside.com/2015/03/16/at-community-forum-in-berkeley-w-kamau-bell-and-elmwood-cafe-launch-implicit-bias-training-initiative</ref> Bells account was eventually featured in an episode of the radio series This American Life entitled Birds & Bees.<ref name="TAL">{{cite web work=This American Life title=557: Birds & Bees Transcript date=May 15, 2015 url=http://www.thisamericanlife.org/radio-archives/episode/557/transcript}}</ref> After a Criticism of Starbucks#Philadelphia arrests similar incident resulted in the arrests of two black men at a Starbucks in April 2018, Bell renewed his criticism of the Elmwood Cafe, and it closed shortly thereafter.<ref>http://www.berkeleyside.com/2018/04/20/elmwood-cafe-closed-as-2015-racism-charge-gets-new-life-with-starbucks-arrest</ref><ref>http://www.berkeleyside.com/2018/04/20/opinion-whatever-the-reason-it-closed-the-elmwood-cafe-missed-a-real-opportunity-to-be-a-force-for-change-in-berkeley</ref><ref>http://www.berkeleyside.com/2018/04/20/after-closure-elmwood-cafe-owner-says-worker-co-op-nonprofit-could-take-over</ref>
