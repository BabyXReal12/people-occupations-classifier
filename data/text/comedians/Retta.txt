Marietta Sangai Sirleaf<ref name=NBC-ParksRec-Bio>{{cite news title=Cast: Donna Meagle, Played by Retta url=https://www.nbc.com/parks-and-recreation/credits/character/donna-meagle work=Parks and Recreation publisher=NBC}}</ref> (born April 12, 1970), better known as Retta, is an American stand-up comedy stand-up comedian and actress. She is best known for her role as Donna Meagle on NBCs Parks and Recreation. She has appeared in several films and television shows, and has performed stand-up on Comedy Centrals Premium Blend. She stars as Ruby Hill in NBCs Good Girls (TV series) Good Girls.<ref name=Vogue-GoodGirlsS1-2018>{{cite news last1=Read first1=Bridget title=Retta Moves on From Parks and Recreation in Good Girls, But Never Say Never url=https://www.vogue.com/article/retta-good-girls-nbc-parks-and-recreation-interview work=Vogue (magazine) Vogue date=26 February 2018 language=en}}</ref>

==Early life==
Retta was born in Newark, New Jersey. She is of Liberian descent.<ref name=WashingtonPost-Aunt-Nobel-2011>{{cite news last1=Chaney first1=Jen title=What’s the connection between a Nobel Prize winner and ‘Parks and Recreation’? url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/celebritology/post/whats-the-connection-between-a-nobel-prize-winner-and-parks-and-recreation/2011/10/07/gIQAjGkoSL_blog.html work=The Washington Post date=8 October 2011}}</ref> She grew up around New Jersey, specifically in Edison, New Jersey Edison and Cliffwood Beach.<ref name=NJ-GeeksWhoDrink-JerseyRoots-2015>{{cite news last1=Feuer first1=Ryan title=Comedian Retta talks Jersey roots, Parks and Rec and Geeks Who Drink url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2015/07/comedian_retta_talks_jersey_roots_parks_and_rec_an.html work=NJ.com date=16 July 2015}}</ref> In 1988, Retta graduated from Matawan Regional High School in Aberdeen Township, New Jersey.<ref name=NJ-GeeksWhoDrink-JerseyRoots-2015 /><ref name=MatawanHS-Pillar-1998>{{cite book title=Pillar 1998 date=1988 publisher=Matawan Regional High School pages=99, 125, 144, 208, 224 url=http://www.digifind-it.com/matawan/DATA/yearbooks/1988.pdf}}</ref> In 1992, Retta graduated from Duke University in Durham, North Carolina, where she was pre-med and graduated with a degree in sociology.<ref name=NJ-GeeksWhoDrink-JerseyRoots-2015 /><ref name=DukeMagazine-ParksRec-2014>{{cite news last1=Fishbein first1=Anne title=Chatting With "Parks and Recreations" Retta Sirleaf 92 url=http://dukemagazine.duke.edu/article/chatting-with-parks-and-recreations-retta-sirleaf-92 work=Duke Magazine publisher=Duke University date=25 September 2014 language=en}}</ref><ref name=Essence-ParksRec-7Reasons-2012>{{cite news last1=Sangweni first1=Yolanda title=7 Reasons You Should Know Parks and Recreation Star, Retta url=https://www.essence.com/2012/09/20/7-reasons-you-should-know-parks-and-recreation-star-retta work=Essence (magazine) Essence date=20 September 2012 language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
After graduating from Duke, Retta worked as a chemist. In her spare time, she did stand-up at Charlie Goodnights Comedy Club in Raleigh, North Carolina.<ref name=DukeToday-LunchStudents-2015>{{cite news last1=Holder first1=Christina title=Retta Sirleaf Returns to Duke url=https://today.duke.edu/2015/02/retta-sirleaf-returns-duke work=Duke Today publisher=Duke University date=25 February 2015 language=en format=includes video}}</ref> Eventually she moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in comedy.<ref name="RPN">{{Cite news last=Azzi  first=Amy  title=Comedian took long route to comedy  publisher=University of Wisconsin-Whitewater  work=Royal Purple News  date=January 28, 2009  url=http://media.www.royalpurplenews.com/media/storage/paper1225/news/2009/01/28/WebExtras/Comedian.Took.Long.Route.To.Comedy-3599833.shtml  accessdate=November 17, 2009 }}{{dead link date=April 2017  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Retta started performing stand-up comedy in 1996, although she said she did not start "earning money" until 1998, when she began touring on the college circuit. Retta said she used to get "really nauseated" before a performance, but that the feeling passed with experience.<ref name="Eells">{{Cite news last=Eells  first=Kelly Feser  title=T-150 seats down for stand-up guys  work=Ojai Valley News  date=June 6, 2003  url=http://www.ojaivalleynews.com/issues2003/06-June2003/06-06-03/06-06-03news5.html  accessdate=November 17, 2009  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723032720/http://www.ojaivalleynews.com/issues2003/06-June2003/06-06-03/06-06-03news5.html  archivedate=July 23, 2011  df= }}</ref> Retta has said her stand-up material tends to be slightly embellished stories from her regular everyday life, family, and friends.<ref name="RPN" /><ref name="UIS">{{Cite news last=Dunn  first=Valeree  title=Performer Spotlight on... Retta  work=UIS Journal  date=October 14, 2009  url=http://www.uis.edu/journal/artsEntertainment/2009/20091014spotlightonretta.html  accessdate=November 17, 2009  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5yBlCXaLK?url=http://www.uis.edu/journal/artsEntertainment/2009/20091014spotlightonretta.html  archivedate=April 24, 2011  df= }}</ref> Retta has stated she would give up stand-up comedy for a full-time acting career if possible: "Im not married to stand-up, just because its a road thing. Its very lonely with all the traveling."<ref name="Echo">{{Cite news  last=Haynes  first=Robert  title=Comedian Retta brings glee to bear country  publisher=University of Central Arkansas work=The Echo  date=November 17, 2009}}</ref>

Retta has served as the opening act for such comedians as Shirley Hemphill and Bobby Collins.<ref name="Eells" /> She has made television appearances on Bravos Welcome to the Parker, E! E! Entertainment Televisions The Soup,<ref name="UIS" /> Freddie (TV series) Freddie, Moesha, Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia,<ref name="Echo" /> the "Comedy Divas Showcase" segment of The Jenny Jones Show,<ref>{{Cite news last=Ory  first=Marie  title=Laughter -- the medicine of life  work=The Nicholls Worth  date=March 15, 2001  url=http://media.www.thenichollsworth.com/media/storage/paper262/news/2001/03/15/Lagniappe/Laughter.The.Medicine.Of.Life-55719.shtml  accessdate=November 17, 2009 }}{{dead link date=April 2017  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Retta has performed on Premium Blend, a Comedy Central show featuring up-and-coming comedians.<ref name="Eells" />

In 2009, Retta started making regular guest appearances on the NBC comedy series Parks and Recreation as Donna Meagle, an employee in the Parks Department of the fictional town, Pawnee, Indiana. During a stand-up performance at the University of Illinois at Springfield, Retta said the acting job on the show was stressful because it was unclear how long the show would stay on the air, due to the poor reviews it received during the Parks and Recreation (season 1) first season.<ref name="UIS" /> Alan Sepinwall, a television columnist with The Star-Ledger, said Parks and Recreation (season 2) season 2 episodes of Parks and Recreation afforded more personality and funnier jokes to Donna and other minor characters.<ref>{{Cite news  last=Sepinwall  first=Alan  title=Parks and Recreation, "Ron and Tammy": Megan Mullally guests  work=The Star-Ledger  date=November 5, 2009  url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/tv/index.ssf/2009/11/parks_and_recreation_ron_and_t.html  publisher=New Jersey On-Line  accessdate=November 6, 2009}}</ref> She was upgraded to a full-time regular cast member in the third season.<ref name="NJ">{{cite web  url= http://www.nj.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2015/07/comedian_retta_talks_jersey_roots_parks_and_rec_an.html title= Comedian Retta talks Jersey roots, Parks and Rec and Geeks Who Drink last1= Feuer first1=  Ryan last2=  first2=  date= 16 July 2015 website= Nj.com publisher=  access-date= 13 December 2015 quote=}}</ref>

Retta also hosted the 3rd Critics Choice Television Awards in 2013.<ref>{{cite web  url= http://www.cbs.com/shows/big_bang_theory/news/1000743/ title= Awards News: Critics Choice Nominations last1=  first1=  last2=  first2=  date=  website= CBS publisher=  access-date= 13 December 2015 quote=}}</ref> In 2014, she appeared on Hollywood Game Night as a contestant along with the other celebrities Paget Brewster, Michael Chiklis, Mario Lopez, Thomas Lennon (actor) Thomas Lennon, and Alyssa Milano.<ref>{{cite web  url= http://www.ew.com/article/2014/01/15/retta-mario-lopez-hollywood-game-night title= Retta takes on Mario Lopez in the Olympics on Hollywood Game Night -- EXCLUSIVE last1= Snetiker first1= Marc last2=  first2=  date= 15 January 2014  website= Ew.com publisher=  access-date= 13 December 2015 quote=}}</ref>

In 2015, Retta was one of the audiobook narrators for Welcome to Night Vale (novel) Welcome to Night Vale, a novel tie-in to the Welcome to Night Vale eponymous podcast series in which she is the voice of Old Woman Josie.<ref name="NJ"/> The same year she appeared in season 2 of Bravo (U.S. TV network) Bravos Girlfriends Guide to Divorce.<ref name="NJ"/>

In 2018, Retta co-starred in the NBC series, Good Girls (TV series) Good Girls, opposite Christina Hendricks and Mae Whitman.<ref name=Vogue-GoodGirlsS1-2018 />
