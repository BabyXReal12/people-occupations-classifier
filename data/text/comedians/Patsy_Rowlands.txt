
Patricia Amy "Patsy" Rowlands (19 January 1931 &ndash; 22 January 2005)<ref name=bbc>{{Cite web   url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/4203011.stm   title=BBC News - Carry On Star Patsy Rowlands dies   accessdate=21 November 2013 }}</ref><ref name=IndObit >{{Cite web   url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/4203011.stm   title=Patsy Rowlands - Obituaries - The Independent   accessdate=21 November 2013 }}</ref> was an English people English actress who is best remembered for her roles in the Carry On (franchise) Carry On films series, and as Betty Lewis in the popular ITV Thames sitcom Bless This House (UK TV series) Bless This House, and as Alice Meredith in the Yorkshire Television sitcom Hallelujah! (TV series) Hallelujah!.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Patsy-Rowlands title=Patsy Rowlands - British actress publisher=}}</ref><ref name=stage/>

==Early years==
She was born in Palmers Green, London and attended the Sacred Heart convent school at Whetstone.<ref name=GdnObit /> While attending, an elocution teacher spotted her potential and encouraged her to pursue a career in acting. She applied for the Guildhall School of Music and Drama, and won a scholarship aged fifteen.<ref name=IndObit />

==Early career==
Rowlands began her career in the chorus of Annie Get Your Gun (musical) Annie Get Your Gun, followed by a summer season in Torquay. She then spent several years with the Players Theatre in London, before making her West End debut in Sandy Wilsons musical Valmouth.<ref name=bbc/><ref name=theatricalia/> It was at this time she met her future husband, the composer Malcolm Sircom. They divorced in 1967.{{Citation needed date=March 2011}}

Other West End theatre credits included Semi-Detached with Laurence Olivier and directed by Tony Richardson (with whom she was to work often, appearing in his 1963 film Tom Jones (1963 film) Tom Jones), Shut Your Eyes and Think of England, with Donald Sinden, The Seagull and Ben Traverss The Bed Before Yesterday, both directed by Lindsay Anderson and When We Are Married for Ronald Eyre.<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/2005/01/30/obituaries/patsy-rowlands-actress-in-the-carry-on-comedies-dies-at-71.html?mcubz=0</ref><ref name=GdnObit /> She also starred in Cameron Mackintoshs revival of Oliver! in the mid-1990s, as well as playing Jacks mother in the original London cast of Sondheims Into the Woods.<ref name=theatricalia>{{cite web url=https://theatricalia.com/person/13d0/patsy-rowlands title=Patsy Rowlands - Theatricalia website=theatricalia.com}}</ref> Her final appearance was as Mrs Pearce in the Royal National Theatre National Theatres production of My Fair Lady which also starred Jonathan Pryce.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2001/legit/reviews/my-fair-lady-6-1200467240/ title=Review: ‘My Fair Lady’ first=Matt last=Wolf date=21 March 2001 publisher=}}</ref>

Rowlands also appeared in film and TV versions of two of Frances Hodgson Burnetts books:  "Little Lord Fauntleroy" and "A Little Princess."<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b739b7cf7 title=Little Lord Fauntleroy (1980) publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b739d5f31 title=A Little Princess  Part 5 (1987) publisher=}}</ref>  In the 1980 TV movie "Little Lord Fauntleroy (1980 film) Little Lord Fauntleroy," with Alec Guinness (as Earl of Dorincourt) and Ricky Schroder (as "Ceddy", Little Lord Fauntleroy), she played a village lady named Mrs. Dibble.<ref>{{cite web title=Little Lord Fauntleroy 1980 TV Movie url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0081062/fullcredits?ref_=tt_ov_st_sm website=IMDb accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref>  In the BBC production of the 1986 TV miniseries "A Little Princess (1986 miniseries) A Little Princess," she played the role of the "Bakers Wife," who gives Sara 6 buns for fourpence and later takes under her wing a homeless beggar girl Sara had befriended.<ref>{{cite web title=A Little Princess Full Cast and Crew url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0090474/fullcredits?ref_=tt_ov_st_sm website=IMDb accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref>

Rowlands also appeared  quite frequently on television early in her career. Amongst the various series in which she appeared, were several appearances in Gert and Daisy#Film, radio and television Gert and Daisy (1959) as Bonnie, as well as appearing in 2 episodes of Danger Man (as different, unrelated characters) and in The Avengers (TV series) The Avengers episode "Love All".<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b9f4e8b23 title=Patsy Rowlands publisher=}}</ref><ref>http://danger-man.co.uk/episodeDetails.asp?episodeID=57&seriesNo=4</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.allmovie.com/movie/avengers-love-all-v182515/cast-crew title=Avengers: Love All (1969) - Peter Sykes - Cast and Crew - AllMovie website=AllMovie}}</ref>  Rowlands played the role of a love interest for George called "Beryl" in the 1979 Christmas special and final episode of George and Mildred

==Carry On films==
She made her debut in the Carry On (franchise) Carry On films in Carry On Again Doctor in 1969 and soon became a regular member of the team, usually playing the dowdy, put-upon wife or the long-suffering secretary.<ref name=stage>{{cite web url=https://www.thestage.co.uk/features/obituaries/2005/patsy-rowlands/ title=Patsy Rowlands - Obituaries - The Stage date=1 February 2005 publisher=}}</ref> Between 1969 and 1975 she appeared in nine of the films in increasingly large roles, appearing in Carry On Again Doctor, Carry On Loving, Carry On Henry, Carry On Matron, Carry On Abroad and Carry On Dick - more substantial roles include Carry On At Your Convenience, Carry On Girls and Carry On Behind.<ref name=avelyman/>

On 7 March 1971 she starred in a single episode (Youve Really Landed Me In It This Time) of popular ITV sitcom Doctor at Large (TV series) Doctor at Large, with Barry Evans (actor) Barry Evans and George Layton, as a nymphomaniac secretary, a role she seemed to relish and had played similarly in Carry On Loving.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b87348aa3 title=You&#039;ve Really Landed Me in It This Time (1971) publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://carryonfan.blogspot.co.uk/2017/01/patsy-rowlands-her-carry-on-story.html title=Patsy Rowlands: Her Carry On Story website=carryonfan.blogspot.co.uk}}</ref>

From 1971 to 1976, she played Betty, the feckless neighbour in the popular ITV sitcom Bless This House (UK TV series) Bless This House, which starred fellow Carry On star Sid James.<ref name=avelyman>{{cite web url=http://www.aveleyman.com/ActorCredit.aspx?ActorID=15048 title=Patsy Rowlands website=www.aveleyman.com}}</ref> Her other television credits at this time included appearances with comedians such as Les Dawson and Dick Emery. In the early 1980s, she appeared with Thora Hird in the sitcom Hallelujah! (TV series) Hallelujah!, in which they played an aunt and niece in The Salvation Army. In 1991 she appeared in an episode of Zorro (1990 TV series) Zorro filmed in Madrid, Spain.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0755199/ title=Miracle of the Pueblo date=8 December 1991 publisher= via=www.imdb.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.newworldzorro.com/presskitinfo/prodnotes.html title=New World Zorro Production Notes website=www.newworldzorro.com}}</ref>{{Citation needed date=March 2011}}

==Later years==
Towards the end of her life, Rowlands appeared in several revivals of major musicals such as Oliver! at the London Palladium and My Fair Lady at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.scotsman.com/news/obituaries/patsy-rowlands-1-672697 title=Patsy Rowlands website=www.scotsman.com}}</ref>

Later television credits include The Cazalets, The Canterbury Tales, The Cater Street Hangman#Film.2C TV or theatrical adaptations The Cater Street Hangman, Get Well Soon (TV series) Get Well Soon, Vanity Fair (1998 TV serial) Vanity Fair, Murder Most English, and Bottom (TV series) Bottom for the BBC. In 2002, she was a guest on the paranormal series Most Haunted. Rowlands took part in several DVD audio commentaries along with other surviving stars of the Carry On films in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web   url=http://www.robertross.co.uk/page5.htm   title=Robert Ross DVD   accessdate=21 November 2013   deadurl=yes   archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131205202023/http://www.robertross.co.uk/page5.htm   archivedate=5 December 2013   df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Death==
Rowlands developed breast cancer and had to abandon her plans to become an acting teacher, and quietly retire. She died of the disease in an East Sussex Hospice care hospice, three days after her 74th birthday. She was survived by her only son, Alan (born 1963).<ref name=GdnObit>{{cite news   url=https://www.theguardian.com/news/2005/jan/26/guardianobituaries.film   title=Patsy Rowlands   first=Dennis   last=Barker   newspaper=The Guardian   date=26 January 2005   accessdate=15 July 2012}}</ref>
