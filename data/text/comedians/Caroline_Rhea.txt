Caroline Rhea (born April 13, 1964) is a Canadian Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian and actress who is best known for her role as Hilda Spellman on the ABC show Sabrina the Teenage Witch (1996 TV series) Sabrina the Teenage Witch.

She has performed numerous comedy specials, including three one-hour standup specials for HBO, Showtime, and Bravo. She is also known as the voice of Linda Flynn-Fletcher on the Disney Channel series Phineas and Ferb and as a regular on Hollywood Squares with her friend Whoopi Goldberg. Rhea was chosen by Rosie ODonnell as the new Television presenter hostess of her syndicated talk show, renamed The Caroline Rhea Show and hosted the reality television show The Biggest Loser (U.S. TV series) The Biggest Loser on NBC for the first three seasons. She appears regularly on ABCs Match Game with Alec Baldwin and is reprising her roles as Eugenia Scrimmage in the Macdonald Hall Bruno & Boots movie franchise and Noleta Nethercott in A Very Sordid Wedding.

==Early life==
Rhea was born and raised in Westmount, Quebec, the daughter of Margery, and David Rhea, an obstetrician and gynecologist.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/31/Caroline-Rhea.html  title=Caroline Rhea Biography (1964-)  publisher=Filmreference.com  date=  accessdate=2012-04-23}}</ref> She has two sisters, Cynthia and Celia. She attended The Study, an all-girls private school in Westmount, Quebec Westmount, Quebec; Dalhousie University in Nova Scotia;<ref>{{cite news  title=Laughing matters: Comedian recalls days at Dal as she gets ready for Ha!ifax Comedy Festival  author=Stephen Cooke  url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/ArtsLife/1178359.html   archiveurl  =https://web.archive.org/web/20100424060513/http://thechronicleherald.ca/ArtsLife/1178359.html   archivedate =2010-04-24  newspaper=Chronicle Herald  date=April 21, 2010  accessdate=}}</ref> and the University of Arizona.

==Career==
=== Standup ===
Rhea moved to New York in 1986 to pursue a career as a comedian and actress. She began performing at venues like Catch a Rising Star (comedy clubs) Catch a Rising Star and The Comic Strip Live Comic Strip, where she performed along with comedians including Chris Rock, Louis C.K., Dave Attell, Marc Maron, and Jim Gaffigan. Rhea made her first filmed standup appearances on MTVs Half-Hour Comedy Hour, Comic Strip Live and Carolines Comedy Hour.

Rhea was given a one-hour special for HBO, called One Night Stand. She followed this up with Rheas Anatomy, her one-hour special  for Bravo (U.S. TV network) Bravo, and Give Me My Remote for Showtime (TV network) Showtime.

=== Film and television ===
After finding success in New York as a comedian, Caroline moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career in Hollywood, debuting on NBCs television series Pride & Joy (TV series) Pride & Joy, where she co-starred with Jeremy Piven. She later gained widespread fame for her role as Aunt Hilda on the American Broadcasting Company ABC (and later, The WB Television Network The WB network) television show, Sabrina the Teenage Witch (1996 TV series) Sabrina the Teenage Witch, and for her recurring role on the latest incarnation of Hollywood Squares.

Rhea is also well known for her role as Linda Flynn-Fletcher on the long-running Disney show Phineas and Ferb, for her broadcast syndication syndicated daytime talk show, The Caroline Rhea Show, as the original host of The Biggest Loser (U.S. TV series) The Biggest Loser on NBC, and for playing Noleta Nethercott on the TV series Sordid Lives: The Series for Logo, which has recently been rebooted as a feature called A Very Sordid Wedding, which premiered in March 2017 in Palm Springs.

In 2004, she appeared in the cult-classics Disneys Moms Got a Date with a Vampire and in Christmas with the Kranks. In 2005, Rhea appeared in The Perfect Man, playing a co-worker of Jean (Heather Locklear) and had a recurring role on the hit Disney Channel Original Series, The Suite Life of Zack & Cody as Ilsa Shickelgubermeiger-Von Helsing der Keppelugerhofer, an inspector turned manager of the rival hotel. In 2007, she starred in the original Lifetime Television movie To Be Fat Like Me opposite Kaley Cuoco and in the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox animated series Two Dreadful Children.

In 2008, Rhea starred alongside Justin Guarini and Mircea Monroe in the MarVista Entertainment production of Fast Girl. In 2013, Rhea hosted a travelling live stage show version of the game show Family Feud which toured fairs in the US and Canada, including the Calgary Stampede. In 2017, she has had a recurring role on ABCs revival of Match Game.<ref>[http://www.arkansasonline.com/news/2018/jun/22/north-little-rock-woman-wins-10000-abcs-match-game/ "North Little Rock woman wins $10,000 on ABCs Match Game"] Arkansas Online. Retrieved 2018-07-15.</ref>

Rhea has competed on Bravos Celebrity Poker Showdown and Game Show Network GSNs World Series of Blackjack, and also done a live webcast with author, Meg Cabot.

In 2018, Rhea began hosting Caroline & Friends on GSN. The series features funny videos of animals and children. The studio audience votes for the best video chosen by Rhea and her two guest comedian co-hosts.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Game-Show-Networks-Comedic-Family-Submitted-Video-Show-CAROLINE-AND-FRIENDS-20180531  title=Game Show Networks Comedic Family-Submitted Video Show Caroline and Friends  publisher=broadwayworld.com  access-date=July 16, 2018}}</ref>

=== Charity work ===

Rhea has appeared at Comic Relief (charity) Comic Relief as well as the Ms. Foundations "Women of Comedy from Carolines Comedy Club". She appeared as a contestant in 2001 on a special edition of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire, winning $125,000 for her charity.
