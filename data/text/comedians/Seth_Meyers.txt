
Seth Adam Meyers (born December 28, 1973)<ref name=tvg>{{cite magazine  url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/seth-meyers/197065  title=Seth Meyers: Biography  magazine=TV Guide TVGuide.com  accessdate=August 7, 2014   archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20160310034257/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/seth-meyers/197065   archivedate= March 10, 2016  deadurl=no}}</ref> is an American comedian, writer, political commentator, actor, and television host. He hosts Late Night with Seth Meyers, a late-night talk show that airs on NBC. Prior to that, he was a head writer for NBCs Saturday Night Live (2001–2014) and hosted the shows news parody segment, Weekend Update.

Meyers appeared in the film Journey to the Center of the Earth (2008 theatrical film) Journey to the Center of the Earth in 2008, hosted the 66th Primetime Emmy Awards in 2014 and voiced the character Jeremy "Prock" Awesome in The Awesomes (2013–2015). In 2018, Meyers hosted the 75th Golden Globe Awards.

==Career==
While attending college at Northwestern University, Meyers ran a hot dog stand. During that time, he began performing improv comedy as a member of the Northwestern University improv sketch group Mee-Ow Show. He continued his career at ImprovOlympic with the group Preponderate as well as overseas as a cast member of Boom Chicago, an English language improv troupe based in Amsterdam,<ref name=rapkin>{{cite magazine  last=Rapkin  first=Mickey  date=September 10, 2004  title=Going Dutch  work=Entertainment Weekly  issue=782-783}}</ref> where his brother was also a cast member.<ref name="nbc">{{cite web  title= Seth Meyers — host  url= http://www.nbc.com/late-night-with-seth-meyers/about/bio/seth-meyers  website= NBC.com   accessdate= August 7, 2014}}</ref>
