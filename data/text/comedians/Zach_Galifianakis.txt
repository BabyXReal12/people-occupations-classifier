
Zachary Knight Galifianakis<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.zachgalifianakis.com/biographytext.htm  title=Official web site of Zach Galifianakis  publisher=Zachgalifianakis.com  date=  accessdate=August 10, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161112064511/http://www.zachgalifianakis.com/biographytext.htm  archivedate=November 12, 2016  df=mdy-all }}</ref> ({{IPAc-en ˌ g æ l ɪ f ə ˈ n æ k ɪ s}}; born October 1, 1969)<ref name="filmref">{{cite web url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/51/Zach-Galifianakis.html title=Zach Galifianakis Biography (1969–) publisher=filmreference.com accessdate=June 5, 2010}}</ref> is an American actor, writer and comedian. He came to prominence with his Comedy Central Presents special in 2001 and presented his own show called Late World with Zach on VH1 the following year. He has also starred in films, such as The Hangover (franchise) The Hangover trilogy (2009–2013), Due Date (2010), The Campaign (film) The Campaign (2012), Birdman (film) Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance) (2014), Masterminds (2016 film) Masterminds (2016) and The Lego Batman Movie (2017).

He is the host of the Emmy Awards Emmy Award-winning talk show Between Two Ferns with Zach Galifianakis on the Funny or Die website, and executive produces, writes and stars in the dark comedy series Baskets (TV series) Baskets on FX (TV channel) FX.

==Career==
===Early work===
He began acting on television, guest-starring in Boston Common (TV series) Boston Common and joined Saturday Night Live as a writer but lasted only two weeks.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.gq.com/entertainment/humor/201008/tracy-morgan-paul-rudd-zach-galifianakis-comedy-issue title=Three of Our Most Serious Minds Confer... author=Devin Friedman publisher=GQ}}</ref> Galifianakis co-starred in the film Out Cold (2001 film) Out Cold, and had small roles in Corky Romano, Below (film) Below, Bubble Boy (film) Bubble Boy, Heartbreakers (2001 film) Heartbreakers, Into the Wild (film) Into the Wild, Super High Me, Little Fish Strange Pond and Largo.

File:Zach Galifianakis Mar08 2.jpg thumb left Galifianakis on Inside Joke in New York City in 2006
In September 2001, he appeared in one episode of Comedy Central Presents. It included a stand-up routine, a segment with a piano, and a cappella group The Night Owls  (introduced as his "12 ex-girlfriends") singing "Eternal Flame (song) Eternal Flame" by The Bangles while he made jokes. In 2002, he hosted his own VH1 talk show called Late World with Zach. It featured many of his friends and regular performers from the LA comedy and music venue Largo where he appeared frequently during this time period. One episode featured Largo regulars Jon Brion and Rhett Miller as musical guests. He played Davis (Tru Calling character) Davis in the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox drama series Tru Calling.<ref name="brianpalm"/> He appeared many times on Jimmy Kimmel Live! and played Frisbee in four episodes of Reno 911!.

Galifianakis played Alan Finger on the Comedy Central show Dog Bites Man, a fake news program that caught people during candid moments thinking they were being interviewed by a real news crew. He also guest-starred in the episode of the Comedy Central show The Sarah Silverman Program as Fred the Homeless Guy. He also had a recurring guest role as a doctor on the animated Adult Swim show Tom Goes to the Mayor and appeared in several episodes of Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job! in a recurring role as Tairy Greene. In 2006, Galifianakis appeared in Fiona Apples music video for the song "Not About Love#Video Not About Love", where he is seen lip sync lip-synching the lyrics to the song. A year later, Kanye West employed Galifianakis and indie rock musician Will Oldham for similar purposes in the second version of the video for his song "Cant Tell Me Nothing". In June 2006, Galifianakis released the single "Come On and Get It (Up in Dem Guts)",<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UMJdeqi3g4U title=Alan Finger talks about his song, Up In Them Guts accessdate=August 11, 2012}}</ref> a comedic hip-hop dance song which features Apples vocals.

Galifianakis, Patton Oswalt, Brian Posehn and Maria Bamford, are the four The Comedians of Comedy Comedians of Comedy, a periodic packaged comedy tour in the style of The Original Kings of Comedy and the Blue Collar Comedy Tour. They chose to perform at live rock clubs as opposed to comedy clubs to try to reach a different audience. Much of the tour was taped and has been featured in both a short-lived TV series on Comedy Central and a full-length movie that has appeared at South by Southwest SXSW and on Showtime (TV network) Showtime. On February 22, 2008, he made an appearance on the Jackassworld.com: 24 Hour Takeover. He interviewed various members of the Jackass (TV series) Jackass cast. Galifianakis starred in first leading role in the independent film Visioneers which played in select cities in 2008.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://visioneersthemovie.com/  title=- work=visioneersthemovie.com}}</ref> The film which was released on direct-to-DVD. That same year, Galifianakis appeared in a web video series of advertisements for Absolut vodka, along with Tim Heidecker and Eric Wareheim, creating a parody of the Golden Girls in which one has a deep anger issue, breaking the fourth wall in exasperation and outright violence on the set.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5p0QtJMKt1s A Vodka Movie by Zach Galifianakis, Tim and Eric<!-- Bot generated title -->] from YouTube</ref> He also completed the pilot Speed Freaks for Comedy Central.

===Mainstream performances===
Galifianakis played Alan Garner in the hit comedy The Hangover and earned the MTV Movie Awards MTV Movie Award for the Best Comedic Performance.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/37868/the-twilight-saga-new-moon-a-surprise-winner-mtv-movie-awards-new-eclipse-footage title=The Twilight Saga: New Moon a "Surprise" Winner at MTV Movie Awards / New Eclipse Footage publisher=DreadCentral}}</ref> He was also prominently advertised in subsequent films that featured him in supporting roles, such as G-Force (film) G-Force, Youth in Revolt and the Oscar-nominated film Up in the Air (2009 film) Up in the Air.

Galifianakis also starred in the HBO series Bored to Death and hosted Saturday Night Live on March 6, 2010 Saturday Night Live (season 35) during the shows 35th season, during which he shaved his beard mid-show for a sketch, and closed the show wearing a fake beard.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/video/clips/snl-backstage-zach-shaves-his-beard/1207127/ title=clip from NBC publisher=NBC}}</ref> He hosted again on March 12, 2011 and shaved his head this time, in a Mr. T-like hairstyle, which was allegedly supposed to be used for a sketch that never aired due to time constraints.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/03/15/snl-backstage-zach-galifianakis_n_835908.html title=SNL Backstage: Zach Galifianakis Shaves Head Like Mr. T (VIDEO) work=Huffington Post  location=USA first=Carol last=Hartsell date=March 15, 2011}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In August 2012, Galifianakis married Quinn Lundberg, co-founder of the Growing Voices charity, at the UBC Farm in Vancouver, British Columbia. On September 7, 2013, Lundberg gave birth to their first child, a boy, and Galifianakis missed the premiere of his film Are You Here to attend the birth.<ref name=ZachBaby>{{cite web title=Galifianakis skips TIFF as wife about to give birth  url=http://www.torontosun.com/2013/09/08/galifianakis-skips-tiff-as-wife-about-to-give-birth publisher=Toronto Sun date=September 8, 2013}}</ref> He and his wife welcomed a second son in November 2016.<ref name=ZachBaby2>{{cite web url=http://people.com/babies/zach-galifianakis-welcomes-son-rufus-emmanuel/ title=Zach Galifianakis Welcomes Son Rufus Emmanuel}}</ref>

Lundbergs cousin, Charlie Clark (politician) Charlie Clark, is the mayor of Saskatoon, Saskatchewan. On the day of the election, Galifianakis made a video endorsing Clarks campaign.<ref>[http://saskatoon.ctvnews.ca/charlie-clark-gets-last-minute-shoutout-from-zach-galifianakis-1.3133157 "Charlie Clark gets last-minute shoutout from Zach Galifianakis"]. CFQC-DT, October 26, 2016,</ref><ref>[https://www.saskatoon.ca/sites/default/files/documents/city-clerk/bylaws/8491.pdf Saskatoon Bylaw 8491 see s. 7] which Clark did not declare in his campaign declaration documents filed February 17, 2017, in violation of section 7 of City of Saskatoon Bylaw 8491.</ref>

In January 2014, Galifianakis and A Night of a Thousand Vaginas co-star Sarah Silverman announced their intention to raise $20,000 to help fund the Texas Abortion Fund, part of a nationwide network of funds set up to assist women in obtaining abortions in states whose legislatures had placed restrictions on the practice. The fundraiser was set up in response to the passage of Texas H.B. 2, which established several restrictions that forced a majority of the states abortion clinics to close.<ref name=TexasAbortion>{{cite news title=Sarah Silverman, Zach Galifianakis Help Raise Money for Texas Women Seeking Abortions url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sarah-silverman-zach-galifianakis-help-672639 date=January 20, 2014   work=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref>

Galifianakis owns a farm in Sparta, North Carolina Sparta<ref>[http://blog.bad-diet.com/post/107998080426/zach-galifianakis-leads-an-atv-tour-of-his-farm-in Bad Diet HQ — Zach Galifianakis leads an ATV tour of his farm in] Retrieved 2018-06-30.</ref> in northeastern Alleghany County, North Carolina, and splits his time between the farm and his work. He said, "My farm is a place that I get to think clearly and pretend to know what I am doing."<ref name=ZachFarm>{{cite web title=Zach Galifianakis Has a Farm (Doo-Dah, Doo-Dah) url=http://www.vanityfair.com/online/oscars/2009/05/zach-galifianakis-has-a-farm-doodah-doodah website=Vanity Fair}}</ref>

Galifianakis befriended a homeless woman in her 80s who was living in a Santa Monica laundromat for 18 years. Galifianakis got her an apartment across the street from the laundromat. The story was revealed in the 2015 documentary Queen Mimi.<ref name=Zach>{{cite web title=How This Homeless Woman Became Good Friends With Zach Galifianakis and Renée Zellweger  url=http://www.glamour.com/story/queen-mimi-documentary-homeless-woman-became-friends-zach-galifianakis-renee-zellweger  work=Glamour date=May 13, 2016}}</ref>
