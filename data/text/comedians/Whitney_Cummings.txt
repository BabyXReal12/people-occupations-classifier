
Whitney Ann Cummings (born September 4, 1982)<ref name=Vanity-Fair>{{cite news url=http://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2016/01/whitney-cummings-hbo-special title=Whitney Cummings Got Hooked on Tinder for Her HBO Special date=December 8, 2010 work=Vanity-Fair accessdate=May 11, 2018}}</ref> is an American comedian, actress and producer. She is best known as the creator of the CBS sitcom 2 Broke Girls (2011–2017) and the NBC sitcom Whitney (TV series) Whitney (2011–2013), appearing in the lead role of the latter. Since 2018, Cummings has been a producer and writer for the American Broadcasting Company ABC revival of Roseanne.

==Early life==
Cummings was born and raised in Georgetown (Washington, D.C.) Georgetown, Washington, D.C.<ref name=Vanity-Fair>{{cite news url=http://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2016/01/whitney-cummings-hbo-special title=Whitney Cummings Got Hooked on Tinder for Her HBO Special date=December 8, 2010 work=Vanity-Fair accessdate=May 11, 2018}}</ref><ref name=WashingtonPost-Brazen-2010>{{cite news url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/12/08/AR2010120806415.html title=Comedian Whitney Cummings: Bewitching, brazen and with jokes to make you blush last=Zak first=Dan date=December 8, 2010 work=Washington Post accessdate=November 1, 2011}}</ref> Her mother is Patti Cummings, a former public relations director at Neiman Marcus at Mazza Gallerie.<ref name=WashingtonPost-ReliableSource-2004>{{cite news url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/washingtonpost/access/623413831.html?dids=623413831:623413831&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+25%2C+2004&author=Richard+Leiby&pub=The+Washington+Post&desc=The+Reliable+Source&pqatl=google title=The Reliable Source last=Leiby first=Richard date=April 25, 2004 work=Washington Post accessdate=November 1, 2011}}</ref><ref name=Vulture-Respect-2012>{{cite news last1=Grigoriadis first1=Vanessa title=Can Whitney Cummings Get Some Respect? ==Career==
===Acting===
Cummings moved to Los Angeles after college and worked on Punkd on MTV in 2004<ref name=WashingtonPost-ReliableSource-2004/> and the same year starred in a low-budget thriller, EMR, which was screened at Cannes.<ref name=Variety-EMR-2005>{{cite news url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117926258?refcatid=31 title=Review: ‘EMR’ last=Harvey first=Dennis date=February 17, 2005 work=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=November 1, 2011}}</ref><ref name=TVGuide-Punkd-2004>{{cite news url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/whitney-cummings-ashton-41073.aspx last=Katner first=Ben title=Whitney Rocks Punkd! date=June 11, 2004 work=TV Guide accessdate=November 1, 2011}}</ref>

===Stand-up===
She began performing stand-up in 2004. In 2007, Variety (magazine) Variety named Cummings one of 10 Comics to Watch in 2007.<ref name=Variety-10Comics-2007/> In 2008, Cummings appeared in the San Francisco audition for Last Comic Standing, although she did not pass the showcase. She performed on The Tonight Show with Conan OBrien, and Last Call with Carson Daly.{{cn date=June 2015}}

She co-starred on The Tony Rock Project and appeared in the 2008 movie Made of Honor.  She has also made several appearances on the E! show Chelsea Lately on its round table. She hosted the 2008 Sundance Film Festival Dailies.<ref name=StarPulse-2008>{{cite news url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2008/04/29/talented_comedian_actress_whitney_cummin title=Talented Comedian/Actress Whitney Cummings Talks Made of Honor & More date=April 29,  2008 work=StarPulse accessdate=November 1, 2011}}</ref> She was named one of 12 Rising Stars of Comedy by Entertainment Weekly in 2008.<ref name=EW-12RisingStars-2008>{{cite news url=http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20239791_20542065,00.html#20542078 work=Entertainment Weekly first1=Whitney last1=Pastorek first2=Dan last2=Snierson title=12 Rising Stars of Comedy date=November 13, 2008}}</ref>

Her television appearances have included  Comedians of Chelsea Lately, Live Nude Comedy (which she created, starred and wrote for), The Very bad Show, truTV Presents: Worlds Dumbest..., and the Comedy Central Roasts of Joan Rivers, David Hasselhoff, and Donald Trump.<ref name=WashingtonPost-Brazen-2010/> She released her debut stand-up album, Emotional Ninja. In August 2010, her first one-hour special, titled Whitney Cummings: Money Shot, premiered on Comedy Central.  In 2010 Cummings went on tour with Denis Leary and the Rescue Me (U.S. TV series) Rescue Me Comedy Tour to promote the shows sixth season. She also appeared with Leary on Douchebags and Donuts.<ref>{{cite web title=Denis Leary: Douchebags and Donuts date=January 16, 2011 publisher=Comedy Central url=http://www.cc.com/episodes/gyljo1/stand-up-library-denis-leary--douchebags-and-donuts-season-1-ep-101}}</ref>

In June 2014, Cummings did her second hour-long special, I Love You, on Comedy Central.<ref name=Vulture-Sarah-2014>{{cite web last1=Silverman first1=Sarah title=Sarah Silverman Talks to Whitney Cummings About the Expectation That Comedians Need to Be in Movies url=http://www.vulture.com/2014/06/sarah-silverman-interviews-whitney-cummings.html website=Vulture (blog) Vulture publisher=New York (magazine) New York accessdate=August 18, 2014 date=June 26, 2014}}</ref>

==Influences==
Cummings has described her comedic influences, beginning with Paul Reiser, who she said "made these hysterical, brilliant commentary about the most mundane things and open it up to a hysterical world."<ref name=Fred-TrailerPark-2008 /> Other important influences for her were George Carlin. Later influences were Dave Attell—"a legend now but he’s very edgy", Lenny Bruce and Bill Hicks.<ref name=Fred-TrailerPark-2008>{{cite web last1=Stipp first1=Christopher title=Trailer Park Whitney Cummings url=http://asitecalledfred.com/2008/05/02/trailer-park-whitney-cummings website=Fred accessdate=August 18, 2014 date=May 2, 2008}}  When Dave Attell was asked his opinion on being considered by Cummings to be one of the best comedians alive, he immediately shot himself. </ref>
