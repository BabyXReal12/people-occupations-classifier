Tompkins has appeared on several television programs devoted to discussing politics, popular culture and News current events; however, he says he does not consider himself to be a political comic.<ref name=PUNCHMAG>{{cite web  url=http://punchlinemagazine.tumblr.com/post/681983393/paul-f-tompkins-something-personal  title=Paul F. Tompkins was a contributor to the "Us Peoples Weekly Entertainment" segment of The Daily Show in 1998.<ref name=TORSTAR/><ref name=MNDAILY/> In 2003 he was a writer and correspondent for Real Time with Bill Maher in the shows first season<ref name=DAILYSHOW/><ref name=MNDAILY/> and wrote again for the show in 2009. He appeared on Tough Crowd with Colin Quinn in 2004.<ref name=PRESS1/> In 2004 he also became a pop culture analyst on VH1s Best Week Ever;<ref name=NYT3/><ref name=VULTURE2>{{cite web  url=http://www.vulture.com/2009/06/exclusive_vh1_shelves_best_wee.html  title=Exclusive: VH1 Shelves Best Week Ever, Possibly Permanently  last=Graham  first=Mark  date=June 8, 2009  work=Vulture  publisher=New York Magazine  accessdate=May 31, 2012}}</ref> in 2008 the show was retooled and relaunched as Best Week Ever with Paul F. Tompkins with Tompkins as host.<ref name=NYT3/><ref name=AVCLUB1/><ref name=VULTURE2/> From 2006 to 2008 he was a regular guest on Countdown with Keith Olbermann.<ref name=SFBAYGUARD/><ref name=AVCLUB1/><ref name=IMPOSE/> In 2008 he appeared on Lewis Blacks Root of All Evil<ref name=PRESS1/><ref name=NYPOST>{{cite news  title=Evil Laugh - Lewis Blacks 4-star Farce  first=Linda  last=Stasi  url=http://www.nypost.com/p/entertainment/tv/item_4r97QxSddhV2sPicXGDVjN  newspaper=New York Post  date=March 12, 2008  accessdate=May 31, 2012}}</ref> and took part in a panel on Larry King Live in an episode titled "Politics & Humor".

Tompkins has appeared in documentaries such as Jamie Kennedys Heckler (film) Heckler (2007) and Doug Bensons Super High Me (2007). He also appeared in The Bitter Buddha (2013), a documentary about the career of actor and comedian Eddie Pepitone.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.laughspin.com/2012/03/08/watch-the-bitter-buddha-trailer-with-sarah-silverman-patton-oswalt-more/  title=Watch the Bitter Buddha trailer with Sarah Silverman, Patton Oswalt, more!  author=Laughspin Staff  date=March 8, 2012  work=Laughspin  accessdate=May 31, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120310024810/http://www.laughspin.com/2012/03/08/watch-the-bitter-buddha-trailer-with-sarah-silverman-patton-oswalt-more/  archivedate=March 10, 2012  df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite news  title=The Bitter Buddha: At 54, Comedian Eddie Pepitone Is Finally Ready for His Close-Up  first=Kristin  last=McCracken  url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/kristin-mccracken/the-bitter-buddha-film_b_2687727.html  newspaper=Huffington Post  date=February 26, 2013  accessdate=March 2, 2013}}</ref>
