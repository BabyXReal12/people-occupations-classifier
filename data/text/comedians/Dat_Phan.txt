Dat Phan (born January 25, 1975) is a Vietnamese American stand-up comedian.  He first rose to fame in 2003 after defeating runner-up Ralphie May to win Season 1 of Last Comic Standing.

==Early life==
Phan was born in Saigon. When he was a child, he, his mother, and nine  of his siblings emigrated from South Vietnam to the United States. They suffered financial hardships throughout his childhood. He grew up in the City Heights, San Diego, California City Heights neighborhood of San Diego, California, then spent his teenage years in Santee, California, a suburb of San Diego. Phan was voted most successful of his class at his West Hills High School ten-year reunion, and was voted into Grossmont Colleges Grossmont College#Walk of Fame Walk of Fame.<ref name="NCTimes">{{cite web url=http://www.nctimes.com/entertainment/art-and-theater/theater/article_08631056-e5c2-51a0-b2a9-8d810dd34a5d.html title=Locally raised comedian Phan grateful to be working last=Curran first=James date=2 January 2008 publisher=North County Times accessdate=20 February 2010 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100311182159/http://www.nctimes.com/entertainment/art-and-theater/theater/article_08631056-e5c2-51a0-b2a9-8d810dd34a5d.html archivedate=11 March 2010 df=}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2003 in television 2003, he entered the first season of NBCs stand-up comedy competition reality show, Last Comic Standing.<ref name="NCTimes" /><ref name="AsianWeek">{{cite web url=http://www.asianweek.com/2008/09/16/dat-phan-joins-jade-ribbon-campaign/ title=Dat Phan Joins Jade Ribbon Campaign last=Pang first=Angela date=16 September 2008 publisher=AsianWeek accessdate=20 February 2010 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100311182630/http://www.asianweek.com/2008/09/16/dat-phan-joins-jade-ribbon-campaign/ archivedate=11 March 2010 df=}}</ref> He was picked on by the other contestants due to his relative youth in the comedy world.<ref name="NCTimes" /> Nonetheless, Phan was the surprise winner of Last Comic Standing,<ref name="AsianWeek" /> and subsequently has made cameo appearances in Hollywood films such as Cellular (film) Cellular<ref name="AsianWeek" /> and Love Is the Drug (film) Love Is the Drug. He currently resides in Los Angeles, California Los Angeles, California.

Dat Phan Productions released the DVD Dat Phan: Live and the CD You Touch, You Buy, in stores January 2010, and began pre-production on a movie titled Yellow Fever.<ref name="AsianWeek" /> He has endorsed the Jade Ribbon Campaign established to fight against hepatitis B and liver cancer.<ref name="AsianWeek" />

In 2011, Phan was featured in the DirecTV campaign, "The Whale".

====Last Comic Standing====
Phan was the original winner of Last Comic Standing<ref name="NCTimes" /><ref name="AsianWeek" /> and won in the finals against Ralphie May in 2003. In Fall 2004, he returned for the third season dubbed "Battle of the Best" which was season 1 against season 2. In the fifth round, he and May got eliminated and contestant Dave Mordal who would become the runner-up stayed in. The Season 2 elimination results had winner and future third placeman John Heffron staying in and Kathleen Madigan and Jay London eliminated.
