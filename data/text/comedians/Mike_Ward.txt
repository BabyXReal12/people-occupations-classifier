Michael John Ward (born September 14, 1973 in Quebec City, Quebec) is a French-Canadian comedian.
He performs stand-up comedy comedy in both French and English.

== Career ==

Mike Ward has appeared on television doing stand-up in 20 countries; airing throughout Europe, Africa, Australia and North America. He is a frequent guest of the Anthony Cumia Show podcast.
With other comedian Pantelis, Ward hosts podcast 2 Drink Minimum on Compound Media
== Controversy ==

File:Mike Ward 1.jpg thumb left Ward in May 2014Ward has been the subject of controversy. At Jean-Marc Parents 2008 Just For Laughs French gala, he made a joke about Revenu Québec, in which he mentioned
Disappearance of Cédrika Provencher Cédrika Provencher, a young girl who had been abducted in 2007
in Quebec and whose remains were found in December 2015.

Ward also had a joke ridiculing Jérémy Gabriel, a disabled boy with Treacher Collins syndrome who was 12 years old when first ridiculed by Ward. Jérémys parents filed a complaint with the Quebec human rights commission, and the commission brought the case in front of Quebec’s Human Rights Tribunal seeking $80,000 in damages from Ward. On July 20, 2016, he was ordered to pay $42000 ($35000 to Jérémy, $7000 to his mother). His attorney, Julius Grey appealed the decision.
