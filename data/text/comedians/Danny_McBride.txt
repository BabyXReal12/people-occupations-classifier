
Daniel Richard McBride (born December 29, 1976) is an American actor, comedian, and writer. He starred in the HBO television series Eastbound & Down and Vice Principals, both of which he co-created with frequent collaborator Jody Hill. He has also starred in films, such as The Foot Fist Way (2006), Pineapple Express (film) Pineapple Express (2008), Up in the Air (2009 film) Up in the Air (2009), Your Highness (2011), This Is the End (2013), and Alien: Covenant (2017).

==Career==
In 2006, McBride played Fred Simmons in the low-budget comedy film The Foot Fist Way, which he co-wrote with collaborators Jody Hill and Ben Best (actor) Ben Best. He appeared in character as Simmons to promote the film on Late Night with Conan OBrien on February 26, 2008. He wrote and starred in the HBO original comedy series Eastbound & Down (also a collaboration with Hill and Best), as Kenny Powers, a washed up former major league baseball pitcher with anger management issues. The series was produced by Gary Sanchez Productions, and the pilot episode premiered on February 15, 2009, featuring Will Ferrell and Craig Robinson (actor) Craig Robinson.

On April 8, 2009, HBO announced it had renewed the series for a second season.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thrfeed.com/2009/04/hbo-renews-eastbound-.html title=TV Show News and Ratings - Live Feed website=Thrfeed.com accessdate=30 December 2017}}</ref> On July 2, 2012, HBO renewed the series for a fourth season.<ref>{{cite web last=Levine first=Stuart url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118056210 title=HBO asks for more ‘Eastbound & Down’ work=Variety date=2012-07-02 accessdate=2013-02-06}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Levine first=Stuart url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118026471?refCatId=14 title=HBO re-ups two half-hour comedies work=Variety date=2010-10-27 accessdate=2011-03-23}}</ref> The series ended on November 17, 2013. In March 2009, because of his role as Powers, he received an offer to play professional baseball for the Pensacola Pelicans, a minor league team in the American Association of Independent Professional Baseball.<ref>{{cite web title=Pensacola Pelicans kick off season May 14 with great talent url=http://www.splashpensacolabeach.com/news/2009-05-01/Home/002.html work=Splash! Northwest Floridas Entertainment Magazine accessdate=September 26, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web title=Professional Baseball Team Offers HBOs Kenny Power 2009 Contract url=http://www.oursportscentral.com/services/releases/?id=3787537 work=OurSportsCentral accessdate=September 26, 2013}}</ref>

In 2010, he signed an endorsement deal with K-Swiss shoes and played the eponymous roadie in the Tenacious D music video for "Roadie" in 2012. McBride starred with Walton Goggins in the HBO comedy series Vice Principals from 2016 to 2017.

McBride is currently{{when? date=January 2018}} co-writing a script with David Gordon Green for Halloween (2018 film) a sequel to Halloween (1978 film) Halloween, to be directed by Green and produced by series creator John Carpenter and Jason Blum.<ref>{{cite web url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3424120/david-gordon-green-danny-mcbride-rebooting-halloween-october-2018/ title=David Gordon Green and Danny McBride Rebooting Halloween for October 2018 - Bloody Disgusting! website=bloody-disgusting.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last1=Galluzzo first1=Rob title=David Gordon Green, Danny McBride Will Direct/Write The New HALLOWEEN Movie For Blumhouse! url=http://www.blumhouse.com/2017/02/09/david-gordon-green-danny-mcbride-will-directwrite-the-new-halloween-movie-for-blumhouse/ website=Blumhouse Productions Blumhouse accessdate=15 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web  url=https://www.stereogum.com/1966097/qa-john-carpenter-talks-new-album-anthology-the-upcoming-halloween-sequel/franchises/interview/  title=Q&A: John Carpenter Talks New Album Anthology & The Upcoming Halloween Sequel  last=Daw  first=Robbie  date=2017-10-09  website=Stereogum  access-date=2017-10-09}}</ref> 

In January 2018, two trailers were released online for what was supposedly a Crocodile Dundee sequel, titled Dundee: The Son of a Legend Returns Home. The film was said to star McBride as Brian Dundee, the son of the original Crocodile Dundee. The trailers also feature cameo appearances by Australian actors Chris Hemsworth, Hugh Jackman, Russell Crowe, Margot Robbie, and Ruby Rose.<ref name="Snopes">{{cite web last1=Garcia first1=Arturo title=Is a Crocodile Dundee Sequel Being Released in 2018? url=https://www.snopes.com/fake-crocodile-dundee-trailer/ website=Snopes accessdate=24 January 2018}}</ref> From the beginning, various aspects of the campaign made some publications believe that film was an elaborate hoax.<ref name="Brisbane">{{cite web last1=Moran first1=Rob title=Crocodile Dundee reboot outed as advertising campaign for Tourism Australia url=https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/sport/nfl/crocodile-dundee-reboot-outed-as-advertising-campaign-for-tourism-australia-20180124-h0nlmz.html website=Brisbane Times accessdate=24 January 2018}}</ref> It was later reported that these ads were actually part of a lead up to a Super Bowl commercial for Tourism Australia.<ref name="Brisbane"/>
