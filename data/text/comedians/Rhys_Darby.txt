
Rhys Montague Darby (born 21 March 1974)<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/dominion-post/culture/3040949/Rhys-Darby-not-planning-to-slow-down title=Rhys Darby not planning to slow down first=Greer last=McDonald work=The Dominion Post date=7 November 2009 accessdate=14 June 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/personalities/news/article.cfm?c_id=72&objectid=10536462 title=Roll call: Rhys Darby first=Joanna last=Hunkin work=The New Zealand Herald date=10 October 2008 accessdate=6 July 2012}}</ref> is an actor and comedian from New Zealand, known for his energetic physical comedy routines, telling stories accompanied with mime and sound effects<ref name="Chortle review">{{cite web url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/comics/r/561/rhys_darby/review title=Rhys Darbys review publisher=Chortle accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> of things such as machinery and animals.<ref name="Apiary interview">{{cite web author=Keith Huang url=http://www.theapiary.org/archives/2007/08/inside_with_rhy.html title=Inside With: Rhys Darby publisher=The Apiary date=13 August 2007 accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> He was nominated for the Billy T Award in 2001 and 2002.<ref name="bio"/> He also won the 2012 Fred (Dagg) award at the New Zealand International Comedy Festival, for Best NZ show.<ref name=FredAward/>

Darby is best known for playing Flight of the Conchords (TV series)#Cast Murray Hewitt, the band manager of Flight of the Conchords in the Flight of the Conchords (TV series) television series, a role he originally played in the The BBC Radio Series: Flight of the Conchords BBC radio series under the name Brian Nesbit<ref name="Radio 2">{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio2/comedy/conchords.shtml  title=Radio 2 Comedy publisher=BBC accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> and for playing the boss of Jim Carreys character in Yes Man (film) Yes Man, as well as roles in What We Do in the Shadows, The Boat That Rocked, Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle, A Series of Unfortunate Events (TV series) A Series of Unfortunate Events, and Voltron: Legendary Defender.

==Early life==
Darby was born in Auckland, and brought up in its Pakuranga suburb. He attended Edgewater College.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.times.co.nz/news/global-star-recalls-roots.html title=Global star recalls roots date=12 May 2012 newspaper=Howick and Pakuranga Times}}</ref> A former soldier (signaller trained in morse code), he left the New Zealand Army in 1994 and began studies at the University of Canterbury.<ref name="University Performance">{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sMpOAA5WK4w title=Performance in University Comedy Show work=Youtube accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> In 1996 he formed a comedy duo, Rhysently Granted, with Grant Lobban.<ref name="Mrs Whippy song">{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bTiv6h5qi6w title="Mrs Whippy" song by Rhysently Granted work=Youtube date=2 April 2008 accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> After winning an open mic contest at Southern Blues Bar in Christchurch, they began performing at local venues.<ref name="Green Room Bar Performance">{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ZUW8VPfavQ title=Rhysently Granted performance in Christchurch bar 1997 work=Youtube date=10 April 2008 accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref>

==Career==
Rhysently Granted performed at two international comedy festivals which led to Darby moving back to Auckland to seek more solo stand-up experience.<ref name="May 2000 Stand-up">{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=siR8De662Qc title=May 2000 Stand-up Performance work=Youtube date=2 September 2008 accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> After performing his first solo show at the 2002 Edinburgh Festival Fringe, Darby moved to the UK to pursue his career further.<ref name="bio">[http://rhysdarby.com/download/Rhys_Darby_Biography.pdf Rhys Darby profile] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090806172730/http://rhysdarby.com/download/Rhys_Darby_Biography.pdf  date=6 August 2009 }}</ref> 

In 2004 he appeared in the Flight of the Conchords BBC radio series as the bands manager Brian Nesbit alongside comedy stars such as Rob Brydon, Andy Parsons and Jimmy Carr.{{cn date=February 2016}} Darby plays Murray, the bands manager, on Flight of the Conchords TV show. He provided vocals for the track Leggy Blonde on their Flight of the Conchords (album) self-titled first album and also in the second series of the show on the track "Rejected" during the episode "A Good Opportunity" alongside tenor Andrew Drost.{{cn date=February 2016}}

Darby played the role of Norman, the boss of Jim Carreys character in Yes Man (film) Yes Man.<ref name="Yes Man">{{cite web url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2007/09/24/5824/darby_joins_jim_carrey_movie title=Darby joins Jim Carrey movie publisher=Chortle date=24 September 2007 accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> In July 2008 he appeared in a Nike, Inc. Nike advertisement featuring Roger Federer; he played an impostor posing as Federers coach.<ref>{{cite news last=Brown first=Rich url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/2304662/Roger-Federers-coach-revealed-in-Nike-advert.html title=Roger Federers coach revealed in Nike advert publisher=Telegraph date=2 July 2008 accessdate=25 May 2010 location=London, UK}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://link.brightcove.com/services/link/bcpid1640149465/bctid1641860585 title=Video - watch video clips, live streaming, daily - brightcove.com accessdate=14 February 2016}}</ref>

He appeared on a British childrens television program The Slammer during its first season. He also appeared in several advertisements for New Zealand mobile phone company 2degrees. He appeared on Soccer AM on 18 October 2008 and on Never Mind The Buzzcocks on 23 October. The DVD of his live performance Imagine That! has gone platinum in New Zealand.{{cn date=May 2016}}

Rhys Darbys hair colour is self-described as Electric Copper in the episode of Flight of the Conchords entitled Murray Takes It to the Next Level. He refers to this in his stand-up show Its Rhys Darby Night which he performed at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe from 6–15 August 2009,<ref>{{cite web url=http://edinburghfestival.list.co.uk/event/10001823-rhys-darby-its-rhys-darby-night title=Rhys Darby: Its Rhys Darby Night publisher=Edinburghfestival.list.co.uk accessdate=25 May 2010}}</ref> The Bloomsbury Theatre in London from 27 July–1 August and toured around New Zealand with in October and November that year.

Darby played the role of Angus in the Richard Curtis film The Boat That Rocked<ref name="Boat">{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1131729/fullcredits#cast title=Pirate Radio (2009) publisher=}}</ref> released in the UK on 1 April 2009. This film was released in some countries in November 2009 under the name Pirate Radio.<ref name="imdb"/>

In 2009 while home in New Zealand he began filming T.V. ads for NZs new mobile network 2degrees and appearing in NZ shows Rocked the Nation 2, Jaquie Brown Diaries and Intrepid Journeys. He has also branched out into producing live comedy with his wife and their company Awesomeness International. At the NZ International Comedy Festival they produced shows for local comedians. Darby became a climate ambassador for Greenpeace in its Sign On climate campaign<ref name="Sign On">{{cite web url=http://www.signon.org.nz/community/high-profile title=GREENPEACE New Zealand publisher=Sign On accessdate=14 February 2016}}</ref> and published a poem on [http://www.signon.org.nz/people/rhys-darby-poem-video Youtube] in support of the campaign.

In 2009 Darby became a part of the New Zealand TV One (New Zealand) TV ONE series Intrepid Journeys, Season 5, touring Rwanda. On 10 August 2010, Entertainment Weekly claimed that Darby was in consideration for a role in the television series The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office after Steve Carell left at the end of the 2011 season, however this did not materialise.<ref>{{cite web author=Brian Gallagher url=http://www.movieweb.com/news/NEtg7oMlsMl0ww title=The Office Seeking Danny McBride and Rhys Darby? publisher=MovieWeb.com date=10 August 2010 accessdate=16 May 2012}}</ref>

In May 2011, he also filmed a pilot for a Channel 4 sitcom in the UK, The Fun Police.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/news/story/00000542/vic_reeves_rhys_darby_new_sitcom_pilot title=Vic Reeves and Rhys Darby to star in health and safety sitcom pilot date=18 May 2011 publisher=The British Comedy Guide accessdate=29 May 2011}}</ref> The pilot was broadcast on 16 September. In 2011, Darby and family moved to the USA for Darby to take a role in the CBS sitcom How to Be a Gentleman.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.rhysdarby.com work=How to Be a Gentleman title=rhysdarby.com}}</ref> Darbys second DVD "Its Rhys Darby Night!" was released in NZ on 12 December 2011.<ref name="imdb">{{IMDb name 1055302}}</ref>

He wrote a self-described "autobiographical space novel" entitled "This Way to Spaceship", which was released on 12 April 2012. On 20 May 2012, Darby was awarded the Fred Award by the New Zealand International Comedy Festival for his show of the same name as his book.<ref name=FredAward>{{cite web url=http://tvnz.co.nz/entertainment-news/rhys-darby-wins-top-nz-award-4895181 title=Rhys Darby wins top NZ award work=Television New Zealand date=21 May 2012 accessdate=21 May 2012}}</ref>

In 2013 he was responsible for flying Cornish rappers Hedluv + Passman hedluv + passman to New Zealand for the New Zealand International Comedy Festival,<ref name="nzherald.co.nz">{{cite news url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=10877501 work=The New Zealand Herald title=Rhys Darby: Two Cornish rappers and a Casiotone to go date=15 April 2013}}</ref> this was followed by an appearance on Seven Sharp, where he introduced his rappers<ref name="nzherald.co.nz"/> to the New Zealand public.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kepmX9VCiI4#! title=Hedluv + Passman on Seven Sharp NZ publisher=YouTube date=17 April 2013 accessdate=14 February 2016}}</ref>

Darby summited Mount Kilimanjaro Mt Kilimanjaro in July 2013 as part of a World Vision team of celebrities that included Olympian Mahé Drysdale and musician Boh Runga. On 8 November 2013, Darby made an appearance and performed some standup comedy in his capacity as second guest on the Late Show with David Letterman. He portrayed Anton in the 2014 release comedy horror film What We Do in the Shadows.<ref>{{cite web title=What We Do in Shadows stars FOTCs Rhys Darby, Jonathan Brugh, Cori Gonzalez-Macuer, Stu Rutherford, and Jackie Van Beek publisher=Absolute Radio date=7 July 2014 url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/90530/new-shadows-video-plays-rules accessdate=12 July 2014}}</ref>

As of July 2014, Darby and his family reside in Los Angeles, California.<ref name=GeoffLloyd>{{cite podcast host=Geoff Lloyd title=Geoff Lloyd – Rhys Derby website=absoluteradio.co.uk/onair/geoff/index.html publisher=Absolute Radio date=7 July 2014 url=http://www.absoluteradio.co.uk/podcasts/Geoff-Lloyd-s-Hometime-Show/2014-07-07/20140707201227 accessdate=12 July 2014 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714172409/http://www.absoluteradio.co.uk/podcasts/Geoff-Lloyd-s-Hometime-Show/2014-07-07/20140707201227/ archivedate=14 July 2014 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

In February 2016, he played character Guy Mann in the third episode ("Mulder and Scully Meet the Were-Monster") of The X-Files. Darby, a long time fan of the show, was thrilled by his experience on set. He saw this as an opportunity that only comes around every so often and said hed return to the set the first chance he received.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.vulture.com/2016/02/rhys-darby-x-files-monster.html title=Rhys Darby on Playing an X-Files Monster and the Future of Flight of the Conchords website=Vulture accessdate=12 May 2016}}</ref>
