Yvette Nicole Brown (born August 12, 1971) is an American actress, voice actress, and comedian. Brown has appeared in numerous commercials, television shows, and films throughout her career. She starred as Shirley Bennett on the National Broadcasting Company NBC comedy series Community (TV series) Community. She had a recurring role on the Nickelodeon sitcom Drake & Josh as Helen Dubois, and voiced the character Cookie on the American-Canadian animated series Pound Puppies (2010 TV series) Pound Puppies.  Brown also starred as Dani in the 2015 version of The Odd Couple (2015 TV series) The Odd Couple on CBS.

Brown has also had small guest roles in television shows such as That 70s Show, The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office, Boston Legal, Chuck (TV series) Chuck, and The Soul Man. In addition to television, she has had supporting roles in films such as (500) Days of Summer, Tropic Thunder, Repo Men, and Percy Jackson: Sea of Monsters. She also voiced "Harper" from the video game Minecraft: Story Mode. She currently hosts Syfy cosplay series Cosplay Melee and has been a regular guest on the talk show Talking Dead since 2012.

==Early life and education==
Brown was born in East Cleveland, Ohio. She graduated from Warrensville Heights High School in 1989. Brown studied communication studies at the University of Akron and graduated with her Bachelor of Arts in Communication in 1994.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.uakron.edu/im/online-newsroom/news_details.dot?newsId=1365607&crumbTitle=%27Community%27%20star%20Yvette%20Nicole%20Brown%20to%20be%20honored%20by%20UA title=Community star Yvette Nicole Brown to be honored by UA date=31 August 2010 publisher=The University of Akron}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.uakron.edu/race/keynote/yvette-nicole-brown.dot title=Yvette Nicole Brown publisher=The University of Akron}}</ref> She became a sister of the Delta Pi chapter of Alpha Kappa Alpha as an undergrad at the university.

After graduating, Brown took acting classes in Hollywood, Los Angeles.<ref name="Cleaveland">{{cite news last=Dawidziak first=Mark work=The Plain Dealer title=Yvette Nicole Brown, an East Cleveland native, enjoys role on TV sitcom Community url=http://www.cleveland.com/tv/index.ssf/2009/11/yvette_nicole_brown_an_east_cl.html date=November 13, 2009 accessdate=April 26, 2010}}</ref>

==Career==
Brown appeared as "Yvette" in the music video "1-4-All-4-1" by the East Coast Family, a Michael Bivins project, for Biv10 Records.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xlZv2cpLReM YouTube: East Coast Family 1-4-All-4-1]   Retrieved 11-20-2011   2:22 mark</ref> Brown said of the experience, "It was a great run, but I guess music was just never meant to be for me."<ref>[http://popwatch.ew.com/2009/09/24/community-yvette-nicole-brown-chevy-joel/ EW.com: Community: Get to know Yvette Nicole Brown (and how she got to know Chevy, Joel, Jude, and Gerard)]   09-24-2009   Retrieved 11-20-2011</ref>

Brown first appeared on commercials before entering television shows and films a few years later.<ref name="Wash">{{cite news last=Asim first=Jabari work=The Washington Post title=The Trouble With Black Actresses url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/08/06/AR2006080600806.html date=August 6, 2006 accessdate=April 26, 2010}}</ref> She has since played roles on television shows such as The War at Home (TV series) The War At Home, Malcolm in the Middle, Thats So Raven, and the US version of The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office. She also had a recurring role as movie theater manager Helen Dubois in the Nickelodeon sitcom Drake & Josh. Brown reprised the role in the season 2 episode of Victorious titled "Helen Back Again".<ref name="Cleaveland"/> She also provided the voice of Cookie on Hub Network The Hubs Pound Puppies (2010 TV series) Pound Puppies.

In 2009, Brown began starring as Shirley Bennett on the comedy series Community (TV series) Community. On September 30, 2014, Brown announced that she would be leaving the show after five seasons in order to take care of her ailing father. In her announcement she said, "My dad needs daily care and he needs me. The idea of being away 16 hours a day for five months, I couldnt do it. It was a difficult decision for me to make, but I had to choose my dad."<ref>{{cite web  last=Schneider  first=Michael  url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Yvette-Nicole-Brown-1087724.aspx?rss=news&partnerid=spi&profileid=05  title=Exclusive: Yvette Nicole Brown Departs Community  work=TV Guide  publisher=CBS Interactive  date=September 30, 2014  accessdate=October 1, 2014}}</ref>

She has appeared in television commercials for Hamburger Helper, Big Lots, Pine-Sol, Comcast, Aquapod (bottle) Aquapod, Shout, Fiber One, Yoplait Yogurt, DiGiorno DiGiorno Pizza, Dairy Queen, and Time Warner. She has appeared on episodes of The Thrilling Adventure Hour as "The Troubleshooter" in the Sparks Nevada, Marshall On Mars series.

In 2012, Brown appeared as a celebrity contestant on GSNs version of Pyramid (game show) The Pyramid with her fellow Community co-star Danny Pudi.

In 2014, it was reported that Brown is set to recur on the USA Network series Benched.<ref>{{cite web last1=Dawidziak first1=Mark title=Molly Shannon and Yvette Nicole Brown courted for USA Networks Benched url=http://www.cleveland.com/tv-blog/index.ssf/2014/06/molly_shannon_and_yvette_nicole_brown_courted_for_usa_networks_benched.html website=cleveland.com accessdate=17 June 2014}}</ref> Shortly after her exit of Community, Browns role of Dani on the CBS sitcom The Odd Couple (2015 TV series) The Odd Couple was upgraded to series regular.<ref>{{cite web url=http://tvline.com/2014/10/04/yvette-nicole-brown-the-odd-couple-series-regular-cbs/ title=CBS Odd Couple Reboot Promotes Yvette Nicole Brown to Series Regular last=Ausiello first=Michael work=TV Line date=October 4, 2014 accessdate=October 5, 2014}}</ref> She has also been a regular guest on AMCs Talking Dead. She currently hosts Syfy cosplay series Cosplay Melee.

In July 2018, Brown temporarily replaced Chris Hardwick as the host of Talking Dead and served as moderator for The Walking Dead (TV series) The Walking Dead and Fear the Walking Dead panels at the San Diego Comic-Con<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/yvette-nicole-brown-replace-chris-hardwick-walking-dead-200343502.html title=Yvette Nicole Brown to Replace Chris Hardwick as ‘Walking Dead’ Panel Moderator at Comic-Con publisher=}}</ref> during Hardwick’s suspension.
