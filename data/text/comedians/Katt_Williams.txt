==Career==

===Stand-up career===
Williams started performing comedy in his Cincinnati neighborhood, Evanston, Cincinnati Evanston.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1">{{cite web url=http://www.kattwilliams.com/katt/  title=Biography}}</ref><ref name="Katt Williams Biography 2">{{cite web url=http://www.biography.com/people/katt-williams-21129883  title=Katt Williams biography}}</ref>

Williams honed his comic delivery by performing his routine in clubs nationwide, from Oklahoma to Oakland, California Oakland. By 1999, Katt had become an established comic, appearing on stage at the likes of The Improv, The Comedy Club, The Icehouse and The Hollywood Park Casino. Most notably he appeared on Black Entertainment Television BETs Comic View as Katt "In the Hat" Williams.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/><ref name="Katt Williams Biography 2"/>

Williams starred in his first comedy special in 2006 entitled Katt Williams: Live: Let a Playa Play. His second & first HBO stand-up special was 2006s The Pimp Chronicles, Pt. 1, with his comedy piece "This Shit Right Here Nigga, This Shit Right Here Nigga".<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/> In 2007 Williams was offered a movie/standup which he entitled American Hustle (2007 film) American Hustle. The film had critical success and established Williams as a mainstream comedian.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/><ref name=LASentinel>{{cite news last=Williams first=Kam title=Katt Williams: American Hustle url=http://www.lasentinel.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=8388:katt-williams-american-hustle&catid=58:movies&Itemid=148 accessdate=May 9, 2013 newspaper=Los Angeles Sentinel}}</ref> In 2008 Williams released his second HBO comedy special entitled Its Pimpin Pimpin, with Williams doing more Political standup.<ref>{{cite web url=http://newsblaze.com/story/20081117175231kamw.nb/topstory.html  title=Katt Williams: Its Pimpin DVD Review  publisher=Newsblaze.com  date=2008-11-17  accessdate=2014-05-02}}</ref> In 2012, after a four-year haitus, Williams returned to stand up in his third HBO comedy special, entitled Kattpacalypse.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.chud.com/114161/dvd-review-katt-williams-kattpacalypse/  title=DVD Review: Katt Williams – Kattpacalypse  publisher=CHUD.com  date=  accessdate=2014-05-02}}</ref>

On December 3, 2012, Williams announced the end of his stand-up comedy career, a day after a bizarre incident in Seattle, Washington that landed him in jail.<ref>{{cite news title=Katt Williams: Im kinda done with stand-up comedy career  url=http://www.cnn.com/2012/12/04/showbiz/katt-williams-done-comedy/index.html?eref=rss_mostpopular/   work=CNN.com   date=December 3, 2012}}</ref> However, just 3 days later, Williams announced he was coming out of retirement.<ref>{{cite news title=Katt Williams Not Retiring From Stand-Up Comedy, Just Felt Unappreciated  url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/12/06/katt-williams-not-retiring-stand-up-comedy-quitting-unappreciated_n_2251769.html   work=huffingtonpost.com   date=December 6, 2012}}</ref>

As of late 2013, Williams was on his "Growth Spurt" Tour.<ref>{{cite web author=2013  url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwcomedy/article/Katt-Williams-Presents-THE-KATT-IS-BACK-TOUR-in-Las-Vegas-119-20131108  title=Katt Williams Presents THE KATT IS BACK TOUR in Las Vegas, 11/9 - BWWComedyWorld  publisher=Broadwayworld.com  date=  accessdate=2014-05-02}}</ref>

On August 16, 2014, Williams returned with a new HBO special titled Katt Williams: Priceless: Afterlife, which was directed by Spike Lee.<ref>{{cite web url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/spike-lee-will-direct-katt-williams-return-to-hbo-in-new-comedy-special title=Spike Lee Will Direct Katt Williams Return to HBO In New - Shadow and Act author=Tambay A. ===Film and television career===
In 2002, Williams made his acting debut on NYPD Blue. He gained notoriety on Wild n Out, in which he appeared for several seasons.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/><ref name="Katt Williams Biography 2"/> Williams also appeared in the official music video for Wild n Out colleague Nick Cannons single Gigolo (Nick Cannon song) Gigolo in 2003.

In 2007, he provided the voice of A Pimp Named Slickback in The Boondocks (TV series) The Boondocks; he plays himself as an on-stage Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian in Grand Theft Auto IV performing several routines, including an abbreviated version of one of his routines from Katt Williams: American Hustle; and Williams has appeared in several episodes of My Wife and Kids as character Bobby Shaw. He was also the roastmaster of the Comedy Central Roast of Flavor Flav.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/><ref name="Katt Williams Biography 2"/>

Williams has also played supporting characters in films, such as First Sunday and Norbit, but is more widely recognized for his character Money Mike in Friday After Next.<ref name="Katt Williams Biography 1"/><ref name="Katt Williams Biography 2"/>
