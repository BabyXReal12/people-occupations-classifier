
Lisa Valerie Kudrow<ref>{{cite web  title=Lisa Kudrow says her middle name  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKUcdPm9xhs  publisher=YouTube  accessdate=August 8, 2013}}</ref> ({{IPAc-en  k uː d r oʊ}}; born July 30, 1963)<ref>[http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/39461?c=search&first=lisa&last=kudrow Family Tree Legends] According to the State of California. California Birth Index, 1905–1995. Center for Health Statistics, California Department of Health Services, Sacramento, California. {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111018123157/http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/39461?c=search&first=lisa&last=kudrow  date=October 18, 2011 }}</ref> is an American actress, comedian, writer, and producer. After making guest appearances in several television sitcoms, including Cheers (TV series) Cheers, she came to prominence with her recurring role of Ursula in Mad About You (1993-1999). Kudrow gained worldwide recognition for portraying Phoebe Buffay on the television sitcom Friends (1994–2004), for which she received several accolades, including a Primetime Emmy Award for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series from six nominations, two Screen Actors Guild Awards from 12 nominations and a Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Series, Miniseries or Television Film Golden Globe Award nomination. The character was widely popular during the airing of the series and was later recognized as one of the greatest female characters in American television. {{by whom? date=August 2018}}

Kudrow starred in the cult film cult comedy film Romy and Micheles High School Reunion (1997) and followed it with an acclaimed performance in the romantic comedy The Opposite of Sex (1998), which won her the New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Supporting Actress and a nomination for the Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Female. In 2005, she went on to produce, write and star in the HBO comedy series The Comeback (TV series) The Comeback, which was revived nine years later for a second season. Kudrow received two Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series nominations for both seasons.

Kudrow produced and starred in the Showtime (TV network) Showtime program Web Therapy (TV series) Web Therapy (2011–2015), which was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Short-Format Live-Action Entertainment Program Primetime Emmy Award. She is also one of the executive producers of the TLC (TV network) TLC reality program Who Do You Think You Are? (U.S. TV series) Who Do You Think You Are, which gained three nominations for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Reality Program.

Kudrow has also made several notable film appearances, including roles in Analyze This (1999), Dr. Dolittle 2 (2001), P.S. I Love You (film) P.S. I Love You (2007), Bandslam (2008), Hotel for Dogs (film) Hotel for Dogs (2009), Easy A (2010), Neighbors (2014 film) Neighbors (2014) and its sequel Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising (2016), The Girl on the Train (2016 film) The Girl on the Train (2016), and The Boss Baby (2017).

==Career==

===1989–1994: Early career===
At the urging of her brothers childhood friend, comedian Jon Lovitz,<ref name=zas /> she began her comedic career as a member of The Groundlings, an improv and sketch comedy school in Los Angeles. Kudrow has credited Cynthia Szigeti, her improv teacher at The Groundlings, for changing her perspective on acting, calling her "the best thing that happened, on so many levels."<ref name=thr>{{cite news first=Mike last=Barnes title=Cynthia Szigeti, Groundlings Improv Teacher and Seinfeld Actress, Dies at 66  url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cynthia-szigeti-dead-groundlings-teacher-920431  work=The Hollywood Reporter  date=August 19, 2016  accessdate=September 6, 2016}}</ref><ref name=aatv>{{cite news first= last= title=Lisa Kudrow interview: Part 2 of 4  url=http://www.emmytvlegends.org/interviews/people/lisa-kudrow#  work=Archive of American Television  date=December 22, 2012  accessdate=September 6, 2016}}</ref> Briefly, Kudrow joined with Conan OBrien and director Tim Hillman in the short-lived Improvisational theatre improv troupe Unexpected Company.<ref>[http://www.pxdrive.com/album/LISA+KUDROW_pictures_gyglpic/ Bio] at PX Drive.com</ref> She was also the only regular female member of the Transformers Comedy Troupe.<ref>[http://www.hollywoodauditions.com/Biographies/lisa_kudrow.htm Lisa Kudrow Biography] Hollywood Auditions.com</ref>
She played a role in an episode of the NBC sitcom Cheers.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0539919/ title="Cheers" Two Girls for Every Boyd (TV Episode 1989) author=Bolesroor date=May 5, 2011 work=IMDb}}</ref> She tried out for Saturday Night Live in 1990, but the show chose Julia Sweeney instead.<ref name="LiveFromNewYork">{{cite book last=Shales first=Tom author2=James Andrew Miller  title=Live From New York publisher=Back Bay Books date=October 2003 edition=First paperback pages=273, 386 isbn=0-316-73565-5}}</ref> She had a recurring role as Kathy Fleisher in three episodes of season one of the Bob Newhart sitcom Bob (TV series) Bob (CBS, 1992–1993), a role she played after taking part in the memorable series finale of Newharts previous series Newhart.<ref>[http://www.mentalfloss.com/blogs/archives/29002 Kara Kovalchik, "The Early TV Appearances of Seven Big Stars", Mental Floss], July 15, 2009.] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120118234539/http://www.mentalfloss.com/blogs/archives/29002  date=January 18, 2012 }}</ref> Prior to Friends, she appeared in at least two produced network pilots: NBCs Just Temporary (also known as Temporarily Yours) in 1989, playing Nicole; and CBS Close Encounters (also known as Matchmaker) in 1990, playing a Valley girl.<ref name=filmref />

Kudrow was cast to play the role of Roz Doyle in Frasier, but the role was re-cast with Peri Gilpin during the taping of the pilot episode. Kudrow said in 2000 that when rehearsals started, "I knew it wasnt working. I could feel it all slipping away, and I was panicking, which only made things worse."<ref name=zas /> Her first recurring television role was Ursula Buffay, the eccentric waitress on the NBC sitcom Mad About You. Kudrow would reprise the character on the NBC sitcom Friends, in which Kudrow co-starred as Massage therapy massage therapist Phoebe Buffay, Ursulas twin sister.<ref>{{cite web url= http://www.newsday.com/services/newspaper/printedition/sunday/fanfare/ny-fffast5999979jan18a,0,2982354.story title= Fast Chat: Lisa Kudrow work= Newsday date= January 18, 2009}}{{dead link date=December 2017  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===1994–2004: Breakthrough===
For her starring role as Phoebe on Friends (NBC, 1994–2004) Kudrow won the 1998 Emmy Award<ref>[http://www.emmys.com/celebrities/lisa-kudrow Lisa Kudrow Emmy Nominated]. Emmys.com. Retrieved on July 25, 2012.</ref> for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress - Comedy Series Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series. According to the Guinness Book of World Records (2005), Kudrow and co-stars Jennifer Aniston and Courteney Cox became the highest paid TV actresses of all time, earning $1 million per episode for the ninth and tenth seasons of Friends.<ref>{{cite news last=Carter first=Bill title=Friends Deal Will Pay Each Of Its 6 Stars $22 Million url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/02/12/business/friends-deal-will-pay-each-of-its-6-stars-22-million.html accessdate=May 10, 2013 newspaper=New York Times date=February 12, 2002}}</ref>
During her tenure on Friends Kudrow appeared in multiple comedic films such as Romy and Micheles High School Reunion, Hanging Up, Marci X, Dr. Dolittle 2, Analyze This and its sequel Analyze That, and dramatic films, such as Wonderland (2003 film) Wonderland and The Opposite of Sex.

She also guest starred on numerous television series during Friends, including The Simpsons, Hope and Gloria, King of the Hill, and hosting Saturday Night Live.

===2004–present: Post-Friends===
File:Lisa Kudrow.jpg thumb 235x235px <center>Kudrow at the 1st Streamy Awards</center>

Kudrow starred as protagonist Valerie Cherish on the single-season HBO series The Comeback (TV series) The Comeback (premiered June 5, 2005), about a has-been sitcom star trying for a comeback. She also served as co-creator, writer, and executive producer. The series was cancelled by HBO before being renewed for a second season nearly a decade after being originally cancelled. Kudrow received two Emmy nominations for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series for her work on The Comeback.<ref name="Lisa">{{cite web title=Lisa Kudrow- Biography url=https://movies.yahoo.com/person/lisa-kudrow/biography.html work=Yahoo! ==Awards and nominations==
{{Main List of awards and nominations received by Lisa Kudrow}}
Kudrow has been honored with numerous accolades over her career. For her role in the sitcom Friends, she received six nominations at the Primetime Emmy Awards, winning in 1996 for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series. Kudrow has also received twelve nominations and two wins at the Screen Actors Guild Awards, as well as one win and eight nominations at the American Comedy Awards.
