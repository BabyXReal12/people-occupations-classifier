Michael Hazen James McIntyre (born 21 February 1976) is an English comedian, actor, and TV presenter. In 2012, he was reported to be the highest-grossing comedian in the world.<ref name=2012earnings>{{cite news url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/comedy/9792765/McIntyre-is-worlds-most-successful-comedian.html location=London work=The Daily Telegraph title=McIntyre is worlds most successful comedian date=25 January 2013}}</ref>

In addition to stand-up, McIntyre has hosted his own BBC One  comedy programme Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow, has featured in three episodes of Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo and, in 2011, was a judge on Britains Got Talent. He currently presents his own Saturday night series Michael McIntyres Big Show on BBC One.

==Career==
===Television===
File:Michael McIntyre.jpg thumb left 175px McIntyre at the British Academy Television Awards 2009.
Aged 13, Michael was in the audience of the BBC One Saturday morning show Going Live! on 1 April 1989, where he appeared on camera asking a question to pop group Five Star.

McIntyre has appeared three times on BBC Ones Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo, in 2007, 2008 and 2009. He has released four DVDs, Live and Laughing, Hello Wembley, Showtime and Happy and Glorious, that have sold a combined total of over five million copies in the UK.<ref>{{cite web last=Cox  first=Gordon  url=https://variety.com/2012/legit/news/mcintyre-luring-fans-for-live-and-laughing-1118062107/  title=McIntyre: Luring fans for Live and Laughing  publisher=Variety  date=15 November 2012  accessdate=30 July 2013}}</ref>

McIntyres many comedy panel show and chat show appearances include Chris Moyles Quiz Night, Mock the Week, 8 out of 10 Cats, Have I Got News for You, The Big Fat Quiz of the Year, Would I Lie to You? (TV series) Would I Lie To You?, The Apprentice: Youre Fired!, Alan Carr: Chatty Man and Friday Night with Jonathan Ross.

From 6 June 2009, McIntyre began hosting Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow, which aired Saturday nights on BBC One. On 5 July 2009, McIntyre appeared on the BBC show Top Gear (2002 TV series) Top Gear as the "star in a reasonably priced car", driving around the Top Gear test track in one minute and 48.7 seconds. During his lap of the track he almost rolled the car while going around the final corner; but his hair was kempt.

On 31 March 2010, McIntyre took part in Channel 4s Comedy Gala, a benefit show held in aid of Great Ormond Street Hospital, filmed live at Londons O2 Arena. He also appeared on the years edition of The Big Fat Quiz of the Year on Channel 4, where he was partnered with Alan Carr.

On 14 December 2010, it was announced that McIntyre would join the ITV talent show Britains Got Talent as a judge in 2011 with David Hasselhoff and Amanda Holden. McIntyre and Hasselhoff replaced Piers Morgan and Simon Cowell, although Cowell returned for the live shows. Following the series, McIntyre announced that he would be leaving the show after appearing for one series.<ref>{{cite web url=http://talent.itv.com/2010/news/article/read_new-bgt-judges-revealed_item_100783.html archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713070346/http://talent.itv.com/2010/news/article/read_new-bgt-judges-revealed_item_100783.html title=New BGT judges revealed! archivedate=13 July 2011 work=itv.com}}</ref>

In 2010 and 2014, McIntyre hosted the Royal Variety Performance on ITV (TV network) ITV.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.itv.com/presscentre/press-releases/michael-mcintyre-host-royal-variety-performance-2014 title=Michael McIntyre to host The Royal Variety Performance 2014 work=presscentre}}</ref> He also previously performed on the show twice: in 2006 and 2008.

McIntyre co-hosted various segments of Comic Relief in 2011 and 2013 on BBC One. On Christmas Day 2011, McIntyre hosted a Christmas Special edition of Michael McIntyres Comedy Roadshow. The show was watched by 8.1 million viewers.<ref>{{cite web url= http://www.televisual.com/news-detail/Downton-Abbey-overtakes-EastEnders-in-updated-Xmas-ratings_nid-1019.html title= Downton Abbey overtakes EastEnders in updated Xmas ratings work=televisual.com date=  accessdate=4 January 2012}}</ref>

In 2014 McIntyre fronted his own BBC One talk programme, The Michael McIntyre Chat Show.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/news/a544078/michael-mcintyre-lands-his-own-bbc-one-chatshow.html title=Michael McIntyre lands his own BBC One chatshow work=Digital Spy}}</ref> A Christmas special aired on Christmas Day in 2014.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/tv/michael_mcintyre_xmas_show/ title=Michael McIntyres Very Christmassy Christmas Show - BBC1 Variety publisher=British Comedy Guide comedy.co.uk accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref>

On 5 April 2015, Michael presented Michael McIntyre Presents...Easter Night at the Coliseum, a one-off special for BBC One.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b05ql1nj title=Michael McIntyres Easter Night at the Coliseum - BBC One publisher=BBC Online accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/tv/michael_mcintyre_presents/ title=Michael McIntyres Easter Night At The Coliseum - BBC1 Variety publisher=British Comedy Guide comedy.co.uk accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref>

In November 2015, it was announced that McIntyre would return to BBC One for Michael McIntyres Big Christmas Show. The show was recorded in front of a live audience in the Theatre Royal in London before airing on Christmas Day.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2015/michael-mcintyre-christmas title=BBC - Michael McIntyre’s Big Christmas Show returns to BBC One this Christmas - Media Centre publisher=BBC Online accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/news/story/000001952/michael_mcintyre_big_christmas_show_2015/ title=Michael McIntyre to host another BBC Christmas special date=10 November 2015 publisher=British Comedy Guide comedy.co.uk accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref>

Since April 2016, McIntyre has presented Michael McIntyres Big Show, a Saturday night entertainment series for BBC One. A second series began airing in November 2016. A third series began airing in November 2017.<ref>http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2016/michael-mcintyres-big-show-bbc-one</ref>

===Stage===
McIntyre has performed three times on the Royal Variety Performance, including in 2010 when he became the youngest ever host.<ref>{{cite news url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/8605737.stm title=Michael McIntyre to host Royal Variety Performance work=BBC date= accessdate=25 January 2013}}</ref>

In 2009, McIntyre performed for an estimated total of 500,000 people on his first tour of the United Kingdom that included a record-breaking six nights at Wembley Arena and four at The O2 Arena.

In 2012, his UK tour included 71 arena dates, playing to over 700,000 people, including a record breaking 10 nights at the O2 Arena (London) O2 Arena in London. According to ticket sales company Pollstar the tour helped make McIntyre the worlds biggest selling comedian in 2012, bringing in around £21m.<ref>{{cite web url= http://www.suchsmallportions.com/news/michael-mcintyre-worlds-best-selling-comedian-2012 title= Michael McIntyre worlds best-selling comedian in 2012 work=www. suchsmallportions.com date=  accessdate=8 January 2013}}</ref>

On 20 April 2013 McIntyre played the biggest comedy gig in Africa to 9,000 fans at the Coca-Cola Dome in Johannesburg.{{citation needed date=December 2013}}<!--this link reported as dead by another editor<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.offthekerb.co.uk/news/michael-mcintyre-plays-to-a-sell-out-9000-in-the-biggest-comedy-show-to-date-in-africa/  title=Archived copy  accessdate=21 April 2013  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130506131621/http://www.offthekerb.co.uk/news/michael-mcintyre-plays-to-a-sell-out-9000-in-the-biggest-comedy-show-to-date-in-africa/  archivedate=6 May 2013 }}</ref>-->

In 2015, McIntyre toured the UK and Ireland with his "Happy & Glorious" tour. In February 2016, he took the tour to Norway for two dates, and in October 2016 he took it to Australia for four dates.

Between early 2017 and mid-2019, McIntyre will be performing 104 shows in 5 continents during his world tour, Michael McIntyres Big World Tour.<ref>www.michaelmcintyre.co.uk/big-world-tour/</ref>
