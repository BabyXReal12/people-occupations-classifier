
Blake Raymond Anderson (born March 2, 1984) is an American actor, comedian, producer, screenwriter and fashion designer. He was one of the stars and co-creators of the Comedy Central series Workaholics.

==Early life==
Anderson was born in Sacramento County, California and attended Clayton Valley Charter High School Clayton Valley High School in Concord, California. He was ridiculed for his curly hair, so he shaved it off in 1998 due to peer pressure.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.mailordercomedy.com/bios.html  title=Bios  publisher=Mail Order Comedy  date=2011-04-06  accessdate=2012-09-02}}</ref> He later moved to Los Angeles to work with improvisational theatre improvisational comedy troupes such as The Groundlings and Upright Citizens Brigade.<ref name=ComedyCentralBio>{{cite web title=Blake Andersons Bio url=http://www.comedycentral.com/press/bios/blakeanderson.jhtml?print=true publisher=Comedy Central accessdate=17 September 2013}}</ref> While working as a pizza delivery driver, he attended Orange Coast College in Costa Mesa, California Costa Mesa, where he met future co-star Adam DeVine.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.rollogrady.com/rollo-grady-interview-blake-anderson-of-workaholics/ title=Rollo & Grady Interview :: Blake Anderson of Workaholics author=Rollo author2=Grandy last-author-amp=yes date=September 2, 2011 work=RolloGrandy.com accessdate=2013-09-30}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2006, Anderson formed the sketch comedy group Mail Order Comedy with Anders Holm, Adam DeVine, and Kyle Newacheck. He appeared on several episodes of the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox show Traffic Light (TV series) Traffic Light and has had small cameos in an episode of HBOs Entourage (U.S. TV series) Entourage. He was also in National Lampoons The Legend of Awesomest Maximus (2009) and an episode of the Fox show House (TV series) House. He has appeared on many YouTube shows such as Mail Order Comedy (for which he writes and produces all of the material) and Dungeon of the Nutsack. He starred on the Comedy Central show Workaholics between 2011 and 2017. He has had roles on Community (TV series) Community, Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development, and Loiter Squad. He also tours the country to perform his stand-up comedy.<ref name=ComedyCentralBio/> In June 2016 it was announced that Anderson, Devine, Holm and Newacheck are working on a film with Seth Rogen as producer.<ref>{{cite web

==Personal life==
Anderson is an avid fan of the hip-hop music scene in the East Bay (San Francisco Bay Area) East Bay area, noting Lil B as one of his favorite performers.<ref>{{cite web last=Evers  first=Derek  url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/features/workaholics-blake-anderson  title=Workaholic Blake Anderson &#124; Bothering  publisher=Impose Magazine  date=2011-05-11  accessdate=2012-09-02}}</ref> Anderson is a Golden State Warriors fan. 

On December 17, 2011, Anderson required surgery after Spinal fracture fracturing his spine during a house party by jumping from his roof onto a beer pong table.<ref>{{cite news author=AMANDA MIKELBERG  url=http://articles.nydailynews.com/2011-12-24/news/30552784_1_spinal-surgery-anders-holm-spine  title=WATCH: Workaholics’ star fractures spine after jumping from roof - New York Daily News  publisher=Articles.nydailynews.com  date=2011-12-24  accessdate=2012-09-02}}</ref><ref name=DailyMail>{{cite news last=Durante first=Thomas title=I got painkillers flowing...
