Blake Raymond Anderson (born March 2, 1984) is an American actor, comedian, producer, screenwriter and fashion designer. He was one of the stars and co-creators of the Comedy Central series Workaholics.

==Early life==
Anderson was born in Sacramento County, California and attended Clayton Valley Charter High School Clayton Valley High School in Concord, California. He was ridiculed for his curly hair, so he shaved it off in 1998 due to peer pressure.
He later moved to Los Angeles to work with improvisational theatre improvisational comedy troupes such as The Groundlings and Upright Citizens Brigade.
While working as a pizza delivery driver, he attended Orange Coast College in Costa Mesa, California Costa Mesa, where he met future co-star Adam DeVine.
==Career==
In 2006, Anderson formed the sketch comedy group Mail Order Comedy with Anders Holm, Adam DeVine, and Kyle Newacheck. He appeared on several episodes of the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox show Traffic Light (TV series) Traffic Light and has had small cameos in an episode of HBOs Entourage (U.S. TV series) Entourage. He was also in National Lampoons The Legend of Awesomest Maximus (2009) and an episode of the Fox show House (TV series) House. He has appeared on many YouTube shows such as Mail Order Comedy (for which he writes and produces all of the material) and Dungeon of the Nutsack. He starred on the Comedy Central show Workaholics between 2011 and 2017. He has had roles on Community (TV series) Community, Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development, and Loiter Squad. He also tours the country to perform his stand-up comedy.
