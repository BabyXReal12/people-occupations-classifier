Bridget Everett (born April 21, 1972)<ref>{{Cite web url=https://twitter.com/JoesPub/status/590548354960117760 title=Joes Pub on Twitter website=Twitter access-date=2016-05-27}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web url=http://www.villagevoice.com/news/alt-cabaret-provocateur-bridget-everett-is-the-most-exciting-performer-in-new-york-city-6659332 title=Alt-Cabaret Provocateur Bridget Everett Is the Most Exciting Performer in New York City last=Seabaugh first=Julie date=2014-09-09 website=Village Voice access-date=2016-05-27}}</ref> is an American comedian, actress, singer, writer, and cabaret performer. She has performed stand-up on Inside Amy Schumer<ref name="IMDb">{{cite web title=IMDb url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm3008608/ accessdate=11 November 2014}}</ref> on Comedy Central. Everett has described herself as an “alt-cabaret provocateur.”<ref name=YouTube>{{cite web title=Bridget Everett: No Boundaries, No Filter, Lots of Touching url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ngbtF8KQ34w website=YouTube publisher=Fusion accessdate=28 December 2014}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

==Early life==
Originally from Manhattan, Kansas Manhattan, Kansas,<ref name=NYTimes>{{cite web last1=Isherwood first1=Charles title=A friendly face with a foul mouth that sings url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/01/26/theater/reviews/26pink.html website=NY Times accessdate=11 November 2014 date=26 January 2007}}</ref> she has been performing in New York City for over a decade. Per Everett on the podcast Amy Schumer Presents: 3 Girls, 1 Keith (episode 2: "Moms and Stuff"): “I’m from Manhattan, Kansas- the little apple. And my dad was mayor at one point, and then so was my brother many years later. So we’re kinda like the Kennedy’s of Manhattan, Kansas."

Everett attended college at Arizona State University on a full scholarship to study music and opera.  She worked in the restaurant business for 25 years before she was finally able to quit around the beginning of 2015.<ref>{{cite web title=Bridget Everett Chats About "Fun Mom Dinner" url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PyqLaHgSYuc website=YouTube.com accessdate=25 July 2018 date=1 August 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
Everett met Amy Schumer at the Just for Laughs comedy festival in 2009.<ref>{{cite web title=Bridget Everett: Amy Schumer changed my life Larry King Now Ora.TV url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mXN1xNnMAns website=YouTube.com accessdate=13 July 2018}}</ref>  Everett has been regularly opening for Schumer on her comedy tours since 2012<ref>{{cite web title=Bridget Everett On Inside Amy Schumer and HBO Canada, Singing "T*****s" url=http://thecomicscomic.com/2013/07/03/bridget-everett-on-inside-amy-schumer-and-hbo-canada-singing-ts/ website=thecomicscomic.com accessdate=13 July 2018}}</ref> to present.  Everett has even upstaged Schumer, then leading Schumer to want to have Everett close for her instead, according to Schumer on her new podcast.<ref>{{cite web title=Amy Schumer Presents: 3 Girls, 1 Keith url=https://open.spotify.com/show/2UtdM2mvBQesTqGTyiKn8w website=Spotify.com accessdate=13 July 2018}}</ref>

Everett often performs with her band, The Tender Moments, which includes Adam “Ad-Rock” Horovitz from the Beastie Boys and Carmine Covelli from The Julie Ruin. In October 2013, Everett and Horovitz performed in a show called Rock Bottom that the two co-wrote with Hairspray (musical) Hairspray songwriters Marc Shaiman and Scott Wittman.<ref name=NyMag>{{cite web last1=Langmuir first1=Molly title=129 minutes with Bridget Everett url=http://nymag.com/news/intelligencer/encounter/bridget-everett-2013-12/ website=New York Magazine accessdate=11 November 2014 date=24 November 2013}}</ref> Rock Bottom won Everett the 2015 Obie Awards Special Citation presented by the American Theatre Wing.<ref>Obie Awards, [http://www.obieawards.com/2015/05/2015-obie-award-winners-announced/ 2015 Winners].</ref> Bridget has also performed with Brad Williams (comedian) Brad Williams in a show called Down n Dirty (hosted by Broad City{{’}}s Abbi Jacobson and Ilana Glazer) at the 2014 Bonnaroo Music Festival.

Bridget performed a duet of Me and Bobby McGee with Patti LuPone at Carnegie Hall.<ref name="NyMag" />

In 2013, Bridget Everett and the Tender Moments released an album with twelve tracks entitled Pound It!

Everetts first one-hour TV special, Bridget Everett: Gynecological Wonder, premiered on Comedy Central in 2015. In it, she performs her usual brand of comedy cabaret.

On August 1, 2017 Everett got a standing ovation for her Piece of My Heart karaoke performance<ref>{{Citation last=The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon title=Bridget Everett Gets a Standing Ovation for Her "Piece of My Heart" Karaoke Performance date=2017-08-02 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K8Ab-PuF1DY&feature=youtu.be accessdate=2017-08-03}}</ref> on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon. On January 3, 2018 The Tonight Show aired a repeat of Everett performing Piece of My Heart.
