Steve Rizzo is an American Motivational speaking motivational speaker, author, and former Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian, who is notable for his Humour humorous style of motivational speaking and writing.<ref name=Speaker>{{cite news  url=http://www.nsaspeaker-magazine.org/nsaspeaker/201009?pg=42  title=Change Agents: Reinvention Paid Off for Four Speakers Who Dared to Venture Down New Paths  author=Kelli Vrla  publisher=National Speakers Association  magazine=Speaker  pages=42–43  date=September 2010  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> Rizzo is a member of the National Speakers Association (NSA) and an inductee of its Council of Peers Award for Excellence (CPAE) National Speakers Association#Speakers Hall of Fame Speaker Hall of Fame.<ref name=NSA-1>{{cite web  url=http://www.nsaspeaker.org/community/recognition/cpae-speaker-hall-of-fame/  title=CPAE Speaker Hall of Fame  publisher=National Speakers Association  accessdate=June 5, 2013  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150811205755/http://www.nsaspeaker.org/community/recognition/cpae-speaker-hall-of-fame/  archivedate=August 11, 2015  df= }}</ref> His book, Becoming a Humor Being: The Power to Choose a Better Way won the Writers Digest International Self-Published Book Awards.<ref name=NSA-2>{{cite web  url=http://bureau.espeakers.com/nsas/speaker.php?sid=3314  title=Steve Rizzo, PhB, CPAE  publisher=National Speakers Association  accessdate=June 3, 2013}}</ref>

==Background==
Steve Rizzo is a native of Brooklyn in New York City.<ref name=H&R>{{cite news  url=http://herald-review.com/news/local/community-leaders-breakfast-speaker-finds-success-in-laughter/article_b80f98f5-4684-5640-bc79-3406f2377c51.html  title=Community Leaders Breakfast speaker finds success in laughter  author=Mary Tallon  newspaper=Herald & Review  date=February 8, 2008  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref><ref name=TBB>{{cite news  url=http://trailer-bodybuilders.com/mag/trucks_humor/  title=How to be a Humor Being  author=Rick Weber  publisher=Penton Media  magazine=Trailer/Body Builders  date=March 1, 2002  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> While growing up, "Rizzo was [once] told by a [high school] guidance counselor that he didnt have the intelligence for college." He went on to study at the Long Island University C. W. Post Campus, earning a Bachelor of Arts in English and Education (with Honors student Honors) and Master of Arts in Theater Arts.<ref name=NSA-2/>

==Career==
Rizzo was once an English teacher for the 7th and 8th grade students at Babylon (village), New York#Public Schools West Babylon Junior High School in West Babylon, New York, and counselor to students with behavioral problems in the 9th to 12th grades. He later became a comedian,<ref name=PP>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=YsgdAAAAIBAJ&sjid=gGEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7103,673666&dq=steve-rizzo+comedian&hl=en  title=Night Life: Comedy, Jazz Reign In District Clubs  author=Lenny Litman  publisher=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette  newspaper=The Pittsburgh Press  page=25  date=June 2, 1983  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref><ref name=SM>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=wM8wAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Zt0FAAAAIBAJ&pg=5903,5394952&dq=steve-rizzo+comedian&hl=en  title=Industrial Week  newspaper=The Southeast Missourian  page=40  date=September 28, 1997  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> and was roommate to Drew Carey, had Dennis Miller and Rosie ODonnell open shows for him and shared the stage with Eddie Murphy, Rodney Dangerfield, Jerry Seinfeld and Ellen DeGeneres before moving on to become a motivational speaker.<ref name=McGraw-Hill>{{cite web  url=http://www.mcgrawhill.ca/professional/media/business/get-your-shift-together/  title=Get Your SHIFT Together  publisher=McGraw-Hill  date=January 24, 2013  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> Rizzo left a promising career of over 18 years in stand-up comedy to pursue a new career in motivational speaking; he became a professional speaker in 1994.<ref name=Speaker/>

As a stand-up comic, Rizzo performed at various comedy clubs, including the Pittsburgh Comedy Club in Dormont, Pennsylvania Dormont and the Improv.<ref name=PP/> He also was featured in many television comedy shows, including The Improv An Evening at the Improv, Showtime (TV channel) Showtime Television special Special, The Comedy Channel (United States) The Comedy Channel and Fox Broadcasting Company Fox TVs Comic Strip Live (TV series) Comic Strip Live.<ref name=NSA-2/><ref name=TBB/> In The Pittsburgh Press of June 2, 1983, Lenny Litman described Rizzos comedy style as "whip-cracking satire cushioned with warm and funny stories."<ref name=PP/>

While Rizzo was transitioning from being a stand-up comedian to a motivational speaking motivational speaker, after one of his shows at a New Jersey comedy club, he met Al Parinello, president of the National Speakers Association (NSA)—New Jersey chapter, he encouraged Rizzo to attend a workshop of the National Speakers Association. After the workshop, Rizzo was asked to present How to Add Humor to Your Presentations at a national convention of the National Speakers Association. At this transitioning period, Rizzo also met Nancy Lauterbach, the founder of Five Star Speakers and Trainers after a show in Orlando, Florida, and according to Kelli Vrla in Speaker (a publication of National Speakers Association) of September 2010, "she took him under her wing and helped him structure his presentation. In his first showcase, Rizzo ranked dead last out of 23 speakers because he tried to sound like a "serious motivational speaker." He soon realized his humor was the key to his authenticity. "I was light years ahead of many professional speakers because I had command of an audience," Rizzo says. When Rizzo incorporated humor in his next showcase, buyers immediately hired him. He signed an exclusive agreement with Five Star, and his speaking business took off. Rizzo credits Lauterbach with helping him establish a foothold in his new career."<ref name=Speaker/>

Rizzo had a PBS Television special Special which he created by himself, and he was also the executive producer.<ref name=NSA-2/> In an article published on December 08, 2006 by Oprah Radio (via The Oprah Winfrey Show Oprah.com), Mehmet Oz wrote that "Steve Rizzo is the author of Becoming a Humor Being, and the creator and executive producer of his own nationally syndicated PBS special. After doing stand-up comedy for many years, Steve transitioned to being a professional humorist speaker."<ref name=Oprah>{{cite web  url=http://www.oprah.com/oprahradio/Laughter-is-the-Best-Medicine  title=Laughter is the Best Medicine  author=Dr. Mehmet Oz  author-link=Mehmet Oz  publisher=Oprah Radio  via=The Oprah Winfrey Show Oprah.com  date=December 8, 2006  accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> A publication by McGraw-Hill (introducing Rizzos book, Get Your SHIFT Together: How to Think, Laugh, and Enjoy Your Way to Success in Business and in Life) says, "[Rizzo reaches] well over 50,000 people a year through his talks and weekly Rizzo-Gram e-mails."<ref name=McGraw-Hill/>
