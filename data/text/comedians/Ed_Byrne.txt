Edward Cathal Byrne<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/podcasts/richard_herring_edinburgh/2013_22_ed_byrne_bridget_christie/  title=2013 #22: Ed Byrne, Bridget Christie and Luke Wright - Richard Herrings Edinburgh Fringe Podcast  website=British Comedy Guide  date=23 August 2012  accessdate=12 January 2014}}</ref> (born 10 April 1972)<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0126170/]</ref> is an Irish stand-up comedy stand-up comedian, voice over artist and actor. He has presented television shows Uncut! Best Unseen Ads and Just for Laughs (UK TV series) Just for Laughs, and is a guest on television panel games. As an actor, he played the title character in the 2000 ITV (TV channel) ITV adaptation of the pantomime Aladdin#Adaptations Aladdin.

==Early life==
Byrne was born in Swords, Dublin Swords, Dublin, in Ireland, in 1972, to Jill and Eddie Byrne, the third of four children.{{citation needed date=March 2016}}

==Career==

===Stand-up comedy===
Deciding to study horticulture at the University of Strathclyde, he was made entertainments convener at the Students Union in his second year of study. Byrne started a comedy night called International Comedy Cellar The Comedy Cellar in the basement of the 13th Note public house on Glassford Street, Glasgow. On the first night, Byrne appeared on the roster alongside Armando Ianuccis brother David. Over the following few months, acts booked included Ford Kiernan, Phil Kay, Greg Hemphill, Alan Francis (writer) Alan Francis and Alan Tyler. After a few months he gave up studying and headed to London. He was later booked to appear at The Comedy Cellar by new convenors Ford Kiernan and J.P. Leach.<ref name=DRecord22970549>{{cite news  first=Brian  last=McIver  url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/showbiz/celebrity-interviews/2011/03/06/smaller-venues-are-much-better-for-comedy-not-massive-arenas-says-irish-comic-ed-byrne-86908-22970549/  title=Smaller venues are much better for comedy - not massive arenas, says Irish comic Ed Byrne  newspaper=Daily Record (Scotland) Daily Record  date=6 March 2011  accessdate=12 January 2014}}</ref>

After building up his act on observational comedy, Byrne was nominated for the prestigious Perrier Comedy Award at the 1998 Edinburgh Festival Fringe.

In 2006 he toured the UK, recording a DVD in Leeds, entitled Pedantic and Whimsical, which was largely a "greatest hits" set of his material to date. He has also appeared in Father Ted, and performed voice-over work in the Carphone Warehouse adverts. In February 2007 Byrne appeared on a comedians special edition of The Weakest Link.
Byrne has performed six sell-out shows at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe and three sell-out UK tours. He has also developed huge success on the international circuit where he has performed to full capacity audiences in Canada, France, Australia, New Zealand and the United States.<ref name="Ed Byrne">{{cite web  url=http://www.spoonfed.co.uk/artist/comedy-1524/ed-byrne-18071/  website=Spoonfed.co.uk  title=Ed Byrne  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928040206/http://www.spoonfed.co.uk/artist/comedy-1524/ed-byrne-18071/  archivedate=28 September 2013}}</ref>

He has since garnered a raft of major broadcast credits, including five U.S. appearances on Late Night with Conan OBrien (NBC). In July 2007, Byrne made his début at the Newbury Comedy Festival, having participated in the Cat Laughs Comedy Festival in Kilkenny shortly before. He recorded a live DVD Different Class at The Kings Theatre, Glasgow, on 23 March 2009, which was released on 23 November of that year.

On 15 September 2010, Byrne, along with 54 other public figures, signed an open letter published in The Guardian, stating their opposition to Pope Benedict XVIs state visit to the UK.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2010/sep/15/harsh-judgments-on-pope-religion  title=Letters: Harsh judgments on the pope and religion  newspaper=The Guardian  accessdate=16 September 2010  location=London  date=15 September 2010}}</ref>

Byrne frequently appears on BBC stand-up comedy programme Mock The Week as the most common guest panellist, having appeared on 40 episodes as of 21 July 2016.

==Personal life==
Byrne believes his older brother Stephen was the funniest member of the family as a child. His younger brother Paul is a comedy director and has long been a fixture at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/fringe/2013/fullmooners/  title=Fullmooners - Edinburgh Fringe 2013  website=British Comedy Guide  accessdate=21 January 2014}}</ref> He also has an older sister, Trish.

Byrne married theatre publicist Claire Walker in 2008. The couple live in Essex and have two sons, the elder called Cosmo and the younger named Magnus.<ref>{{cite tweet  first=Ed  last=Byrne  user=MrEdByrne  number=308163626576003072  date=3 March 2013  title=Despite what it says in The Observer...  access-date=25 March 2015}}</ref> Fellow comic Dara Ó Briain was best man at his wedding, returning the favour after Byrne was previously Ó Briains best man in 2006.<ref>{{cite news  last=Leadbeater  first=Chris  title=Dara O’Briain and Ed Byrne head east on the Road to Mandalay  url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/asia/articles/dara-o-briain-ed-byrne-road-to-mandalay-south-east-asia/  accessdate=12 October 2017  newspaper=The Daily Telegraph  date=7 May 2017}}</ref>

Byrne is an atheist and a patron of Humanists UK.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H42HGzynzX4  title=Ed Byrne joins the Census Campaign  website=YouTube  date=  accessdate=12 January 2014}}</ref>

Byrne is an alumnus of the University of Strathclyde.
