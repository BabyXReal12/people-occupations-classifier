Nasim Pedrad ({{lang-fa نسیم پدراد}}; born November 18, 1981)<ref name="tvg-bio">{{cite web   url = http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/nasim-pedrad/bio/298560   title = Nasim Pedrad   publisher= TV Guide TVGuide.com   accessdate= June 9, 2014}}</ref> is an Iranian Americans Iranian-American actress and comedian best known for her five seasons as a cast member on Saturday Night Live from 2009 to 2014. She has since gone on to co-star in sitcoms such as Mulaney, Scream Queens (2015 TV series) Scream Queens, People of Earth (TV series) People of Earth, and New Girl.

==Early life==
Pedrad was born in Tehran, Iran, to a Muslim family.<ref>https://splinternews.com/abc-allegedly-dropped-a-show-about-a-muslim-family-afte-1793858993</ref> Her parents are Arasteh Amani and Parviz Pedrad. Pedrad and her parents lived in Tehran until 1984, before emigrating to the United States. Her younger sister is comedy writer Nina Pedrad.<ref>{{cite web  url= http://splitsider.com/2013/12/30-rock-parks-and-rec/   title= 30 Rock, Parks and Rec, and All the Other Shows Youd Expect Get WGA Nominations   first= Bradford  last= Evans   date= 5 December 2013  work= Splitsider   accessdate= 2 December 2014}}</ref> Both sisters are fluent in Persian language Persian.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.payvand.com/news/10/jan/1274.html  title=Saturday Night Live Hires First Iranian-Born Cast Member  first= Grace  last= Nasri  work= Iran Times  date=2010-01-28  accessdate=2011-02-04}}</ref> The sisters were raised in Irvine, California,<ref name=UCBTheatre>{{cite web  title=Los Angeles - Performers - Nasim Pedrad  work=ucbtheatre.com  url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/performers/8345  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080319000635/http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/performers/8345  dead-url=yes  archive-date=2008-03-19  df= }}</ref> and graduated from University High School (Irvine, California) University High School.<ref>http://www.orangecoast.com/arts-culture/irivne-native-nasim-pedrad-bids-goodbye-new-girl-hello-aladdin/</ref> Nasim graduated from UCLA School of Theater, Film and Television in 2003.<ref name=UCBTheatre /> She was a member of the UCLA Spring Sing#Company  UCLA Spring Sing Company.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://alumni.ucla.edu/stories/nasim-pedrad/ title=Nasim Pedrad {{!}} website=alumni.ucla.edu language=en-US access-date=2017-04-16}}</ref>

==Career==

===Early work===
Pedrad was a performer with the Sunday Company at The Groundlings.<ref name=UCBTheatre /> She frequently performed her one-woman show Me, Myself & Iran at the Los Angeles divisions of ImprovOlympic and the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater. The show was selected for the 2007 HBO Comedy Festival in Las Vegas. She received an LA Weekly Best Comedic Performance of the Year Award as the lead in the comedic spoof After School Special.<ref name=UCBTheatre/>

Pedrad made her first television appearance on an episode of Gilmore Girls. In 2007, she made a guest appearance on The Winner (TV series) The Winner. She had a recurring role on ER (TV series) ER as Nurse Suri.<ref name=UCBTheatre/> In 2009, she had a guest appearance on Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia.<ref name="ratings">{{Cite web url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2009/11/06/its-always-sunny-in-philadelphia-and-the-league-up-over-50-without-world-series/32871 title=Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia and The League up over 50% without World Series work=TV by the Numbers last=Seidman first=Robert date=November 6, 2009 accessdate=August 13, 2010}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
Pedrad joined the cast of Saturday Night Live in 2009 as part of the Saturday Night Live (season 35) 35th (2009–2010) season. Pedrad is one of a handful of cast members born outside North America (joining Italian-born Tony Rosato, New Zealand-born Pamela Stephenson, English-born Morwenna Banks, and Chilean-born Horatio Sanz), and is the shows first female Middle-Eastern cast member. Pedrad became a repertory player in the Saturday Night Live (season 37) 2011–12 season after two years of being a featured player. Pedrad left SNL in 2014 to work on Mulaney,<ref name="Mulaney"/> but the show was cancelled in March 2015.

===Other work===
In 2011, she was a recurring voice on the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox animated series Allen Gregory. She appeared with a small role in the 2011 film No Strings Attached (film) No Strings Attached. In 2012, she had a supporting voice role in the animated feature film The Lorax (film) The Lorax and a small appearance in The Dictator (2012 film) The Dictator. In 2013, Pedrad had another supporting voice role in Despicable Me 2. In  the autumn of 2014, she left Saturday Night Live to star in a new Fox Broadcasting Company Fox sitcom, Mulaney.<ref name="Mulaney">{{cite news   url = http://www.tvguide.com/News/Nasim-Pedrad-Leaving-SNL-Mulaney-1084671.aspx?rss=news   title = Nasim Pedrad Talks Departing SNL for Mulaney: "At Some Point You Have to Leave"   first = Kate   last = Stanhope   date = Jul 20, 2014 <!-- 04:30 PM ET -->    accessdate = 2014-09-08   work = TV Guide TVGuide.com   publisher = CBS Interactive }}</ref> On October 18, 2014, Fox shut down production of the series by reducing the 16-episode order by three episodes. Filming for the thirteenth episode had just been completed prior to the order reduction, and the fourteenth episode was about to enter production.<ref>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2014/10/mulaney-order-cut-13-episodes-fox-854490/ title=‘Mulaney’ Order Trimmed To 13 Episodes author=Nellie Andreeva work=Deadline}}</ref>

Beginning in 2015, Pedrad has a recurring guest role as Los Angeles Police Department LAPD officer Aly Nelson on the Fox sitcom New Girl.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2015/01/08/tuesday-final-ratings-no-adjustments-to-agent-carter-person-of-interest-or-forever/347207/ title=Tuesday Final Ratings: No Adjustments to Agent Carter, Person of Interest or Forever work=TV by the Numbers first=Sara last=Bibel date=January 8, 2015 accessdate=January 8, 2015}}</ref> She portrayed Gigi Caldwell in the first season of Fox Broadcasting Company Fox horror-comedy Scream Queens (2015 TV series) Scream Queens.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/92785/nasim-pedrad-joins-scream-queens/ title=Nasim Pedrad Joins Scream Queens - Dread Central website=www.dreadcentral.com accessdate=17 April 2018}}</ref>

In 2016, she appeared in a commercial for Old Navy alongside comedian Kumail Nanjiani and other SNL cast members Cecily Strong and Jay Pharoah.

In 2017, she joined the cast of second season of the TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBS comedy series People of Earth (TV series) People of Earth. Later that same year she also made guest appearances on Curb Your Enthusiasm and Brooklyn Nine Nine.
