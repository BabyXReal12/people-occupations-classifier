
Morton David Alpern (March 23, 1922 – February 12, 2018), better known as Marty Allen, was an American comedian, actor, and philanthropist. He worked as a comedy headliner in nightclubs, as a dramatic actor in television roles, and was once called "The Darling of Daytime TV". He also appeared in films, notably the 1966 spy comedy The Last of the Secret Agents? During his comedy career, Allen also toured military hospitals, performed for veterans, and for  active military personnel.

Allen was also a philanthropist.  He contributed to the American Cancer Society, The Heart Fund, the March of Dimes, Fight for Sight (U.S.) Fight for Sight, and served on the board of the Epilepsy Foundation.<ref name="Fox"/><ref name="NYTobit"/>

Allen was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, to Jewish parents; Louis Alpern (1898–1977; from Romania/Russia), a restaurant and bar owner<ref name="obit WP">{{cite web url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/obituaries/marty-allen-fuzzy-haired-member-of-popular-1960s-comedy-duo-allen-and-rossi-dies-at-95/2018/02/13/7f7cb81a-10d7-11e8-9570-29c9830535e5_story.html title=Marty Allen, fuzzy-haired member of popular 1960s comedy duo Allen & Rossi, dies at 95 publisher=The Washington Post date=13 February 2018 author=Schudel, Matt}}</ref> and his wife, the former Elsie Moss (1901–1979).{{cn date=March 2018}} He graduated from Taylor Allderdice High School in 1940.  He was inducted into their alumni Hall Of Fame in 2009.<ref>{{cite news last=Hecht first=Steve title=Comedian Marty Allen part of Allderdices first hall class url=http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/local/neighborhoods-east/comedian-marty-allen-part-of-allderdices-first-hall-class-355087/ access-date=July 18, 2012 newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette date=August 27, 2009 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130830192620/http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/local/neighborhoods-east/comedian-marty-allen-part-of-allderdices-first-hall-class-355087/ archivedate=August 30, 2013 df=mdy-all}}</ref>

Allen joined the United States Army Air Corps U.S. Army Air Corps during World War II. He was stationed in Italy where he attained the rank of sergeant. He earned a Soldiers Medal for stopping a fire in a plane that was being refueled.  He saved the lives of the men boarding the burning plane by driving the fuel truck away, returning on foot to the plane, and then putting out the fire by rolling over the flames with his body in uniform. ==Career==
During the early to the mid-1950s, Allen and his first comedy partner, Mitch DeWood, worked as an opening act for stars including Sarah Vaughan, Eydie Gormé, and Nat King Cole.<ref name="Fox"/> Allen and DeWood also worked many clubs, including the Copacabana (nightclub) Copacabana until they broke up in 1958 and went their separate ways.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.msn.com/en-us/tv/news/marty-allen-comedian-and-game-show-regular-dies-at-95/ar-BBJ3CSe?li=BBmkt5R&ocid=ientp title=Marty Allen, Comedian and Game Show Regular, Dies at 95 publisher=msn.com access-date=February 13, 2018 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213222230/http://www.msn.com/en-us/tv/news/marty-allen-comedian-and-game-show-regular-dies-at-95/ar-BBJ3CSe?li=BBmkt5R&ocid=ientp archivedate=February 13, 2018 df=mdy-all}}</ref>

He then became part of the comedy team of Allen & Rossi with Steve Rossi, which resulted in a string of hit comedy albums, 44 appearances on The Ed Sullivan Show (including the famous appearance by The Beatles on 16 February 1964,<ref name="obit WP"/> during which Allen won over the Beatles fans in the audience by announcing "Im Ringos mother!"),<ref>{{cite book last=Nachman first=Gerald title=Right Here On Our Stage Tonight: Ed Sullivans America date=2010 publisher=University of California Press location=Berkeley isbn=978-0520268012 page=226 chapter=Inside the Star-Making Machine}}</ref> and the film The Last of the Secret Agents? (1966).<ref name="WPO">{{cite web url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/tv/comedian-marty-allen-dies-in-las-vegas-at-95/2018/02/12/61e3a4e4-1073-11e8-a68c-e9374188170e_story.html title=Comedian Marty Allen Dies in Las Vegas at 95 publisher=The Washington Post date=February 13, 2018 accessdate=February 13, 2018 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213222230/https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/tv/comedian-marty-allen-dies-in-las-vegas-at-95/2018/02/12/61e3a4e4-1073-11e8-a68c-e9374188170e_story.html archivedate=February 13, 2018 df=mdy-all}}</ref> They worked together from 1957 to 1968, parted ways amicably, and reunited for shows from the 1970s through the 1990s.<ref name="latobit"/>

In 1961 and 1962, Allen appeared on Broadway in Let It Ride (musical) Let It Ride! at the Eugene ONeill Theatre and then went on to perform in the pre-Broadway tour and Broadway performances of I Had a Ball in 1964.<ref name="NYTobit"/>

He eventually began performing dramatic roles. His debut as a serious actor came on The Big Valley television series as the hapless Waldo Diefendorfer.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.allmovie.com/artist/marty-allen-1041 title=Allmovie Database publisher=allmovie.com access-date=December 1, 2010 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090917005057/http://www.allmovie.com/artist/marty-allen-1041 archivedate=September 17, 2009 df=mdy-all}}</ref> Throughout the 1970s and into the 1980s, he made hundreds of television appearances, becoming a regular on The Hollywood Squares.<ref name="latobit"/> He appeared on Circus of the Stars, in a cameo on The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!, on game shows such as Password (game show) Password, and in ten Television film made-for-television movies.<ref name="WPO"/><ref name="NYTobit"/> He also appeared in theatrical films such as The Great Waltz (1972 film) The Great Waltz (1972), The Harrad Experiment Harrad Summer (1974) and A Whale of a Tale (1976).<ref name="NYTobit"/><ref name="WPO"/><ref name="latobit"/>

From the 1980s he and his wife, singer-songwriter Karon Kate Blackwell, teamed up to perform their musical comedy act to audiences around the country.<ref name="WPO"/> In 2007, the duo began performing at the Gold Coast Hotel and Casino in Las Vegas Valley Las Vegas and went on to perform at the South Point Hotel, Casino & Spa Southpoint Casino, at Palace Station, and on cruise ships.<ref>[http://classicshowbiz.blogspot.com/2011/05/interview-with-marty-allen-part-one.html An Interview with Marty Allen] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723160844/http://classicshowbiz.blogspot.com/2011/05/interview-with-marty-allen-part-one.html  date=July 23, 2011 }}, classicshowbiz.blogspot.com (May 2011).</ref> In 2015, the couple continued to perform in venues around the country to overflow crowds, at the Rampart Casino<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.vegas24seven.com/?p=46929  title=Legendary Comedian Marty Allen Performs at Rampart Casino, April 17 –  website=Vegas24seven.com  date=  access-date=March 24, 2017}}</ref> and the Downtown Grand in Las Vegas.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.latimes.com/travel/lasvegas/lasvegasnow/la-tr-lvn-las-vegas-shows-marty-allen-20150304-story.html  title=Comedian Marty Allen celebrates his 93rd birthday with two Vegas shows  first=Susan  last=Stapleton  newspaper=Los Angeles Times  date=March 5, 2015  deadurl=no  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160414020834/http://www.latimes.com/travel/lasvegas/lasvegasnow/la-tr-lvn-las-vegas-shows-marty-allen-20150304-story.html  archivedate=April 14, 2016  df=mdy-all }}</ref> In 2016, they performed at the Metropolitan Room in New York City.<ref>[http://www.thespectrum.com/story/entertainment/2016/03/17/marty-allens-still-making-em-laugh/81818590/ Interview with Marty Allen], thespectrum.com, March 2016.</ref>

==Charitable work==
In 1968, he made a "Hello Dere" tour of military hospitals in the United States (a tour named after a catchphrase he popularized).<ref name="NYTobit">{{cite web url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/13/obituaries/marty-allen-wild-eyed-comedy-star-is-dead-at-95.html title=Marty Allen, Wild-Eyed Comedian, Dies at 95 work=The New York Times date=February 13, 2018 accessdate=February 13, 2018 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213222230/https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/13/obituaries/marty-allen-wild-eyed-comedy-star-is-dead-at-95.html archivedate=February 13, 2018 df=mdy-all}}</ref> He repeated the tour annually until 1972.<ref name="latobit"/> During the tours, he talked with and entertained wounded soldiers who had just returned from Vietnam War Vietnam.<ref name="Fox">{{cite web url=http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2018/02/13/comedian-marty-allen-dead-at-95.html title=Comedian Marty Allen Dies at 95 publisher=Fox date=February 2018 deadurl=no archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213141852/http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2018/02/13/comedian-marty-allen-dead-at-95.html archivedate=February 13, 2018 df=mdy-all}}</ref> He was also involved in a number of charitable causes including the American Cancer Society, The Heart Fund, March of Dimes, Fight for Sight (U.S.) Fight for Sight, cerebral palsy Cerebral Palsy, and was on the board of the Epilepsy Foundation.<ref name="Fox"/><ref name="NYTobit"/>

==Death==
