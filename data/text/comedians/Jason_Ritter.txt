Jason Ritter (born February 17, 1980) is an American actor. He is known for his roles as Kevin Girardi in the television series Joan of Arcadia, Ethan Haas in The Class (TV series) The Class, Sean Walker in the National Broadcasting Company NBC series The Event, and as the voice of Dipper Pines in Gravity Falls.  He also played the recurring role of Mark Cyr in the NBC television series Parenthood (2010 TV series) Parenthood, for which he received an Primetime Emmy Awards Emmy Award nomination. In 2017, he starred in the TV comedy series Kevin (Probably) Saves the World.

==Early life==
Ritter was born on February 17, 1980 in Los Angeles, California, to actors Nancy Morgan and John Ritter. He is also a grandson of actors Tex Ritter and Dorothy Fay. His stepmother is actress Amy Yasbeck. The actor has three siblings, including actor Tyler Ritter.

Ritter first appeared in the opening credits of his fathers show, Threes Company. He was the little blonde haired boy running up to Joyce DeWitt in the petting zoo scene.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrities/jason-ritter title=Jason Ritter Bio journal=US Weekly  accessdate=27 April 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/jason-ritter/bio/156004  title=Jason Ritter Biography journal=TV Guide accessdate=27 April 2013}}</ref>

He attended middle and high school at the Crossroads School (Santa Monica, California) Crossroads School in Santa Monica, California, with Simon Helberg, who became his roommate at NYU.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.nerdist.com/2011/07/making-it-1-jason-ritter/ title=Making It #1: Jason Ritter last=Lindhome first=Riki work=The Nerdist Podcast Nerdist accessdate=26 July 2011}}</ref> He then attended and graduated from New York Universitys Tisch School of the Arts, where he studied at the Atlantic Theater Company. He also studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London, UK.

==Career==
File:JasonRitter.jpg thumb left 215x215px Ritter in 2007
Ritter appeared in The Dreamer of Oz: The L. Frank Baum Story, co-starring as Baums son alongside his own father. In 2002, he appeared in the movies PG and Swimfan. In 2003, Ritter had a major role in the slasher film slasher/horror film Freddy vs. Jason as Will Rollins. He appeared in Raise Your Voice and Happy Endings (film) Happy Endings. He portrayed Jeb Bush in the Oliver Stone film W. (film) W. In 2007, he also voiced substitute teacher, Mr. Fisk, in an episode of All Grown Up!, the grown up version of Rugrats, a teacher whom Angelica falls in love with.

In 2008, he released Good Dick, a movie which he produced and starred in along with his long term girlfriend at the time, Marianna Palka (with whom he began a relationship after meeting in Queens, New York Queens in October 1999),<ref name="LALovers">{{cite web title=L.A. Is For Lovers: Jason Ritter & Marianna Palka  first=Whitney  last=Spaner  publisher=Paper Magazine  url=http://www.papermag.com/2009/01/la_is_for_lovers_jason_ritter_marianna_palka.php  date=January 2009  access-date=2015-10-26  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402163250/http://www.papermag.com/2009/01/la_is_for_lovers_jason_ritter_marianna_palka.php  archivedate=April 2, 2015 }}</ref> who also wrote and directed the film.

Among his theater credits are Wendy Wassersteins Third (play) Third at Lincoln Center, for which he won the Clarence Derwent Award and the Martin E. Segal Award for his portrayal of the title character; the Off Broadway production of The Beginning of August, and the role of Tim in the world premiere of Neil LaButes play The Distance From Here at Londons Almeida Theatre. Ritter has volunteered as an actor with the Young Storytellers Program.

In 2012, Ritter starred in the Disney Channel series, Gravity Falls, a show about twins Dipper Pines Dipper and Mabel Pines who are sent to live with their "Grunkle" Stanley Pines Stan, and decipher the weird events of Gravity Falls, Oregon. Ritter voiced Dipper in the show. <ref>{{cite web  title=Gravity Falls (TV Series 2012–2016) - IMDb  url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1865718/  website=IMDb  publisher=IMDb  accessdate=22 June 2018}}</ref>

Ritter has appeared in the MTV show Punkd. Ritter also starred as Sean Walker in the season-long NBC drama series The Event, which premiered on September 20, 2010.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2010/05/07/nbc-picks-up-three-new-series-for-2010-11-season-with-the-event-outsourced-and-love-bites/20100507nbc01  title=NBC Picks Up Three New Series for 2010-11 Season with "The Event", "Outsourced" and "Love Bites" date=May 7, 2010 work=The Futon Critic accessdate=May 16, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://livefeed.hollywoodreporter.com/2010/05/nbc-fall-schedule-2010-11.html  archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100518141626/http://livefeed.hollywoodreporter.com/2010/05/nbc-fall-schedule-2010-11.html  dead-url=yes  archive-date=May 18, 2010  title=NBCs fall schedule, upfront revealed  date=May 16, 2010  last=Hibberd  first=James  series=The Live Feed  work=The Hollywood Reporter  accessdate=May 16, 2010 }}</ref> Ritter will be seen alongside Kate French in the upcoming short film, Atlantis, a romance film centered on two strangers who fall in love during the preparation for the final launch of the NASA space shuttle Space Shuttle Atlantis Atlantis.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.orlandosentinel.com/entertainment/movies/os-atlantis-shuttle-launch-movie-20110709,0,2593868.story work=Orlando Sentinel title=Movie was filmed as space shuttle Atlantis took off from KSC date= 9 July 2011 accessdate= 9 July 2011}}</ref> He had a recurring role on NBCs Parenthood (2010 TV series) Parenthood.<ref>{{cite web last=Ng first=Philiana title=Parenthood: Jason Ritter to Return in Season 3, Teaming Up With Jason Katims for Drama url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/parenthood-jason-ritter-return-season-208861 work=The Hollywood Reporter accessdate=November 29, 2013 date=July 7, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last=Masters first=Megan title=Parenthood Scoop: Jason Ritter Will Return in Season 4… But For How Long? It was confirmed in early March 2013 that he would play Gavin opposite Alexis Bledels Stacey in Us & Them, Fox Broadcasting Company FOXs sitcom television pilot pilot based on the British television show Gavin & Stacey.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/03/alexis-bledel-to-star-in-fox-comedy-pilot-friends-family/ last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=Alexis Bledel To Star in FOX Comedy Pilot Friends & Family work=Deadline Hollywood date=March 2, 2013 accessdate=27 April 2013}}</ref>

Ritter has also been featured in the American period sitcom Another Period as Lord Frederick Bellacourt. His character is the illiterate twin brother of Beatrice, with whom he is involved in an incestuous relationship.

In 2018, Ritter had a supporting role in The Tale, an autobiographical feature film written and directed by Jennifer Fox (documentary filmmaker) Jennifer Fox. The film premiered at the 2018 Sundance Film Festival on January 20, 2018 and later on HBO on May 26th, 2018.
