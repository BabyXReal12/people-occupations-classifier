
Cecily Legler Strong  (born February 8, 1984)<ref name="sjr1">{{cite web url=http://www.sj-r.com/top-stories/x1665863890/New-SNL-comedian-has-shallow-Springfield-roots title=New SNL Comedian has shallow Springfield roots work=The State Journal-Register location=Springfield, Illinois first=Chris last=Dettro accessdate=October 5, 2013 date=January 19, 2013 quote=Strongs father, Bill Strong, was the Associated Press bureau chief at the Statehouse when Cecily was born in February 1984. archivedate=November 3, 2014 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141103123230/http://www.sj-r.com/x1665863890/New-SNL-comedian-has-shallow-Springfield-roots deadurl=no}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/nbcsnl/status/299955455151718400 title=Happy Birthday Cecily Strong!! publisher=Saturday Night Live official Twitter page date=February 8, 2013 accessdate=October 5, 2013}}</ref> is an American actress and comedian who has been a cast member of Saturday Night Live since 2012.<ref>{{cite news last=Metz first=Nina title=Aidy Bryant, Tim Robinson, Cecily Strong join cast of SNL url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2012-09-10/entertainment/chi-join-cast-on-snl-20120910_1_snl-executive-producer-weekend-update-io-theater newspaper=Chicago Tribune date=September 10, 2012}}</ref>

==Early life==
Strong was born in Springfield, Illinois, and was raised in Oak Park, Illinois, an inner ring suburb of Chicago.<ref name=sjr2>{{cite web url=http://www.sj-r.com/entertainment/x1837085501/Cecily-Strong-to-co-anchor-SNLs-Weekend-Update title=Cecily Strong to co-anchor SNLs Weekend Update publisher=Sj-r.com date=September 16, 2013 accessdate=October 5, 2013}} {{subscription required date=October 2013}}</ref> She is the daughter of Penelope and William "Bill" Strong, who worked as an Associated Press bureau chief and is now managing partner at a Chicago public relations firm.<ref name="sjr1"/><ref name=sjr2/> Strongs parents are divorced.<ref name=trib/> Strong grew up adoring SNL as a child, reenacting sketches with her friend<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/02/09/magazine/cecily-strong-is-being-serious.html title=Cecily Strong Is Being Serious work=The New York Times date=February 7, 2014 accessdate=November 3, 2014}}</ref> and watching old SNL commercials on VHS. "I had a tape of the best commercials, and I wore it out, every day." She has stated that she was inspired by Phil Hartman.<ref>{{Cite video  url=https://vimeo.com/210965557  title=Cecily Strong - Late Night with Jimmy Fallon (2013)  date=February 19, 2013}}</ref>

She attended Oak Park and River Forest High School before transferring for her senior year to the Chicago Academy for the Arts, where she graduated in 2002.<ref name=trib/> She then studied acting at California Institute of the Arts (CalArts), graduating in 2006 with a Bachelor of Fine Arts BFA in theatre.<ref>{{cite web url=http://scvnews.com/2012/12/11/calarts-grad-cecily-strong-joins-cast-of-snl/ title=CalArts Grad Cecily Strong Added to Cast of SNL date=December 11, 2012 publisher=SCV News accessdate=October 5, 2013}}</ref><ref name=NBC>{{cite web title=Cast Bios url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/bios/cecily-strong_38.shtml work=Cecily Strong publisher=NBC accessdate=September 12, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/snl-weekend-update-cecily-strong-1073130.aspx title=Meet Saturday Night Lives New "Weekend Update" Anchor Cecily Strong work=TV Guide date=November 8, 2013 first=Bruce last=Fretts accessdate=November 3, 2014}}</ref> After graduating from CalArts, Strong returned to Chicago, where she studied at the The Second City Training Center Second City Conservatory and iO Theater iO Chicago.

==Career==
Strong performed regularly at The Second City and IO Theater iO Chicago.<ref name=trib>{{cite web url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-06-14/entertainment/chi-interview-cecily-strong-20130613_1_box-office-second-city-aidy-bryant title=Interview: SNL star Cecily Strong returns home amid dream year work=Chicago Tribune date=June 14, 2013 first=Luis last=Gomez accessdate=November 3, 2014}}</ref> Strong has performed on a "cruise ship with fellow Second City members for four months".<ref>http://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2016/01/cecily-strong-saturday-night-live</ref> She appeared at the Chicago Sketch Fest, Just for Laughs Chicago Just for Laughs, New York Sketchfest, the Edinburgh Fringe Festival,<ref name=NBC/> at the Goodman Theater, Bailiwick Repertory Theatre Bailiwick Theater, the Mercury Theater, and with the all-female improv troupe Virgin Daiquiri.<ref>{{cite web url=http://cecilystrong.tumblr.com/ title=Cecily Strong publisher=Tumblr accessdate=October 3, 2012}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
Strong debuted as a featured player on Saturday Night Live on September 15, 2012.<ref name=NBC/><ref>{{cite news last=Levin first=Gary title=Saturday Night Live adds three new cast members url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/entertainment/post/2012/09/Saturday-Night-Live-adds-three-new-cast-members-70000041/snl-adds-three-new-cast-members/70000041/1 newspaper=USA Today date=September 10, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news title=Saturday Night Live adds 3 performers for upcoming season after departures of Wiig, Samberg url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/tv/saturday-night-live-adds-3-performers-for-upcoming-season-after-departures-of-wiig-samberg/2012/09/10/e1bc7170-fb5f-11e1-98c6-ec0a0a93f8eb_story.html newspaper=The Washington Post (The Associated Press) date=September 10, 2012}}</ref> Strong co-anchored the recurring Weekend Update segment with Seth Meyers, beginning with the season 39 premiere. Strong later co-anchored with Colin Jost,<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304279904579518191375460658 title=Cecily Strong and Colin Jost Are Newest Weekend Update Anchors on Saturday Night Live work=The Wall Street Journal date=May 9, 2014 first=Richard last=Morgan accessdate=November 3, 2014}}</ref> and was replaced on Weekend Update with writer Michael Che, beginning with the season 40 premiere in September 2014, partly at her own request to focus on doing sketches as a part of the regular cast.<ref>Reed, Ryan (September 12, 2014). [https://www.rollingstone.com/tv/news/snl-replaces-cecily-strong-as-weekend-update-anchor-20140912 "SNL Replaces Cecily Strong as Weekend Update Anchor"]. Rolling Stone.</ref>

Strongs recurring characters include an extremely ditzy, unintelligent and unnamed pseudo-activist known as "The Girl You Wish You Hadnt Started a Conversation With at a Party",<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/March-2013/Questions-for-Saturday-Night-Lives-Cecily-Strong/ title=Questions for Saturday Night Lives Cecily Strong work=Chicago Magazine date=February 20, 2013 first=Elly last=Fishman accessdate=November 3, 2014}}</ref> Dana (one of a pair of loud-mouthed, unfriendly retail employees who always insult their coworkers out of fear of being fired), Heather the One-Dimensional Female Character from a Male-Driven Comedy, Kyra from "The Girlfriends Talk Show", Cathy Anne, the drug addict neighbor of Michael Che, who has a strong opinion on current events, and an unnamed blond former porn star-turned-model/commercial actress who hawks elegant items. Most of her recurring characters are either very feminine, polished, youthful and/or mentally unstable. Her celebrity impressions include Huma Abedin, Brooke Baldwin, Maria Bartiromo, Gloria Borger, Paula Broadwell, Erin Burnett, Marion Cotillard, Stormy Daniels, Lana Del Rey, Fran Drescher, Gloria Estefan, Dianne Feinstein, Carly Fiorina, Ariana Grande, Hope Hicks, Scottie Nell Hughes, Anjelica Huston, Kendall Jenner, Khloe Kardashian, Jill Kelley, Megyn Kelly, Sarah Koenig, Tara Lipinski, Rachel Maddow, Kate Middleton,  Lin-Manuel Miranda, Liza Minnelli, Alanis Morissette, Jeanine Pirro, Michelle Rodriguez, Melania Trump, Ivana Trump, Sofia Vergara and Allison Williams (actress) Allison Williams.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://snlarchives.net/Cast/?CeSt title=SNL Archives {{!}} Cast {{!}} Cecily Strong website=snlarchives.net access-date=2018-01-28}}</ref>

===Other work===
Strong was the featured entertainer at the 2015 White House Correspondents Association dinner (cracking that she was the first straight woman to be so in twenty years).<ref>{{Citation last=C-SPAN title=Cecily Strong complete remarks at 2015 White House Correspondents Dinner (C-SPAN) date=April 25, 2015 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uH5XAeKdrjM accessdate=February 12, 2017}}</ref> She took digs at the various news organizations in attendance, politicians of all persuasions, and President Obama. She also took shots at the US Secret Service, host location the Washington Hilton, Brian Williams, Sarah Koenig and the state of Indiana.<ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/reliable-source/wp/2015/04/26/cecily-strongs-harshest-burns-in-her-white-house-correspondents-dinner-speech/ Cecily Strong’s harshest burns in her White House correspondents’ dinner speech], Washington Post, April 26, 2015. Retrieved May 1, 2015</ref>

In 2016, she appeared in a Television advertisement commercial for Old Navy,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.campaignlive.com/article/old-navy-enlists-snl-alums-improvise-series-online-spots/1393554 title=Old Navy enlists SNL alums to improvise series of online spots publisher=Campaign Live accessdate=August 20, 2016}}</ref> alongside other SNL cast members Nasim Pedrad and Jay Pharoah. That year, Strong also joined the climate change documentary show Years of Living Dangerously as a celebrity correspondent.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/david-letterman-national-geographic-channel-climate-change-1201596066/ title=David Letterman Will Explore Climate Change For National Geographic Docu-Series publisher=Variety accessdate=June 17, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, she guest starred as Samantha Stevens in TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBSs Angie Tribeca and Catherine Hobart in FOXs Scream Queens (2015 TV series) Scream Queens. She appeared in a series of Television advertisement commercials for Triscuit in 2017.
