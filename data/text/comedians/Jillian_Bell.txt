Jillian Leigh Bell<ref>{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/jilliandeltoro/status/475837305677045761 title=Jillian Bell on Twitter work=Twitter accessdate=December 22, 2015}}</ref> (born April 25, 1984)<ref>{{cite web url=http://thespazmatics.com/jillian-bells-30th/ title=Jillian Bell’s 30th publisher= accessdate=December 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://twitter.com/lennyjacobson/status/592034394707271680 title=Lenny Jacobson on Twitter work=Twitter accessdate=December 22, 2015}}</ref> is an American comedian, actress, and screenwriter. She is best known for her recurring roles as Jillian Belk on Workaholics and Dixie on the final season of Eastbound & Down, as well as appearing in 22 Jump Street and Fist Fight.

==Early life==
Bell was born and raised in Las Vegas, Nevada, the daughter of Tanzy and Ron Bell.<ref name=variety>{{cite web author=Justin Shady  url=https://variety.com/2012/scene/people-news/bell-workaholic-aces-master-class-1118056715/  title=Bell: Workaholic aces ‘Master’ class  publisher=Variety  date=July 24, 2012  accessdate=October 21, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.lvol.com/gossip/2012/lvgossip305.html  title=Claire Voyant - Past Column from July 06, 2012  publisher=Lvol.com  date=  accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref> Her grandmother was Romanians Romanian.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jimmy-mcnamara/from-curb-to-partners-jil_b_1859850.html title=From Curb to Partners: Jillian Bell - Jimmy McNamara date=September 6, 2012 work=The Huffington Post accessdate=December 22, 2015}}</ref> Bell began studying Improvisational theatre improv at the age of eight.<ref name=huffpo>{{cite web url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jimmy-mcnamara/from-curb-to-partners-jil_b_1859850.html  title=Jimmy McNamara: From Curb to Partners: Jillian Bell  publisher=Huffingtonpost.com  date=September 6, 2012  accessdate=October 21, 2013}}</ref> She graduated from Bishop Gorman High School in 2002.<ref>{{cite journal url=http://issuu.com/bishopgorman/docs/gazette_summer_2012 title=Seven Questions With Jillian Bell 02 work=Bishop Gorman High School Alumni Newsletter date=Summer 2012 page=12 accessdate=February 10, 2014}}</ref> After high school, she moved to Los Angeles, California and became a member of the Groundlings.<ref name=variety/> 

==Career==
An alum of The Straitjacket Society in Los Angeles, CA, Jillian auditioned for Saturday Night Live, and though she did not join the cast, she became a writer for the show in 2009 for the Saturday Night Live (season 35) thirty-fifth season.<ref name=variety/> The same year Bell appeared in an episode of Curb Your Enthusiasm as an assistant who wears revealing clothing at work.<ref name=huffpo/>

In 2011, she began to play "Jillian Belk" on the Comedy Central series Workaholics. Film director Paul Thomas Anderson noticed her in Workaholics and cast her in a small role in his 2012 film, The Master (2012 film) The Master and yet again in another small role in his 2014 film, Inherent Vice (film) Inherent Vice.<ref name=variety/> She had a recurring role as Dixie in the fourth and final season of Eastbound and Down. In 2014, she appeared in 22 Jump Street alongside Jonah Hill and Channing Tatum.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thewrap.com/workaholics-star-jillian-bell-joins-jonah-hill-channing-tatum-in-22-jump-street-exclusive/  title=Workaholics Star Jillian Bell Joins Jonah Hill, Channing Tatum in 22 Jump Street (Exclusive)  publisher=TheWrap  date=September 12, 2013  accessdate=February 10, 2014}}</ref> In 2015, she co-starred in Goosebumps (film) Goosebumps and The Night Before (2015 film) The Night Before.

In January 2016, Comedy Central debuted a new series, Idiotsitter, in which Bell portrays the constantly misbehaving daughter of wealthy parents; her character, Gene Russell, is serving time under house arrest, and is being mentored by Billie Brown (Charlotte Newhouse), who is hired by Genes parents. Idiotsitter was launched as a web series in early 2014, before being picked up in June 2014 by Comedy Central for network airing.<ref>{{cite web last1=Miller first1=Liz Shannon title=If Comedy Centrals Idiotsitter is Half as Funny As Talking to Its Creators, It Could Be the Funniest Show on TV url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/comedy-central-idiotsitter-creators-jillian-bell-charlotte-newhouse-interview-it-could-be-the-funniest-show-on-tv-20160114 website=Indiewire accessdate=March 7, 2016 date=January 14, 2016}}</ref>
