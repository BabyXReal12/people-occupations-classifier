Aisling Bea ({{IPAc-en ˈ æ ʃ l ɪ ŋ}}; born Aisling Cliodhnadh OSullivan, 16 March 1984) is an Irish actress, comedian and writer.

==Early life==
Bea was born and raised in rural County Kildare.<ref name=ideastap>{{cite web url=http://www.ideastap.com/ideasmag/all-articles/Aisling-Bea-five-essentials title=Five Essentials: Actor accessdate=27 July 2012}}</ref><ref name=OfficialSite1/> Her father, Brian, was an equine Veterinary physician vet who died suddenly when she was three years old.<ref name="tourists">{{cite news title=In another life: Aisling Bea url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/my-job-was-to-teach-tourists-all-about-horse-sex-xdgdcqg33 accessdate=12 April 2018}}</ref> Bea and her younger sister, Sinéad,<ref name=Independent-Fringe-2015>{{cite news last1=Jones first1=Alice title=Aisling Bea interview: The prize-winning comedian with the CV of a veteran on bringing her second stand-up show to the Fringe url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/edinburgh-festival/aisling-bea-interview-the-prizewinning-comedian-on-growing-up-on-a-stud-farm-juggling-three-careers-and-making-the-leap-into-standup-10452549.html accessdate=18 August 2015 work=The Independent date=12 August 2015}}</ref> were raised by their mother, Helen (née Moloney), a secondary school teacher, jockey trainer<ref>http://www.edfestmag.com/20120815aislingbea/</ref> and former professional jockey.<ref>{{cite news title=Insider Interrogation: writer and actor Aisling Bea - Independent.ie url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/television/insider-interrogation-writer-and-actor-aisling-bea-30718799.html accessdate=12 April 2018 work=Independent.ie language=en}}</ref><ref name=IrishExaminer-Trollied-2014>{{cite news last1=Fitzpatrick first1=Richard title=Funny woman, Aisling Bea, is of good stock url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/artsfilmtv/funny-woman-aisling-bea-is-of-good-stock-275801.html accessdate=18 August 2015 work=Irish Examiner date=18 July 2014}}</ref> Her family were "obsessed" with horses and race meetings and, during her teenage years, Bea worked as a tour guide at the Irish National Stud.<ref name="tourists"/> However, she knew from a young age that she was not interested in the racing industry "as a career" and instead enjoyed performing.<ref name="tourists"/> 

Her grandfather was the Irish language novelist and poet Mícheál Ó Súilleabháin (writer) Mícheál Ó Súilleabháin; her great-aunt was the playwright Siobhán Ní Shúilleabháin.<ref>{{cite news title=From horses to hilarity url=http://www.advertiser.ie/galway/article/64322/from-horses-to-hilarity accessdate=11 April 2018 work=Galway Advertiser}}</ref> Musician Liam OFlynn was a family friend.<ref>{{cite tweet user=WeeMissBea title=Aisling Bea number=973958399791542273 date=15 Mar 2018}}</ref>

When Bea was thirteen, her mother revealed that her father had died by suicide.<ref name="The Guardian">{{cite web title=My fathers death has given me a love of men, of their vulnerability and tenderness url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2017/nov/04/aisling-bea-my-fathers-death-has-given-me-a-love-of-men-of-their-vulnerability-and-tenderness}}</ref> She adopted the stage name ‘Bea’ as a tribute to her father<ref name="Fitzpatrick">{{cite web last1=Fitzpatrick first1=Richard title=Funny woman, Aisling Bea, is of good stock url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/artsfilmtv/funny-woman-aisling-bea-is-of-good-stock-275801.html website=Irish Examiner accessdate=15 April 2018 date=18 July 2014}}</ref><ref name=Independent-Fringe-2015/> and wrote an article for The Guardian in 2017 about her familys experience of suicide.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://amp.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2017/nov/04/aisling-bea-my-fathers-death-has-given-me-a-love-of-men-of-their-vulnerability-and-tenderness?CMP=Share_iOSApp_Other&__twitter_impression=true title=Aisling Bea: ‘My father’s death has given me a love of men, of their vulnerability and tenderness’ date=2017-11-04 work=the Guardian accessdate=5 January 2018}}</ref>

Bea was educated at a Catholic convent school<ref name="3careers"/> and then studied French and philosophy at Trinity College Dublin.<ref name=OfficialSite1>{{cite web title=Aisling Bea Official Site  url=http://www.aislingbea.com/about.php  publisher=  website=AislingBea.com  accessdate=15 January 2014  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140116134632/http://www.aislingbea.com/about.php  archivedate=16 January 2014 }}</ref><ref name="IrishmanAbroad">{{cite podcast authorlink=Jarlath Regan  author=Jarlath Regan edition=105 work=An Irishman Abroad title=Aisling Bea publisher=SoundCloud date=19 September 2015 url=https://soundcloud.com/an-irishman-abroad/aisling-bea-episode-105 accessdate=22 September 2015}}</ref><ref name="3careers">{{cite news title=Prize-winning comedian Aisling Bea on making the leap into stand-up url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/edinburgh-festival/aisling-bea-interview-the-prize-winning-comedian-on-growing-up-on-a-stud-farm-juggling-three-careers-10452549.html accessdate=11 April 2018 work=The Independent date=12 August 2015}}</ref> She was part of a student sketch comedy group<ref>{{cite web title=Interviews - Development & Alumni url=https://www.tcd.ie/alumni/alumni/interviews/aisling-bea.php accessdate=15 April 2018}}</ref> and moved to London after graduation to study acting. In 2008, she completed a two-year course at the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art (LAMDA).<ref>{{cite news title=The buzz about funny girl Aisling Bea url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/you/article-5229381/The-buzz-funny-girl-Aisling-Bea.html accessdate=12 April 2018 work=Mail Online}}</ref>

==Career==
Image:MK17450 Aisling Bea.jpg thumb right Bea on stage
After drama school, Bea spent two years trying to get work in theatre as a dramatic actress.<ref>https://soundcloud.com/uclsound/humour-me-aisling-bea</ref> Instead, she found she was cast mainly in comedic television series including Cardinal Burns and Dead Boss (both 2012).<ref name="Gannon">{{cite web last1=Gannon first1=Louise title=The buzz about funny girl Aisling Bea url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/you/article-5229381/The-buzz-funny-girl-Aisling-Bea.html website=Mail Online accessdate=15 April 2018}}</ref><ref name="Jones">{{cite web last1=Jones first1=Alice title=Prize-winning comedian Aisling Bea on making the leap into stand-up url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/edinburgh-festival/aisling-bea-interview-the-prize-winning-comedian-on-growing-up-on-a-stud-farm-juggling-three-careers-10452549.html website=The Independent accessdate=15 April 2018 date=12 August 2015}}</ref> While filming Dead Boss in 2011, Bea decided to try stand-up comedy.<ref name="3careers"/><ref name="Biography">{{cite web title=Biography.... WHO IS THIS BROAD? url=http://www.aislingbea.com/biography/ website=www.aislingbea.com accessdate=15 April 2018 language=en}}</ref> In 2012, she won the The Gilded Balloon Gilded Balloon So You Think Youre Funny award at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe<ref name=BBC1>{{cite news url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-19366992 title=Aisling Bea wins So You Think Youre Funny accessdate=24 August 2012 publisher= work=BBC News date=24 August 2012}}</ref> and, in 2013, was nominated for Best Newcomer at the Edinburgh Comedy Awards for her show Cest La Bea.<ref>{{cite web last=Duffy first=Claire title=Aisling Bea is nominated for Edinburgh Fringe Best Newcomer Award url=http://entertainment.ie/comedy/feature/Aisling-Bea-is-nominated-for-Edinburgh-Fringe-Best-Newcomer-Award/209/4708.htm publisher=Entertainment.ie accessdate=15 January 2014}}</ref>

The exposure brought by these awards marked a "turning point" in Beas career<ref name="theguardian.com">{{cite news last1=Parkinson first1=Hannah Jane title=Aisling Bea: ‘I am making no money in LA, but creatively it is rewarding’ url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2016/sep/18/aisling-bea-interview-the-fall-jamie-dornan accessdate=15 April 2018 work=the Guardian date=18 September 2016 language=en}}</ref> and she began to appear as a regular guest on panel shows including QI, Insert Name Here and The Big Fat Quiz of the Year.<ref>{{cite news title=Interview: Rarely Asked Questions – Aisling Bea url=https://www.beyondthejoke.co.uk/content/1613/interview-rarely-asked-questions-aisling-bea accessdate=12 April 2018 work=Beyond The Joke date=19 April 2015 language=en}}</ref> Bea and Yasmine Akram co-wrote and co-hosted the BBC Radio 4 comedy folkflore series Micks and Legends (2012, 2015);<ref>{{cite news title=Comedy Irish Micks and Legends back on BBC Radio 4 url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2015/0121/674618-irish-micks-and-legends/ accessdate=15 April 2018 work=RTE.ie date=21 January 2015 language=en}}</ref> it was nominated for a Chortle Awards Chortle Award in 2013.<ref>http://www.radiolistings.co.uk/programmes/i/ir/irish_micks_and_legends.html</ref> Bea won the 2014 British Comedy Awards British Comedy Award for Best Female TV Comic and returned to Edinburgh in 2015 with the live show Plan Bea. In 2016, she became a team captain on 8 Out of 10 Cats and was a cast member on Taskmaster (TV series) Taskmaster in 2017.

Bea has continued to act in television sitcoms including Trollied (2014-2015) and The Delivery Man (TV series) The Delivery Man (2015), and the crime dramas The Fall (TV series) The Fall (2016) and Hard Sun (2018). In 2018, she and Sara Pascoe began to co-host the BBC Radio 2 comedy chat show Whats Normal?.<ref>https://www.comedy.co.uk/radio/news/3037/sara_and_aisling_whats_normal/</ref> She has recorded a 15 minute stand-up special to be aired on Netflix in late 2018.

== Personal life ==
Bea is a vocal supporter of the Thirty-sixth Amendment of the Constitution Bill 2018 (Ireland) Repeal the 8th campaign to introduce legal abortion in Ireland,<ref name="theguardian.com"/><ref>{{cite news title=Standup for Choice - Repeal Eight url=https://www.repealeight.ie/event/standup-for-choice/ accessdate=15 April 2018 work=Repeal Eight}}</ref> and contributed an essay to Una Mullallys Repeal the 8th in 2018.<ref>{{cite news title=Autonomy edited by Kathy D’Arcy, Repeal the 8th edited by Una Mullally review url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/books/autonomy-edited-by-kathy-d-arcy-repeal-the-8th-edited-by-una-mullally-review-1.3444320 accessdate=15 April 2018 work=The Irish Times}}</ref> She previously campaigned for Same-sex marriage in the Republic of Ireland same-sex marriage legislation in the successful 2015 Irish referendum.<ref name="theguardian.com"/>
