Michael Patrick "Mike" Lawrence is an American comedian and writer from Davie, Florida.  He currently lives in New York City.<ref name="We Love DC - Simple Coverage" />

==Early life==
Lawrence was born in Miami but grew up in Davie, Florida.  He attended Western High School (Davie, Florida) Western High School and earned a bachelors degree from Florida Atlantic University in English.

When he was 16 years old, he began working at a McDonalds in southern Florida.  He attributes his ability to deal with hecklers to his time spent at McDonalds working the drive-thru.  In his podcast interview on WTF with Marc Maron, he stated, "Heckling doesnt bother me because Ive been yelled at for McNuggets."<ref name="Good Morning America - McDonalds Info">{{cite news last=Kim first=Sussana title=Working at a McDonalds Drive-thru Gave Comedian Confidence url=http://abcnews.go.com/Business/york-comedian-mike-lawrence-practiced-telling-jokes-mcdonalds/story?id=16212218 accessdate=October 3, 2013 newspaper=Good Morning America date=April 26, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite podcast  url=http://www.wtfpod.com/podcast/episodes/episode_241_-_mike_lawrence  title=WTF with Marc Maron   publisher=Earwolf   host=Marc Maron   date=December 6, 2012   accessdate=October 17, 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
Lawrence started his career at the age of 15 by performing poetry at open mic nights in 1998.  After realizing that the part he liked most about it was making people laugh, he moved to comedy.<ref>{{cite web last1=Irvin first1=Zuri title=Writing Jokes with Mike Lawrence url=https://medium.com/@withzuri/writing-jokes-with-mike-lawrence-30d4cae07514#.oicqzwok0 website=Medium publisher=Medium.com accessdate=20 Dec 2015}}</ref><ref name="We Love DC - Simple Coverage" />

In 2010, Lawrence was named one of the New Faces at the largest comedy festival in the world, Just for Laughs.<ref name="LaughSpin - Just For Laughs New Faces">{{cite news last=Gadino first=Dylan P. title=Just For Laughs: Feimster, Lawrence, Jost impress at New Faces url=http://www.laughspin.com/2010/07/16/just-for-laughs-feimster-lawrence-jost-impress-at-new-faces/ accessdate=October 3, 2013 newspaper=LaughSpin date=July 16, 2010 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130426110556/http://www.laughspin.com/2010/07/16/just-for-laughs-feimster-lawrence-jost-impress-at-new-faces/ archivedate=April 26, 2013 df=mdy-all}}</ref> He later performed at the festival in 2012 at both the Montreal and Chicago festivals.<ref name="LaughSpin - New Faces">{{cite news title=Nikki Glaser, Brody Stevens, Jonah Ray and 14 more comedians to film half-hour specials for Comedy Central url=http://www.laughspin.com/2013/02/14/nikki-glaser-brody-stevens-jonah-ray-and-14-more-comedians-to-film-half-hour-specials-for-comedy-central/ accessdate=October 3, 2013 newspaper=LaughSpin date=February 14, 2013}}</ref> While in Montreal, he recorded a set for HBO Canadas Funny as Hell.

In 2011, Lawrence performed at Lance Bangs Come Laugh With Us at the Birdgetown Comedy Festival with fellow features Kristen Schaal, Fred Armisen, Jon Daly (comedian) Jon Daly, and Brett Gelman.<ref name="LaughSpin - Bridgetown Comedy Festival">{{cite news last=Kate first=Meagan title=Bridgetown Comedy Festival: Day 2 url=http://www.laughspin.com/2011/04/23/bridgetown-comedy-festival-day-2/ accessdate=October 3, 2013 newspaper=LaughSpin date=April 23, 2011}}</ref> Mike also performed on John Oliver (comedian) John Olivers John Olivers New York Stand-Up Show New York Stand-Up Show with fellow features Hannibal Buress, Michael Ian Black, and Hari Kondabolu.<ref name="LaughSpin - John Olivers Show">{{cite news last=Gardner first=Lucas title=John Olivers New York Stand-Up Show is back July 20, so lets watch a preview! url=http://www.laughspin.com/2012/07/06/john-olivers-new-york-stand-up-show-is-back-july-20-so-lets-watch-a-preview/ accessdate=October 3, 2013 newspaper=LaughSpin date=July 6, 2012}}</ref> He regularly opened for Oliver soon after.

In 2012, Lawrence also made his first appearance on Conan OBriens TV show, Conan (talk show) Conan.

===Podcasts===
Lawrence hosts the Nerd of Mouth podcast with Jake Young on the New York City-based comedy podcast network, Cave Comedy Radio.  Episodes are published weekly.  Topics discussed on the podcast include comic books, video games, TV shows, and movies; things that Lawrence enjoys.<ref name="Nerd of Mouth Description">{{cite web title=Nerd of Mouth url=http://cavecomedyradio.com/nerdofmouth publisher=Cave Comedy Radio accessdate=October 3, 2013 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130818190745/http://cavecomedyradio.com/nerdofmouth archivedate=August 18, 2013 df=mdy-all}}</ref> Lawrence and the podcast were featured on an episode of Toy Hunter in the episode called Toy Hunter#Season 2 (2013) Nerd of Mouth, in 2013.<ref name="TV.com - Toy Hunter Listing">{{cite web title=Toy Hunter, Season 2, Episode 8 url=http://www.tv.com/shows/toy-hunter/nerd-of-mouth-2848283/ publisher=TV.com accessdate=October 3, 2013}}</ref><ref name="Marvel Interview">{{cite news last=Garris first=Blake title=The Marvel Life: Mike Lawrence url=http://marvel.com/news/story/20668/the_marvel_life_mike_lawrence accessdate=October 8, 2013 newspaper=Marvel Comics date=May 23, 2013}}</ref>

Lawrence has also been a guest on many notable podcasts, including You Know What Dude! with comedian Robert Kelly (comedian) Robert Kelly, You Made It Weird with Pete Holmes You Made It Weird with comedian Pete Holmes, WTF with Marc Maron WTF podcast with comedian Marc Maron, The Adam Carolla Show (podcast) The Adam Carolla Show with Adam Carolla, Keith and The Girl with Keith Malley, The K Ohle with Kurt Braunohler The K Ohle Show with Kurt Braunohler, Earwolf#Hollywood Handbook Hollywood Handbook with Sean Clements and Hayes Davenport, The Artie Lange Show hosted by comedian Artie Lange, "What Say You?" with Brian "Q" Quinn and Sal Vulcano of Impractical Jokers, and "What Do You Know?" with Joel Heyman of Rooster Teeth.
