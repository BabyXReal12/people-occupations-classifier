 
Michael "Mike" Henry (born November 7, 1965) is an American voice actor, writer, producer, and comedian. He is known for his work on the animated TV series Family Guy (1999–present), where he writes and produces episodes along with voicing Cleveland Brown, Herbert (Family Guy) Herbert, List of Family Guy characters#Bruce Bruce, and List of Family Guy characters#Consuela Consuela. Henry is also known for co-creating and starring in the spin-off, The Cleveland Show (2009–2013) and for his recurring role as Dann in the television series, The Orville.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.campustimes.org/2008/09/11/mike-henry-of-family-guy-talks-voices-gags-and-instinct/ title=Mike Henry of "Family Guy" talks voices, gags and instinct - Campus Times date=2008-09-11 work=Campus Times access-date=2018-05-01 language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation title=The Cleveland Show url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1195935/ others=Mike Henry, Sanaa Lathan, Kevin Michael Richardson accessdate=2018-05-01}}</ref><ref>{{Citation title=Family Guy url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0182576/ others=Seth MacFarlane, Alex Borstein, Seth Green accessdate=2018-05-01}}</ref><ref>{{Citation title="The Orville" Cupids Dagger (TV Episode 2017) url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6850956/fullcredits/ accessdate=2018-05-10}}</ref>

==Early life==
Henry and his younger brother Patrick were born to artist parents and raised in Richmond, Virginia. His parents divorced when he was six years old and he was primarily raised by his mother. Although money was tight, he was awarded a scholarship and attended the nearby preparatory Collegiate School (Richmond, Virginia) Collegiate School.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/08/30/arts/television/30itzk.html title=With The Cleveland Show, Mike Henry Spins Off Into Uncharted Cartoon Territory last=Itzkoff first=Dave date=2009-08-27 work=The New York Times access-date=2018-05-01 language=en-US issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Henry graduated from Washington and Lee University (1988) where he earned his B.A. in history and served as class president his sophomore-senior years. Although interested in comedy, Mike never believed he could make a career of it so instead opted for more “business stuff.”<ref>{{Cite news url=http://panthernow.com/2014/10/24/family-guy-voice-actor-visits-university/ title=Family Guy voice actor visits University - PantherNOW date=2014-10-24 work=PantherNOW access-date=2018-05-01 language=en-US}}</ref>

At the age of 24, after a brief career in advertising, Henry moved to California to pursue acting. There he began taking classes at the [http://www.groundlings.com/ Groundlings Theater] and performing stand-up comedy.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news url=https://www.backstage.com/interview/playing-in-cleveland/ title=Playing in Cleveland work=Backstage.com access-date=2018-05-02}}</ref> After three years in LA, Mike returned to Virginia to shoot short comedy films that he wrote and acted in.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.styleweekly.com/richmond/welcome-back-cleveland/Content?oid=1377329 title=Welcome Back, Cleveland website=Style Weekly language=en access-date=2018-05-02}}</ref> During that time, Mike often acted in his brother Patrick’s student films at the Rhode Island School of Design. It was there that he was introduced to his brother’s college roommate, Seth MacFarlane.<ref name=":1" /> The two quickly became friends. "We immediately hit it off and cracked each other up and kept in touch," Henry says.<ref name=":0" />

Henry later moved to New York City where he acted in commercials, studied improvisation at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre Upright Citizens Brigade Theater<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.metacritic.com/person/mike-henry-vi?filter-options=tv title=Mike Henry (VI) website=Metacritic access-date=2018-05-02}}</ref> and wrote, produced, starred-in and co-directed (with his brother Patrick) comedy shorts for Lorne Michaels Lorne Michaels’ Burly Bear Network.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.styleweekly.com/richmond/voice-of-the-people/Content?oid=1367039 title=Voice of the People last=Burruss first=Mary website=Style Weekly language=en access-date=2018-05-10}}</ref>

==Career==

===Family Guy===
Henry’s break came in 1998, when MacFarlane contacted him about being part of a new show called Family Guy. Mike agreed and joined the project as a writer and voice actor. Henry has stated that the inspiration for Clevelands voice was based on “this guy that [he] had once played basketball with.”<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tRBOx3f_ZRs title=Mike Henry: The Origins of Cleveland and Herbert accessdate=2009-04-16}}</ref> During the shows first four seasons, he was credited as a guest star, but beginning with Family Guy (season 5) season fives "Prick Up Your Ears (Family Guy) Prick Up Your Ears" he has been credited as a main cast member.<ref name="Henry">{{cite news title=Mike Henry of "Family Guy" talks voices, gags and instinct  date=September 11, 2008  work=Campus Times  url=http://www.campustimes.org/mike-henry-of-family-guy-talks-voices-gags-and-instinct-1.743902  accessdate=September 8, 2009  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091016192410/http://www.campustimes.org/mike-henry-of-family-guy-talks-voices-gags-and-instinct-1.743902  archivedate=October 16, 2009 }}</ref> Since its debut on January 31, 1999, List of Family Guy episodes 306 episodes of Family Guy have been broadcast with Mike having credits in nearly all of them.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1396611/ title=Mike Henry website=IMDb access-date=2018-05-02}}</ref> 

Two episodes into the second season, Family Guy was taken off the networks permanent schedule and shown irregularly thereafter. The show returned in March 2000 to finish airing the second season which contained 21 episodes; all the cast came back for the series return. During its second season, Fox publicly announced that the show had been cancelled.<ref>{{cite news url=http://archive.boston.com/ae/tv/articles/2005/04/30/family_guy_returns_just_as_funny_as_ever/ title=Family Guy Returns, Just As Funny As Ever last=Gilbert first=Matthew date=April 30, 2005 work=Boston.com accessdate=August 24, 2009}}</ref> Despite the announced cancellation, in 2003 Fox decided to make the third season.<ref>{{cite news url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/television/news/2003-11-18-family-guy_x.htm title=Family Guy may return last1=Levin first1=Gary date=November 18, 2003 work=USAtoday.com accessdate=September 27, 2009}}</ref>  During the third season, Fox announced that the show was canceled for good.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://familyguy.tktv.net/news.html  title=Family Guy has finally been officially canceled by Fox  work=TKtv  date=May 16, 2002  accessdate=August 24, 2009}}</ref><ref name="NYT McKinley">{{cite news  url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9A03E3D61E31F931A35756C0A9639C8B63  title=Canceled and Resurrected, on the Air and Onstage  work=The New York Times  first=Jesse  last=McKinley  date=May 2, 2005  accessdate=August 24, 2009}}</ref> Soon after Family Guy was cancelled, Henry and his brother created the popular web series Kicked in the Nuts, a spoof of hidden camera shows. Family Guy was renewed again in 2005 for its fourth season due to strong DVD sales and its Broadcast syndication syndication on basic cable networks.<ref>{{cite news url=http://articles.latimes.com/2005/apr/13/business/fi-family13 title=Fox Reuniting Itself with Family Guy last=James first=Meg date=April 13, 2005 newspaper=Los Angeles Times accessdate=September 3, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news title=Yet another Family reunion  newspaper=Chicago Sun-Times  first=Phil  last=Rosenthal  page=53  date=November 20, 2003  accessdate=September 3, 2009}}</ref> Once again Henry and the rest of the cast came back for their voice works. In October 2017, Family Guy was renewed for its 16th season.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://heavy.com/entertainment/2017/05/family-guy-renewed-cancelled-will-return-season-16-2017-spoilers/ title=‘Family Guy’ Renewed or Cancelled: Is it Coming Back for Season 16? last=Levine first=Daniel S. date=2017-05-22 work=Heavy.com access-date=2018-05-02 language=en-US}}</ref>

===The Cleveland Show===
On September 27th 2009, The Cleveland Show premiered on Fox.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news.aspx?id=20090615fox01 title=FOX Announces Fall Premiere Dates For The 2009–2010 Season date=June 15, 2009 work=The Futon Critic accessdate=August 23, 2009}}</ref> The project was created and executive produced by Henry, Seth MacFarlane, and American Dad! show runner Rich Appel. The show focused on the Family Guy character Cleveland Brown. Cleveland referenced the spinoff at the end of the Family Guy episode, "Baby Not on Board".<ref name="s03e10">{{cite episode title=Baby Not On Board series=Family Guy network=Fox season=7 number=4 episodelink=Baby Not on Board serieslink=Family Guy airdate=November 2, 2008}}</ref> The first season consisted of 22 episodes and was picked up by Fox for a second 13 episode season. The announcement was made on May 3, 2009 before the first season even premiered.<ref>{{cite news url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cleveland-rocks-fox-83327 title=Cleveland rocks at Fox last= first= date= work= access-date= archiveurl=https://archive.is/20120703141634/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/h/content_display/news/e3id8ce138992a53121b4b8137dc51ea285 archivedate=2012-07-03 deadurl=yes df=}} </ref> Due to strong ratings, Fox picked up an additional nine season 2 episodes, bringing the total episode count of the show to 44. The show was renewed for a third and fourth season on May 9, 2011.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2010/06/10/fox-picks-up-third-season-of-the-cleveland-show/20100610fox01/ title=Breaking News - FOX Picks Up Third Season of "The Cleveland Show" {{!}} TheFutonCritic.com website=www.thefutoncritic.com access-date=2018-05-03}}</ref> The series was canceled after its fourth season on May 19, 2013<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/tv-movies/24-returns-fox-fall-article-1.1342450?pgno=1 title=Fox announces 2013-14 fall schedule, which includes return of Kiefer Sutherlands 24 - NY Daily News last=Hinckley first=David work=nydailynews.com access-date=2018-05-03 language=en-US}}</ref> but reruns continue to air on TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBS and on Cartoon Network Cartoon Networks Adult Swim in the United States and on MuchMusic in Canada. The series ran for a total of List of The Cleveland Show episodes 4 seasons and 88 episodes.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.foxflash.com/div.php/main/page?aID=1z2z2z252z1z2 title=Fox Primetime – The Cleveland Show – Fact Sheet work=Fox Flash archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100117131748/http://foxflash.com/div.php/main/page?aID=1z2z2z252z1z2 archivedate=January 17, 2010 deadurl=yes accessdate=August 29, 2009 df=}}</ref>
