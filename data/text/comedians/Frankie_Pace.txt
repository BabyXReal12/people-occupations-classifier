Pace began his career in a  Long Island comedy club called "The White House Inn" working alongside Eddie Murphy, Rob Bartlett, Jackie Martling, Bob Nelson (comedian) Bob Nelson, Bob Woods, Richie Minnervini, Rosie ODonnell, Jim Myers and Don McHenry. After a few years he went to New York City where Rick Newman saw him and passed him as a regular at Catch a Rising Star (comedy clubs) Catch a Rising Star.

Pace performed on the April 14, 1984 episode of Saturday Night Live which featured George McGovern as the host.<ref>{{cite web date=April 16, 2016 url=https://www.bendouwsma.com/blog/2016/8/13/classic-snl-review-april-14-1984-george-mcgovern-madness website=Bendouwsma.com title=Classic SNL Review: April 14, 1984: George McGovern / Madness}}</ref> Producer Cynthia Friedman had Pace write and host his own television show for Night Flight (TV series) Night Flight which appeared on the USA Network called "Rick Shaws Takeout Theater".<ref name=NF/>

Pace later performed for on The Joan Rivers Show and acted on The Cosby Show and The Sopranos.<ref name=NYT/> He also performed shows for Bill Boggs Comedy Tonight, Carolines on Broadway Carolines Comedy Hour and Comic Strip Live with John Mulrooney.

When the comedy club scene slowed down, Pace revamped his act and looked for work in the Catskill Mountains which later led to corporate shows, casino shows and cruise ship performances.<ref name=CJ/> Pace later worked for Freddie Romans "Catskills on Broadway".<ref name=CJ/>

In 1999 Pace appeared in the David Spade film Lost & Found (1999 film) Lost & Found.<ref>{{cite web publisher=Rotten Tomatoes url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/frankie_pace title=Frankie Pace}}</ref>
