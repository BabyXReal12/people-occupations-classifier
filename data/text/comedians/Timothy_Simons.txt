
Timothy Simons (born June 12, 1978) is an American actor and comedian best known for his role as Jonah Ryan on the HBO television series Veep,<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/30/arts/television/timothy-simons-of-veep-on-hbo-is-going-places.html  title=Hail to the Mischief Maker  date=March 30, 2014  work=The New York Times  accessdate=February 6, 2017}}</ref> for which he has received three nominations for the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.sagawards.org/awards/nominees-and-recipients/20th-annual-screen-actors-guild-awards  title=The 20th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards  work=sagawards.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.sagawards.org/awards/nominees-and-recipients/21st-annual-screen-actors-guild-awards  title=The 21st Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards  work=sagawards.org}}</ref> He has also had acting roles in the films The Interview, Christine (2016 film) Christine, and The Boss (2016 film) The Boss.

==Personal life==
He was born in Readfield, Maine, the son of Susan and Ron Simons, and was raised in the state.<ref name="auto"/> Simons is married to Annie Simons and they have twins born in December 2011.<ref name="auto">{{cite web  url=http://bangordailynews.com/2012/05/21/living/maine-native-tim-simons-finds-success-on-hbo-comedy-series-veep/  title=Maine native Tim Simons finds success on HBO comedy series ‘Veep’  first1=Emily  last1=Burnham  publisher=Bangor Daily News  accessdate=February 6, 2017}}</ref> He currently resides in Los Angeles.
