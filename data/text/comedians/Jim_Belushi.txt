James Adam Belushi ({{IPAc-en b ə ˈ l uː ʃ i}}; born June 15, 1954) is an Albanian-American actor, voice actor, comedian, singer and musician.<ref name=tvg>{{cite web  url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/jim-belushi/157017/ title= Jim Belushi  publisher= TV Guide TVGuide.com   accessdate= July 7, 2016}}</ref>

He is the younger brother of comic actor John Belushi. He is the father of actor Robert Belushi. He played the role of Jim on the sitcom According to Jim (2001–2009). His other television roles include Saturday Night Live (1983–1985), Wild Palms (1993), Aaahh!!! Real Monsters (1994–1997), Show Me a Hero (2015) and Twin Peaks (2017 TV series) Twin Peaks (2017).

Belushi has appeared in films such as Thief (film) Thief (1981), Trading Places (1983), About Last Night (1986 film) About Last Night (1986), Salvador (film) Salvador (1986), Red Heat (1988), K-9 (film) K-9 (1989), Mr. Destiny (1990), Curly Sue (1991), Jingle All the Way (1996), Underdog (film) Underdog (2007) and The Ghost Writer (film) The Ghost Writer (2010).

==Early life==
Belushi was born in Chicago,<ref name=tvg/> to Adam Anastos Belushi, an Albanians Albanian from the city of Korçë, in Albania,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.genealogy.com/ftm/m/a/h/Denny-Mahoney/WEBSITE-0001/UHP-0528.html title=Denny-Mahoney - User Trees - Genealogy.com website=www.genealogy.com accessdate=June 15, 2017}}</ref> and Agnes Demetri (Samaras) Belushi,<ref name="mother" /> who was the daughter of Albanians from Korçë.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.genealogy.com/ftm/m/a/h/Denny-Mahoney/WEBSITE-0001/UHP-1342.html title=Denny-Mahoney - User Trees - Genealogy.com website=www.genealogy.com accessdate=June 15, 2017}}</ref> He was raised in Wheaton, Illinois Wheaton, a Chicago suburb, along with his three siblings: older brother John Belushi John, older sister Marian, and younger brother Billy.<ref name="mother">{{cite web url=http://www.genealogy.com/ftm/m/a/h/Denny-Mahoney/WEBSITE-0001/UHP-0531.html title=Denny-Mahoney - User Trees - Genealogy.com publisher=}}</ref><ref name="snl" /><ref name="filmref bio" /><ref name="Broyard" /> After graduating from Wheaton Warrenville South High School Wheaton Central High School, Jim Belushi attended the College of DuPage and graduated from Southern Illinois University Carbondale with a degree in Speech and Theater Arts.

==Career==
File:Belushi.jpg thumb left Belushi at the 39th Venice International Film Festival in 1982
From 1977 to 1980, Belushi, like his older brother John Belushi, worked with the Chicago theater group The Second City. During this period, Belushi made his television debut in 1978-79 United States network television schedule 1978s Whos Watching the Kids and also had a small part in Brian De Palmas The Fury (1978 film) The Fury. His first significant role was in Michael Mann (film director) Michael Manns Thief (film) Thief (1981). After his elder brother Johns death, History of Saturday Night Live (1980-1985) from 1983 to 1985 he appeared on Saturday Night Live; he portrayed characters such as Hank Rippy from "Hello, Trudy!" and "That White Guy". Belushi also appeared in the film Trading Places as a drunk man in a gorilla suit during a New Years Eve party. He made a guest appearance in Faerie Tale Theatres third-season episode Pinocchio, starring Paul Reubens as the titular puppet.

Belushi rose to greater prominence with his supporting roles in The Man with One Red Shoe (1985), About Last Night..., Salvador (film) Salvador and Little Shop of Horrors (film) Little Shop of Horrors (as Patrick Martin) (all 1986), which opened up opportunities for lead roles. He has starred in films including Real Men (film) Real Men, The Principal, Red Heat, Homer and Eddie, K-9 (film) K-9, Dimenticare Palermo, Taking Care of Business (film) Taking Care of Business, Mr. Destiny, Only the Lonely (film) Only the Lonely, Curly Sue, Once Upon A Crime, Wild Palms, Royce, Race the Sun, Jingle All The Way, Separate Lives (1995 film) Separate Lives, Retroactive (film) Retroactive, Gang Related (film) Gang Related, Angels Dance and Joe Somebody (2001).

His voice work includes Mighty Ducks (TV series) The Mighty Ducks, The Pebble and the Penguin, Babes in Toyland (1997 film) Babes in Toy land, Gargoyles (TV series) Gargoyles and Hey Arnold!, and the more recent Hoodwinked, Scooby-Doo! and the Goblin King and The Wild. He also lent his vocal talents for 9: The Last Resort (a personal computer PC game released in 1995), in which he portrayed "Salty", a coarse yet helpful character. In 1997, he portrayed the "Masked Mutant" in the Goosebumps PC video game, alongside Adam West as "The Galloping Gazelle". On January 4, 2001, Belushi appeared on the ER (TV series) ER episode "ER (season 7) Piece of Mind". The episode focused on both Dr. Mark Greenes life-or-death brain surgery in New York and Belushis character, who had been in a car accident with his son in Chicago. Belushis performance contributed to his re-emergence in the public eye, and the following year he was cast as the title role in ABCs According to Jim.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.biography.com/people/james-belushi-20851499#recent-years  title=Jim Belushi stars as suburban father in According to Jim  last=  first=  date=  website=Biography.com  access-date=August 23, 2016}}</ref> His first animation voice-over was as a pimple on Krums head in Aaahh!!! Real Monsters on Nickelodeon. That performance led him to be cast in the continuing role as Simon the Monster Hunter in that series, where he ad-libbed much of his own dialogue.

==Personal life==
Belushi has been married three times and has three children. He married Sandra Davenport on May 17, 1980, and the couple had a son, Robert Belushi Robert James (also an actor, born October 23, 1980), they divorced in 1988.<ref>https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/jim-belushis-wife-files-for-divorce/</ref> Belushi was married to actress Marjorie Bransfield from 1990 to 1992.<ref>https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/jim-belushis-wife-files-for-divorce/</ref> He has been married to Jennifer Sloan since May 2, 1998, the couple have a daughter, Jamison Bess (born July 28, 1999) and a son, Jared James (born February 8, 2002).<ref>https://www.people.com/tv/jim-belushi-wife-jennifer-sloan-files-divorce/amp/</ref> On March 5, 2018 Jennifer Sloan filed for divorce.<ref>https://www.people.com/tv/jim-belushi-wife-jennifer-sloan-files-divorce/amp/</ref> Belushi is closely linked to his Albanian heritage and received honorary Albanian citizenship from the President of Albania, Bamir Topi.<ref name="Xhejms" /> Belushi is an avid fan of the Chicago Blackhawks, Chicago Bears, Chicago Cubs, Chicago Bulls and the Chicago Fire Soccer Club.<ref>{{cite web title=Jim Belushi loves his teams—and his haters work=RedEye Chicago url=http://articles.redeyechicago.com/2013-09-25/sports/42400371_1_bears-da-coach-movie  accessdate=September 25, 2013}}</ref>
