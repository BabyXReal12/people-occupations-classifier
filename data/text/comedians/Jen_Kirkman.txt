Jennifer Ann Kirkman<ref>[http://www.earwolf.com/episode/jen-kirkman/ Jen Kirkman, episode #6 of Ronna & Beverly on Earwolf]. Earwolf.com (August 6, 2011). Retrieved on 2015-12-08.</ref> (born August 28, 1974) is an American stand-up comedian, screenwriter, podcaster, and actress. She is known for her regular appearances as a round-table panelist on Chelsea Lately and as a guest on @midnight, as well as for her appearances on the Funny or Die sketch series Drunk History, and its continuation television series on Comedy Central.

She has released three comedy albums, Self Help (2006), Hail to the Freaks (2011) and Im Gonna Die Alone (And I Feel Fine) (2016), the latter also serving as her debut stand-up feature for Netflix. Her second stand-up feature, Just Keep Livin?, premiered in January 2017. Her debut book, I Can Barely Take Care of Myself: Tales From a Happy Life Without Kids, was published in April 2013, and became a New York Times bestseller.

==Early life==
Kirkman was born in Needham, Massachusetts on August 28, 1974,<ref>{{cite news last=Anderman first=Joan title=A funny thing happened to Jen Kirkman url=http://www.boston.com/ae/theater_arts/comedy/articles/2010/06/11/comedian_jen_kirkman_sees_star_rise_as_both_writer_and_performer/ work=Boston Globe article accessdate=June 25, 2011 date=June 11, 2010}}</ref> and is the youngest of three sisters. She is of German, Polish, French-Canadian and English heritage.<ref>[http://www.nerdist.com/2013/03/you-made-it-weird-132-jen-kirkman/ You Made It Weird #132: Jen Kirkman]. Nerdist. Retrieved on December 8, 2015.</ref>

Kirkman majored in acting at Emerson College in Boston.<ref>{{cite news last=Roe first=Michelle title=Jen Kirkman Says Shes Happy Being 40, Single...Really! url=http://www.palmspringslife.com/Palm-Springs-Life/Desert-Guide/June-2014/Jen-Kirkman-Says-Shes-Happy-Being-40-Single-...-Really!/ work=Palm Springs Life article accessdate=July 8, 2014 date=June 24, 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
===Stand-up comedy===
Kirkman performs stand-up comedy in Los Angeles, California, appearing regularly at the Hollywood Improv, The Laugh Factory, Largo (nightclub) Largo and The Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre. She has performed with comedians Maria Bamford, Greg Behrendt, Mike Birbiglia and Chelsea Handler. In 2009 and 2010, she performed as part of the Comedians of Chelsea Lately. In 2009, Entertainment Weekly named her one of the "Top 12 Rising Stars in Comedy,"<ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20239791_6,00.html   title = 12 Rising Stars of Comedy   last1 = Pastorek   first1 = Whitney   last2 = Snierson   first2 = Dan    date = November 13, 2008   publisher = Entertainment Weekly   accessdate = September 28, 2010}}</ref> and in May 2010, both MSN.com MSN and The Huffington Post named her one of the top comedians to follow on social networking service Twitter.<ref>{{cite web   url = http://specials.msn.com/A-List/Funny-people-on-Twitter.aspx?cp-documentid=24270297&imageindex=4   title = 10 funny folks to follow on Twitter   publisher = MSN.com   accessdate = September 28, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2009/08/25/50-funny-people-you-shoul_n_267417.html   title = 50 Funny People You Should Be Following On Twitter   date = April 9, 2010   publisher = The Huffington Post   accessdate = September 28, 2010}}</ref>

===Television===
Kirkman was a narrator on two episodes of the Funny or Die-produced series Drunk History on HBO, which received the jury prize for "Best Short Film" at the 2010 Sundance Film Festival. Though it is often questioned, she was actually drunk for both episodes.<ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.serialoptimist.com/interviews/jen-kirkman-comedian-writer-actress-my-fantasy-best-friend-384.html   title =Serial Optimist   date = August 19, 2010}}</ref> She has made guest appearances on several United States American television talk shows, including Conan (talk show) Conan, The View (U.S. TV series) The View, and The Late Show with Stephen Colbert, and has performed on The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, Late Friday, Premium Blend, @midnight and Special Sauce. In 2007, she appeared as a cast member on the short-lived sketch comedy show Acceptable.TV on VH1. She has also made regular voice-over appearances on the Adult Swim series Home Movies (TV Series) Home Movies and the political satire animated series SuperNews! on Current TV. In 2009, she wrote two episodes of the Disney Channel animated series Phineas and Ferb.

===Comedy writing===
Kirkmans first book, I Can Barely Take Care of Myself: Tales From a Happy Life Without Kids, was published in hardcover on April 16, 2013, and was listed on the New York Times New York Times Best Seller list Non-Fiction Best Seller list. In late 2013, she signed her second book deal with Simon & Schuster, a follow-up to her best-selling debut.<ref name="deadline-series" /> Her second book, I Know What Im Doing and Other Lies I Tell Myself: Dispatches from a Life Under Construction, was published in April 2016. She also has several published essays, which have been included in Rejected: Tales of the Failed, Dumped & Canceled and Bad Sex: We Did It So You Don’t Have To.

In 2001, Kirkman founded Girlcomic.net with fellow comedian Becky Donohue. The website featured interviews with comedians such as Joy Behar, Margaret Cho, Sue Murphy, Amy Poehler and Sarah Silverman. On the intention of the website, Kirkman stated, "[that the] one thing about feeling like there are still threads of sexism is women can get discouraged and we wanted [to encourage] girls to write funny stories and we wanted to interview famous female comics to let their stories get out. It was meant to be positive and cool and we included men on the site too."<ref name="dead-frog" /> Regular updates to the website ceased in 2003, and the domain was ultimately closed in 2006.

==Influences==
Kirkman has cited Joan Rivers, Howard Stern, Roseanne Barr, Dave Chappelle, Chris Rock and David Spade as having an influence on her sense of humor.<ref name="dead-frog">{{cite web   url = http://www.dead-frog.com/blog/entry/interview_jen_kirkman_stand_up_comedian/   title = Interview: Jen Kirkman, Stand-Up Comedian   last = Jackson   first = Todd   date = March 19, 2007   publisher = Dead-Frog   accessdate = February 4, 2011}}</ref><ref name="bed-joan">{{cite podcast url=http://inbedwithjoan.com/2013/12/episode-40-jen-kirkman  title=In Bed With Joan  publisher=CCF Productions  host=Joan Rivers  date=December 18, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news url=http://the828.com/2017/10/18/jenkirkman/ title=Comedian Jen Kirkman Prepares For The Peel last= first= date=2017-10-18 work=The 828 access-date=2017-10-19 archive-url= archive-date= dead-url= language=en-US}}</ref>

As an eight-year-old, she would sneak over to a friends house to watch Sam Kinison and George Carlin comedy specials on HBO. “[Kinison] was a big thing for me,” she said. “Someone is yelling, I’m not in trouble, and it’s funny.”<ref name="bed-joan" /> She has cited Morrissey as perhaps her greatest influence, and although she is "nothing like him," his ability "to say morbid things with a sense of humor is now in my DNA."<ref>{{cite web  url= http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/comedy/legendary-comedians-inspired-todays-humourists-20150318-142nnt.html   title = Legendary comedians inspired todays humourists   date = March 22, 2015   publisher = Sydney Morning Herald}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
On August 21, 2009, Kirkman married writer/director Neil Mahoney in a private ceremony at the Wayside Inn in Sudbury, Massachusetts.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.boston.com/ae/theater_arts/comedy/articles/2010/06/11/comedian_jen_kirkman_sees_star_rise_as_both_writer_and_performer/ title=A funny thing happened to Jen Kirkman last=Anderman first=Joan date=June 11, 2010 accessdate=September 28, 2010 publisher=The Boston Globe}}</ref> On the August 25, 2011 episode of Chelsea Lately, she revealed she was no longer married. On the January 14, 2012 episode of the Pod F. Tompkast, she confirmed that she and Mahoney had divorced.
