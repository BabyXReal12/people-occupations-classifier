Jonathan Romesh Ranganathan (born January 30, 1978) is a British stand-up comedian and actor. He was nominated for Best Newcomer at the 2013 Edinburgh Comedy Awards.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.independent.ie/woman/celeb-news/comedy-awards-shortlist-unveiled-29518008.html  title=Comedy awards shortlist unveiled  newspaper=Irish Independent  date=21 August 2013  accessdate=7 October 2013}}</ref> He has made multiple appearances on comedy panel shows and in 2016, he co-presented Its Not Rocket Science (TV series) Its Not Rocket Science, alongside Rachel Riley and Ben Miller.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/news/story/000001262/  title=Stand Up for the Week announces new line-up  date=7 October 2013  publisher=British Comedy Guide  accessdate=7 October 2013}}</ref> He is known for his deadpan comedy.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.wscountytimes.co.uk/what-s-on/entertainments/review-russell-kane-romesh-ranganathan-and-charlie-baker-at-christ-s-hospital-1-6642500 title=Review: Russell Kane, Romesh Ranganathan and Charlie Baker at Christ’s Hospital last=Tamplin first=Harley date=19 March 2015 work=West Sussex County Times accessdate=19 November 2015}}</ref>

He is a regular panellist on The Apprentice: Youre Fired!, and was formerly a regular on Play to the Whistle and the eleventh series of The Museum of Curiosity. In 2016, he completed his first major tour, Irrational Live, in which he performed in some iconic venues such as the Hammersmith Apollo. This tour was also released as a DVD. Ranganathan will also join A League of Their Own (UK game show) A League of Their Own as a regular panellist in 2018, replacing Jack Whitehall.

==Career==
Ranganathan started performing as a comedian whilst working as a mathematics teacher at Hazelwick School in Crawley, and The Beacon School, Banstead, Surrey.<ref>{{cite news url=https://www.kentlive.news/news/celebs-tv/7-famous-people-who-you-506152 title=7 famous people who you may not have realised went to school in Crawley last=Truelove first=Sam date=3 October 2016 work=Kent Live accessdate=17 June 2018}}</ref>

Ranganathan started presenting Newsjack on Radio 4 Extra in March 2014.<ref>{{BBC programme id=b03wtj3x title=Newsjack}}</ref>

He appeared on The Great British Bake Off: An Extra Slice in 2014, during which he presented his homemade vegan chocolate brownies. Ranganathan is a vegan, having been a vegetarian up until 2013.<ref>{{BBC programme id=b04gb578 title=Edinburgh Comedy Fest Live 2014, Part 2}}</ref>

Ranganathan has guested on multiple episodes of 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.channel4.com/programmes/8-out-of-10-cats-does-countdown/on-demand/60792-005  title=8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown  publisher=Channel 4}}</ref> He has also appeared on Would I Lie to You? (TV series) Would I Lie to You?, Holby City, Soccer AM, Russell Howards Good News, Sweat the Small Stuff, The Last Leg, Virtually Famous, Have I Got News for You, QI and Mock the Week. He was a regular contestant on the first series of Taskmaster (TV series) Taskmaster and also appeared in the third episode of Jon Richardson Grows Up.

He also performed at The Royal Variety Performance in 2015.

In 2015, he presented a series for BBC Three, Asian Provocateur, in which he travelled to Sri Lanka to explore his ancestral country.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2015/sep/30/asian-provocateur-romesh-ranganathan-in-sri-lanka title=Romesh Ranganathan: ‘I was a bumbling Englishman in a Sri Lankan disguise’ work=The Guardian}}</ref> In October 2016, the second series was aired in which Ranganathan and his mother travel to North America to meet more of his relatives.

He also joined The Apprentice: Youre Fired! in 2015 as a regular panellist. The show is a spin-off from The Apprentice (UK TV series) The Apprentice. In December 2015, he appeared in Josh Widdicombes self-titled sitcom, Josh (TV series) Josh. In 2016, he co-presented Its Not Rocket Science (TV series) Its Not Rocket Science, a new entertainment series for ITV (TV network) ITV, alongside Ben Miller and Rachel Riley. In 2017, he became the eleventh co-presenter to John Lloyd (producer) John Lloyd on the BBC Radio series The Museum of Curiosity.

On February, 9, 2018, it was announced that Ranganthan will join Season 13 of A_League_of_Their_Own_(UK_game_show) A League of Their Own (UK game show) in 2018.<ref>https://www.radiotimes.com/news/2018-02-09/romesh-ranganathan-to-replace-jack-whitehall-on-a-league-of-their-own/</ref>

Romesh also has starred in his own 10 episode docu-comedy called Just_Another_Immigrant Just Another Immigrant that premiered on Showtime_(TV_network) Showtime June 8, 2018.

==Personal life==
File:Romesh Ranganathan in 2015.jpg thumb Romesh Ranganathan in 2015

Ranganathan was raised in Crawley, West Sussex. He is married and has three children. In 2014, Ranganathans wife, Leesa, was admitted to the Edinburgh Royal Infirmary during The Edinburgh Fringe Festival where Ranganathan was appearing, and gave birth to their son.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.crawleyobserver.co.uk/news/local/comic-romesh-celebrates-success-and-birth-of-a-son-1-6331462  title=Comic Romesh celebrates success and birth of a son  date=3 October 2014  newspaper=Crawley Observer  accessdate=17 May 2015}}</ref>

Prior to his comedy career, he was a freestyle rapper under rap name "Ranga" and once reached the finals of the UK freestyle competition.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.londonisfunny.com/the-spotlight-on-romesh-ranganathan/  title=The Spotlight on ... Romesh Ranganathan  work=londonisfunny.com  date=11 November 2011  accessdate=17 May 2015}}</ref>

Ranganathan studied Mathematics at Birkbeck College, University of London.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.unipaper.co.uk/article/romesh-ranganathan-i-was-a-proper-wannabe-rudeboy-at-university title= Romesh Ranganathan: I was a proper wannabe rudeboy at university  website=www.unipaper.co.uk}}</ref> He subsequently taught Maths at Hazelwick School in Crawley before becoming a professional comedian in 2012. His wife was a Drama teacher at Hazelwick.<ref>{{cite web  url= http://www.crawleynews.co.uk/Crawley-teacher-comedian-supported-Ricky-Gervais/story-20467062-detail/story.html  title=Former Crawley teacher now a comedian who has supported Ricky Gervais and appeared on Live at the Apollo  work=Crawley News  date=20 January 2014  accessdate=21 September 2015}}</ref>

He is of Sri Lankan Tamil people Sri Lankan Tamil people Tamil descent<ref>{{cite web last=Hodges  first=Michael  url=http://www.radiotimes.com/news/2015-10-07/9-things-romesh-ranganathan-learnt-when-his-mum-took-him-on-a-cultural-crash-course-to-sri-lanka  title=Asian Provocateur: What comic Romesh Ranganathan learnt about Sri Lanka in his new BBC3 show  work=Radio Times  date=7 October 2015  accessdate=24 March 2017}}</ref> and a keen supporter of Arsenal F.C.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.thesun.co.uk/tvandshowbiz/2974180/romesh-ranganathan-comedian-play-to-the-whistle title= Who is Romesh Ranganathan? Stand up comedian and regular panellist on Play to the Whistle date=20 January 2015 newspaper=The Sun access-date=2 August 2017}}</ref>

Ranganathan is a vegan<ref>{{cite news last1=Jones first1=Alice title=Soya think youre funny? Introducing the UKs first Vegan Comedy Festival url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/news/soya-think-youre-funny-introducing-the-uks-first-vegan-comedy-festival-10042349.html accessdate=22 October 2015 work=The Independent date=12 February 2015}}</ref> and was raised in the Hindu faith.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://twitter.com/romeshranga/status/337328681712222208 title=Romesh Ranganathan on Twitter newspaper=Twitter access-date=4 January 2017}}{{Primary source inline date=December 2017}}</ref> His mother has said that he "always prays before he leaves the home",<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/tv/entries/e2d50d79-d452-4b18-8938-6a6d3d52670b title=Romesh Ranganathan proves even TV comedians get embarrassed by their mums date=1 October 2015 website=TV blog  publisher=BBC  access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> although Ranganathan describes himself as "not massively spiritual".<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.radiotimes.com/news/2015-10-07/9-things-romesh-ranganathan-learnt-when-his-mum-took-him-on-a-cultural-crash-course-to-sri-lanka title=9 things Romesh Ranganathan learnt when his mum took him on a cultural crash course to Sri Lanka newspaper=Radio Times access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> He suffers from a right-sided Ptosis (eyelid) ptosis (drooping eyelid), which he describes as a "lazy eye".<ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.sbs.com.au/topics/life/culture/article/2017/03/21/teaching-maths-performing-comedy-prince-harry-meet-romesh-ranganathan title=From teaching maths to performing comedy for Prince Harry: meet Romesh Ranganathan work=Topics access-date=2018-03-14 language=en}}</ref>

His father died December 13, 2011.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.thegazette.co.uk/notice/L-60080-1542235 title=Details of the Deceased date=7 March 2012 website=The Gazette accessdate=14 August 2018}}</ref>
