Thomas Loren Lenk (born June 16, 1976) is an American stage and television actor best known for starring as Andrew Wells in the television series Buffy the Vampire Slayer (TV series) Buffy the Vampire Slayer and its spin-off Angel (1999 TV series) Angel.

==Early life==
Lenk was born in Camarillo, California, the son of Pam Sherman, a teacher, and Fred Lenk, a tuba player, high school music teacher, and school district computer network administrator.
He attended Adolfo Camarillo High School. He graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles with a Bachelor of Arts.
==Career==
File:Buffy The Vampire Slayer cast.jpg thumb right 250px (From left to right) Lenk, Emma Caulfield, Alexis Denisof, Alyson Hannigan, Anthony Stewart Head, Joss Whedon, Michelle Trachtenberg at the Buffy cast party
Lenk has appeared in episodes of Buffy the Vampire Slayer (27 episodes), Angel (1999 TV series) Angel,
NBCs Joey (TV series) Joey, House (TV series) House, Six Feet Under (TV series) Six Feet Under, Eli Stone and How I Met Your Mother.

He had small parts in the films Date Movie, The Number 23, and Transformers (film) Transformers. Lenk appeared in the web series Border Patrol,
which premiered in June 2008 on Atom.com. In 2009 Lenk appeared as a guest star on Nip/Tuck.

Besides acting, Lenk is a singer and playwright. He has toured with the European cast of Grease (musical) Grease and has written three plays. Lenk took over the role of Franz in the Broadway musical Rock of Ages (musical) Rock of Ages as of September 14, 2009, having originated the role in the Las Vegas and Los Angeles productions in 2006. From 2009 to 2010, Lenk posted video blogs of himself on YouTube summing up his experiences on Broadway, usually under the title "Toms Broadway Blogs". Lenk also participates in video content with Felicia Days YouTube channel Geek and Sundry and parodies fashion on Instagram.
Lenk appeared in an episode of Psych that poked fun at Buffy the Vampire Slayer (original Buffy actress Kristy Swanson appears in the episode as well). He performed in the June 2012 edition of Dont Tell My Mother!, a monthly showcase in which authors, screenwriters, actors and comedians share true stories they would never want their mothers to know.
In 2013, Lenk joined the Lifetime (TV network) Lifetime series Witches of East End (TV series) Witches of East End as Hudson Rafferty.
In 2016, Lenk appeared in the third season finale of Transparent (TV series) Transparent.
==Personal life==
Lenk is coming out openly gay.
