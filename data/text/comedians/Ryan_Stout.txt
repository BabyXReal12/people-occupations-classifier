
Ryan Matthew Stout (born September 30, 1982) is an American stand-up comedian and television host.<ref name="Pullen">Pullen, Doug (2011) "[http://www.elpasotimes.com/living/ci_19570091 The life of Ryan: Comedian from El Paso finds his niche by poking fun where few dare]", "El Paso Times, December 18, 2011, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>

==Early life==
Stout was born in Cleveland, Ohio, grew up in El Paso, Texas,<ref name="Murtagh" /> and graduated from J.M. Hanks High School in 2000.<ref name="Pullen" />  He attended The College of Santa Fe (now Santa Fe University of Art and Design) as a Theater Arts major (2000–2001), but left the school after his first year.  He later enrolled at San Francisco State University as a Creative Writing major.<ref name="Pullen" /><ref name="Murtagh">Murtagh, Heather (2005) "[http://xpress.sfsu.edu/archives/arts/003124.html Comic Slow Dances With the Dark Side]", Golden Gate XPress, March 17, 2005, retrieved 2011-07-11</ref>

Stout first performed stand-up on his seventeenth birthday at a high school talent show.<ref name="Pullen" />  He continued to perform, although only a handful of times, at various poetry open-mics while attending the College of Santa Fe.  In June 2001 he moved to San Francisco, California<ref name="Pullen" /> where the comedy scene allowed him more frequent stage time.  He ingrained himself there for the next few years, performing over three hundred shows each year.

He was one of the last residents of the San Francisco Comedy Condo.

Ryan Stout was awarded the Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America) Eagle Scout rank in 1997 and is a member of the National Eagle Scout Association.

As of March 2006, he lives in Los Angeles.<ref name="Pullen" /><ref name="AVclub" />

==Career==
Ryan Stout is the winner of the 2005 Boston Comedy Festival.<ref name="Pullen" /><ref name="LaughFactory">The Laugh Factory (2013) "[http://www.laughfactory.com/RyanStout  Ryan Stout Biography]", "LaughFactory.com, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>  He is also one of the few comedians to perform at both Home Box Office HBOs The Comedy Festival in Las Vegas, Nevada Las Vegas and HBOs U.S. Comedy Arts Festival in Aspen, CO.<ref name="LaughFactory" />  He made his television debut on Comedy Centrals Live at Gotham in 2007.  That same year he filmed pilot episodes for two separate television shows for MTV, "I.Q." and a remake of the hit show "Singled Out," co-hosted by, Miss USA 2006, Tara Conner.  By the end of the year MTV had him hosting the talk show A Shot at Love: The Hangover.<ref name="LaughFactory" /><ref name="MTVpress">MTV Press (2008) "[http://mtvpress.com/press/release/have_another_round_on_mtv_a_shot_at_love_2_with_tila_tequila Have Another Round on MTV… "A Shot at Love 2]", "MTV Press, April 03, 2008, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref><ref name="Hood">Hood, Micaela (2013) "[http://www.southflorida.com/events/sf-go-ryan-stout-improv-0651713-20130516,0,4793177.story  Ryan Stout: Hecklers beware]", "SouthFlorida.com, May 16, 2013, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>  In early 2008, he hosted a special called MTVs Most Valuable Players, went on to host MTVs Spring Break 2008,<ref name="Pullen" /><ref name="Hood" /><ref name="Loeffler">Loeffler, William (2008) "[http://www.pittsburghlive.com/x/valleyindependent/teenscene/s_595445.html Ryan Stout suits up for outrageous stand-up comedy]", Tribune-Review, October 30, 2008, retrieved 2011-07-11</ref> and hosted a series called MTVs Ranked.<ref name="LaughFactory" />  Concurrent with his television work he continued doing stand-up throughout the United States and performed at the Just For Laughs International Comedy Festival in Montreal, Quebec, Canada.<ref name="LaughFactory" />  Hosting opportunities continued at MTV, including several more game show pilots and conducting reunion episodes of several of the networks reality shows.  He also started making appearances on WGN America WGNs The Bob and Tom Show <ref name="LaughFactory" /> and E! Entertainment Televisions Chelsea Lately.<ref name="Pullen" /><ref name="Hamilton">Hamilton, Jon (2011) "[http://community.brownpapertickets.com/wp/?p=197#more-197 Interview with Comedian Ryan Stout]", "Brown Paper Tickets, September, 2011, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>

Most recently, his Comedy Central Presents half-hour stand-up special premiered on April 30, 2010 which earned him the #10 spot in Comedy Centrals yearly Stand-up Showdown in 2011.<ref name="Hamilton" /> Later that year Stout made his late-night stand-up debut on Conan (talk show) Conan.<ref name="Stout Appears on Late Night Television">{{cite web last=Stout first=Ryan title=Stout Appears on Conan OBrien url=http://teamcoco.com/content/comedian-ryan-stout-reveals-upside-stds work=Late Night television publisher=Teamco accessdate=1 November 2011}}</ref>

His debut album Touché (Ryan Stout album) Touché was released on December 6, 2011, on Comedy Central Records.<ref name="Raubicheck">Raubicheck, Marie (2011) "[http://www.comedycentral.com/press/press_releases/2011/112111-ryan-stout-digital-cd.jhtml?print=true Comedy Central Records Releases Ryan Stouts Touche Debut Digital Album]", "Comedy Central Press Release, November 21, 2011, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>

Ryan Stout was guest on The CTSM Jan 30th 2012.

On August 6, 2012, he appeared on WTF with Marc Maron WTF with Marc Maron.<ref name="AVclub">A.V. Club (2012) "[http://www.avclub.com/articles/episode-302-ryan-stout,83409/ WTF With Marc Maron Episode 302 - Ryan Stout]", "A.V. Club, August 6, 2012, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>

He was featured as "The Announcer" in the "Crickets" music video for hip hop group Drop City Yacht Club.<ref name="Lastfm">Last.fm (2013) "[http://www.last.fm/music/Drop+City+Yacht+Club]", "Last.fm, March 26, 2013, retrieved 2013-05-21</ref>

On January 27, 2016, he appeared on Comedy Central’s program @midnight with Chris Hardwick <ref name="@Midnight">@midnight (2016) "[http://www.cc.com/full-episodes/60gcsj/-midnight-with-chris-hardwick-wednesday--january-27--2016-season-3-ep-03055]", “@midnight, January 27, 2016, retrieved 2016-02-02</ref>
