Neal Brennan (born October 19, 1973)<ref name=NYT>{{cite news title=Novice Director, a Veteran of Comedy first=Josh last=Eells url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/08/09/movies/09eels.html?_r=1 newspaper=New York Times date=August 7, 2009 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=CBS>{{cite news title=Chappelles Trip To The Top first=Rebecca last=Leung url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2005/05/27/60ii/main698261.shtml newspaper=CBS News date=February 11, 2009 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=SANDIEGO>{{cite web url=http://www.sandiego.com/articles/2011-06-22/obama-twitter-and-pokez-neal-brennan-sketch-comedy-diet title=Obama, Twitter and Pokez: The Neal Brennan Sketch Comedy Diet last=Downs first=Gordon date=June 22, 2011 work=SanDiego.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> is an American comedian, writer, producer, and director.

He is known for co-creating and co-writing the Comedy Central series Chappelles Show with Dave Chappelle.<ref name=VANSUN>{{cite news title=ComedyFest: Neal Brennan enters Vancouver laughing first=Mike last=Hager url=http://www.vancouversun.com/entertainment/movie-guide/ComedyFest+Neal+Brennan+enters+Vancouver+laughing/6206681/story.html newspaper=Vancouver Sun date=February 24, 2012 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}{{dead link date=February 2018  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref name=MSNBC>{{cite news title=Dave Chappelle inks $50 million deal first=Andrew last=Wallenstein url=http://msnbc.msn.com/id/5591225/site/todayshow/ns/today-entertainment/t/dave-chappelle-inks-million-deal/#.T0xoq3lc-So newspaper=MSNBC date=August 3, 2004 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=AUSTIN>{{cite news title=Saturday Interview - Neal Brennan first=Chase last=Hoffberger url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/music/2011-11-04/saturday-interview-neal-brennan/ newspaper=Austin Chronicle date=November 4, 2011 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> By the end of the second season, it was ranked as the most popular Comedy Central show. It premiered in January 2003 and continued until 2006.

==Early life==
Brennan grew up in Villanova, Pennsylvania, near Philadelphia,<ref name="NYT"/><ref>{{Cite podcast url=http://www.nerdist.com/2011/11/you-made-it-weird-7-neal-brennan/ title=You Made It Weird: Neal Brennan website=nerdist.com publisher=Nerdist Industries host=Pete Holmes Holmes, Pete date=November 23, 2011 time=19:07 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> and lived from 1978 to 1986 in Wilmette, Illinois.<ref name=IFC>{{cite web url=http://www.ifc.com/fix/2011/11/neal-brennan-comedy-interview title=Neal Brennan talks about his influences, writing race-sensitive material and the state of contemporary comedy date=November 30, 2011 website=IFC.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=CHICAGONOW1>{{cite news title=Just for Laughs exclusive - Neal Brennan interview first=Scott last=King url=http://www.chicagonow.com/class-act-comedy/2011/06/just-for-laughs-exclusive-neal-brennan-interview/ newspaper=Chicago Now publisher=Chicago Tribune date=June 14, 2011 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> He was born into an Irish Catholic family and is the youngest of 10 children.<ref name=NYT/><ref name=IFC/><ref name=HUMORCODE>{{cite web url=http://humorcode.com/2012/02/14/humor-code-q-a-unabridged-neal-brennan-on-life-after-chappelles-show-failed-irish-jokes-and-feeding-the-comedy-baby/ title=Humor Code Q & A (Unabridged): Neal Brennan on Life after Chappelle’s Show, Failed Irish Jokes and Feeding the Comedy Baby last=McGraw first=Dr. Peter author2=Warner, Joel date=February 14, 2012 work=humorcode.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> According to Brennan, his fathers side of the family was funny, as were his five older brothers.<ref name=NYT/><ref name=HUMORCODE/> Brennan said that he realized he was funny and liked comedy at about 8 or 9 years of age; he was already performing planned material for his classmates in a style that emulated comics Richard Lewis (comedian) Richard Lewis, Jerry Seinfeld and David Brenner.<ref name=CAREER>{{cite web url=http://thecareercookbook.com/article.php?article_id=2 title=Neal Brennan last=Maniaci first=Paul date=September 2, 2006 work=thecareercookbook.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> He also watched a large amount of comedy on TV during his high school years, often staying up late to watch Late Night with David Letterman and The Arsenio Hall Show.<ref name=CAREER/>

Brennans older brother Kevin Brennan (comedian) Kevin became a comedian and writer who started doing stand-up comedy while Neal was still in high school.<ref name=CHICAGONOW1/> Neal attended Kevins performances at The Improv in New York City on weekends,<ref name=NYT/> where he would meet comedians such as Ray Romano, Dave Attell, Mike Royce and David Juskow.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=ALIVE/> Brennan said in an interview with IFC (U.S. TV channel) Independent Film Channel (IFC) that, after watching his brother do stand-up, he realized it was possible to make a living in comedy.<ref name=IFC/>

Brennan moved to New York to attend film school at NYU, but he dropped out after a year.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=ALIVE>{{cite news title=Comedy Q&A: Neal Brennan first=Jesse last=Tigges url=http://www.columbusalive.com/content/stories/2011/07/13/comedy-qa-neal-brennan.html newspaper=Columbus Alive! date=July 13, 2011 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> He began as a doorman at the now-defunct Boston Comedy Club in Greenwich Village where he met Dave Chappelle, a frequent performer.<ref name="CBS"/><ref name=NYT2/> The two became friends and Brennan would often pitch jokes to Chappelle.<ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=NYT2/> Brennan also shared an apartment with comedian Jay Mohr while living in New York.<ref name=NYT/>

After six months of working as a doorman at the Boston Comedy Club, in 1992, Brennan first performed stand-up at 18 years of age.<ref name="SANDIEGO"/> He later recounted: "I got no laughs;" he didnt perform stand-up again until 1997.<ref name=SANDIEGO/> Brennan also wrote for The Source (magazine) The Source magazine in 1992.<ref name=IFC/><ref>{{Cite podcast url=http://www.nerdist.com/2011/11/you-made-it-weird-7-neal-brennan/ title=You Made It Weird: Neal Brennan website=nerdist.com publisher=Nerdist Industries host=Holmes, Pete date=November 23, 2011 time=56:09 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
File:Neal Brennan.jpg thumb Brennan on stage
In the mid-1990s, Brennan moved to Los Angeles. In 1995, he became a writer for the dating show, Singled Out, hosted by Jenny McCarthy and Chris Hardwick.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=CHICAGONOW1/> This was followed by writing jobs for the game show Bzzz! in 1996, the sketch comedy-variety show All That from 1996 to 1997, and the teen sitcom Kenan & Kel in 1997.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=IMDB>{{IMDb name 0107366 Neal Brennan}}</ref><ref>{{cite podcast url=http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/4611 title=The Joe Rogan Experience, Episode #205 website=joerogan.net host=Rogan, Joe date=April 12, 2012 time=1:47:54 accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120417041922/http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/4611 archivedate=April 17, 2012 df=}}</ref>

In 1997, Brennan and Chappelle collaborated for the first time on the screenplay for the film Half Baked.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=NYT2>{{cite news title=A Comic Who Wont Hold Back; Nothing Is Out of Bounds For Dave Chappelles Show first=Lola last=Ogunnaike url=https://www.nytimes.com/2004/02/18/arts/a-comic-who-won-t-hold-back-nothing-is-out-of-bounds-for-dave-chappelle-s-show.html?pagewanted=3&src=pm newspaper=New York Times date=February 18, 2004 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> The film was released in January 1998 and starred Chappelle, Jim Breuer, Harland Williams and Guillermo Díaz (actor) Guillermo Díaz.<ref name=CAREER/><ref name=NYT3>{{cite news title=Half Baked (1998) FILM REVIEW; Marijuana Moments, Many of Them first=Lola last=Ogunnaike url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B02E0D91738F934A25752C0A96E958260 newspaper=New York Times date=January 18, 1998 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> Half Baked was a commercial failure and received mainly negative reviews but has become a cult classic.<ref name=NYT/><ref name=BUZZBIN>{{cite web url=http://www.buzzbinmagazine.com/home/2011/07/18/taking-the-leap-with-neal-brennan/ title=Taking the Leap with Neal Brennan last=Flannigan first=W. date=July 18, 2011 work=buzzbinmagazine.com accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120509074949/http://www.buzzbinmagazine.com/home/2011/07/18/taking-the-leap-with-neal-brennan/ archivedate=May 9, 2012 df=}}</ref><ref name=VARIETY>{{cite news title=Half Baked first=Brendan last=Kelly url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117436781?refcatid=31 newspaper=Variety date=January 19, 1998 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref>

In a 2006 interview, Brennan referred to the period of the films release as "probably the worst year of my life, creatively and personally."<ref name=CAREER/> In an interview that same year on Inside the Actors Studio, Chappelle recounted how he and Brennan lost touch with each other after the release of Half Baked, saying that it was "like leaving a crime scene".<ref>{{cite episode title=Dave Chappelle url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=84NjYRTHpfU  accessdate=April 20, 2012 series=Inside the Actors Studio airdate=February 12, 2006 time=55:00}}</ref>

===Chappelles Show===
Four years later, Brennan and Chappelle came together to co-create, co-write and co-Executive producer executive produce the eponymous sketch comedy Chappelles Show, which premiered in January 2003.<ref name=NYT/><ref name=MSNBC/><ref name=NYT2/> Brennan said that he and Chappelle read the book Live from New York: An Uncensored History of Saturday Night Live (2002), by Tom Shales when they started writing sketches for the show, and found the book very helpful.<ref name=CHICAGONOW2>{{cite news title=What really happened with Chappelles Show and more: Neal Brennan interview Part 2 first=Scott last=King url=http://www.chicagonow.com/class-act-comedy/2011/06/what-really-happened-with-chappelles-show-and-more-neal-brennan-interview-part-2/ newspaper=Chicago Now publisher=Chicago Tribune date=June 16, 2011 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> The duo wrote the shows sketches with minimal outside help<ref name=SANDIEGO/><ref name=EW>{{cite web url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,1058529,00.html title=Half-Baked Theories last=Susman first=Gary date=May 6, 2005 work=EW.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> and agreed never to divulge who was responsible for writing which sketch.<ref name=VANSUN/><ref>{{cite podcast url=http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/3195 title=The Joe Rogan Experience, Episode #114 website=joerogan.net host=Rogan, Joe date=June 14, 2011 time=1:15:22 accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120505164519/http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/3195 archivedate=May 5, 2012 df=}}</ref>

Brennan directed some sketches in the shows second season, including the sketch featuring Chappelle as musician Rick James.<ref name=NYT/><ref name=CAREER/><ref name=IMDB/> Brennan was nominated for three Emmy Awards in 2004 for his work on the show as a director, writer and producer.<ref name=TAHOE>{{cite news title=Chappelles Showwriter debuts at Tahoe Improv first=Howie last=Nave url=http://www.tahoedailytribune.com/article/20120321/NEWS/120329974/1017&parentprofile=1058 newspaper=Tahoe Daily Tribune date=March 21, 2012 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{IMDb title 0353049 Awards for "Chappelles Show"}}</ref> By the end of its second season, Chappelles Show was Comedy Centrals highest-rated program.<ref name=BEAST>{{cite web url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/newsweek/2005/05/15/fears-of-a-clown.html title=Fears of a Clown last=Gordon first=Devin date=May 15, 2005 work=The Daily Beast accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120118153307/http://www.thedailybeast.com/newsweek/2005/05/15/fears-of-a-clown.html archivedate=January 18, 2012 df=}}</ref>

Members of the musical group The Roots worked as music directors on the second and third seasons of the show.<ref name=HUFFPO>{{cite news title=The Roots are ready to rock, rap, jazz and even disco the set of Late Night with Jimmy Fallon first=Danny last=Gallagher  url=http://www.chicagonow.com/class-act-comedy/2011/06/just-for-laughs-exclusive-neal-brennan-interview/ newspaper=The Huffington Post date=February 27, 2009 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=SEATTLEPI>{{cite news title=The Roots: House-band gig enabled us to survive first=Jake last=Coyle url=http://www.seattlepi.com/ae/music/article/The-Roots-House-band-gig-enabled-us-to-survive-1300977.php newspaper=SeattlePi agency=Associated Press publisher=Hearst Communications date=February 24, 2009 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> Brennan later recommended the band to Jimmy Fallon as his house band on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon.<ref name=HUFFPO/><ref name=SEATTLEPI/><ref name=TAMPA>{{cite news title=Hip-hop band the Roots prepares for TV gig on Late Night With Jimmy Fallon first=Eric last=Deggans url=http://www.tampabay.com/features/media/article979876.ece newspaper=Tampa Bay Times date=March 1, 2009 accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121212062045/http://www.tampabay.com/features/media/article979876.ece archivedate=December 12, 2012 df=}}</ref>

Chappelles Show was doing well and Chappelle had signed a $50 million deal in 2004 to produce two more seasons,<ref name=CBS/><ref name=MSNBC/><ref name=EW/><ref name=OPRAH>{{cite episode title=Chappelles Story url=http://www.oprah.com/oprahshow/Chappelles-Story accessdate=April 20, 2012 series=The Oprah Winfrey Show airdate=February 9, 2006}}</ref> but he abruptly left the show in April 2005 prior to the premiere of the shows third season.<ref name=CBS/><ref name=EW/><ref name="OPRAH"/> He left without warning Brennan or others of the shows crew.<ref name=CHICAGONOW2/><ref name=EW/><ref name=LATIMES/><ref name=NPR>{{Cite episode title=Comedian Chappelle Surfaces in Time url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=4653985 accessdate=April 20, 2012 series=All Things Considered serieslink=All Things Considered credits=Presenter: Michele Norris network=National Public Radio date=May 16, 2005}}</ref> As a result, the premiere of season three was delayed; Brennan compiled the remaining sketches and aired them in July 2006 as the "List of Chappelles Show episodes#Season 3 ("The Lost Episodes"): 2006 lost episodes."<ref name=LATIMES>{{cite news title=Chappelle returns, sort of first=David last=Bauder url=http://articles.latimes.com/2006/jul/08/entertainment/et-chappelle8 newspaper=Associated Press date=July 8, 2006 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name=NYT4>{{cite news title=The Long-Awaited, Albeit Brief, Return of Dave Chappelle first=Lola last=Ogunnaike url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/07/06/arts/television/06dave.html?_r=1 newspaper=New York Times date=July 6, 2006 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref>

===2006 to present===
In 2006, Brennan directed and co-wrote the Television film made-for-TV movie Totally Awesome.<ref name=TAHOE/><ref name=LAIST>{{cite news title=LAist Interview: Neal Brennan first=Tim last=Hammer url=http://laist.com/2007/10/17/laist_interview_52.php newspaper=LAist date=October 7, 2007 accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090717015205/http://laist.com/2007/10/17/laist_interview_52.php archivedate=July 17, 2009 df=}}</ref> Brennan directed the 2009 film The Goods: Live Hard, Sell Hard The Goods starring Jeremy Piven.<ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=TAHOE/> In 2011, he directed a series of commercials for the ESPY Award ESPYS.<ref name=SANDIEGO/><ref name=ALIVE/>

As an actor, Brennan had small roles in the films Half Baked and Get Him to the Greek; he also appeared in various sketches on Chappelles Show.<ref name=IMDB/> Brennan has also written comedy material for the 83rd Academy Awards, as well as for Seth Meyers speech at the White House Correspondents Association#White House Correspondents Dinner White House Correspondents Dinner in 2011.<ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=WIRED>{{cite web url=https://www.wired.com/underwire/2012/03/humor-code-neal-brennan/ title=The Humor Code: Neal Brennan on Comedic Polymaths and the Future of Funny last=Warner first=Joel author2=McGraw, Peter date=March 1, 2012 work=Wired.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref>

Brennan continues to perform stand-up regularly in the Los Angeles area as well as nationally.<ref name=WIRED/><ref name=ARC>{{cite news title=Half Baked first=Trevor last=Horn author2=Frields, Philip url=http://www.arcurrent.com/arts-culture/2011/10/26/qa-with-%E2%80%98chappelles-show%E2%80%99-co-creator-neal-brennan/ newspaper=American River Current date=October 26, 2011 accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref> He has also appeared on Last Call with Carson Daly, Late Night with Jimmy Fallon, Lopez Tonight, and Conan (talk show) Conan.<ref name=CHICAGONOW1/><ref name=TAHOE/><ref>[http://teamcoco.com/video/comedian-neal-brennan Video of Neal Brennan on Conan], September 27, 2011</ref>

In 2011, Brennan, comedian Moshe Kasher, and DJ Douggpound (Doug Lussenhop of Tim and Eric Nite Live!), started a podcast called The Champs.<ref name=SANDIEGO2>{{cite web url=http://www.sandiego.com/articles/2011-07-20/living-edge-moshe-kasher  title=Living On The Edge with Moshe Kasher last=Downs first=Gordon date=July 20, 2011 work=SanDiego.com accessdate=April 20, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite podcast url=http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/3443 title=The Joe Rogan Experience, Episode #131 website=joerogan.net host=Rogan, Joe date=August 17, 2011 time=1:49:07 accessdate=April 20, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120505173329/http://blog.joerogan.net/archives/3443 archivedate=May 5, 2012 df=}}</ref> Kasher said of the podcast: 
"Its Doug dropping sound effects and beats over me and Neal kind of hosting an hour of ridiculous chat. We have a rotating Black people black guy guest, theres a different black guest every week."<ref name=SANDIEGO2/> Guests on the show have included actor/comedians Wayne Brady, Chris Rock, Mario Joyner, Shawn and Marlon Wayans, and David Alan Grier, Pornographic film adult film star Lexington Steele, rapper Too $hort, and professional basketball player Blake Griffin. The show has strayed from its guest format on occasion with guests such as comedian and actor Bobby Lee, former pornographic actress Sasha Grey, former Major League Baseball player Jose Canseco and comedian/actor Aziz Ansari. In 2014 The Champs was named "Best Podcast" as part of LA Weekly{{s}} "Best of L.A." issue.<ref>{{cite journal title=Best Podcast Los Angeles 2014 — The Champs url=http://www.laweekly.com/los-angeles/best-podcast/BestOf?oid=5108130 journal=LA Weekly date=October 2–8, 2014 volume=36 issue=46 accessdate=September 29, 2014}}</ref>

In 2013, Brennan directed 10 episodes of Inside Amy Schumer.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2578508/fullcredits/ title=Inside Amy Schumer (TV Series 2013– ) publisher= via=www.imdb.com}}</ref>

In 2015, Brennan developed and performed a comedy show, 3 Mics, in Los Angeles. He brought it to New York City in 2016, opening on March 3 at the Lynn Redgrave Theater. It was directed by Drew Barr. John Legend and his Get Lifted partners, Mike Jackson and Ty Stiklorius, produced the limited engagement. The show has Brennan alternating among three microphones.<ref>[http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/3-mics-neal-brennan-show-860362 Lee, Ashley]  The Hollywood Reporter (29 January 2016)</ref> At the first mic, he reads one-liners from index cards; at the second mic, he talks about dealing with depression and his relationship with his father; and at the third mic, he does traditional stand-up comedy.

In 2017, 3 Mics was released as a Netflix original comedy special.
