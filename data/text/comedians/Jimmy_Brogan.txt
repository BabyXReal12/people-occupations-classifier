Jimmy Brogan (born September 18, 1948), sometimes credited as Jim Brogan, is an American stand-up comedian, writer and actor.  He has made numerous standup appearances on the talk show circuit including The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson and Late Night with David Letterman.<ref name=LATimes>{{cite web last1=Strauss first1=Duncan title=Brogan: A Stand-Up Comic Who Stands Alone url=http://articles.latimes.com/1988-04-04/entertainment/ca-337_1_jimmy-brogan website=LA Times publisher=LATimes accessdate=5 April 2015}}</ref>  He was a writer on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno for 9 years.<ref name=imdb>{{cite web last1=Brogan first1=Jimmy title=Artists page url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0111156/ website=Imdb publisher=Imdb accessdate=5 April 2015}}</ref>  As an actor, he starred in the ABC sitcom Out of the Blue (1979 TV series) Out of the Blue.<ref name=imdb/>

== Comedian ==
Brogan is well known in the comedy clubs for his off the cuff adlib-style act.  The Los Angeles Times has called Brogan “an absolute master at interacting with the crowd” and says, “he elevated crowd interaction to a high art.”<ref name=LATimes/>

Brogan started doing stand-up comedy in New York City in 1975.  Within his first year and a half of doing stand-up he was a regular performer at the The Improv Improv, Catch a Rising Star (comedy clubs) Catch a Rising Star and the Comic Strip.<ref name=LATimes/> Brogan guest starred on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson 7 times from 1984 to 1992.  He also appeared on Late Night with David Letterman and the Merv Griffin Show.<ref name=imdb/>
	
He hosted Laffathon on Showtime 1980-81, Comic Strip Live on Fox 1987, You Asked for it Again on the Family Channel 1992.<ref>http://www.anshe.org/comedy.htm</ref><ref name=caldwell>{{cite journal title=Caldwell Chr journal=Caldwell Chronicle date=February 2007 volume=40 issue=2 page=2 url=http://www.cccti.edu/Chronicle/2007/Febraury%202%20Chronicle.pdf accessdate=5 April 2015}}</ref>  He also hosted The Late Show (1986 TV series) The Late Show on Fox in 1987 after Joan Rivers left the show.<ref name=caldwell/> From 1992 until 2001 he appeared many times on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno doing stand-up, in written comedy pieces and in remote segments.
	
He warmed up the studio audiences for sitcoms Taxi (TV series) Taxi, Cheers, Newhart and Seinfeld as well as talk shows The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, Late Night with David Letterman and The Oprah Winfrey Show Oprah.<ref name=caldwell/>

Almost every Sunday for the last 18 years, he has been opening for Jay Leno at the Comedy & Magic Club in Hermosa Beach.<ref>http://losangeles.metromix.com/events/comedy_event/jay-leno-</ref> "Leno calls Brogan every Sunday to ask if theyre riding together. He picks me up in one of his 80 exotic cars, Brogan said. We ride in a different one every Sunday. This has been a ritual since 1991."<ref>http://www.sheckymagazine.com/2007/04/brogan-leno-exotic-cars.html</ref>

== Writer ==
Brogan served as a writer for the opening monologue on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno from 1992-2001.  The Los Angeles Times Magazine called him "L.A.s premier joke scientist …dissecting, analyzing, adding or subtracting ingredients" in Lenos opening monologue.<ref>http://articles.latimes.com/1998/feb/22/magazine/tm-21820</ref> During the show "Jimmy would sit to one side of the audience while Jay told his jokes.  On occasion, Jimmy and Jay would make a bet to see if a certain joke worked or not.  If Jimmy won, Jay would walk down the stage, and pay Jimmy.  If Jay won, Jimmy would come to the stage and pay off the bet.  All this was done while the camera was running."<ref name=imdb/>

== Actor ==
In 1979, Brogan moved to Los Angeles to star in the sitcom Out of the Blue (1979 TV series) Out of the Blue on ABC as Random the Angel.<ref name=imdb/>  ABC aired 9 episodes, including the premiere episode which co-starred Robin Williams as Mork from Mork and Mindy.
	
Brogan also appeared in an episode of Happy Days entitled Chachi Sells his Soul as Random to cross promote his show Out of the Blue (1979 TV series) Out of the Blue.<ref name=imdb/>
	
He had guest parts on other TV shows including Just the Ten of Us, Brooklyn Nine Nine and Stand-Up Confidential Jerry Seinfeld’s Stand-up Confidential.<ref name=imdb/>
	
Brogan also appeared in three major motion pictures: Punchline (film) Punchline (with Tom Hanks and Sally Field) playing the younger clergyman; Steven Soderbergh’s The Informant! (with Matt Damon) as psychiatrist, Dr. Miller; and Bridesmaids (with Kristen Wiig) as the minister at the wedding.<ref name=imdb/>
