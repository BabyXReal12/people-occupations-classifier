
Sara Pascoe (born 22 May 1981){{citation needed date=October 2017}}<!-- WP:RS needed; removed link to WP:SPS (Twitter, FB, WP)--> is an English writer, stand-up comedian and actress. She has appeared on TV programmes such as 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown for Channel 4, and Taskmaster (TV series) Taskmaster for digital channel Dave (TV channel) Dave.

==Early life==
Pascoe was born in Dagenham, east London.<ref name=EveStand2014/><ref>{{cite news  newspaper= The Independent  title= My student days: Six musicians, actors and comedians fondly reminisce  date= 14 August 2014  author= Elisa Bray  url= https://www.independent.co.uk/student/into-university/clearing/my-student-days-six-musicians-actors-and-comedians-fondly-reminisce-9666657.html }}</ref> Her father is Derek Pascoe, vocalist with 70s pop group Flintlock (musical group) Flintlock. Her great-grandmother was the music writer Rosa Newmarch. 

She studied English at the University of Sussex.<ref name=EveStand2014/> She later worked as a tour guide in London.<ref>{{cite web  title=Sara Pascoe at Wells Comedy Fest  work=The Bristol Magazine  url=http://thebristolmag.co.uk/sara-pascoe-wells-comedy-fest/  year=2016  accessdate=20 May 2017 }}</ref>

==Career==
Pascoe has appeared in many television programmes and panel shows, including Stand Up for the Week, The Thick of It, Mock the Week, The Increasingly Poor Decisions of Todd Margaret, Campus (TV series) Campus, Being Human (UK TV series) Being Human, Twenty Twelve, QI and W1A (TV series) W1A as well as all-female sketch show Girl Friday (part of Channel 4s Comedy Showcase), which she co-wrote.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/guide/tv/girl_friday/  title=Girl Friday – C4 Sketch Show – British Comedy Guide  publisher=Comedy.co.uk  accessdate=16 December 2011}}</ref>

She began performing stand-up in 2007.<ref>{{cite web author=Brian Logan  url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/aug/07/sara-pascoe-nietzsche  title=Sara Pascoe: And now for Nietzsche ... &#124; Stage  work=The Guardian  date=7 August 2013  accessdate=14 September 2013}}</ref> In August 2010, she performed her first show at the Edinburgh Festival, Sara Pascoe Vs Her Ego. The show received positive reviews.<ref>{{cite news author=Julian Hall  url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/reviews/sara-pascoe-vs-her-ego-pleasance-courtyard-2047951.html  title=Sara Pascoe vs Her Ego, Pleasance Courtyard – Reviews, Comedy  work=The Independent  date=10 August 2010  accessdate=16 December 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web author=Duska Radosavljevic  url=http://ed.thestage.co.uk/reviews/1022  title=Sara Pascoe vs Her Ego – The Stage / Edinburgh 2011  work=The Stage  date=10 August 2010  accessdate=16 December 2011}}</ref>

On 19 February 2012, she appeared on Episode 11 of the The Comedians Comedian with Stuart Goldsmith Comedians Comedian Podcast hosted by Stuart Goldsmith.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.comedy.co.uk/podcasts/comedians_comedian_podcast/11_sara_pascoe/ title=Episode 11 – Sara Pascoe (Live) – The Comedians Comedian Podcast with Stuart Goldsmith – British Comedy Guide last=Guide first=British Comedy website=British Comedy Guide access-date=5 November 2016}}</ref> In 2012, she appeared on Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g_t_wB7t5Yo title=Sara Pascoe Live at the Apollo date=2 December 2012 publisher=YouTube accessdate=20 February 2015}}</ref> She appeared on BBC Television BBC TV panel show QI in 2013 and won the episode with +28 points.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b039wgpz  title=BBC Two – QI, Series K, Knees & Knockers  publisher=BBC  date=21 February 2014  accessdate=23 April 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, she performed at the List of Edinburgh festivals Edinburgh Festival and toured the UK for the first time with the show Sara Pascoe Vs History.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.beyondthejoke.co.uk/content/835/news-sara-pascoe-announces-first-uk-tour title=News: Sara Pascoe Announces First UK Tour publisher=Beyond the Joke date=29 May 2014}}</ref> The show was nominated for the Fosters Edinburgh Comedy Awards Edinburgh Comedy Award 2014 for Best Comedy Show.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/aug/23/john-kearns-wins-the-fosters-edinburgh-comedy-award-2014 title=John Kearns wins the Fosters Edinburgh comedy award 2014 work=The Guardian author=Brian Logan date=23 August 2014}}</ref> In October 2014 she appeared in Never Mind the Buzzcocks and stood in line at the identity parade round as a former dancer and back-up singer for entertainer (and Robbie Williamss father) Pete Conway.<ref name=Guardian1014>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2014/oct/19/sara-pascoe-vs-history-comedy-tour-interview title=Sara Pascoe: Female sexuality is an active thing  work=The Guardian author=Stephanie Merritt date=19 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{BBC programme id=b04m0lzb title=Never Mind the Buzzcocks, Series 28, Episode 3}}</ref> Also that month, she appeared on the topical panel quiz Have I Got News for You as a panellist and in one of the sketches of the Channel 4 charity night Stand Up to Cancer UK Stand Up to Cancer.

In 2015, she appeared as a panellist on two Radio 4 programmes, science discussion programme The Infinite Monkey Cage in February, and comedy quiz programme The Unbelievable Truth (radio show) The Unbelievable Truth in September.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b068tsvn title=BBC Radio 4 – The Unbelievable Truth, Series 15, Episode 3 date=23 September 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, along with numerous other celebrities, Pascoe toured the UK to support Jeremy Corbyns bid to become Prime Minister of the United Kingdom Prime Minister.<ref name="jc4pmtour">{{cite web  url=http://www.jc4pmtour.com/ title=#JC4PM publisher=jc4pmtour date=28 July 2015 accessdate=15 July 2017}}</ref><ref name="telegraph">{{cite news  last=Wilkinson first=Michael url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/politics/Jeremy_Corbyn/12134051/Celebrities-to-tour-Britain-in-Jeremy-Corbyn-For-Prime-Minister-musical-show.html title=Celebrities to tour Britain in Jeremy Corbyn For Prime Minister musical show work=The Daily Telegraph date=1 February 2016 accessdate=15 July 2017}}</ref>

Her first book, Animal: The Autobiography of a Female Body, was published in April 2016 by Faber and Faber.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thebookseller.com/news/comedian-tackles-female-body-faber title=Pascoe tackles the female body for Faber  date=2 March 2015 author=Joshua Farrington publisher=The Bookseller}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.faber.co.uk/shop/non-fiction/science/9780571325221-animal.html title=Animal website=Public Store View accessdate=19 May 2018}}</ref> In 2016 she went on tour again with the show Animal. She participated in series 3 of the comedy challenge show, Taskmaster (TV series) Taskmaster, which aired on Dave (TV channel) Dave in October and November 2016.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.comedy.co.uk/tv/news/2080/taskmaster_series_3_lineup title=Taskmaster Series 3 line-up revealed author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->  date=16 April 2016 website=British Comedy Guide}}</ref>

She has appeared in all three of Frankie Boyles Autopsy BBC programmes (2014–2016), discussing political issues. In 2017 she starred in his Frankie Boyles New World Order topical series. In 2017 she also performed a half-hour stand-up set on episode 1 of series 2 of Live from the BBC.

On 27 October 2017 she appeared in an episode of British travel documentary series Travel Man on Channel 4.
In February 2018, she started a BBC Radio 4 series called "Modern Monkey".

In April 2018 she appeared as a panelist in two episodes of the BBC Radio 4 panel show Just A Minute, coming fourth in both episodes. In May 2018 she starred in a BBC comedy short entitled "Sarah Pascoe vs Monogamy."<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2018/comedy-shorts title=BBC – Sara Pascoe, Tim Key, Nick Helm and Spencer Jones make new BBC Comedy Shorts  – Media Centre website=www.bbc.co.uk language=en-GB access-date=2018-07-31}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pascoe is a vegan.<ref>http://www.sarapascoe.com/veganism.html</ref> She lives in London.

From 2013 to late 2016, Pascoe dated comedian John Robins (comedian) John Robins,<ref name=EveStand2014 /><ref name=Guardian1014 /> and her reflections on that relationship and its aftermath are the basis of her 2017 show LadsLadsLads at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe.<ref>{{cite news   url = https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2017/aug/05/sara-pascoe-lads-lads-lads-review-pleasance-courtyard-edinburgh  author=Brian Logan  title=Sara Pascoe: LadsLadsLads review – breakup tales from a woman reborn  work=The Guardian  date=5 August 2017 }}</ref>
