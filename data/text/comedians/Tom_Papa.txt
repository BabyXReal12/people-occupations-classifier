
Thomas Papa (born November 10, 1968) is an American stand-up comedian, actor, writer, producer, and television/radio host.<ref>{{cite web   work=HBO  title=Behind the Candelabra year=2013 url=http://www.hbo.com/movies/behind-the-candelabra/cast-and-crew/index.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite web work=The New York Times title=EPIX "Comedy Unbound" Summer Season Continues with Tom Papa date=June 27, 2013  url=http://markets.on.nytimes.com/research/stocks/news/press_release.asp?docTag=201306271056BIZWIRE_USPRX____BW5990&feedID=600&press_symbol=183103}}</ref> He is currently the head writer and a regular performer on Live from Here (formerly A Prairie Home Companion).<ref>{{cite web url=http://tompapa.com/#papabio title=Tom Papa website=tompapa.com accessdate=22 July 2018}}</ref>

==Career==

===Stand-up comedy===
He began in New York City in 1993 playing open mics and hosting at the Stand Up New York comedy club. He toured with Jerry Seinfeld after meeting him at the Comedy Cellar in New York. In 2005 he released his debut comedy album Calm, Cool, & Collected. His successful one-man show Only Human opened at the Montreal Just for Laughs Comedy Festival.

He has recorded three stand up specials for Comedy Central. The most recent, Tom Papa Live in New York City, directed by Rob Zombie, aired on 8 January 2012 and was shot at the Union Square Theater. His third special, Freaked Out, aired in July 2013. He completed his fourth special, Human Mule, in December 2016. Papa has appeared thirteen times on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno. He was a frequent guest on Late Night with Conan OBrien and Late Show with David Letterman.

===Radio===
Papa hosted the Sirius/XM radio show Come to Papa on channel 99, with guests including Mel Brooks, Ray Romano, Dick Cavett, Carl Reiner and Jerry Seinfeld. He writes and performs the monthly Come to Papa Live, a version of a classic radio play combined with standup, music, and sketches. Come to Papa Live is alternately hosted between The Village Underground in New York and Largo in Los Angeles. Guests on Come to Papa Live have included Matt Damon, Sarah Silverman, Joel Mchale, Andy Richter, and Jim Gaffigan. Tom first appeared as a panelist on NPRs "Wait Wait...Dont tell Me" on June 30th 2018

===TV/Film===
Papa had a recurring role as Luff in the Home Box Office HBO and Cinemax series The Knick, starring Clive Owen. He appeared in Chris Rocks movie Top Five, and starred as Ray Arnett alongside Michael Douglas and Matt Damon in Behind the Candelabra, which aired on HBO on May 26, 2013.  This is his second film with director Steven Soderbergh.

Papa played opposite Matt Damon in Steven Soderberghs 2009 film The Informant!. He also played the title character of El Superbeasto and starred opposite Paul Giamatti in the Rob Zombie animated feature The Haunted World of El Superbeasto. He played himself in Comedian (film) Comedian, and lent his voice to the animated feature Bee Movie.

In 2010, Papa hosted NBCs The Marriage Ref (U.S. TV series) The Marriage Ref,<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.northjersey.com/arts_entertainment/86120747_North_Jersey_native_Tom_Papa_makes_the_call_on_married_couples__spats_in_a_new_comedy_show.html title=North Jersey native Tom Papa makes the call on married couples spats in a new comedy show last=Rohan first=Virginia date=3 March 2010 publisher=NorthJersey.com accessdate=20 March 2012}}</ref> which ran for two seasons.

Papas other TV appearances include Inside Amy Schumer, The Jim Gaffigan Show, a recurring role on The New Adventures of Old Christine,  hosting the FOX show Boom! which aired in summer 2015, VH1s I Love the 70s (U.S. TV series) I Love the 70s, I Love the 80s (U.S. TV series) I Love the 80s, and I Love the 90s (U.S. TV series) I Love the 90s series, National Geographic Channels US version of the British game show Duck Quacks Dont Echo (US TV series) Duck Quacks Dont Echo.<ref>[http://channel.nationalgeographic.com/channel/duck-quacks-dont-echo/ Duck Quacks Dont Echo], </ref>
