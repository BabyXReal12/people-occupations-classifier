
Jon Rudnitsky (born November 22, 1989)<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/blog/jon-rudnitsky-joins-snl-cast  title=Jon Rudnitsky Joins SNL Cast &#124; Blog  publisher=NBC  date=  accessdate=2015-09-19}}</ref> is an American actor and comedian who was a cast member on Saturday Night Live for the Saturday Night Live (season 41) 2015-2016 season.

==Early life==
Rudnitsky is a native of Harrington Park, New Jersey.<ref name="cast">{{cite news  last=Zhu  first=Danielle  url= http://www.ew.com/article/2015/08/31/jon-rudnitsky-saturday-night-live-best-sketches  title=Meet Jon Rudnitsky, SNLs newest cast member  work=Entertainment Weekly  date=2015-08-31  accessdate=}}</ref> His mother, Karen, is in charge of the gerontology center at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood.<ref>http://njjewishnews.com/article/27884/michael-orens-parents-recall-his-out-there-youth#.WwvBM0iFPcs</ref> He attended Northern Valley Regional High School at Old Tappan.<ref>Sheridan, Angeline. [http://npascackvalley.macaronikid.com/article/1053981/heres-whats-buzzing-in-bergen-this-week "Heres Whats Buzzing in Bergen This Week"], Macaroni Kid Northeast Bergen, September 3, 2015. Accessed September 8, 2015. "NBC recently announced the addition of 25 year old Jon Rudnitsky as a featured player on Saturday Night Live. Rudnitsky, who grew up in Harrington Park, is a stand-up comedian who got his start at Northern Valley Old Tappan High School."</ref>  He subsequently studied acting at the University of Southern California. His uncle (mothers brother) is Michael Oren, the former Israeli ambassador to the United States.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Mazel-Tov-MK-Michael-Oren-congratulates-nephew-on-joining-cast-of-SNL-415297  title=MK Michael Oren congratulates nephew on joining cast of SNL - Israel News - Jerusalem Post  publisher=Jpost.com  date=  accessdate=2015-09-19}}</ref> Rudnitsky is Jewish.<ref>{{cite web first=Nate last=Bloom url=http://www.jweekly.com/2015/09/18/celebrtiey-jews0918/ title=celebrity jews  date=18 September 2015 work=J. The Jewish News of Northern California}}</ref>

==Career==
Rudnitsky performed with the Groundlings.<ref name="featured">{{cite news  last=Andreeva  first=Nellie  url= http://deadline.com/2015/08/saturday-night-jon-rudnitsky-new-featured-player-2015-1201510559/  title=‘Saturday Night Live’ Adds Comedian Jon Rudnitsky To Cast As Featured Player  work=Deadline.com  date=2015-08-31  accessdate=}}</ref> On August 31, 2015, it was announced that Rudnitsky would join the cast of Saturday Night Live as a featured player for the shows Saturday Night Live (season 41) forty-first season.<ref name="snl">{{cite news  last=Arkin  first=Daniel  url= http://www.nbcnews.com/pop-culture/tv/jon-rudnitsky-stand-comic-joins-saturday-night-live-featured-player-n418826  title=Jon Rudnitsky, Stand-Up Comic, Joins Saturday Night Live as Featured Player  work=NBC News  date=2015-08-31  accessdate=}}</ref> In the third episode of that season, Rudnitsky portrayed Anderson Cooper in a sketch, acting as moderator for a Democratic presidential debate, for which he was criticized by Cooper.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/10/23/saturday-night-live-anderson-cooper-impression title=Anderson Cooper offended by SNL newcomers impression access-date=2016-07-02}}</ref> He has also done impressions of Wolf Blitzer, John Mayer, Martin Scorsese, Tom Cruise, and Vladimir Putin (in a dress rehearsal sketch on the episode hosted by Donald Trump), while also doing a Dirty Dancing sketch for Weekend Update, which was seen as one of his high points of the season.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bustle.com/articles/140179-snl-newbie-jon-rudnitsky-finally-makes-his-mark-on-the-show-with-a-dirty-dancing-parody title=SNL Newbie Jon Rudnitsky Finally Makes His Mark On The Show With A Dirty Dancing Parody first=Marisa last=LaScala work=Bustle.com}}</ref> On August 9, 2016, Rudnitsky departed the show.<ref>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2016/08/saturday-night-live-jon-rudnitsky-featured-player-depart-1201800755/ title=‘Saturday Night Live’ Featured Player Jon Rudnitsky To Depart first=Nellie last=Andreeva date=9 August 2016 work=Deadline.com}}</ref>

In 2017, Rudnitsky starred alongside Reese Witherspoon in the romantic comedy film Home Again (2017 film) Home Again.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/jon-rudnitsky-joins-reese-witherspoon-home-again-943219 title=SNL Alum Jon Rudnitsky Joins Reese Witherspoon in Home Again (Exclusive) newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter access-date=2016-11-21}}</ref> In 2018 Jon played Mike in the Netflix release Set It Up.

He has been announced as starring alongside Jane Lynch and Genevieve Angelson in the NBC pilot Relatively Happy.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://variety.com/2017/tv/news/jane-lynch-nbc-pilot-relatively-happy-1202008986/ title=Jane Lynch Joins NBC Pilot ‘Relatively Happy’ last=Schwindt first=Oriana date=2017-03-14 work=Variety access-date=2017-07-17 language=en-US}}</ref>
