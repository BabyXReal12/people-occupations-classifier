==Television career==
Handler was a member of the all-female cast of Oxygen (TV channel) Oxygen networks hidden camera Reality television#Hidden cameras reality television series Girls Behaving Badly,<ref>"Cast Biographies". Girls Behaving Badly. Volume 1. Season 1 DVD. Disc 2 Bonus Features. Starlight Video. December 26, 2006.</ref> which aired from 2002 to 2005. Other shows on which Handler has appeared include Weekends at the D.L., The Bernie Mac Show, My Wife and Kids, and The Practice. She was a regular commentator on E! and Scarborough Country, as well as a correspondent on The Tonight Show. She hosted the first episode of the reality TV show On the Lot, but quit before the second episode aired, saying later that she left "because I smelled the disaster happening before it did."<ref>{{cite news url= http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/07197/801962-352.stm title= Summer Press Tour: Posh Beckham, Chelsea Handler accessdate= January 3, 2009 date= July 16, 2007 publisher= Pittsburgh Post-Gazette}}</ref> The show, produced by Steven Spielberg and Mark Burnett (executive producer) Mark Burnett, was a competition for aspiring filmmakers vying for a chance at stardom. It lasted one season. Handler was replaced by former Robin & Company entertainment anchor, Adrianna Costa.

In April 2006, Handler began hosting The Chelsea Handler Show on E!, which lasted two seasons. In 2007, Handler performed with Comedy Central Comedy Centrals Hour Stand-Up Comedy Tour across the United States. Her stand-up comedy has also been televised on VH1 VH1s Love Lounge, Comedy Centrals Premium Blend, and HBO HBOs broadcast of the Aspen Comedy Festival. She was a guest on Red Eye w/ Greg Gutfeld and The View (U.S. TV series) The View, and she co-hosted The View on August 2, 2007, and September 5, 2008. On August 18, 2010, it was announced that Handler would be the host of the 2010 MTV Video Music Awards 2010 MTV Video Music Awards (VMAs). The announcement was surprising to many, including MTV representatives, who claimed that the final decision was unexpected.<ref>{{cite web url=http://tv.msn.com/the-wrap/handler-hosts-vmas/story/?GT1=28103  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100823051322/http://tv.msn.com/the-wrap/handler-hosts-vmas/story/?GT1=28103  archivedate=August 23, 2010  title=Chelsea Handler to host 2010 MTV VMAs – MSN TV News  publisher=Tv.msn.com  date=September 17, 2010  accessdate=September 21, 2010}}</ref> This made Handler the second woman in the history of the VMAs to be the sole host of the ceremony, behind Roseanne Barr, who hosted in 1994.<ref>{{cite web author=9/2/10  url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1646050/20100819/story.jhtml  title=Chelsea Handler To Host The 2010 MTV Video Music Awards – Music, Celebrity, Artist News  publisher=MTV  date=August 19, 2010  accessdate=September 21, 2010}}</ref>  The event took place at the Nokia Theatre in Los Angeles on September 12, 2010.

In July 2007, Handler began starring on her own half-hour, late-night comedy series on E! titled Chelsea Lately. The show proved to be a hit by averaging more than a half-million viewers since its premiere (much more than the average for a late night cable program) and having clips on YouTube with more than one million views. In a 2008 interview, Handler said, "the worse the guests are, the more pathetic they are, the funnier the show is."<ref name="Jacob Bernstein">{{Cite journal title=Chelsea Handler, Insult Queen author=Jacob Bernstein publisher=W magazine date=December 1, 2008 url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/chelsea-handler accessdate=November 19, 2008}}</ref> Chelsea Lately was broadcast weekdays on the E! Network in the U.S., Canada, U.K, Australia, Germany, and New Zealand. After more than 1,000 episodes, the final episode of Chelsea Lately aired on August 26, 2014.<ref>{{cite press release url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2014/05/28/chelsea-lately-series-finale-to-air-tuesday-august-26-on-e/268330/ title=Chelsea Lately Starring Chelsea Handler Officially Cancelled by E!; Series Finale to Air Tuesday, August 26 last=Bibel first=Sara work=TV by the Numbers date=May 28, 2014 accessdate=May 28, 2014}}</ref>

The show had a spinoff, the mockumentary After Lately, in which the various cast members, writers, crew, guests and even "hangers-on" of Chelsea Lately are shown bickering over trivial matters and competing for airtime on the show. They also are shown seeking personal approval from Handler, who appeared in very few scenes of the program, and was always portrayed as having open and utter disdain for everyone beneath her. After Lately debuted on E! on March 6, 2011. Guest stars included: Reese Witherspoon, Jennifer Aniston, Jay Leno, Tori Spelling, Jenny McCarthy, Sharon Osbourne, Dave Grohl, Johnny Knoxville, and Jane Fonda.<ref>[http://www.eonline.com/on/personalities/bio/chuy.jsp Chuy Bravo – On E! Personalities   E! Online]. De.eonline.com. Retrieved on March 18, 2012.</ref>

In 2007, Handler appeared in the Internet-based program In the Motherhood with Leah Remini and Jenny McCarthy (since January 2008). On September 8, 2008, it was announced that ABC would be turning In The Motherhood into a series starring Jessica St. Clair, Megan Mullally, and Cheryl Hines.<ref>{{cite web url=http://inthemotherhood.com/node/185 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090208172112/http://inthemotherhood.com/node/185 archivedate=February 8, 2009 title=About the show  publisher=Inthemotherhood.com  accessdate=September 21, 2010}}</ref>

Are You There, Chelsea? was an NBC sitcom based on Handlers 2008 best-selling book Are You There, Vodka? Its Me, Chelsea, which aired from January 11 to March 28, 2012. On May 11, 2012, the network canceled it after only one season.<ref>{{cite web   url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2012/05/11/awake-are-you-there-chelsea-best-friends-forever-and-bent-canceled-by-nbc/133644/   title=Awake, Are You There Chelsea?, Best Friends Forever, and Bent Canceled by NBC   last=Seidman   first=Robert   date=May 11, 2012   work=TV by the Numbers   accessdate=May 11, 2012}}</ref> Laura Prepon played the main character Chelsea Newman, a cocktail waitress while Handler portrayed Chelseas Born-Again Christianity born-again Christian sister Sloane, a married, conservative, new mom who had little in common with her carefree sister. Dottie Zicklin (of Dharma & Greg) and Julie Larson (of The Drew Carey Show) were creators and executive producers. Handler served as executive producer, along with Tom Werner (of That 70s Show), Mike Clements (of The Life & Times of Tim), and Tom Brunelle (of Chelsea Lately).

In 2016, Handler filmed a documentary series for Netflix titled Chelsea Does, consisting of four episodes on marriage, Silicon Valley, racism, and drugs.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/chelsea-does-tv-review-858206 title=Chelsea Does: TV Review work=The Hollywood Reporter date= January 22, 2016 accessdate=January 22, 2016}}</ref> In May 2016, she began presenting her new Netflix Netflix Original Programing original Television comedy comedy talk show Chelsea (TV series) Chelsea.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/entertainthis/2016/03/17/chelsea-handler-new-show-on-netflix/81903844/ title=Chelsea Handler gives deets of her new Netflix show in a letter to herself author=Erin Jensen work=USA Today}}</ref> The first season streamed Wednesday, Thursday and Friday each week; season 2 streams every Friday.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/news/chelsea-handler-netflix-talk-show-title-guests-set-1201732831/ title=Chelsea Handler Announces Title for Netflix Talk Show: Chelsea author=Elizabeth Wagmeister work=Variety}}</ref> In February 2015, Handler said she envisioned a show more serious than Chelsea Lately: "I would like a healthy mix of everything that goes on around the world, mixed with interesting stuff in our country.
