Wendy Joan Williams-Hunter (born Wendy Joan Williams; July 18, 1964)<ref name="Wendy Williams.Biography"/> is an American television host, actress, author, fashion designer, and former radio personality. She has hosted the broadcast syndication nationally syndicated television talk show The Wendy Williams Show since 2008.

Prior to television, Williams was a radio DJ and host and quickly became known in New York as a "shock jockette". She gained notoriety for her on-air spats with celebrities and was the subject of the 2006 VH1 reality TV series The Wendy Williams Experience which broadcast events surrounding her radio show. She was inducted into the National Radio Hall of Fame in 2009.

She has written a The New York Times Best Seller list New York Times best-selling autobiography and six other books, and has created various product lines including a fashion line, a jewelry collection and a wig line. On her 50th birthday, the council of Asbury Park#Notable people Asbury Park renamed the street on which she grew up to Wendy Williams Way.

[http://njmonthly.com/articles/lifestyle/njmyway/mixing-media.html "Mixing Media"], New Jersey Monthly, June 9, 2008, Accessed July 22, 2008</ref> and, from 1982 to 1986, she attended Northeastern University (Boston, Massachusetts) Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts, where she graduated<ref name="vibe2003">{{cite book  title=Drama Queen url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2yYEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA160 accessdate=March 25, 2013 author= Lola Ogunnaike date= October 3, 2003 publisher=Vibe Media Group pages=160–}}</ref> with a B.A. in communications and was a DJ for the college radio station WRBB.

==Career==
===Radio===
Williams began her career working for WVIS in the United States Virgin Islands.<ref name="vibe2003"/> Less than a year later, she obtained an afternoon position at Washington, D.C.-based station WOL (AM) WOL. Williams commuted between DC and Queens, New York, to work an overnight weekend shift at WQHT.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.businessinsider.com/how-wendy-williams-got-her-start-2013-6 title=Every College Grad Should Hear Wendy Williams Story About Struggling To Make It Big accessdate=June 27, 2017}}</ref>

In 1989, Williams began at urban contemporary WRKS (now WEPN-FM) in New York City as a substitute disc jockey. WRKS hired her full-time for its morning show. A year later, Williams moved to an afternoon drive-time shift, eventually winning the Billboard magazine Billboard Award for "Best On-Air Radio Personality" in 1993.{{citation needed date=March 2013}} In December 1994, Emmis Broadcasting purchased WRKS and switched Williams to the companys other New York property, hip-hop formatted WQHT ("Hot 97"), as WRKS was reformatted into an urban adult contemporary outlet. She was fired from Hot 97 in 1998.<ref name=NYmagProfile/>

Williams was hired by a Philadelphia urban station, WUSL ("Power 99FM"). Her husband, Kevin Hunter, became her agent.<ref name=NYmagProfile/> She was very open about her personal life on air, discussing her miscarriages, breast enhancement surgery<ref name=NYmagProfile/> and former drug addiction.<ref name="vibe2003"/> She helped the station move from 14th place in the ratings to 2nd.<ref name=NYmagProfile/>

Williams has stated that Bill Cosby attempted to get her fired in 1991 and 1992. She also believes that Bill Cosby sexual assault allegations Cosby is guilty of sexual assault.<ref name="EURweb 2017">{{cite web   title=Wendy Williams Reveals Bill Cosby Tried to Get Her Fired... TWICE   website=EURweb   date=July 29, 2017   url=https://www.eurweb.com/2017/07/wendy-williams-reveals-bill-cosby-tried-to-get-her-fired-twice/   access-date=July 30, 2017}}</ref>

In 2001, Williams returned to the New York airwaves when WBLS hired her full-time for a syndicated 2–6 p.m. time slot. Williams friend, MC Spice of Boston, offered his voiceover services to the show, often adding short rap verses tailored specifically for Williams show. The New York Times stated that her "show works best when its elements – confessional paired with snarkiness – are conflated," and cited a 2003 interview with Whitney Houston as an example.<ref name="NYT2008">{{cite news  url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/13/arts/television/13drak.html?_r=0 title=TELEVISION; A Radio Shock Jock Whos Ready for TV last=Drake first=Monica date=July 13, 2008 work=The New York Times page=17 accessdate=April 15, 2013}}</ref> During the highly publicized interview<ref name="FOX">{{cite news  url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,389263,00.html title=Omarosa: Wendy Williams a Fake and a Phony  work=Fox News Channel accessdate=April 15, 2013 date=July 23, 2008}}</ref> that "went haywire" and included "a lot of bleeped language", Williams "asked [Houston], insistently, about her drug and spending habits".<ref name="Stanley2008">{{cite news  url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/22/arts/television/22stan.html title=Talk Show Is Less Talk, More Alpha-Female Action  author=Alessandra Stanley  date=July 22, 2008  work=The New York Times accessdate=April 15, 2013}}</ref>

By 2008, she was syndicated in Redondo Beach, California; Shreveport, Louisiana; Wilmington, Delaware; Toledo, Ohio; Columbia, South Carolina; Emporia, Virginia; Lake Charles, Louisiana; Tyler, Texas; and Alexandria, Louisiana, among other markets.{{citation needed date=March 2013}}

Williams left her radio show in 2009 to focus on her television program and spend more time with her family. She was inducted into the National Radio Hall of Fame.<ref name="Hall of fame">{{cite web title=National Radio Hall of Fame: Wendy Williams, Talkshow Host url=http://www.radiohof.org/talkshowhost/wendywilliams.html access-date=December 25, 2009 archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091218105840/http://www.radiohof.org/talkshowhost/wendywilliams.html archive-date=December 18, 2009 dead-url=yes df=mdy-all}}</ref>
