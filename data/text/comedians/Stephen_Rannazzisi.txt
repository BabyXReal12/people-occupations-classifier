Stephen Rannazzisi (born July 4, 1977)<ref name=tvg>{{cite web  title=Stephen Rannazzisi  url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/stephen-rannazzisi/bio/271444/  publisher= TV Guide TVGuide.com   accessdate=September 16, 2015}}</ref><ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/BWNhuXMj8bS/?taken-by=katieaselton&hl=en</ref> is an American actor, and stand-up comedian. He acted in the FXX (TV channel) FXX comedy series The League as Fantasy football (American) fantasy football league player Kevin MacArthur.

==Personal life==
Rannazzisi, born in Smithtown, New York Smithtown, New York (state) New York on July 4, 1977, briefly attended the Catholic St. Anthonys High School (New York) St. Anthonys High School in South Huntington, New York on Long Island, leaving after a year in what he called a mutual decision. He went on to graduate from Smithtown, New York#Smithtown High School Smithtown High School<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.danspapers.com/2014/06/steve-rannazzisi-performs-at-bay-street-theater-monday/  title=Steve Rannazzisi Performs at Bay Street Theater Monday  date= June 27, 2014  first=Brendan J.  last=OReilly   work = Dans Papers   location =Southampton, New York accessdate= September 16, 2015 archivedate= September 16, 2015  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150916191212/http://www.danspapers.com/2014/06/steve-rannazzisi-performs-at-bay-street-theater-monday/  deadurl=no}}</ref> in 1996.<ref>{{cite web   url =http://smithtownhighschool.org/alumni/4428377/stephen-rannazzisi.html   title=Stephen Rannazzisi: Smithtown High Class of 1996   publisher= Smithtown High School    accessdate= September 16, 2011}}</ref> Rannazzisi graduated from State University of New York at Oneonta, where he majored in communications.<ref name=nyt>{{cite news  url =https://www.nytimes.com/2015/09/17/arts/television/steve-rannazzisi-comedian-who-told-of-9-11-escape-admits-he-lied.html  title=Steve Rannazzisi, Comedian Who Told of 9/11 Escape, Admits He Lied  first= Serge F.   last =Kovaleski  date= September 16, 2015  accessdate= September 16, 2015 work =The New York Times}}</ref> He is of Italian and Irish descent.<ref>{{cite web last1=Donnelly first1=Shawn title=Steve Rannazzisi Talks World Cup, Italian Hair url=http://www.mademan.com/steve-rannazzisi-talks-world-cup-italian-hair/ accessdate=28 May 2016}}</ref>

===9/11 Controversy===
For 14 years, Rannazzisi said he worked in the South Tower of the World Trade Center (1973–2001) World Trade Center at Merrill Lynch, on the 54th floor during the September 11 attacks, and described his experience escaping death.<ref>{{cite podcast  url = http://www.wtfpod.com/podcast/episodes/episode_31_steve_rannazzisi_listener_email  title=WTF with Marc Maron: Episode 31 – Steve Rannazzisi / Listener Email  date=December 17, 2009  accessdate= September 16, 2015  archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20110521074332/http://www.wtfpod.com/podcast/episodes/episode_31_steve_rannazzisi_listener_email   archivedate= May 21, 2011 deadurl=no}} {{subscription}}</ref> <!---His future wife Tracy worked on the 24th floor, but was in mid-commute at the time of the attacks.--> He said the events inspired him to move to Los Angeles and pursue stand-up comedy. In September 2015, after being contacted by a reporter from The New York Times for an article debunking his claim,<ref name=nyt /> Rannazzisi admitted his story was a lie.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://twitter.com/SteveRannazzisi/status/644146873469546496  title=Center towers on 9/11....  publisher=Steve Rannazzisi verified Twitter account  date=September 16, 2015  accessdate=September 16, 2015  archivedate=September 16, 2015  archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6bapUYV3V?url=https://twitter.com/SteveRannazzisi  deadurl=no  df=}}</ref><ref>Rannazzisi, Twitter, [https://twitter.com/SteveRannazzisi/status/644147004856074240 "I don’t know why I said this."]</ref><ref>Rannazzisi, Twitter, [https://twitter.com/SteveRannazzisi/status/644147153565151232 "silence, I could somehow erase...."]</ref><ref>Rannazzisi, Twitter, [https://twitter.com/SteveRannazzisi/status/644147402652282882 "it is to the victims of 9/11...."]</ref> Rannazzisi was never employed by Merrill Lynch, which did not have offices in the World Trade Center then, and did not attend the State University of New York at Purchase, as he additionally had claimed.<ref name=nyt />
