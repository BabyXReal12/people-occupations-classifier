
Robert Rolla Woolsey (August 14, 1888 &ndash; October 31, 1938) was an United States American stage and screen comedian and half of the 1930s comedy team Wheeler & Woolsey.

==Early life ==
He was born in Oakland, California.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=XFnfnKg6BcAC&pg=PA1229&lpg=PA1229&dq Cullen, Frank; Hackman, Florence; McNeilly, Donald (2007). Vaudeville, Old & New. Routledge]. {{ISBN 0-415-93853-8}} p. 1229.</ref> Woolsey always had a slight build, and as a young adult he tried to capitalize on it by becoming a jockey. After he fell from a horse and sustained an injury, he quit racing and turned instead to the stage. In 1925 he was featured as "Mortimer Pottle" in W. C. Fieldss Broadway hit Poppy (1923 musical) Poppy.

==Wheeler and Woolsey ==
Woolsey was teamed with comedy star Bert Wheeler in 1928, for the Broadway musical Rio Rita (musical) Rio Rita. RKO Radio Pictures filmed the play in 1929, launching Wheeler and Woolsey as movie personalities. Of the twenty-two films Woolsey would make, twenty-one of them would be with his comedy partner, Wheeler.

Woolsey became terminally ill in 1937 and struggled to finish his last picture, High Flyers. He was then confined to bed for almost a full year, before dying of kidney failure in 1938.<ref>{{cite news  author=  coauthors=  title=Robert Woolsey, Film Comedian, 49. ==Personal life ==
From 1921 to his death in 1938, Woolsey was married to Mignonne Park Reed, who lived on to age 94.<ref name=Grave>{{cite web title=Mignonne R Reed Woolsey (1894-1989) FindAGrave Memorial url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/85552450 publisher=Ancestry.com accessdate=31 March 2013}}</ref><ref name=IMDb>{{cite web title=Robert Woolsey-Biography url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0941298/bio publisher=IMDb.com Inc accessdate=31 March 2013}}</ref>
