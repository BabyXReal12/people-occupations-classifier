
Mathew Frazer Horne (born 6 September 1978) is an English actor, comedian, television presenter, and narrator. He is best known for appearing on several BBC sketch shows and sitcoms, most notably Gavin & Stacey (as List of Gavin & Stacey characters#Gavin Shipman Gavin Shipman), The Catherine Tate Show, 20 Things to do Before Youre 30, Teachers (UK TV series) Teachers, Horne and Corden, and Bad Education (TV series) Bad Education.<ref name="tin"/>

==Early life==
Horne was born on 6 September 1978<ref name="flix"/><ref name="rc"/> in Nottingham,<ref>{{cite news  url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/my-secret-life-matthew-horne-actor--comedian-31-2004026.html   location=London   work=The Independent   title=My Secret Life: Matthew Horne, Actor & comedian, 31   date=19 June 2010}}</ref> the son of Brian Horne and Glenis Reynolds. He grew up in Burton Joyce Burton Joyce, Nottinghamshire.<ref>{{cite web author=Ben Machell  url=http://cma.staging-thetimes.co.uk/tto/arts/tv-radio/article2821977.ece  title=Mat Horne on loving success and hating celebrity publisher=Cma.staging-thetimes.co.uk  date=26 November 2010  accessdate=2014-01-16}}</ref> Horne has an elder brother, Daniel. He attended Burton Joyce Primary School. He studied Advanced Level (UK) A level performing arts at Southwell Minster School in Nottinghamshire, and has a degree in drama from the University of Manchester.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/moslive/article-560503/Matthew-Horne-Are-having-laugh.html  title=Matthew Horne: Are you having a laugh? &#124; Mail Online  publisher=Dailymail.co.uk  date=19 April 2008  accessdate=2014-01-16  location=London}}</ref> Here, Horne met Bruce Mackinnon in the first few weeks of term; they realised that they shared a similar sense of humour, but did not write material together until the third year of their studies.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/comics/m/2868/mathew_horne  title=Mathew Horne, comedian : Chortle : The UK Comedy Guide  publisher=Chortle  date=29 November 2010  accessdate=2014-01-16}}</ref>

==Career==
Horne began his career as a comedian and later became one half of the comedy duo Mat and MacKinnon<ref name="M&amp;M"/>&nbsp;– first performing at the Edinburgh Festival in 2000.<ref name="chortle"/> He was spotted at the festival by Catherine Tate who invited him to perform in The Catherine Tate Show.
Since then Horne has continued to appear in several Catherine Tates Nan specials. He reprised his role as Jamie in two festive episodes of Catherine Tates Nan in 2015.

In 2003, he starred in Channel 4s 20 Things to do Before Youre 30 and a year later earned the role of Ben,<ref name="teachers"/> an atheist Religious Education RE teacher, in the Channel 4 series Teachers (UK TV series) Teachers.

He also appeared in the first series of ITV Network ITV production Doc Martin. In 2005, Horne appeared in an episode of The Smoking Room, playing Dominic the nephew of Janet who was conducting a psychological study of the employees.

In 2007 he starred in the BBC sitcom Romans Empire and in November that year appeared in comedy sketches in The Kylie Show on ITV1 alongside Kylie Minogue, playing her assistant.<ref name="sun" /> Horne was also a guest star in the second series of Robin Hood (2006 TV series) Robin Hood.<ref name="rt" />

From 2007 to 2010 he starred in the double BAFTA winning series Gavin & Stacey as main character List of Gavin & Stacey characters#Gavin Shipman Gavin Shipman, a kind-hearted young man who meets up with a girl he meets through their work phone calls and falls in love with her. The programme ran for three series with a Christmas special and received widespread critical acclaim.

In 2008, Horne appeared in a modern adaptation of the Three Billy Goats Gruff as part of the BBCs Fairy Tales season.<ref name="deacon" /> He also narrated the ITV2 series The Passions of Girls Aloud.<ref name="ew" />

Horne starred in the film Lesbian Vampire Killers alongside James Corden<ref name="lf" /> and made his stage debut in a 2009 revival of Entertaining Mr Sloane at the Trafalgar Studios.<ref name="london theatre" /> On 3 April 2009 it was reported that Horne collapsed on-stage during a West End performance of the play.<ref name="bbc030409" /> He remained on-stage until medical assistance arrived, at which point he was taken to hospital. A statement released soon after said that he was exhausted after working flat out for 3 months and it was later revealed that he was suffering from a virus.<ref name="telegraph070409" />

Horne also guest-starred in the video for the Maccabees 2009 free-download single "No Kind Words"

On 18 February 2009, he presented the 2009 BRIT Awards alongside James Corden and Kylie Minogue.

In 2010, he appeared in an episode of Miss Marple (ITV TV series) Miss Marple, named "The Secret of Chimneys", as Bill Eversleigh. Horne appeared alongside fellow Gavin & Stacey star Ruth Jones.

He played the Culture Club drummer, Jon Moss, in the 2010 BBC docudrama about the teenage years of Boy George, Worried About the Boy.<ref name="bbc210810"/>

In 2016 he toured British theatres with Catherine Tate in The Catherine Tate Show Live.{{citation needed date=December 2016}}

Appeared as young Mr. Grace in "Are You Being Served?" 2016.

==Work with James Corden==
{{Main Horne & Corden Lesbian Vampire Killers}}
Since working on the BBC sitcom Gavin & Stacey, he has collaborated a number of times with its co-creator James Corden<ref name="interview"/>

Horne and Corden presented the Big Brother spin-off show Big Brothers Big Mouth<ref name="bbbm"/> as guests in 2007 and were then regulars for the run covering E4 (channel) E4s Big Brother: Celebrity Hijack

Together they presented the NME Shockwaves Awards<ref name="blog"/> in 2008 and a section of Comic Relief in March 2009.

In 2008 Horne and Corden toured the UK with a stand-up show in preparation for their BBC sketch show Horne & Corden.<ref name="h&amp;c"/> The first episode was broadcast on show of 10 March 2009 on BBC Three. It was presented in front of a live audience, in a style reminiscent of Morecambe and Wise or The Two Ronnies. A variety of pre-filmed sketches and live performances were included.

2009 also saw the release of Lesbian Vampire Killers, a comedy horror film. Reviews of the film were largely negative.<ref name="bbc200309"/> It holds a 29% approval rating on review aggregator Rotten Tomatoes with an average score of 4/10.<ref name="rotten"/> James Christopher of The Times described it as "profoundly awful", stating that it is an "instantly forgettable lads mag farce" and an "appalling waste of a perfectly decent title".<ref name="james"/> Allan Hunter of the Daily Express called it "badly written and hastily executed" and "takes all the easy options of bad taste, bosoms and body fluids".<ref name="hunter"/> Anthony Quin writing in The Independent gave the film 1 star out of 5, describing it as woeful and stating that Horne and Corden had "overstretched their appeal" and looked in danger of becoming todays Hale and Pace.<ref name="quinn"/> Peter Bradshaw of The Guardian described the film as "mostly pretty awful, but there are one or two crass laughs".<ref name="bradshaw">[https://www.theguardian.com/film/2009/mar/19/lesbian-vampire-killers-film-review Lesbian Vampire Killers], The Guardian, 2009-03-20. Retrieved on 2009-03-21</ref>

The Sun (United Kingdom) The Sun, however, gave it a positive review; Sun reviewer the Sneak gave Lesbian Vampire Killers a rating of 3 out of 5, saying that "other reviewers will undoubtedly say that LVK is another British comic flop but the Sneak wont be driving that stake into its heart".<ref name="sun190309"/> Similarly, Nicholas Yanes of Scifipulse.net found it to be "a great B film" worth becoming a cult classic.<ref name="scifi"/> Whilst on the comedy panel show The King is Dead in September 2010, Corden commented that watching the film would be too harsh a punishment for prisoners being held at Guantanamo Bay and that it was "a pile of shit".<ref name="king"/>

Horne appeared alongside Corden in the CGI film Planet 51, with a minor part voicing a soldier, Vesklin.

==Personal life==
Horne is a fan of Tottenham Hotspur F.C. Tottenham Hotspur FC, and is often seen at White Hart Lane.
