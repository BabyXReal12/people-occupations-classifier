
Estelle Getty (given names née Scher; also known as Estelle Gettleman; July 25, 1923 – July 22, 2008) was an American Actor actress and comedian, who appeared in film, television, and theatre. She was best known for her role as Sophia Petrillo on The Golden Girls from 1985 to 1992, which won her an Emmy and a Golden Globe, on The Golden Palace from 1992 to 1993, and on Empty Nest from 1993 to 1995. In her later years, after retiring from acting, she battled Dementia with Lewy bodies Lewy body dementia.<ref name="guardian-obit">{{cite news  last=Carlson first=Michael  title=Obituary: Estelle Getty  url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2008/jul/24/television.television  accessdate=October 13, 2013  newspaper=The Guardian  date=July 24, 2008}}</ref>

==Early life==
Estelle Getty was born Estelle Scher on July 25, 1923 to Sarah and Charles Scher. Her parents were History of the Jews in Poland Polish Jewish immigrants who owned a glass store in New York City.<ref>1930 United States Federal Census</ref> She had a sister, Roslyn, and a brother, David.<ref name=filmr>{{cite web title=Estelle Getty Biography (1923-) url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/49/Estelle-Getty.html work=Film Reference accessdate=October 13, 2013}}</ref> Getty got her start in the Yiddish theater and also as a comedian in the Catskills borscht belt resorts.<ref>{{cite news title=Goodbye Golden Girl: Comic actress Estelle Getty dies at 84 url=http://www.haaretz.com/jewish-world/2.209/goodbye-golden-girl-comic-actress-estelle-getty-dies-at-84-1.250261 accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=Haaretz date=July 23, 2008}}</ref>

==Career==

Getty played the mother of Harvey Fiersteins character in the original Broadway theatre Broadway run of Torch Song Trilogy.<ref>{{cite news last=Simonson first=Robery title=Estelle Getty, Star of "Golden Girls," Dies at 84 url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/119713-Estelle-Getty-Star-of-Golden-Girls-Dies-at-84 archive-url=https://archive.is/20131013122146/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/119713-Estelle-Getty-Star-of-Golden-Girls-Dies-at-84 dead-url=yes archive-date=October 13, 2013 accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=Playbill date=July 22, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite news   author=Gussow, Mel   title=Theatre Review: Fiersteins Torch Song   url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/11/01/theater/theater-fierstein-s-torch-song.html   work=The New York Time   date=November 1, 1981   accessdate=June 24, 2008}}</ref>

Getty is best known for her role as Sophia Petrillo on the popular 1980s sitcom The Golden Girls.<ref>{{cite news last=Weber first=Bruce title=Estelle Getty, Golden Girls Matriarch, Dies at 84 url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/23/arts/television/23getty.html accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=The New York Times date=July 23, 2008}}</ref> Her character was the Sicily Sicilian mother of Dorothy Zbornak, played by Bea Arthur Beatrice Arthur, although Getty was, in fact, one year younger than Arthur.<ref>{{cite news last=Lansden first=Pamela title=Estelle Getty Zings for Her Supper as Bea Arthurs Zap-Happy Mom on Golden Girls url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20093266,00.html accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=People (magazine) People date=March 31, 1986}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Lawson first=Sarah title=Great Moments in Age-Inappropriate Casting url=http://6thfloor.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/11/19/great-moments-in-age-inappropriate-casting/ accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=The New York Times date=November 19, 2012}}</ref> In 1988, the role earned Getty the Emmy Award for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series Outstanding Supporting Actress.<ref>{{cite news last=Bernstein first=Adam title=Estelle Getty, 84; Golden Girl Actress Won an Emmy Award url=https://articles.washingtonpost.com/2008-07-23/news/36768221_1_barry-gettleman-arthur-gettleman-comedy-series accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=The Washington Post date=July 23, 2008}}</ref>

During her time on The Golden Girls, Getty, along with Steve Delsohn, co-wrote an autobiography entitled If I Knew Then, What I Know Now... So What? (Contemporary Books, 1988).<ref name=filmr/> She further capitalized on her success by releasing an exercise video for senior citizens in 1993.<ref>{{cite news last=Glenn Haas first=Jane title=Estelle Getty leads a workout for seniors url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/1993-12-31/features/1993365117_1_estelle-getty-picaud-exercise accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=Baltimore Sun date=December 31, 1993}}</ref>

In 1992, following her seven-season role on The Golden Girls, Getty, with Betty White and Rue McClanahan returned as their characters Sophia Petrillo, Rose Nylund and Blanche Devereaux respectively, in the spin-off series, The Golden Palace. The new show saw the three remaining cast members from the original series invest in a Miami hotel. The series was cancelled after just one season. Getty reprised her role of Sophia once again for a guest appearance in Nurses (TV series) Nurses, and for the final time when she joined Empty Nest, another spin-off series from The Golden Girls. She appeared in the series for the final two seasons.

Getty made numerous guest appearances on a diverse variety of popular television series, including Fantasy Island (1977 TV series) Fantasy Island, Cagney & Lacey, Blossom (TV series) Blossom, Touched by an Angel, Mad About You and The Nanny. Her numerous film roles include Tootsie, Mask (film) Mask and Stop! Or My Mom Will Shoot, alongside Sylvester Stallone, which earned her a Razzie Award for Worst Supporting Actress.

==Personal life ==
Getty attended Seward Park High School in New York City.<ref name="Luther">{{cite news last=Luther first=Claudia title=Estelle Getty, 84; Golden Girls actress brought humor, depth to mother roles url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/newsletter/la-me-getty23-2008jul23,0,3217229.story accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=Los Angeles Times date=July 23, 2008}}</ref>

She was married to Arthur Gettleman (whose name she adapted into her stage name) from December 21, 1947 until his death in 2004.<ref name="Luther"/> The couple had two sons: Carl Gettleman, who lives in California, and Barry Gettleman, who lives in Florida.<ref name="getty-faq">{{cite web title=Frequently Asked Questions url=http://www.estellegetty.com/faq.html work=EstelleGetty.com accessdate=November 16, 2013 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110412025419/http://www.estellegetty.com/faq.html archivedate=April 12, 2011 df=mdy-all}}</ref>

In 1991, as later reported in Star (magazine) Star magazine, Getty helped to nurse her 29-year-old nephew Steven Scher, who was near death and suffering from the final stages of HIV/AIDS AIDS.<ref>{{cite web title=Estelle Getty url=http://www.gayglobe.us/estellegetty.html work=Gay Globe Magazine Média TV accessdate=November 16, 2013}}</ref> Because Schers parents lived in England and his friends were no longer able to care for him in Greensboro, North Carolina, Getty had him flown to California and admitted to hospice care. He died in January 1992.

==Illness and death==
File:Estelle Getty Grave.jpg thumb upright Grave of Estelle Getty at Hollywood Forever Cemetery
In 2000, Getty stopped making public appearances after revealing she had Parkinsons disease and osteoporosis. Upon reassessment, Gettys diagnosis was revised to Dementia with Lewy bodies Lewy body dementia, a form of dementia that has similar symptoms to both Parkinsons and Alzheimers disease.<ref name="getty-faq"/> Golden Girls cast members who attempted to talk to her on the phone or in person found that she couldnt remember them, or anything about the show. Bea Arthur said that even during production of the series, Getty had so much trouble remembering her lines that they should have known something was wrong from the beginning.<ref>{{cite news title=A Golden Girls Tragic Battle with Dementia url=http://et.tv.yahoo.com/tv/2004/11/19/goldengirlsegetty/ accessdate=October 13, 2013 newspaper=Entertainment Tonight date=November 19, 2004 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20050208032459/http://et.tv.yahoo.com/tv/2004/11/19/goldengirlsegetty/ archivedate=February 8, 2005 deadurl=yes df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Fonseca first=Nicholas title=Golden Girls: A 20th Anniversary Oral History url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,1100651,00.html accessdate=October 14, 2013 newspaper=Entertainment Weekly date=April 24, 2009 page=3}}</ref>

In 2003, Lifetime (TV network) Lifetime television hosted a Golden Girls reunion, The Golden Girls: Their Greatest Moments. In 2008, the show was awarded the Pop Culture award at the Sixth Annual TV Land Awards. Getty did not participate in either project due to declining health.

Getty died of complications from Lewy body dementia on July 22, 2008, three days before her 85th birthday.<ref name="guardian-obit" /> She is interred at Hollywood Forever Cemetery in Los Angeles.
