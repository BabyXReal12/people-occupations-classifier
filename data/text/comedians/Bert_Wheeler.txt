
Albert Jerome "Bert" Wheeler (April 7, 1895 &ndash; January 18, 1968) was an American comedian who performed in Broadway theatre, American comedy feature films, and vaudeville acts. He was the comedy partner of Robert Woolsey, and together they formed a successful double act called Wheeler & Woolsey.<ref name=obit/>

==Biography ==
Wheeler was born in Paterson, New Jersey on April 7, 1895.<ref name=obit/>

He worked with Robert Woolsey on Broadway until their film debut in 1929, Rio Rita (1929 film) Rio Rita, established them in the Hollywood film industry.<ref name=obit/><ref>{{cite news  author=Mardaunt Hall  coauthors=  title=Rio Rita (1929)  url=https://www.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B03EFDF1E3EE13ABC4F53DFB6678382639EDE  quote=The acting is uniformly capable. Robert Woolsey is excellent as the fast-talking, deep-voiced, narrow-shouldered Lovett. Bert Wheeler does exceedingly well as Chick Bean.   newspaper=The New York Times  date=October 7, 1929  accessdate=2015-01-05 }}</ref>

In the early 1940s, after Robert Woolsey had died, Bert Wheeler struggled to restart his career. Their friend and former film costar Dorothy Lee (actress) Dorothy Lee agreed to tour with him in a vaudeville act. He also worked on radio on The Frank Sinatra Show (radio program) programs starring Frank Sinatra.<ref>{{cite news title=Swing Shift url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/2876522/the_brooklyn_daily_eagle/ newspaper=The Brooklyn Daily Eagle date=October 7, 1943 page=15 via = Newspapers.com accessdate = July 24, 2015}} {{Open access}}</ref>

He continued to work off and on through the 1960s. His later appearances were mostly on American television; his last theatrical films were two slapstick short films for Columbia Pictures, filmed in 1950 and produced by Jules White. In 1955 Wheeler co-starred with Keith Larsen in the Columbia Broadcasting System CBS Western (genre) western television series series Brave Eagle; Wheeler played the "half-breed" Smokey Joe, known for his tall tales and tribal wisdom.

Bert Wheeler died of emphysema in New York City on January 18, 1968.<ref name=obit>{{cite news  author=  coauthors=  title=Bert Wheeler, Vaudeville Comic With Rubbery Face, Dead at 72. His Over 50-Year Career in Show Business Spanned Films, the Follies and TV  url=https://www.nytimes.com/1968/01/19/archives/bert-wheeler-vaudeville-comic-with-rubbery-face-dead-at-72-his-over.html  quote=  newspaper=The New York Times  date=January 18, 1968  accessdate=2015-01-04 }}</ref>

==Marriages ==
Wheeler was married five times. His only child, with Speer, was Patricia Anne Wheeler.
* Margaret Grae (m. 27 April 1915 to 15 November 1926) divorced.
* Bernice Speer (m. 15 April 1928 to 19 February 1936) divorced, they had 1 child.<ref>{{cite news  author=  coauthors=  title=Bert Wheeler Is Divorced.  url=https://www.nytimes.com/1936/02/20/archives/bert-wheeler-is-divorced.html  quote= Mrs. Bernice S. Wheeler won an interlocutory divorce today from Bert Wheeler, screen comic, after testifying that his actions saddened her. He said he didnt want to live with me, she testified. Custody of the couples daughter, Patricia, was granted to Mrs. Wheeler. ...  newspaper=The New York Times  date=February 20, 1936  accessdate=2015-01-05 }}</ref>
* Sally Haines (m. 26 February 1937 to 1939) divorced.<ref>{{cite news  author=  coauthors=  title=Wife Sues Bert Wheeler  url=https://www.nytimes.com/1939/05/13/archives/wife-sues-bert-wheeler.html  quote=Bert Wheeler ... was sued for divorce today by Sally Haines ... The Wheelers married in Beverly Hills Feb. 26, 1937, and separated April 20, 1939  newspaper=The New York Times  date=May 13, 1939  accessdate=2015-01-04 }}</ref>
* Patsy Orr (c1940 to c1950) divorced.
* Olga Desmondae Rieman (m. 1951 - 8 August 1966) to her death.
