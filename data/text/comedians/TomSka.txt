
Thomas James Ridgewell (born 27 June 1990),<ref name=":0">{{Cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2013/apr/07/youtube-uk-20-online-video-bloggers title=YouTube UK: 20 of Britains most popular online video bloggers last=Fox first=Killian last2=Lewis first2=Tim date=2013-04-06 website=the Guardian access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> known online as TomSka, is an English YouTube celebrity, writer, producer, director, actor, and filmmaker, as well as founder of the Turbopunch Ltd. media production company. He is known for his animated comedy web film series asdfmovie{{efn "asdf" pronounced {{IPAc-en ˈ æ z d ə f}}}} and webtoons Eddsworld and Crash Zoom.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.thevideoink.com/features/comedies-tomska-wants-better-youtube-fans/ title=Between Comedies, TomSka Also Educates His YouTube Fans - VideoInk date=2014-08-17 website=VideoInk language=en-US access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> As of July 2018, his YouTube channel has over 5.1 million subscribers and his videos have garnered over 1.1 billion views.

==Early life and education==
As a child, Ridgewell began making short films using his parents video camera. Not long after YouTube was established, Ridgewell created CakeBomb, a website which he posted his video creations, including his viral animated video series asdfmovie and his friend Edd Goulds Eddsworld. Ridgewell graduated from University of Lincoln,<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.lincolnshireecho.co.uk/University-Lincoln-students-guest-present-Radio-1/story-22836679-detail/story.html title=Former University of Lincoln students to guest present on Radio 1 date=2014-08-27 website=Lincolnshire Echo access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref>, where he created a series of unofficial "advertisements" for the university which received millions of views.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.brandrepublic.com/news/1085410 title=Appointment to view: The University of Lincoln goes viral website=www.brandrepublic.com access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref>

He was raised a Jehovahs Witness, but no longer practices this faith.<ref>{{Citation last=DarkSquidge title=The Day My Faith Died date=2015-10-07 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XXRK76x_D8Q accessdate=2016-06-13}}</ref> Despite this, he has expressed a belief in a god.<ref>{{Citation last=DarkSquidge title=7 Facts About TomSka date=2013-01-28 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HZ2wwLh1dhU accessdate=2016-06-13}}</ref>

==YouTube career==
As a professional YouTuber, Ridgewell earns money from revenue from his videos on the website.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/the-tom-ridgewell-show-the-22yearold-making-10000-a-month-from-his-bedroom-on-youtube-8530893.html title=The Tom Ridgewell show: the 22-year-old making £10,000 a month from his bedroom on YouTube date=2013-03-12 website=Evening Standard language=en-GB access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> He also has made short films for BBC and Comedy Central. He has been discussed and interviewed in relation to this, especially when the medium of online video was in its infancy.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/13136416 title=YouTube star TomSka makes thousands every month last=reporter first=Dan WhitworthNewsbeat technology access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> He has also appeared as a guest on the BBCs The One Show, and has appeared as a radio and TV guest internationally.<ref>{{Citation last=thepod96 title=TomSka and Bing on The One Show - BBC One date=2011-11-23 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D-hLCjzjOvs accessdate=2016-06-13}}</ref>

In 2008, Ridgewell released the first installment of asdfmovie, an animated sketch comedy series featuring very short clips of minimally-detailed characters in Surreal humour surreal and occasionally Black comedy darkly humorous situations. There have been ten episodes as of April 2017, as well as two "deleted scene" videos, a promotional video with Marmite, and four music videos; "I Like Trains", "Mine Turtle", "Everybody Do The Flop", and "The Muffin Song".<ref>{{Citation last=TomSka title=asdfmovie10 date=2017-04-01 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=foFKXS6Nyho accessdate=2017-04-02}}</ref> The song "Beep Beep Im a Sheep" by Canadian musician Todd "LilDeuceDeuce" Bryanton with vocals by Ridgewell and Gabriel "BlackGryph0n" Brown, released alongside asdfmovie10, was featured in the dance rhythm game Just Dance 2018.<ref>https://www.destructoid.com/random-time-beep-beep-like-a-sheep-in-just-dance-2018-456534.phtml</ref>

In 2012, Ridgewell founded a media production company, TurboPunch Ltd., located in London. Ridgewell currently works there alongside co-writer Eddie Bowley and video editor Elliot Gough.<ref>http://www.datalog.co.uk/browse/detail.php/CompanyNumber/07912863/CompanyName/TURBOPUNCH+LIMITED</ref>

Also in 2012, he took charge of the production of Eddsworld after the shows original creator, Edd Gould, died of Acute lymphoblastic leukemia leukemia. He left the series in 2016, handing the show over to Edds family. In 2013, Ridgewell was featured in YouTubes first "Comedy Week".<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/comedy/comedy-news/10068492/YouTubes-Comedy-Week-shows-how-much-comedy-has-changed.html title=YouTubes Comedy Week shows how much comedy has changed website=Telegraph.co.uk access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> He was featured on the cover of Wired UK in February 2013 as part of a feature titled "How YouTube Reinvented the Entertainment Business"<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.wired.co.uk/magazine/archive/2013/02/features/talent-tube title=Talent Tube: how Britains new YouTube superstars built a global fanbase website=WIRED UK language=en-GB access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref> and was selected by YouTube as a guest host for its Geek Week series in August. The BBC announced that Ridgewell will be appearing as a guest presenter on the Dan and Phil show from September 2014 on BBC Radio 1.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2014/r1-youth-presenters title=BBC - New youth presenters for Radio 1 - Media Centre website=www.bbc.co.uk access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2013/apr/07/youtube-superstars-new-generation-bloggers title=YouTube superstars: the generation taking on TV – and winning last=Lewis first=Tim date=2013-04-06 website=the Guardian access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/media/tv-radio/radio-1-to-hire-youtubefamous-vloggers-to-broadcast-online-9686208.html title=Radio 1 to hire YouTube-famous vloggers to broadcast online date=2014-08-22 website=The Independent language=en-GB access-date=2016-06-13}}</ref>

In January 2015, Ridgewell launched a new webtoon, Crash Zoom, on his TomSka channel. It is animated by Ben "Wonchop" Smallman, who also animated some of the asdfmovie episodes.<ref>{{Citation last=DarkSquidge title=IM NOT AN ANIMATOR date=2015-06-26 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mcrsyXyd34A accessdate=2016-06-13}}</ref> It features three young characters, Lucy, Ben, and Kate, in their various surreal exploits.<ref>{{Citation last=TomSka title=CRASH ZOOM - Movie Massacre date=2015-01-09 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TnyBXvB3_ho accessdate=2016-06-13}}</ref>

== DarkSquidge ==
DarkSquidge is the name of Ridgewells secondary channel on YouTube created in October 2007.<ref name=":1" /> As of March 2018, the channel has over 780,000 subscribers and more than 101 million views.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/user/DarkSquidge/about title=DarkSquidge channel - about section last= first= date= website= publisher= access-date=}}</ref> The content that Ridgewell uploads on this channel differs greatly from that of his main channel. While his main channel consists of sketch comedy and animations,<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/user/TomSka/about title=TomSka channel - about section last= first= date= website= publisher= access-date=}}</ref> DarkSquidge shows behind the scenes footage of TomSka related projects and videos, as well as Video blog vlogging. Ridgewell also engages in social commentary on this platform, covering a range of topics such as mental health, sexual health, and body positivity. The YouTuber uploaded weekly vlogs to the channel dubbed as "Last Week". After ending "Last Week", Tom then made a monthly vlogging series called "Last Month".
