Carol Denise "Niecy" Nash (Married and maiden names née Ensley;<ref>According to the State of California. California Birth Index, 1905-1995. Center for Health Statistics, California Department of Health Services, Sacramento, California. At Ancestry.com</ref> born February 23, 1970) is an American comedian, model, actress, and producer, best known for her performances on television.

Nash hosted the Style Network show Clean House from 2003 to 2010, for which she won an Daytime Emmy Award Emmy Award in 2010. As an actress, she played the role of Deputy Characters on Reno 911!#Dep. Williams Raineesha Williams in the Comedy Central comedy series Reno 911! (2003–2009). Nash received two Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series and a Critics Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series nominations for her performance as nurse Denise "DiDi" Ortley in the HBO comedy Getting On (U.S. TV series) Getting On (2013–2015). She also starred as Lolli Ballantine on the TV Land sitcom The Soul Man (2012 TV series) The Soul Man (2012–2016), and played Denise Hemphill in the Fox horror-comedy anthology series, Scream Queens (2015 TV series) Scream Queens (2015–2016).<ref name=Scream>{{cite web url=http://www.eonline.com/news/628684/niecy-nash-joins-scream-queens-and-she-s-going-to-kick-butt title=Niecy Nash Joins Scream Queens and Shes Going to Kick Butt work=E! Online accessdate=17 July 2015}}</ref> In 2017, she began starring as Desna Simms, a leading character, in the TNT crime comedy-drama, Claws (TV series) Claws.

Nash has also played a number of roles in films and has made many guest appearances on television shows. In 2014, Nash played the role of civil rights activist Richie Jean Jackson in the historical drama film Selma (film) Selma directed by Ava DuVernay.

==Early life==

M.A.V.I.S.s mission is to inform the public of the School violence violence children encounter on school campuses. ===Early works===
Nash made her professional acting debut in the 1995 film Boys on the Side.<ref name=tvg/> On television, she later guest-starred in NYPD Blue, Judging Amy, Reba (TV series) Reba, Girlfriends (U.S. TV series) Girlfriends, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation and ER (TV series) ER. She also appeared in 1999 film Cookies Fortune, and had a recurring role of CBS drama series, City of Angels (2000 TV series) City of Angels, in 2000.

From 2003 to 2009, Nash played the role of Deputy Characters on Reno 911!#Dep. Williams Raineesha Williams and T.T. on the Comedy Central comedy series Reno 911!. In addition, Nash hosted Clean House on the Style Network from 2003 to 2010, as well as providing the voice of List of Slacker Cats characters Mrs. Boots on the ABC Family animated series Slacker Cats, and starred as Rhonda, opposite Jerry OConnell, in the short-lived Fox Broadcasting Company Fox sitcom Do Not Disturb (TV series) Do Not Disturb in 2008. She also has guest starred on The Bernie Mac Show as Bernie McCullough Bernies sister Bonita from 2003 to 2005. Nash won a Daytime Emmy in 2010 as the producer/host of "Clean House: The Messiest Home in the Country" in the category of Outstanding Special Class Special.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.niecynash.com/archives/2010/06/29/i-won-a-2010-daytime-emmy-award/  title=I won a 2010 Daytime Emmy Award!! from Niecy Nash Official Web Site  publisher=Niecynash.com  date=  accessdate=2014-06-05}}</ref> On August 4, 2010 Nash announced she was leaving Clean House on the Style Network but the show will continue without her.<ref>Niecy Nash, [http://www.niecynash.com/archives/2010/08/04/the-end-of-clean-house/ The End of Clean House], August 4, 2010</ref>
