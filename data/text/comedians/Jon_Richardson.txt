Jon Joel Richardson (born 26 September 1982)<ref name="Last">{{cite web  title=Jon Richardson  url=http://www.last.fm/music/Jon+Richardson  website=Last FM  accessdate=24 August 2013 }}</ref> is an English comedian. He is known for his appearances on 8 Out of 10 Cats and 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown and his work as co-host with Russell Howard on BBC Radio 6 Music BBC 6 Music.

==Early life and career==
Richardson was born and raised in Lancaster, Lancashire, and attended Ryelands Primary School and Lancaster Royal Grammar School.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.thevisitor.co.uk/news/local/night-of-laughs-for-hospice-appeal-1-5834807  title=Night of laughs for hospice appeal  website=The Visitor  date=8 July 2013}}</ref> He left the University of Bristol after taking Hispanic Studies for a year and a half, and subsequently spent some time working as a chef.<ref name=2UT /> He then decided to pursue a career in comedy, and lived with fellow comedians Russell Howard, Mark Olver (comedian) Mark Olver and John Robins (comedian) John Robins for a year in Bristol.<ref name="littlebitocd">{{cite web  url=http://www.channel4.com/programmes/jon-richardson-a-little-bit-ocd  title=A Little Bit OCD  website=Channel 4}}</ref>

Richardson entered and won his heat of the BBC New Talent Comedy Search in May 2003. He also reached the semi-finals of the Laughing Horse New Act of the Year competition in 2004.<ref>{{cite web  first=Alex  last=Petty  url=http://www.laughinghorsecomedy.co.uk/newactcomp/pastyears.htm  title=Laughing Horse New Act of the Year  website=Laughinghorsecomedy.co.uk  date=  accessdate=20 June 2012}}</ref> Quickly developing throughout 2004, he was one of six acts to reach the final of the J2O Last Laugh Comedy Search. Having impressed a sell-out crowd in Bristol, he marked the end of his first year in comedy with a performance at the Criterion Theatre in Londons West End of London West End. Judges Jasper Carrott and Dave Spikey were both "extremely impressed" with Richardsons performance.

In 2006, Richardson appeared in The Comedy Zone at the Edinburgh Festival. He also completed a 60-date British and Irish tour with Alan Carr, and made an appearance on Paramounts The Comedy Store. In 2007, Spatula Pad (Richardsons first solo show at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe) earned him a Best Newcomer nomination at the Edinburgh Comedy Awards.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.comedyawards.co.uk/best-newcomer  title=Best Newcomer 2009  website=Edinburgh Comedy Awards  date=  accessdate=8 May 2010}}</ref> Richardson won the 2008 Chortle Awards Chortle Award for Best Breakthrough Act,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/features_static/awards/chortle.php  title=The UK Comedy Guide  website=Chortle  date=  accessdate=8 May 2010}}</ref> and performed Dogmatic (his second solo show) at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe. He later took this show around the country in his first solo tour. He appeared as a team captain in Simon Mayos 2008 series Act Your Age on BBC Radio 4.

Richardson co-hosted a Sunday morning radio show on BBC Radio 6 Music BBC 6 Music with fellow comedian and friend Russell Howard, and after Howard left, he continued to present the show himself until 7 March 2010.

In early 2009, Richardsons show This Guy at Night was nominated for the Edinburgh Comedy Awards main prize.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.comedyawards.co.uk/2009_shortlist.asp  title=2009 Shortlist  website=Edinburgh Comedy Awards  date=29 August 2009  accessdate=8 May 2010  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090918201350/http://www.comedyawards.co.uk/2009_shortlist.asp  archivedate=18 September 2009  df=dmy-all }}</ref> That year, he appeared on the BBC music quiz show Never Mind the Buzzcocks. He also appeared on Have I Got News for You in December 2009 and October 2010, and was a guest on David Mitchell (comedian) David Mitchells The Bubble (game show) The Bubble.

Richardson appeared on the BBC Radio 5 Live show Fighting Talk in February 2010. He was curator of The Museum of Curiosity for its third series. On 21 March 2010, he presented a BBC Three programme about compulsions and strange habits, entitled Different Like Me. Richardson has performed at comedy festivals in Leeds, Guildford and Bristol, and has headlined university gigs for Off the Kerb and Avalon. He has also master of ceremonies compèred in the French Alps, headlined in Barcelona, and performed a one-man show in Greece. In 2010 he also performed at the Melbourne Comedy Festival.<ref name=2UT>{{cite web  url=http://www.2uptop.com/news/The-Jon-Richardson-Interview/  title=The Jon Richardson Interview  website=2-UpTop  date=April 2010}}</ref>

Starting with the 11th series of Channel 4s panel show 8 Out of 10 Cats, Richardson took over from Jason Manford as a team captain. Filming for the series began in June 2011. Its Not Me, Its You!, Richardsons first book, was published during the same month. Later in 2011, he became a regular comic on the Channel 4 show Stand Up for the Week, which was hosted by Kevin Bridges during its second series. Upon Bridgess departure, Richardson took over as host, a role he left after series 4 in 2012. Since January 2012, he has also been a regular on Channel 4s 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown, where it was revealed that he is unable to count to 100 in 30 seconds.<ref>Channel 4, 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown, 29 December 2014</ref>

Richardson presented a 2012 documentary entitled A Little Bit OCD, in which he studied the lives of people diagnosed with Obsessive–compulsive disorder OCD and worried that he might have the same condition; he was indeed revealed to exhibit symptoms of OCD, but did not show signs of the accompanying dysfunction. The show won the 2013 Mind Award in the documentary category.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.mind.org.uk/news-campaigns/news/scott-mills-announces-winners-at-20th-mind-media-awards,-sponsored-by-virgin-money-giving/  title=Scott Mills announces winners at 20th Mind Media Awards, sponsored by Virgin Money Giving  website=Mind (charity) Mind  date=5 November 2013}}</ref>

On 19 November 2012, Richardson released his first stand up DVD, Funny Magnet. The performance was recorded at the Apollo Theatre in Hammersmith on 9 September 2012. The DVD also features Richardsons set from Live at the Apollo (TV series) Live at the Apollo and audio commentary with his housemate, comedian Matt Forde.

In December 2012, Channel 4 broadcast The Real Mans Road Trip: Sean & Jon Go West,<ref>{{Cite web  url=http://www.channel4.com/programmes/the-real-mans-road-trip-sean-jon-go-west  title=The Real Mans Road Trip – Sean and Jon Go West  website=Channel 4}}</ref> a two-part series in which Richardson and his 8 Out of 10 Cats colleague Sean Lock travelled to Louisiana to experience the local culture and lifestyle. The show was filmed in September 2012.

Richardson began Work in Progress shows in late 2013, and in 2014 took his new show Nidiot on the road culminating in a DVD of the same name.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://jonrichardsoncomedy.com/2014/12/01/nidiot-dvd-available-now/  title=Nidiot DVD available now  website=jonrichardsoncomedy.com  date=1 December 2014}}</ref> In August 2013 he headlined the comedy stage at the V Festival.

Appearing on Radio 5s Fighting Talk on 11 January 2014, Richardson said his worst ever gig was a couple of months previously at The Dome Leisure Centre Doncaster Dome. He claimed the initial smattering of applause quickly reduced through his time on stage.<ref>BBC Radio 5, Fighting Talk, 11 January 2014</ref>

==Personal life==
Richardson lived in Swindon for five years,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.gigglebeats.co.uk/2012/11/jon-richardson-nothing-i-say-deserves-repeating-in-posterity/  title=Jon Richardson: "Nothing I say deserves repeating in posterity"  website=Giggle Beats first=Peter  last=Dixon date=11 November 2012}}</ref> then in Surbiton, Royal Borough of Kingston upon Thames Kingston upon Thames, London, with fellow comedians Matt Forde and Danny Buckler. He also lived with comedians John Robins and Russell Howard <ref name="littlebitocd" /> In a 2012 interview with Woman magazine, he mentioned he had bought a house in the Lake District for his mother.<ref>{{Cite web  url=http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10152278449810562&set=o.2260900000&type=3&theater  title=Jon Richardson Appreciation Society  website=Facebook  date=24 November 2012}}</ref> He is also a vegetarian,<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.natgeotraveller.co.uk/smart-travel/features/the-funny-man-jon-richardson/ title=Interview: Jon Richardson {{!}} National Geographic Traveller (UK) website=www.natgeotraveller.co.uk language=en-US access-date=2017-03-23}}</ref> and a football fan and supporter of Leeds United F.C. Leeds United.

Richardson married comedian Lucy Beaumont (comedian) Lucy Beaumont in April 2015 after dating for about two years. Their first child, a daughter named Elsie, was born in September 2016.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2014/sep/13/jon-richardson-grows-up-ocd  title=Jon Richardson: I didnt have any sex, I didnt do any drugs  first=James  last=Kettle  newspaper=The Guardian  date=13 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://podbay.fm/show/821623813/e/1429965395  title=Elis James and John Robins XFM Podcast, Episode 63  date=25 April 2015  website=XFM}}</ref>
