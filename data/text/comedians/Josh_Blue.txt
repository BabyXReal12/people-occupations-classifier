Josh Blue (born November 27, 1978) is an United States American comedian.  He was voted the Last Comic Standing on NBCs reality show Last Comic Standing during its Last Comic Standing 4 fourth season, which aired May–August 2006.<ref name="About">{{cite web url=http://specialchildren.about.com/b/2006/08/11/josh-blue-wins-last-comic-standing.htm title=Josh Blue wins "Last Comic Standing" last=Mauro first=Terri date=August 11, 2006 publisher=About.com accessdate=2009-03-26}}</ref> Blue has cerebral palsy, and much of his self-deprecating humor is centered on this.

==Early life==
Blue was born in Cameroon where his father, Walter Blue, was a professor of Romance languages at Hamline University teaching during a Mission (station) mission. Blue grew up in Saint Paul, Minnesota, and graduated from Como Park Senior High School in 1997. He began his career as a comedian while seeking a creative writing degree at The Evergreen State College.<ref name="LCSbiography" />

During college, he went back to Africa to volunteer as an intern at Parc de Hahn, a zoo in Senegal.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.miusa.org/ncde/stories/blue  title=Archived copy  accessdate=2014-01-28  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201203753/http://www.miusa.org/ncde/stories/blue  archivedate=2014-02-01  df= }}</ref> At one point for a joke on a busy weekend, Josh locked himself in an empty animal exhibit for 8 hours. A bewildered crowd threw him bananas and peanuts anyway, to which Josh quips "that was the best day I ate in Senegal!"<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.takemehomepodcast.com/44-josh-blue/ title=44 – Josh Blue - Take Me Home Podcast work=Take Me Home Podcast accessdate=3 September 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
Blue got his start in comedy doing open mic sets while attending The Evergreen State College. Audiences reacted enthusiastically to his self-deprecating humor, and Blue started making a name for himself on the comedy circuit. In 2002, he won the prestigious Comedy Works New Talent Search contest. He has appeared several times on Comedy Centrals Mind of Mencia.<ref name="LCSbiography" /> Blue won $10,000 for finishing in first place at 2004 Las Vegas Comedy Festivals 2004 Royal Flush Comedy Competition.<ref name="LCSbiography" />

After winning the 2006 season of NBCs reality show Last Comic Standing,<ref name="About"/> he was the first comedian to do stand-up on The Ellen DeGeneres Show.<ref name="Zanies" /> His other television credits include Live with Regis and Kelly, Comics Unleashed, plus numerous appearances on Fox Broadcasting Company Fox, CBS, American Broadcasting Company ABC, and MSNBC.<ref name="Zanies" />

He appeared in the 2009 low-budget horror film Feast III: The Happy Finish.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm2333144/ title=Josh Blue work=IMDb accessdate=3 September 2015}}</ref>

Josh was voted the 13th best comedian by viewers in Comedy Centrals Stand-up Comedy Showdown 2010.

Blue appeared in Boulder band Rose Hill Drives music video  "The Psychoanalyst".<ref>{{cite av media url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=66oqIUnvP_E&feature=grec_index title=Rose Hill Drive - Psychoanalyst featuring Josh Blue date=7 July 2011 work=YouTube accessdate=3 September 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Blue currently resides in Denver, Colorado,<ref name="LCSbiography" /> with his two children: a son, Simon, and a daughter, Seika.

Blue played a part in the 2004 U.S. Paralympic Games Paralympic Paralympic football soccer team.<ref name="LCSbiography" /> He also creates and sells sculptures and paintings. 

Blue has cerebral palsy,<ref name="About" /> specifically classified as spastic hemiplegic cerebral palsy, and many of his jokes center on living with his disability, how he deals with it, and how other people view him.<ref name="LCSbiography">{{cite web url=http://www.nbc.com/Last_Comic_Standing/comics/josh_blue.shtml title=Last Comic Standing biography publisher=National Broadcasting Corporation NBC accessdate=2009-03-26}}</ref> Blue appeared on Last Comic Standing to "make people aware of the fact that people with disabilities can make an impact."<ref name="LCSbiography" /> He coined the term "palsy punch" during his final set of the final round of the show, when he said that the palsy punch is effective in a fight because "first of all, they dont know where the punch is coming from, and second of all, neither do I." One of Blues competitors said he has "an unreasonable amount of likability" while another of his competitors said "he is just a good guy".<ref name="Zanies">{{cite web url=http://www.nashville.zanies.com/news.php?NewsSection=Josh+Blue title=Season 4 WINNER of NBCs "LAST COMIC STANDING" publisher=Zanies Comedy Night Club accessdate=2009-03-26}}</ref> Blue also joked that signing an autograph takes 45 minutes and that to write down his phone number he has to find a "big ol stack of paper."
