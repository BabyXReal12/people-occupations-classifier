Kathryn Hahn (born July 23, 1973)
is an American actress and comedian. She began her career on television, playing Lily Lebowski in the NBC crime drama series Crossing Jordan (2001–07). Hahn went on to appear as a supporting actress in a number of major comedy films, including roles in the films How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days (2003), Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy (2004), Step Brothers (film) Step Brothers (2008), Our Idiot Brother (2011), Were the Millers (2013), The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013 film) The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013), and The Visit (2015 American film) The Visit (2015).

As a lead actress, Hahn starred in the 2013 comedy-drama film Afternoon Delight (film) Afternoon Delight, directed by Jill Soloway. She had a starring role in the comedy film Bad Moms (2016), and its sequel, A Bad Moms Christmas (2017). She has also appeared in a number of dramatic films, including Revolutionary Road (film) Revolutionary Road (2008), This Is Where I Leave You (2014), Tomorrowland (film) Tomorrowland (2015), The Visit (2015 American film) The Visit (2015), and Captain Fantastic (film) Captain Fantastic (2016). In 2017, she received a nomination for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series for her work in Transparent (TV series) Transparent (2014–present).

==Early life==
Hahn was born in Westchester, Illinois, the daughter of Karen and Bill Hahn.
She has German, Irish, and English ancestry. She was raised Catholic.
Hahn grew up in Cleveland Heights, Ohio
and attended Beaumont School (Ohio) Beaumont School.
She attended Northwestern University, where she obtained a bachelors degree in theatre before attending Yale University.
While attending Yale School of Drama, Hahn starred as Sally Bowles in Cabaret (musical) Cabaret and as Célimène in Molières play The Misanthrope.{{citation needed date=May 2017}}

==Career==
===Early work===
Hahns first appearance on any television program was Hickory Hideout, a local puppet show for children for then-NBC Owned-and-operated television stations in the United States owned-and-operated station WKYC in Cleveland. (By the time of its cancellation, Hickory Hideout was being transmitted on all of NBCs O&amp;O stations.) While attending a theater festival, she met an NBC casting director,{{Who date=July 2018}} who, in turn, introduced her to creator/producer Tim Kring. Hahn impressed Kring so favorably that he created the character of Lily Lebowski in Crossing Jordan specifically for her.
