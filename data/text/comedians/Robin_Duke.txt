
Robin Duke (born March 13, 1954) is a Canadian actress, comedian, and voice actress. Duke may be best known for her work on the television comedy series Second City Television SCTV and, later, Saturday Night Live.  She co-founded Women Fully Clothed, a sketch comedy troupe which toured Canada. She teaches writing as a faculty member at Humber College in Toronto and has a recurring role playing Wendy Kurtz in the sitcom Schitts Creek.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://creativearts.humber.ca/faculty/robin-duke.html  title=Robin Duke Biography  publisher=Humber College  access-date=October 30, 2017}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Duke was born in Toronto, Ontario.<ref>{{cite web last1=Wright first1=Megh title=Saturday Nights Children url=http://splitsider.com/2012/04/saturday-nights-children-robin-duke-1981-1984/ website=Splitsider}}</ref> She went to high school with Catherine OHara at Burnhamthorpe Collegiate Institute in Etobicoke; they first met in home room class. In 1976 Duke joined OHara as part of the Toronto version of the stage comedy troupe The Second City, while also making several appearances in the troupes television series, SCTV.  Duke became a regular on SCTV from 1980–81. She joined the cast of Saturday Night Live in 1981 when OHara suddenly dropped out of that show.

=== NBCs Saturday Night Live (SNL) (1981–1984)===
Duke was an actress and writer on SNL from 1981 to 1984.<ref>{{cite book last=Shales first=Tom author-link=Tom Shales title=Live From New York: The Complete, Uncensored History of Saturday Night Live as Told by Its Stars, Writers, and Guests date=2015 publisher=Back Bay Books isbn=978-0316295062 page=184}}</ref> Duke was hired in place of Catherine OHara, who was chosen as a cast member when Dick Ebersol was hired following the termination of Jean Doumanian and most of her cast members. OHara backed out after SNL writer Michael ODonoghue yelled at cast and crew members for ruining the show during its sixth season. Dukes most popular character was probably The Whiners Wendy Whiner, a woman who, with her equally whiny husband (Joe Piscopo), annoyed everyone she met.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://mentalfloss.com/article/50632/post-snl-careers-5-castmembers  title=The Post-SNL Careers of 5 Featured Players  first=Erik  last=van Rheenen  website=Mental Floss  date=May 18, 2013}}</ref> She is also remembered for appearing with Mr. T as the equally bizarre "Mrs. T" in a faux commercial for the real-life product "Mr. and Mrs. Ts Bloody Mary (cocktail) Bloody Mary Mix".

===Film acting credits===
Duke went on to appear in such films as Club Paradise (1986), Groundhog Day (film) Groundhog Day (1993), Stuart Saves His Family (1995) and Portrait of a Serial Monogamist (2015), along with many television guest appearances.

===Voice-over acting credits===
Duke is also the voice of Penny in the childrens animated television series George and Martha. Her other voice roles include:
*Bob and Margaret
*Atomic Betty
*Marvin the Tap-Dancing Horse

===Women Fully Clothed (2004)===
In 2004, Duke along with Kathy Greenwood Kathryn Greenwood, Debra McGrath, Jayne Eastwood and Teresa Pavlinek created Women Fully Clothed, a sketch comedy troupe. The group has toured the United States, Canada and appeared in Scotland at the Edinburgh Festival.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.nowtoronto.com/stage/story.cfm?content=174868  title=Women Fully Clothed: Older and Hotter  first=Glenn  last=Sumi  newspaper=Now (newspaper) Now  date=May 6, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news  url=https://beta.theglobeandmail.com/arts/theatre-and-performance/more-bladder-busting-laughs-from-women-fully-clothed-quartet/article629133/  title=More bladder-busting laughs from Women Fully Clothed quartet  first=Brad  last=Wheeler  newspaper=The Globe and Mail  date=June 15, 2011}}</ref>

===Schitts Creek (2016)===
In 2016, Duke joined the cast of Schitts Creek playing dress shop owner Wendy Kurtz. The sitcom reunites Duke with her old SCTV castmate Eugene Levy.
