
Matthew Paul "Matt" Mira (born August 5, 1983) is an United States American podcast host, stand-up comedian and session guitarist in Los Angeles. He has been a co-host of The Nerdist Podcast since 2010.<ref name="leijon">Leijon, Eric (July 28, 2011). [http://www.montrealmirror.com/wp/2011/07/28/the-dean-of-dweebs/ The dean of dweebs.] Montreal Mirror</ref><ref name="burrows">Burrows, Leah (April 8, 2011). [http://news.bostonherald.com/entertainment/arts_culture/view.bg?&articleid=1329168 Chris Hardwick singles out his nerdiness.] Boston Herald</ref>

==Life and career==
Mira was born in Stoneham, Massachusetts on August 5, 1983 and grew up in Lowell, Massachusetts. He is the youngest of four children. His parents are John and Mary Mira. He has two older sisters Kimberly and Julie, and an older brother John. He was raised Catholic, and attended Lowell Catholic High School. After high school he attended local colleges. He left Massachusetts for Los Angeles in 2007 to pursue a career in comedy.

From 2008 to 2010, while working as an Genius Bar Apple Genius, Mira produced the podcast Comedy and Everything Else featuring Todd Glass and Jimmy Dore.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.zvents.com/z/mountain-view-ca/matt-mira--performers--8432545 title=Zvents Performers: Matt Mira}}</ref> Since February 2010, Mira has co-hosted The Nerdist Podcast, with Jonah Ray and Chris Hardwick.<ref>{{cite web first=Chris last=Hardwick url=http://www.nerdist.com/2010/02/the-nerdist-podcast-now-a-thing/ title=The Nerdist Podcast: Now A Thing! publisher=nerdist.com date=February 8, 2010}}</ref>  The podcast was named one of 2010s best by The A.V. Beginning in July 2012, Mira co-hosted Attack of the Show up until its final episode on January 23, 2013.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.g4tv.com/videos/59991/matt-mira-on-tsa-robots-google/ title=Mira Co-Host AoTS}}</ref>

A video featuring a golf-cart running over Matt has been featured on the TV shows tosh.0, Country Fried Home Videos, and Worlds Dumbest....

Beginning in March 2012, Matt began co-hosting an occasional podcast with film producer Scott Mosier called FEaB (Four Eyes and Beard), a joint production of The Nerdist Podcast Nerdist Industries and SModcast SModcast.com.

As of September 2013, Matt co-hosts James Bonding with Matt Gourley (and guests), an Earwolf podcast where they discuss James Bond movies.

Since October 2013, Mira has been writing for the Chris Hardwick hosted @midnight on Comedy Central.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm4579890/?ref_=ttfc_fc_wr1 title=Mira writes on @Midnight}}</ref>

On June 19, 2015, Kevin Smith announced via Facebook that he and Mira "will provide a commentary track for every single episode of Frasier" on a new podcast entitled Talk Salad & Scrambled Eggs - Frasier Reconsidered w/ Matt Mira & Kevin Smith.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=10152834327816930&substory_index=0&id=6985046929 title=Kevin Smith – New Podcast! @mattmira & I will provide a commentary track for every single episode of Frasier on Talk Salad & Scrambled Eggs!}}</ref>

As of 2016, Mira currently is writing for The Goldbergs (2013 TV series) The Goldbergs on ABC. He also hosts a podcast with his wife Doree Shafrir about their journey about making a baby with science called Matt & Dorees Eggcelent Adventure.

Starting January 8, 2017, Matt began co-hosting the podcast Star Trek: The Next Conversation with fellow The Goldbergs writer Andrew Secunda.

On September 22, 2017, Matt was named host of After Trek, a live after-show companion to Star Trek: Discovery broadcast on CBS All Access.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.startrek.com/article/matt-mira-to-host-after-trek title=Matt Mira To Host After Trek}}</ref>
