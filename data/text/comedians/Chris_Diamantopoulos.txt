Christopher Diamantopoulos (born May 9, 1975; {{lang-el Χριστόφορος "Κρις" Διαμαντόπουλος }}) is a  Canadian actor and comedian.

==Career==
Diamantopoulos began appearing in TV commercials and professional theatre productions at the age of 9, and by the time he turned 18, he had left home to partake in a series of United States U.S. national tours.
A short time later, he worked on Broadway theatre Broadway where he played leading parts in The Full Monty (musical) The Full Monty (2002) and Les Misérables (musical) Les Misérables (2003).
Diamantopoulos attended East York Collegiate Institute.

He portrayed Robin Williams in Behind the Camera: The Unauthorized Story of Mork &amp; Mindy alongside costar Erinn Hayes. He has guest-starred in several series, including Kevin Hill, Charmed, The Sopranos, Nip/Tuck, and Boston Legal. In 2007, he appeared opposite Debra Messing in The Starter Wife (miniseries) The Starter Wife, where he played the role of Rodney, the interior decorator. 

Diamantopoulos had a recurring role on State of Mind (TV series) State of Mind. He also guest-voiced on American Dad! in 2009.

On the eighth season of 24 (TV series) 24, he played President of the United States US President Allison Taylors White House Chief of Staff Chief of Staff Rob Weiss. He played Frank Sinatra in The Kennedys (miniseries) The Kennedys. Diamantopoulos played Moe Howard in the 2012 The Three Stooges (2012 film) The Three Stooges film. He also is the current spokesman for the firm Charles Schwab.
He understudied Donny Osmond on tour of Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat.

He had recurring roles on the NBC sitcoms Up All Night (TV series) Up All Night, as Maya Rudolphs on and off lover, Julian; The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office, as Brian, a former member of the shows documentary film crew; and on Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development, as Marky, Lindsays face-blind environmentalist boyfriend. He is also the voice of Mickey Mouse for the Mickey Mouse (TV series) eponymous animated series.
.

He has recently featured in the UK/US television sitcom Episodes (TV series) Episodes playing the part of TV network boss Castor Sotto, Mr. Chris in the TV adaptation of About a Boy (TV series) About a Boy, and billionaire investor Russ Hanneman in  Silicon Valley (TV series) Silicon Valley.

In 2017 Diamantopoulos starred in the musical Waitress (musical) Waitress on Broadway.

==Personal life==
Diamantopoulos grew up splitting his time between Canada and Greece. He is of Greeks Greek descent and is a practicing member of the Greek Orthodox Church Greek Orthodox church.
He met his wife, actress Becki Newton, in a New York City Subway subway station in New York City New York. The couple married on May 12, 2005. Newton gave birth to their first child, a boy, in November 2010.
In 2014 they had a daughter.
