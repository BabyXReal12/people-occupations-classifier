John Morgan (September 21, 1930 – November 15, 2004) was a Wales Welsh-born Canadians Canadian comedian.

Born in Aberdare, Wales Aberdare, Wales, Morgan played numerous characters on the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation CBC sketch comedy television program television series Royal Canadian Air Farce from 1993 to 2001 and its predecessor on CBC Radio, including perpetually disgusted Scotland Scotsman Jock McBile, socialite Amy De La Pompa, and monosyllabic Mike, from Canmore Mike from Canmore, Alberta Canmore, as well as satire satirical portrayals of such prominent individuals as Herb Gray, Deborah Grey, and Boris Yeltsin. He was a co-founder of the series, and critics called him its heart.

Morgan wrote the television pilot pilot for the popular Canadian situation comedy sitcom, King of Kensington. He and writing partner Martin Bronstein were also co-creators of the CBC radio series, Funny You Should Say That. He had his own BBC Radio series called Its All in the Mind of John Morgan.

In 2000, he was nominated for a Canadian Comedy Award. Along with the original cast of Air Farce Live Air Farce&nbsp;– Roger Abbott, Don Ferguson (actor) Don Ferguson and Luba Goy&nbsp;– Morgan received the Governor Generals Performing Arts Award for Lifetime Artistic Achievement in 1998.<ref>{{cite web url=http://ggpaa.ca/award-recipients/1998/the-royal-canadian-air-farce-(roger-abbott-1946-20.aspx publisher=Governor Generals Performing Arts Awards Foundation accessdate=4 February 2015}}</ref>

Before launching his comedy career in 1966, Morgan worked as a journalist, editing editor, teacher, and pub owner.

On November 15, 2004, Morgan died at his home in Toronto from a myocardial infarction heart attack at the age of 74.
