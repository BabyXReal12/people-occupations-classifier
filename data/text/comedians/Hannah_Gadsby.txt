
Hannah Gadsby is an Australian comedian and writer. She rose to prominence after winning the national final of the Raw Comedy competition for new comedians in 2006. She has toured internationally and appeared on Australian and New Zealand television. In 2018, Gadsbys Netflix special, Nanette (show) Nanette, brought her to the attention of international audiences.<ref name=NYTimes-Nanette-2018>{{cite news last1=Zinoman first1=Jason title=Introducing a Major New Voice in Comedy (Who Also Attacks Comedy) url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/19/arts/hannah-gadsby-comedy-nanette.html work=The New York Times date=19 March 2018 language=en}}</ref><ref name=Guardian-Nanette-Review-2017>{{cite news last1=Logan first1=Brian title=Hannah Gadsby review – electrifying farewell to standup url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2017/aug/19/hannah-gadsby-review-nanette work=The Guardian date=19 August 2017 language=en}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Gadsby grew up in Smithton, Tasmania Smithton in Tasmania, the youngest of five children.<ref name="HG" /><ref>[http://www.suchsmallportions.com/feature/hannah-gadsby-interview-%E2%80%9Ci-talk-about-my-shows-theyre-ships%E2%80%9D Hannah Gadsby interview: I talk about my shows like theyre ships   Such Small Portions 10 November 2011]. Suchsmallportions.com (10 November 2011). Retrieved on 2017-05-14.</ref> She attended Smithton, Tasmania Smithton High School from 1990 to 1995, Launceston College, Tasmania Launceston College and the University of Tasmania in Hobart. She later graduated from the Australian National University in 2003 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Art History and Curatorship.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.anu.edu.au/students/program-administration/program-management/graduate-search title=Graduate search date=12 November 2014 publisher=}}</ref> Before her comedy career, Gadsby worked as a cinema projectionist and as a tree planter on a farm.

== Career ==
=== Art lectures ===
Although Gadsby is best known for her comedy work, she also combines her comedy talents with her art qualifications (she has a degree in Art History and Curatorship) by presenting comedy art tours in conjunction with the National Gallery of Victoria. Since 2009, Gadsby has presented tours with themes such as paintings of the Holy Virgin, Dadaism, Modernism, Impressionism and the nude in art. Gadsby also gives talks on art and opens exhibitions.<ref>[http://www.theage.com.au/entertainment/art-and-design/reclining-nudes-get-standup-treatment-20130319-2gd5d.html Reclining nudes get stand-up treatment   The Age 20 March 2013. === Acting ===
Gadsby co-starred on the Australian ABC1 ABC television network show Adam Hills Tonight (formerly known as Adam Hills in Gordon Street Tonight). She had regular segments called On This Day and Hannah Has A Go and also featured on the couch, contributing as host Adam Hills interviews his guests.<ref name="ABC">[http://www.abc.net.au/tv/adamhillsIGST/about/default.htm Adam Hills Tonight   ABC Television website. She has also appeared as a guest on Good News Week, Spicks and Specks (TV series) Spicks and Specks,<ref name="ABC" /> and TV3 (New Zealand) New Zealand TV 3s 7 Days (New Zealand) 7 Days.

She has written and presented a three-part series on ABC TV, Hannah Gadsbys Oz, which aired in March 2014.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/tv/programs/hannah-gadsbys-oz/ ABC TV Programs March 2014. Retrieved 21 October 2014]. Abc.net.au. Retrieved on 14 May 2017.</ref>

She featured as Hannah, a fictional version of herself, in the second, third and fourth seasons of fellow comedian Josh Thomas (comedian) Josh Thomass series Please Like Me in 2015.

=== Stand-up ===
As a comedian, Gadsby was a regular on the local and international comedy festival circuit for over 10 years. In 2018, Netflix released Nanette (show) Nanette, which brought her to the attention of an international audience.<ref name="Guardian-Nanette-Review-2017" />
