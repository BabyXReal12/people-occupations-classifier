
John Paul Pinette ({{IPAc-en p ɪ ˈ n ɛ t}} {{respell pi NET}}) (March 23, 1964 – April 5, 2014) was an American actor, Broadway performer, and stand-up comedian. He toured the comedy club circuit beginning in the 1980s and appeared in cinema and on television. Besides stand-up, Pinette did impressions of Alvin and the Chipmunks The Chipmunks, Elvis Presley, Gollum from The Lord of the Rings, Hervé Villechaize (Tattoo from Fantasy Island), an Ewok, actor Marlon Brando (notably Brandos role in The Godfather),  as well as various ethnic accents. He occasionally sang, for example "Over the Rainbow" from The Wizard of Oz (1939 film) The Wizard of Oz, and "Dont Cry for Me Argentina," in his stand-up routines.

==Career==
Pinette got a break when asked to tour with Frank Sinatra. Pinette was a regular guest on The Tonight Show and The View (talk show) The View.<ref name="JohnPinette.com"/>

Pinette was in the films Duets (film) Duets, Simon Sez, The Last Godfather, Dear God (film) Dear God, and Junior (1994 film) Junior. He was a regular on the series Parker Lewis Cant Lose and, in 1998, starred as the Carjacking carjacking victim in the The Finale (Seinfeld) final episode of Seinfeld. In 2004, Pinette was Bumpo in Artisan Entertainments The Punisher (2004 film) The Punisher, starring Thomas Jane and John Travolta.

Pinette was named Stand-Up Comedian of the Year by the American Comedy Awards in 1999 and received a Gemini Awards Gemini Award nomination for his televised performance at Montreals Just for Laughs Comedy Festival in 2000.<ref>{{cite web  title=John Pinette  publisher=Comix at Foxwoods Comedy Club  url=http://www.comixatfoxwoods.com/bio/john-pinette  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121018182233/http://www.comixatfoxwoods.com/bio/john-pinette  archivedate=October 18, 2012  deadurl=yes  accessdate=September 29, 2014  df=mdy-all }}</ref> At the time of his death, he still held the record for the highest-selling one-person show in the history of Just for Laughs.<ref>{{cite web  title=In Memory of John Pinette – A Just for Laughs Legend  url=http://www.hahaha.com/en/memory-john-pinette  publisher=Just for Laughs  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140929042531/http://www.hahaha.com/en/memory-john-pinette  archivedate=September 29, 2014  deadurl=no}}</ref>

In 2004, Pinettes stand-up material was featured in Comedy Centrals animated series Shorties Watchin Shorties. In 2007, Pinette performed at the 42nd annual The Jerry Lewis MDA Labor Day Telethon Jerry Lewis MDA Telethon. He performed at the Edinburgh Comedy Festival in Edinburgh, Scotland, in 2008, and toured in cities in 2010 beginning in April. During this tour, Pinette recorded a Comedy Central special titled John Pinette: Still Hungry. The taping took place at the The Vic Theatre Vic Theatre in Chicago. He was the host of All You Can Eat (TV series) All You Can Eat, a TV series taking a humorous look at American cuisine. The show debuted on the H2 (TV network) H2 network in the United States in late June 2013.

==Death==
Pinette was pronounced dead at 2:30&nbsp;p.m. on April 5, 2014, at the Station Square Sheraton Station Square Hotel in Pittsburgh. The Allegheny County Medical Examiners office said Pinette had been suffering from liver and heart disease. An autopsy was not performed, as Pinettes personal doctor signed off on his cause of death as pulmonary embolism.<ref name=Hotel>{{cite news last=Polke first=Clarece title=Comedian John Pinette, 50, dies in Pittsburgh hotel url=http://www.post-gazette.com/local/city/2014/04/06/Comedian-John-Pinette-50-dies-in-Pittsburgh-hotel/stories/201404060212 accessdate=April 6, 2014 newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette date=April 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news title=John Pinette Dead: Comedian And Seinfeld Actor Dies At 50 url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/04/06/john-pinette-dead_n_5101932.html accessdate=April 6, 2014 newspaper=The Huffington Post date=April 6, 2014}}</ref>
