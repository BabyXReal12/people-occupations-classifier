Jack McBrayer ({{IPAc-en m ə k ˈ b r ɛər}}; born May 27, 1973) is an American actor and comedian, who gained national exposure for his portrayal of characters on Late Night with Conan OBrien and as Kenneth Parcell in 30 Rock, for which he was nominated for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series at the 61st Primetime Emmy Awards.<ref>{{cite press release  title=Academy of Television Arts and Sciences 61st Primetime Emmy Awards  url=http://www.emmys.tv/sites/emmys.tv/files/61stemmys_noms_1.pdf  publisher=Academy of Television Arts and Sciences.  date=2009-07-16  format=PDF  accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref> During his time at The Walt Disney Company, he has voiced a major character in the 2012 film Wreck-it Ralph and later its Ralph Breaks the Internet: Wreck-It Ralph 2 2018 sequel, as well as recurring characters in Wander Over Yonder, Phineas and Ferb, Puppy Dog Pals, and the American Broadcasting Company ABC sitcom The Middle (TV series) The Middle.

==Personal life==
McBrayer was born in Macon, Georgia, and moved to Conyers, Georgia when he was 15. He studied theater administration at the University of Evansville, Indiana.<ref name=NYT>{{cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/04/06/arts/television/06itzk.html title=Not-So-Alter Ego of Kenneth the Page, Still Soaking It In  author=Itzkoff, Dave date=2008-04-06 work= The New York Times  accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref> From 1995 to 2002 he worked at The Second City and IO Theater in Chicago,<ref name="NYT"/> an experience that introduced him to 30 Rock creator Tina Fey and prepared him for his role on her show.<ref name="chicago09">{{cite web  url= http://www.chicagonow.com/blogs/show-patrol/2009/10/jack-mcbrayer-kenny-the-page-30-rock.html   title= Jack McBrayer or Kenny the Page? Same thing, says 30 Rock star   date= October 9, 2009   publisher= ChicagoNow   work= Show Patrol (blog)   accessdate=2010-07-20}}</ref>

==Career==
McBrayer appeared in over 80 sketches on Late Night with Conan OBrien (2002–04), often lampooning his own Southern United States Southern upbringing by playing stereotypical hillbilly characters. On August 10, 2007 and September 20, 2008 he made cameo appearances on Late Night playing Kenneth Parcell from 30 Rock. During the first appearance, OBrien remarked to him, "I thought you were above this now." McBrayer later reprised his role as Kenneth twice (November 24, 2009 and January 13, 2010) after OBriens switch to The Tonight Show with Conan OBrien The Tonight Show, and appeared on OBriens The Legally Prohibited from Being Funny on Television Tour Legally Prohibited From Being Funny on Television Tour at stops in Eugene, Oregon, Universal City, California (on the Universal Studios lot where OBrien taped Tonight), and the final tour stop in Atlanta, in his home state of Georgia (U.S. state) Georgia.<ref>{{cite web url=http://nymag.com/daily/entertainment/2010/04/conans_first_tour_stop_self-pl.html title=Conan’s First Tour Stop: Triumph, Self-Pleasuring Pandas, and Spoon date=2010-04-13 work= New York (magazine) New York  accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.deadline.com/2010/04/coanan-back-at-nbc-universal-for-two-nights-bad-ideas-are-being-greenlit-here/ title=Conan Back at NBC Universal (For Two Nights): "Bad Ideas Are Being Greenlit" Here author=Andreeva, Nellie date=2010-04-25 website=Deadline Hollywood accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref>

In 2008, he was featured throughout the video for "Touch My Body (Mariah Carey song) Touch My Body" by Mariah Carey. McBrayer said he accidentally hit Carey in the face with a Frisbee during the shooting for the video.<ref name=NYT/> McBrayer also appears in the first episode in the fifth season of Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job! as a spokesman in a spoof advertisement for the fictional "Diarrhea-phragm."<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/tim-eric-awesome-show-great-job-season-5-premiere,38658/ title=Tim & Eric Awesome Show, Great Job! - Season 5 premiere author=Heisler, Steve date=2010-03-01 work= The A.V. Club accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref> He worked as a voice actor in the American animated series Ugly Americans (TV series) Ugly Americans<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/37205/ugly-americans-season-finale-kong-queens title=Ugly Americans Season Finale: Kong of Queens author=Moore, Debi date=2010-04-27 work= Dread Centralteacher accessdate=2010-11-13}}</ref> and in a recurring role on the second season of Phineas and Ferb, playing the part of List of Phineas and Ferb characters#Irving Irving, a fan of Phineas and Ferb.

In 2010, he played a role in Cats & Dogs: The Revenge of Kitty Galore. He regularly appears in the skit "Knock Knock Joke of the Day" on the hit childrens television show, Yo Gabba Gabba! since season 2<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.monstersandcritics.com/smallscreen/news/article_1464960.php/Jack_Black_to_guest_star_on_Yo_Gabba_Gabba title=Jack Black to guest star on Yo Gabba Gabba author=MacIntyre, April date=2009-03-16 work=Monsters and Critics accessdate=2010-11-13 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100811231821/http://www.monstersandcritics.com/smallscreen/news/article_1464960.php/Jack_Black_to_guest_star_on_Yo_Gabba_Gabba archivedate=2010-08-11 df=}}</ref> and stars in Craig McCrackens new TV show Wander Over Yonder on the Disney Channel. In the fall of 2012, Jack lent his voice to the Disney animated film Wreck-It Ralph, playing the title characters close friend and video game opponent Fix-It Felix, Jr. More recently, he has starred alongside Los Angeles Clippers player Blake Griffin in Kia Motors Kia commercials, and in 2013 appeared in a commercial for Barnes & Noble.{{citation needed date=December 2014}} McBrayers Adult Swim series The Jack and Triumph Show premiered in February 2015. In September 2016, on the season 10 premiere episode of The Big Bang Theory, McBrayer played Pennys older brother Randall.

In 2018, McBrayer was one of the actors who voiced the audiobook A Day in the Life of Marlon Bundo.<ref name="Bundo AV Club">{{cite news  last=Perkins  first=Dennis  date=March 19, 2018  title=John Oliver hijacks homophobe Mike Pences bunny book with a better one in A Day In The Life Of Marlon Bundo  url=https://www.avclub.com/john-oliver-hijacks-homophobe-mike-pences-bunny-book-wi-1823879129  work=AV Club  location=  access-date=March 19, 2018 }}</ref>
