Abby Elliott (born June 16, 1987) is an American actress and comedian who was a cast member on Saturday Night Live for four seasons (from 2008 to 2012) and co-starred on the Bravo (U.S. TV network) Bravo series Odd Mom Out. She is the daughter of actor/comedian Chris Elliott.

==Life and career==
Elliott was born in New York City, the elder of two children. She is the daughter of Paula Niedert, a talent coordinator, and actor/comedian Chris Elliott. Her grandfather is radio comedian Bob Elliott (comedian) Bob Elliott.<ref name="NY Times">{{cite news url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/11/29/magazine/29Elliott-t.html?_r=1&ref=todayspaper&pagewanted=all title=A Professionally Funny Family last=Zuckerman first=Ed work=The New York Times date=November 11, 2009 accessdate=January 7, 2011}}</ref> Her younger sister is actress Bridey Elliott; they were both raised in Wilton, Connecticut.<ref name="wilton">{{cite web url=http://wilton.dailyvoice.com/neighbors/happy-birthday-wilton-s-abigail-abby-elliott title=Happy Birthday To Wilton’s Abigail Abby Elliott date=June 16, 2014 publisher=wilton.dailyvoice.com accessdate=March 7, 2015}}</ref> She attended high school at Immaculate High School in Danbury, Connecticut, where she acted in school plays and musicals.  After graduating from Immaculate High School in 2005, she attended Marymount Manhattan College in New York City but dropped out during the first semester.<ref name="NY Times"/.

Elliott took comedy classes at The Groundlings and eventually began training and performing in various sketch comedy shows at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre (UCB) in Los Angeles.<ref name="HR1">{{cite news last= Nellie first=Andreeva title=SNL taps Abby Elliott, Michaela Watkins  url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/snl-taps-abby-elliott-michaela-122861  work=The Hollywood Reporter  date=November 12, 2008}}</ref>

In 2006, Elliott appeared with supporting roles for the Television pilot pilots Youve Reached The Elliotts and Chrissy: Plain & Simple, both sitcoms that star her father, Chris Elliott.<ref name="wilton"/> While at UCBT, she frequently performed with The Midnight Show sketch troupe. She has also occasionally performed comedy with her younger sister Bridey. Before joining the cast of SNL, Elliott had also done guest voices roles on King of the Hill and Minoriteam.<ref name="littleton">{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2008/scene/news/elliott-watkins-join-snl-cast-1117995768/ title=Elliott, Watkins join ‘SNL’ cast last=Littleton first=Cynthia date=November 12, 2008 publisher=variety.com accessdate=March 7, 2015}}</ref> She has also been featured in a number of Late Night with Jimmy Fallon sketches such as "Jersey Floor" and "Studio 6-Bee".<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/jimmy-fallon-jersey-shore-186198 title=Tina Fey, Amy Poehler, Jimmy Fallon Mock NBC in Jersey Shore Spoof (Video) last=Power first=Lindsay date=May 6, 2011 work=The Hollywood Reporter accessdate=March 7, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.ew.com/article/2010/02/02/glee-jimmy-fallon-studio-6-bee title=Jimmy Fallon parodies Glee: Living on a prayer for cue cards last=Barrett first=Annie date=February 2, 2010 publisher=ew.com accessdate=March 7, 2015}}</ref> Elliott made her film debut with a small role in No Strings Attached (film) No Strings Attached and has since had supporting roles in the films High Road (film) High Road and Fun Size.<ref name="Elle2012">{{cite web title=Exclusive: Abby Elliott on Her New Movie, A Role on How I Met Your Mother, and Leaving SNL url=http://www.elle.com/news/culture/abby-elliott-interview-fun-size-saturday-night-live accessdate=October 12, 2012 work=Elle}}</ref><ref name="IrishNews">{{cite news url=http://www.irishnews.com/features/not-so-fun-size-comedy-1202690  title=Not so Fun Size comedy  work=The Irish News  date=November 2, 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107212700/http://www.irishnews.com/features/not-so-fun-size-comedy-1202690  archivedate=January 7, 2014 }}</ref>

Since leaving SNL in 2012, Elliott has made guest appearances on television programs such as 2 Broke Girls, How I Met Your Mother, Happy Endings (TV series) Happy Endings, and Inside Amy Schumer.<ref>{{cite web url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2012/12/14/happy-endings-abby-elliott/ title=Happy Endings -- Abby Elliott to guest star - Inside TV - EW.com work=EW.com accessdate=December 15, 2014}}</ref> From 2015 to 2017, she co-starred on the Bravo (U.S. TV network) Bravo series Odd Mom Out.

===Saturday Night Live===
Elliott joined the cast of SNL midway through the Saturday Night Live (season 34) 2008–2009 season (season 34), in November 2008, following the departure of Amy Poehler.<ref name="littleton"/> She is the third generation of her family to have been featured on SNL (the second to be hired as a cast member, and her tenure on the show was longer than both her father and grandfather). Her father Chris Elliott was an SNL cast member during the Saturday Night Live (season 20) 1994–1995 season (season 20) and her grandfather Bob Elliott was one half of the popular comedy duo Bob & Ray. Bob Elliott co-starred on a Christmas episode in the Saturday Night Live (season 4) 1978–1979 season (fourth season).<ref name="NY Times"/>

After four seasons on SNL, Elliott was let go from the show prior to its Saturday Night Live (season 38) 38th season.<ref name="Elle2012"/>
