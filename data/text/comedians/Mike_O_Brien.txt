Michael Patrick<ref name="eejit">{{cite news first=Bradford last=Evans title=SNL Writer Michael Patrick OBrien Is Also Joining the Cast as a Featured Player work=Splitsider date=August 23, 2013 accessdate=August 29, 2014 url=http://splitsider.com/2013/08/snl-writer-michael-patrick-obrien-is-also-joining-the-cast-as-a-featured-player/}}</ref> OBrien (born June 22, 1976),<ref name="snl-bio-2013">{{cite web url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/cast/mike-obrien/795952/  title=Mike OBrien Bio &#124; Featured Player &#124; Saturday Night Live  publisher=NBC  archivedate=2013-10-15  archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20131015044903/http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/cast/mike-obrien/795952/  accessdate=October 7, 2013}}</ref> is an American actor, writer, and comedian known for his work as a writer and short filmmaker on Saturday Night Live. He is the creator of the 2018 NBC comedy series A.P. Bio.

==Career  ==

OBrien attended the University of Michigan, where he majored in film-video and was the founder and editor of a monthly comedy newspaper named The Anti-Daily.<ref>{{cite web  title= Alumni Profile: SNLs Mike OBrien  url= http://www.michigandaily.com/blog/wire/alumni-profile-snl-featured-player-mike-obrien  deadurl= yes  archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20150402091326/http://www.michigandaily.com/blog/wire/alumni-profile-snl-featured-player-mike-obrien  archivedate= April 2, 2015  df= mdy-all }}</ref>

OBrien moved to Chicago and began taking improv and sketch classes. At iO Chicago he performed with a group called The Reckoning (2001–present). He trained at the Second City in Chicago, eventually joining the company’s mainstage, where he performed for one year as a part of "America: All Better".<ref>{{cite web  title= IO Profile  url= http://iowatercooler.com/mike-obrien/}}</ref>

In 2015, OBrien released his sketch comedy album Tasty Radio, featuring many of the cast members and writers he worked with on Saturday Night Live.

In 2018, he created the comedy series A.P. Bio, starring Glenn Howerton and Patton Oswalt. OBriens former SNL colleagues Lorne Michaels and Seth Meyers are also executive producers on the show. The series premiered on February 1, 2018.

==Saturday Night Live==
OBrien worked as a staff writer on Saturday Night Live for seven seasons, joining the show in 2009, and also appeared as a featured player for the Saturday Night Live (season 39) 2013–14 season.<ref name=umich>{{cite web  title= Alumni Profile: SNL Featured Player Mike OBrien  first= Claire  last= Bryan  work= The Michigan Daily  date= December 5, 2013  url= https://www.michigandaily.com/blog/wire/alumni-profile-snl-featured-player-mike-obrien  deadurl= yes  archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20131215181117/https://www.michigandaily.com/blog/wire/alumni-profile-snl-featured-player-mike-obrien  archivedate= December 15, 2013  df= mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web   url = http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/about/bio/mike-obrien   title = Mike OBrien, Featured Player   publisher = NBC Universal   accessdate = 2014-06-24 }}</ref> After his sole season as a featured player, he left the cast but continued to write for the show in his final season from 2014 to 2015.<ref name=ew>{{cite web title=Mike OBrien Will Not Return As SNL Player url=http://www.ew.com/article/2014/09/15/mike-obrien-will-not-return-as-an-snl-player-this-season}}</ref> Some of OBriens sketches include "Monster Pals" starring James Franco as well as "Puppet Class" starring Bill Hader. His short films "Grow a Guy" and "The Jay-Z Story" were each given the title card, "A Mike OBrien Picture" in season 40.<ref name=Splitsider>{{cite web title=Breakding Down Each Cast Members Contribution to SNL Season 39  url=http://splitsider.com/2014/05/looking-at-the-cast-of-snl-season-39/}}</ref> After leaving the writing staff in season 41, OBrien has continued to contribute short films under the title, A Mike OBrien Picture.
