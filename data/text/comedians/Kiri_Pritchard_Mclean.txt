Kiri Pritchard-Mclean (born Kiri Mclean, 6 November 1986) is a British comedian. Born in Gloucester, she was raised on a farm in Anglesey, Wales. She has performed for several consecutive years at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe and won two Chortle awards.

==Comedy career==

She is the director and writer for sketch group Geins Family Giftshop who were List of Edinburgh Comedy Award winners nominated for Best Newcomer in 2014 for the Edinburgh Comedy Awards Edinburgh Comedy Award.
As a sketch group they were also nominated for Chortle (website) Chortle Awards in 2015. She took her debut show Hysterical Woman to the Edinburgh Festival Fringe Edinburgh Fringe Festival in 2016.
It transferred to the Soho Theatre for a run in June 2017.

Pritchard-Mclean has appeared on Russell Howards Stand-up Central Stand Up Central on Comedy Central and ITV (TV network) ITVs Elevenish and has made multiple appearances on the BBC Radio 4 shows The Now Show, The News Quiz and Elis James Ellis James State Of the Nation.

She is also co-host of the podcast All Killa No Filla along with fellow comedian Rachel Fairburn.
==Comedy awards==

Pritchard-Mclean was nominated as Chortle Awards Best Compere in 2016 &amp; 2017. She won Best Compere and Best Club Comedian at the Chortle Awards in 2018.
