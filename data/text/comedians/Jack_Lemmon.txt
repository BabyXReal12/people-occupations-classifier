John Uhler "Jack" Lemmon III (February 8, 1925 – June 27, 2001) was an American actor and musician. Lemmon was an eight-time Academy Awards Academy Award nominee, with two wins. He starred in over 60 films, such as Some Like It Hot, The Apartment, Mister Roberts (1955 film) Mister Roberts (for which he won the 1955 Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor), Days of Wine and Roses (film) Days of Wine and Roses, The Great Race, Irma la Douce, The Odd Couple (film) The Odd Couple and its sequel The Odd Couple II (and other frequent collaborations with Odd Couple co-star Walter Matthau), Save the Tiger (for which he won the 1973 Academy Award for Best Actor), The Out-of-Towners (1970 film) The Out-of-Towners, The China Syndrome, Missing (1982 film) Missing, Glengarry Glen Ross (film) Glengarry Glen Ross, Tuesdays with Morrie (film) Tuesdays with Morrie, Out to Sea, Grumpy Old Men (film) Grumpy Old Men, and Grumpier Old Men.

==Early life==
Lemmon was born on February 8, 1925, in an elevator
at Newton-Wellesley Hospital in Newton, Massachusetts, a suburb of Boston. He was the only child of Mildred Burgess LaRue  and John Uhler Lemmon, Jr., the president of a doughnut company.
His father was of Irish heritage, and Lemmon was raised Catholic.
He attended John Ward Elementary School in Newton and the Rivers School in Weston, Massachusetts. During his acceptance of his lifetime achievement award, he stated that he knew he wanted to be an actor from the age of eight. Lemmon attended Phillips Academy (Class of 1943) and Harvard College (Class of 1947), where he lived in Eliot House (Harvard College) Eliot House
and was an active member of several Drama Clubs – and president of the Hasty Pudding Club
