William Gardell (born August 20, 1969) is an American stand-up comedian, actor, game show host and voice artist. Gardell played Chicago police officer Mike Biggs on Mike & Molly. He also made an appearance in a dozen episodes of My Name Is Earl as a police officer.<ref>{{cite news last=Fink first=Jerry url=http://www.lasvegassun.com/news/2009/jul/01/face-you-might-know-tv-appear-onstage/ title=Face you might know from TV to appear onstage date=2009-07-01 publisher=lasvegassun.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> Gardell voiced Santa Claus Santa in Ice Age: A Mammoth Christmas, as well as starring on Sullivan & Son in the recurring role of Lyle Winkler.

==Early life==
Born in Swissvale, Pennsylvania, near Pittsburgh,<ref>[http://www.pittsburghmagazine.com/Pittsburgh-Magazine/December-2010/Billy-Gardell/ Billy Gardell]</ref> Gardell attended Winter Park High School in Orange County, Florida during 1985.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2011">{{cite news last=Boedeker first=Hal url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2011-12-29/entertainment/os-billy-gardell-orlando-20111229_1_billy-gardell-mike-molly-molly-flynn title=Mike & Molly & standup - Billy Gardell returns home to help Central Florida welcome the new year date=2011-12-29 publisher=orlandosentinel.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> As a child, he moved to Florida with his mother and younger brother and sister after his parents divorced. After that, he only visited Pennsylvania in the summers.<ref name="USAToday-2011">{{cite news last=Mills first=Nancy url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/people/2011-02-05-billy-gardell_N.htm title=The small things in life are big enough for Billy Gardell date=2011-02-05 publisher=usatoday.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> He has said living in Florida was a positive experience.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2001">{{cite news last=Anderson first=Jamie J. url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2001-07-13/entertainment/0107120369_1_billy-gardell-bonkerz-comedian title=Daydreams Punchline Is Oh-so-sweet - Did You Hear The One About An Orlando Comedian Who Works Hard For His Success? date=2001-07-13 publisher=orlandosentinel.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> At the age of 15, he started working at a department store warehouse in the receiving area where he would unload trucks and stack pallets.<ref name="USAToday-2011" /> In 1987, he started cleaning bathrooms, seating people, and answering phones at a local comedy club, Bonkerz.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2011" /> On December 28, he started performing at the comedy clubs open-mic nights after he made a dare with some co-workers. "If I didnt do an open mic night, I couldnt cover the bet. I was running my mouth that I would do it. They bet me I wouldnt," he explained. He eventually started opening for Dennis Miller and George Carlin.<ref name="USAToday-2011" />

Gardell was a member of International Thespian Society Troupe 850 whose other notable alumni include Amanda Bearse, also a graduate of Winter Park High School, and Ben Rock of The Blair Witch Project.<ref>{{cite news last=Garisto first=Julie url=http://m.cltampa.com/tampa/mollys-better-half/Content?oid=2271516 title=Mollys better half date=2011-05-12 publisher=cltampa.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref>

==Career==
Gardell is known for his comedic roles and is influenced by Jackie Gleason.<ref name="USAToday-2011" /> He has also listed Richard Pryor, George Carlin, John Candy,<ref>{{cite news last=Duck first=Allison url=http://www.lasvegasweekly.com/news/2011/jul/06/comedian-billy-gardell-hits-halftime/ title=Comedian Billy Gardell hits ‘Halftime’ date=2011-07-06 publisher=lasvegasweekly.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> and John Belushi as his influences.<ref>{{cite news last=Oldenburg first=Ann url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/people/story/2011-10-02/Billy-Gardell-Mike-amp-Molly-Melissa-McCarthy/50637080/1 title=Mike & Molly star Billy Gardell is one happy husband date=2011-10-03 publisher=usatoday.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> Although Bonkerz helped launch his comedy career, he has credited the support from his grandmother, saying "She told me when I was 8 years old that I could be a comic".<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2001" /> Before Mike & Molly, Gardell revealed that he considered a career in radio after being on the road for his comedy act began to affect his family time.<ref name="USAToday-2011" /> He has performed in several feature films, including Bad Santa (with Billy Bob Thornton) You, Me, and Dupree (with Owen Wilson) and Avenging Angelo (with Sylvester Stallone and Anthony Quinn) as well as appearing numerous times in recurring roles in several US television series, including NBCs Heist (TV series) Heist, The Practice, Yes, Dear, Desperate Housewives, Lucky (TV series) Lucky, Bones (TV series) Bones, My Name Is Earl, Monk (TV series) Monk, and The King of Queens. Gardell appeared as himself on the Comedy Central series Make Me Laugh. Subsequently, he appeared on Miller’s television program Dennis Miller, telecast on the US financial news network CNBC (conducting "man-on-the-street" interviews). Notably, he created and staged a Saturday Night Live SNL-style charity performance of a piece called Winter Park Live, the proceeds from which were donated to Comic Relief (charity) Comic Relief.

Gardell continues to make weekly appearances on hometown local radio station, WDVE, appearing on The DVE Morning Show.<ref>{{cite news last=Owen first=Rob url=http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/ae/tv-radio/billy-gardell-relishes-role-in-mike-molly-264663/ title=Billy Gardell relishes role in Mike & Molly date=2012-03-29 publisher=post-gazette.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref> Gardell also appeared on Chelsea Lately, as a roundtable participant. Gardell appeared on a special for Comedy Central Presents in 2007 that aired on April 4, 2008. Gardell also played the host of Pizza Talk, an imaginary TV series, in commercials for Round Table Pizza.

In 2006, Gardells first comedy album, Billy Gardell: Throwback, was released.<ref name="WatertownDaily-2011">{{cite news last=Brock first=Chris url=http://www.watertowndailytimes.com/article/20111009/CURR04/710099861 title=Billy Gardell in Clayton Oct. 14 date=2011-10-09 publisher=watertowndailytimes.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref>

From September 2010 to May 2016, Gardell starred in Mike & Molly, alongside Melissa McCarthy.

In November 2011, he lent his voice to Santa Claus Santa in Ice Age: A Mammoth Christmas.

His new stand-up special Halftime was released in 2011 and aired on Comedy Central. It was filmed in Pittsburgh and profiles working-class America.<ref name="WatertownDaily-2011" /> As of 2011, he continues to tour as a stand-up comedian in addition to acting.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2011" /> In a 2011 Orlando Sentinel article, Gardell said he wouldnt mind doing movies again.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2011" />

In 2015, Gardell started hosting the lottery game show Monopoly Millionaires Club (TV series) Monopoly Millionaires Club, featuring contestants who bought tickets for the Monopoly Millionaires Club lottery game; the latter began on October 19, 2014.

==Personal life==
Gardell has been married to Patty Knight since 2001.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2001" /><ref name="WatertownDaily-2011" /> They have a 13-year old son, William as of 2016. In a 2011 USA Today article, Gardell said he may retire when his son is a teenager to spend more time with him.<ref name="USAToday-2011" /> His parents still live in Florida where he occasionally visits them.<ref name="OrlandoSentinel-2011" /> Regarding his weight, Gardell has explained that he "always had a little gut" and weighed 350 pounds at one point.<ref name="USAToday-2011" /> Gardell is allergic to dogs.<ref>{{Cite episode  title=The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson  network= CBS  date=2014-05-13  season=10  number=1928  language=English}}</ref>

Gardell is a Pittsburgh Steelers fan, having participated in several Terrible Towel waves with Steeler Nation other Steeler fans at Heinz Field. His favorite player growing up was Jack Lambert (American football) Jack Lambert, with his son liking former Steeler Hines Ward.<ref>{{cite news last=Varley first=Teresa url=http://www.steelers.com/news/article-1/Talking-Steelers-Football-with-Billy-Gardell/8b39929e-4883-4c40-a40b-c5add9af2b97 title=Talking Steelers Football with Billy Gardell date=2011-11-16 publisher=steelers.com accessdate=2013-02-03}}</ref>
