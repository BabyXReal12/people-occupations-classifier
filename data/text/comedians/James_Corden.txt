James Kimberley Corden<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/news/james-corden-reveals-he-was-teased-over-middle-name-1921992.html title=James Corden reveals he was teased over middle name last=Shaw first=Vicky date=16 March 2010 work=The Independent}}</ref> {{postnom country=UK OBE}} (born 22 August 1978)<ref name=attentionplease>{{cite book date=29 September 2011 author=James Corden isbn=978-1-8460-5935-3 title=May I Have your Attention Please? publisher=Century}}</ref> is an English actor, writer, producer, comedian, television host, and singer. He hosts The Late Late Show with James Corden, a late-night television talk show on CBS.

Along with Welsh actress Ruth Jones, Corden co-created, co-wrote and starred in the critically acclaimed BBC sitcom Gavin & Stacey (2007–2010) for which he won the British Academy Television Awards BAFTA Television Award for British Academy Television Award for Best Comedy Performance Best Comedy Performance. He was featured on the UK No.1 single "Shout (Shout for England song) Shout", along with Grime (music) grime artist Dizzee Rascal, an unofficial anthem of the England football team for the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa, and duetted with Australian singer Kylie Minogue on a cover of "Only You (Yazoo song)#Kylie Minogue version Only You" in 2015. Appearing on the UK charity telethon Comic Relief in 2011, Corden created his Carpool Karaoke sketch when he drove around London singing songs with George Michael. Since 2009, Corden has presented the Brit Awards ceremony five times, four by himself and once with Minogue and Mathew Horne. In 2016, he hosted the 70th Tony Awards; he went on to host the Grammy Awards in 59th Annual Grammy Awards 2017 and 60th Annual Grammy Awards 2018. He has presented the sports-based comedy Panel discussion panel show A League of Their Own (UK game show) A League of Their Own on Sky 1 since 2010.

In 2011, he played the lead part in the comedy play One Man, Two Guvnors, which transferred from the Royal National Theatre National Theatre to the West End theatre West End and then to Broadway theatre Broadway and was also cinecast worldwide via National Theatre Live. For his performance in the Broadway run of the play, Corden won the 66th Tony Awards Tony Award for Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actor in a Play Best Actor in a Play.<ref name="unitedagents">[http://unitedagents.co.uk/james-corden#profile-1 Profile], United Agents; accessed 28 December 2014.</ref> In 2015 he received the BAFTA Britannia Awards Britannia Award for British Artist of the Year.<ref name=bafta15>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2015/08/britannia-awards-meryl-streep-sam-mendes-james-corden-1201502512/ title=Meryl Streep, Sam Mendes & James Corden To Be Lauded At 2015 Britannia Awards website=Deadline Hollywood date=20 August 2015}}</ref>

==Early life==
Corden was born in Hillingdon, Greater London, the son of Margaret and Malcolm Corden. His father was a musician in the Royal Air Force band and his mother was a social worker.<ref name="thirdsector">{{cite web url=http://www.thirdsector.co.uk/Communications/article/1180208/sharon-kelly-osbourne-give-backing-fashion-targets-breast-cancer-2013 title=Sharon and Kelly Osbourne give their backing to Fashion Targets Breast Cancer 2013 publisher=Thirdsector.co.uk date=30 April 2013 accessdate=28 December 2014}}</ref> He grew up in Hazlemere, Buckinghamshire,<ref name="stannit"/> and attended Park Middle School and Holmer Green Senior School Holmer Green Upper School.<ref name=attentionplease/> He has an older sister, Andrea Henry, and a younger sister, Ruth Corden.<ref name="femail"/> He was raised in the Salvation Army church but no longer considers himself a Christian.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/james-corden-admits-turning-back-8648732 title=James Corden admits turning his back on Christian upbringing after doubting his beliefs date=17 August 2016 work=Daily Mirror accessdate=21 September 2016}}</ref>

==Career==

===Early career===
Cordens first stage appearance was at the age of 18 with a one-line part in the 1996 musical Martin Guerre (musical) Martin Guerre.<ref name=guardian/> His first TV reporter role was on the BBCs Good Morning with Anne and Nick.<ref>[https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2016/08/james-corden-meat-loaf-interview-1995 Baby James Corden’s 1995 Meat Loaf Interview Is a Delight] Vanity Fair. 23 August 2013. Retrieved 23 January 2018.</ref> His early television work included Gareth Jones in the 1999 series Boyz Unlimited. He also starred in Tango advertisements in 1998 and had a role as a bookish student in Teachers (UK TV series) Teachers and in 2000 a small part in an episode of Hollyoaks. Corden had guest appearances on Little Britain and Dalziel and Pascoe, both in 2004. Cordens early film credits include Whatever Happened to Harold Smith? (1999), Mike Leighs All or Nothing (film) All or Nothing (2002), Heartlands (film) Heartlands (2002), and Cruise of the Gods (2002).<ref name="imdb"/>

===Rise to prominence===
From 2000 through early 2005, Corden starred in the British television series Fat Friends as Jamie Rymer. He garnered a nomination for the 2000 Royal Television Society Award for Network Newcomer On Screen for his work. Beginning in 2004, Corden played the role of Timms in the original London stage production of Alan Bennetts play The History Boys, as well as in the Broadway (theatre) Broadway, Sydney, Wellington and Hong Kong productions and radio and The History Boys (film) 2006 film adaptation versions of the play.<ref name="Broadway"/><ref name="National Theatre"/><ref name="Wellington"/> Also in 2006, he appeared in the film Starter for 10 (film) Starter for 10.<ref name="imdb"/>

From 2007 through early 2010, Corden co-starred in his own series, the BBC Three sitcom Gavin & Stacey. He co-wrote the series with his Fat Friends co-star Ruth Jones; Corden and Jones played the friends of the title characters, with Corden starring as Smithy. The series proved popular and was well-received critically. For the show, Corden won Best Male Comedy Performer and Gavin & Stacey won Best New British Television Comedy at the 2007 British Comedy Awards.<ref name="sitcom"/> At the 2008 Television BAFTAs, Corden won the BAFTA for British Academy Television Award for Best Comedy Performance Best Male Comedy Performance, and Gavin & Stacey won the BAFTAs Audience Award for Programme of the Year.<ref name="unitedagents"/><ref name="bafta2008"/> In December 2008, the show won Best TV Comedy in the 2008 British Comedy Awards. Gavin & Stacey also won the award for Most Popular Comedy Programme at the National Television Awards in 2010.<ref name="who"/>

He collaborated again with Horne on a 2009 sketch show named Horne & Corden, described by the BBC as a "traditional comedy entertainment show in the style of Morecambe and Wise".<ref name="horne"/> The show ran for only one series and was poorly received by the critics, with Corden later admitting "the absolute truth is I wasnt good enough."<ref name="sketch"/>

In 2009, Corden starred as the lead character in the film Lesbian Vampire Killers, which was not a success. That year he played Clem Cattini in the Joe Meek biopic Telstar (film) Telstar, and likewise in the animated Planet 51 along with Mathew Horne.<ref name="imdb"/><ref name="horne"/> In February 2009, he co-presented the Brit Awards with Mathew Horne and Kylie Minogue.<ref name="brit"/> On 13 March 2009, he appeared in a sketch for the UK charity telethon Red Nose Day 2009 Comic Relief giving the England national football team England football team a motivational talk, and later presented a section with Horne showing their best bits of comedy from the previous two years along with highlights from the night.<ref name="horne"/>

In March 2010, Corden began hosting the Sky1 comedy/sports panel show A League of Their Own (UK game show) A League of Their Own alongside team captains Andrew Flintoff and Jamie Redknapp. In March 2010, he presented Sport Relief 2010 alongside Davina McCall and others, and contributed a "sequel" to the 2009 England football team sketch, this time giving a motivational talk to various sports stars including David Beckham and motor racing driver Jenson Button.<ref name="sport"/>
