==Career==
===Career beginnings===
In the 1990s, Sudeikis began his career in improvisational theatre, specifically improv comedy. He began performing at ComedySportz (now called Comedy City) in Kansas City, Missouri. Sudeikis moved to Chicago, Illinois, where he studied at the Annoyance Theatre and IO Theater (formerly known as ImprovOlympic) and was one of the founding members of the longform team, J.T.S. Brown. He also performed with Boom Chicago in Amsterdam, Netherlands.

Sudeikis was later cast in The Second City The Second City Touring Company and performed greatest hit shows while on the road. In the early 2000s, he became a founding member of The Second City Las Vegas, where he performed at the Flamingo Las Vegas Flamingo.<ref>{{cite web last1=Second City first1=The title=Jason Sudeikis The Second City Biography url=https://www.secondcity.com/people/other/jason-sudeikis/ website=The Second City publisher=The Second City accessdate=14 February 2018}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
In 2003, while a regular performer at The Second City Las Vegas, Sudeikis was hired as a sketch writer for Saturday Night Live, and would occasionally make bit appearances as audience members or extras. In May 2005, he became a featured player on the show, and was upgraded to repertory status at the beginning of the shows Saturday Night Live (season 32) 32nd season on September 30, 2006. In July 2013, Sudeikis announced that he was leaving Saturday Night Live.<ref>{{cite news last=Ng first=Philiana title=Jason Sudeikis Not Returning to SNL url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/jason-sudeikis-not-returning-snl-592498 accessdate=August 28, 2013 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=July 24, 2013}}</ref> In 2015 and 2016, he made occasional appearances on the show.
