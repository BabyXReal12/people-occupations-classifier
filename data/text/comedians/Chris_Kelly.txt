Chris Kelly (born September 7, 1983) is an Americans American writer and director known for his work on Saturday Night Live, and writing and directing the autobiographical film Other People (film) Other People that premiered at the 2016 Sundance Film Festival. He has received five Emmy nominations for his work on SNL.

==Career==

===Early career===
Kelly grew up in Sacramento, California, the son of Joanne née Kellogg (1960–2009) Kelly. He has a sister, Janelle, and a half sister, Katie, from his mothers re-marriage.<ref>[http://www.egcitizen.com/obituaries/joanne-piazza/article_a4a78f99-833f-5e53-adaf-15b5e0769a55.html Joanne Kelly Obituary] accessed 11/23/2016</ref><ref name=tumblr2>{{cite web title=I Think I Have Overstayed My Welcome In Sacramento url=http://chriskelly.tumblr.com/post/221604888/i-think-i-have-overstayed-my-welcome-in-sacramento website=tumblr.com accessdate=28 January 2016}}</ref> He attended college at UC Irvine.<ref name="Dang">{{cite news last1=Dang first1=Mike title=Inside the Thriving Comedy Career of ‘SNL’ and ‘Broad City’ Writer Chris Kelly url=https://thebillfold.com/inside-the-thriving-comedy-career-of-snl-and-broad-city-writer-chris-kelly-78e1fcda8d02#.x9niruhsh accessdate=20 September 2016 work=The Billfold date=8 March 2016}}</ref> He was a staff writer and director at Funny or Die and Onion News Network, the latter of which won the 2009 Peabody Award. He was also Head Writer for Matt Bessers Comedy Central special This Show Will Get You High in 2010. Kelly also performed at Upright Citizens Brigade UCBNY with his storytelling show Chris Kelly: Americas Princess Diana, and before that, wrote, directed and starred in the play, Oh My God, I Heard Youre Dying! He was on various Maude Teams as both a writer and actor, including Stone Cold Fox, 27 Kidneys, and Thunder Gulch, and was a frequent monologist at ASSSSCAT in both NYC and LA.<ref name=UCB>{{cite web title=Chris Kelly url=http://ucbcomedy.com/user/5923 website=ucbcomedy.com accessdate=27 January 2016}}</ref>

===Saturday Night Live===
Currently, Kelly is a List of Saturday Night Live writers supervising writer for Saturday Night Live having joined the show as a staff writer for the Saturday Night Live (season 37) 37th season and promoted to supervising writer on the Saturday Night Live (season 40) 40th season. Along with his SNL writing partner, Sarah Schneider, Kelly has created many sketches including: "(Do It On My) Twin Bed," "Back Home Ballers," "The Beygency" (about Beyoncé), and "First Got Horny 2 U."<ref name=EW>{{cite news last1=Steiner first1=Amanda Michelle title=Sarah Schneider & Chris Kelly talk First Got Horny 2 U, the ladies of SNL, Beyoncé, and more url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/11/20/snl-writers-chris-kelly-sarah-schneider-interview accessdate=28 January 2016 publisher=Entertainment Weekly date=November 20, 2015}}</ref> He and Schneider primarily write for Aidy Bryant and Kate McKinnon,<ref name=EW/> including "Dyke and Fats."<ref name=Aboveaverage>{{cite web title=‘SNL’s’ Sarah Schneider & Chris Kelly Reminisce About Writing ‘Back Home Ballers’ Sketch url=http://aboveaverage.com/snls-sarah-schneider-chris-kelly-reminisce-about-writing-back-home-ballers-sketch/ website=aboveaverage.com accessdate=28 January 2016}}</ref>  Kelly has been nominated for five Primetime Emmy Awards for his work on SNL.

In August 2016, it was announced that Kelly and Schneider would be co-head writers for SNLs Saturday Night Live (season 42) 42nd season.<ref>{{cite news last1=Jones first1=Nate title=Chris Kelly and Sarah Schneider Are Your Newest SNL Head Writers url=http://www.vulture.com/2016/08/chris-kelly-schneider-are-newest-snl-head-writers.html accessdate=13 August 2016 work=Vulture publisher=New York Magazine date=August 12, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Wright">{{cite news last1=Wright first1=Megh title=‘SNL’ Promotes Sarah Schneider and Chris Kelly to Co-Head Writers url=http://splitsider.com/2016/08/snl-promotes-sarah-schneider-and-chris-kelly-to-co-head-writers/ accessdate=20 September 2016 work=Splitsider date=12 August 2016}}</ref> Kelly is the first openly gay head writer of SNL.<ref name="Reynolds">{{cite news last1=Reynolds first1=Daniel title=Chris Kelly Makes SNL History as First Out Head Writer url=http://www.advocate.com/television/2016/8/16/chris-kelly-makes-snl-history-first-out-head-writer accessdate=20 September 2016 work=The Advocate date=16 August 2016}}</ref>

===Broad City===
Kelly is also a writer and consulting producer on Comedy Centrals Broad City, which earned him a nomination for the Writers Guild of America Award for Television: Comedy Series.<ref name=WGA/> He was a self described fan of the Broad City web series and joined the show as a writer and consulting producer when it was picked up as a series on Comedy Central.<ref name=LATimes>{{cite news last1=Blake first1=Meredith title=Broad City brings female misadventures to male-oriented Comedy Central url=http://articles.latimes.com/2014/jan/17/entertainment/la-et-st-broad-city-comedy-central-20140119/2 accessdate=28 January 2016 publisher=Los Angeles Times date=January 17, 2014}}</ref>

===Film===
Kelly wrote and directed his first feature film, Other People (film) Other People, that premiered at the 2016 Sundance Film Festival in the opening slot.<ref name=Wrap>{{cite web last1=Jeff Sneider And Matt Donnelly title=Sundance 2016: 13 Hot Directors to Watch url=http://www.thewrap.com/sundance-2016-13-hot-directors-to-watch/ website=TheWrap accessdate=27 January 2016 language=en-US}}</ref> The film is about a struggling comedy writer (played by Jesse Plemons), who has just broken up with his boyfriend and moves from New York City to Sacramento to help his sick mother (played by Molly Shannon).<ref name="Yohannes">{{cite news last1=Yohannes first1=Alamin title=SNL Writer Chris Kelly Taps Into His Life Story for Other People url=http://www.nbcnews.com/feature/nbc-out/chris-kelly-taps-his-story-directorial-debut-other-people-n621696 accessdate=20 September 2016 work=NBC News date=August 3, 2016}}</ref> He has to live with his conservative father and younger sisters for the first time in many years and feels like a stranger in his childhood home. As his mother’s health deteriorates, David tries to extract meaning from the awful experience and convince everyone that hes doing okay.<ref name=Sundance>{{cite web title=Other People url=http://www.sundance.org/projects/other-people website=www.sundance.org accessdate=27 January 2016}}</ref> The film is loosely based on his own life drawing from his experience of losing his mother to cancer in 2009.<ref name=AVclub>{{cite news last1=Dowd first1=A.A. title=Sundance begins with a divisive "Sundance movie," go figure url=http://www.avclub.com/article/sundance-begins-divisive-sundance-movie-go-figure-231133 accessdate=27 January 2016 work=www.avclub.com date=January 22, 2016}}</ref> He was labeled one of the "13 Hot Directors to Watch" at Sundance in 2016.<ref name=Wrap/>
