
Gregory Behrendt (born July 21, 1963) is an American comedian and author. His work as a script consultant to the HBO sitcom Sex and the City, starring Sarah Jessica Parker, paved the way for co-authoring of the New York Times bestseller Hes Just Not That into You (2004), later adapted into a film by the Hes Just Not That Into You (film) same name.<ref name="tr">{{cite news url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/edinburgh-festival/6020512/Greg-Behrendt-why-Sex-and-the-City-needed-me.html title=Greg Behrendt: why Sex and the City needed me date=August 13, 2009 work=The Telegraph   location=London   first=Veronica   last=Lee}}</ref> Apart from that he also hosted two short-lived television shows, The Greg Behrendt Show (2006) and Greg Behrendts Wake Up Call (2009).<ref name="la">{{cite news url=http://articles.latimes.com/2009/feb/08/entertainment/ca-monitor8 title=Hes Just Not That into You and Greg Behrendts Wake Up Call date=February 8, 2009 work=Los Angeles Times   first=Jon   last=Caramanica}}</ref><ref name="w">{{cite news url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/02/12/AR2009021204209.html title=If Im the Last Stop, Youre in Trouble. date=February 15, 2009 work=Washington Post}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Greg Behrendt graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in theater from the University of Oregon in 1991, though initially he had enrolled to be a business major and to play rugby.<ref name="va">{{cite news url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117802701.html?categoryid=1043&cs=1 title=Greg Behrendt: Masculinity given new dimensions date=July 12, 2001 publisher=Variety   first=Steve   last=Chagollan}}</ref>

==Career==
After graduation, Behrendt moved to San Francisco, where he joined an improvisational troupe, and met fellow comic Margaret Cho. In his early years, he performed comedy and Improvisational theatre improv in San Francisco.  He was a member of the improv troupe Crash and Burn, whose members included Margaret Cho. He came up as a performer alongside such comics as David Cross (with whom he roomed for years) and Patton Oswalt.<ref name=oc/>

He went on to appear on The Tonight Show and Late Night with Conan OBrien. His stand-up special, Greg Behrendt is Uncool, debuted on Comedy Central in January 2006.

In June 2006, he appeared on Celebrity Poker Showdown, playing for a domestic violence charity. Behrendt was a script consultant for the HBO sitcom Sex and the City, "offering script notes from the perspective of a straight man to the writing team, composed of women and gay men".<ref name=za/>

With Liz Tuccillo, Behrendt co-wrote the 2004 self-help book Hes Just Not That into You.  It was adapted by Drew Barrymores Flower Films/Warner Bros. as a Hes Just Not That Into You (film) movie of the same name, premiering February 6, 2009.

With his wife Amiira Ruotola-Behrendt, he co-wrote Its Called a Breakup Because Its Broken.

He released the book Its Just a Date!<ref>[https://www.amazon.com/dp/0007228325 Its Just a Date] amazon.com.</ref> in the UK.

His daily daytime "self-help" talk show, The Greg Behrendt Show, premiered September 12, 2006, four months later Sony Pictures Television announced, in January 2007 that the series would not have a second season, and the show aired its last new episodes in February 2007.<ref>{{cite web url=http://tvseriesfinale.com/tv-show/the-greg-behrendt-show-syndicated-self-help-cancelled/ title=The Greg Behrendt Show: Syndicated Self-Help Cancelled date=January 25, 2007 publisher=TVseriesfinale}}</ref>

He also did a mid-season program for the 2006â€“07 television season, Greg Behrendts Wake-Up Call, for American Broadcasting Company ABC, which never aired on the network. The program began airing in January 2009 on ABCs sister cable network SoapNet.<ref>[https://www.reuters.com/article/televisionNews/idUSTRE4AA23K20081111 Reuters]</ref> He also appeared on the Great Debate at the 2010 Melbourne Comedy Festival, in which he argued as the third speaker for the negative team on the debate "Food is Better Than Sex". Behrendts team, made up of New Zealand comedian Cal Wilson, English comedian Russell Kane and Behrendt himself, won the comedy event.

He started the podcast Walking the Room with his friend Dave Anthony.

==Personal life==
He is married to Amiira Ruotola. They have two daughters, Mighty Luna and Bella True.<ref name="oc">[http://www.ocregister.com/articles/behrendt-says-people-2225719-guy-candy Just who is Greg Behrendt?] OC Register, November 11, 2008</ref> He is a recovering alcoholic.<ref name="za">{{cite web url=http://www.zap2it.com/tv/news/tvfeatures/zap-gregbehrendtfixyouup,0,4250190.story title=Greg Behrendt Wants to Fix You Up date=July 12, 2008 publisher=Zap2it}}</ref><ref>Interview, KUPD morning show Holmbergs Morning Sickness, September 25, 2008</ref> He plays guitar in his own surf/rockabilly instrumental band, The Reigning Monarchs.<ref>[https://www.myspace.com/thereigningmonarchs MySpace: The Reigning Monarchs]</ref>

In June 2015, Behrendt announced<ref>{{cite web last1=Behrendt first1=Greg title=Quick update on HOW TO KEEP YOUR MARRIAGE FROM SUCKING  url=https://www.facebook.com/18934830605/photos/a.126488675605.103303.18934830605/10153442690635606/?type=1&permPage=1 website=Greg Behrendt FB Fan Page publisher=Facebook accessdate=9 June 2015}}</ref> via his Facebook Facebook Fan Page Fan Page that he was battling (an unspecified)  cancer, and had undertaken three rounds of chemotherapy. He discussed his diagnosis on episode 16TF of Jimmy Pardos podcast Never Not Funny as well as Wil Andersons Podcast "Wilosophy" on 9 July 2015. He stated that he had a form of lymphoma. His cancer is in remission as of autumn of 2015.
