Jason Andrew Narvy (born March 27, 1974) is an American actor known for playing the role of Bulk and Skull Eugene "Skull" Skullovitch on the various iterations of Power Rangers and film franchise from 1993 to 2012, beginning with Mighty Morphin Power Rangers in 1993.

==Career==
Narvy grew up in Los Angeles.<ref name=OfficialBio>[http://www.jasonnarvy.com/film-bio "Biography"] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120109034148/http://www.jasonnarvy.com/film-bio  date=2012-01-09 }}. JasonNarvy.com. Retrieved October 1, 2013.</ref> He studied at the Lee Strasberg Theater Institute. He is best known for playing Bulk and Skull Skull, one half of the bullying rival duo on Fox Televisions childrens TV series Mighty Morphin Power Rangers,<ref>{{cite news title=Borrowing From Bard for Bucks : Actor Jason Narvy will direct Newbury High students in an abridged version of Hamlet. date=May 26, 1994 first=Jane last=Hulse work=Los Angeles Times url=http://articles.latimes.com/1994-05-26/news/vl-62169_1_newbury-park-high-school}}</ref> a role he reprised on its spin-offs, Power Rangers Zeo, Power Rangers Turbo, and Power Rangers in Space, before leaving the franchise to go to college. He made a cameo appearance in the last episode of Power Rangers Super Samurai. He also appeared in the feature film adaptations, Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: The Movie in 1995 and the Turbo: A Power Rangers Movie in 1997.

He also supplied the voice of Combat Chopper on the sci-fi childrens show Masked Rider (TV series) Masked Rider.

Narvy appeared in a lead role in a ABC Family Fox Family pilot, Men in White. He also appeared numerous commercials, public service announcements and voiced numerous toys, tie-ins, and video games for Fox, ABC, and Disney.

He received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Franklin and Marshall College, and got his masters degree from Mary Baldwin College, where he studied with the American Shakespeare Center, performing in the title role in Henry V (play) Henry V, Pericles, and Hamlet. He also appeared as Richard of Gloucester and Suffolk in a conflation of the Henry VI trilogy. Jason also worked with the Legitimate Theater Company of New York, a Lower East Side company that emphasizes new plays and experimental works.<ref name=ConcordiaU>[https://www.cuchicago.edu/faculty/theater/jason-narvy/ "Jason Narvy, PhD: Assistant Professor of Theatre"]. Concordia University, Chicago. Retrieved October 1, 2013.{{Primary source inline date=October 2013}}</ref>{{Primary source inline date=October 2013}}

Narvy is a certified actor/combatant with the British Academy of Stage and Screen Combat, a member of the Screen Actors Guild, and holds a PhD in Theater Studies from the University of California, Santa Barbara.<ref name=ConcordiaU/> He is currently an associate professor in the Theatre Department at Concordia University, Chicago Concordia University in Chicago.<ref name=ConcordiaU/>

To this date, Narvy remains very good friends with his co-stars Paul Schrier and Jason David Frank.
