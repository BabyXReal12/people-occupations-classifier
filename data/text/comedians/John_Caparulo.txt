John Caparulo (born September 22, 1975) is an United States American Stand-up comedy stand-up comedian. He is perhaps best known for his appearances on the late night E! talk show, Chelsea Lately.

==Early life==
Caparulo grew up in East Liverpool, Ohio and graduated from Kent State in 1998. Caparulo started his stand-up career in night clubs in Cleveland, Ohio and Pittsburgh in 1997. He then spent several years working as a doorman (profession) doorman at the The Comedy Store (California) Comedy Store in Hollywood, Los Angeles, California Hollywood, California and as a groundskeeper at a local golf course.<ref name=Gadino>{{cite news last1=Gadino first1=Dylan title=Chelsea Lately Put Comedian John Caparulo Center Stage, But Married Life Presents Far More Challenges url=http://www.hollywoodtake.com/chelsea-lately-put-comedian-john-caparulo-center-stage-married-life-presents-far-more-challenges accessdate=December 7, 2015 work=Hollywood Take issue=September 28, 2013}}</ref><ref name=McIntyre>{{cite news last1=McIntyre first1=Michael K. title=John Caparulo steps off your TV screen and on the Hilarities stage url=http://www.cleveland.com/dining/index.ssf/2011/12/john_caparulo_steps_off_your_t.html accessdate=December 7, 2015 work=Cleveland.com date=December 7, 2011}}</ref><ref name=McNulty>{{cite news last1=McNulty first1=Timothy title=For Ohio comedian, tour was colossal url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/2008/02/08/For-Ohio-comedian-tour-was-colossal/stories/200802080150 accessdate=December 8, 2015 work=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette date=February 8, 2008}}</ref>

==Career==
John "Cap" Caparulo has a headlining residency at Harrahs in Las Vegas every Thursday, Friday, and Saturday. Caparulo first appeared at the 2003 Just for Laughs festival in Montreal,<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G-8f_kqmwmU  title=John Caparulo - Just For Laughs 2006  work=YouTube.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref> where he was offered a sitcom,{{citation needed date=December 2015}} and a spot on The Late Late Show (CBS TV series) The Late Late Show with Craig Kilborn.<ref name="tv.com">{{cite web  url=http://www.tv.com/the-late-late-show-with-craig-kilborn/james-marsden-alex-kingston-and-john-caparulo/episode/1227951/summary.html  title=The Late Late Show with Crain Kilborn James Marsden, Alex Kingston, and John Caparulo  work=TV.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref> He has since appeared on The Tonight Show,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.hulu.com/watch/9816/john-caparulo  title=The Tonight Show: John Caparulo  work=Hulu.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}} {{Dead link date=October 2010 bot=H3llBot}}</ref> was featured on Blue Collar Comedy Tour#The Next Generation Blue Collar Comedy Tour: The Next Generation hosted by Bill Engvall,{{Citation needed date=October 2010}} and was one of the comedians documented on Wild West Comedy Show: 30 Days & 30 Nights - Hollywood to the Heartland Vince Vaughns Wild West Comedy Tour.<ref name="Variety">{{cite web  url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117979963.html?categoryid=2031&cs=1  title=John Caparulo (Stars in Vince Vaughns Wild West Comedy Show)  work=Variety (magazine) Variety.com  first=Variety  last=Staff  date=February 1, 2008 accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.metacafe.com/watch/1046083/john_caparulo_clip/  title=John Caparulo Clip  work=Metacafe.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref> He hosted Mobile Home Disaster on Country Music Television CMT, and played the part of Brian on the short-lived ABC sitcom Work It (TV series) Work It. He also has a comedy special that aired on Comedy Central on December 21, 2008 called John Caparulo: Meet Cap which was directed by Dave Higby. The special was released on Compact Disc CD on February 10, 2009 and on DVD on April 14, 2009.<ref name="Tower">{{cite web  url=http://www.tower.com/meet-cap-john-caparulo-cd/wapi/113109204  title=Meet Cap (CD) By: John Caparulo (Artist)   work=Tower.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.walmart.com/catalog/product.do?product_id=10973697  title=DVD: John Caparulo: Meet Cap (Full Frame)  work=Wal-mart.com  accessdate=March 17, 2009}}</ref> Caparulo intermittently hosts The Mad Cap Hour radio show with his wife Jamie Caparulo and comedian Mark Ellis on Sirius XM, toadhopnetwork.com, and iTunes. In addition to touring, he regularly appears on the E! show Chelsea Lately, and voices the character Headphone Joe on Disneys Fish Hooks. Caparulo released his second album titled Come Inside Me in August, 2013 on Netflix.{{citation needed date=December 2015}} In February 2014, Cap began releasing Caplets, a brand-new set of comedy every 30 days that he shoots at the Comedy and Magic Club in Hermosa Beach, California and distributes them on his website for free.

==Personal life==
On the November 4th, 2010 episode of Chelsea Lately, John announced that he proposed to Jamie Marie Kelly after his October 29 performance at the Comedy & Magic Club in Hermosa Beach, California. John and Jamie were wed at Water Street Tavern near their alma mater, Kent State University, on May 27, 2012. The couple currently resides in Calabasas, California and Las Vegas, Nevada with their three dogs, a basset hound, a puggle, and a rescued collie mix. On their October 26, 2014 #26 Domestic Disputes podcast Jamie reveals to John that they are having a child. On November 27, 2014 #28 of their podcast they reveal that their child is going to be a girl and that they are going to name her Madden Jae. They welcomed Madden Jae on May 22, 2015.
