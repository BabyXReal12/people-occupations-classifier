Jonah Hill Feldstein (born December 20, 1983) is an American actor, director, producer, screenwriter, and comedian. Hill is known for his comedic roles in films such as Accepted (film) Accepted (2006), Grandmas Boy (2006 film) Grandmas Boy (2006), Superbad (film) Superbad (2007), Knocked Up (2007), Forgetting Sarah Marshall (2008), Get Him to the Greek (2010), 21 Jump Street (film) 21 Jump Street (2012), This Is the End (2013), 22 Jump Street (2014), and War Dogs (2016 film) War Dogs (2016), as well as his performances in Moneyball (film) Moneyball (2011) and The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film) The Wolf of Wall Street (2013), for which he received Academy Awards Academy Award nominations for Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor Best Supporting Actor.

Hill ranked 28th on Forbes magazines ranking of worlds highest paid actors from June 2014 to June 2015, bringing in $16{{nbsp}}million.<ref>{{cite journal last1=Robehmed first1=Natalie title=Seth Rogen, Jonah Hill Among Newcomers To Highest-Paid Actors List url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/natalierobehmed/2015/08/04/seth-rogen-jonah-hill-among-newcomers-on-highest-paid-actors-list/ work=Forbes accessdate=August 4, 2015}}</ref> As a screenwriter, he contributed to the stories of 21 Jump Street, 22 Jump Street, Sausage Party and Why Him? (2016). Hill will make his directorial debut from a screenplay he wrote, titled Mid-90s (film) Mid-90s.
