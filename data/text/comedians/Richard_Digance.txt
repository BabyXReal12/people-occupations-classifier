Richard Digance (pronounced DYE-jance; born 24 February 1949) is an English comedian and folk music folk singer.

== Early life ==
Digance was born in Plaistow, Newham Plaistow, Essex<!-- Do not change to London, Plaistow was in Essex in 1949 --> (now part of east London). After his family moved to nearby East Ham, he attended Vicarage Lane Primary School and then Langdon Academy Thomas Lethaby Secondary Modern. After gaining two A-Level passes in English Literature and Modern British History, he moved to Glasgow, where he studied mechanical engineering at Reid Kerr College, Paisley, Renfrewshire Paisley,<ref name="devon">{{cite web  url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/local/devon/hi/tv_and_radio/newsid_8604000/8604959.stm  title = Richard Digance  date = 6 April 2010  website = BBC Devon  accessdate= 16 January 2014}}</ref> during which time he was inspired by Billy Connolly and Mike Harding.<ref name="yorkshire">{{cite web  url = http://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/yorkshire-living/arts/theatre/the-big-interview-richard-digance-1-5287090  title = The Big Interview: Richard Digance  date = 7 January 2013  website = Yorkshire Post  accessdate= 16 January 2014}}</ref>

== Career ==
In the 1970s he toured the United States. Though failing to make much of a name, he ended up a support act for Steve Martin.<ref name="devon"/> He supported Tom Jones (singer) Tom Jones in 87 concerts.

In 1971, he recorded an LP whilst with the group Pisces for the Trailer label.<ref>{{cite web  url = http://www.mustrad.org.uk/lea_txt3.htm  title = Traditional Records Discography - file 3  website = Musical Traditions  accessdate= 16 January 2014}}</ref> It features the moorland ballad Jack_o_Legs "Jack OLegs" about a 14-foot-tall highwayman.

Richard Digance began his TV career on Sound of The City for Thames TV, produced by Richard Newman, in the early 1970s. This debut appearance was followed by The Old Grey Whistle Test and then the Today programme. The Whistle Test was his only BBC TV appearance except for appearing on The Ronnie Corbett Show years later and then link-man for BBC2 coverage of The Cambridge Folk Festival.  He then became a regular contributor on The 6 oclock Show with Michael Aspel. He had to wait over a decade to be given his own show.

His first own TV Special was in 1985 for Thames TV, A Dabble With Digance, and featured newsreader Carol Barnes. After the success of this special he was signed to Television South TVS in Southampton after being a studio warm-up act there for Matthew Kelly and this first series of six programmes screened on Thursday nights. He also filmed a pilot The Three Busketeers featuring himself, Chris Barrie who went on to star in Red Dwarf, and Adrian Hedley. At this time he still continued as a studio warm-up act for The Des OConnor Show. He was also appearing regularly on late night shows for Tyne Tees Television with Gillian Reynolds.

Richard left for London Weekend Television under Greg Dyke and filmed numerous Saturday night TV Specials for ITV. His guests included Status Quo, Brian May, Elkie Brooks, The Moody Blues, Marc Cohn, Buffy St Marie, Joe Pasquale, Juan Martin, Julia Fordham, Chris de Burgh and many others. An additional series with Jim Davidson Wednesday At 8 made him a popular guest during this period from 1985 to 1995. His guest-spots are almost endless; Surprise, Surprise (TV series) Surprise Surprise with Cilla Black - The Gloria Hunniford Show - The Jim Davidson Show - Des OConnor Show - Live From Her Majestys - Live From Piccadilly - Summertime Special with Michael Barrymore -  Live From The Palladium with Roy Orbison - The Comedians (1971 TV series) The Comedians - Magpie (TV series) Magpie - Celebrity Snooker and Fish oMania with Steve Davis - Pebble Mill At One - Saturday Night At The Mill - Thats Entertainment with Kenny Everett and Julian Clary - Crosswits - The Tom OConnor Show - The Parkinson Show with Tommy Steele and many more. He received a BAFTA Nomination as TV Entertainer Of The Year.

He came to public attention as a regular turn on the popular Sunday evening Live from... (Her Majestys Theatre Her Majestys/the Piccadilly/the London Palladium Palladium) variety series (produced by LWT for ITV Network ITV) and also on Summertime Special, a variety showcase of the 1980s. Digance also was the support act for U.K. singer Elkie Brooks on many occasions.

He is known for his television one-hour specials, starting in 1985 with A Dabble of Digance. Abracadigance was a series of four shows in 1988. The 1992 show, Richard Digances Greatest Bits, recorded at the Brighton Brighton Dome, included some of his most popular routines from stage and screen, including the "Nursery Rhymes", "Remembers" and "Jungle Cup Final".

Appearances on TV Specials included HRH The Prince of Wales Princes Trust Galas at The London Palladium with Elton John and Robin Williams - All At Sea, again before HRH The Prince of Wales - Christmas Eve Forces Special from The Berlin Wall - The Zeebrugge Disaster Concert at The London Palladium with Bernard Manning.

In the next decade he made two series for Carlton Westcountry TV visiting villages in a Morris Traveller and using locals as guests.<ref name="yorkshire"/>

Digance also had a short-lived BBC Radio 2 programme titled Another Digance Indulgence, which ran over eight episodes in the summer of 1987.

Many albums featuring his comic and serious music have been released. His first novel, Run Out In The Country, was published in 1983, and his autobiography, A Wealth of Comedy, in 1999.

He was a regular guest in Dictionary Corner on the Channel 4 game show Countdown (game show) Countdown, during which he performed comic rhymes before the commercial break.<ref name="yorkshire"/>

Digance was the managing director of Creative Results and Sound TV, a Sky (UK and Ireland) satellite channel based on variety show variety, which launched in February 2005.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.thestage.co.uk/features/feature.php/7855  publisher=The Stage  title= Rejuvenating variety with Sound TV  date=19 May 2005}}</ref> However, he resigned in August 2005, and the channel went into administration.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.thestage.co.uk/news/newsstory.php/10749/variety-channel-sound-tv-off-air-within-year 
publisher=The Stage  title= Variety channel Sound TV off air within year of launch  date=6 December 2005}}</ref> He also co-founded SKD Media, later to become Entertainment Rights; these are involved in releasing nostalgic programming on DVD.

In October 2003 he received the Gold Award from the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors for his services to music.<ref name="devon"/><ref>{{cite web  url = http://www.goldbadgeawards.com/legacy/legacy2003.htm  title = Gold Badge Awards 2003  website = Gold Badge Awards  accessdate = 16 January 2014  deadurl = yes  archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20140116220356/http://www.goldbadgeawards.com/legacy/legacy2003.htm  archivedate = 16 January 2014  df = dmy-all }}</ref>

He also annually takes to the stage at Cropredy Festival for the first set on the final day.

He appeared at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe in 2013,<ref>{{cite web  url = https://www.edfringe.com/whats-on/music/richard-digance  title = Richard Digance - Edinburgh Festival Fringe  website = Edinburgh Festival Fringe  accessdate = 16 January 2014  deadurl = yes  archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20131020075122/https://www.edfringe.com/whats-on/music/richard-digance  archivedate = 20 October 2013  df = dmy-all }}</ref> performing a solo show, and also his childrens musical, War of the Worms, narrated by his daughter, Rosie. He aimed to perform 22 completely different shows across 22 nights.<ref>{{cite web  title=Interview: Richard Digance  date=2 August 2013   url=http://www.gigglebeats.co.uk/2013/08/interview-richard-digance/  work=Giggle Beats}}</ref>
