
Christopher DElia (born March 29, 1980) is an Americans American Stand up comedian stand-up comedian, actor, writer and podcast host. He is known for playing Alex Miller on the NBC sitcom Whitney (TV series) Whitney; Danny Burton on the sitcom Undateable, also on NBC; and Kenny on The Good Doctor (TV series) The Good Doctor on American Broadcasting Corporation ABC.

==Early life==
DElia was born in New Jersey, the son of TV producer and director Bill DElia,<ref name=Emmy-DElias-2013>{{cite web last1=Tomashoff first1=Craig title=Like Father, Like Son url=http://stephenschofield.files.wordpress.com/2013/03/delias.jpg website=Emmy accessdate=20 July 2014 year=March 2013}}</ref> and interior decorator Ellie DElia (née Dombroski). He has a younger brother, filmmaker/actor Matt DElia. His father is first generation Italian-American.<ref name=WPMag-Billfather-2001>{{cite web last1=Martin first1=Babara E. title=Bill D Elia: Hollywood Storyteller url=http://ww2.wpunj.edu/adminsrv/pub-info/billhollywood.htm website=WP: The Magazine of William Paterson University accessdate=July 18, 2014 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20011230112149/http://ww2.wpunj.edu/adminsrv/pub-info/billhollywood.htm archivedate=30 December 2001 date=Winter 2001}}</ref>

He was raised in Montclair, New Jersey until age twelve, when his family relocated to Los Angeles, California.<ref name=SanDiego-Interview-2011>{{cite web last1=Downs first1=Gordon title=Interview With Comedian Chris DElia url=http://www.sandiego.com/articles/2011-01-19/interview-comedian-chris-delia website=San Diego accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=January 19, 2011}}</ref> He graduated from La Cañada High School in 1998.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.latimes.com/socal/la-canada-valley-sun/community/tn-vsl-me-102030-20170522-story.html work=The Los Angeles Times title=La Cañada History: Comedian Chris DElia has role in LCHS production of ‘The Pajama Game’ date=May 22, 2017 accessdate=June 28, 2017 author=Cormaci, Carol}}</ref>

==Career==
DElia started acting in high school, and had some guest starring parts on Chicago Hope.<ref name=LastWeek-BenGleib-2012>{{cite web last1=Gleib first1=Ben title=#034: Guest Chris DElia: "God Particle Testicle Fish" url=http://smodcast.com/episodes/guest-chris-delia-god-particle-testicle-fish website=Last Week on Earth with Ben Gleib publisher=Smodcast accessdate=August 15, 2014 format=Audio interview/podcast date=July 11, 2012 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150427111131/http://smodcast.com/episodes/guest-chris-delia-god-particle-testicle-fish/ archivedate=April 27, 2015}}</ref>

He attended New York University and studied acting but dropped out after a year because he didnt like college.<ref name=LastWeek-BenGleib-2012/> He then appeared in a movie that went straight to DVD. During his downtime as an actor he started writing scripts. When he was 25 he decided to do standup, which he had always wanted to do.<ref name=LenaLamoray-Interview-2011>{{cite web last1=Lamoray first1=Lena title=Exclusive Interview Chris D’Elia, Chris plays STANKOWSKI in GLORY DAZE on TBS url=http://www.lenalamoray.com/2011/02/02/exclusive-interview-chris-delia-chris-plays-stankowski-in-glory-daze-on-tbs/ website=Lena Lamoray accessdate=July 20, 2014 date=February 2, 2011}}</ref>

DElia has been doing stand-up comedy since 2006.<ref name=SanDiego-Interview-2011/> He considers himself to be a standup comedian who acts.<ref name=LenaLamoray-Interview-2011/> He has been featured on Comedy Centrals Live at Gotham, Comedy Central Presents, and Showtime (TV network) Showtimes Live Nude Comedy. He was introduced to a broader audience as a regular on the series Glory Daze (TV series) Glory Daze,<ref name=Chronicle-Whitney-2011>{{cite news last1=Wiegand first1=David title=Whitney review: Too much stand-up in sitcom url=http://www.sfchronicle.com/tv/article/Whitney-review-Too-much-stand-up-in-sitcom-2309219.php accessdate=July 20, 2014 work=San Francisco Chronicle page=E1 date=21 September 2011}}</ref> which ran for one season on TBS (U.S. TV channel) TBS, playing "the Oracle", William Stankowski. He was originally only cast for the pilot, but was made a series regular.<ref name=LenaLamoray-Interview-2011/>

He co-starred in the NBC comedy series Whitney (TV series) Whitney, opposite comedian Whitney Cummings, for two seasons.<ref name=Chronicle-Whitney-2011 /><ref name=HuffPo-DatingDealBreakers-2012>{{cite web last1=Clements first1=Erin title=Whitney Cummings, Chris DElia Talk Dating Deal-Breakers, Go-To Excuses For HuffPosts #nofilter url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/11/15/whitney-cummings-chris-delia-no-filter_n_2131916.html website=Huffington Post accessdate=July 20, 2014 date=November 15, 2012}}</ref>

DElia was one of three hosts of the Ten Minute Podcast (from 2012 to 2015), along with Bryan Callen and Will Sasso. Since February 2017, he has hosted the weekly podcast Congratulations with Chris DElia, which has charted in the top 20 comedy podcasts in multiple countries.{{citation needed date=March 2018}} Other podcasts have been heavily influenced by his style, including The Fighter and the Kid, hosted by friend Bryan Callen and former MMA fighter turned E! host Brendan Schaub.<ref>{{Citation last=The Fighter and The Kid title=The Fighter and The Kid - Episode 265: Chris DElia date=2017-06-05 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KfeCYPWyj7s accessdate=2018-03-24}}</ref>

DElia also gained over 2 million followers on Vine (service) Vine.

In January 2013, DElia released his debut parody rap album, as MC "Chank Smith", called Such Is Life (produced by Mr. Green (record producer) Mr. Green).<ref name=LAWeekly-Rapper-2013>{{cite news last1=Gutierrez first1=Juan title=Chris DElias Loveably Clueless Rap Persona url=http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2013/09/12/chris-delias-loveably-clueless-rap-persona accessdate=July 20, 2014 work=LA Weekly date=September 12, 2013}}</ref>

On December 6, 2013, DElias first one-hour stand-up special, White Male Black Comic, aired on Comedy Central.<ref name=GQ-CCSpecial-2013>{{cite web last1=Ortiz first1=Jen title=The GQ+A: Comedian Chris DElia on His Comedy Central Special and Getting Heckled by Drunk Girls url=https://www.gq.com/blogs/the-feed/2013/12/the-gqa-comedian-chris-delia-on-his-comedy-central-special-and-getting-heckled-by-drunk-girls.html website=GQ accessdate=July 20, 2014 date=December 2, 2013}}</ref> It was directed by his father.<ref name=LarryKingNow-2013/>

In May 2014, DElia was cast in the lead role of the NBC sitcom Undateable, which premiered on May 29, 2014,<ref>{{cite web last=Kasperowicz first=Leslie url=http://www.cinemablend.com/television/Whitney-Chris-Elia-Joins-NBC-Undateable-Pilot-53445.html title=Whitneys Chris DElia Joins NBCs Undateable Pilot work=Cinemablend accessdate=March 4, 2014 date=March 3, 2013}}</ref> executive produced by Bill Lawrence (TV producer) Bill Lawrence.<ref name=NYTimes-UndateableReview-2014>{{cite news last1=Stanley first1=Alessandra title=A Bunch of Friends? Yeah, Again: ‘Undateable’ Keeps a Sitcom Formula Alive url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/05/29/arts/television/undateable-keeps-a-sitcom-formula-alive.html?_r=0 accessdate=July 18, 2014 work=New York Times date=May 28, 2014}}</ref> The show is a multi-camera sitcom with a live audience,<ref name=Starry-DElia-Law-2014>{{cite web last1=Steinberg first1=Lisa title=Interviews – TV: Chris DElia & Bill Lawrence – Undateable url=http://starrymag.com/?p=3165 website=Starry Constellation Magazine accessdate=July 20, 2014 date=May 23, 2014}}</ref> based on the book Undateable: 311 Things Guys Do That Guarantee They Wont Be Dating or Having Sex by Ellen Rakieten and Anne Coyle.<ref name=HReporter-UndateableCastLead-2013>{{cite web last1=Goldberg first1=Lesley title=NBCs Bill Lawrence Comedy Undateable Casts Lead  url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/nbcs-bill-lawrence-comedy-undateable-422689 work=The Hollywood Reporter accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=February 20, 2013}}</ref>

The show often incorporates improv, with DElia playing Oscar to Brent Morins straight-man Felix à la The Odd Couple (1970 TV series) The Odd Couple. The cast includes stand-up comics who were good friends before the show.<ref name=WE-5ReasonstoWatch-2014>{{cite web last1=Ray first1=Lincee title=5 reasons to watch Undateable url=http://community.ew.com/2014/06/19/five-reasons-to-watch-undateable website=Entertainment Weekly accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=June 19, 2014}}</ref><ref name=Collider-Talks-2014>{{cite web last1=Radish first1=Christina title=Brent Morin Talks Undateable, Performing Stand-Up, His Weird Life Journey with Chris D’Elia, Being Funny on Camera, Singing on the Show, and More url=http://collider.com/brent-morin-undateable-interview/ publisher=Collider (website) Collider accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=July 6, 2014}}</ref><ref name=LarryKing-Cast-2014>{{cite web last1=King first1=Larry title=The Cast of "Undateable" url=http://www.ora.tv/larrykingnow/the-cast-of-undateable-0_3yambvfisshv website=Larry King Now accessdate=26 July 2014 date=June 11, 2014}}</ref>

In 2015, Bill Lawrence and the cast (DElia, Brent Morin, Ron Funches, and Rick Glassman) went on a series of stand-up tour dates to promote the show.<ref name=Variety-HitRoad-2014>{{cite web last1=Littleton first1=Cynthia title=Bill Lawrence and Undateable Stars Hit the Road to Promote NBC Comedy Undateable url=https://variety.com/2014/tv/news/bill-lawrence-and-undateable-stars-hit-the-road-to-promote-nbc-comedy-undateable-1201120161/ website=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=February 25, 2014}}</ref><ref name=Grantland-LawrenceTourDiary-2014>{{cite web last1=Lawrence first1=Bill title=The ‘Undateable’ Comedy Tour: How to Launch (or Not Launch) a TV Show in 2014 url=http://grantland.com/hollywood-prospectus/the-undateable-comedy-tour-how-to-launch-or-not-launch-a-tv-show-in-2014 website=Grantland accessdate=July 18, 2014 date=May 28, 2014}}</ref>

In 2016, DElia headlined the Leafly 420 Comedy Tour show in Chicago with special guest Ron Funches.<ref name="events.leafly.com">{{Cite web url=https://events.leafly.com/events/leafly-comedy-tour-chicago/?_ga=1.230673301.828060596.1451429102 title=Leafly Comedy Tour Chicago website=Leafly Events access-date=2016-04-23}}</ref>

DElia has developed a significant fanbase. He jokingly calls this following his "cult".<ref>{{Cite news url=https://twitter.com/chrisdelia/status/772678653746434048?lang=en title=Chris Delia Tweet about starting cult last= first= date= work=Twitter access-date=2018-03-24 archive-url= archive-date= dead-url= language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
DElia was raised Catholic. In a standup routine, he joked about how creepy certain aspects of Catholic mass are.{{when date=January 2013}}

In 2006, DElia married actress Emily Montague. They divorced in 2010.{{citation needed date=February 2018}}

Despite the fact that his characters in Whitney (Alex Miller) and Undateable (Danny Burton) are both portrayed as frequent drinkers, DElia has never used drugs or alcohol.<ref name=TVLine-Whitney2-2012>{{cite web last1=Roots first1=Kimberly title=Whitney Season 2 Preview: Whitney Cummings on Alexs Issues, the Possible Patter of Little Feet url=http://tvline.com/2012/11/12/whitney-season-2-spoilers-whitney-cummings-kids website=TV Line accessdate=July 20, 2014 date=November 12, 2012}}</ref> At the Chicago Leafly 420 show, DElia told the audience that he does not use cannabis.<ref name="events.leafly.com"/>

DElia lives in the Beachwood Canyon, Los Angeles Beachwood Canyon area of Los Angeles.<ref name=Variety-FergusonHouse-2013>{{cite news last1=David first1=Mark title=Comedian Craig Ferguson Sells to Comic Chris DElia url=https://variety.com/2013/dirt/real-estalker/comedian-craig-ferguson-sells-to-comic-chris-delia-1201236319/ accessdate=August 15, 2014 work=Variety (magazine) Variety date=August 8, 2013}}</ref>

During an episode of The Joe Rogan Experience, he confirmed that he frequently uses a walking aid called "rollator" when shopping. He tries to work against the stigma that old people carry. "I try to make the use of Rollators okay for the young ones. Not great, just okay. Old people matter," he told the bewildered Rogan.<ref>{{Citation last=PowerfulJRE title=Joe Rogan Experience #533 - Chris DElia date=2014-08-11 url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t930gQuODIo accessdate=2017-03-20}}</ref>
