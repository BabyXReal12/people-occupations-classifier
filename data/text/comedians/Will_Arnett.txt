William Emerson Arnett ({{IPAc-en ɑː r ˈ n ɛ t}}; born May 4, 1970)<ref>{{cite journal title=Monitor journal=Entertainment Weekly date=May 10, 2013 issue=1258 pages=30}}</ref> is a Canadian-American actor, producer and comedian. He is best known for his role as Gob Bluth George Oscar "Gob" Bluth II in the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox/Netflix series Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development (2003–2006, 2013-present). He has appeared in films such as Blades of Glory (2007), Hot Rod (film) Hot Rod (2007) and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014 film) Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014). He is a prolific voice actor with film roles including Ratatouille (film) Ratatouille (2007), Monsters vs. Aliens (2009), Despicable Me (2010), The Nut Job (2014) and Batman in the The Lego Movie (franchise) Lego Movie franchise. He also plays the titular character in the Netflix series BoJack Horseman (2014–present).

==Career==
In February 1996, Arnett made his first television pilot with Kevin Pollak and his wife, Lucy Webb, for CBS, that was not picked up.<ref name="twop4" /> In 1999, Arnett starred in another pilot for The Mike OMalley Show on NBC as the protagonists friend Jimmy. The show was picked up, but was canceled after only two episodes. Arnett has referred to 2000, the year after that show was cancelled, as "the darkest year of [his] life" and he admits that he "didnt get a lot of work" and "drank those years away."<ref name="twop4"/> In summer 2000, a friend helped pull Arnett out of his battle with alcoholism, and he began to get his career back on track.<ref>{{cite web last=Chun first=Wing title=Page 5 url=http://www.televisionwithoutpity.com/show/the_will_arnett_interview.php?page=5 work="JASON IS SO MUCH LIKE A SISTER TO ME" accessdate=March 8, 2011}}</ref> In 2001, Arnett was cast in the CBS television pilot, Loomis (TV pilot) Loomis as the slacker brother of a local news reporter (Cheri Oteri), that was not picked up. In 2002, Arnett was cast in a fourth television pilot which was for the CBS sitcom Still Standing (TV series) Still Standing, which was picked up and ran for several seasons, but his character was cut from the series after the pilot.<ref name="variety" /> Arnett became so frustrated, after his fourth failed pilot, that he "swore off pilots"<ref name="variety"/> altogether, until his agent persuaded him to audition for the pilot for Arrested Development.

===Voice work===

Arnetts distinctive gravelly voice has earned him voice-over work for CBS television promos, film trailers and numerous advertisements, including Lamisil medication. Perhaps most recognizable is Arnetts voice saying, "Its not more than you need, just more than youre used to" in ads for GMC (General Motors division) GMC trucks.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0004715/otherworks "Will Arnett – Other works"], IMDb, 2007-04-22. Arnett was the announcer for the faux trailer "Dont" in the 2007 film Grindhouse (film) Grindhouse. He has also voiced characters in animated films, including Vlad in Horton Hears a Who! The production featured a Ford Mustang as K.I.T.T. Arnett opted to withdraw from the project and he was replaced by Val Kilmer.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117980382.html?categoryid=14&cs=1 work=Variety title=Val Kilmer voices Knight Rider first=Josef last=Adalian date=February 6, 2008}}</ref> Arnett made a commercial cameo for the video game Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1450746/ "Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 – voice over cast"], IMDb, 2012-02-13.
