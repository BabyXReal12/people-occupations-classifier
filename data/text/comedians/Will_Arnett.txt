William Emerson Arnett ({{IPAc-en ɑː r ˈ n ɛ t}}; born May 4, 1970)
is a Canadian-American actor, producer and comedian. He is best known for his role as Gob Bluth George Oscar "Gob" Bluth II in the Fox Broadcasting Company Fox/Netflix series Arrested Development (TV series) Arrested Development (2003–2006, 2013-present). He has appeared in films such as Blades of Glory (2007), Hot Rod (film) Hot Rod (2007) and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014 film) Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014). He is a prolific voice actor with film roles including Ratatouille (film) Ratatouille (2007), Monsters vs. Aliens (2009), Despicable Me (2010), The Nut Job (2014) and Batman in the The Lego Movie (franchise) Lego Movie franchise. He also plays the titular character in the Netflix series BoJack Horseman (2014–present).

==Early life==
Arnett was born in Toronto, Ontario,
the son of Edith Alexandra "Alix" (née Palk) and Emerson James "Jim" Arnett, who was a corporate lawyer and brewer, among other occupations.
His parents were originally from Winnipeg Winnipeg, Manitoba, and he has roots on both sides of his family in Manitoba going back many generations.
Arnett has two older sisters and a younger brother.
His father, a graduate of Harvard University and corporate lawyer, served as the president and CEO of Molson Molson Breweries from 1997 to 2000.
Arnett briefly attended Lakefield College School in Lakefield, Ontario, but was asked not to return after a semester for being a troublemaker.
The Subway Academy II allowed him to take theatre classes at the Tarragon Theatre. He eventually graduated from Leaside High School and attended Concordia University (Montreal) Concordia University, Montreal for a semester, but dropped out.
