==Early life==
Amsterdam was born in Chicago, Illinois, the youngest of the three sons of  Max and Jennie (Finder) Amsterdam, Jewish immigrants from Austria-Hungary.
He began working in vaudeville in 1922 as the Straight man (stock character) straight man for his older brothers jokes. He was a cello cellist, a skill he used throughout his career. By 1924, he was working in a speakeasy operated by Al Capone.
