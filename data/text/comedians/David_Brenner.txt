David Norris Brenner (February 4, 1936&nbsp;– March 15, 2014) was an American stand-up comedian, actor and author. The most frequent guest on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson in the 1970s and 1980s, Brenner "was a pioneer of observational comedy."<ref>{{cite journal  url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2014/03/david-brenner-1936-2014.html  title=David Brenner: 1936-2014  first=Ryan  last=Bort  date=March 17, 2014  journal=Paste (magazine) Paste}}</ref>

==Early life==
Brenner was born to Jewish parents in 1936<ref>[http://www.howardstern.com/howard-on-air/howard-100-101/2005-02-04#gallery-17432 Happy birthday Brenner!] Howard Stern</ref> and raised in poor areas of South Philadelphia South and West Philadelphia West Philadelphia.<ref name=hr/> His father, Louis, was a vaudeville comedian, singer and dancer performing under the stage name of Lou Murphy, who gave up his career and a film contract, to please Brenners grandfather, a rabbi, who objected to his working on the Sabbath.<ref name=hr/> Once he became successful, Brenner regularly sent his parents on cruises, and both of Brenners parents would eventually die at advanced ages while on cruises aboard the Queen Elizabeth 2, approximately two years apart.<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g7-eG3sRrw0  title=Interview with David Brenner: "Always Leave Em Laughing"  publisher=Reel Change Films  date=June 29, 2012  accessdate=16 August 2015}}</ref>

After high school, Brenner spent two years in the U.S. Army, serving in the 101st Airborne and as a cryptographer of the 595th Signal Corps (United States Army) Signal Corps in Böblingen, Germany. After being discharged, he attended Temple University, where he majored in mass communication and graduated with honors.<ref name=hr/>

==Career==
Brenner was a writer, director or producer of 115 television documentaries and headed the documentary units of Westinghouse Broadcasting and Metromedia, winning nearly 30 awards including an Emmy, before moving to comedy.<ref name=hr/><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.davidbrennersite.com/bio.html  title=The Brenner Accounting Blog  work=The Brenner Accounting Blog  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140316093017/http://www.davidbrennersite.com/bio.html  archivedate=March 16, 2014  df=mdy }}</ref> His first paid gig was at The Improv in June 1969, and following that he frequently performed at clubs in Greenwich Village.<ref name=hr/> After making his national television debut in 1971, on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson, he became the shows most frequent guest, with 158 appearances. He guest-hosted for Johnny Carson 75 times between 1975 and 1984, placing him fifth on the list of Carsons most frequent guest hosts. Brenner was ranked No. 53 on Comedy Central Presents: 100 Greatest Stand-Ups of All Time. At one point, he had appeared more often on major TV talk shows than any other entertainer.<ref>The Book of Lists #2 p. 203.</ref> He also wrote five books,<ref>{{cite web  url=https://www.amazon.com/David-Brenner/e/B001HMQS6A  title=Amazon.com: David Brenner: Books, Biography, Blog, Audiobooks, Kindle  publisher=  accessdate=February 5, 2016}}</ref> and starred in four HBO Specials.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20797387,00.html  title=David Brenner Dies of Cancer at 78  journal=People (magazine) People  agency=Associated Press}}</ref>

Brenner released the comedy album Excuse Me, Are You Reading That Paper? on MCA Records in 1983 (The title arose from a gag wherein a fellow passenger on a subway asked Brenner if he was reading a newspaper on which he was sitting. The punch line was Brenner saying he was, standing up, turning a page, and sitting down again.) Brenner also penned five books<ref name=hr/> including Soft Pretzels With Mustard (1983), Revenge is the Best Exercise (1984), Nobody Ever Sees You Eat Tuna Fish (1986), If God Wanted Us to Travel... (1990), and I Think Theres a Terrorist in My Soup: How to Survive Personal and World Problems with Laughter—Seriously (2003) which was also released as two audiobooks.

Brenner was the star of the 1976 TV series Snip (TV series) Snip (alternative title: Flip), which was inspired by the film Shampoo (film) Shampoo and set in a hair dressing salon.<ref name=hr/> The situation comedy was cancelled by NBC before it aired due to its inclusion in the supporting cast of what would have been List of situation comedies with LGBT characters one of the first gay characters in an American situation comedy. He also played a charity ball auctioneer in the 1989 romantic comedy Worth Winning (with Mark Harmon, Madeleine Stowe, and Lesley Ann Warren, who was also Brenners co-star on Snip).<ref name=hr/>

In 1986, King World Entertainment gave Brenner his own 30-minute syndicated late-night talk show, Nightlife (TV series) Nightlife, in an attempt to compete with Carson, but it was cancelled after one season. The show premiered September 8, 1986, on 102 stations<ref>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1144&dat=19861129&id=gYMcAAAAIBAJ&sjid=_WIEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5351,2961830  title= And the latenight winner is...  newspaper=Pittsburgh Press  date=November 29, 1986  first=Lee  last=Winfrey  accessdate=March 6, 2018}}</ref> and was touted as "Alternative comedy alternative".<ref name=b/> Filmed in Manhattan and featuring a casually dressed Brenner,<ref name=b/> it was unique among the late night talk genre for not having a monologue.<ref name=b>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1901&dat=19860829&id=QVQgAAAAIBAJ&sjid=UtIEAAAAIBAJ&pg=1495,8343291  title= Brenner Waking Up Nightlife With Own Late Show  first=Michael E.  last=Hill  newspaper=The News-Journal  location=Daytona Beach  agency=Los Angeles Times–Washington Post News Service  date=August 29, 1986}}</ref> It gave some comedians, such as Bobby Slayton, their national television premieres.<ref name="hollywoodreporter.com">{{cite web url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/david-brenner-dead-comedian-bobby-688892  title=David Brenner Dead: Comedian Bobby Slayton Remembers His Friend  first=Kimberly  last=Nordyke  newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter  date=March 15, 2014}}</ref>

In addition to the Tonight Show, Brenner also appeared on The Ed Sullivan Show, David Frost The David Frost Show, The Mike Douglas Show, Late Night with David Letterman and the Late Show With David Letterman, Real Time With Bill Maher and The Daily Show and was a frequent guest on The Howard Stern Show.<ref name=hr/> In later years he appeared on both MSNBC and Fox News Channel shows commenting on current events.<ref name=apobit/> 

The Broadcast Pioneers of Philadelphia named Brenner their Person of the Year in 1984 and inducted him into their Hall of Fame in 2003.<ref>{{cite web  title=Person of the Year Recipients: David Brenner  website=Broadcast Pioneers of Philadelphia  url=http://www.broadcastpioneers.com/davidbrenner.html  accessdate=October 30, 2016}}</ref> From 1994 to 1996, Brenner hosted a talk-radio program, taking over the timeslot of the long-running Larry King Show on the Mutual Broadcasting System.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://articles.latimes.com/1994-06-24/entertainment/ca-8138_1_talk-radio  title=Todays Talk-Radio Topic: The Future of Talk Radio  first=Claudia  last=Puig  work=Los Angeles Times  date=June 24, 1994}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/127831/david-brenner-dies-top-comedian-hosted-radio-show-  title=David Brenner Dies; Top Comedian Hosted Radio Show In 90s  work=All Access  date=March 17, 2014}}</ref> He had earlier hosted a syndicated weekly radio show, David Brenner Live, for three months in 1985.<ref>{{cite journal  url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yCQEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PT20&lpg=PT20&dq=%22david+brenner+live%22&source=bl&ots=9IY33GuRB-&sig=3B0U5yYzhTJ_qfNsrJ2E28Oo45I&hl=en&sa=X&ei=WGiBVJyEOISgyQT0iYG4DQ&ved=0CEEQ6AEwBg#v=onepage&q=%22david%20brenner%20live%22&f=false  title=Radio: Featured Programming  journal=Billboard (magazine) Billboard  date=August 31, 1985  accessdate=March 6, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1915&dat=19851102&id=CjdSAAAAIBAJ&sjid=SzYNAAAAIBAJ&pg=1498,266783  title=David Brenner show on radio is dropped  date=November 2, 1985  newspaper=The Day (New London) The Day  location=New London, Connecticut  accessdate=March 6, 2018  agency=Knight-Ridder News Agency}}</ref>

His friend, comedian Richard Lewis (comedian) Richard Lewis, described Brenner as "the king of hip, observational comedy."<ref>{{cite news  title=Comedian David Brenner dead at 78  url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2014-03-15/news/chi-comedian-david-brenner-dead-20140315_1_comedian-david-brenner-johnny-carson-documentaries  accessdate=March 16, 2014  newspaper=Chicago Tribune  date=March 15, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Brenner had three children—Cole, Slade and Wyatt.<ref>David Brenner: Back with a Vengeance!, 2000, HBO.</ref> He and the mother of Cole, his first son, fought a custody battle for several years. Brenner finally won child custody in 1992. Because family courts would have regarded him as an absentee father if he were away from home more than 50 nights a year, Brenner substantially reduced the number of appearances in his stand-up comedy work, including performances on the Tonight Show in order to secure and maintain custody of his son.<ref name=hr /><ref name=apobit>{{cite news  title=Comedian David Brenner, Tonight favorite, dies  url=https://news.yahoo.com/comedian-david-brenner-tonight-favorite-dies-222814425.html  accessdate=March 16, 2014  agency=Associated Press  date=March 15, 2014}}</ref>  Brenner married Elizabeth Slater of New York, the mother of his sons Slade and Wyatt, in the closing minutes of his David Brenner: Back with a Vengeance! HBO Special recorded in Las Vegas, on February 19, 2000. They divorced a little over a year later and also then fought two custody battles, both of which Brenner won. Brenner was engaged to Tai Babilonia in 2005, but they never married.<ref name=hr /><ref name=apobit /> For a while in the 2000s, Brenner lived in Las Vegas.<ref name="hollywoodreporter.com" />

==Death==
Brenner died on March 15, 2014, at age 78 from pancreatic cancer at his Manhattan home.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.tmz.com/2014/03/15/tonight-show-david-brenner-dead-dies-cancer/  title=Tonight Show Legend David Brenner -- On Death Bed  work=TMZ}}</ref><ref>{{cite news  url=http://reviewjournal.com/columns-blogs/news/comedy-legend-david-brenner-dead-78  title=Comedy Legend David Brenner Dead At 78  first=Norm  last=Clarke  newspaper=Las Vegas Review-Journal  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140316033659/http://www.reviewjournal.com/columns-blogs/news/comedy-legend-david-brenner-dead-78  archivedate=March 16, 2014  df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name=usobit>{{cite news  title=David Brenner, Comedian and Actor, Dies at the Age of 78  url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/david-brenner-comedian-and-actor-dies-at-the-age-of-78-2014153#ixzz2w9WhnlUv  accessdate=March 16, 2014  first=Stephanie  last=Webber  newspaper=Us Weekly  date=March 15, 2014}}</ref>
