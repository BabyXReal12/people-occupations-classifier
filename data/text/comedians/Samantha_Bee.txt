Samantha Jamie Bee (born October 25, 1969)<ref name="bio" /><ref name="TCE" /> is a Canadian-American comedian, writer, producer, political commentator, actress, and television host. Bee rose to fame as a correspondent on The Daily Show The Daily Show with Jon Stewart, where she became the longest-serving regular correspondent.<ref name="alumni" /> In 2015, she departed the show after 12 years to start her own show, Full Frontal with Samantha Bee.

Bee became a US citizen in 2014, while retaining her Canadian citizenship. In 2017, Time (magazine) Time named her one of the 100 most influential people in the world on their annual Time 100 Time 100 list.

==Early life and education==
Bee was born in Toronto, Ontario, the daughter of Debra and Ronald Bee.<ref name="book" /> She has said of her family: "Dating from well before the turn of the 20th century, if there has ever been a successful, happy marriage in my family lineage, Ive yet to hear about it."<ref name="Fresh Air">{{cite web url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=127217785 title=The Not-So-Secret Life Of Samantha Bee date=June 2, 2010 publisher=NPR work=Fresh Air accessdate=November 1, 2010}}</ref> Bees parents split up soon after her birth, and she was initially raised by her grandmother, who worked as a secretary at the Catholic school Bee attended,<ref name="Crashed">{{cite web url=https://www.rollingstone.com/tv/features/how-samantha-bee-crashed-the-late-night-boys-club-20160630 title=How Samantha Bee Crashed the Late-Night Boys Club work=Rolling Stone date=June 30, 2016 accessdate=July 3, 2016}}</ref> on Roncesvalles Avenue during her childhood. She attended Humberside Collegiate Institute and York Memorial Collegiate Institute.<ref name=TorontoStar>{{cite web url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/stage/2009/10/10/samantha_bee_a_beeautiful_life.html title=Samantha Bee: A Bee-autiful Life last=Ouzounian first=Richard work=Toronto Star date=October 10, 2009 accessdate=March 21, 2015}}</ref>

After graduating from high school, Bee attended McGill University, where she studied humanities. Dissatisfied with a range of issues at the school, she transferred to the University of Ottawa after her first year. At the University of Ottawa, Bee signed up for a theatre class thinking it would be easy. The class led to Bee discovering her love of performing.<ref name="bio">{{cite web url=http://ic.galegroup.com/ic/bic1/BiographiesDetailsPage/BiographiesDetailsWindow?failOverType=&query=&prodId=BIC1&windowstate=normal&contentModules=&display-query=&mode=view&displayGroupName=Biographies&limiter=&currPage=&disableHighlighting=false&displayGroups=&sortBy=&search_within_results=&p=BIC1&action=e&catId=&activityType=&scanId=&documentId=GALE%7CK1650004669&source=Bookmark&u=mlin_b_northest&jsid=3de1204273193c50638555a32fe31d7b title=Samantha Bee publisher=Biography in Context {{subscription required}} accessdate=February 9, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Crashed" /> Bee later enrolled in the George Brown Theatre School in Toronto.<ref name="TorontoStar" />

==Career==

===Career beginnings===
Bee started auditioning for acting roles in Toronto while working as a waitress.<ref name="Crashed" /> At age 26, Bee toured with a stage production of Sailor Moon where she played the titular role.<ref name="Crashed" /><ref>{{cite news title=Daily Shows Bee helps keep Canada in the "news": However, correspondent does not hide her past as Sailor Moon at the CNE last=Wyatt first=Nelson date=July 22, 2005 work=Edmonton Journal agency=The Canadian Press}} (754 words)</ref> Bee performed in Sailor Moons "A" cast and future husband Jason Jones (actor) Jason Jones was a member of the "B" cast.<ref name="Crashed" />

Bee was one of the four founding members of Toronto-based sketch comedy troupe The Atomic Fireballs.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/2016/07/28/toronto-native-samantha-bee-has-a-message-for-unhappy-americans-canada-is-full.html title=Toronto native Samantha Bee has a message for unhappy Americans: Canada is full last=Sullivan first=Margaret date=July 28, 2016 website=thestar.com publisher=Toronto Star access-date=July 28, 2016}}</ref> The Fireballs were all women. Demonstrating mutual support, the group would try to perform as many of each others ideas as they could.<ref name="Smirking">{{Cite news url=http://nymag.com/thecut/2016/01/samantha-bee-full-frontal-c-v-r.html# title=Smirking in the Boys Room with Samantha Bee last=Taister first=Rebecca date=January 26, 2016 work=The Cut publisher=New York Media, LLC access-date=December 28, 2016}}</ref>
