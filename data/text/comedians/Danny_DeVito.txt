
Daniel Michael DeVito Jr. (born November 17, 1944) is an American actor and filmmaker. He gained prominence for his portrayal of the taxi dispatcher Louie De Palma in the television series Taxi (TV series) Taxi (1978–1983), which won him a Golden Globe and an Emmy.

A major film star, he is known for his roles in Tin Men, Throw Momma from the Train, One Flew Over the Cuckoos Nest (film) One Flew Over the Cuckoos Nest, Ruthless People, Man on the Moon (film) Man on the Moon, Terms of Endearment, Romancing the Stone, Twins (1988 film) Twins, Batman Returns, Look Whos Talking Now, Big Fish, Other Peoples Money, Get Shorty (film) Get Shorty, Be Cool and L.A. Confidential (film) L.A. Confidential, and for his voiceover in such films as Space Jam, Hercules (1997 film) Hercules and The Lorax (film) The  Lorax.

DeVito and Michael Shamberg founded Jersey Films. Soon afterwards, Stacey Sher became an equal partner. The production company is known for films such as Pulp Fiction, Garden State (film) Garden State, and Freedom Writers. DeVito also owned Jersey Television, which produced the Comedy Central series Reno 911!. DeVito and wife Rhea Perlman starred together in his 1996 film Matilda (1996 film) Matilda, based on Roald Dahls childrens novel. DeVito was also one of the producers nominated for an Academy Award for Best Picture for Erin Brockovich (film) Erin Brockovich.

He stars as Frank Reynolds (Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia) Frank Reynolds on the FX (TV channel) FX and FXX sitcom Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia. He directed, produced and appeared in graphic, short, horror films for his Internet venture The Blood Factory.<ref>{{cite web  last1=Decker  first1=Sean  title=Exclusive: Danny DeVito Talks The Blood Factory and More - Dread Central  url=https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/23195/exclusive-danny-devito-talks-the-blood-factory-and-more/  website=www.dreadcentral.com  date=25 March 2011}}</ref>

DeVitos short stature is the result of multiple epiphyseal dysplasia multiple epiphyseal dysplasia (Fairbanks disease), a rare genetic disorder that affects bone growth in those afflicted.<ref>{{cite web last1=Jenkins first1=Mark title=For Richer And For Poorer, But What Of That Vanishing Middle? url=https://www.npr.org/2013/09/26/224525155/for-richer-and-for-poorer-but-what-of-that-vanishing-middle website=NPR accessdate=5 October 2015 date=26 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last1=Joseph first1=Pat title=Lights, Camera, Economics  Robert Reich brings his message to the big screen. url=http://alumni.berkeley.edu/california-magazine/fall-2013-film-issue/lights-camera-economics website=Berkeley accessdate=5 October 2015}}</ref>

==Early life==
DeVito was born in Neptune Township, New Jersey, the son of Daniel DeVito Sr., a small business owner, and Julia DeVito ({{nee}} Moccello).<ref>{{Cite web title=Biography - Yahoo! Life is good url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/apr/15/danny-devito-interview-sunshine-boys work=The Guardian author=Barbara Ellen date=14 April 2012}}</ref> He is of Italian descent; his family is originally from San Fele, Basilicata.<ref>{{cite web url=http://articles.cnn.com/2010-06-29/travel/tl.italian.getaway.devito_1_sea-view-rooms-restaurant?_s=PM:TRAVEL  title=My favorite place: Danny DeVito  author=Dani Shapiro  date=29 June 2010  work=CNN  accessdate=31 May 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101111000532/http://articles.cnn.com/2010-06-29/travel/tl.italian.getaway.devito_1_sea-view-rooms-restaurant?_s=PM%3ATRAVEL  archivedate=11 November 2010  df= }}</ref> He was raised in Asbury Park, New Jersey.<ref>Shaw, David.[http://www.cigaraficionado.com/Cigar/CA_Profiles/People_Profile/0,2540,8,00.html "DeVito! "Danny DeVito was born in 1944 in the shore town of Neptune, New Jersey—hence the name of his production company—and raised in neighboring Asbury Park, the youngest of five children (two of whom died before he was born)."</ref>

DeVito was raised as a Catholic and attended Oratory Preparatory School, a boarding school in Summit, New Jersey, graduating in 1962. He went to the boarding school when he was 14 after he persuaded his father to send him there to keep him out of trouble.<ref name=Guardian/> After leaving the boarding school, he trained at the American Academy of Dramatic Arts, from which he graduated in 1966. In his early theater days, he performed with the Colonnades Theater Lab, at the Eugene ONeill Theater Center in Waterford, Connecticut, and, along with his future wife, Rhea Perlman, appeared in plays produced by the Westbeth Playwrights Feminist Collective.

==Career==

===Film acting===
File:Danny DeVito by Gage Skidmore.jpg thumb right upright DeVito at the San Diego Comic-Con International in July 2010
DeVito played Martini in the 1975 film One Flew Over the Cuckoos Nest (film) One Flew Over the Cuckoos Nest, reprising his role from the 1971 One Flew Over the Cuckoos Nest (play) off-Broadway play of the same title. He gained fame in 1978 playing Louie De Palma, the short but domineering dispatcher for the fictional Sunshine Cab Company, on the hit TV show Taxi (TV series) Taxi. After Taxi ended, DeVito began a successful film career, starting with roles in 1983s Terms of Endearment, as the comic rogue in the romantic adventure Romancing the Stone, starring Michael Douglas and Kathleen Turner, and its 1985 sequel, The Jewel of the Nile. In 1986, DeVito starred in Ruthless People with Bette Midler and Judge Reinhold, and in 1987, he made his feature-directing debut with the dark comedy Throw Momma from the Train, in which he starred with Billy Crystal and Anne Ramsey. Two years later, DeVito reunited with Douglas and Turner in War of the Roses (film) The War of the Roses, which he directed and in which he co-starred.

DeVitos work during this time includes Other Peoples Money with Gregory Peck, director Barry Levinsons Tin Men as a competitive rival salesman to Richard Dreyfuss character, two co-starring vehicles with Arnold Schwarzenegger (the comedies Twins (1988 film) Twins and Junior (1994 film) Junior), and playing Penguin (comics) The Penguin as a deformed sociopath in director Tim Burtons Batman Returns (1992) as well in the 1996 film adaptation Matilda (1996 film) Matilda in which he played the villainous car dealer and Matildas father Harry Wormwood.

Although generally a comic actor, DeVito expanded into dramatic roles with The Rainmaker (1997 film) The Rainmaker (1997), Hoffa (1992), which he directed and in which he co-starred with Jack Nicholson, Jack the Bear (1993), L.A. Confidential (film) L.A. Confidential, The Big Kahuna (film) The Big Kahuna, and Heist (film) Heist (2001 in film 2001), as a gangster nemesis of Joe Moore (Gene Hackman).

DeVito has an interest in documentaries: In 2006, he began a partnership with Morgan Freemans company ClickStar, on which he hosts a documentary channel called Jersey Docs. He was also interviewed in the documentary Revenge of the Electric Car, about his interest in and ownership of electric vehicles.

===Theatre===
In April 2012, DeVito made his West End theatre West End acting debut in a revival of the Neil Simon play The Sunshine Boys as Willie Clark - alongside Richard Griffiths.<ref name=Esquire /> The play previewed at the Savoy Theatre in London from 27 April 2012, opened on 17 May and played a limited 12-week season until 28 July.<ref>{{cite web title=The Sunshine Boys - Reviews url=http://www.whatsonstage.com/west-end-theatre/reviews/05-2012/the-sunshine-boys_4231.html publisher=Whats On Stage accessdate=25 April 2014 date=18 May 2012}}</ref>

DeVito made his Broadway theatre Broadway debut in a Roundabout Theatre Company revival of the Arthur Miller play The Price (play) The Price as Gregory Solomon, for which he was nominated for a Tony Award. The production began preview performances at the American Airlines Theatre on February 16, 2017 and opened on March 16 for a limited run through May 7.

===Producing===
DeVito has become a major film and television producer. Through Jersey Films, he has produced many films, including Pulp Fiction (film) Pulp Fiction, Get Shorty (film) Get Shorty, Erin Brockovich (film) Erin Brockovich (for which he received an Academy Award nomination for Best Picture), Gattaca, and Garden State (film) Garden State. In 1999, DeVito produced and co-starred in Man on the Moon (film) Man on the Moon, a film about the unusual life of his former Taxi co-star, Andy Kaufman, who was played in the film by Jim Carrey. DeVito also produced the Comedy Central series Reno 911!, as well as the film spin-off Reno 911!: Miami.{{cn date=June 2018}}

DeVito made his directorial debut in 1984 with The Ratings Game. He then directed and also starred in Throw Momma from the Train (1987),<ref>{{cite web   first=Janet last=Maslin authorlink=Janet Maslin   title=Throw Momma from the Train  url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B0DE5D81139F932A25751C1A961948260   work=The New York Times   date=1987-12-01}}</ref> The War of the Roses (film) The War of the Roses (1989), Hoffa (1992), Matilda (1996 film) Matilda (1996), Death to Smoochy (2002) and Duplex (film) Duplex (2003). The War of the Roses (film) The War of the Roses was a commercial and critical success, as was the film adaptation of Roald Dahls Matilda (novel) Matilda. However, Death to Smoochy and Duplex (film) Duplex both had mixed reviews.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/chart/?view=Director&id=dannydevito.htm  title=Danny DeVito Movie Box Office Results  publisher=Boxofficemojo.com  date= accessdate=2010-02-10}}</ref> He also directed the film Queen B in 2005.<ref name=Variety>{{cite web last=Schneider first=Michael title=DeVito king of ‘Queen B’ url=https://variety.com/2005/scene/news/devito-king-of-queen-b-1117919178/ work=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=25 April 2014 date=9 March 2005}}</ref>
