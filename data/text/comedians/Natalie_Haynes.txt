==Books==
Haynes has written a non-fiction book, The Ancient Guide To Modern Life, on the subject of how living well in the present requires some recourse to the ancient world. It was published by Profile Books in November 2010.

Her first childrens novel, The Great Escape, was published by Simon & Schuster in September 2007. It won a PETA Proggy award, for best animal-friendly childrens book, in 2008.

Haynes debut novel Amber Fury (titled The Furies in the U. S.) was released in 2014. Her second novel Children of Jocasta, a retelling of Antigone (Sophocles play) Antigone and Oedipus Rex, was released in 2017.<ref>{{cite web   title = The Children of Jocasta   url = https://www.panmacmillan.com/authors/natalie-haynes/the-children-of-jocasta   author = <!--Not stated-->   website = Pan Macmillan   date = 5 April 2017    accessdate = 16 May 2017}}</ref>

Haynes contributed an essay to Serenity Found, a book about Joss Whedons television show Firefly (TV series) Firefly, edited by Jane Espenson, which was published in 2007 by BenBella Books. Her entries on subjects from Agatha Christie to E. F. Benson E.F. Benson can be found in Cassells Little Black Book of Books, published in 2007.
