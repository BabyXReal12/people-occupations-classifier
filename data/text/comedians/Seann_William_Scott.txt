Seann William Scott (born 3 October 1976) is an American actor, comedian, and producer. His most recognized roles are Steve Stifler in the American Pie (series) American Pie film series (1999–2012) and Doug Glatt in both Goon (film) Goon (2011) and Goon: Last of the Enforcers (2017). He has also starred in films including Final Destination (film) Final Destination (2000), Road Trip (film) Road Trip (2000), Dude, Wheres My Car? (2000), Evolution (2001 film) Evolution (2001), The Rundown (2003), The Dukes of Hazzard (film) The Dukes of Hazzard (2005), Role Models (2008), and Cop Out (2010 film) Cop Out (2010). He voiced Crash the opossum in four theatrical films and two television specials within the Ice Age (franchise) Ice Age series (2006–2016).

==Early life==
Seann was born in Cottage Grove, Minnesota. He is the son of Patricia Anne (Married and maiden names née Simons), a homemaker, and William Frank Scott, a factory worker. Seann is the youngest of seven children. He has English Americans English, Irish Americans Irish, Scottish Americans Scottish, and German Americans German ancestry.

==Career==
File:Seann William Scott TIFF 2011.jpg thumb 150px right Seann in 2011
Seanns first commercial success came with the 1999 film American Pie (film) American Pie, as Steve Stifler, a role he reprised in three sequels, American Pie 2 (2001), American Wedding (2003), and American Reunion (2012).
For his supporting role in the first film, Seann was paid only $8,000.
Seann was granted the lead role in American Wedding, though he only received second billing, and the character propelled him into the public consciousness. Seanns fear of Typecasting (acting) typecasting led him to play different types of characters post-American Pie, such as a hapless nerd in the horror film Final Destination (film) Final Destination and a friendly stoner in Dude, Wheres My Car?. He also appeared in the film Bulletproof Monk.

Seann appeared in Kevin Smiths Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back as a guitar-playing animal rights activist, and had a starring part as a police officer in Southland Tales, where he reunited with Dwayne Johnson Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson, with whom he had previously worked in The Rundown. Seann hosted the MTV Movie Awards 2003 with Justin Timberlake. The two performed several skits, including scenes from The Matrix Reloaded, which are on The Matrix Reloaded DVD. He has also hosted Saturday Night Live and appeared as a guest co-host on Live with Regis and Kelly. He voiced the character Crash in Ice Age: The Meltdown and reprised his role in its sequel Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs (2009) and Ice Age: Continental Drift (2012). He co-starred in the action comedy Cop Out (2010 film) Cop Out.
In 2011 he starred in the Sports film sports comedy film Goon (film) Goon as Doug The Thug Glatt.
In 2012, he starred in Movie 43 in the segment Happy Birthday.{{citation needed date=July 2015}} Seann reprised his role as Steve Stifler in 2012 American Reunion. On October 2 2013, Seann guest-starred in Episode 5, Season 9 (entitled "Mac Day") of the FX (TV channel) FX television series Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia, playing Macs cousin, "Country Mac." In 2014 he starred in the Comedy-drama comedy-drama film, Just Before I Go as Ted Morgan.{{citation needed date=July 2015}} He reprised his roles as Crash in Ice Age: Collision Course (2016)
and Doug The Thug Glatt in Goon: Last of the Enforcers
(2017).
Seann made a cameo appearance as a Vermont State Trooper in the 2018 film sequel, Super Troopers 2. On May 13, 2018, it was announced that Scott was cast in Fox Broadcasting Company Foxs Lethal Weapon (TV series) Lethal Weapon as a new character named Wesley Cole replacing Clayne Crawford as Martin Riggs as the new series lead.
==Personal life==
On March 16 2011, Seann was admitted to a treatment facility for "health and personal issues". His representatives declined to confirm any details about the decision, including specifying the facility.
On 13 April, it was reported that Seann had successfully completed the treatment program.
In March 2012, Seann confirmed that he was engaged to model Lindsay Frimodt.
In January 2013, US Weekly reported that this engagement had ended and that the two had split, but would remain friends.
