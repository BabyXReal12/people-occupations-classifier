==Early years==
Pudi was born in Chicago, Illinois, the son of Teresa (née Komendant), a programmer and analyst,<ref name=poles>{{cite web  url= http://www.poles.org/db/p_names/PudiKomendant_TK.html   title= Teresa K. Pudi (Komendant)   publisher= Poles.org   accessdate= 10 June 2014 }}</ref> and Abraham Pudi. His father is American of Indian people Indian origin, and his mother is Polish people Polish (born in Pokośno, Poland, to Marianna (Organek) and Boleslaw Komendant).<ref name="souri200911">{{cite news  url= http://www.indiacurrents.com/news/view_article.html?article_id=23821dac0d05b3f450fba42258a3e12c  title= Desis on Cable: Reshma Shetty and Danny Pudi  work= India Currents  first= Ranjit  last= Souri  date=November 2009}}</ref><ref name=poles /><ref name=birth>{{cite news   url = http://articles.redeyechicago.com/2012-01-23/entertainment/30656694_1_twins-ken-jeong-nbc-sitcom   title= Community star Danny Pudi, wife have twins   work= Show Patrol   publisher= RedEye  location= Chicago   date= 23 January 2012  first= Curt  last= Wagner  accessdate= 10 June 2014}}</ref> Pudi grew up speaking Polish language Polish with his mother and grandmother.<ref name=10Things>{{cite news  url= http://www.marquette.edu/magazine/recent.php?subaction=showfull&id=1264489261   title= 10 things you don’t know about Danny Pudi   publisher= Marquette University   work= Marquette Magazine   date= Spring 2004  accessdate= 10 June 2014 }}</ref> He grew up on Chicagos south side, but he attended Notre Dame College Prep in Niles, Illinois,{{r souri200911}}<ref>The Bonnie Hunt Show, 9 October 2009.</ref> a northern Chicago suburb. In 2013, he gave the commencement address at Notre Dame College Prep.

He studied dance in Chicago and attended Marquette University in Milwaukee, Wisconsin Milwaukee, Wisconsin, graduating with a degree in Communication and Theatre in 2001.<ref name=breakout>{{cite news  title= Breakout star Danny Pudi was a hit at Marquette first   first= Nicole  last= Sweeney Etter   publisher= Marquette University   work= Marquette Magazine  url= http://www.marquette.edu/magazine/recent.php?subaction=showfull&id=1263573720&archive=  accessdate= 10 June 2014 }}</ref>

At Marquette University, Pudi was the first winner of the Chris Farley Scholarship.<ref name=breakout /> Beyond paying for a year of school, the scholarship led to him performing at an Improvisational theatre improv comedy event featuring Jim Breuer and Dave Chappelle, increasing his interest in studying improv.<ref name="souri200911" /> Pudi performed in summer stock theatre in Wisconsin after Marquette, then studied at The Second City in Chicago.<ref name="souri200911" />

==Career==
Pudi became a recruiter for an executive search firm before moving to Los Angeles in 2005 to seek  television and film roles.{{r souri200911}}{{r halverson20090916}} His telecommuting home-based job permitted him to attend auditions, helping him avoid the "struggling actor thing in terms of waiting tables, temping and so on". He performed in several television pilots before joining the cast of Community (TV series) Community.{{r souri200911}}

Pudi has appeared in multiple television advertisements for such products as Snickers, Verizon, McDonalds, T-Mobile, and Pokémon.{{r souri200911}}{{r halverson20090916}}<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/37932/community-member-becomes-one-knights-badassdom title=Escape with Melanie Lights Latest Short Film - Dread Central date=29 September 2012 publisher=}}</ref> In 2012, he appeared as a celebrity contestant on Game Show Network GSNs version of The Pyramid with fellow Community cast member Yvette Nicole Brown.  

He portrayed Arash in the comedy film Road Trip: Beer Pong,<ref>[http://themovingpicture.net/trailer-for-road-trip-ii-beer-pong-arrives] {{dead link date=May 2018}}</ref> which received mainly negative reviews, and starred in the comedy horror film Knights of Badassdom,<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.bloody-disgusting.com/news/20518 title=Community Funnyman Grabs His Sword for Knights of Badassdom! - Bloody Disgusting! date=9 June 2010 publisher=}}</ref> which was one of the highlights of San Diego Comic-Con International Comic Con 2011, according to HitFix.com. He starred in the Jones Street Station music video for "The Understanding".<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72LOA6WqHh4&feature=youtu.be title=Jones Street Station "The Understanding" date=11 August 2011 publisher=YouTube}}</ref> 

He has made cameo appearances in many internet videos, including BriTANicKs "A Monologue for Three". Pudi had a cameo appearance in Captain America: The Winter Soldier, and the live episode of Hot in Cleveland. 

In the fall of 2014, Pudi appeared in the Off-Broadway musical FOUND.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.theatermania.com/new-york-city-theater/news/08-2014/atlantic-found-casting-announced_69497.html title=Community Star Danny Pudi and More Cast in New Musical Found work=TheaterMania.com access-date=2018-03-28}}</ref> In February 2017, Pudi began his role as Teddy on the NBC sitcom Powerless (TV series) Powerless. In 2017, Pudi is currently voicing Huey in the DuckTales (2017 TV series) DuckTales reboot.<ref>{{cite web url=http://deadline.com/2016/12/ducktales-david-tennant-danny-pudi-ben-schwartz-voice-cast-disney-xd-reboot-1201872151/ title=‘DuckTales’: David Tennant, Danny Pudi, Ben Schwartz Among Voice Cast For Disney XD Reboot first=Denise last=Petski date=16 December 2016 website=Deadline.com accessdate=19 May 2018}}</ref>
