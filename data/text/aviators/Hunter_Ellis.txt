
Hunter Ellis (born July 5, 1968, Alexandria, Virginia) is an United States American military veteran and television personality.<ref name="HC2">{{cite web url=http://www.history.com/content/manmomentmachine/about-the-host title=Hunter Ellis History Channel bio publisher=History (U.S. TV channel) History Channel accessdate=December 28, 2009 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100208005611/http://www.history.com/content/manmomentmachine/about-the-host archivedate=February 8, 2010}}</ref>

A former naval aviator, he was first noted for his participation on Survivor (TV series) Survivor before going on to host several television shows for the History (U.S. TV channel) History Channel, including Tactical to Practical; Man, Moment, Machine; and Digging for the Truth, before hosting The CW Television Network The CW reality show In Harms Way (TV series) In Harms Way.

==Background==
Part of a family with a strong tradition in the United States Navy, Ellis is the grandson of Naval Aviator and Vice Admiral Donald D. Engen. He graduated from the University of Southern California with a bachelors degree in political science, before entering the Navy as a commissioned officer and being designated as a Naval Aviator following completion of flight training. Ellis graduated first in his flight training class and, during his ten-year military career, he amassed 433 carrier landings and more than two thousand hours of flight time in the F/A-18 Hornet.{{cn date=May 2018}}

After resigning his commission, Ellis worked for a time as a pilot for FedEx Corporation.<ref name="HC2"/><ref name="B2">{{cite web url=http://www.cbs.com/primetime/survivor/bio/hunter_4/bio.php title=Hunter Ellis Survivor: Marquesas bio at CBS publisher=CBS accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ellis is a member of the Fraternities and sororities fraternity Alpha Tau Omega. He currently resides in Austin, Texas, with his wife and daughter.{{cn date=May 2018}}

==Career==
Ellis was a participant in the reality TV program Survivor: Marquesas, which was filmed in 2001 and aired in 2002.  A member of the Maraamu tribe, Ellis was the third contestant voted off the show.<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2002/03/14/survivor/main503745.shtml title=Heave-Ho For Hunky Hunter last=Cosgrove-Mather first=Bootie date=March 14, 2002 publisher=CBS News accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,218117,00.html title=Plane Awful last=Shaw first=Jessica date=March 15, 2002 publisher=Entertainment Weekly accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.the-top-tens.com/lists/worst-survivor-players.asp title=Hunter Ellis profile publisher=Top-tens.comzAutos.com accessdate=May 14, 2018}}</ref> 

Subsequently, Ellis became the host of several nationally televised programs. The first of these was Tactical to Practical<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117885716.html?categoryid=1614&cs=1&query=%22Hunter+Ellis%22 title=History net launching new fronts last=Oei first=Lily date=May 6, 2003 publisher=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref> (also known as Tactical to Practical With Hunter Ellis), which aired on History (U.S. TV channel) The History Channel as a program that took a historical look at the development of common consumer electronic products which originated as military research projects.<ref name="V2"/><ref>{{cite news url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/sun_sentinel/access/405019761.html?dids=405019761:405019761&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Sep+13%2C+2003&author=Scott+Sandell++Los+Angeles+Times+Zap2it.com&pub=South+Florida+Sun+-+Sentinel&desc=PUTTING+MILITARY+TECHNOLOGY+TO+CIVILIAN+USE&pqatl=google title=PUTTING MILITARY TECHNOLOGY TO CIVILIAN USE last=Sandell first=Scott date=September 13, 2003 publisher=South Florida Sun-Sentinel accessdate=December 28, 2009}}</ref> It lasted for three seasons, with 38 episodes airing in 2003-04.

In 2004, he  began co-hosting 9 on the Town, a half-hour program airing five days a week on KCAL-TV (channel 9), an Independent station (North America) independent station in Los Angeles, California. In 2005, he began hosting another show on History (U.S. TV channel) The History Channel, called Man, Moment, Machine.<ref name="V2"/> Episodes of this show focus on the historical consequences of the momentary interaction between a particular individual and a specific technology.<ref name="HC2"/>

In 2007, Ellis became the host of Digging for the Truth,<ref name="HC1">{{cite web url=http://www.history.com/content/diggingforthetruth/meet-the-new-host title=Meet the New Host publisher=History (U.S. TV channel) History Channel accessdate=December 28, 2009 archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090429205707/http://www.history.com/content/diggingforthetruth/meet-the-new-host archivedate=April 29, 2009}}</ref><ref name ="V2">{{cite news url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117965643.html?categoryid=14&cs=1&query=%22Hunter+Ellis%22 title=History to do more Digging last=Dempsey first=John date=May 22, 2007 publisher=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=December 30, 2009}}</ref> and in 2008 became host of In Harms Way (TV series) In Harms Way.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117990132.html?categoryid=14&cs=1&query=Hunter+Ellis title=Hunter Ellis to host In Harms Way last=Levine first=Stuart date=August 5, 2008 publisher=Variety (magazine) Variety accessdate=December 29, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.realitytvworld.com/news/ex-survivor-castaway-hunter-ellis-host-cw-in-harm-way-7610.php title=Ex-Survivor castaway Hunter Ellis to host The CWs In Harms Way date=August 6, 2008 publisher=Reality TV World accessdate=December 28, 2009}}</ref>
