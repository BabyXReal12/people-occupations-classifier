
Michael Eugene "Mike" Mangold (October 10, 1955 â€“ December 6, 2015) was an American Boeing 767 and Boeing 757 757 commercial pilot for American Airlines and an aerobatics pilot. Mangold competed in the Red Bull Air Race World Series from 2004 through 2009, where he repeatedly placed first and won the World Championship in the 2005 Red Bull Air Race World Series season 2005 World Series, as well as the 2007 Red Bull Air Race World Series season 2007 World Series. His nickname and call sign in the military was "Mongo".

==Biography==

===Early life===
Mangold was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, and was the oldest of three children. He moved to California at 3 years old and then to Pennsylvania for his high school years.<ref name="vvng">{{cite news url=http://www.vvng.com/famous-red-bull-pilot-killed-in-apple-valley-plane-crash/  title=Famous Red Bull Pilot Killed in Apple Valley Plane Crash  work=Vector Valley News  date=December 6, 2015  accessdate=December 8, 2015  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208000722/http://www.vvng.com/famous-red-bull-pilot-killed-in-apple-valley-plane-crash/  archivedate=December 8, 2015  df= }}</ref>

===Career===
Image:Mike Mangold Perth Race - cropped.JPG 250px left thumb Mangold racing in Perth during the 2006 series.
Mangold began his aviation career in 1974 as a skydiver while attending the United States Air Force Academy.<ref name="vvdailypress">{{cite news url=http://www.vvdailypress.com/article/20151206/NEWS/151209840 title=Two die in plane crash work=Daily Press date=December 6, 2015 accessdate=December 8, 2015 first=Shea last=Johnson}}</ref> He went on to USAF pilot training in 1977 and learned to fly fighters, graduating from the academy the following year with a B.S. degree, and eventually attending the United States Air Force Fighter Weapons School. Mangold graduated first in his class in 1983 and earned the "Outstanding Graduate" award. During his military career Mike served in the Pacific and Contiguous United States CONUS theaters, flying nuclear, conventional, smart weapons, and air intercept missions in the F-4 Phantom, including the F-4G Wild Weasel variant. He flew Phantoms for the USAF for about 10 years.<ref name="racingjets">{{cite web url=http://racingjets.com/race_pilot/Mike%20Mangold title=Mike Mangold publisher=Racing Jets Incorporated accessdate=December 8, 2015 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151210180925/http://www.racingjets.com/race_pilot/Mike%20Mangold archivedate=December 10, 2015 df=}}</ref> After leaving active duty in 1989, he became a commercial airline pilot for US Airways and then American Airlines, flying a variety of jetliners, including the Boeing 767.<ref name="racingjets"/><ref name="redbull">{{cite web url=http://www.redbull.com/en/stories/1331763856509/mike-mangold-tribute-story publisher=Red Bull title=Tributes are paid to former world champion Mangold accessdate=December 8, 2015 date=December 7, 2015}}</ref>

Although Mangold was a jet pilot, he continued to enjoy skydiving. He was a member of the United States Parachute Association U.S. Parachute Team from 1981 to 1985 and won multiple national awards as a skydiver. He participated in a record-setting formation skydive in Anapa, Russia in 1996 as one of 296 parachutists.<ref name="vvdailypress"/><ref name="aopa">{{cite web title=Mangold killed in crash url=http://www.aopa.org/News-and-Video/All-News/2015/December/07/Mike-Mangold-killed-in-crash date=December 7, 2015 accessdate=December 8, 2015 publisher=Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association}}</ref>

Mangold started his competitive aerobatic and air show career in 1990, flying a American Champion Decathlon Super Decathlon. He went on to win medals at the U.S. National Aerobatic Championship, as well as the L. Paul Soucy Award from the International Aerobatic Club in 2002 as the highest-scoring unlimited pilot of the year.<ref name="racingjets"/> He first participated in the Red Bull Air Race in 2004 and raced in the competition until 2009, winning the World Series in 2005 Red Bull Air Race World Series season 2005 and 2007 Red Bull Air Race World Series season 2007.<ref name="redbull"/>

In 2010, Mangold retired from active Red Bull Air Racing, and became the RBAR rookie (Challenger) coach, as well as an air race commentator,<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.australianflying.com.au/news/former-red-bull-champion-dies-in-jet-crash work=Australian Flying title=Former Red Bull Champion dies in Jet Crash date=December 7, 2015 accessdate=December 8, 2015}}</ref> including for FOX Sports.<ref name="vvdailypress"/> He served for four years as president of the Racing Jets International board of directors, presiding over the Reno Air Race until 2013.<ref>{{cite web url=http://racingjets.com/content/rji-elects-new-board publisher=Racing Jets International date=September 14, 2013 accessdate=December 8, 2015 title=RJI elects new board deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151210182923/http://racingjets.com/content/rji-elects-new-board archivedate=December 10, 2015 df=}}</ref> Mangold also sat on the board of directors for the Classic Jet Aircraft Association.<ref name="vvdailypress"/>

===Death===
Mangold died on December 6, 2015, in an air crash. His Aero L-39 Albatros crashed and exploded shortly after takeoff at about 2:20 p.m. from Apple Valley Airport (California) Apple Valley Airport, California, killing Mangold and one other person on board.<ref name="vvng"/><ref name="aopa"/> He was survived by his wife Julie, also an aerobatic pilot, and their children Nick and Melissa.<ref name="vvdailypress"/>
