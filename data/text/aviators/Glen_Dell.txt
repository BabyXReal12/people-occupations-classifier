
Glen Dell (9 April 1962 â€“ 12 October 2013) was a South African commercial airline trainer and aerobatics pilot, who was qualified to race in the Red Bull Air Race World Championship in seasons 2008_Red_Bull_Air_Race_World_Series_season 2008 and 2009_Red_Bull_Air_Race_World_Championship 2009.

Dells father was a World War II pilot, and he himself got his Private Pilot License around the year 1979. He then joined the South African Air Force to fly helicopters. After obtaining his Commercial Pilot License, he joined South African Airways in 1994, where he became a Senior Training Captain.<ref>[http://www.fakr.co.za/press_releases.htm Krugersdorp Flying Club] {{webarchive url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070112220311/http://www.fakr.co.za/press_releases.htm  date=12 January 2007 }}</ref><ref name="glendellaerobatics.com">[http://www.glendellaerobatics.com Glen Dell Aerobatics - Passion for and Excellence in Aviation<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref name="slick360.com">[http://www.slick360.com Slick Aircraft - The ultimate edge in aerobatic aircraft<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

Starting in 1985, he won the National Aerobatic Championships in various categories 12 times. He competed in the World Aerobatic Championships since 1994, and became Advanced World Aerobatic Champion in 2004.<ref name="glendellaerobatics.com"/><ref name="slick360.com"/><ref>[http://www.air-races.com/pilots/Glen_Dell.htm Red Bull Air Races]</ref>

Dell died in Sunninghill Hospital in Johannesburg suffering burns sustained when his aircraft, an Extra EA-330 crashed on 12 October 2013 at the Secunda Airshow in Secunda, Mpumalanga Secunda, Mpumalanga.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.news24.com/SouthAfrica/News/Aerobatic-pilot-dies-after-show-crash-20131012  title=Aerobatic pilot dies after show crash  publisher=News24  date=  accessdate=2013-10-13}}</ref>
