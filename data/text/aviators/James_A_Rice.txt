
James A. Rice (born November 15, 1957<ref name=":0">{{Cite web url=http://courts.mt.gov/supreme/bios/rice title=courts.mt.gov > supreme > bios > rice website=courts.mt.gov language=en-US access-date=2017-12-19}}</ref>) is an United States American attorney, judge, and politician. He is one of the six Associate Justices currently on the Montana Supreme Court.  Rice won an unopposed retention vote in 2014; his current term will expire in 2022.

Rice was born to parents in the United States Military who were stationed at CFS Ramore Ramore Air Force Base, near Kirkland Lake, Ontario, Canada<ref name=":0" />.  Rice grew up in eastern Montana, and graduated in 1975<ref name=":0" />.  He obtained his pilots license at 17, and worked in aviation-related jobs while attending Montana State University - Bozeman Montana State University.<ref name=":0" />  He graduated with a B.A. in political science in 1979, and then earned a J.D. from the University of Montana University of Montana School of Law in 1982<ref name=":0" />.  Rice subsequently worked as a public defender in Lewis and Clark County for four years<ref name=":0" />.

He was elected to three terms in the Montana House of Representatives, and served as Whip (politics) House Majority Whip in the 1993 session<ref name=":0" />.  He became a partner in the law firm of Jackson & Rice in Helena, Montana, in 1985<ref name=":0" />.  On March 15, 2001, he was sworn in as an Associate Justice of the Montana Supreme Court<ref name=":0" /> after appointment by Governor of Montana Governor Judy Martz<ref name=":1">{{Cite news url= title=Rice gets high court seat last=Billings first=Erin P. date=February 23, 2001 work=Independent Record access-date= archive-url= archive-date= dead-url=}}</ref>.  Rice filled the seat vacated by Karla M. Gray when she was elected Chief Justice of the Montana Supreme Court, November 2000<ref name=":1" />. In 2002, Rice won election to the remainder of the judicial term to which he was appointed<ref name=":0" />.  In 2006, he won an unopposed retention vote<ref name=":0" />. In 2014, he defeated W. David Herbert to win a third term.<ref>https://www.bozemandailychronicle.com/news/politics/wheat-rice-win-re-election-to-montana-supreme-court/article_0b6449cc-64b2-11e4-80a4-6be7969980c7.html</ref>
