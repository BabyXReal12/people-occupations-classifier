Charles Stephen Rogers (born March 28, 1942)<ref name="normandin"/> was a British Columbia politician and cabinet minister from 1975 to 1991. Rogers ran for the leadership of the British Columbia Social Credit Party in the 1986 leadership race, but lost to Bill Vander Zalm.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=wy5mAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Kn8NAAAAIBAJ&pg=1459,1972505  title=Two ministers quit B.C. jobs  newspaper=Calgary Herald  date=August 6, 1986  page=5  accessdate=2012-02-15}}</ref>

He was born in Vancouver, British Columbia, the son of Forrest Rogers and Gwynneth Thomas, and was educated in Vernon, British Columbia Vernon and Vancouver. He was elected as a Social Credit Legislative Assembly of British Columbia MLA in Vancouver South (provincial electoral district) Vancouver South in British Columbia general election, 1975 1975, British Columbia general election, 1979 1979, British Columbia general election, 1983 1983 and British Columbia general election, 1986 1986.  His career culminated in serving as Speaker of the Legislative Assembly of British Columbia Speaker until 1991 when he returned to his career as an airline pilot with Air Canada. He retired from Air Canada in 2002.<ref>{{cite news  url=http://www.ctv.ca/mini/election2006/candidates/59033_CON.html  publisher=CTV  title=Election 2006  accessdate=2012-02-15 }}{{Dead link date=June 2018  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=no }}</ref>

Rogers was forced to resign from the position of Minister of Energy, Mines and Petroleum Resources in 1986 after it was disclosed that he was in a conflict of interest position involving a personal tax shelter investment.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=yXdkAAAAIBAJ&sjid=F38NAAAAIBAJ&pg=1476,462284  title=Conflict issue snares second B.C. minister   newspaper=Calgary Herald  date=January 22, 1986  page=5  accessdate=2012-02-15}}</ref> He resigned as Minister of Health later that same year just before he was charged with failure to disclose financial holdings as required by law.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=nYJWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=V0ENAAAAIBAJ&pg=1244,2985025  title=Former minister pleads guilty to not disclosing finances  newspaper=Leader-Post  location=Regina  date=April 12, 1986  page=11  accessdate=2012-02-15}}</ref> In 1987, Rogers resigned as Minister of the Environment following conflict of interest allegations related to a change in boundaries for Strathcona Provincial Park.<ref>{{cite news  url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=kO5VAAAAIBAJ&sjid=dOEDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4715,680538  newspaper=Eugene Register-Guard  title=Environment Minister Resigns  date=March 4, 1987  page=9  accessdate=2012-02-15}}</ref>

He ran as the Conservative Party of Canada candidate for the riding of Vancouver Quadra in the Canadian federal election, 2004 2004 election and again in Canadian federal election, 2006 2006, losing both times to Liberal Stephen Owen (politician) Stephen Owen.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.parl.gc.ca/About/Parliament/FederalRidingsHistory/hfer.asp?Include=Y&Language=E&rid=763&Search=Det  title=Vancouver Quadra, British Columbia (1947 - )  work=History of Federal Ridings since 1867  publisher=Parliament of Canada  accessdate=2012-02-15}}</ref>

Rogers is a lifelong resident of Vancouver.
