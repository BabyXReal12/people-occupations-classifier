Ryan Shore (born 29 December 1974 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada) is a Canadian composer, songwriter, and music director for film composer film, Television show television, virtual reality, Albums records, Video game composer games, concerts, and theater.  He is a graduate of the Berklee College of Music.<ref name="Berklee College of Music">{{cite news  last=Small  first=Mark  title= Looking for Challenges  url=http://www.berklee.edu/bt/berklee-today-spring-2012/ryan-shore  publisher=Berklee College of Music  date=Spring 2012}}</ref>

==Music career==

Shore was Assistant Music Director and Contributing Composer / Arranger / Orchestrator for the 87th Academy Awards, where he worked with artists Lady Gaga, John Legend, Adam Levine, Anna Kendrick, Common (rapper) Common, Tim McGraw, Jack Black, Neil Patrick Harris, and Jennifer Hudson.

Shore’s composing credits include Star Wars Forces of Destiny Star Wars: Forces of Destiny (Lucasfilm), Julies Greenroom (Netflix) starring Julie Andrews with all new puppets from the Jim Henson Company, Penn Zero: Part-Time Hero Penn Zero: Part Time Hero (Disney), Scooby-Doo! WrestleMania Mystery (Warner Bros), Scooby-Doo! and WWE: Curse of the Speed Demon (Warner Bros),<ref name="filmmusicdaily.com">{{cite web url=http://filmmusicdaily.com/2016/07/28/scooby-doo-wwe-demon-ryan-shore/ title=Gangway! Get Ready for Ryan Shores Score to Scooby Doo! And WWE: Curse Of the Speed Demon! Watch the Animated Hijinks on Digital August 9 publisher=filmmusicdaily.com}}</ref>  R.L. Stines Monsterville: Cabinet of Souls (Universal Studios Universal)(Emmy Award nomination, Outstanding Music Direction and Composition),<ref>{{cite news url=http://www.axs.com/interview-emmy-award-nominated-composer-ryan-shore-82841 title=Interview: Emmy Award-nominated composer Ryan Shore last=Frederick first=Brittany date=April 28, 2016}}</ref> Spy Hunter (Warner Bros),<ref name="classicalmpr">{{cite news url=http://www.classicalmpr.org/story/2012/10/19/ryan-shore-spy-hunter-top-score-henry-mancini title=Spy Hunter Gets New Soundtrack by Ryan Shore last=Reese first=Emily work=ClassicalMPR.org  publisher=Minnesota Public Radio accessdate=2012-10-19}}</ref> The Shrine (film) The Shrine (Grammy Award nomination, Best Score) Prime (film) Prime (Universal Studios Universal) starring Meryl Streep, Cop and a Half: New Recruit (Universal), Harvard Man (Lions Gate), The Girl Next Door (2007 film) The Girl Next Door (Anchor Bay Entertainment Anchor Bay).  He also composed on-camera music for Fur (film) Fur, starring Nicole Kidman and Robert Downey, Jr., and was seen in the film performing his original music.

Shore has conducted orchestras around the world in recordings and concerts including The New York Philharmonic, The Hollywood Symphony Orchestra, The Skywalker Symphony Orchestra, The Czech Philharmonic, as well as conducting concerts for Pokémon: Symphonic Evolutions and Soundtracks Live! featuring music from Star Wars: The Force Awakens.

Shores Broadway theater Broadway and theater credits include orchestrations and arrangements for Broadway concerts starring Tony Award winners Sutton Foster, Idina Menzel, Whoopi Goldberg, Matthew Broderick, Kristin Chenoweth, Heather Headley, and Faith Prince, as well as music directing the Los Angeles production of Heathers: The Musical, directed by Andy Fickman.<ref name=Broadway.com>{{cite news  last=Snetiker  first=Marc  title=Heathers the Musical, Based on the ‘80s Movie, Set for World Premiere in Los Angeles  url=http://www.broadway.com/buzz/171449/heathers-the-musical-based-on-the-80s-movie-set-for-world-premiere-in-los-angeles/  publisher=Broadway.com  date=August 29, 2013}}</ref><ref name=PlayBill>{{cite news  last=Ehren  first=Christine  title=Film and Broadway Stars to Shine at "First Your Dream" Benefit June 12  url=http://www.playbill.com/article/film-and-broadway-stars-to-shine-at-first-you-dream-benefit-june-12-com-89423  publisher=Playbill.com  date=May 23, 2000}}</ref>

Shore plays saxophone and has performed with artists including John Williams, Matchbox Twenty, Barry Manilow, Johnny Mathis, Natalie Cole, Dave Koz, Arturo Sandoval, Gerry Mulligan, Ana Gasteyer, Mark Ballas, and Clark Terry.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.classic107.com/news/composer-and-conductor-ryan-shore-is-sarah-jo-s-guest-in-the-diamond-lane-thursday/  title= Composer and Conductor Ryan Shore in the Diamond Lane  publisher= classic107.com  date=  accessdate=January 17, 2017}}</ref>
