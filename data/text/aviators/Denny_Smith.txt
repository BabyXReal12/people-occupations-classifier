Dennis Alan "Denny" Smith (born  January 19, 1938) is a businessman and former United States congressman from the state of Oregon. A native of the state, he served in the Air Force before working in the airline industry and taking over the familys newspaper business. A Republican, he served ten years in Congress from 1981 until 1991. His father was former Oregon Governor Elmo Smith.

==Early life==
Dennis Smith was born in the Eastern Oregon city of Ontario, Oregon, on January 19, 1938.
In 1956, he graduated from Grant Union High School (John Day, Oregon) Grant Union High School in John Day, Oregon John Day and enrolled at Willamette University in Salem, Oregon Salem.
He joined the United States Air Force in 1958, and served until 1960, when he joined the Oregon Air National Guard.
Smith then earned a bachelor of arts degree from Willamette in 1961.
He returned to the Air Force in 1962 and remained there until 1967,
serving as a fighter pilot in the Vietnam War.
In the war, he flew the F-4C Phantom and completed about 180 missions.
Smith remained in the aviation field as a flight engineer and co-pilot for a commercial airline, flying from 1967 until 1976.
In 1968, he also took over the familys newspaper business when his father died.
He worked for Pan American Airlines, and in 1974 was chosen by fellow employees to serve as the unions lobbyist in Washington, DC.
He was inducted into the Oregon Aviation Hall of Honor in 2009.

==Political career==
Smith was first elected to the United States House of Representatives in United States House elections, 1980 1980 to represent Oregons 2nd congressional district as a Republican Party (United States) Republican.
He defeated 12-term Democratic Party (United States) Democrat Al Ullman by only 3,700 votes.
Smith was undoubtedly helped by Ronald Reagan winning every county in the sprawling district.  When the United States Census, 1980 1980 census gave Oregon another congressional district, Smiths home in Salem, Oregon Salem was placed in the newly created Oregons 5th congressional district 5th district.  He was elected from this district in United States House elections, 1982 1982 and was reelected three more times.  While in the House, Smith served on the United States House Committee on the Budget Budget, the United States House Committee on Resources Interior and Insular Affairs, and United States House Committee on Veterans Affairs Veterans Affairs committees.

Smith was comfortably reelected in 1984 and 1986.  In 1988, however, he was nearly defeated by Democratic Party (United States) Democrat Michael J. Kopetski Mike Kopetski, winning by only 707 votes.  Kopetski sought a rematch in 1990 and won by attacking Smith for his ties to several collapsed savings and loan crisis savings and loans.
While still serving in Congress, Smith established and headed Oregonians Against Crime, which placed Oregons 1988 Anti-crime Bill on the ballot by initiative initiative petition and successfully campaigned for its passage. In Oregon gubernatorial election, 1994 1994, Smith was the Republican nominee for Oregon governor, losing to Democrat John Kitzhaber.

==Later years==
Smith is now the chairman of Eagle Newspapers (Oregon) Eagle Newspapers, a printing and publishing company in the Pacific Northwest.
