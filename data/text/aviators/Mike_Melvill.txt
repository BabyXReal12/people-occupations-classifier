
Michael Winston "Mike" Melvill (born November 30, 1940 Johannesburg <ref>{{cite web url=https://global.britannica.com/biography/Michael-Melvill title=Michael Melvill - American pilot and astronaut publisher= accessdate=24 October 2017}}</ref>) is a world-record-breaking pilot<ref>{{cite web title=Chronological listing of all known flights around the World  url=http://www.earthrounders.com/cgi/chrono.php  publisher=Earthrounders.com  accessdate=3 July 2012  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120712175742/http://www.earthrounders.com/cgi/chrono.php  archivedate=12 July 2012  df= }}</ref>  and one of the test pilots for SpaceShipOne, the experimental spaceplane developed by Scaled Composites. Melvill piloted SpaceShipOne on its first flight past the edge of space, SpaceShipOne flight 15P flight 15P on June 21, 2004,<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.scaled.com/projects/tierone/combined_white_knight_spaceshipone_flight_tests  title=Scaled Composites: combined white knight spaceshipone flight tests  publisher=Scaled.com  date=  accessdate=2010-08-02}}</ref> thus becoming the first commercial astronaut and the 434th person to go into space.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.worldspaceflight.com/bios/m/melvill-m.php  title=M.Melvill  publisher=Worldspaceflight.com  date=  accessdate=2010-08-02}}</ref> He was also the pilot on SpaceShipOnes SpaceShipOne flight 16P flight 16P, the first competitive flight in the Ansari X Prize competition.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.spacefacts.de/bios/astronauts/english/melvill_michael.htm  title=Astronaut Biography: Michael Melvill  publisher=Spacefacts.de  date=  accessdate=2010-08-02}}</ref>

==Life and career==

In 1978, Melvill met aerospace designer and Scaled Composites founder Burt Rutan when he flew to California to show Rutan the Rutan VariViggen VariViggen he had built at his home. Rutan then hired him on the spot. In 1982, he was named Rutans lead test pilot.<ref name=Astronautix>{{cite web  url=http://www.astronautix.com/astros/melvill.htm  title=Melvill  publisher=Astronautix.com  date=  accessdate=2010-08-02  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101020020000/http://astronautix.com/astros/melvill.htm  archivedate=2010-10-20  df= }}</ref>

In 1997, Melvill and Dick Rutan, Burts brother, flew two Rutan Long-EZ Long-Eze aircraft that they built side-by-side around the world. This "around the world in 80 nights" flight was called The Spirit of Experimental Aircraft Association EAA Friendship World Tour, and some legs of it lasted for over 14 hours.<ref>{{cite web url=http://airportjournals.com/dick-rutan-the-frontiers-of-flight-the-last-great-world-record/ title=Dick Rutan The Frontiers of Flight – The Last Great World Record date=10 October 2014 publisher= accessdate=24 October 2017}}</ref>

Later in his career he became Vice President/General Manager at Scaled Composites.<ref name=Astronautix/>

==Awards and achievements==
File:Scott Crossfield, passes the torch of Space Travel to Mike Melville by D Ramey Logan.jpg thumb  The first "Commercial Astronaut", Mike Melvill, reviews the first private space flight with Scott Crossfield
Melvill is the sole or joint holder of nine Fédération Aéronautique Internationale FAI aviation world records in various categories.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://records.fai.org/data?p=707  title=History of Records: Michael W. MELVILL (USA)  publisher=records.fai.org  date=  accessdate=2010-08-02  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100729102740/http://records.fai.org/data?p=707  archivedate=2010-07-29  df= }}</ref>

He was awarded the Iven C. Kincheloe Award in 1999 for high altitude, developmental flight-testing of the Scaled Composites Proteus model 281 Proteus aircraft.<ref name=Astronautix/>

Through SpaceShipOne flight 15P in 2004, he is known as the first privately funded human spaceflight mission pilot to reach space.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.flyincruisein.com/MMelvill.html  title=Mike Melvill  publisher=Flyincruisein.com  date=2004-06-21  accessdate=2010-08-02}}</ref>
