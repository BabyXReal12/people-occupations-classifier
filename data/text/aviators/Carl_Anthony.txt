
Carl Anthony (born February 8, 1939) is an African Americans African American architect, regional planner, social justice Activism activist, and author. He is the founder and co-director of Breakthrough Communities, a project dedicated to building multiracial leadership for sustainable communities in California and the rest of the nation.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.reimaginerpe.org/20years title=Carl Anthony: Earth Day and Environmental Justice - Then and Now {{!}} Reimagine! website=www.reimaginerpe.org language=en-US access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref> He is the former President of the Earth Island Institute, and is the co-founder and former executive director of its urban habitat program, one of the first environmental justice organizations to address race and class issues.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.yesmagazine.org/issues/cities-of-exuberance/diverse-green-beautiful-cities-an-interview-with-carl-anthony title=Diverse, Green, Beautiful Cities: an interview     with Carl Anthony last=Gelder first=Sarah Ruth van work=YES! Magazine access-date=2017-07-10 language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
{{BLP unsourced section date=January 2018}}
Carl Anthony was born in a predominantly African American neighborhood in Philadelphia Philadelphia, PA known as Black Bottom, Philadelphia Black Bottom. His parents, Lewis Anthony (born William Edwards) and Mildred Anthony (née Cokine), sent Carl and his older brother Lewie to B.B. Comegys, an integrated elementary school in which only about a dozen of the 300 students were African American, rather than the segregated school called Woodrow Wilson, which was only a block away from their home. They later went on to attend Dobbins Vocational School, where Anthony was enrolled in the carpentry and cabinet-making shop. His teachers were impressed by his drawings and suggested that he transfer to the architectural drafting homeroom, where he fostered his interest in architecture.

== Education ==
Anthony graduated from Columbia University Columbia University Graduate School of Architecture, Planning, and Preservation in 1969. Upon his graduation, he was awarded the William Kinne Fellowship, a grant to enrich students’ education through travel. Anthony visited traditional towns and villages in West Africa, studying the ways in which people utilized their few resources to shape their environments.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/CARL-ANTHONY-The-head-of-the-Earth-Island-2989472.php title=CARL ANTHONY / The head of the Earth Island Institutes Urban Habitat Program is an environmentalist who strives to interweave the traditions of Martin Luther King Jr. and John Muir work=SFGate access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref>

== Early Career: Architect’s Renewal Committee and UC Berkeley ==
Anthony began his professional career in the late 1960s at the Architects Renewal Committee in Harlem, one of the first community design centers in the United States{{Citation needed date=January 2018}}. Upon his return to the United States from West Africa in 1971, he relocated to California and taught at the University of California, Berkeley as an Assistant Professor of Architecture in the UC Berkeley College of Environmental Design College of Environmental Design, later becoming a faculty member of the university’s College of Natural Resources.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web url=http://iop.harvard.edu/fellows/carl-anthony title=Carl Anthony {{!}} The Institute of Politics at Harvard University website=iop.harvard.edu language=en access-date=2017-07-06}}</ref> In 1980 he left UC-Berkeley to work as an architect and urban planner.

== Urban Habitat (1989–2000) ==
Anthony served as President of Earth Island Institute from 1991 to 1998. During this time, in spring 1996, he was an appointed Fellow at the Harvard Institute of Politics Institute of Politics, housed within the John F. Kennedy School of Government, at Harvard University.<ref name=":3" /> Alongside his colleague Luke Cole at the California Rural Legal Assistance Foundation, Anthony founded and published the Race, Poverty, and the Environmental Journal,<ref>{{Cite news url=http://mesarefuge.org/people/carl-anthony-2/ title=Carl Anthony - Mesa Refuge work=Mesa Refuge access-date=2017-07-10 language=en-US}}</ref> which was the United States’ first environmental justice periodical.<ref>{{Cite book url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VJgYDAAAQBAJ&pg=PA163&lpg=PA163&dq=%22carl+anthony%22+urban+habitat&source=bl&ots=PfRhGmWKNn&sig=lQCSNBeZ-7G-ODxmza927wYgReg&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjKytbKi__UAhUEcD4KHVLVCFwQ6AEIggEwEg#v=onepage&q=%22carl%20anthony%22%20urban%20habitat&f=false title=Activists Speak Out: Reflections on the Pursuit of Change in America last=NA first=NA date=2016-04-30 publisher=Springer isbn=9781349627592 language=en}}</ref> In 1989, Anthony founded Earth Island Institute’s Urban Habitat Program,<ref>{{Cite journal last=Yuen first=Eddie last2=Bunin first2=Lisa J. last3=Stroshane first3=Tim date=1997-09-01 title=Multicultural ecology: An interview with Carl Anthony url=https://dx.doi.org/10.1080/10455759709358748 journal=Capitalism Nature Socialism volume=8 issue=3 pages=41–62 doi=10.1080/10455759709358748 issn=1045-5752}}</ref> the mission of which is to combine education with advocacy and coalition building to advance environmental and social justice in low-income communities in the San Francisco Bay Area Bay Area, with David Brower David Bower and Karl Linn, and he served as the initiative’s Executive Director until 2000.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/kt5m3nf4b2/ title=Finding Aid to the Urban Habitat Program Records, 1970-2001 website=www.oac.cdlib.org access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref> Anthony directed various projects of Urban Habitat:
* Bay Area Justice and Sustainability Project: developed and promoted a regional agenda for justice and sustainability while addressing planning policies that lead to inner city abandonment.
* Leadership Institute for Sustainable Communities: leadership training program for land use policies and practices.
* Transportation and Environmental Justice Project: advocated for changing the priorities of the Metropolitan Transportation Commission (MTC) and the Bay Area Air Quality Management District toward addressing the transit needs of low-income communities of color.
* Brownfields Community Leadership Project: worked with leaders of low-income communities of color in the Bay Area to ensure Brownfields redevelopment addressed their needs.
* Hunter’s Point Environmental Health Project: trained residents and community leaders in Bayview Hunter’s Point in environmental health, justice issues, and laws. Partnership with the Southeast Alliance for Environmental Justice and Golden Gate University Environmental Law and Justice Clinic.
* Parks and Open Space for All People: worked toward revitalizing San Francisco Parks System by focusing on the needs of low-income communities of color, ensuring that a diverse range of people could have access to the parks.<ref name=":4" />

== Ford Foundation (2000–2008) ==
In 2000, Anthony joined the Ford Foundation. There, he served as Acting Director of the Community and Resource Development Unit. He was also Director of the Sustainable Metropolitan Communities Initiative for seven years, and funded the Conversation of Regional Equity, a dialogue between policy analysts and advocates concerning racial justice and sustainability.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://earthhousecenter.org/about-earth-house/ title=About Earth House - Earth House Center - Oakland, California work=Earth House Center -- Oakland, California access-date=2017-07-06 language=en-US}}</ref>

== Breakthrough Communities (2008–) ==
In 2008, Anthony co-founded Breakthrough Communities, a project of Earth House Center,<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.reimaginerpe.org/rpe/radio/anthony title=Carl Anthony: Earth Day and EJ {{!}} Reimagine! website=www.reimaginerpe.org language=en-US access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref> an advocacy nonprofit for regional equity and environmental and climate justice and is serving as the co-director.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web url=http://www.urbanhabitat.org/about/board title=Board of Directors {{!}} Urban Habitat website=www.urbanhabitat.org access-date=2017-07-06}}</ref> Anthony founded Six Wins, an initiative in the Bay Area addressing the mitigation of carbon dioxide emissions.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.urbanhabitat.org/campaigns/6-wins-social-equity-network title=6 Wins for Social Equity Network {{!}} Urban Habitat website=www.urbanhabitat.org access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref>

== The Earth, the City, and the Hidden Narrative of Race (2017) ==
Anthony’s memoir, The Earth, the City, and the Hidden Narrative of Race, addresses regional equity and climate change.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web url=http://www.newvillagepress.net/book/?GCOI=97660100341370& title=New Village Press website=www.newvillagepress.net language=EN access-date=2017-07-06}}</ref>
