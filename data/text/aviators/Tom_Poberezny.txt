
Thomas Paul "Tom" Poberezny (born October 3, 1946) is a former aerobatic world champion, as well as chairman of the annual Experimental Aircraft Association (EAA) EAA AirVenture Oshkosh Fly-In and Convention from 1977 to 2011 and president of EAA from 1989 to 2010. He succeeded his father, Paul Poberezny, who founded the organization in 1953.<ref name="b"/><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.nbaa.org/news/pr/2011/20110726-064.php title=NBAA Salutes EAAs Tom Poberezny for Remarkable Contributions to Industry publisher=}}</ref> 

He is also one of the founders of the Young Eagles, an EAA program created to give children the opportunity to experience flight and to learn about general aviation.<ref name=NahfInduction/> From his involvement in the EAA, Poberezny is often credited with having led the introduction of the light sport aircraft industry.<ref name="NAHF bio">{{cite web url=http://www.nationalaviation.org/our-enshrinees/poberezny-tom/ title=Poberezny, Tom publisher=National Aviation Hall of Fame}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.eaa.org/en/eaa/eaa-news-and-aviation-news/news/09-29-2016-tom-poberezny-into-national-aviation-hall-of-fame title=Tom Poberezny Into National Aviation Hall of Fame publisher=EAA.org}}</ref>

==Life and career==

File:Eagles Aerobatic Team Display.jpg thumb right Eagles Aerobatic Team aircraft flown by Poberezny, Hillard, and Soucy on display at the EAA Aviation Museum
Tom Poberezny was surrounded by aviation from the very early stages of his life. Because of his fathers early key involvement with EAA, the basement of Toms childhood home in Hales Corners, Wisconsin was considered "the regional social center of [aircraft] homebuilding."<ref name="AircraftSpruce">{{cite web url = http://www.aircraftspruce.com/catalog/kitspages/pobereznytom.php?clickkey=55532  title = Tom Poberezny and the Maturing of EAA accessdate = 2014-11-08 last = Gustafson first = David authrolink =  date = }}</ref> Poberezny graduated from Northwestern University in 1970 with a degree in industrial engineering, and became preoccupied with aviation soon after. He joined the US National Unlimited Aerobatic Team and was part of the team that won the World Championship in 1972 at Salon, France. In 1973 he won the individual US National Unlimited Aerobatic Championship.<ref name="b"/>

In 1971, Poberezny, Charlie Hillard, and Gene Soucy formed the aerobatic team The Red Devils (soon renamed the Eagles Aerobatic Team) and went on to perform at airshows until the Daytona Skyfest in 1995.<ref name="b">{{cite web url = http://www.wisconsinaviationhalloffame.org/inductees/poberezny_t.htm  title = Hall of Fame Inductee Tom Poberezny accessdate = March 31, 2016 last = Wisconsin Aviation Hall of Fame first =  authorlink =  date = }}</ref><ref name="a">{{cite web url=http://www.airventure.org/media/Tom_Poberezny.pdf title=Tom Poberezny one-sheet publisher=Experimental Aircraft Association date=April 2009 accessdate=July 9, 2009}}</ref> This makes the Eagles the longest-performing aerobatic team with one group of members.<ref name=ICAS>{{cite web  last =  first =  authorlink =  vauthors =  title =Charlie Hillard, Air Show Hall of Fame  work =  publisher =  date =  url =http://www.icasfoundation.org/hall_fame/1997/hf_hillard.htm  doi =  accessdate =July 11, 2008  deadurl =yes  archiveurl =https://web.archive.org/web/20070815234052/http://www.icasfoundation.org/hall_fame/1997/hf_hillard.htm  archivedate =August 15, 2007  df =}}</ref> Poberezny also appeared as a pilot in the movie Cloud Dancer in 1980.<ref name="c">http://images.imdb.de/title/tt0080545/fullcredits#cast</ref>

He was appointed to chairman of the EAA Convention and Fly-In (now known as AirVenture) in 1977. This annual event takes place in Oshkosh, Wisconsin and attracts over 750,000 visitors with 10,000 aircraft from 68 countries, making it the worlds largest aviation gathering.<ref>{{cite web  last=  first =  authorlink =  vauthors =  title = AirVenture – A History of The World’s Largest Airshow  work =  publisher =  date =  url = http://www.aviationschoolsonline.com/blog/history-of-airventure  doi =   accessdate =April 27, 2015}}</ref> Much of the conventions subsequent growth occurred under the leadership of Tom Poberezny.<ref name="AircraftSpruce"/> In the late 1970s, he led the campaign to build the present-day EAA Aviation Museum at Wittman Regional Airport in Oshkosh, which officially opened in 1983.<ref name="a"/>

In 1989 Poberezny was elected president of the Experimental Aircraft Association.<ref name=EAA2>{{cite web url=http://www.eaa.org/news/2007/2007-05-21_tomp.asp title=Tom Poberezny Named A Distinguished Wisconsin Aviator date=May 21, 2007 publisher=Experimental Aircraft Association accessdate=2009-07-09 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202024004/http://www.eaa.org/news/2007/2007-05-21_tomp.asp archivedate=December 2, 2008 df=}}</ref>  EAA promotes the hobby of building and flying small aircraft and has over 180,000 members worldwide. In 1992 he led the creation of the Young Eagles program, which introduces young people to aviation. The goal of giving one million kids a ride in an aircraft was met in October 2003;<ref name="a"/> and in July 2016, the two millionth Young Eagle was flown by actor and former chairman of the organization, Harrison Ford.<ref>{{cite web title=Harrison Ford Flies 2 Millionth Young Eagle url=http://www.eaa.org/en/airventure/eaa-airventure-news-and-multimedia/eaa-airventure-news/eaa-airventure-oshkosh/07-27-2016-2-millionth-young-eagle-flies-today accessdate= 25 August 2016}}</ref> 

Poberezny was a member of the Centennial of Flight Commission,<ref name="d">{{cite web url=http://www.centennialofflight.net/ title=U.S. Centennial of Flight Home Page publisher=}}</ref> a six-person board created by Congress in 1999 to coordinate the nations celebration of the 100th anniversary of the Wright brothers 1903 historic first flight.<ref name="b"/>

Poberezny has also promoted the EAAs role in the light-sport aircraft category, bringing new opportunities for people to learn to fly or keep flying.<ref name=LivingLegend>[http://spirit.eaa.org/news/2013/2013-01-23_tom-poberezny-named-living-legend.asp] EAA News (23 January 2013), Tom Poberezny Named A Living Legend</ref>

In March 2009, Paul Poberezny stepped down as chairman of EAA and Tom Poberezny took on these duties as well, with Rod Hightower as president and CEO from September 7, 2010. Tom Poberezny retained the positions of chairman of both EAA and AirVenture.<ref name=EAA1>{{cite web url=http://www.eaa.org/news/2009/2009-03-04_poberezny.asp title=EAA Names Tom Poberezny Chairman date=March 4, 2009 publisher=Experimental Aircraft Association accessdate=July 9, 2009 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090309035118/http://www.eaa.org/news/2009/2009-03-04_poberezny.asp archivedate=March 9, 2009 df=}}</ref>

On July 26, 2011, Poberezny and the EAA announced that he would be retiring from EAA effective August 1, 2011. The president and CEO, Rod Hightower, would assume Pobereznys duties until a replacement was found.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.aopa.org/oshkosh/2011/news/110726tom-poberezny-retires-as-eaa-chairman.html title=Tom Poberezny retires as EAA chairman publisher=}}</ref> However, on 22 October 2012, Hightower resigned as president and CEO of EAA, and on the same day, Jack J. Pelton was elected chairman of the EAA board of directors. He issued a press announcement saying that he would assume all leadership duties of the organization until suitable replacements could be named.<ref>[http://www.flyingmag.com/news/hightower-out-eaa-pelton-new-chairman] Flying Magazine (22 October 2012), Hightower Out, Pelton is new EAA Chairman</ref>

Poberezny currently serves on the boards of several aviation organizations, including the Board of Directors for Garmin International and the Advisory Boards of Aircraft Kit Industry Association (AKIA), Cirrus Aircraft, Citation Jet Pilots Association, and Angel Flight West.<ref name="NAHF bio"/><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.garmin.com/en-US/company/leadership/thomas-poberezny/ title=Thomas P. Poberezny publisher=Garmin}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.akia.aero/?page_id=35 title=Advisory Board publisher=AKIA}}</ref>

==Recognition==

Poberezny was inducted into the Wisconsin Aviation Hall of Fame in October 1996.<ref name="b"/> He was also awarded the Distinguished Wisconsin Aviator Award in May 2007. Past recipients of this award include astronaut Mark C. Lee, Major General Albert Wilkening, Major General Fred R. Sloan, and astronaut Jim Lovell.<ref name=EAA2/>

In early 2013, Poberezny received the prestigious Living Legends of Aviation Living Legend of Aviation award at a ceremony in Beverly Hills, California.<ref name=LivingLegend/> Later that year, a campaign and website was launched dedicated to honoring Poberezny and his accomplishments during the 20 years he led EAA. The website also included a Roster of Support for others to add to the cause. Notable proponents behind the effort consisted of aerospace engineer Burt Rutan, Cirrus Aircraft CEO and Co-founder Klapmeier brothers Dale Klapmeier, and retired test pilot test, fighter pilot fighter and air show pilot Bob Hoover.<ref>{{cite web url=http://generalaviationnews.com/2013/10/15/honorpoberezny-com-launches/ title=HonorPoberezny.com launches author=General Aviation News Staff work=General Aviation News}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.flyingmag.com/pilots-places/pilots-adventures-more/petition-urges-eaa-honor-tom-poberezny title=Petition Urges EAA to Honor Tom Poberezny author= Pia Bergqvist work=Flying Magazine}}</ref>

In 2011, Poberezny was inducted into the International Air & Space Hall of Fame at the San Diego Air & Space Museum.<ref>Sprekelmeyer, Linda, editor. These We Honor: The International Aerospace Hall of Fame. Donning Co. Publishers, 2006. International Standard Book Number ISBN Special:BookSources/978-1-57864-397-4 978-1-57864-397-4.</ref>

Poberezny was inducted into the National Aviation Hall of Fame on 1 October 2016 in Dayton, Ohio.<ref name=NahfInduction>{{cite web url=http://www.eaa.org/en/eaa/eaa-news-and-aviation-news/2015-news/11-19-2015-tom-poberezny-to-be-inducted-into-national-aviation-hall-of-fame title=Tom Poberezny to Be Inducted Into National Aviation Hall of Fame author=EAA work=EAA News}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.avweb.com/avwebflash/news/Poberezny-Joins-Hall-Of-Fame-227060-1.html title=Poberezny Joins Hall Of Fame work=AVweb accessdate=3 October 2016}}</ref>
