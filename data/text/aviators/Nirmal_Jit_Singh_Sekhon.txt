==Early life==
Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon was born on 17 July 1943
in the village of Isewal, Ludhiana district Ludhiana, Punjab Province (British India) Punjab Province, British Raj British India.
He was the son of M.W.O. (Honorary) Flight Lieutenant Tarlok Singh Sekhon.
He was commissioned into the Indian Air Force on 4 June 1967 as a Pilot Officer.

==Param Vir Chakra Award==
During the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971, he was serving with the No. 18 Squadron IAF No.18 Squadron, "The Flying Bullets" of IAF, flying the Folland Gnat fighter aircraft based at Srinagar. On 14 December 1971, Srinagar airfield was attacked by six Pakistan Air Force F-86 Sabre F-86 jets of 26 Sqn from PAF base Peshawar. Flying Officer Sekhon was on readiness duty at that time. As soon as the first aircraft attacked, Sekhon rolled for take-off as No 2 in a two-Gnat formation, with Flt Lt Ghumman in lead, just as the first bombs were falling on the runway. Only delayed due to dust kicked up by the preceding Gnat, Sekhon lost no time in singling out the first Sabre pair, which was re-forming after the bombing run. The Gnat Leader, Flt Lt Ghuman, lost visual with his wingman just after take-off, remained out of the fight leaving Sekhon to handle the muddle all by himself.
