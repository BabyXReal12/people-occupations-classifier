Air chief marshal (Australia) Air Chief Marshal Mark Donald Binskin, {{post-nominals country=AUS size=100% AC}} (born 20 March 1960) is former senior officer in the Royal Australian Air Force. He served as Chief of Air Force (Australia) Chief of Air Force (2008–11), Vice Chief of the Defence Force (Australia) Vice Chief of the Defence Force (2011–14), and Chief of the Defence Force (Australia) Chief of the Defence Force from June 2014 until his retirement in July 2018.

==Early life==
Binskin was born in Sydney and grew up in Campbelltown, New South Wales, where he attended Campbelltown Public School. He completed secondary schooling at Hurlstone Agricultural High School.<ref>{{cite web url=http://campbelltown.yourguide.com.au/news/local/general/flying-high/1224629.html  archive-url=https://archive.is/20120707044513/http://campbelltown.yourguide.com.au/news/local/general/flying-high/1224629.html  dead-url=yes  archive-date=7 July 2012  title=Flying high  accessdate=1 May 2008  last=Gharahkani  first=Soraiya  date=16 April 2008  work=Cambelltown Macarthur Advertiser }}</ref>

==Service history==
Binskin joined the Royal Australian Navy (RAN) in May 1978, and on completion of flying training was posted to fly McDonnell Douglas A-4G Skyhawk A-4G Skyhawk aircraft at Naval Air Station HMAS Albatross (air station) HMAS Albatross.<ref name="raaf.gov.au - Chief of Air Force bio" /> In January 1982, he was selected as the first RAN pilot to undergo an exchange with the Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF), flying Dassault Mirage III Mirage III aircraft. On completion of this exchange, and with the disbanding of the Navys fixed wing capability, he transferred to the RAAF in 1984.<ref name="raaf.gov.au - Chief of Air Force bio">{{cite web url=http://www.raaf.gov.au/Leaders/caf.aspx  title=Chief of Air Force  year=2009  publisher=raaf.gov.au  accessdate=12 April 2010  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100317143050/http://www.raaf.gov.au/leaders/caf.aspx  archivedate=17 March 2010 }}</ref>

Binskin served as the Commanding Officer of No. 77 Squadron RAAF No. 77 Squadron at Williamtown during the period 1998 and 1999, and later Air Combat Group RAAF Commander of Air Combat Group from 2004 until 2005.<ref name="raaf.gov.au - Chief of Air Force bio" />

Binskins flying qualifications include Fighter Combat Instructor and Tactical Reconnaissance Pilot. Additionally, he has served as the RAAF F/A-18 Hornet Demonstration Pilot, and in this position represented the RAAF throughout Australia, Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore and New Zealand, and has over 3,500 hours in single-seat fighter aircraft. He was awarded a Member of the Order of Australia for his performance in these two positions.<ref name=AM>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=879262&search_type=quick&showInd=true Its an Honour entry] – Member of the Order of Australia – 12 June 1989<br>Citation: In recognition of service to the RAAF as an F/A-18 display pilot and the No77 Squadron Fighter Combat Instructor.</ref>

During Australias 2003 invasion of Iraq 2003 contribution to the war in Iraq, Binskin served as Chief of Staff at Headquarters Australian Theatre. Following this, he served as the first dedicated non-United States Air Force Director of the US Central Air Force Combined Air and Space Operations Centre, where he was responsible for the conduct of all Coalition air operations in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation Enduring Freedom (ADF operations Operation Catalyst Catalyst and Operation Slipper Slipper). For his leadership capabilities in this appointment, he was awarded a Commendation for Distinguished Service.<ref name=CDS>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=1058360&search_type=quick&showInd=true Its an Honour entry] – Commendation for Distinguished Service – 26 January 2005<br>Citation: For distinguished performance of duties in warlike operations as the Director of the Coalition Combined Air Operations Center in the Middle East Area of Operations during Operation CATALYST.</ref>

Binskin was appointed to the position of RAAF Air Command Air Commander Australia on 26 July 2007.<ref>{{cite news   first=   last=   author2=   title=Bio for Air Vice Marshal Mark Binskin   date=1 November 2007   publisher=   url =http://www.safeskiesaustralia.org/speakers_binskin.html   work =Safeskies Aviation Safety Conference   pages =   accessdate = 1 May 2008   language = }} {{Dead link date=October 2010 bot=H3llBot}}</ref> He assumed the position of Chief of Air Force (Australia) Chief of the Air Force (CAF) on 4 July 2008;<ref>[http://raaf.gov.au/media/download/2008/Mar/20080319/index.htm New Defence leadership team announced]{{dead link date=June 2017  bot=InternetArchiveBot  fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> upon assuming this appointment, he was promoted to Air Marshal (Australia) air marshal on 4 July 2008.<ref name="raaf.gov.au - Chief of Air Force bio" />

Binskin was appointed an Officer of the Order of Australia in the 2010 Queens Birthday Honours for distinguished service to the Australian Defence Force in senior command and staff appointments.<ref name=AO>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=879262&search_type=quick&showInd=true Its an Honour Binksin AO] retrieved 14 June 2010</ref> He has additionally been honoured with Singapores Pingat Jasa Gemilang (Tentera) Meritorious Service Medal, and the Order of National Security Merit (Korea) Gugseon Medal of the Order of National Security Merit from South Korea.<ref name="RAAF bio">{{cite web url=http://www.airforce.gov.au/leaders/caf.aspx title=Chief of Air Force: Air Marshal Mark Binskin accessdate=14 October 2010 work=Leaders publisher=Royal Australian Air Force}}</ref>

On 1 June 2011, it was announced that Binskin would be appointed Vice Chief of the Defence Force (Australia) Vice Chief of the Defence Force (VCDF) from 4 July for a term of three years in the Defence leadership change over.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.minister.defence.gov.au/Smithtpl.cfm?CurrentId=11900  title=New Chiefs for the Australian Defence Force  accessdate=2 June 2011  work=  publisher=Department of Defence  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110601173912/http://www.minister.defence.gov.au/Smithtpl.cfm?CurrentId=11900  archivedate=1 June 2011  df= }}</ref> For his "eminent service to the Australian Defence Force" as CAF and VCDF, Binskin was advanced to a Companion of the Order of Australia in the 2014 Australia Day Honours.<ref name=AC>{{cite web url=http://www.gg.gov.au/sites/default/files/files/honours/ad/Media%20Notes%20-%20AC(Mil)%20(final).pdf  title=Companion (AC) in the Military Division of the Order of Australia  accessdate=27 January 2014  format=PDF  work=Australia Day 2014 Honours Lists  publisher=Australian Honours and Awards Secretariat  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140127075137/http://www.gg.gov.au/sites/default/files/files/honours/ad/Media%20Notes%20-%20AC%28Mil%29%20%28final%29.pdf  archivedate=27 January 2014  df= }}</ref>

On 4 April 2014, Prime Minister Tony Abbott announced that Binskin will take over as Chief of the Defence Force (CDF) when General David Hurleys term expires on 4 July 2014 and the current Chief of Navy (Australia) Chief of the Navy, Vice Admiral Ray Griggs will replace him as VCDF.<ref name=SBS-newcdf>{{cite news  author=Australian Associated Press AAP  title=Air Marshal Mark Binskin has been announced as the new head of the Australian defence force.  url=http://www.sbs.com.au/news/article/2014/04/04/air-marshal-mark-binskin-named-new-defence-force-chief  newspaper=SBS News  location=  publisher=Special Broadcasting Service  date=4 July 2014  accessdate=4 July 2014}}</ref> The change of command occurred slightly earlier than advised, with Binskin promoted to Air Chief Marshal (Australia) air chief marshal and appointed CDF during a ceremony on 30 June.<ref>{{cite web url=http://news.defence.gov.au/2014/06/30/chief-of-defence-force-change-of-command/ title=Chief of Defence Force change of command accessdate=30 June 2014 work=Defence Media Release publisher=Department of Defence deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811100347/http://news.defence.gov.au/2014/06/30/chief-of-defence-force-change-of-command/ archivedate=11 August 2014 df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Binskin is married to Gitte. They have two sons. His interests include camping, motor sports, dancing and motorcycle riding.<ref name="raaf.gov.au - Chief of Air Force bio" />
