
Gabriella (Gaby) Angelini (1911 - 3 December 1932) was an Italian aviator.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book title=Politics of the Visible: Writing Women, Culture, and Fascism last=Pickering-Iazzi first=Robin publisher=University of Minnesota Press year=1997 isbn= location=Minneapolis, MN pages=113 quote= via=}}</ref>

Angelini was born in Milan to a wealthy family. She earned her pilots license at the age of 19, and quickly became a popular figure in Italy.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web url=http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/gaby-angelini/ title=Angelini, Gaby nellEnciclopedia Treccani website=www.treccani.it access-date=2017-01-26}}</ref> She was the first Italian woman to complete a trans-European flight, travelling to eight European countries, for which she received a Golden Eagle Medal. In 1932 she left Italy on a solo flight to Delhi, India, travelling in a Breda Ba.15 aeroplane. Angelini encountered difficulties during a sandstorm in Libya en route, and crashed in the desert and died.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2507&dat=19321212&id=xaBAAAAAIBAJ&sjid=dqUMAAAAIBAJ&pg=3452,6207412 title=The Glasgow Herald - Google News Archive Search website=news.google.com access-date=2017-01-26}}</ref> More than 120,000 people attended her funeral in Milan.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://mauroquattrina.jimdo.com/gaby-angelini/ title=GABY ANGELINI website=mauroquattrina.jimdo.com language=it-IT access-date=2017-01-26}}</ref>

In 2014 a book was published about Angelinis life, titled La Legendda di Little Gaby (The Legend of Little Gaby).<ref>{{Cite web url=http://www.astilibri.com/cultura/little_gaby.htm title=La leggenda di Little Gaby website=www.astilibri.com access-date=2017-01-26}}</ref>
