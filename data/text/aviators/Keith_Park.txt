==Early life and army career==
Park was born in Thames, New Zealand. He was the son of James Park (geologist) James Park from Scotland, geologist for a mining company and later a professor at the University of Otago in Dunedin.

An undistinguished young man, but keen on guns and riding, Keith Park was educated at Kings College, Auckland until 1906
and then at Otago Boys High School, Dunedin where he served in the cadets. Later he joined the New Zealand Army Army as a New Zealand Army#Territorial Force Territorial soldier in the Royal Regiment of New Zealand Artillery New Zealand Field Artillery.
In 1911, at age 19, he went to sea as a purser aboard collier and passenger steamships, earning the family nickname "skipper".

When the First World War broke out, Park left the ships and joined his artillery battalion.
