Gregory "Pappy" Boyington (December 4, 1912 – January 11, 1988) was an American combat Pilot (aircraft) pilot who was a United States Marine Corps Fighter aircraft fighter flying ace ace during World War II. He received both the Medal of Honor and the Navy Cross (United States) Navy Cross.

Boyington was initially a US Marine Corps aviator with the Pacific fleet before being recruited by the legendary American Volunteer Group "Flying Tigers" (1st American Volunteer Group) in the Republic of China Air Force in Burma at the end of 1941 and part of 1942, during the Second Sino-Japanese War military conflict between Republic of China China and Empire of Japan Japan, and the beginning of World War II.

In September 1942, he rejoined the Marine Corps. In early 1943, he deployed to the South Pacific area South Pacific and began flying combat missions as a Marine Vought F4U Corsair F4U Corsair fighter pilot. In September 1943, he took command of Marine fighter squadron VMA-214 VMF-214 ("Black Sheep"). In January 1944, Boyington, outnumbered by Japanese Mitsubishi A6M Zero "Zero" planes, was shot down into the Pacific Ocean after downing one of the enemy planes. He was captured by a Japanese submarine crew and was held as a prisoner of war for more than a year and a half. He was released shortly after the surrender of Japan, and a few days before the official surrender documents were signed.

The television series Baa Baa Black Sheep (TV series) Baa Baa Black Sheep was inspired by Boyington and his men in the "Black Sheep" squadron. It ran for two seasons in the late 1970s.
