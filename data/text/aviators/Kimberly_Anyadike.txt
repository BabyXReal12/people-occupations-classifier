
Kimberly Anyadike (born 1994) is a pilot from Compton, California. In 2009, at the age of 15, she became the youngest Nigerian American woman to complete a transcontinental flight across the United States, from Los Angeles, California to Newport News, Virginia. Anyadike was accompanied by an adult safety pilot, and by a retired Air Force pilot who had served with the WWII Tuskegee Airmen. Her plane was autographed by about 50 Tuskegee airmen as she stopped at different cities across the US, and she completed the round-trip journey in a single-engine Cessna 172 in 13 days. In 2015, Anyadike received the first Young Aviators Award from the Tuskegee Airmen in recognition of her achievements. In 2016, she graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles with an undergraduate degree.

== Early life  ==
Born in 1994, Anyadike grew up in Compton, California.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web url=http://milwaukeecourieronline.com/index.php/2015/07/04/compton-15-year-old-became-the-youngest-african-american-female-to-pilot-plane-cross-country/ title=Compton 15-Year-Old Became the Youngest African American Female to Pilot Plane Cross-Country {{!}} Milwaukee Courier Weekly Newspaper website=milwaukeecourieronline.com language=en access-date=2017-07-11}}</ref> She has an older brother and sister.<ref name=":1" /> Her parents are originally from Nigeria,<ref name=":5">{{Cite news url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201508172091.html title=Nigeria: Kimberly Anyadike – First African-American Teen to Fly Across the United States last=Ogunsina first=Bukola date=2015-08-17 work=Leadership (Abuja) access-date=2017-07-14}}</ref> and their surname Anyadike means "eye of the warrior" in the Igbo language Igbo language.<ref>{{Cite news url=https://amazingafrica.planetfem.com/kimberly-anyadike/ title=Amazing Young Africans: Kimberly Anyadike date=2015-08-09 work=Amazing Africa – African inspired fashion, design and style access-date=2017-07-29 language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=https://newsofnigeria.com/kimberly-anyadike-the-nigerian-the-first-female-african-american-teen-to-fly-an-airplane/ title=Kimberly Anyadike the Nigerian, the first Female African-American Teen to Fly an Airplane website=newsofnigeria.com access-date=2017-07-14}}</ref>

Anyadike took dance lessons at the Lula Washington Dance Academy, served as a junior lifeguard at Venice beach Venice Beach for five years, and volunteered as a youth leader at her local church.<ref name=":5" /> At a young age, she showed a strong interest in medical science and anatomy; her mother taught her to identify and name all 206 bones in the body. Anyadike later attended science classes at the Charles R. Drew University of Medicine and Science Charles Drew University Saturday Science Academy, an L.A.-based Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics STEM subjects program for youth ranging in age from preschool to grade 12.<ref name=":1" />

== Aviation training ==
Anyadike took her first flying lessons at the age of 12, attending an after-school aviation program offered by Tomorrows Aeronautical Museum for disadvantaged youths.<ref name=":0">{{cite news url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/world/kimberly-anyadike-15-youngest-african-american-female-pilot-plane-cross-country-article-1.399825 title=Kimberly Anyadike, 15, becomes youngest African American female to pilot plane cross-country date=14 July 2009 publisher=New York Daily News}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web url=https://wn.com/kimberly_anyadike_on_cnn_with_kiran_chetry title=Kimberly Anyadike On Cnn With Kiran Chetry website=World News language=en access-date=2017-07-11}}</ref> She was inspired to take lessons after reading about Jonathan Strickland, an earlier participant of the program who became the youngest person to fly a fixed-wing aircraft and a helicopter solo in a single day.<ref name=":1" /> Anyadike paid for her flight lessons with "museum dollars" earned by washing airplanes and working odd jobs around the Compton Airport.<ref name=":4" /> As part of the programs requirements, she maintained a strong academic record throughout the period.<ref name=":5" />

Despite having a fear of heights, Anyadike says that being in the cockpit of a plane is "an amazing experience".<ref name=":1" />

== Transcontinental flight ==
As Anyadike gradually built up 100 hours of flight time, she conceived the idea of flying across the country.<ref name=":6">{{Cite journal date=2009-10-01 title=A Dream Takes Wing! url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-1864342901.html}}</ref> She wanted to inspire other youth, while also honouring the Tuskegee Airmen, an African-American air combat unit that served during World War II.<ref name=":7" /> Departing on June 29, 2009,<ref name=":8" /> 15-year-old Anyadike flew a single-engine Cessna 172 from Compton, California to Newport News, Virginia and back, completing the journey in 13 days.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web url=http://newsroom.ucla.edu/stories/ucla-senior-has-a-new-flight-plan title=UCLA senior has a new flight plan last=Kendall first=Rebecca website=UCLA Newsroom language=en access-date=2017-07-11}}</ref><ref name=":2" /> She was accompanied by adult safety pilot Ronnell Norman, and Major Levi H. Thornhill, an 87-year-old retired United States Air Force U.S. Air Force pilot who had served as a member of the Tuskegee Airmen.<ref name=":2" />

Anyadike made at least a dozen scheduled stops along the way, and navigated through thunderstorms as she flew across Texas.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/lanow/2009/07/teenage-girl-flies-a-plane-across-the-county.html title=Teen becomes youngest African American female to complete flight across U.S. date=2009-07-11 website=LA Times Blogs – L.A. NOW language=en-US access-date=2017-07-13}}</ref> In Washington, D.C., Congresswoman Laura Richardson came to congratulate the young pilot, and in Tuskegee, Alabama, Mayor Omar Neal proclaimed July 2 to be "Kimberly Anyadike Day".<ref name=":8">{{Cite journal date=2009-07-23 title=15 year-old girl has made history by flying sea to shining sea url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-1830974491.html}}</ref> During stops in different cities, Anyadikes plane was autographed by about 50 different Tuskegee Airmen.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":3" /> Major Thornhill acted as Anyadikes representative to make the necessary introductions with regional chapters of the airmen.<ref name=":6" /> Although the record had never been officially tracked before her journey,<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":7">{{Cite journal date=2009-07-12 title=Young pilots trip across country honors history.(Front) url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-203437537.html}}</ref> Anyadike is considered the youngest black American woman — and possibly the youngest person of any race or gender — to have successfully piloted a plane coast-to-coast across the United States.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":7" />

Upon her arrival home on July 11, 2009, Anyadike received an award from the County of Los Angeles, and was personally invited by Arnold Schwarzenegger Governor Arnold Schwarzenegger to visit the California State Capitol.<ref name=":8" /> In May 2015, Anyadike received the Tuskegee Airmens inaugural Young Aviators Award in Tuskegee, Alabama, in recognition of her achievements as a pilot.<ref name=":1" /> In 2017, Anyadike was interviewed about her transcontinental flight for a special Disney Channel segment for Black History Month entitled True Heroes Are Timeless.<ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.essence.com/entertainment/disney-channel-launches-black-history-month-programming title=First Look: Disney Channel Launches Be Inspired Programming For Black History Month date=2017-02-01 work=Essence.com access-date=2017-07-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news url=http://www.twistmagazine.com/posts/nathaniel-potvin-s-black-history-month-video-for-disney-channel-at-tomorrow-s-aeronautical-museum-124538/photos/nathaniel-potvin-230073?age_gate%5Bbirth_year%5D= title=Nathaniel Potvin Shares About His Experience Working On Disney Channels New Black History Month Feature date=2017-02-02 work=Twist access-date=2017-07-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web url=https://goodblacknews.org/tag/floyd-norman/ title=Floyd Norman {{!}} GOOD BLACK NEWS website=goodblacknews.org language=en access-date=2017-07-15}}</ref>

== College education ==
For her college education, Anyadike enrolled at the University of California, Los Angeles University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), majoring in physiological science with minors in African-American studies and Spanish. During her senior year, she was also working as a registered emergency medical technician, and was volunteering for her local chapter of the The Flying Samaritans Flying Samaritans to bring medical care to Tijuana, Mexico.<ref name=":1" /> She graduated with her undergraduate degree from UCLA in June 2016.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://newsroom.ucla.edu/stories/ucla-graduates-of-2016-are-told-that-the-world-needs-you title=UCLA graduates are told ‘the world needs you’ last=Hewitt first=Alison website=UCLA Newsroom language=en access-date=2017-07-29}}</ref> When asked about future aspirations, Anyadike has said that she plans to enroll in medical school and become a cardiovascular surgeon.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":5" />
