Terry Deitz (born October 10, 1959) is a retired U.S. Navy pilot, television host, and two-time contestant on the American reality television show Survivor (U.S. TV series) Survivor, where he placed third in the Survivor: Panama 12th season. He also competed in the Survivor: Cambodia 31st season, placing 15th.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://www.survivorfever.net/s12_bootsheet.html  title=Survivor: Panama Boot Sheet website=Survivorfever.net accessdate=11 July 2012 }}</ref> He is currently a commercial airline pilot and host of Great Planes on the Military Channel.

==Biography==
Deitz was born and raised in Matawan, New Jersey. He was a student at St. John Vianney High School (New Jersey) St. John Vianney High School and also attended the United States Naval Academy, where he played Varsity Division I baseball.<ref name=sdsu>{{cite web last1= first1= title=Kayla Deitz Bio – San Diego State Official Athletic Site url=http://www.goaztecs.com/sports/w-lacros/mtt/kayla_deitz_864064.html website=San Diego State Official Athletic Site – San Diego State University publisher=CBS Interactive accessdate=May 14, 2016}}</ref> He graduated Annapolis in 1982.<ref name=satva>[http://www.tvsa.co.za/actorprofile.asp?actorID=5785 Terry Deitz actor profile], The South African TV Authority. Accessed July 29, 2007. "He then attended St. John Vianney High School in Holmdel, New Jersey, where he earned 11 Varsity letters in football (quarterback), baseball (catcher) and wrestling."</ref>

Deitz served on the USS Carl Vinson (CVN-70) USS Carl Vinson with VF-51 flying the F-14 Tomcat and then served at the west coast Replacement Air Group as an instructor. He attended pilot school in Pensacola, Florida Pensacola in 1984.  He left active service in 1992, and continued flying in the Navy Reserves with VR-52, on Logistics missions around the world. He retired in 2001 with the rank of Commander#United States Navy Commander and is currently a pilot with American Airlines.

Deitz married his wife Trish in 1991. The couple have two children, Kayla and Daniel, and reside in Simsbury, Connecticut.

Deitz has been an athlete throughout his life and competed in baseball, basketball, football, swimming, and wrestling. He earned 11 varsity awards in high school and was named to the All State Baseball 1st Team. He was the quarterback of his high school team and led them to their first undefeated regular season and first state playoffs.<ref name=satva />  Also, his daughter Kayla is a lacrosse Field lacrosse#Midfielders midfielder at San Diego State University.<ref name=sdsu />

==Survivor==

===Panama===
Deitz competed on Survivor: Panama Survivor: Panama — Exile Island in 2005. He was noted for dominating the Survivor (TV series)#Challenges challenges, with an unbroken string winning five Immunity (reality television) immunity challenges and four reward challenges. He was also the first contestant to find a Survivor (TV series)#Hidden immunity idols hidden immunity idol at Survivor (TV series)#Exile Island Exile Island. However, because of his prowess in the immunity challenges, he never had to play it. Deitz finished in third place, behind Aras Baskauskas (winner) and Danielle DiLorenzo (runner-up).

With five consecutive immunity challenge wins, Deitz is currently tied with Colby Donaldson of Survivor: The Australian Outback for the record of the highest-amount of consecutive immunity challenge wins in Survivor history. He is also tied with Donaldson, Tom Westman, Ozzy Lusth, Mike Holloway (Survivor contestant) Mike Holloway, and Brad Culpepper for the record of most immunity challenge wins in a season overall. Also, with four additional reward challenge wins, Deitz is tied with Holloway for the record of the highest amount of overall post-merge challenge wins in a single season. However, he is the only one of these five individuals to have never reached the finals.

After his appearance on Survivor: Panama, Deitz was considered as one of the ten past castaways to return for the 16th season, Survivor: Micronesia, but was ultimately cut.<ref>{{cite web title=Jeff Probst Media Teleconference url=http://www.survivorfever.net/s16_jp_teleconference.html website=Survivorfever.net accessdate=2010-05-18 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100216131458/http://www.survivorfever.net/s16_jp_teleconference.html archivedate=2010-02-16 df=}}</ref> He was also considered to return for the 27th season, Survivor: Blood vs. Water, where he would have competed alongside his daughter, but was cut from consideration since his daughter was then under the minimum age requirement.<ref>{{cite web author=  url=http://survivoroz.wordpress.com/2013/12/17/survivor-blood-vs-water-episode-14-reunion-recap-featuring-terry-deitz/#more-18339  title=Survivor Blood vs. Water-Episode 14 & Reunion Recap featuring Terry Deitz! &#124; Survivor Oz  website=Survivoroz.wordpress.com  date=  accessdate=2015-12-19}}</ref>

===Cambodia===
On May 6, 2015, it was revealed that Deitz was one of the 16 men eligible to be voted in by viewers for Survivor<nowiki></nowiki>s 31st season, Survivor: Cambodia. He was one of two representatives on the ballot from Panama, the other being Shane Powers. It was later revealed that Deitz and Powers had formed a pre-game alliance consisting of themselves, Jeff Varner (from Survivor: The Australian Outback) and Kelly Wiglesworth (from Survivor: Borneo). Although Powers did not make it onto the season, the other three members all were voted in<ref>{{cite web last=Ross  first=Dalton  url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/05/20/survivor-cambodia-second-chance-cast-revealed  title=Survivor: Cambodia—Second Chance cast revealed  website=EW.com  date=2015-05-20  accessdate=2015-12-19}}</ref> and originally were placed on the same tribe, Ta Keo.<ref>{{cite web  url=http://insidesurvivor.com/2015/09/survivor-second-chance-episode-1-ghost-town/  title=Survivor Second Chance: Episode 1 – Ghost Town  website=Insidesurvivor.com  date=  accessdate=2015-12-19  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151029144158/http://insidesurvivor.com/2015/09/survivor-second-chance-episode-1-ghost-town/  archivedate=2015-10-29  df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=921_65C9pCg  title=The Survivor Funeral of Shane Powers  publisher=YouTube  date=  accessdate=2015-12-19}}</ref> After the tribal split on Day 7, from two tribes to three, Deitz was one of only two contestants who remained on the Ta Keo tribe, the other being Kelley Wentworth. On Day 13, host Jeff Probst came to the Ta Keo camp in the middle of the night to inform Terry about his son Dannys heart condition, and Deitz immediately pulled himself from the game.
